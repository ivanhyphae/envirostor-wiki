## Key claims
- Bimonthly groundwater monitoring from ten wells (MW-1 through MW-10) in Stanislaus County.
- Groundwater flow toward the southeast.
- Dissolved metals and general minerals predominantly below water quality thresholds.
- Barium ranged from 60–300 µg/L (MCL: 1,000 µg/L), strontium 430–1,100 µg/L (HA: 4,000 µg/L), arsenic 2.1–5.1 µg/L (MCL: 10 µg/L).
- Exceedances: manganese (MW-8), nitrate (up to 24 mg/L in MW-5, exceeding 10 mg/L MCL), TDS (up to 640 mg/L in MW-5 and MW-10).

## Methodology
- Depth-to-water measurements, well purging, sample collection using disposable bailers.
- Dissolved metals via ICP-MS (EPA 6020), cold vapor AA (EPA 7470), anions via IC (EPA 300.0), TDS (SM 2540C).
- QA/QC: blanks, LCS, duplicates, matrix spikes. Matrix spike recoveries outside limits (M1/M2/M3 flags).

## Results
- Primary chemicals of concern (barium, lead, strontium) below MCLs/HAs.
- Nitrate exceedances in multiple wells (MW-5: 24 mg/L vs. 10 mg/L MCL).
- All 17 target SVOCs not detected.

## Limitations
- Matrix interference caused some QA/QC flags requiring dilution (D6 flag).
- Data quality limitations noted for certain analytes.
