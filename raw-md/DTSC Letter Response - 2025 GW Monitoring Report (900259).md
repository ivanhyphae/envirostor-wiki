Thank you for your submission of the Well Destruction Report and of the Storm Water Sampling Report both dated June 18, 2025, and prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. on behalf of California Department of Transportation (Caltrans). The subject property is located south of the intersection of State Route 99 and Kansas Avenue in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California and referred to as the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles (Site) also known as the State Route 132 West Expressway by the California Department of Toxic Substance Control (DTSC).

The Site consisted of three stockpiles that contain soil contaminated with barium since the 1960s. During 2020, part of the implementation of Phase I of the Remedial Action Plan design included moving the soil from the Stockpile 3 to the structural section of Stockpiles 1 and 2. The remaining two stockpiles will be completed with the implementation of Phase II of the Remedial Action Plan to be utilized as a 4-lane highway, State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway.

In 2012, ten groundwater monitoring wells were installed to monitor for potential contamination moving to groundwater while the plan for encapsulation of the stockpiles was being prepared. The Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board, in addition to DTSC, reviewed the groundwater monitoring reports and after years of data supporting no metals were leaching into the groundwater from the stockpiles, agreed to their decommissioning. Six of the ten wells were decommissioned in 2019. This was necessary to continue the construction of the Caltrans highway. The Well Destruction Report outlines the procedures to destroy the remaining four wells.

In addition to groundwater sampling, surface water run-off is also collected to ensure the metals in the soil are not displaced into the surface water. The Storm Water Sampling Report describes the results of storm water sampling used to evaluate the integrity of the stockpile containment system during qualifying rain events. The results of the Storm Water Sampling Report were all within reporting limits.

DTSC's review process is coordinated by the Project Manager and includes input from technical staff. The Storm Water Sampling Report was reviewed by geologists from our Geological Services Branch (GSB) and toxicologists from our Human and Ecological Risk Office (HERO) and is hereby approved.

The Well Destruction Report was reviewed by geologists from our GSB. DTSC conditionally approves the Well Destruction Report, provided the following comment is incorporated in the final version and provided to DTSC.
