# **SITE BACKGROUND**

Around 1930, Barium Products Ltd. occupied the property at 1200 Barium Road (now Graphics Drive) in the city of Modesto, California. The property is located just east of SR 99 between Woodland Avenue and Kansas Avenue. Barium Products Ltd. was a chemical manufacturing company that processed a variety of ores and minerals including barite and celestite. Materials produced included barium and strontium compounds used for greases, lubricating oil, paint pigment blanks, etc. Sodium sulfide (Na2S) was also generated as a by-product of barite processing and was sold as a caustic that was used as a reagent in the mining industry.

Barium Products Ltd. was purchased by Westvaco Chlorine Products Corporation in the 1943. In 1948, Westvaco merged with Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC Corporation). From the 1950's to the late 1970's, the liquid residue from the processing operations was discharged to unlined evaporation / percolation ponds along the western side of the FMC facility, just north of the Site (Figure 1).

In 1961, a 4.3-acre parcel at the southwest corner of the FMC site was purchased by Caltrans for right-ofway (ROW) needed to construct SR 99. Aerial photographs from 1957 indicate that a portion of the southernmost holding pond on the FMC property was within the area purchased for the SR 99 ROW. Soil in and around the pond was excavated during construction and, according to provisions of the construction contract, stockpiled within the ROW at the location of the future SR 99/132 expressway project. ## **DEPTH TO GROUNDWATER MEASUREMENTS – NOVEMBER 2, AND DECEMBER 13, 2022**

On November 2, 2022, Stantec performed the groundwater gauging event on wells MW-4, MW-6, and MW-9. Stantec was unable to locate well MW-10 due to a large volume of wood mulch that had been spread over the area. The monitoring well caps on wells MW-4, MW-6, and MW-9 were opened to allow groundwater levels to equilibrate with ambient pressure. Stantec personnel inspected each well box for signs of damage or tampering. No damage or tampering was noted. Stantec personnel gauged the depth to water and total depth in each well using a GeoTech water level meter, to the nearest 0.01 foot below the top of casing (BTOC), from the surveyed reference points at each well casing. The groundwater gauging field sheet is included in Appendix A. The water level meter was decontaminated in a LiquinoxTM and deionized (DI) wash solution and then double rinsed in DI water prior to use at each well to minimize the potential for cross-contamination.
