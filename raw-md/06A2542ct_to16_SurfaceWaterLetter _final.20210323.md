## **SITE BACKGROUND**

Around 1930, Barium Products Ltd. occupied the property at 1200 Barium Road (now Graphics Drive) in the city of Modesto, California. The property is located just east of SR 99 between Woodland Avenue and Kansas Avenue. Barium Products Ltd. was a chemical manufacturing company that processed a variety of ores and minerals including barite and celestite. Materials produced included barium and strontium compounds used for greases, lubricating oil, paint pigment blanks, etc. Sodium sulfide (Na2S) was also generated as a byproduct of barite processing and was sold as a caustic and used as a reagent in the mining industry.

Barium Products Ltd. was purchased by Westvaco Chlorine Products Corporation in the 1943. In 1948, Westvaco merged with Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC Corporation). From the 1950's to March 24, 2021 Mr. Richard Stewart, PG Page 2 of 4

## **PAST SURFACE WATER SAMPLING ACTIVITIES**

The original surface water sampling event at the soil stockpiles was conducted in March 2006 by Shaw in general accordance with its January 2006 SAP. A total of seven surface water samples were collected during a qualifying rain event (i.e. an event meeting the qualifying criteria of visible runoff and 72 hours of prior dry weather). Shaw constructed shallow depressions within the Caltrans ROW in order to collect runoff from the stockpiles as there was no surface water migration beyond the ROW. The samples were analyzed for dissolved metals, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), nitrate, sulfate, and sulfide.

Subsequently, Geocon collected surface water samples from five designated surface runoff locations (PL1 through PL5) and two background locations (BG1 and BG2) on an on-call basis between April 2013 and April 2019 in accordance with the SAP Addendum. The sample locations are shown on Figure 2. Sample locations PL1 and PL2 were along North Emerald Avenue to collect runoff from Stockpiles 1 and 2, respectively. Sample location PL3 was along the southern edge of Stockpile 2. Sample location PL4 is located near Stockpile 3 where the storm water enters the northern portion of the drainage basin. Sample location PL5 is located near the gate valve at the southern end of the drainage basin. The gate valve, if opened, would release the storm water from the drainage basin to the Tuolumne River. The gate valve would only be opened if the storm water basin were filled to near capacity and would jeopardize the northbound lanes of SR 99. The sample locations were approved by the DTSC and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB). Geocon did not observe runoff migration from the Caltrans ROW during its inspections.

## **FIELD ACTIVITIES**

On December 17, 2020, Stantec mobilized to the Site at approximately 0845 hours to perform the surface water inspection. The rain event was minor with no rain falling at the time of inspection. The California Irrigation Management Information System (CIMIS), that monitors the Water Use and Efficiency Branch, California Department of Water Resources (DWR) has an integrated network of over 140 automated active weather stations located throughout California. Based on the CIMIS Modesto weather station the rain began on the night of December 16 and continued into the morning of December 17, 2020. Precipitation was measured as 0.04 inches and 0.05 inches on December 16th and 17th respectively. However, the University of UCANR Modesto weather station reported 0.0 inches of rain on December 17, 2020.

The Site has been under construction for the past year with construction of the future roadways and Site improvements. In areas where improvements have occurred, there are straw waddles used as Best Management Practices (BMPs) for storm water management. Stantec inspected the area around the former surface water sample locations PL1 through PL5 and the background surface water locations BG1 and BG2. The ground surface and pavements were wet however, there was no visible discharge/runoff of surface water occurring from the BMPs or the Site. A photographic record is attached as Appendix A.

The following was observed during the surface water inspection:

- The area around the background surface water locations BG1 and BG2 has not changed, there was no visible water ponding in these areas during the inspection.
- The concrete abutments for the overpass have been constructed along the east and west side of Emerald Avenue. The former surface water sample locations for PL1 and PL2 no longer exist. Construction is ongoing within this area and straw waddles have been placed at the base of the slopes and abutments to minimize potential offsite discharges of storm water.
- The area along the southern edge of Stockpile 2, near sample location PL3, has been regraded and sloped. The footing for a perimeter wall has been excavated within this area. No runoff or ponding water was observed.
- The storm water basin in the area of PL4 and PL5 has been enlarged and is identified as Basin 6B. The former gate valve that was located at the southern end of the former drainage basin has been removed. No water was flowing from the discharger pipe at PL4 however, a few isolated puddles were observed within the basin.
- There was no visible discharges/runoff of surface water throughout the project area.

## **SURFACE WATER MANAGEMENT**

BMPs have been installed around the project construction areas. No discharge/runoff of surface water beyond the BMPs and/or Site were observed during the December 17, 2020 surface water inspection. Due to the grading on the Site, the ability to collect future samples at the currently established locations is unknown.
