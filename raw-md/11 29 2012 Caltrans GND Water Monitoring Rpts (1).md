Dear Mr. Stewart:

The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region (RWQCB) has reviewed: 1) "Groundwater Monitoring Report - May 2012, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route (SR) 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California"; 2) "Additional Well Installation and Groundwater Monitoring Report - June 2012, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California"; and 3) "Groundwater Monitoring Report - July 2012, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California" prepared by Geocon Consultants and dated November 2012. The subject reports present analytical results of groundwater monitoring well sampling implemented in May, June, and July 2012 respectively. The June 2012 report also discusses the installation of monitoring wells identified as MW-9, located south of Kansas Avenue and west of SR 99; and MW-10, located south of Kansas Avenue and east of SR 99.

The soil stockpiles consist of excess native soils and pond tailings that were generated when Caltrans constructed a segment of SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue in the early 1960's. Excavating the segment traversed a portion of a 4.3-acre parcel purchased from Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC). An evaporation pond was located in the southernmost corner of the parcel. FMC (and its predecessors) was a chemical manufacturing company that processed barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores and other minerals. The primary chemicals of concern (COC) are barium, lead, and strontium.
