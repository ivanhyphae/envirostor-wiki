# COMMENT RESPONSES

The following provides a summary of how the DTSC and RWQCB comments (in italics) have been addressed in our Final Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan (Final Workplan) dated September 18, 2012:

# Field Activities

*DTSC requests that additional discretionary boring locations be included to address features associated with erosion and soil/sediment migration based on field observations. Additionally, explain the rationale for differences in sampling depths associated with "Fenceline Borings" (5-foot-deep), "Perimeter Borings" (3-foot-deep), and "Cadmium Borings" (20-feet-deep).* 

Representatives of DTSC, Caltrans and Geocon performed a site survey on September 17, 2012 to review staked proposed boring locations as identified in the Draft Workplan. Fenceline and Perimeter borings were added or adjusted at each of the stockpile locations based on field conditions. The new and revised boring locations in addition to the results of the site survey are presented in the Final Workplan. Details regarding the rationale for proposed boring depths are further provided.

*It is DTSC's understanding that this sampling methodology applies to all boring categories and locations.* 

Additional details have been added in the Final Workplan to clarify that the Fenceline and Perimeter borings will be continuous cored to respective depths of 5 feet and 3 feet. Soil samples will be collected from depth intervals of 0 to 6 inches, and from one-foot intervals (1 to 2 feet, 2 to 3 feet, 3 to 4 feet and 4 to 5 feet) thereafter. Soil samples from the Cadmium borings will be collected from 0 to 6 inches and from approximate 5-foot intervals (4 to 5 feet, 9 to 10 feet, 14 to 15 feet and 19 to 20 feet), thereafter.

*DTSC and RWQCB requests that all rinse water associated with equipment decontamination be contained, analyzed and managed appropriately.* 

Text has been added to this section of the Final Workplan stating that decontamination water will be contained, sampled and analyzed for metals, and appropriately disposed of.

## Laboratory Analysis

*It is DTSC's understanding that the laboratory analysis methodology (e.g., EPA Test Method 6010B and 7471) described above applies to all boring location categories and may be applied to all samples for all boring location categories pending initial analysis and discussion with DTSC and RWQCB.* 

Text has been added to this section of the Final Workplan to clarify that the laboratory analysis methodology applies to all boring location categories and that additional analysis will be performed after review of the initial analysis and as directed by the DTSC and RWQCB.

## Summary Report

*DTSC requests that the referenced summary report be limited to the findings associated with the "Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan". Information related to the nature and extent of contamination and human risk health assessment evaluation should be presented in separate reports (e.g., an "Updated Site Characterization Summary Report" which summarize this data and previous characterization data and an "Updated Human Health Risk Assessment" which incorporates this data with previous data).* 

Text has been added to this section of the Final Workplan to clarify that the Summary Report will be limited to the findings of the Supplemental Site Investigation and that information related to the nature and extent of contamination and human health risk assessment will be presented in separate reports.

### Appendix A

*DTSC requests that the laboratory reporting format also include analysis for soluble metals with respective laboratory MDL and PQL. All detection limits need to be at concentrations suitable for use in preparing a human health risk assessment and for comparison to water quality goals. All concentrations detected between MDL and PQL should be reported and noted with a "J-flag" as an estimate.* 

We have added an example laboratory report for soluble metals with method detection limit (MDL) and practical quantitation limit (PQL) values. Text has further been added in the Laboratory Analysis section of the Final Workplan indicating that "J-flag" will be noted on the laboratory reports for estimated concentrations detected between the MDL and PQL.

## Other Comments

DTSC requests that a site "Health and Safety Plan" be included as an appendix to the Workplan, including measures to control dust which may be generated during drilling activities.

We have appended our April 2012 Health and Safety Plan (HSP) for Modesto Stockpiles to the Final Workplan. In addition to references in the HSP regarding using wet sampling methods to control dust, text has been added to the Field Activities section of the Final Workplan to present additional dust control measures.

# SUPPLEMENTAL SITE CHARACTERIZATION WORKPLAN, MODESTO SOIL STOCKPILES, STATE ROUTE 132 AND 99, STANISLAUS COUNTY, CALIFORNIA

Dear Mr. Stewart:

The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region (RWQCB) has reviewed the "Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State route 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California" (Workplan), prepared by Geocon Consultants and dated August 2102. It was submitted as a draft document to DTSC and RWQCB on 8/28/2012. The draft Workplan is to provide additional characterization of soil stockpiles at the location of the proposed State Route 132/99 West Expressway to evaluate and finalize a remedy for the management of hazardous substances in the soil stockpiles. The additional characterization data is to evaluate potential lateral and vertical migration of contaminants from the soil stockpiles and to update the Human Health Risk Assessment.

The soil stockpiles consist of excess native soils and pond tailings that were generated when Caltrans constructed a segment of SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue in the early 1960's. Excavating the segment traversed a portion of a 4.3-acre parcel purchased from Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation Inc. (FMC). An evaporation pond was located in the southernmost corner of the parcel. FMC (and its predecessors) was a chemical manufacturing company that processed barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores and other minerals. The primary Chemicals of concern (COC) are barium, lead, and strontium.

#### Comments

Following are DTSC's comments arranged according to the format of the draft Workplan.

### Field Activities

This section defines the categories of boring locations which consist of 1) "Fenceline" Borings" at stockpile perimeter/fenceline locations adjacent to residential and commercial Mr. Richard Stewart, P.G. September 12, 2012 Page 2

development; 2) "Perimeter Borings" at perimeter and stockpile end locations; and 3) "Cadmium Borings" in the vicinity of elevated cadmium detections reported during Caltrans' 2004 investigations and the number/depth of borings for each category. It also discusses other field activities and states that some proposed boring locations may require field adjustments based on site conditions, utilities, buried structures, and access. DTSC requests that additional discretionary boring locations be included to address features associated with erosion and soil/sediment migration based on field observations.

Additionally, explain the rationale for differences in sampling depths associated with "Fenceline Borings" (5-foot-deep), "Perimeter Borings" (3-foot-deep), and "Cadmium Borings" (20-foot-deep).

This section states that borings will be advanced using a "direct-push rig". Continuous soil samples will be collected in new three foot long acetate sleeves. Soil samples will be collected from the surface (0 to 6 inches) and at one foot intervals thereafter to a depth of five feet. A minimum of one discrete six inch long soil sample will be cut from the top end of each core at depths greater than five feet and where indications of potential contamination are observed. Hand-auger borings may be performed in areas inaccessible to the direct-push rig. It is DTSC's understanding that this sampling methodology applies to all boring categories and locations.

This section also states that rinse water (associated with equipment decontamination) will be discharged to the stockpile surface. DTSC and RWQCB requests that all rinse water associated with equipment decontamination be contained, analyzed, and managed appropriately.

### Laboratory Analysis

This section discusses laboratory analysis for each of the three boring categories as noted below.

- 1. Fenceline Borings analyzed for Title 22 metals and strontium using U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) test Method 6010B and 7471 for mercury.
- 2. Perimeter Borings surface samples collected will be analyzed for barium using EPA Test Method 6010B.
- 3. Cadmium Borings will be analyzed for cadmium and barium using EPA Test Method 6010B.

This section also states that the remaining soil samples will be held pending results of the initial analysis and additional analysis for total and/or soluble metals will be assigned after initial review by Caltrans, DTSC, and the RWQCB. It is DTSC's understanding that the laboratory analysis methodology (e.g., EPA Test Method 6010B and 7471) described above applies to all boring location categories and may be applied to all samples for all boring location categories pending initial analysis and discussion with DTSC and RWQCB.

### Summary Report

This section discusses information to be included in a summary report which presents findings and conclusions. The report will include: project background; nature and extent of contaminants; screening level human risk evaluation; tabulated soil sample analytical data; a vicinity map and site plan describing boring locations; site photographs; and appendices which include boring logs, laboratory reports, and chain of custody documents. DTSC requests that the referenced summary report be limited to the findings associated with the "Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan". Information related to the nature and extent of contamination and human risk health assessment evaluation should be presented in separate reports (e.g., an "Updated Site Characterization Summary Report" which summarize this data and previous characterization data and an "Updated Human Health Risk Assessment" which incorporates this data with previous data).

# Appendix A

Appendix A includes an example laboratory format for total metals analyses, including laboratory method detection limits (MDL) and practical quantitation limits (PQL). DTSC requests that the laboratory reporting format also include analysis for soluble metals with respective laboratory MDL and PQL. All detection limits need to be at concentrations suitable for use in preparing a human health risk assessment and for comparison to water quality goals. All concentrations detected between MDL and PQL should be reported and noted with a "J-flag" as an estimate.

### Other Comments

DTSC requests that a site "Health and Safety Plan" be included as an appendix to the Workplan, including measures to control dust which may be generated during drilling activities.
