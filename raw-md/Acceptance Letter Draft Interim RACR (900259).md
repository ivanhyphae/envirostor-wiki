ACCEPTANCE OF THE DRAFT INTERIM REMOVAL ACTION COMPLETION REPORT – CALTRANS MODESTO SOIL STOCKPILES, PHASE 1 INTERIM STATE ROUTE 132 WEST PROJECT, MODESTO, STANISLAUS COUNTY, CALIFORNIA (SITE CODE: 900259)

### Dear Mr. Juhrend:

The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) has reviewed the Draft Removal Action Completion Report – Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Interim Phase 1 State Route 132 West Project (Draft Phase 1 RACR) submitted by Geocon Consultants Inc. (Geocon) on behalf of the Department of Transportation (Caltrans). The Draft Phase 1 RACR describes the consolidation and capping activities that have been completed to date for the barium containing soils (BCS) as part of the Interim Phase 1 Construction effort at the State Route 132 West project in Modesto, California (Site).

## **Site Description**

Prior to the remediation activities, the Site consisted of BCS Stockpiles 1 through 3 within the Caltrans State Route 132 right-of-way in Modesto. Stockpile 1 was located approximately 150 feet westerly of Emerald Avenue and was roughly 600 feet long in the east-west direction, 160 feet wide, and had an estimated fill volume of 34,000 cubic yards (yd3). BCS Stockpile 2, located between Emerald Avenue and State Route 99, was roughly 1,650 feet long in the east-west direction, 160 feet wide, and had an estimated fill volume of 102,000 yd3. BCS Stockpile 3, formerly located on the east side

of State Route 99, was roughly 1,100 feet long, 120 feet wide, and had an estimated fill volume of 24,000 yd3.

### **Site Remediation Activities – Soil Removal and Consolidation**

In accordance with the approved Remedial Action Plan (RAP) and approved Interim Phase 1 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP), the Draft Phase 1 RACR describes the work that was completed to excavate Stockpile 3, the western and eastern ends of Stockpile 2, and the removal of soil from the southern slopes of stockpile 1 and 2 to support the construction of the new State Route 132 Expressway. The removal of BCS Stockpile 3 included the excavation of approximately 27,675 yd3 of soil that was transported by truck and placed as embankment fill within the BCS Stockpile 1 containment zone. Twenty-nine confirmation soil samples were collected from the bottom of the BCS Stockpile 3 excavation area and analyzed for barium and lead. Three of the confirmation samples showed barium concentrations above the cleanup goal of 1,000 milligrams per kilogram (mg/kg) for this contaminant. These three areas were subsequently deepened by approximately one foot and resampled. The final confirmation samples showed that the remaining soil had concentrations below the cleanup goals for both barium and lead (80 mg/kg).

The removal of the western and eastern ends of BCS Stockpile 2 soil included the use of scrapers to remove the material from these areas and place it directly within the containment zone of BCS Stockpile 2 as embankment fill. Verification samples for barium and lead were collected from eight sample locations on the western end and 12 sample locations on the eastern end from native soil following removal of the contaminated material. Initial confirmation sample results indicated four areas above the 1,000 mg/kg cleanup goal for barium. These four areas were deepened by an additional foot and the resulting confirmation samples showed that all remaining soil in the western and eastern excavation areas were below the verification thresholds of 1,000 and 80 mg/kg for barium and lead, respectively.

Lastly, soil was removed from the southern slopes of BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2 to steepen them and allow for the construction of a mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall along this length of the new expressway. Prior to conducting this work, soil samples were collected at 200-foot intervals along the length of the southern slope of the two stockpiles at depth intervals of one foot, down to a maximum of five feet below the surface to determine if the material being removed could be used as clean cap soil. Results of the sampling indicated that all concentrations for barium and lead were below the previously mentioned cleanup goals for the two contaminants and lead was within background concentrations. However, barium was found above the site-specific

background concentration of 120 mg/kg, so all of the material removed was placed in the BCS Stockpile 1 and 2 containment zones as embankment fill using scrapers.

In addition to BCS soil removal efforts, soil with aerially deposited lead was removed from the unpaved shoulder of a portion of the existing State Route 99 adjacent to the location of BCS Stockpile 3 in accordance with the 'Soil Management Agreement for Aerially Deposited Lead-Contaminated Soils' between DTSC and Caltrans (DTSC, 2016). This material was placed in the containment zone in the eastern portion of BCS Stockpile 1.

### **Site Remediation Activities – Clean Fill and Capping**

The containment zones for BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2 were capped with clean fill material identified near the project site. Four locations were identified as potential clean fill source areas and underwent sampling as per the "Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Material" (DTSC, 2001). Three of the four areas were found to be acceptable sources based on the sampling results. Once in place, the capped material on the northerly slopes was vegetated with a hydroseed mix and an erosion control blanket was installed along with fiber rolls to minimize potential erosion. No such erosion control measures were necessary on the south side due to the construction of the MSE wall on that side of the new expressway.

#### **Acceptance of the Draft Removal Action Completion Report**

The Draft Phase 1 RACR indicates that the work completed to remediate the Site to date was accomplished as per the approved RDIP or the two variance requests to the RDIP approved on April 17, 2020 and September 21, 2020. DTSC is accepting the document as a technical status report with no comments.

As indicated in the document, As-Built Plan and cross section figures remain to be completed as does the final survey data and Operation and Maintenance (O&M) Plan for the Interim Phase 1 Construction Effort. These components will be included in the Final Phase 1 Removal Action Completion Report on the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Interim Phase 1 State Route 132 Project (Final Phase 1 RACR). The O&M Plan will be submitted for review such that an approved version can be incorporated into the Final Phase 1 RACR which is anticipated for submittal in July or August of 2022. The O&M Plan will require a soil management plan, an annual inspection of the pavement and other features of the containment remedy, as well as groundwater monitoring. As per DTSCs March 14, 2018 letter approving the RAP, following approval of the Final Phase 1 RACR, DTSC will enter into a O&M Agreement with Caltrans that will include the O&M Plan and complete a land use covenant for the containment areas.

However, certification of the RAP will not occur until after the Ultimate Phase 2 State Route 132 build-out effort is completed (Ultimate Phase 2).

The Ultimate Phase 2 effort is anticipated to begin in 8 to 10 years and will include adding additional lanes and completing the final build out of the State Route 132 project. This effort will include a supplemental RDIP for DTSCs review and approval as well as field oversight, an Ultimate Phase 2 RACR and revisions to the O&M Plan and O&M Agreement if necessary. The Ultimate Phase 2 State Route 132 project will be conducted under a separate agreement between DTSC and Caltrans prior to commencing the work.
