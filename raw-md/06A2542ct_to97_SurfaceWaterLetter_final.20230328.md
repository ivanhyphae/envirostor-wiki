# **SITE BACKGROUND**

Around 1930, Barium Products Ltd. occupied the property at 1200 Barium Road (now Graphics Drive) in the city of Modesto, California. The property is located just east of SR 99 between Woodland Avenue and Kansas Avenue. Barium Products Ltd. was a chemical manufacturing company that processed a variety of ores and minerals including barite and celestite. Materials produced included barium and strontium compounds used for greases, lubricating oil, paint pigment blanks, etc. Sodium sulfide (Na2S) was also generated as a by-product of barite processing and was sold as a caustic and used as a reagent in the mining industry.

Barium Products Ltd. was purchased by Westvaco Chlorine Products Corporation in the 1943. In 1948, Westvaco merged with Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC Corporation). From the 1950's to March 28, 2023 Mr. Richard Stewart, PG Page 2 of 7

# **PAST SURFACE WATER SAMPLING ACTIVITIES**

The original surface water sampling event at the soil stockpiles was conducted in March 2006 by Shaw in general accordance with its January 2006 SAP. A total of seven surface water samples were collected during a qualifying rain event (i.e., an event meeting the qualifying criteria of visible runoff and 72 hours of prior dry weather). Shaw constructed shallow depressions within the Caltrans ROW in order to collect runoff from the stockpiles as there was no surface water migration beyond the ROW. The samples were analyzed for dissolved metals, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), nitrate, sulfate, and sulfide.

Subsequently, Geocon collected surface water samples from five designated surface runoff locations (PL1 through PL5) and two background locations (BG1 and BG2) on an on-call basis between April 2013 and April 2019 in accordance with the SAP Addendum. The sample locations are shown on Figure 2. Sample locations PL1 and PL2 were along North Emerald Avenue to collect runoff from Stockpiles 1 and 2, respectively. Sample location PL3 was along the southern edge of Stockpile 2. Sample location PL4 was located near Stockpile 3 where the storm water enters the northern portion of the drainage basin. Sample location PL5 was located near the gate valve at the southern end of the drainage basin. The gate valve, if opened, would release the storm water from the drainage basin to the Tuolumne River. The gate valve would only be opened if the storm water basin was filled to near capacity and would jeopardize the northbound lanes of SR 99. The sample locations were approved by the DTSC and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB). Geocon did not observe runoff migration from the Caltrans ROW during its inspections.

## **ANALYTICAL RESULTS**

#### **Dissolved Metals**

Analytical results for dissolved metals are summarized on Table 2.

The following metals were detected in one or more of the surface water samples collected on March 10, 2023: arsenic, barium, chromium, copper, lead, molybdenum, selenium, vanadium, zinc, strontium, and mercury. The laboratory flagged some of the data with a "J" value, meaning that the value is estimated. Antimony, beryllium, cadmium, cobalt, nickel, silver, and thallium were not reported above the MDLs in the background and surface water samples.

The reported metal detections were below their Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), where such exist. In the case of antimony, the MDL (8.8 micrograms per liter [µg/L]) exceeded the Primary MCL of 6.0 µg/L. The analytical results from the March 10, 2023 sampling event were the first surface water samples collected since road construction was completed and the stockpiles encapsulated. The analytical results from the background surface water sample BG1 were generally consistent with the results from previous events. The laboratory data from samples BG3, PL6, and PL7 were the first analytical results from these locations. Consequently, there are no historical results for comparison.

#### **General Minerals**

General minerals, including nitrates as nitrogen; sulfide, and total suspended solids were analyzed as part of this background/surface water sampling event per the Task Order. The analytical results for the general minerals are summarized on Table 3. The general mineral results were below the Primary and Secondary MCLs. The analytical results from sample BG1 were similar to its historic results except that turbidity was higher than previously reported.

The data validation procedure is based on the principles of the United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) National Functional Guidelines and EPA Region 9 requirements and is designed to ensure completeness and adequacy of the data set. Samples were collected and submitted for analysis to Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) in Signal Hill, California. Samples were analyzed in accordance with one or more of the following methods:

- Dissolved Metals by EPA Methods SW6010B and SW7470A
- Total Suspended Solids by Standard Method (SM) 2540D
- Nitrate as Nitrogen by EPA Method 300.0
- Dissolved Sulfide by SM4500-S D

The Data Validation Report/Checklist summarizes compounds that were qualified and is attached to this summary. Data were validated based on Regional EPA and EPA National Functional Guidelines*.* Data validation was performed in accordance with the Scope of Work to ensure the quality of project data. Sample delivery group (SDG) 2300466 was validated.

The data were validated and reviewed for the following:

- Completeness of data deliverables (chain of custody records, laboratory data, laboratory quality assurance and quality control (QA/QC) data)
- Sample holding time
- Sample preservation
- Method and equipment rinsate blank data
- Laboratory control sample (LCS) recovery
- Matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate (MS/MSD) recovery
- MS/MSD precision
- Laboratory duplicate sample precision
- Overall data assessment

The following summarizes the results of the validation:

- 1. Data Completeness: Data were validated for four surface water samples collected March 10, 2023. Sample analyses were performed per the chains of custody. The project goal of 90 percent completeness was achieved.
- 2. Sample Holding Times: All samples were analyzed within the recommended holding times with one exception:
  - a. Nitrate-N was analyzed outside of the 48-hour recommending holding time in all four samples; the results were qualified as estimated with a potential low bias (J-).

- 3. Sample Preservation: All samples collected in properly preserved containers and received at the appropriate temperature.
- 4. Method Blanks: No target compounds were detected in any method blanks with the following exception:
  - a. Nine metals were detected in the method blank for batch B3C0693. Metals results that were detected between the Method Detection Limit (MDL) and Practical Quantitation Limit (PQL) in associated samples were qualified as not detected (U) and the non-detect value was raised to the PQL. Associated metals results that were detected above the PQL, but below ten-times the blank result, were qualified as estimated with a potential positive bias (J+).
- 5. Laboratory Control Samples: LCS percent recoveries were within acceptance criteria.
- 6. Laboratory Duplicate Samples: Laboratory duplicate relative percent differences were within acceptance limits.
- 7. Matrix Spike and Matrix Spike Duplicates: MS/MSD samples were analyzed to assess accuracy and to evaluate matrix effects on data analysis. The percent recoveries and Relative Percent Differences (RPDs) were within laboratory-established acceptance limit.
- 8. No field duplicate samples were collected.
- 9. Data were validated according to EPA guidance documents and the project scope of work. All data are considered useable as qualified; no data were qualified as "rejected". The Data Validation Reports/Checklists summarize constituents that were qualified during the validation process and are attached to this summary.
