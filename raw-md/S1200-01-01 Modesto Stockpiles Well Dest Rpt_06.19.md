Mr. Stewart:

In accordance with the California Department of Transportation Contract No. 06A2184, Task Order No. 1, we performed well destruction activities for six groundwater monitoring wells associated with the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (the Site) in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. This report describes the procedures and methods employed by Geocon to complete the requested monitoring well destruction activities.

## **BACKGROUND AND PURPOSE**

Prior to commencing highway construction activities, six groundwater monitoring wells required abandonment in general accordance with the requirements of California Well Standards Bulletin 74- 90, Monitoring Well Standards, Part III. Two wells were located within the future highway travel lanes and four wells were located in the areas of future drainage improvements. Four wells were left in place at the Site. The site location is depicted on the Vicinity Map, Figure 1. The site layout and former and existing well locations are depicted on the Site Plan, Figure 2.

## **SCOPE OF SERVICES**

The following services were performed to destroy six site-related groundwater monitoring wells in general accordance with City of Modesto permit requirements.

### **Pre-field Activities**

- Obtained a well destruction permit (No. ENC2019-50174) and paid requisite fees to the City of Modesto. A copy of the permit is attached.
- Marked the well locations with white paint on April 8, 2019. Provided the required 72-hour notification to Underground Service Alert prior to job site mobilization (Ticket Nos. W910200748, W910200753, W910200756, W910200763, W910200770, W910200773).
- Notified the City of Modesto at least 48 hours prior to performing well destruction activities.
- Retained the services of Gregg Drilling and Testing (Gregg), a Caltrans-approved and Californiacertified subcontractor, to perform the well destruction activities.

### **Field Activities**

On April 29, 2019, Gregg destroyed wells MW1, MW2, and MW5. On April 30, 2019, Gregg destroyed wells MW3, MW7, and MW8. Geocon staff member Lauren Short supervised the destruction activities. Caltrans personnel were also onsite during the destruction activities.

### **Well Destruction Activities**

Gregg destroyed the six wells by pressure grouting each casing to the maximum well depth. Gregg measured the depth to groundwater and the depth of each well prior to destruction.

Gregg filled each well with grout via tremie pipe then applied approximately 20 to 25 pounds per square inch of pressure to the well for approximately 5 minutes. Gregg prepared the grout using 3 gallons of water per 47-pound sack of neat cement in 5-gallon plastic buckets and mixed with a shovel until smooth. Minimum calculated grout volumes for each of the wells ranged from 6.4 to 7.4 gallons. The wells took between 13 and 20 gallons of grout to reach the top of the casings. Once the grout stabilized, Gregg removed the well boxes and drilled out the top 5 feet of each well casing using a hollow-stem auger drilling rig equipped with an 8-inch-diameter auger. The top five feet of each well was backfilled with a combination of cuttings, native material, and bagged topsoil.

### **Containment and Disposal of Soil and Well Destruction Debris**

Gregg deposited the well box materials and concrete generated each day in Caltrans' onsite pile of construction debris. We completed the attached Well Completion Reports for each of the destroyed wells and forwarded them to the Department of Water Resources (DWR) and Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Health.
