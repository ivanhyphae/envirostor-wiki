## Key claims
- Documents consolidation of barium-containing soil (BCS) Stockpiles 1–3 and ADL soil as highway embankment fill, capped with DTSC-approved clean fill.
- Stockpile 3: ~27,675 yd³ (3,075 truckloads) fully excavated and placed on Stockpile 1.
- Stockpile 2: ~102,000 yd³ graded and consolidated within BCS Containment Zones.
- Final verification: barium 54–760 mg/kg, lead 1.5–35 mg/kg after re-excavation of hotspots.
- Verification thresholds: barium ≤ 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection); lead ≤ 80 mg/kg (DTSC residential screening).

## Remedial actions
- Phase 1 (interim): BCS consolidation, clean fill capping, MSE wall construction.
- Clean cover: Class 3 aggregate subbase (min 4 inches) approved via two DTSC variances.
- Record rain event January 2021 caused erosion on unvegetated slopes; repaired by September 2021.

## Monitoring
- Air monitoring: PM-10 below action levels (total dust < 4.0 mg/m³, barium < 25 µg/m³, lead < 1.5 µg/m³).
- Groundwater: four events (Nov 2019–Mar 2021) below regulatory thresholds; no offsite migration.
- Stormwater: three events (Dec 2019–Dec 2020) within limits.

## Statistical evaluation
- ProUCL v5.14 for 95% UCL calculations.
- MSE wall footing data: 95% UCL = 171.9 mg/kg barium (exceeds site-specific background of 120 mg/kg but deemed suitable as capping material).
- H-statistic UCL unstable; nonparametric methods preferred.

## Responsible parties
- Caltrans (owner), Geocon (environmental consultant), WSP (construction manager), BCPG (contractor), Dokken (design), Kleinfelder (compaction testing).

## Risks and liabilities
- Land use covenant to restrict future land use with five-year reviews.
- Supplemental RDIP required before Phase 2 (scheduled 2028).
- Final closure certification awaits Phase 2 completion.
