## Key claims
- DTSC approves Alternative 4 (Containment) as selected remedy for Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles.
- Containment achieved by constructing retaining walls, bridge abutments, and roadway over stockpiles; unpaved areas receive clean fill cover.
- Remedy eliminates exposure pathway to human receptors and minimizes surface water infiltration.
- RAP satisfies CEQA requirements; EIR/EA certified with Finding of No Significant Impact under NEPA.
- DTSC independently determined no significant environmental effect with implementation of controls.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision**: Formal approval of Remedial Action Plan
- **Agency**: DTSC
- **Conditions**: RDIP required before each phase; start work notice (English/Spanish) two weeks before construction; OMA/OMP after Phase 1; land use covenant; annual pavement inspection and groundwater monitoring; five-year remedy evaluation.
- **Phased implementation**: Phase 1 (2019–2020), Phase 2 (2028–2029)

## Responsible parties
- Caltrans: owner/operator
- DTSC: lead regulatory agency
- Central Valley RWQCB: co-regulatory agency

## Risks
- Contamination remains in place indefinitely; remedy relies on containment rather than removal.
- Long-term effectiveness depends on continued O&M, monitoring, and five-year evaluations.
- Land use covenant restricts future land use to prevent exposure.

## Limitations
- No quantitative performance metrics provided in the approval letter.
- OMA/OMP may need revision after Phase 2.
