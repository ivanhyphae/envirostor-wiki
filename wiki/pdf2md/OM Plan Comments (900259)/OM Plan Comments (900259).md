COMMENTS ON THE DRAFT OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE PLAN, CALTRANS MODESTO SOIL STOCKPILES, INTERIM PHASE 1 STATE ROUTE 132 WEST PROJECT, STANISLAUS COUNTY, CALIFORNIA, DATED MARCH, 2022 (SITE CODE: 900259)

## Dear Mr. Juhrend:

The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), has reviewed the Draft Operation and Maintenance Plan (Draft O&M Plan) noted above which will be included in the Remedial Action Completion Report as Appendix G. The Draft O&M Plan was submitted by Geocon Consultants Inc. on behalf of the Department of Transportation and outlines the activities associated with the inspection, maintenance, and repair of the portion of State Route 132 that is utilized as the clean cap cover over two barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles. Included with the Draft O&M Plan was a blank copy of the O&M Site Inspection Form that will be used by field crews conducting inspections to document the condition of the cap/SR 132 Freeway.

After reviewing both documents, the following comments are provided:

- 1. The word "Maintenance" is misspelled in the Subject line of the Draft O&M Plan
- 2. Section 2.1 This section should include a minimum number of days for the advance notification to DTSC of any BCS disturbance. DTSC recommends

- 10 working days advance notice to ensure prior written authorization from DTSC is provided before the planned disturbance begins.
- 3. Section 2.4 DTSC recommends that this section include text to indicate that Health and Safety Code (HSC) 25123.3(b)(4)(A) requirements will be met. DTSC notes that the minimum thickness of 6 millimeters provided in this section will meet HSC requirements, but since the HSC uses mils (not millimeters), adding the recommended text above will eliminate any ambiguity. DTSC also notes that the word millimeter is currently misspelled and should be corrected.
- 4. Section 3.1 DTSC recommends this section be revised to indicate that in addition to the annual inspections, inspections will also occur after events such as fires, substantial rainfall (1 inch in 24 hours), significant seismic events, traffic accidents that may cause damage to the cap, etc. This will help ensure the integrity of the cap is maintained especially on the north side of the freeway which will remain a vegetated slope until the final phase of the freeway is complete.
- 5. Section 2.7 The periodic patrols referenced in this section should also note if unauthorized access such has a homeless encampment has resulted in any impact to the cap. Especially on the north vegetated slope.
- 6. O&M Site Inspection Form DTSC recommends expanding the line items under each stockpile on this form with subheadings that list specific elements for each feature. Examples include the following:
  - MSE Wall Bulging areas, out of plane
  - Highway Pavement Cracks, potholes
  - Median and Shoulders Cracks, potholes
  - North Slope Vegetation, Rills, Slumping
  - Storm Drains Debris/obstructions, Erosion around inlets/outlets

Please incorporate the recommended changes above and submit the revised O&M Plan as Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report.
