Hi Richard,

DTSC and the Water Board have reviewed the Variance Request to the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (VR) dated April 9, 2020. The VR indicates that the upper foot of planned mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall footing excavation soil along the southern boundary of Stockpile 1 would not be suitable as clean fill due to elevated barium concentrations. This material will thus be placed in Stockpile 1 and 2 permanent fill areas. However, the soil extending from 1 to 5 feet from existing grade in this area contained barium concentrations with a calculated 95% upper confidence limit (UCL) of 113.1 mg/kg which is within the range of site-specific naturally occurring background. Both agencies agree that this material can therefore be used as clean cap soil on the project. Likewise, should the UCL of the MSE wall footing excavation located along the southern boundary of Stockpile 2 be under naturally occurring background concentrations, it may also be used as clean cap soil on the project. This e-mail serves as DTSCs official acceptance of the VR for the soil along the southern boundary of Stockpile 1. The official approval or rejection of the soil along the southern boundary of Stockpile 2 will be provided once that data has been submitted and reviewed. Thanks,
