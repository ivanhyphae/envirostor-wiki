# **Chapter 1** Introduction

## 1.1. A Public Hearing was Held

The California Department of Transportation (Caltrans), the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), in conjunction with the Stanislaus Council of Governments (StanCOG), held a public hearing with an informal open house format to review design concepts, share information, present displays, and accept written and oral comments on the Draft EIR/EA and the Draft Final RAP prepared for the State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project.

The public hearing was held on Wednesday, February 22, 2017 from 6:00 p.m. to 8:00 p.m. at Mark Twain Junior High School at 707 South Emerald Avenue in Modesto, California.

## 1.2. Announcement of the Public Meeting and the Public Comment Period

To announce the public hearing and public comment period, a public notice was published by StanCOG in *The Modesto Bee* (English version) and the *Vida en el Valle* (Spanish version) on January 18, 2017. The public comment period ran from January 18, 2017 to March 17, 2017. On January 30, 2017, the public hearing venue was changed from the Red Event Center to Mark Twain Junior High School. An English and Spanish postcard advertising this change was mailed on February 8, 2017 to about 2,500 residents, tenants and business owners within the project area. DTSC also sent out a Community Update for the Draft Final RAP in English and Spanish to the project mailing list on February 6, 2017. A revised public notice with the new location was published by StanCOG in *The Modesto Bee* and the *Vida en el Valle* on February 8, 2017. The public notice was published one last time in the same newspapers above on February 15, 2017. The notices were also posted on the Caltrans, StanCOG, and DTSC websites.

The Notice of Availability for the Draft EIR/EA and the Community Update for the Draft Final RAP were also mailed to federal, state, and local officials, as well as residents and other interested groups on January 13, 2017.

See Appendix B for copies of the announcements.

## 1.3. Purpose and Goals of the Public Meeting

The purpose of the public hearing was to explain the proposed project to the public and other interested parties, to answer any questions attendees may have, and to gather public feedback on the Draft EIR/EA and the Draft Final RAP prepared for the State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project.

## 1.4. Format of the Public Meeting

The public hearing was conducted in an informal open house format to facilitate communication and the exchange of information between the project team and the public. When the attendees arrived, they were asked to sign in and were handed a project information sheet and a Community Update (see Appendix A for the project information sheet and Appendix B for the Community Update). Staff invited each attendee to view the displays throughout the room, ask questions, place their written comments in the drop box or mail/email them to Caltrans, or give their oral comments to the court reporter onsite. A Spanish translator was provided for Spanish-speaking attendees.

Stations with display boards were set up around the room for the public to review. Each station was manned by staff to provide information as needed. The stations are described in Chapter 2. The display boards are shown in Appendix A. Maps of the project layout were also set up in the middle of the room. See Appendix C for photographs of the public hearing activities.

# **Chapter 2** Public Hearing Proceedings

## 2.1. Welcome

Attendees were welcomed to the public hearing at the sign-in table. They were asked to sign in and given project information sheets. A Community Update provided by DTSC was distributed. English and Spanish versions of the handouts were available. A Spanish interpreter was also available to translate questions and answers. Photographs of the proceedings and public hearing activities are provided in Appendix C.

## 2.2. Displays

The informational display boards and exhibits at the public hearing are described below (copies of the informational display boards and graphics are provided in Appendix A).

### 2.2.1. Welcome Board and Sign-In Table

A welcome board greeted attendees as they entered the meeting room. Attendees were asked to sign in so that Caltrans, StanCOG, DTSC, and RWCQB staff could maintain an attendance record and ensure that all interested parties were added to the project mailing list. The list of attendees is provided in Appendix E. Staff gave each attendee information sheets stating the project description, purpose, cost, funding source, timeline, and a contact name for those interested in obtaining more information. An information sheet also contained a map showing the project locations. The project information sheet is provided in Appendix A. Attendees were also provided with comment cards to record their feedback on the information presented. All handouts were provided in English and Spanish. Staff explained the format of the meeting, and attendees were encouraged to ask questions of the project team in attendance.

### 2.2.2. Purpose of Tonight's Meeting

At the entrance of the meeting room, this board stated the purpose of the meeting and how to submit comments regarding the project and the Draft Final RAP.

### 2.2.3. Agency Involvement

This board included a complete list of all the agencies participating in the project and the Draft Final RAP and their roles.

## 2.2.4. Project Description

This board provided an overview of the project: the proposed construction of a four-lane freeway/expressway south of Kansas to east of State Route 132 at the Needham Street Bridge Overcrossing. It also described the proposed encapsulation of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles as described in the Draft Final RAP.

## 2.2.5. Purpose and Need

This board explained the purpose and need of the project: to improve regional and interregional circulation in Modesto and Stanislaus County, relieve traffic congestion along existing State Route 132 (Maze Boulevard) and improve traffic operations for the existing and proposed transportation network.

## 2.2.6. Comparison of Alternatives

These two boards compared the two proposed build alternatives under consideration as well as a no-build option for both the human and physical environments. The boards also summarized findings for resource topics within the Draft EIR/EA.

## 2.2.7. Project Funding and Costs

This board explained projected costs for both Phase 1 and Phase 2 of the project and identified sources of funding.

## 2.2.8. Project Schedule

This board provided the proposed timeline—from the selection of the preferred alternative through construction.

### 2.2.9. State Route 132 Cross Sections

These boards displayed typical below-grade and at-grade cross-sectional views of the project. Illustrations of the proposed roadway layout enabled attendees to visualize how lanes would be configured.

### 2.2.10. State Route 132 Soundwall Simulation

### 2.2.11. Environmental Process

#### 2.2.12. Environmental Studies

demolition. The board also included a website address for more information from Caltrans Right-of-Way.

# Chapter 3 Public Input

# 3.1. Oral Comments Received at the Public Hearing

Project team staff from Caltrans, DTSC, RWQCB, and StanCOG addressed comments and questions heard at the public hearing. Below is a list of the comments, by topic or issue.

A court reporter was also present at the hearing to record oral comments from attendees. Sixteen people took advantage of this opportunity. The complete transcription is provided in Appendix F.

## 3.1.1. General Support for the Project

Several attendees gave support for the project, in favor of diverting State Route 132 traffic to Dakota and the new alignment.

## 3.1.2. Project Purpose and Need

Several attendees questioned the project purpose and need.

### 3.1.3. Preferred Alternatives

Several attendees expressed a preference for the No-Build Alternative or the widening of the existing State Route 132 along Maze Boulevard. Other attendees expressed a preference for Alternative 2 because it seemed to have the least impact to businesses on Kansas Avenue.

#### 3.1.4. North Dakota Avenue

Several attendees expressed concerns regarding the potential relocation and traffic impacts along North Dakota Avenue.

### 3.1.5. Logical Termini

Attendees voiced questions about the determination of the western end of the project at North Dakota Avenue.

### 3.1.6. Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Remediation

Several attendees voiced concerns about the remediation plan for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, noted that they preferred the removal of the stockpiles, and questioned the safety of encapsulation.

### 3.1.7. Traffic Impacts

Attendees provided comments on traffic impacts to existing State Route 132, the removal of the southbound State Route 99 I Street off-ramp, and limited access to the new alignment.

## 3.1.8. Right-of-Way Impacts

Several residents and business owners requested clarification on the potential right-of-way impacts to their properties, and relocation compensation and assistance.

## 3.1.9. Noise Impacts

Attendees had questions and comments about the locations of proposed soundwalls.

## 3.1.10. Public Meeting and Outreach

Several attendees commented that they would have preferred a meeting format that included a presentation and the opportunity to make oral comments before all attendees and the project team.

# 3.2. Written Comments Submitted at the Meeting

Caltrans received 16 comment cards submitted at the meeting. Additional comment cards were submitted to Caltrans via mail following the meeting (see Section 3.3). The comments are summarized below and provided in Appendix D.

## 3.2.1. Project Purpose and Need

One attendee questioned whether improvements could be made to the existing State Route 132/Maze Boulevard, thereby avoiding the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, which would be encountered on the new alignment.

### 3.2.2. Preferred Alternatives

Several commenters opposed both build alternatives and preferred the No-Build Alternative. Reasons for general opposition included loss of farmland, project costs, potential bottlenecks, disruption of community cohesion, doubts about the project's ability to meet the purpose and need, and other improvement projects that were considered more pressing.

Many residents expressed support for Alternative 2. Several attendees noted that Alternative 2 seemed to result in fewer impacts to businesses on Kansas Avenue.

## 3.2.3. Right-of-Way Impacts

Several residents and business owners requested clarification on the potential right-of-way impacts to their properties.

## 3.2.4. Business Impacts

Several business owners were concerned about economic impacts as a result of the project, particularly during construction and due to the loss of direct access to Kansas Avenue from State Route 99.

### 3.2.5. Logical Termini

Some attendees questioned the western end point of the project at North Dakota Avenue.

## 3.2.6. Homeless Populations

One attendee expressed concerns that the berms created by encapsulating the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles would increase the presence of homeless people in her neighborhood.

# 3.3. Written Comments Submitted by Mail

Following the meeting, Caltrans received seven comment cards by mail (see Appendix D). The following provides a summary of comments, by topic or issue, received via mail from commenters who attended the public hearing.

## 3.3.1. Project Purpose and Need

Many commenters suggested alternative projects and/or improvements, instead of the project build alternatives. Several attendees were concerned that the project would not fix traffic or roadway problems. Another attendee stated that traffic on Carpenter Avenue has increased more than traffic on State Route 132, so Carpenter Avenue should be improved instead of the proposed project. Another attendee recommended that Caltrans deed its reserved right-of-way to the City of Modesto for mixed-use housing development.

## 3.3.2. Project Design

One commenter expressed concern that the freeway/expressway design was inadequate because it would allow for two 90-degree turns and for private driveway access along Dakota Avenue. Other attendees requested for clarity about the number of lanes on State Route 132 and on Dakota Avenue, and possible right-of-way impacts associated with the on- or off-ramp.

### 3.3.3. Soil Contamination and Remediation

One commenter expressed concerns about the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Alternative 3 – Removal (excavation and offsite disposal) and the amount of soil that would potentially be removed at the expense of the landowners. Some commenters were in favor of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Alternative 3 – Removal. Another commenter inquired about potential health risks associated with the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles and expressed doubt in the efficacy of various soil sampling tests that had been prepared. Several attendees stated that the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles should be transported offsite and not encased and that they might contain toxic materials that had been transported from the original site. Others noted that toxic soils could run off into the drainage system.

### 3.3.4. Project Alternatives

One commenter was in favor of the No-Build Alternative.

## 3.3.5. Traffic Safety

Several commenters felt that addressing safety and accident rates on existing State Route 132 and investing in maintenance of current roads would be more beneficial. Others felt that the project improvements would increase risk of death and accidents.

## 3.3.6. Community Impacts

One commenter stated that the project would have adverse effects on air pollution, noise pollution and soil contamination and requested that "water, wealth, contentment and health" are considered in all county-level decisions.

## 3.3.7. Noise Impacts

One commenter noted that there should be soundwalls along the at-grade portions of the expressway.

# Información del proyecto

# DESCRIPCIÓN del PROYECTO

El Departamento de Transporte de California (Caltrans), en colaboración con el Consejo de Gobiernos de Stanislaus (StanCOG), propone la construcción de una autopista/vía rápida de cuatro carriles a lo largo de la ya aprobada Ruta Estatal 132 —al sur de Avenida Kansas desde la Avenida Dakota (Hito Milla {PM} 11.0) al este de la Ruta Estatal 99, al paso sobre nivel de la Calle Needham (PM 15.00). El largo total del Proyecto Autopista Ruta Estatal 132-Oeste/Vía Expresa es de aproximadamente cuatro millas e incluiría empalmes con la Ruta Estatal 99 desde el PM 15,7 al PM 15,5. El proyecto se haría por etapas, implementando una de dos alternativas de construcción (Alt.1 o Alt.2), o decidiendo por la Alternativa de "No-Construir".

Los Acopios de Caltrans en Modesto ocupan tres áreas dentro el derecho-de-vía ubicado al sur de la Avenida Kansas. Caltrans propone cubrir o enclaustrar los acopios contaminados dentro de la construcción del proyecto Autopista Ruta Estatal 132-Oeste/ Vía Expresa, tal y como se detalla en la Propuesta Final de Acción Correctiva (RAP).

### OBJETIVO

- O Mejorar la circulación vial regional e interregional en Modesto y el Condado Stanislaus
- O Aliviar la congestión vehicular a lo largo de la Ruta Estatal 132 (Maze Boulevard)
- O Mejorar la operación del tránsito dentro de la red de transporte actual y propuesta

### NECESIDAD

Se espera que las deficiencias de la carretera actual —aunadas al aumento del tráfico regional e interregional— perpetúen la congestión vial. El proyecto presentado aliviaría la congestión vial a lo largo de la Ruta Estatal 132 (Maze Blvd.) dada la actual situación que experimenta la Ruta Estatal 132 (Maze Blvd.) que se espera empeore en proporción al aumento del tráfico. # **Project Description**

The California Department of Transportation (Caltrans), working in cooperation with the Stanislaus Council of Governments (Stan-COG), proposes to construct a four-lane freeway/expressway along the adopted route south of Kansas Avenue from Dakota Avenue (Post Mile {PM} 11.0) to east of State Route 99 (SR-99) at the Needham Street Bridge Overcrossing (PM 15.0).

The total length of the SR-132 West Freeway/Expressway Project would be approximately four miles and would include connections on SR-99 from PM 15.7 to PM 17.5. The project involves phased construction of one of the two build alternatives (Alt 1 or Alt 2) or the decision to implement the No-Build Alternative.

The Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles occupy three areas within state right-of-way south of Kansas Avenue. Caltrans proposes to cover or encapsulate the soil stockpiles in combination with the construction of the SR-132 West Freeway/Expressway Project, as detailed in the Draft Final Remedial Action Plan (RAP).

# **Project Purpose & Need**

# Purpose:

- Improve regional and interregional circulation in Modesto and Stanislaus County
- Relieve traffic congestion along existing SR-132 (Maze Boulevard)
- Improve traffic operations for the existing and proposed transportation network

# Need:

The deficiencies of the existing highway and increases in regional and interregional traffic are anticipated to result in continued traffic congestion. The proposed project would relieve traffic congestion along existing SR-132 (Maze Boulevard) because the existing SR-132 (Maze Boulevard) currently experiences, and would continue to be burdened by increased traffic. The proposed project would also improve operations for the existing and proposed transportation network because the operational efficiency is reduced by the proximity and direct access to schools, churches, businesses, and residences by way of existing driveways along existing SR-132 (Maze Boulevard).

# Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Proposed Containment

![](_page_41_Figure_2.jpeg)

In the Stockpile Containment Alternative, stockpile soil would be isolated behind retaining walls and bridge abutments and beneath highway pavements. Phase 1 of the two-phase SR-132 West roadway project would construct east and west-bound lanes of SR-132 over the southern half of stockpiles 1 and 2 and place an engineered clean soil cover over the northern portions. Phase 1 would also consolidate stockpile 3 within the eastern abutment of the proposed SR-132/SR-99 interchange.

In Phase 2 of the SR-132 West roadway project the northern half of stockpiles 1 and 2 would be paved, completely isolating all stockpile soil within the proposed alignment as proposed in the Containment Alternative. Containment was identified as the best alternative during the remedial alternative selection process because it is protective of human health; is technically feasible; cost-effective; and minimizes the potential for contaminants to migrate to groundwater or be eroded by stormwater runoff.

# You May Review and Comment on the Draft Final Cleanup Plan for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Site

The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) invites you to review and comment on a proposed remedial action for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Site (site) located south of Kansas Avenue between Carpenter Avenue and just east of State Route 99 in Modesto, California. The selected remedy, contained in a draft final Remedial Action Plan (RAP), summarizes how the owner of the site, Caltrans, proposes to address contamination in the stockpiles, which resulted from excavation through an industrial waste pond during construction of State Route 99 in Modesto during the 1960s.

The draft final RAP prepared by Caltrans proposes containment of the stockpiles within a structural section of the proposed State Route 132 West Expressway/Freeway project (State Route 132 West). DTSC and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) have reviewed the draft final RAP and approved it for public notice and comment. The stockpiles would be contained behind retaining walls, bridge abutments, and beneath roadway pavement of the State Route 132 project between Needham Street and Carpenter Avenue.

# Site Description and History

The site consists of three separate and distinct soil stockpiles totaling 160,000 cubic yards. The site is within state right-of-way located south of Kansas Avenue and east and west of the State Route 99/Kansas Avenue interchange.

# Site Investigation

Since 2004, Caltrans did numerous site investigations including stockpile soil, groundwater, and stormwater runoff assessments. The investigations showed that the primary contaminants in the stockpile soil are barium, strontium, and lead.

Caltrans installed 10 groundwater monitoring wells at the site. Groundwater beneath the base of the stockpiles has varied from 30 to 44 feet. Groundwater flows consistently toward the southeast. Groundwater samples were originally collected in 2006, and

monitoring has occurred bi-monthly or quarterly beginning in 2012. In 2014, analytical results from

all groundwater sampling events were evaluated with respect to background concentrations at the stockpiles and groundwater contaminants that are monitored by FMC. Groundwater is currently monitored annually. Three active water supply wells maintained by the City of Modesto exist within a one-mile radius southeast of the site.

Caltrans has also collect-ed surface water runoff samples from annual

wintertime storm events—nine times since 2013. Caltrans analyzed the samples for metals, including primary contaminants of concern and general mineral parameters. Contaminants of Concern for groundwater and surface water are below Water Quality Objectives.

## **Human Health Risk Assessment**

A 2007 Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) concluded that, as currently managed, soil and groundwater at the site do not pose an unacceptable human risk for off-site residents, trespassers, or Caltrans workers. Caltrans based this conclusion on 1) maintaining the perimeter fence; 2) limiting access to Caltrans workers; and 3) maintaining a vegetative cover. Caltrans also would have to 4) prohibit placement or removal of soil from the site; and 5) maintain groundwater monitoring. Currently, there are no drinking wells at the site. Additional soil sampling in 2012 confirmed results of the 2007 HHRA. However, containment of the stockpiles within the State Route 132 West project will achieve the overall goal of long-term protection of human health.

![](_page_53_Picture_15.jpeg)

# Remedial Actions Considered

Caltrans, DTSC and RWQCB considered the following cleanup alternatives:

- Alternative 1: No Action The stockpiles would remain in place without fences, monitoring or maintenance. This alternative is used as a baseline against which to compare other alternatives.
- Alternative 2: Institutional Controls Prevents unauthorized site access to stockpiles by establishing fences; signage; public notices; a deed restriction and land use covenant; site inspections; and air and water quality monitoring.
- Alternative 3: Removal Removes the contaminant source by excavating and transporting 160,000 cubic yards of stockpile soil to an off-site disposal facility.
- Alternative 4: Containment Contains stockpiles behind retaining walls, bridge abutments, and beneath roadway pavement of the State Route 132 West project. Unpaved portions would have a clean soil cover in Phase 1. (If the State Route 132 West project were not

constructed, then containment would consist of a clean soil cap over the stockpiles.) Caltrans would establish and maintain a deed restriction and land use covenant, site inspections, and water quality monitoring in both cases.

# Proposed Remedial Action Plan

Based on an evaluation of the remedial actions, Caltrans recommends Alternative 4, containment by construction of the State Route 132 West project. DTSC and RWQCB concur with this recommendation. This alternative offers long-term protection of human health and the environment, while balancing contaminant isolation with removal/disposal costs and the need for elevated fill material necessary to construct the State Route 132 West project between Needham Street and Carpenter Avenue.

If the draft final RAP is approved, DTSC and RWQCB will oversee the development of a Remedial Design Implementation Plan. This plan will establish details on how to contain the stockpiles as part of the State Route 132 project.

Caltrans plans to build the State Route 132 West project in two phases: initial construction phase and ultimate build-out phase. The initial construction phase (see Phase 1: Typical Soil Stockpile Cross-Section) will build retaining walls and bridge abutments, and pave the southern half of the site. The northern half will be covered with a graded engineered soil layer. The opening phase would begin in 2018 and end in 2020. # California Environmental Quality Act

As the lead agency under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), Caltrans produced an Environmental Impact Report/Environmental Assessment (EIR/EA) that determined the containment of the stockpile site along with mitigation would not have a significant environmental impact. DTSC and RWQCB are Responsible Reviewing Agencies for CEQA for this project.

# Acciones Correctivas a Considerar

Caltrans, DTSC y RWQCB consideraron las siguientes alternativas de limpieza:

- Alternativa 1: No Hacer Nada Los montones se mantienen donde están sin bardas, seguimiento ni mantenimiento. Esta alternativa se usa como base y comparativo a otras alternativas.
- Alternativa 2: Controles Institucionales Previene el acceso sin autorización a los Montones estableciendo bardas; señalización de seguridad, advertencias al público, una restricción de transferencia y compromiso de uso de suelo; inspecciones a terreno; y evaluaciones periódicas de calidad de agua y aire.
- Alternativa 3: Extracción Extraer la fuente de contaminación excavando y transportando 160 mil yardas cúbicas del material hasta un vertedero designado fuera del área.
- Alternativa 4: Contención Contención de los montones tras bardas de retención, contrafuertes de

puentes y bajo el pavimento caminero del proyecto Ruta Estatal 132 Oeste. Secciones sin pavimentar contarían con una cubierta de tierra limpia en la 1ª Etapa. (Si el proyecto Ruta Estatal 132 Oeste no se llevase a cabo, entonces la contención consistiría en una capa de tierra limpia sobre los Montones). Caltrans establecería y mantendría una restricción de transferencia y convenio de uso de suelo, inspecciones a terreno, y seguimiento a la calidad del agua y del aire en ambos casos.

# Propuesta de Plan de Acciones Correctivas

Basados en la evaluación de las acciones correctivas, Caltrans recomienda la Alternativa 4, contención a través de la construcción del proyecto Ruta Estatal 132, Oeste. El DTSC y el RWQCB concurren en esta recomendación. Esta alternativa ofrece protección a largo plazo a la salud humana y al medioambiente, al tiempo que equilibra el aislamiento de los contaminantes con el costo de extracción y eliminación, con la necesidad de material de relleno para la construcción del proyecto de la Ruta Estatal 132 Oeste entre la Calle Needham y la Avenida Carpenter.

### (WHAT IS PLANNED?

The California Department of Transportation (Caltrans), as the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) lead agency and as assigned by the Federal Highway Administration under the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) at the time of the signing of this environmental document, working in cooperation with the Stanislaus Council of Governments (StanCOG), proposes to construct a four-lane freeway/expressway along the adopted route of State Route (SR) 132 south of Kansas Avenue from Dakota Avenue to east of SR 99 at the Needham Street Bridge Overcrossing in the city of Modesto.

•

![](_page_61_Figure_14.jpeg)

The Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles occupy three areas within state right-of-way south of Kansas Avenue: between Carpenter Avenue and Emerald Avenue, Emerald Avenue and SR 99, and east of SR 99. Caltrans proposes to cap the soil stockpiles as part of the SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway construction project. As required by state law, the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) have reviewed the Draft Final Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the stockpiles and approved it for public noticing.

The three stockpiles total approximately 160,000 cubic yards of soil containing metals (primarily barium, lead, and strontium). Caltrans prepared the Draft Final RAP under the oversight of the DTSC and RWQCB. If approved, the RAP would allow for the placement of impacted soil beneath roadway pavement, within bridge abutments, and behind retaining walls. This action would avoid potential future impacts to human health and the environment.

### WHY THIS PUBLIC NOTICE?

Caltrans has studied the effects this project may have on the environment. A Draft Environmental Impact Report (Draft EIR)/Environmental Assessment (EA) and Draft Final RAP were prepared and are available for public review and comment. You have the opportunity to offer your comments or concerns, and your comments will become part of the public record. An open forum public hearing will be held to give you an opportunity to talk about certain design features of the project with Caltrans staff and discuss the Draft Final RAP for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles with DTSC and RWQCB representatives. No formal presentation will be given, and you are welcome to attend the hearing any time between 6:00 p.m. and 8:00 p.m.

## ¿QUÉ SE PLANIFICA?

El Departamento de Transporte de California (Caltrans), en su condición de agencia-líder en cumplimento al Acta de Calidad Medioambiental en California (CEQA) y como dicta la Administración Federal de Carreteras bajo el Acta Nacional de Políticas Medioambientales (NEPA) -al momento de la firma de este documento medioambiental— y en colaboración con el Consejo de Gobiernos de Stanislaus (StanCOG), propone la construcción de una autopista/via rápida de cuatro carriles a los largo de la ya aprobada Ruta Estatal 132 —al sur de Av. Kansas desde la Av. Dakota al este de la Ruta Estatal 99, al cruce de Needham Street Bridge en la ciudad de Modesto.

![](_page_62_Figure_13.jpeg)

Los Montones de Caltrans en Modesto ocupan tres áreas dentro el derecho-de-vía ubicado al sur de la Av. Kansas: entre la Av. Carpenter, Av. Emerald y Ruta 99, y al este de la Ruta 99. Caltrans propone aplicar una capa de tierra limpia sobre los montones contaminados como parte de la construcción del proyecto Autopista Ruta Estatal 132-Oeste/ Vía Expresa. En cumplimiento a la ley Estatal, el Departamento de Control de Sustancias Tóxicas de California (DTSC) y el Directorio de Control Regional de Calidad del Agua del Valle Central (RWQCB) han analizado ésta, la última Propuesta Final de Acción Correctiva (RAP) y la han aprobado para su notificación pública.

Los tres montones suman 160 mil yardas cúbicas de tierra que contiene metales pesados (principalmente bario, plomo y estroncio). Caltrans preparó su propuesta de RAP Final bajo la supervisión del DTSC y el RWQCB. De aprobarse, el RAP permitiría la colocación de suelo contaminado debajo del pavimento de la carretera, dentro de los contrafuertes de los puentes y detrás de los muros de contención. Esta acción prevendría un futuro riesgo a la salud humana y el medioambiente.
