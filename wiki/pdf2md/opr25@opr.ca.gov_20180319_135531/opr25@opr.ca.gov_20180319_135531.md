Project Description: The project consists of containment of three stockpiles totaling approximately 160,000 cubic yards of soil containing metals (primarily barium, strontium, and lead) during construction of the SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project. The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) has approved the Remedial Action Plan for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Site.

The stockpiles occupy three areas within the state right-of way located south of Kansans Avenue: between Carpenter Avenue and East Emerald Avenue, Emerald Avenue and SR 99, and east of SR 99. The stockpiles will be contained behind retaining walls, bridge abutments, and beneath the roadway. Unpaved portions will have clean fill cover. This containment remedy provides long-term protection of people and the environment by preventing exposure and minimizing the infiltration of surface water into groundwater under the stockpiles. Land use controls and inspection of the payement and other features of the containment remedy and groundwater monitoring will be conducted annually to ensure the integrity of the remedy into the future.

Caltrans, acting as the Lead Agency under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), prepared an Environmental Impact Report/Environmental Assessment(EIR/EA) and Remedial Action Plan RAP). Caltrans certified the EIR/EA on March 2, 2018. DTSC, as a CEQA Responsible Agency, is utilizing the EIR/EA for the RAP CEQA analysis. Although Caltrans completed a Statement of Overriding Considerations\* for the project, it is not applicable to DTSC's project.

As Lead Agency a Responsible Agency under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), DTSC approved the above-described project on March 14, 2018 and has made the following determinations:

- 1. The project will not have a significant effect on the environment.
- 2. An Environmental Impact Report that included this project was prepared and certified by the California Department of Transportation pursuant to the provisions of CEQA.
- 3. Mitigation measures were made a condition of project approval.
- A \*Statement of Overriding Considerations was not adopted for this project.
- 5. Findings were made pursuant to the provisions of CEQA.
