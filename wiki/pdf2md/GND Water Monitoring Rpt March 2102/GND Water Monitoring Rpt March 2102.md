GROUNDWATER MONITORING REPORT, CALIFORNIA DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION MODESTO SOIL STOCKPILES - STATE ROUTE 99 AND 132, MARCH 2012, MODESTO, CALIFORNIA

Dear Mr. Stewart:

The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region (RWQCB) has reviewed "Groundwater Monitoring Report, California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) Modesto Soil Stockpiles - State Route (SR) 99 and 132, Modesto, California", prepared by Geocon Consultants and dated May 2012. The subject report presents analytical results of groundwater monitoring well sampling conducted on March 12, 2012, October 5, 2006, and June 14, 2006. The monitoring wells were installed in 2006 to monitor groundwater quality in the vicinity of three soil stockpiles. The RWQCB collected "split" water samples during the March 12, 2012 sampling event.

The soil stockpiles consist of excess native soils and pond tailings that were generated when Caltrans constructed a segment of SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue in the early 1960's. Excavating the segment traversed a portion of a 4.3-acre parcel purchased from Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC). An evaporation pond was located in the southernmost corner of the parcel. FMC (and its predecessors) was a chemical manufacturing company that processed barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores and other minerals. The primary Chemicals of Concern (COC) are barium, lead, and strontium.

DTSC concurs with the Groundwater Monitoring Report, dated May 2012 and requests that future groundwater monitoring reports include a discussion comparing groundwater monitoring results/concentration trends from previous sampling events as more data becomes available.
