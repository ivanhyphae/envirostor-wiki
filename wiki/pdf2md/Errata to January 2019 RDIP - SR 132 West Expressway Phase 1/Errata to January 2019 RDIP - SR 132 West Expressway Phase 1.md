## **INTRODUCTION**

Following the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) approval of a Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for mitigation of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, a Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) was prepared for Caltrans by Geocon Consultants, Inc. At the time of its preparation, Geocon was under an on-call contract with Caltrans for the performance of hazardous waste services including preparation of decision documents required by the DTSC to implement the "containment alternative" for remediation of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles via the SR 132 West project. The RDIP was submitted to the Department of Toxic Substances and subsequently approved on April 3, 2019.

At the time it was prepared, Geocon was identified as Caltrans' agent to ensure that containment of the stockpiles was accomplished in accordance with the RAP and RDIP. However, regardless of the consultant who will perform the work, Caltrans is the responsible party to make sure the work will be performed according to the RDIP. This Post Construction Contract Award Errata updates the pertinent sections of the RDIP to reflect changes to previously identified roles and responsibilities, namely striking Geocon as the consultant employed by the state.

## **2.1 Project Team Responsibilities**

Caltrans is responsible for the implementation of the RAP and this RDIP under regulatory oversight and approval by DTSC in cooperation with the CVRWQCB. Caltrans and DTSC are further responsible for public outreach and participation.

DTSC reviewed the July 18, 2018 Draft RDIP prepared by Geocon and prepared a Conditional Approval Letter dated August 20, 2018 (Appendix A). The DTSC letter provides conditional approval of the Draft RDIP pending inclusion of administrative (non-technical) text revisions and review of the 95% and 100% design plans. DTSC reviewed the September 14, 2018 RDIP prepared by Geocon that incorporated the recommended text revisions and prepared a Conditional Approval Letter dated October 12, 2018 (Appendix A). This RDIP includes applicable excerpts of the 100% design plans (Appendix C). A complete set of the 100% design plans and final project specifications will be submitted to DTSC and the CVRWQCB in early 2019.

Caltrans is responsible for verifying that the highway construction work associated with the soil stockpiles is performed in substantial conformance with the RAP and RDIP requirements. Specifically, Caltrans will:

Supervise the proper abandonment of site-related groundwater monitoring wells impacted by planned Interim Phase 1 highway construction; Observe and photodocument barium-containing soil excavation, fill placement and capping activities; Perform barium-containing soil removal verification soil sampling and analysis; Perform proposed clean soil import source sampling and analysis; Perform worker zone and perimeter air monitoring; Perform periodic stormwater monitoring including observations and sampling; Review RDIP requirements with the construction manager and highway construction contractor; Interact with and periodically update the Caltrans Environmental Manager and DTSC and CVRWQCB Project Managers; Provide monthly status reports; and Prepare a Remedial Action Completion Report.

The City is responsible for Interim Phase 1 and Ultimate Phase 2 project design and construction. Dokken, under contract with the City, will provide final design and construction specifications for the SR-132 West Project. The City's, construction management firm and highway construction contractor will be responsible for executing the following work associated with the management of barium-containing soil with oversight coordination by Caltrans: Worker health and safety (awareness training); Mobilize equipment, material and personnel; Site grading and retaining wall/bridge structure construction; Barium-containing soil removal/consolidation within designated areas; Management of placed barium-containing fill material; Dust control; Stormwater best management practices; and Placement of pavement and approved imported clean cover soil and vegetated ground cover over designated barium-containing soil consolidation areas.

## **4.4 Barium-containing Soil Excavation and Fill Placement Area Preparation**

Stockpile 1 through 3 clearing operations performed by the highway construction contractor will include removal and proper offsite disposal of any remnant homeless encampments, debris materials, and perimeter fencing in planned highway grading/improvement areas.

Prior to any excavation or fill placement of barium-containing soil, the surface of Stockpiles 1 through 3 will be mowed to a minimum remaining vegetation thickness of two inches. Designated barium containing soil consolidation fill placement area surfaces will then be disced/ripped, moisture conditioned, and recompacted per project geotechnical specifications.

A Caltrans representative of will be onsite fulltime during any site preparation, excavation, fill placement and clean fill containment of barium-containing soil. Air monitoring for potential fugitive dust emissions, as described in Section 5.3, will also be implemented during these activities.

#### **4.6 Verification Sampling and Analysis**

The soil stockpile removal activities will be overseen by a California Professional Geologist (PG) or a field geologist/engineer working under the direct supervision of a PG. The field representative will determine when the designated soil stockpile removals have been completed based on survey data compared to documented original ground surface elevations, observations for native soil conditions, and stockpile thickness and perimeter soil data generated during prior site investigation studies. The individual

performing the oversight will also be responsible for preparing and maintaining daily field logs that, at minimum, document the personnel onsite, activities performed, where air monitoring is taking place, whether visible dust is observed, description of active soil stockpile excavation, record quantities of materials excavated, and confirmation of removal area limits (confirm native soil conditions). Written daily field reports will be supplemented by photographs as applicable. The individual performing the oversight will also collect stockpile removal verification samples and prepare accompanying chain-of-custody documentation.

The completed removal of barium-containing soil at the eastern and western ends (bridge abutment areas) of Stockpile 2 and entire Stockpile 3 will be determined by monitoring targeted excavation depths based on original ground surface elevations, observations by the Project Geologist, and verification soil sampling and analytical testing. Verification soil samples will be obtained from the completed barium-containing soil excavation areas (including loading, unpaved haul roads and track-out prevention areas outside of Stockpiles 1 and 2) at the rate of one sample per 5,000 square feet of removal area and one sample per 100 lineal feet of activity area. The soil samples will be obtained from the exposed excavation soil surface using hand-sampling methods (new and precleaned stainless trowels) and placed in new laboratory-provided glass containers, capped, labelled, and delivered to a California-certified analytical laboratory using chain-of-custody protocol. The verification samples will be submitted for analysis of barium and lead using Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) Test Method 6010B with a targeted reporting limit of 1.0 mg/kg. The verification soil sample analytical results will be compared to the threshold levels of 1,000 mg/kg for barium and 80 mg/kg for lead. The stockpile removal activities will be confirmed completed upon receipt of verification soil sample analytical data (total values or calculated 95% UCLs) that is less than the threshold levels.

### **4.7 Grading Control and Containment Measures**

The highway construction contractor will be responsible for maintaining proper dust control, erosion control, and stormwater best management practices (BMPs) in accordance with project design, Caltrans specifications and the approved Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) during the highway grading activities. Anticipated BMPs include runoff and erosion control, storm drain inlet protection and soil track-out prevention per Caltrans' standard specifications. It is anticipated that water truck/spray will be sufficient to properly maintain dust control (no visible dust) in areas of soil excavation, hauling on unpaved surfaces and in fill placement areas. Grading within barium-containing soil will be suspended during periods of high winds, when dust control measures are determined to be inadequate to control generation of visible dust, or where uncontrolled water runoff or soil trackout is observed until acceptable corrective measures are implemented. Caltrans' environmental consultant in coordination with the construction manager will have stop work authority if any of these conditions arise during barium-containing soil grading operations.

The highway construction contractor shall provide survey data of the final bariumcontaining soil containment zones (including ADL soil and clean cover materials) within Stockpiles 1 and 2. The survey data shall include precise latitude, longitude and elevations (North American Vertical Datum 1988), and corresponding SR-132 postmile limits (approximate SR-132 stations 1125+60 through 1131+95 [Stockpile 1] and 1135+90 through 1148+30 [Stockpile 2]) for Caltrans' use in recording a regulatoryrequired land use covenant (LUC)/deed restriction with the Stanislaus County Clerk-Recorder Office along with the original Assessor's Parcel Number(s) associated with that portion of the highway alignment. Per DTSC, a LUC is only required for the completed barium-containing soil containment zones. The completed soil stockpile removal areas including the western and eastern ends of Stockpile 2 and removed Stockpile 3 will not require a LUC.

The surface of the completed Stockpile 1 and 2 barium-containing soil and ADL consolidation zones (including east- and west-facing temporary slopes) will be capped with minimum 12-inch-thick clean fill material consisting of approved imported fill and/or minimum 4-inch-thick AB materials (pending placement of structural pavement section, hardscape and/or additional clean embankment fill). The proposed source of clean imported fill soil consists of currently vacant and agricultural land within planned excavation areas along the SR-132 ROW between the Carpenter Road and Rosemore Avenue overcrossings as depicted on Figure 1. The specific clean fill source areas (once defined by the highway construction contractor) will be sampled for laboratory analysis in general accordance with DTSC's Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Material (Appendix E) for pre-approval by DTSC prior to placement. Construction geotechnical inspectors will confirm proper barium-containing and clean imported fill compaction through observation and in-place density testing.

#### **5.1 Groundwater/Stormwater Monitoring**

Caltrans currently performs groundwater and stormwater monitoring at the soil stockpiles that will continue during Interim Phase 1 construction activities. Groundwater monitoring activities are performed on an annual basis (usually April) in general accordance with monitoring protocols presented in the DTSC-approved Final Work Plan, Groundwater Assessment prepared by Shaw Environmental, Inc., and dated January 2006 (Shaw, 2006).
