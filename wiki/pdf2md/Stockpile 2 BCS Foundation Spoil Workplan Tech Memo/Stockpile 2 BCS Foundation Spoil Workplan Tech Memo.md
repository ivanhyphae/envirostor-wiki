## **Technical Memorandum**

Date: February 28, 2022

To: Rick Demi, PE, Construction Manager

WSP/City of Modesto

From: John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG, Project Manager, Geocon

Subject: Stockpile 2 BCS Foundation Spoil Assessment Workplan

State Route 132 Project, Modesto, California

This Technical Memorandum has been prepared to address proper management and disposal/reuse of barium containing soil (BCS) spoils generated from the following structure foundation excavations within the southern portion of the Stockpile 2 BCS Containment Zone:

- **Weather Station Foundation Slab/Anchor** (approximate Station 1138+60): 4.5 foot-deep foundation excavations completed in July/August 2021 generating approximately 2 cubic yards of BCS material that was spread on the adjacent ground surface (see below photograph).
- **Overhead Sign #1** (approximate Station 1139+30): planned 5-foot-diameter by 25 foot-deep foundation excavation that will generate approximately 18.2 cubic yards of BCS material.

![](_page_0_Picture_11.jpeg)

Weather Station

Highway improvement plans depicting the foundation excavation locations are attached.

The excavated BCS material generated from the weather station and overhead sign foundations (conservative estimate of 40 cubic yards) will be stockpiled prior to offsite landfill disposal or onsite reuse. The pre-moistened BCS material will be placed on and covered with minimum 6-milimeter-thick plastic sheeting surrounded by erosion control measures (i.e. straw wattles, hay bales, etc.) as necessary. The stockpile cover will be secured with ropes and weights (i.e. sand bags, tires, etc.) as necessary.

![](_page_0_Picture_15.jpeg)

Eight discrete soil samples will be collected from the stockpile on a grid pattern at various depths. The samples will be collected in labelled stainless steel sample tubes, placed in a chilled cooler and transported to California Laboratory Services under chain-of-custody protocol. The laboratory will be instructed to composite the eight discrete soil samples into two 4-part composite samples. The composite soil samples will be analyzed for Title 22 metals following Environmental Protection Agency Test Methods 6010B and 7471A (mercury) under expedited 48-hour turn-around time. Soluble metals testing using the Waste Extraction Test and Toxicity Characteristic Leaching Procedure may be required for samples with elevated total metal concentrations to determine if the BCS material is a California or Federal Resource Conservation and Recovery Act hazardous waste.

The total barium and lead concentrations will be compared to the following site-specific background levels:

```
Barium - 120 milligrams per kilogram (mg/kg)
Lead - 3.8 mg/kg
```

In the event the total barium and lead analytical data does not exceed respective site-specific background concentrations, the stockpiled BCS may be suitable for onsite reuse provided Caltrans and DTCS approval of the proposed reuse location.

In the event Caltrans and/or DTSC do not approve of onsite reuse of the regulated BCS stockpile soil, the BCS material will be profiled for acceptance at Forward Landfill in Manteca or other accepting landfill facility. An authorized Caltrans representative shall sign any required non-hazardous waste and/or hazardous waste manifests as the generator. The designated transporter of the BCS material to the accepting landfill facility shall maintain the appropriate licensing. The transport trucks will be required to have covered soil loads prior to leaving the project site.

Following completed disposition of the BCS stockpile material, a letter summary report will be prepared to include the following information:

- A description of the completed BCS disturbance activities
- Locations and depth of BCS disturbance
- Volume of BCS material removed from the Stockpile 2 Containment Zone
- Site plan depicting the BCS disturbance areas
- Copies of BCS material characterization data (laboratory reports and chain-of-custody documentation), copies of landfill waste disposal manifests and tonnage receipts, and site photographs

This information will be included in the Removal Action Completion Report.
