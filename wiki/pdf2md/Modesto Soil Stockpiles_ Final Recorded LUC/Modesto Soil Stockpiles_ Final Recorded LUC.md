### LAND USE COVENANT AND AGREEMENT ENVIRONMENTAL RESTRICTIONS

County of Stanislaus
Caltrans Modesto Soll Stockpiles, State Route 132
Department Site Code: 900259

This Land Use Covenant and Agreement ("Covenant") is made by and between the California Department of Transportation (the "Covenantor"), the current owner of property located adjacent and west of Emerald Avenue between approximate State Route (SR) 132 Stations 1125+50 and 1133+50 (Stockpile 1) and between Emerald Avenue and SR 99 between approximate SR 132 Stations 1135+35 and 1148+30 (Stockpile 2) in Modesto, in the County of Stanislaus, State of California (the "Property"), and the Department of Toxic Substances Control (the "Department"). Pursuant to Civil Code section 1471, the Department has determined that this Covenant is reasonably necessary to protect present or future human health or safety or the environment as a result of the presence on the land of hazardous materials as defined in Health and Safety Code section 25260. The Covenantor and the Department hereby agree that, pursuant to Civil Code section 1471 and Health and Safety Code section 25355.5, the use of the Property be restricted as set forth in this Covenant and that the

Covenant shall conform with the requirements of California Code of Regulations, title 22, section 67391.1.

## ARTICLE I STATEMENT OF FACTS

- 1.1. <u>Property Location</u>. The Property that is subject to this Covenant, totaling approximately 9.3 acres, is more particularly described in the attached Exhibit A, "Legal Description", and depicted in Exhibit B, Plat to Accompany Legal Description. The Property is located in the area now generally bounded by SR 99 to the north, residential property to the east, commercial property to the west, and North Carpenter Road, approximately 1,800 feet to the south.
- 1.2. Remediation of Property. This Property has been investigated and/or remediated under the Department's oversight. The Department approved a Remedial Action Plan in accordance with Health and Safety Code, division 20, chapter 6.8. The remediation activities conducted at the Property include consolidation and capping of hazardous substances consisting of barium containing soil (BCS). Concentrations of barium as high as 72,000 milligrams per kilogram remain at the Property which are above levels acceptable for unrestricted land use.
- 1.3. <u>Basis for Environmental Restrictions</u>. As a result of the presence of hazardous substances, which are also hazardous materials as defined in Health and Safety Code section 25260, at the Property, the Department has concluded that it is reasonably necessary to restrict the use of the Property in order to protect present or future human health or safety or the environment, and that this Covenant is required as part of the Department-approved remedy for the Property. The Department has also concluded that the Property, as remediated and when used in compliance with the Environmental Restrictions of this Covenant, does not present an unacceptable risk to present and future human health or safety or the environment.

## ARTICLE II DEFINITIONS

- 2.1. <u>Department</u>. "Department" means the California Department of Toxic Substances Control and includes its successor agencies, if any.
- 2.2. <u>Environmental Restrictions</u>. "Environmental Restrictions" means all protective provisions, covenants, restrictions, requirements, prohibitions, and terms and conditions as set forth in this Covenant.
- 2.3. <u>Improvements</u>. "Improvements" includes, but is not limited to buildings, structures, roads, driveways, improved parking areas, wells, pipelines, or other utilities.
- 2.4. <u>Lease</u>. "Lease" means lease, rental agreement, or any other document that creates a right to use or occupy any portion of the Property.
- 2.5. Occupant. "Occupant" or "Occupants" means Owner and any person or entity entitled by ownership, leasehold, or other legal relationship to the right to occupy any portion of the Property.
- 2.6. Owner. "Owner" or "Owners" means the Covenantor, and any successor in interest including any heir and assignee, who at any time holds title to all or any portion of the Property.

## ARTICLE III GENERAL PROVISIONS

3.1. Runs with the Land. This Covenant sets forth Environmental Restrictions that apply to and encumber the Property and every portion thereof no matter how it is improved, held, used, occupied, leased, sold, hypothecated, encumbered, or conveyed. This Covenant: (a) runs with the land pursuant to Civil Code section 1471 and Health and Safety Code section 25355.5; (b) inures to the benefit of and passes with each and every portion of the Property; (c) is for the benefit of and is enforceable by the Department; and (d) is imposed upon the entire Property unless expressly stated as applicable only to a specific portion thereof.

- 3.2. Binding upon Owners/Occupants. This Covenant: (a) binds all Owners of the Property, their heirs, successors, and assignees; and (b) the agents, employees, and lessees of the Owners and the Owners' heirs, successors, and assignees. Pursuant to Civil Code section 1471, all successive Owners of the Property are expressly bound hereby for the benefit of the Department; this Covenant, however, is binding on all Owners and Occupants, and their respective successors and assignees, only during their respective periods of ownership or occupancy except that such Owners or Occupants shall continue to be liable for any violations of, or non-compliance with, the Environmental Restrictions of this Covenant or any acts or omissions during their ownership or occupancy.
- 3.3. <u>Incorporation into Deeds and Leases</u>. This Covenant shall be incorporated by reference in each and every deed and Lease for any portion of the Property.
- 3.4. Conveyance of Property. The Owner and new Owner shall provide Notice to the Department not later than 30 calendar days after any conveyance or receipt of any ownership interest in the Property (excluding Leases, and mortgages, liens, and other non-possessory encumbrances). The Notice shall include the name and mailing address of the new Owner of the Property and shall reference the site name and site code as listed on page one of this Covenant. The notice shall also include the Assessor's Parcel Number(s) noted on page one. If the new Owner's property has been assigned a different Assessor Parcel Number, each such Assessor Parcel Number that covers the Property must be provided. The Department shall not, by reason of this Covenant, have authority to approve, disapprove, or otherwise affect proposed conveyance, except as otherwise provided by law or by administrative order.
- 3.5. Costs of Administering the Covenant to Be Paid by Owner. The Department has already incurred and will in the future incur costs associated with this Covenant. Therefore, the Covenantor hereby covenants for the Covenantor and for all subsequent Owners that, pursuant to California Code of Regulations, title 22, section 67391.1(h), the Owner agrees to pay the Department's costs in administering, implementing, and enforcing this Covenant.

## ARTICLE IV RESTRICTIONS AND REQUIREMENTS

- 4.1. <u>Prohibited Uses</u>. The Property shall not be used for any of the following purposes without prior written approval by the Department:
  - (a) A residence, including any mobile home or factory-built housing, constructed or installed for use as residential human habitation.
  - (b) A hospital for humans.
  - (c) A public or private school for persons under 18 years of age.
  - (d) A day care center for children.
- 4.2. <u>Soil Management</u>. Soil management activities at the Property are subject to the following requirements in addition to any other applicable Environmental Restrictions:
  - (a) No activities that will disturb the soil (e.g., excavation, grading, removal, trenching, filling, earth movement, mining, or drilling) shall be allowed at the Property without a Soil Management Plan pre-approved by the Department in writing, as established in the June 20, 2023 Final Operation and Maintenance Agreement for the Property (Exhibit C).
  - (b) Any soil brought to the surface by grading, excavation, trenching or backfilling shall be managed in accordance with all applicable provisions of state and federal law.
- 4.3. <u>Prohibited Activities</u>. The following activities shall not be conducted at the Property:
  - (a) Drilling for any water, oil, or gas without prior written approval by the Department.
  - (b) Extraction or removal of groundwater without a Groundwater Management Plan pre-approved by the Department in writing.
  - (c) Activity that may alter, interfere with, or otherwise affect the integrity or effectiveness of, or the access to, any investigative, remedial, monitoring,

operation, or maintenance system (e.g., cap, vapor extraction system, monitoring system, groundwater extraction system) or activity required for the Property without prior written approval of the Department.

- 4.4. Access for Department. The Department shall have reasonable right of entry and access to the Property for inspection, investigation, remediation, monitoring, and other activities as deemed necessary by the Department in order to protect human health or safety or the environment.
- 4.5. Access for Implementing Operation and Maintenance. The entity or person responsible for implementing the operation and maintenance activities, if any, shall have reasonable right of entry and access to the Property for the purpose of implementing such operation and maintenance activities until the Department determines that no further operation and maintenance activity is required.
- 4.6. Inspection and Reporting Requirements. In accordance with the June 20, 2023 O&M Plan, Exhibit C, or its amendments or revisions that have been approved by the Department, the Owner shall conduct an annual inspection of the Property verifying compliance with this Covenant and shall submit an annual inspection report to the Department for its approval by January 18th of each year. The annual inspection report must include the dates, times, and names of those who conducted the inspection and reviewed the annual inspection report. It also shall describe how the observations that were the basis for the statements and conclusions in the annual inspection report were performed (e.g., drive-by, fly over, walk in, etc.). If any violation is noted, the annual inspection report must detail the steps taken to correct the violation and return to compliance. If the Owner identifies any violations of this Covenant during the annual inspection or at any other time, the Owner must within 10 calendar days of identifying the violation:
  - (a) determine the identity of the party in violation;
  - (b) send a letter advising the party of the violation of the Covenant; and
  - (c) demand that the violation cease immediately.

Additionally, a copy of any correspondence related to the violation of this Covenant shall be sent to the Department within 10 calendar days of its original transmission.

## ARTICLE V ENFORCEMENT

5.1. Enforcement. Failure of the Owner or Occupant to comply with this Covenant shall be grounds for the Department to require modification or removal of any Improvements constructed or placed upon any portion of the Property in violation of this Covenant. Violation of this Covenant, such as failure to submit (including submission of any false statement) record or report to the Department, shall be grounds for the Department to pursue administrative, civil, or criminal actions, as provided by law.

## ARTICLE VI VARIANCE, REMOVAL AND TERM

- 6.1. <u>Variance from Environmental Restrictions</u>. Any person may apply to the Department for a written variance from any of the Environmental Restrictions imposed by this Covenant. Such application shall be made in accordance with Health and Safety Code section 25223.
- 6.2. Removal of Environmental Restrictions. Any person may apply to the Department to remove any of the Environmental Restrictions imposed by this Covenant or terminate the Covenant in its entirety. Such applications shall be made in accordance with Health and Safety Code section 25224.
- 6.3. <u>Term.</u> Unless ended in accordance with paragraph 6.2, by law, or by the Department in the exercise of its discretion, this Covenant shall continue in effect in perpetuity.

OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE AGREEMENT

Health and Safety Code Section 25355.5 (a)(1)(C) and/or Section 2518714

The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the California Department of Transportation (Proponent) enter into this Operation and Maintenance Agreement (Agreement) for the site located adjacent and west of Emerald Avenue between approximate State Route (SR) 132 Stations 1125+50 (Stockpile 1) and between Emerald Avenue and SR 99 between approximate SR 132 Stations 1135+35 and 1148+30 (Stockpile 2) in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. Stockpiles 1 and 2 collectively constitute the Site. DTSC and the Proponent agree as follows:

- 1.0 <u>Jurisdiction</u>. This Agreement is entered into by DTSC and Proponent pursuant to Health and Safety Code section 25355.5(a)(1)(C) and/or section 25187 which authorizes DTSC to enter into an enforceable agreement to oversee the investigation and/or remediation of a release or threatened release of any hazardous substance or hazardous waste at or from the Site, and to oversee the operation and maintenance of any remedy installed at the Site.
- 2.0 <u>Installed Remedy</u>. A DTSC-approved remedy has been installed at the Site for the remediation of soil. The remedy consists of consolidation and clean fill capping of barium-containing soil (BCS) as highway embankment fill for the SR 132 West Expressway. The Site is owned by the Proponent. A project location map is attached as Exhibit A. A legal description and a diagram showing the locations of the installed remedy are attached as Exhibit B and Exhibit C.
- 3.0 Operation and Maintenance of Installed Remedy. Operation and maintenance of the installed remedy are required at the Site. Proponent shall leave the installed remedy in place, and continue its operation and maintenance, until and except to the extent that DTSC authorizes Proponent in writing to discontinue or modify part or all of the installed remedy.
- 4.0 <u>Implementation of Operation and Maintenance Plan</u>. Proponent shall fully implement the DTSC-approved Operation and Maintenance Plan dated

December 1, 2022, attached as Exhibit D, including any requirements for inspections, monitoring, reporting, and record keeping.

- 5.0 <u>Modification or Discontinuation of Installed System</u>. Proponent shall submit a written request for DTSC's authorization for any modification or discontinuation of the Installed Remedy or any part thereof at least 60 days, to the extent feasible, prior to the intended date of any proposed modification or discontinuation. Proponent may seek modification or discontinuation of the Installed Remedy or any part thereof if:
  - a. The Proponent has met the remediation objectives for the Site:
  - b. The modification would better achieve the remediation objectives;
  - c. The Installed Remedy could not achieve the remediation objectives and other cleanup methods will be implemented; or
  - d. It has been demonstrated that the maximum achievable remediation has occurred.

The written request to DTSC shall include the reasons for the request, a detailed description of any work to be done or modification to be made, and a map showing the exact location of the proposed work.

- 6.0 <u>DTSC-Required Modification</u>. DTSC may require modification, replacement, or additions to the Installed Remedy if the Installed Remedy or part of thereof is not achieving the remediation objectives or is not protecting human health or the environment. DTSC may require additional evaluations, designs and the construction and operation of facilities to achieve these objectives.
- Remedy after a period of five years from the approval of the Remedial Action Completion Report, which occurred on January 18, 2023, and every five years thereafter. The review and reevaluation shall be conducted to determine if human health and the environment are being adequately protected by the Installed Remedy. Proponent shall submit a report of the results of the five-year review. The report shall describe the results of all sampling analyses, tests and other data generated or received by Proponent and evaluate the adequacy of the implemented remedy in protecting human health and the environment. As a result of any review work performed under this Agreement, DTSC may require Proponent to perform additional review work or modify the review work previously performed by Proponent.
- 8.0 Quality Control/Quality Assurance (QC/QA). All sampling and analysis conducted by Proponent under this Agreement shall be performed in accordance with the QC/QA procedures submitted by Proponent and approved by DTSC pursuant to this Agreement.

## OPERATIONS AND MAINTENANCE PLAN CALTRANS MODESTO SOIL STOCKPILES SR 132 WEST PROJECT - INTERIM PHASE 1

#### 1.0 INTRODUCTION

This Operations and Maintenance Plan (O&M Plan) has been prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. on behalf of the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (the Site). The Site is located along the eastern portion of the alignment of the newly constructed State Route (SR) 132 West Project in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California (Project Location Map, Figure 1). The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in cooperation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB), is the lead regulatory agency providing site mitigation oversight under a Caltrans Interagency Agreement.

This O&M Plan has been prepared for barium-containing soil (BCS) Stockpiles 1 through 3 that were consolidated, capped with clean fill material, and utilized as highway embankment fill during Interim Phase 1 construction of the SR 132 West Project in accordance with the 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) and Caltrans-approved project design plans and specifications. The RDIP provided the technical approach and detailed plans for interim implementation of the selected remedial remedy identified in the 2018 Final Remedial Action Plan (RAP). The selected remedial alternative to mitigate exposure to BCS was consolidation and containment by incorporating the stockpiles as highway embankment fill and covering with clean fill and structural pavement section during Interim Phase 1 and Ultimate Phase 2 construction of the SR 132 West Project.

Interim Phase 1 of the SR 132 West Project included the construction of two of four planned traffic lanes from east of SR 99 to North Dakota Avenue and related highway bridge structures and interchanges. BCS Stockpiles 1 through 3, portions of which contain elevated levels of barium and lead, have been contained during Interim Phase 1 of the project by utilizing them as clean capped embankment material within the overall limits of BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2 located adjacent and extending westerly of SR 99 to beyond Emerald Avenue (Preconstruction Site Plan, Figure 2). SR 132 was opened to traffic on November 3, 2022. Ultimate Phase 2 construction will include highway widening to four traffic lanes.

#### 1.1 Site History and Background

From the 1930s to 1970s, property beneath and northeast of the SR 99/Kansas Avenue Interchange was occupied by chemical processing facilities operated by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC) and its predecessors. Ores and minerals including barite (barium sulfate) and celestite (strontium sulfate) were processed for use in greases, lubricating oil and pigment blanks. Sodium sulfide was generated as a by-product and sold as a caustic and reagent. From the 1950s to the 1970s, liquid residue ("tailings") generated at this facility was discharged to unlined evaporation ponds. In 1961, the State of California purchased a 4.3-acre parcel in the southwestern portion of the FMC facility, including a

portion of the ponds, for the construction of the SR 99 freeway through Modesto. Pond tailings and underlying soils from the FMC property along with native soils excavated south of the SR 99/Kansas Avenue interchange were subsequently placed to create BCS Stockpiles 1 through 3.

Prior to construction of Interim Phase 1 of the SR 132 West Project, the Site consisted of BCS Stockpiles 1 through 3 within Caltrans SR 132 right-of-way (ROW) east and west of SR 99. The BCS Stockpile material is primarily comprised of layered, poorly graded sand and silty sand similar to underlying native alluvial deposits of the Modesto Formation. The average maximum stockpile fill thickness was approximately 20 feet with a total estimated volume of 160,000 cubic yards.

The potential for the BCS Stockpiles to contain residual chemicals associated with the former FMC impoundments was identified in a 2003 *Initial Stie Assessment* performed during the highway project development process. Caltrans subsequently entered into an interagency agreement with DTSC, with cooperation of the CVRWQCB, for assessment and mitigation regulatory oversight.

#### 1.2 Environmental Investigations and Remedial Actions

Several environmental investigations were conducted at the Site by Caltrans including the following major technical studies:

- Site investigations including stockpile characterization, groundwater monitoring well installation, and stormwater sampling performed between 2004 and 2006.
- Preliminary Endangerment Assessment including human health risk assessment (HHRA) completed in 2009.
- Additional Stockpile 3 characterization completed in 2012.
- Supplemental site investigation including stockpile perimeter and fence line sampling and updated HHRA completed in 2013.
- Groundwater and stormwater monitoring reporting since 2012.
- Feasibility Study (FS) presenting evaluation of remedial alternatives completed in 2014.
- RAP presenting the selected remedial alternative completed in 2018.
- RDIP completed in 2019 presenting the technical details of implementing the RAP during Interim Phase 1 construction of the SR 132 West Project.

Completed remedial actions at the Site include partial removal of Stockpile 3 in 2012 to facilitate an off-ramp widening project and consolidation, and containment and clean capping of Stockpiles 1 through 3 between 2020 and 2022 during construction of Interim Phase 1 of the SR 132 West Project. Stockpile 3 BCS material was entirely removed from the east side of SR 99 and placed within the Stockpile 1 Containment Zone. A summary of the completed BCS stockpile remedial actions at the Site is presented in the 2022 Removal Action Completion Report (RACR).

Copies of the environmental investigation and monitoring reports for the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones at the SR 132 West Project are accessible from the following regulatory websites:

#### 1.3 BCS Containment Zones (Restricted Areas)

BCS at the Site has been consolidated and clean capped within the designated Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones (highway embankment fills) during construction of Interim Phase 1 of the SR 132 West Project. #### 2.0 SOIL MANAGEMENT PROCEDURES

This section describes procedures to be implemented should BCS material within the Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones (Restricted Areas) be disturbed. Those involved with disturbance of BCS material within the Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones are responsible for implementing the procedures in this O&M Plan.

Caltrans general highway maintenance procedures may be accessed from the below website:

https://dot.ca.gov/programs/maintenance/maintenance-manual

Where requirements presented herein conflict with standard Caltrans maintenance procedures, the requirements presented within this O&M Plan shall take precedence.

#### 2.1 Notification of BCS Disturbance

Any disturbance of BCS material within the Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones must be performed in compliance with this O&M Plan. Caltrans is responsible for minimum ten working day advance notification to DTSC of any planned activities that may disturb BCS material and/or associated clean cover fill materials. No BCS disturbance is permitted without prior written authorization from DTSC.

#### 2.2 Health and Safety

Workers involved with and in the vicinity of BCS material disturbance/handling shall implement reasonable health and safety precautions and wear appropriate personal protective equipment. Workers shall attend onsite health and safety briefings to communicate the potential human health risks associated with exposure to elevated barium and lead concentrations in soil. At a minimum, onsite worker health and safety measures should include the following:

- Using gloves and wearing long sleeve/long pant clothing to minimize dermal exposure to BCS material.
- Washing face and hands before eating, drinking, or smoking to minimize potential ingestion of BCS material.
- Decontaminating excavation equipment and tools in contact with BCS material.

Contractors/workers involved with disturbance of BCS material shall be required to comply with all applicable California Occupation Safety and Health Administration rules and regulations.

#### 2.3 Dust Mitigation

Proper dust mitigation measures shall be implemented during any BCS material disturbance activities to minimize fugitive dust emissions in accordance with San Joaquin Valley Air Pollution Control District applicable rules. Water spray application should be sufficient to maintain proper dust control during BCS material handling in addition to covering of temporary stockpiles and truck loads. Work should be suspended during high winds or where dust control measures are inadequate to control generation of visible dust.

#### 2.4 BCS Material Stockpiling

Excavated BCS material requiring temporary storage (less than 30 days) prior to onsite replacement or offsite landfill disposal shall be placed in a designated onsite and secured stockpile area. The premoistened BCS material shall be placed on and covered with minimum 6-millimeter-thick plastic sheeting surrounded by erosion control measures (i.e. straw wattles, hay bales, etc.) in accordance with California Health and Safety Code 25123.3(b)(4)(A). The stockpile cover shall be secured with ropes and weights (i.e. sand bags, tires, etc.).

#### 2.5 BCS Material Reuse/Offsite Disposal

Removed BCS material may be placed back within the Containment Zone excavation from where it was generated from provided the BCS material is properly compacted and covered with minimum 12-inch-thick DTSC-approved clean fill material and/or structural pavement section. The proposed source(s) of clean fill material shall be properly evaluated based on DTSC's 2001 *Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Material*.

Excess excavated BCS material requiring offsite disposal will require stockpile sampling and characterization testing for California-licensed landfill acceptance. The required sampling frequency and laboratory analysis is determined by the landfill facility. At a minimum the BCS stockpile characterization samples shall be analyzed for Title 22 metals following Environmental Protection Agency Test Methods 6010B and 7471A (mercury). Soluble metals testing using the Waste Extraction Test and Toxicity Characteristic Leaching Procedure may be required for samples with elevated total metal concentrations to determine if the BCS material is a California or Federal Resource Conservation and Recovery Act hazardous waste.

An authorized Caltrans representative shall sign any required non-hazardous waste and/or hazardous waste manifests as the generator. The designated transporter of the BCS material to the accepting landfill facility shall maintain appropriate licensing.

#### 2.6 Stormwater Management

Caltrans is responsible for maintaining stormwater best management practices to minimize uncontrolled runoff and potential discharge of pollutants. Caltrans further performs period stormwater sampling in the vicinity of the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones during significant precipitation events. The BCS material is buried beneath highway pavement and clean fill material along the northern fill slopes of the Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones and therefore is not in direct contact with stormwater runoff.

#### 2.7 Site Access and Security

The Site is a portion of an active State highway with no non-motorized public access. The perimeter of the SR-132 ROW in the vicinity of the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones has perimeter fencing further restricting public access. Caltrans will periodically patrol the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones (Restricted Areas) to confirm no active trespassing or other unauthorized access (i.e. homeless encampments) that may have impacted the clean cap. Any breaches to the perimeter security fencing and resulting clean cap damage will be documented on an O&M Site Inspection Form as described in Section 3.1.
