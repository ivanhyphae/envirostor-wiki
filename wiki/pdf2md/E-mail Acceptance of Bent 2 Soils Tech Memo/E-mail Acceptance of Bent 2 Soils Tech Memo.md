Hi Richard,

The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Water Board (WB) have reviewed the Technical Memorandum (Tech Memo) dated October 30, 2020 regarding the use of Bent 2 excavation soil as clean cap material. The soil sample results do not meet the clean cap criteria as established in the April 9, 2020 Variance Request approved by DTSC. However, both DTSC and the WB agree that this soil may be placed on top of Stockpile 2 at a thickness of 6 inches or less as long as it is covered with clean base material and the asphalt/concrete used to construct the roadway. This will allow efficient use of the Bent 2 soil while not jeopardizing the integrity of the remedy.

Based on discussions and e-mails subsequent to the Tech Memo's submittal, it is understood that due to the current project schedule the Bent 2 soils may be temporarily exposed to the elements during the winter months. It is thus very important that the rainy season surface water sampling effort and the erosion control measures outlined in the Revised Stormwater Prevention Plan (SWPPP) Amendment dated October 22, 2020 are followed as strictly as possible. These efforts will help ensure contaminants remain on site and do not impact the surrounding area.

This e-mail serves as DTSCs official acceptance of the Tech Memo and Revised SWPPP Amendment. Sincerely,
