# OPERATIONS AND MAINTENANCE PLAN CALTRANS MODESTO SOIL STOCKPILES SR 132 WEST PROJECT - INTERIM PHASE 1

## 1.0 INTRODUCTION

This Operations and Maintenance Plan (O&M Plan) has been prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. on behalf of the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (the Site). The Site is located along the eastern portion of the alignment of the newly constructed State Route (SR) 132 West Project in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California (Project Location Map, Figure 1). The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in cooperation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB), is the lead regulatory agency providing site mitigation oversight under a Caltrans Interagency Agreement.

This O&M Plan has been prepared for barium-containing soil (BCS) Stockpiles 1 through 3 that were consolidated, capped with clean fill material, and utilized as highway embankment fill during Interim Phase 1 construction of the SR 132 West Project in accordance with the 2019 *Remedial Design Implementation Plan* (RDIP) and Caltrans-approved project design plans and specifications. The RDIP provided the technical approach and detailed plans for interim implementation of the selected remedial remedy identified in the 2018 *Final Remedial Action Plan* (RAP). The selected remedial alternative to mitigate exposure to BCS was consolidation and containment by incorporating the stockpiles as highway embankment fill and covering with clean fill and structural pavement section during Interim Phase 1 and Ultimate Phase 2 construction of the SR 132 West Project.

Interim Phase 1 of the SR 132 West Project included the construction of two of four planned traffic lanes from east of SR 99 to North Dakota Avenue and related highway bridge structures and interchanges. BCS Stockpiles 1 through 3, portions of which contain elevated levels of barium and lead, have been contained during Interim Phase 1 of the project by utilizing them as clean capped embankment material within the overall limits of BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2 located adjacent and extending westerly of SR 99 to beyond Emerald Avenue (Preconstruction Site Plan, Figure 2). SR 132 was opened to traffic on November 3, 2022. Ultimate Phase 2 construction will include highway widening to four traffic lanes.

### 1.1 Site History and Background

From the 1930s to 1970s, property beneath and northeast of the SR 99/Kansas Avenue Interchange was occupied by chemical processing facilities operated by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC) and its predecessors. Ores and minerals including barite (barium sulfate) and celestite (strontium sulfate) were processed for use in greases, lubricating oil and pigment blanks. Sodium sulfide was generated as a by-product and sold as a caustic and reagent. From the 1950s to the 1970s, liquid residue ("tailings") generated at this facility was discharged to unlined evaporation ponds. In 1961, the State of California purchased a 4.3-acre parcel in the southwestern portion of the FMC facility, including a

portion of the ponds, for the construction of the SR 99 freeway through Modesto. Pond tailings and underlying soils from the FMC property along with native soils excavated south of the SR 99/Kansas Avenue interchange were subsequently placed to create BCS Stockpiles 1 through 3.

Prior to construction of Interim Phase 1 of the SR 132 West Project, the Site consisted of BCS Stockpiles 1 through 3 within Caltrans SR 132 right-of-way (ROW) east and west of SR 99. The BCS Stockpile material is primarily comprised of layered, poorly graded sand and silty sand similar to underlying native alluvial deposits of the Modesto Formation. The average maximum stockpile fill thickness was approximately 20 feet with a total estimated volume of 160,000 cubic yards.

The potential for the BCS Stockpiles to contain residual chemicals associated with the former FMC impoundments was identified in a 2003 *Initial Site Assessment* performed during the highway project development process. Caltrans subsequently entered into an interagency agreement with DTSC, with cooperation of the CVRWQCB, for assessment and mitigation regulatory oversight.

### 1.2 Environmental Investigations and Remedial Actions

Several environmental investigations were conducted at the Site by Caltrans including the following major technical studies:

- Site investigations including stockpile characterization, groundwater monitoring well installation, and stormwater sampling performed between 2004 and 2006.
- Preliminary Endangerment Assessment including human health risk assessment (HHRA) completed in 2009.
- Additional Stockpile 3 characterization completed in 2012.
- Supplemental site investigation including stockpile perimeter and fence line sampling and updated HHRA completed in 2013.
- Groundwater and stormwater monitoring reporting since 2012.
- Feasibility Study (FS) presenting evaluation of remedial alternatives completed in 2014.
- RAP presenting the selected remedial alternative completed in 2018.
- RDIP completed in 2019 presenting the technical details of implementing the RAP during Interim Phase 1 construction of the SR 132 West Project.

Completed remedial actions at the Site include partial removal of Stockpile 3 in 2012 to facilitate an off-ramp widening project and consolidation, and containment and clean capping of Stockpiles 1 through 3 between 2020 and 2022 during construction of Interim Phase 1 of the SR 132 West Project. Stockpile 3 BCS material was entirely removed from the east side of SR 99 and placed within the Stockpile 1 Containment Zone. A summary of the completed BCS stockpile remedial actions at the Site is presented in the 2022 *Removal Action Completion Report* (RACR).

### 1.3 BCS Containment Zones (Restricted Areas)

BCS at the Site has been consolidated and clean capped within the designated Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones (highway embankment fills) during construction of Interim Phase 1 of the SR 132 West Project. The Stockpile 1 Containment Zone is located adjacent and west of Emerald Avenue between approximate SR 132 Stations 1125+50 and 1133+50. The Stockpile 2 Containment Zone is located between Emerald Avenue and SR 99 between approximate SR 132 Stations 1135+35 and 1148+30.

The BCS material contains elevated barium concentrations (up to 72,000 milligrams per kilogram that exceeds regulatory screening levels) and to a lesser extent elevated lead concentrations. Aerially-deposited lead (ADL) impacted soil generated from the northbound shoulder of SR 99 containing lead concentrations exceeding California hazardous waste thresholds was further placed within the Stockpile 1 BCS Containment Zone.

As-built plans depicting the aerial extent of the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones are presented on Figures 3-1 and 3-2. Profiles of the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones are presented on Figures 5-1 and 5-2. Photographs of the completed SR 132 highway improvements, and a legal description and boundary survey of the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones are attached.

## 2.0 SOIL MANAGEMENT PROCEDURES

This section describes procedures to be implemented should BCS material within the Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones (Restricted Areas) be disturbed. Those involved with disturbance of BCS material within the Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones are responsible for implementing the procedures in this O&M Plan.

Caltrans general highway maintenance procedures may be accessed from the below website:

https://dot.ca.gov/programs/maintenance/maintenance-manual

Where requirements presented herein conflict with standard Caltrans maintenance procedures, the requirements presented within this O&M Plan shall take precedence.

### 2.1 Notification of BCS Disturbance

Any disturbance of BCS material within the Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones must be performed in compliance with this O&M Plan. Caltrans is responsible for minimum ten working day advance notification to DTSC of any planned activities that may disturb BCS material and/or associated clean cover fill materials. No BCS disturbance is permitted without prior written authorization from DTSC.

### 2.2 Health and Safety

Workers involved with and in the vicinity of BCS material disturbance/handling shall implement reasonable health and safety precautions and wear appropriate personal protective equipment. Workers shall attend onsite health and safety briefings to communicate the potential human health risks associated with exposure to elevated barium and lead concentrations in soil. At a minimum, onsite worker health and safety measures should include the following:

- Using gloves and wearing long sleeve/long pant clothing to minimize dermal exposure to BCS material.
- Washing face and hands before eating, drinking, or smoking to minimize potential ingestion of BCS material.
- Decontaminating excavation equipment and tools in contact with BCS material.

Contractors/workers involved with disturbance of BCS material shall be required to comply with all applicable California Occupation Safety and Health Administration rules and regulations.

### 2.3 Dust Mitigation

Proper dust mitigation measures shall be implemented during any BCS material disturbance activities to minimize fugitive dust emissions in accordance with San Joaquin Valley Air Pollution Control District applicable rules. Water spray application should be sufficient to maintain proper dust control during BCS material handling in addition to covering of temporary stockpiles and truck loads. Work should be suspended during high winds or where dust control measures are inadequate to control generation of visible dust.

### 2.4 BCS Material Stockpiling

Excavated BCS material requiring temporary storage (less than 30 days) prior to onsite replacement or offsite landfill disposal shall be placed in a designated onsite and secured stockpile area. The premoistened BCS material shall be placed on and covered with minimum 6-millimeter-thick plastic sheeting surrounded by erosion control measures (i.e. straw wattles, hay bales, etc.) in accordance with California Health and Safety Code 25123.3(b)(4)(A). The stockpile cover shall be secured with ropes and weights (i.e. sand bags, tires, etc.).

### 2.5 BCS Material Reuse/Offsite Disposal

Removed BCS material may be placed back within the Containment Zone excavation from where it was generated from provided the BCS material is properly compacted and covered with minimum 12-inch-thick DTSC-approved clean fill material and/or structural pavement section. The proposed source(s) of clean fill material shall be properly evaluated based on DTSC's 2001 *Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Material*.

Excess excavated BCS material requiring offsite disposal will require stockpile sampling and characterization testing for California-licensed landfill acceptance. The required sampling frequency and laboratory analysis is determined by the landfill facility. At a minimum the BCS stockpile characterization samples shall be analyzed for Title 22 metals following Environmental Protection Agency Test Methods 6010B and 7471A (mercury). Soluble metals testing using the Waste Extraction Test and Toxicity Characteristic Leaching Procedure may be required for samples with elevated total metal concentrations to determine if the BCS material is a California or Federal Resource Conservation and Recovery Act hazardous waste.

An authorized Caltrans representative shall sign any required non-hazardous waste and/or hazardous waste manifests as the generator. The designated transporter of the BCS material to the accepting landfill facility shall maintain appropriate licensing.

### 2.6 Stormwater Management

Caltrans is responsible for maintaining stormwater best management practices to minimize uncontrolled runoff and potential discharge of pollutants. Caltrans further performs period stormwater sampling in the vicinity of the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones during significant precipitation events. The BCS material is buried beneath highway pavement and clean fill material along the northern fill slopes of the Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones and therefore is not in direct contact with stormwater runoff.

### 2.7 Site Access and Security

The Site is a portion of an active State highway with no non-motorized public access. The perimeter of the SR-132 ROW in the vicinity of the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones has perimeter fencing further restricting public access. Caltrans will periodically patrol the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones (Restricted Areas) to confirm no active trespassing or other unauthorized access (i.e. homeless encampments) that may have impacted the clean cap. Any breaches to the perimeter security fencing and resulting clean cap damage will be documented on an O&M Site Inspection Form as described in Section 3.1. Any related security fencing and/or clean cap damage shall be repaired in a timely fashion.

## 3.0 OPERATIONS AND MAINTENANCE PROCEDURES

This section summarizes the O&M procedures that will be implemented by Caltrans to ensure the integrity of the clean capped Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones. The O&M activities will be completed pursuant to the O&M Agreement entered into between DTSC and Caltrans.

### 3.1 Inspections

Caltrans will perform minimum annual inspections during the third quarter of each year (July through September) prior to the rainy season. During the annual site inspections, the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones (Restricted Areas) will be observed for any visual indicators of damage to the cap. The annual inspections will include the following:

- Observations of Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zone surface cover including mechanical stabilized earth retaining walls along the southern boundary, highway pavement, and northerly clean capped fill slopes.
- Observations of drainage swales, storm drain inlets and slope down drains, and other stormwater conveyance structures.
- Observations of perimeter security fencing and indicators of unauthorized access and trespassing.
- Documentation of any significant pavement cracks/distress, erosion, or other indicators of compromised clean cover materials.
- Documentation of required maintenance.

In addition to the annual inspections, supplemental inspections will also occur after significant events such as fires, substantial rainfall (1 inch in 24 hours), seismic activity, traffic accidents that may damage the clean cap, homeless encampment cleanups, etc.

The field observations will be documented on the attached O&M Site Inspection Form during the annual and supplemental site inspections. The findings of the inspections will further be documented in an annual report submitted to DTSC as discussed in Section 4.3.

### 3.2 Maintenance and Repairs

Maintenance and repairs will be performed on an as-needed basis based on the findings of the annual inspection. Required maintenance will be completed within 30 days of the site inspection unless an extension is approved by DTSC. Typical routine maintenance and repair activities may include, but are not limited to the following:

- Pavement pothole repairs and crack sealing
- Repair of stormwater runoff and erosion damage
- Repair of slope failures
- Fencing repairs

DTSC requires advance notification of any planned work within the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones that may expose and/or disturb BCS material. The BCS work notifications shall include the following information:

- The scope and purpose of the planned work
- Estimated start and completions dates
- Type and depth of BCS disturbance
- Estimated volume of BCS material to be stockpiled
- Final destination of stockpiled BCS material (reuse within Containment Zone and/or landfill disposal)
- Site plan depicting work areas

The Caltrans designated contact will coordinate with DTSC prior to initiation of the work. No BCS disturbance activities will be permitted without prior written DTSC authorization.

### 4.2 Documentation for BCS Disturbance Activities

Within 30 days of completing work within the Stockpile 1 and 2 Containment Zones that disturbs BCS material, Caltrans shall provide written documentation of the activities to DTSC. The written documentation shall include the following information:

- A description of the completed BCS disturbance activities
- Locations and depth of BCS disturbance
- Volume of BCS material removed from Containment Zones
- Volume of BCS material stockpiled for offsite landfill disposal
- Site plan depicting BCS disturbance areas
- Copies of BCS material characterization data (laboratory reports and chain-of-custody documentation), copies of landfill waste disposal manifests and tonnage receipts, and site photographs

### 4.3 O&M Annual Inspection Summary Report

An annual report will be prepared by Caltrans to summarize any BCS material disturbance activities, and to document the findings of the annual inspection and any required associated maintenance activities. The annual report will be submitted to DTSC by January 31<sup>st</sup> for the prior calendar year.

The annual report will include the following elements:

- A summary of completed BCS disturbance activities
- A summary of the annual site inspections and any periodic inspections
- A summary of any completed maintenance actions and/or repairs
- Details of any significant changes to site conditions
- Copies of the O&M Site Inspection forms
- Photographs depicting site conditions and any areas requiring maintenance and/or repairs
- Conclusions regarding the ongoing effectiveness of the clean cap/containment system

The annual reports will document the findings of the site inspections and confirm that the clean cover materials over the Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS Containment Zones are effective in preventing direct exposure to contaminated soil. The annual reports will further confirm that any required repairs and/or maintenance activities have been completed, and that the Site remains compliant with the DTSC-required Land Use Covenant.

### 4.4 Five-Year Review

Caltrans will perform five-year reviews to assess the effectiveness of the interim Stockpile 1 and 2 BCS containment measures. The five-year reviews will focus on monitoring and maintenance of the implemented containment measures and potential for exposure to contained and capped BCS/ADL soil materials. Monitoring of groundwater and stormwater are routinely reported to DTSC and the CVRWQCB until permanently suspended and therefore would not be a focus of the reviews. DTSC will provide project certification following completion of the final containment measures implemented during Ultimate Phase 2 of the SR 132 West Project.

Five-Year Review Reports will include the following elements:

- Introduction
- Site background
- Physical setting
- Site chronology
- Removal actions
- Five-year review process
- Technical assessment
- Issues
- Conclusions
- Recommendations
- Cost impacts

The "Technical Assessment" section will include a summary of the previous four Annual O&M Inspection Summary Reports. The Five-Year Review Reports will also contain photographs depicting site conditions.
