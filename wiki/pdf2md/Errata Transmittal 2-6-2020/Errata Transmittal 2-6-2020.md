Following the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) concurrence with the June 2014 Feasibility Study and June 2014 Remedial Action Plan for mitigation of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, a Remedial Design Implementation Plan was prepared and subsequently accepted by the DTSC in January 2019.

These decision documents and namely the RDIP, established the prescribed remedy for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles via the "containment alternative" during construction of the SR 132 West Expressway project.

At the time the RDIP was prepared, Geocon, Inc. was under contract to perform environmental services with the state and identified as Caltrans' sole agent to ensure that containment of the stockpiles was accomplished in accordance with the RAP and RDIP. However, following acceptance of the RDIP by the DTSC, Geocon is no longer under contract with the state.

The attached Post Construction Contract Award Errata updates the pertinent sections of the RDIP to reflect changes to previously identified roles and responsibilities and, strikes Geocon as the agent employed by the state.

Regardless of the consultant who will perform the work necessary to ensure that the stockpiles are remedied in accordance with the "containment alternative", Caltrans is the responsible party that is required to demonstrate the work was performed as described in the RDIP.
