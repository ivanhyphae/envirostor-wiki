# **SITE BACKGROUND**

Around 1930, Barium Products Ltd. occupied the property at 1200 Barium Road (now Graphics Drive) in the city of Modesto, California. The property is located just east of SR 99 between Woodland Avenue and Kansas Avenue. Barium Products Ltd. was a chemical manufacturing company that processed a variety of ores and minerals including barite and celestite. Materials produced included barium and strontium compounds used for greases, lubricating oil, paint pigment blanks, etc. Sodium sulfide (Na2S) was also generated as a by-product of barite processing and was sold as a caustic and used as a reagent in the mining industry.

# **PAST SURFACE WATER SAMPLING ACTIVITIES**

The original surface water sampling event at the soil stockpiles was conducted in March 2006 by Shaw in general accordance with its January 2006 SAP. A total of seven surface water samples were collected during a qualifying rain event (i.e., an event meeting the qualifying criteria of visible runoff and 72 hours of prior dry weather). Shaw constructed shallow depressions within the Caltrans ROW in order to collect runoff from the stockpiles as there was no surface water migration beyond the ROW. The samples were analyzed for dissolved metals, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), nitrate, sulfate, and sulfide.

Subsequently, Geocon collected surface water samples from five designated surface runoff locations (PL1 through PL5) and two background locations (BG1 and BG2) on an on-call basis between April 2013 and April 2019 in accordance with the SAP Addendum. The sample locations are shown on Figure 2. Sample locations PL1 and PL2 were along North Emerald Avenue to collect runoff from Stockpiles 1 and 2, respectively. Sample location PL3 was along the southern edge of Stockpile 2. Sample location PL4 was located near Stockpile 3 where the storm water enters the northern portion of the drainage basin. Sample location PL5 was located near the gate valve at the southern end of the drainage basin. The gate valve, if opened, would release the storm water from the drainage basin to the Tuolumne River. The gate valve would only be opened if the storm water basin were filled to near capacity and would jeopardize the northbound lanes of SR 99. The sample locations were approved by the DTSC and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB). Geocon did not observe runoff migration from the Caltrans ROW during its inspections.

# **FIELD ACTIVITIES**

On November 2, 2022 a Site walk was performed with the Caltrans TO Manager and Stantec. During the Site walk, revised surface water and background sampling locations were identified. Background sample BG2 was removed from the surface water sampling network and a new background sample BG3 was added. BG3 is located along the east side of North Carpenter Road approximately, 700 feet south of the southeast corner of North Carpenter Road and Kansas Avenue (Figure 2). Additionally, surface runoff locations PL6 and PL7 were added to the surface water sampling network. Location PL6 is located at a surface water discharge point on the north side of SR 132 near SR 99 and location PL7 is located along the north side of SR132 at the discharge pipe along storm water basin 5 located at the northeast corner of SR 132 and North Carpenter Road (Figure 2). As part of the surface water inspection, Caltrans requested that Stantec inspect the road embankment along the north side of SR 132.

On February 28, 2023, Stantec mobilized to the Site at approximately 8:45 am to perform the surface water inspection. The rain event had occurred overnight with only minimal rain at the time of surface water inspection. Precipitation was measured as 0.09 inches on February 28, 2023. A photographic record is attached as Appendix A.

The following was observed during the surface water and SR 132 northern road embankment inspection:

- The area around the background surface water location BG1 has not changed, there was no visible water ponding in the area during the inspection.
- There was no visible water ponding in the area around background surface water location BG3.
- Surface water sample location PL6, there was no active runoff from the stormwater pipe.
- Surface water sample location PL7, there was trash and debris blocking the end of the discharge pipe. There was no active runoff from the stormwater pipe.
- Soil erosion was observed on the east side of stormwater basin 5, (Appendix A, Photograph 4).
