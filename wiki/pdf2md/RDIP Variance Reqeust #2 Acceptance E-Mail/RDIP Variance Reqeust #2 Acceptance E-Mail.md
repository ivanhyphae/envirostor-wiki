The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Water Board (WB) have reviewed the Second Variance to the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (Request) dated September 2, 2020. The Request provides the characteristics of Class 1, Class 2, and Class 3 aggregate subbase material and indicates that a minimum of 4 inches of Class 3 subbase material would provide adequate clean cover over the Barium Containing Soil/Aerially Deposited Lead (BCS/ADL) stockpiles until completion of the pavement and hardscape is in place. Both DTSC and the WB agree that Class 3 subbase can be used as an acceptable clean cap alternative however, a visual inspection of this stockpiled material conducted on September 15, 2020 revealed that it is primarily a fine sand with most of the grains likely small enough to pass through a No. 4 sieve. Given its abundant fine grained nature, both agencies agree that a minimum cap thickness of 6 inches rather than 4 inches is warranted and that increased monitoring may be necessary. This is especially true since no pavement or hard scaping will be completed prior to the upcoming rainy season.

In addition, DTSC and the WB are requesting a single, composite sample be obtained for barium and lead analysis following Environmental Protection Agency Test Method 6010B from the stockpile of Class 3 material adjacent to the Rosemore Overcrossing. This is necessary to ensure this soil meets the standards imposed in the RDIP since it may be used as Class 3 subbase clean cap over the BCS/ADL stockpiles.

This e-mail serves as DTSCs official acceptance of the Request for use of Class 3 subbase. Sincerely,
