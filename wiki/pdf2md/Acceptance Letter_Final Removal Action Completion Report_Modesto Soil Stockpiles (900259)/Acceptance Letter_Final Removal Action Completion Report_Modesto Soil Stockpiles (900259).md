ACCEPTANCE OF THE INTERIM REMOVAL ACTION COMPLETION REPORT – CALTRANS MODESTO SOIL STOCKPILES, PHASE 1 INTERIM STATE ROUTE 132 WEST PROJECT, MODESTO, STANISLAUS COUNTY, CALIFORNIA (SITE CODE: 900259-00)

## Dear Mr. Juhrend:

The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) has reviewed the Removal Action Completion Report – Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Interim Phase 1 State Route 132 West Project (RACR), dated December 1, 2022 and submitted by Geocon Consultants Inc. (Geocon) on behalf of the Department of Transportation (Caltrans). The RACR describes the consolidation and capping activities that have been completed to date for the barium containing soils (BCS) as part of the Interim Phase 1 Construction effort at the State Route 132 West project in Modesto, California (Site).

As per DTSCs acceptance of the Draft RACR dated February 24, 2022, the soil removal, consolidation, and capping efforts were completed as per the approved Remedial Action Plan (RAP), Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP), and the two variance requests to the RDIP that were approved on April 17, 2020 and September 21, 2020. In addition, the As-Built Plans, cross section figures, final survey data, and final Operation and Maintenance (O&M) Plan were included in this version of the RACR. DTSC has no comments on the RACR and is accepting the document as final.

With this acceptance DTSC will prepare a draft O&M Agreement and submit the document for Caltrans review within 30 days of this letter. A Land Use Covenant (LUC) for the remediated area will also be completed and sent for review, signature and recording with the Stanislaus County Assessor's office.

This acceptance letter and the completion of the tasks mentioned in the preceding paragraph mark the conclusion of the Interim Phase 1 effort. However, final certification of the RAP for this project will not occur until after the Ultimate Phase 2 State Route 132 build-out effort is completed (Ultimate Phase 2). The Ultimate Phase 2 work is anticipated to begin in 8 to 10 years and will include adding additional lanes and completing the final build out of State Route 132. This effort will include a supplemental RDIP for DTSCs review and approval as well as field oversight, an Ultimate Phase 2 RACR, and revisions to the O&M Plan and O&M Agreement if necessary. The Ultimate Phase 2 State Route 132 project will be conducted under a separate agreement between DTSC and Caltrans prior to commencing the work.

DTSC and the RWQCB thank you for the efforts to ensure this site is cleaned up for its intended use. Please contact Mr. Dean Wright of my staff at (916) 255-3591 or via email at [Dean.Wright@dtsc.ca.gov](mailto:Dean.Wright@dtsc.ca.gov) if you have any questions.
