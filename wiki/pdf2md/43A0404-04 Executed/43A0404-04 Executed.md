# I. Task Order Purpose

This Task Order between the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) and the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) is to reimburse costs incurred by DTSC for activities as appropriate to regulate activities at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles – State Route 132/99 (Site) during construction of the Route 132 West Expressway conducted by Caltrans.

# II. Scope of Services

DTSC will provide professional staff services to conduct all activities as needed, in providing regulatory oversight to support activities associated with the project identified above.

The DTSC specific tasks include, but are not limited to, the following:

- A. Review of plans, reports, and technical documents pertaining to mitigation of existing or potential impacts to human health and the environment related to the Site, including:
  - a. Field oversight of Caltrans activities, as needed;
  - b. Document review:
  - c. Participation in meetings and phone calls regarding activities; and,
  - d. Activities to implement and manage this Task Order.

This Task Order may be amended by the Department to address any mutually agreed modifications to, or expansions of, this Scope of Work.

# III. Period of Performance

Work under this Task Order shall begin on June 1, 2020 and terminate on June 30, 2025.

# IV. Schedule

Work items will be implemented as needed and shall progress in such a manner that the work is complete. DTSC shall exercise due diligence to ensure that all activities are conducted in a timely manner.

# V. Reports and Deliverables

- A. DTSC will provide written comments and recommendations based on submittal of data, reports, and other information.
- B. Each billing invoice submitted by DTSC shall include summary of activities of indirect and direct charges and daily logs provided by DTSC to Caltrans for the invoice time period.
- C. There will be no formal progress reports, or other reports, required under this Task Order. Caltrans may request a copy of the daily logs and timesheets of staff who are listed in the quarterly invoice from DTSC.

# VI. Meetings

The DTSC Task Order Manager shall meet with the Caltrans Task Order Manager and Contract Manager as necessary or upon request from Caltrans.

# VII. Cost

- A. DTSC will be paid in accordance with Agreement No. 43A0404 Exhibit B, Item 3 Payment, the DTSC's Cost Proposal referenced as Attachment 1, "Estimated Cost Proposal," to Agreement No. 43A0404 which is incorporated by reference.
- B. In addition, DTSC will be paid for actual direct costs, other than salary costs, that are identified in the attached cost estimate pursuant to exhibit B, item 2, "Budget Contingency Clause," of Agreement No. 43A0404.
- C. The cost for this task order is defined in the Agreement. The total amount payable by the Caltrans under this Task Order shall not exceed \$100,000.00 for the duration of this task order.
- D. Caltrans and DTSC agree (in the interest of avoiding work stoppages) that DTSC shall have the discretion to utilize suitable personnel classifications as shown in attachment 3, "Personnel Classifications and Hourly Rates," of Agreement No. 43A0404 to perform the required tasks provided that the following two conditions are met: 1) the substitution does not result in an exceedance of the total amount payable under part C of section VI, "Cost," of this Task Order and 2) the Caltrans Task Order Manager and the Contract Manager both agree to the proposed substitution. Substitution of this kind, involving the approved class codes shown in attachment 2, "Estimated Cost Proposal," of Agreement No. 43A0404, do not require an amendment to the Task Order.

# VIII. Invoices

Each quarterly billing invoice submitted by DTSC will include brief description of services provided by DTSC to Caltrans for the reporting period.

- Invoice number
- Date
