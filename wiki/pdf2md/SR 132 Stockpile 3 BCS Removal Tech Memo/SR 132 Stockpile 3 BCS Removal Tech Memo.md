Subject: Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Verification Testing

State Route 132 Project, Modesto, California

This Technical Memorandum presents a summary of barium containing soil (BCS) removal verification testing completed at former Stockpile 3 within the State Route 132 right-of-way (see attached plan sheet). Stockpile 3 BCS material located adjacent and east of State Route 99 between Kansas Avenue and the Modesto Irrigation District (MID) water canal was excavated and transported for placement as embankment fill within the designated Stockpile 1 fill containment zone. A total of 3,075 truck-loads of BCS material totaling approximately 38,440 cubic yards (preliminary estimate based on 12.5 cubic yards per truck) was transported to Stockpile 1.

The BCS removal verification testing was performed in general accordance with the January 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) prepared for the State Route 132 Project. The RDIP provides the following verification testing thresholds, and Stockpile 3 excavation target elevation and minimum number of verification soil samples:

## **BCS Removal Verification Testing Removal Thresholds**

Barium - 1,000 milligrams per kilogram (mg/kg, groundwater protection) Lead - 80 mg/kg (residential screening level)

| Excavation Area | Original Ground Surface Elevation (Above Mean Sea Level [MSL]) | Minimum Number of Verification Samples |
|-----------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Stockpile 3     | 84                                                             | 24                                     |

Stockpile 3 was excavated to approximate elevations ranging between 82.5 and 84 feet above MSL. A total of 24 verification soil samples (S3 1-0 through S3 24-0) were collected between February 28 and March 12, 2020 from the base of the excavation. Following receipt of the analytical data indicating elevated barium concentrations above the verification threshold for sample locations S3 9- 0 through S3 11-0, these excavation areas were deepened approximately one foot and resampled. Verification soil samples S3 SE BERM and S3 SW BERM were collected from the top of the berm at the southern end of the Stockpile 3 excavation adjacent to the MID water canal.

![](_page_0_Picture_14.jpeg)

The BCS removal verification soil samples were collected from the base of the removal areas that exposed native soil conditions with the exception of sample location S3 16-0 that was collected from storm drain backfill materials adjacent to State Route 99. The verification soil samples were collected using new stainless-steel sample tubes that were transported to Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) under chain of custody protocol. Each soil sample was analyzed for barium and lead following Environmental Protection Agency Test Method 6010B. The approximate verification soil sample locations are depicted on the attached plan sheet. A summary of the verification soil sample analytical data is on Table 1. Copies of the ATL laboratory reports are attached.

Each of the final excavation soil samples contained barium and lead below the RDIP verification thresholds. Barium concentrations ranged from 46 to 970 mg/kg, and lead concentrations from 1.6 to 40 mg/kg. Based on the data presented herein, the Stockpile 3 BCS removal has met the barium and lead verification thresholds and is suitable for placement of clean embankment fill and other highway improvements without further BCS RDIP monitoring requirements. This information will be included in the Removal Action Completion Report to be submitted following completion of the Stockpile 1 and 2 clean fill capping activities.
