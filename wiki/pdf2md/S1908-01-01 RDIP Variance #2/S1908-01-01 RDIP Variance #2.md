In accordance with your request on behalf of the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), we have prepared this Second Variance to Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP Variance #2) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles located south of the State Route (SR) 99/Kansas Avenue interchange in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. This RDIP Variance #2 has been prepared to address clean cover fill material proposed by the highway contractor to properly cap the upper surface of consolidated barium-containing soil (BCS) within Stockpiles 1 and 2 during the SR 132 West Project.

Section 3.2.2 of the RDIP contains the following text with respect to BCS stockpile clean cover fill materials:

"Placement of minimum 12-inch-thick clean cover fill material over the barium-containing soil and ADL consolidation zones. As an alternative, minimum 4-inch-thick aggregate base (AB) materials may be placed on the upper stockpile surface pending subsequent placement of structural pavement and hardscape."

The intent of the RDIP option for capping the upper surface of the completed BCS and aerially-deposited lead (ADL) fill containment zones of Stockpiles 1 and 2 with 4 inches of aggregate base materials was to preserve fill capacity with the understanding that the structural pavement section and hardscape would provide the ultimate clean cap for the upper surface of the stockpiles. The specified structural pavement section for the Interim Phase I of the SR 132 West Project consists of the following:

0.85 feet (10.2 inches) concrete pavement 0.25 feet (3.0 inches) hot mix asphalt 0.70 feet (8.4 inches) Class 3 aggregate subbase

Areas outside of the highway structural pavement section on the upper surface of the stockpiles will be capped with hardscape.

Review of the gradation summaries indicates that the aggregate subbase materials are primarily comprised of sand and gravel with lesser fines content similar to aggregate base materials. The planned placement of 4 inches of Class 3 aggregate subbase materials on the upper surface of Stockpiles 1 and 2 would provide adequate interim clean cover (equivalent to aggregate base for purposes of cover material) until the remainder of the pavement section/hardscape is placed.

Based on the information presented herein, the use of a minimum of 4 inches of Class 3 aggregate subbase materials as clean cover fill on the upper surface of Stockpiles 1 and 2 meets the intent of the RDIP and would provide adequate clean cover until the remaining pavement section/hardscape is placed.
