The California Department of Transportation (Department) received the California Department of Toxic Substance Control (DTSC) letter dated March 1, 2012 regarding State Route (SR) 132 West Expressway/Freeway (Caltrans Soil Stockpiles), Modesto, Stanislaus County. Your letter requests that Caltrans implement the following actions:

## 1. Limit Access to Caltrans Authorized Personnel

A chain link fence with locking gates currently exists around stockpile #1 and stockpile #2. Although stockpile #3 is fenced and gated along the east, the west side has remained unfenced due to limited access to the stockpile from State Route 99 (controlled access freeway).

In order to limit further access to the stockpiles, the Department will implement the following actions:

- a. Install a chain link fence and locking gates along the west side of stockpile #3. The new fence will connect to the existing fence to establish a perimeter around stockpile #3. The fence is currently under construction and should be completed early next week. "No Trespassing" signs will be posted on the new section of fence.
- b. Inspect the perimeter fencing around each stockpile every two weeks. Following inspection, the on-call fence contractor will be notified and authorized to conduct repairs, which will include before and after photographs. A column entry for all inspections and repairs will be included in a Site Report that will be submitted to the DTSC monthly.
- c. Inspect and remove trash and debris within each stockpile-fenced area quarterly. The monthly Site Report will document trash inspections and removal activities.

## 2. Maintain the Current Grade and Vegetative Cover

The stockpiles currently comprise of ruderal habitat consisting of non-native vegetation dominated by ripgut grass, wild radish, and wild oats (2007 stockpile biological survey). While the vegetation on stockpiles #1 and #3 is thick and well established, vegetation on the southern side slope and top deck of stockpile #2 is somewhat sparse in places. The lack of established vegetation on stockpile #2 is due in part to a past fire (side slope) and the presence of gravel and asphalt areas on the top deck.

There are currently no activities occurring on the stockpiles that would affect existing grade and drainage. Existing grade and drainage will be maintained as they presently exist.

In order to address non vegetated areas on stockpile #2, the Department will consult with its Maintenance and Landscaping divisions to identify the best options for reducing and minimizing bare soil exposure. The Department will present a report describing the options to the DTSC by April 20, 2012.

Quarterly inspection and reporting on activities taken to reduce soil exposure will be noted in the monthly Site Report.

## 3. <u>Maintain the Groundwater Monitoring System</u>

Eight monitoring wells presently exist at the stockpile site. The wells were constructed and secured to ensure the collection of representative groundwater samples on a repeated basis.

On March 8<sup>th</sup>, the total depth of each well was sounded and compared to respective well construction details. The well vaults and locking caps were also inspected. Based on the findings of the inspection, each well was determined to be fully functional.

On March 12<sup>th</sup>, quarterly groundwater monitoring resumed. Samples were collected in accordance with the Groundwater Assessment Work Plan (January 26, 2006) approved by the DTSC. All analytical parameters in the work plan, including the addition of strontium and general minerals, were analyzed. A groundwater monitoring report for the March sampling event will be prepared and submitted to the DTSC and Regional Water Quality Control Board.
