This document is a **Groundwater Monitoring Report** for the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles** site in Stanislaus County, California, prepared by **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** for **Caltrans District 6** and submitted to the **California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)** and the **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)** in August 2014. It reports on the results of groundwater samples collected from ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) on June 4-5, 2014.

**Key Findings:**

- **Exceedances:** While most dissolved metals remained below regulatory thresholds, **Nitrate (as Nitrogen)** exceeded the Primary Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) of 10 mg/L in several wells: MW-1 (15 mg/L), MW-5 (29 mg/L), MW-6 (15 mg/L), MW-9 (10 mg/L), and MW-10 (18 mg/L). Additionally, **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)** exceeded the Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in wells MW-1 (530 mg/L), MW-4 (510 mg/L), MW-5 (680 mg/L), MW-6 (580 mg/L), and MW-10 (620 mg/L).

- **Wells of Note:** Well MW-5 consistently showed the highest concentrations of nitrate and TDS. The data also indicate two distinct groundwater types (calcium-dominant and sodium-dominant), with MW-5 shifting to a sodium-dominant type in 2013, suggesting potential mixing.

- **General Conditions:** Other parameters like barium, strontium, arsenic, and vanadium were detected but remained well below their respective health advisory levels or MCLs. Lead was not detected above practical quantitation limits.

**Data Sources and Quality:**

The primary analytical data was produced by **Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL)** via a Certificate of Analysis dated June 13, 2014. The report also includes Stiff diagram appendices from Geocon. The data has several QA/QC flags, particularly for metals analysis in high-ionic-strength samples (e.g., "M1" for matrix spike issues and "E" for results above quantitation range). Despite these, the data was validated and deemed acceptable by the preparer.

**Regulatory and Operational Context:**

No formal regulatory decisions or remedial actions are documented in this report, which is a standard monitoring submittal for oversight by DTSC and CVRWQCB. The key condition is that quarterly monitoring continues, with the next event scheduled for September 2014. PAH analysis was discontinued with regulatory concurrence, and purge water from the event was containerized for offsite disposal. Geocon Consultants performed the monitoring, with sampling protocols following a 2006 work plan from Shaw Environmental, Inc.
