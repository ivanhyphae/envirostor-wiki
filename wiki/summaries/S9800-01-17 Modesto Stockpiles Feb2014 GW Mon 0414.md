**Combined Summary of "raw/S9800-01-17 Modesto Stockpiles Feb2014 GW Mon 0414.pdf"**

This document reports the results of groundwater monitoring conducted at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California, during February 2014. Ten monitoring wells (MW‑1 through MW‑10) were sampled on February 25–26, 2014, following standard protocols that included water‑level measurement, purging of 2–3 well volumes, and collection with disposable bailers. Depth to groundwater ranged from 33.40 ft (MW‑1) to 42.04 ft (MW‑5), and potentiometric contours indicated a southeast groundwater flow direction with a gradient of 0.0008.

Samples were analyzed by Advanced Technology Laboratories for dissolved metals (EPA 6020/7470A), anions (EPA 300), alkalinity (SM 2320B), total dissolved solids (SM 2540C), total sulfide (SM 4500‑S²⁻), and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (EPA 8270C SIM). Field parameters (temperature, pH, conductivity, dissolved oxygen, turbidity, ORP) were recorded during purging.

**Key findings:**

- **Dissolved metals**: Most metals were below regulatory thresholds. Notable exceptions included manganese in MW‑10 (73 µg/L), which exceeded the secondary maximum contaminant level (SMCL) of 50 µg/L. Barium (48–310 µg/L), strontium (260–970 µg/L), arsenic (≤5.1 µg/L), chromium (≤8.2 µg/L), nickel (≤5.8 µg/L), and vanadium (≤33 µg/L) were all below applicable MCLs or health advisories. Mercury was below detection (0.20 µg/L) in all samples.
- **Nitrate as N**: Concentrations ranged from 2.8 to 34 mg/L. The primary MCL of 10 mg/L was exceeded in MW‑1, MW‑5, MW‑6, and MW‑10.
- **Total dissolved solids (TDS)**: Ranged from 340 to 750 mg/L; the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L was exceeded in MW‑5, MW‑6, and MW‑10.
- **Major ion chemistry**: Two distinct water types persisted—calcium‑dominant in MW‑1,‑2,‑4,‑7,‑9,‑10 and sodium‑dominant in MW‑3,‑5,‑6,‑8. Cation‑anion balances were acceptable, and bicarbonate alkalinity was generally 320–380 mg/L as CaCO₃. Most PAHs and sulfide were below detection limits.

**Quality control**: Although most laboratory control samples (LCS) and blanks were acceptable, several matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate recoveries fell outside acceptance limits (70–130%), attributed to sample matrix interference. These outliers were flagged (M1, M2) and were validated by acceptable LCS performance. A few analytes required dilution due to high concentrations (notes D6, D1). One nitrate duplicate had an RPD exceeding 20%. Overall, the analytical batch was considered valid.

The report also includes long‑term monitoring data (2006‑2014) as context, and notes that some earlier results (e.g., 2007) were reproduced for comparison. No new health‑based exceedances beyond those noted were identified.
