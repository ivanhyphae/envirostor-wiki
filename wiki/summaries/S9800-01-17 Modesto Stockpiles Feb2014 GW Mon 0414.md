The document reports the February 2014 groundwater monitoring event for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California, with additional laboratory certificate-of-analysis details and historical comparison data for monitoring wells MW-1 through MW-10. Sampling was conducted on February 25–26, 2014 after purging each well, with field measurements of water levels and basic water-quality parameters and laboratory analysis by a California-certified lab under chain-of-custody. The analytical suite included dissolved metals, major ions/general minerals, total dissolved solids (TDS), alkalinity, sulfide, mercury, and PAHs (SIM), with QA/QC performed using blanks, duplicates, matrix spikes, and lab control samples. PAHs were generally non-detect and were no longer a focus after prior non-detect trends.

Overall, groundwater quality was mixed but generally within drinking-water thresholds for most dissolved metals, including the site’s primary chemicals of concern identified by DTSC: barium, lead, and strontium. Lead was not detected above practical quantitation limits, while barium and strontium were detected at low to moderate levels but remained below their applicable thresholds. Other metals such as arsenic, chromium, nickel, and vanadium were detected in many samples but also stayed below regulatory limits. The main recurring exceedances were for nitrate as nitrogen and total dissolved solids, with one isolated manganese exceedance; these findings indicate that groundwater quality is impacted primarily by mineralization and nitrate rather than by widespread metal contamination.

Hydrogeologically, groundwater levels ranged from about 33.4 ft below ground surface in MW-1 to 42.0 ft bgs in MW-5, and the groundwater gradient indicated southeast flow at a very low average slope. Chemistry varied by well, with elevated alkalinity, sodium, calcium, magnesium, chloride, sulfate, nitrate, and TDS in several locations. Nitrate was notably elevated in multiple wells, including a strong high in MW-5, and TDS values were commonly above typical background levels. Stiff diagram interpretation showed two dominant water types across the site: calcium-dominant and sodium-dominant groundwater, with some temporal changes and possible mixing near MW-5. Overall, the report concludes that the site groundwater is chemically heterogeneous, moderately mineralized, and affected by persistent nitrate/TDS exceedances, while most metals remain below health-based standards.
