## Key claims
- The Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill area soil has been characterized through sampling and analysis.
- Composite soil sample results are consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations.
- The soil meets the Barium Containing Soil clean cap criteria.
- The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) has formally accepted the Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Technical Memorandum, validating its conclusions.

## Methodology
The sampling and analysis followed the "Carpenter Shoofly Area Clean Fill Sampling Plan" (revised April 5, 2021), which was reviewed and approved by DTSC and the Water Board via email on April 7, 2021. The Plan was described as consistent with previous borrow area sampling efforts. Soil samples (composite sampling) were collected from the Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill area. The specific number of samples, analytical methods, and laboratory procedures are not detailed in the email chain. Results were compiled in a Technical Memorandum dated April 12, 2021, which was then reviewed by DTSC and the Water Board.

## Results
- Composite soil sample results were found to be consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations.
- The soil meets the "Barium Containing Soil clean cap criteria," a site-specific standard for clean fill. No specific numerical concentration values (e.g., mg/kg of barium) are provided in the email.

## Limitations
- This is a regulatory acceptance email, not a full research paper. Therefore, no limitations are explicitly stated by the authors. Inherent limitations not addressed include: lack of raw data (sample counts, detection limits, specific analytical results), and the document does not discuss potential variability within the fill area or long-term monitoring requirements.
