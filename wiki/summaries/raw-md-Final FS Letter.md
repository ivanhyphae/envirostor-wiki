## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose**: DTSC approval letter for the “Final Feasibility Study, Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route 132, West Freeway/Expressway Project, Stanislaus County, California.” The letter communicates DTSC’s concurrence with the study after addressing prior comments.
- **Author or preparer**: Randy S. Adams, C.E.G., Senior Engineering Geologist, Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program, Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).
- **Date or period covered**: Letter dated June 30, 2014. The Final Feasibility Study (Final FS) is dated June 24, 2014; the preceding Draft Final FS is dated December 18, 2013, with DTSC comments issued February 11, 2014.

## Key claims
- The Final FS “addresses DTSC’s comments for the Draft Final Feasibility Study” (DTSC comments dated February 11, 2014 on the December 18, 2013 Draft Final FS).
- “DTSC concurs with the Final Feasibility Study.”

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision**: DTSC, in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), concurs with (i.e., approves) the Final Feasibility Study.
- **Agency**: DTSC (Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program) with Central Valley RWQCB as co‑regulator.
- **Date of decision**: June 30, 2014.
- **Conditions imposed**: None stated in this letter; the concurrence implies the FS is acceptable as a basis for future remedial decisions.
