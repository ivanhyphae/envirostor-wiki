Here is a structured summary of the provided technical memorandum.

## Key claims
The memorandum concludes that soil from the proposed clean cap borrow area is suitable for use as cover for barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles on the State Route 132 Project. This conclusion is based on the finding that the concentrations of Title 22 metals in the soil samples are consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations in the area. Specifically, antimony, cadmium, selenium, thallium, and mercury were not detected above laboratory reporting limits, and organochlorine pesticides (OCPs) were not detected in the 0–2 foot interval samples.

## Methodology
- **Site:** A proposed clean cap borrow area adjacent to Carpenter Road within the State Route 132 right-of-way in Modesto, California. The area is approximately 1,250 feet long, 200 feet wide, with an excavation depth of 5 feet.
- **Sampling:** 12 direct-push borings (B1 through B12) were completed on a grid pattern on December 21, 2020. Continuous core soil samples were collected from each boring at two depth intervals: 0 to 2 feet and 2 to 5 feet.
- **Sample Preparation:** Samples from each depth interval were composited into four 3-part composites per depth interval (e.g., B1+B2+B3, B4+B5+B6, B7+B8+B9, B10+B11+B12).
- **Analytical Methods:** All eight composite samples were analyzed for Title 22 metals using EPA Test Methods 6010B (ICP-AES) and 6461A/7471A (Cold Vapor AA for mercury). The four composite samples from the 0–2 foot depth interval were also analyzed for organochlorine pesticides (OCPs) using EPA Test Method 8081A.
- **Benchmarking:** Analytical results were compared to site-specific background concentrations from a 2007 Shaw background data summary.

## Results
- **Metals Concentrations (mg/kg):** Reported concentration ranges for Title 22 metals in the composite samples were as follows, with background values in parentheses:
    - Arsenic: 1.0 to 1.5 (1.2)
    - Barium: 60 to 110 (72.8)
    - Beryllium: 1.3 to 1.6 (0.2)
    - Chromium: 7.5 to 11 (7.1)
    - Cobalt: 3.2 to 4.9 (4.4)
    - Copper: 5.3 to 9.9 (7.5)
    - Lead: 1.7 to 7.0 (2.0)
    - Nickel: 5.6 to 8.2 (5.3)
    - Silver: 1.6 to 2.5 (0.2)
    - Vanadium: 16 to 25 (31.3)
    - Zinc: 21 to 41 (26.3)
- **Non-Detects:** Antimony, cadmium, selenium, thallium, and mercury were not detected in any sample above their respective Practical Quantitation Limits (PQLs). All OCPs analyzed were not detected above their PQLs in the 0-2 foot samples.

## Limitations
- The background data used for comparison is from a 2007 study.
- The sampling strategy was based on a DTSC-advisory document, not a formal regulatory standard.
- This memo is part of a larger project (Removal Action Completion Report) to be submitted later, meaning these are interim, though definitive, findings for this specific characterization.
- The report is a technical memorandum, not a peer-reviewed paper; its conclusions are for a specific, practical engineering project.
