The memo concludes that the proposed clean borrow area in the SR 132 right-of-way adjacent to Carpenter Road is suitable as a source of clean cover fill for capping BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2. To evaluate the area, 12 direct-push borings were advanced on a grid across the planned borrow footprint and sampled from 0–2 ft and 2–5 ft depths. The samples were chilled, submitted under chain of custody to Advanced Technology Laboratories, and analyzed as depth-discrete composites for Title 22 metals, mercury, and organochlorine pesticides using EPA Methods 6010B, 7471A, and 8081A, respectively. Results were compared to site-specific background data from 2007.

Overall, the analytical data indicate naturally occurring/background soil chemistry rather than contamination. Title 22 metals were detected at low mg/kg levels and were broadly consistent with background concentrations. Representative metal results across the composites included arsenic around 1.0–1.5 mg/kg, barium 60–110 mg/kg, chromium 7.5–11 mg/kg, cobalt 3.2–4.9 mg/kg, copper 5.3–9.9 mg/kg, lead 1.7–7.0 mg/kg, nickel 5.6–8.2 mg/kg, vanadium 16–25 mg/kg, and zinc 21–41 mg/kg, with antimony, cadmium, molybdenum, selenium, and thallium reported as nondetect in the excerpted results. Mercury was not detected at the method reporting limit of 0.10 mg/kg. Organochlorine pesticides were also not detected in any analyzed composites, including compounds such as DDT/DDE/DDD, aldrin, chlordane isomers, dieldrin, endosulfans, endrin compounds, heptachlor, methoxychlor, and toxaphene.

The memo also notes that field observations did not show signs of contamination, such as staining, odors, or debris, during sampling. Quality-control results generally supported the validity of the analytical batches, even where some matrix spike recoveries were outside acceptance limits, because associated laboratory control samples met criteria. Together, the field observations and laboratory results support the conclusion that the borrow area is suitable for clean fill extraction.
