The technical memorandum evaluates a proposed clean cap borrow area on State Route 132, adjacent to Carpenter Road. This area, measuring approximately 1,250 ft × 200 ft × 5 ft deep, is intended to provide clean cover fill material for capping consolidated barium-containing soil (BCS) in Stockpiles 1 and 2.

To characterize the soil, twelve direct-push borings were collected on a grid pattern, with samples composited by depth (0–2 ft and 2–5 ft) to create eight total composite samples. The samples were analyzed for Title 22 metals (using EPA Method 6010B) and organochlorine pesticides (using EPA Method 8081A on the shallow composites). Quality control was performed in accordance with DTSC guidelines and included laboratory control samples, matrix spikes, and duplicates.

Results showed the soil is a brown to tan, damp, clayey to fine sandy silt with no field indicators of contamination. All tested organochlorine pesticides were non-detect. For metals, concentrations were low and generally consistent with site-specific background values from a 2007 study (e.g., arsenic 1.0–1.5 mg/kg, barium 60–110 mg/kg, chromium 7.5–11 mg/kg). Antimony, cadmium, selenium, thallium, and mercury were all non-detect. While laboratory control sample recoveries were acceptable, one matrix spike recovery for pesticides was outside the acceptance window, suggesting possible sample matrix interference Kendrick Clean Cap.

Key limitations of the study include the single characterization event and the use of compositing, which may obscure localized variability. Pesticide analysis was limited to shallow samples, and the background data used for comparison was from 2007 without recent re-verification. Overall, the analytical data support the claim that the borrow area soil is suitable for use as clean cap material.
