The February 2014 groundwater monitoring report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County summarizes sampling and laboratory results from ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10), along with supporting well-construction and QA/QC information. Field sampling was conducted on February 25–26, 2014, after purging roughly 1.5 to 3.5 well volumes from each well and recording depth to groundwater and key field parameters such as pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, dissolved oxygen, and ORP. Samples were analyzed by ATL/Advanced Technology Laboratories using standard EPA and SM methods for dissolved metals, mercury, major ions, alkalinity, sulfide, total dissolved solids, and related geochemical indicators; PAHs were included in the broader project history but were not analyzed in this event and had not been detected in prior analyses, leading to discontinuation of that parameter after November 2012.

Overall, groundwater conditions were consistent with earlier monitoring: groundwater flowed to the southeast under a low hydraulic gradient, and the chemistry showed clear spatial variability across the site. The data indicate two recurring groundwater facies, one calcium-dominant and one sodium-dominant, as shown by Stiff diagrams and ion balance results, suggesting limited mixing and heterogeneous geochemical conditions. Major ions and dissolved solids were elevated in several wells, with TDS and alkalinity varying notably among locations. Nitrate and TDS were persistent concerns in some wells, with nitrate particularly elevated in wells such as MW-5, MW-6, MW-10, and MW-1; because MW-1 is upgradient, its nitrate may reflect background conditions rather than site impact.

Most dissolved metals were below applicable drinking-water thresholds or advisory values, though some metals were present at measurable concentrations across the site. Barium and strontium were detected in many wells, but generally at levels below concern; the one notable exception was manganese, which exceeded the relevant benchmark in MW-10. Mercury was not detected in the reported samples. The report concludes that groundwater quality remains influenced by elevated mineral content and nitrate in portions of the site, but does not show evidence of a new PAH issue, and the overall hydrogeologic and geochemical patterns remain broadly consistent with historical monitoring.
