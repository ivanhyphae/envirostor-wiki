The groundwater monitoring report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (Stanislaus County, California) presents the results of a single sampling event conducted on February 25–26, 2014, in accordance with a DTSC-approved work plan. The primary chemicals of concern are barium, lead, and strontium, though the analysis covered a broader suite of dissolved metals, major cations and anions, total dissolved solids (TDS), alkalinity, sulfide, and dissolved mercury.

Samples were collected from ten existing monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) using disposable bailers after purging; dissolved metals were field-filtered (0.45-micron). Laboratory analysis was performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (Signal Hill, CA) using EPA Method 6020 (ICP-MS) for dissolved metals, EPA 7470A for mercury, EPA 300.0 for anions, SM 2320B for alkalinity, SM 2540C for TDS, and SM 4500-S²⁻ D for sulfide. Quality control included method blanks, laboratory control samples, matrix spikes, and duplicates.

**Hydrogeology:** Groundwater depth ranged from 33.40 (MW-1) to 42.04 feet (MW-5) below top of casing, with flow directed southeast at an average gradient of 0.0008. The aquifer lithology is laterally discontinuous.

**Dissolved metals (February 2014):** Barium (48–310 µg/L) and strontium (260–970 µg/L) were detected in all wells but remained well below their respective thresholds (1,000/700 µg/L for barium; 4,000 µg/L for strontium). Lead was not detected above its practical quantitation limit (PQL). Other detected metals included vanadium (18–33 µg/L), arsenic (1.9–5.1 µg/L), chromium (3.4–8.2 µg/L), nickel (1.5–5.8 µg/L), and molybdenum (0.61–4.8 µg/L). Manganese exceeded the Secondary MCL of 50 µg/L only in MW-10 (73 µg/L). Antimony, beryllium, cadmium, silver, thallium, zinc, selenium, and mercury were consistently reported as not detected or below their PQLs.

**General minerals:** Nitrate as nitrogen ranged from 2.8 to 34 mg/L and exceeded the Primary MCL of 10 mg/L in four wells: MW-1 (10), MW-5 (34), MW-6 (17), and MW-10 (15). TDS (340–750 mg/L) exceeded the Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in MW-5 (750), MW-6 (570), and MW-10 (630). Sulfide was not detected. Major cations (calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium) were elevated, with sodium reaching 120,000 µg/L in MW-5 and MW-6. Bicarbonate was the dominant anion.

**Groundwater classification:** Stiff diagrams reveal two water types—calcium-dominant (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-7, MW-9, MW-10) and sodium-dominant (MW-3, MW-5, MW-6, MW-8). A shift in type was noted for MW-5 in 2013.

**Limitations:** This report summarizes a single event and does not assess long-term trends. Several matrix spike recoveries fell outside control limits (attributed to matrix interference), though the batches were validated by laboratory control samples. One nitrate duplicate RPD (63.5%) exceeded acceptance criteria. MW-1, the upgradient well, had a nitrate concentration of 10 mg/L, which may reflect background conditions rather than stockpile impact. The data were considered acceptable for the reporting purpose.
