Based on the eight section summaries, the source document is a groundwater monitoring report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, covering data from 2006 to 2014 with a focus on February 2014. The study involves 10 monitoring wells (MW‑1 through MW‑10) and includes measurements of groundwater elevation, flow direction, and analytical results for dissolved metals, general minerals, and PAHs.

**Methodology**  
Groundwater elevations were measured using well casing elevations (feet MSL) and depth to water. Wells were purged (2–3 well volumes) using a submersible pump or bailer, and field parameters (pH, EC, temperature, turbidity, DO, ORP) were recorded. Samples were collected with disposable bailers; dissolved metals were filtered to 0.45 µm. Analysis followed Title 22 methods: dissolved metals by EPA 6020 (ICP‑MS) and EPA 7470A (cold‑vapor AA for mercury); general minerals (chloride, nitrate, sulfate) by EPA 300.0; alkalinity by SM 2320B; TDS by SM 2540C; sulfide by SM 4500‑S²⁻; and PAHs by EPA 8270C SIM. Quality control included method blanks, LCS, duplicates, and matrix spikes. Some matrix spike recoveries fell outside control limits (e.g., sulfide matrix spike recovery 8.5%), but sample results were validated by LCS, so no data qualification was necessary. Stiff diagrams were constructed from major cation and anion data.

**Results**  
Groundwater flow direction is southeast at an average gradient of 0.0008. Stiff diagrams show bicarbonate dominance: wells MW‑1, 2, 4, 7, 9, and 10 are calcium‑dominant; MW‑3, 5, 6, and 8 are sodium‑dominant.

Dissolved metals were all below primary MCLs, except manganese which exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L in MW‑10 (73 µg/L) and in MW‑5 (88 µg/L on 7/17/2012). Barium (48–310 µg/L) and strontium (260–970 µg/L) remained below their respective thresholds (1,000/700 µg/L and 4,000 µg/L). Other metals (arsenic, chromium, nickel, vanadium) were below MCLs or notification levels. Mercury was not detected.

General minerals: nitrate as N ranged from 2.8 to 34 mg/L, exceeding the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in wells MW‑1, MW‑5, MW‑6, and MW‑10. TDS ranged from 340 to 750 mg/L, exceeding the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in MW‑5, MW‑6, and MW‑10. Other general minerals (chloride, sulfate, alkalinity) varied spatially and temporally but were mostly below applicable limits. PAHs (SIM) were below detection limits (<0.20 µg/L) in all samples.

**Limitations**  
Data prior to March 2012 are reproduced from a previous Shaw Environmental report. Some wells (MW‑5, MW‑6, MW‑10) went dry during purging. Not all parameters were analyzed for every date. Several matrix spike recoveries were outside control limits, but the laboratory control samples validated the results, so no qualification was required.

**Key Conclusions**  
Groundwater quality at the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site shows exceedances of the primary MCL for nitrate in four wells and of secondary MCLs for TDS and manganese in three wells and one well, respectively. All other monitored constituents (trace metals, PAHs) remained below regulatory thresholds. The data provide a long‑term (2006–2014) baseline for assessing groundwater conditions beneath the soil stockpiles.
