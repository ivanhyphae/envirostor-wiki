## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, southerly of the intersection of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue, Stanislaus County, California. The site includes Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3, which consist of barium-contaminated fill material excavated during SR 99 construction (circa 1961). The stockpiles originated from soil removed from former FMC property containing an evaporation pond used by a predecessor barium-processing facility.
- **Media addressed:** Stormwater (surface water)
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** Sample locations PL1 (west side of North Emerald Avenue, between Stockpiles 1 and 2), PL2 (east side of North Emerald Avenue, between Stockpiles 1 and 2), PL3 (south of Stockpile 2, north terminus of Bennett Street, outside Caltrans right-of-way), PL4 (drainage basin outfall adjacent to Stockpile 3), PL5 (within 3 ft of gate valve in drainage basin south of Stockpile 3), BG1 (drop inlet on Loletta Avenue south of Stockpile 2), BG2 (North Emerald Avenue north of Stockpiles 1 and 2 – dry, not sampled).

## Key claims
- **Claim 1:** No stormwater runoff was observed migrating offsite; stormwater falling on the stockpiles appeared to be retained within the boundary of the Site.
    - **Maker:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (preparer)
    - **Support:** Supported by field observations and site reconnaissance conducted during the sampling event.
- **Claim 2:** The interceptor berm at the base of the gravel-covered south side access ramp to Stockpile 2 functioned as designed, directing stormwater runoff to an area adjacent to the ramp where it remained within the Stockpile 2 boundary.
    - **Maker:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
    - **Support:** Supported by field observations.
- **Claim 3:** Only one exceedance of a primary or secondary MCL was identified: manganese in sample PL2 at 60 µg/l exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/l. All other dissolved metals and general minerals were below respective MCLs.
    - **Maker:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
    - **Support:** Supported by laboratory analytical results (dissolved metals by EPA 6020/7470A; general minerals by EPA 300.0, SM 2320B, 2540C, 2540D, 4500-S-D). Data are presented in Tables 2 and 3.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units:**
    - **Dissolved metals (µg/l) in stormwater:** antimony, arsenic, barium, beryllium, cadmium, calcium, chromium, cobalt, copper, lead, magnesium, manganese, mercury, molybdenum, nickel, potassium, selenium, silver, sodium, strontium, thallium, vanadium, zinc.
        - Barium: PL1 = 24; PL2 = 35; PL3 = 210; PL4 = 59; PL5 = 65; BG1 = 53.
        - Manganese: PL2 = 60; all other sample locations reported ND or <10.
        - Calcium: PL1 = 19,000; PL2 = 12,000; PL3 = 20,000; PL4 = 8,100; PL5 = 7,400; BG1 = 9,600 (all in µg/l).
        - All other dissolved metals were reported at concentrations below their respective primary or secondary MCLs.
    - **General minerals (mg/l) in stormwater:** chloride (0.24–3.6), nitrate as N (0.15–0.85), sulfate (2.6–17), total alkalinity as CaCO₃ (25–56), total dissolved solids (61–130), total suspended solids (9–67), sulfide (ND–0.024).
        - None exceeded respective MCLs.
- **Applicable thresholds:** California primary and secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for drinking water (SWRCB, September 23, 2015). MCLs listed: antimony 6 µg/l; arsenic 10 µg/l; barium 1,000 µg/l; beryllium 4 µg/l; cadmium 5 µg/l; chromium 50 µg/l; copper 1,300 µg/l (secondary); lead 15 µg/l; manganese 50 µg/l (secondary); mercury 2 µg/l; selenium 50 µg/l; thallium 2 µg/l; zinc 5,000 µg/l (secondary); chloride 250 mg/l (secondary); sulfate 250 mg/l (secondary); TDS 500 mg/l (secondary).
- **Wells or locations exceeding thresholds:** Sample PL2 (manganese at 60 µg/l exceeds secondary MCL of 50 µg/l by 10 µg/l).
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations:**
    - All samples were outside holding time for nitrate analysis (collected Saturday).
    - Samples PL1–PL5 and BG1 required dilution due to high concentration of target analytes (calcium; PL3 also diluted for barium and potassium).
    - Matrix spike recoveries for calcium, magnesium, nitrate, and sulfate were outside acceptance limits. The laboratory stated the analytical batch was validated by the laboratory control sample.
    - Other QC notes: matrix spike recovery for vanadium in one MS was 170% (outside limits); the batch was validated by LCS. Some duplicates showed elevated RPDs for manganese, zinc, and strontium associated with target concentrations above the quantitation range.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** No active remedial construction was reported. Existing stormwater management measures were described:
    - Maintaining vegetative/fiber cover and straw wattles on the western slope of Stockpile 2 to intercept and redirect surface water flow to areas within Caltrans right-of-way.
    - The interceptor berm at the base of the south side access ramp to Stockpile 2 (north end of Bennett Street) was observed to be functioning as designed.
- **Phase status:** Ongoing management; Caltrans plans to conduct additional surface water sampling during qualifying rain events in the future.
