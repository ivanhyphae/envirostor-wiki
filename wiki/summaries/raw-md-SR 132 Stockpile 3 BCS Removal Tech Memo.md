# Combined Summary of SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo

## Document Identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Technical memorandum summarizing barium-containing soil (BCS) removal and verification testing at former Stockpile 3, SR 132 Project, Modesto, CA. Includes supporting laboratory analytical reports (EPA 6010B/3050B for barium and lead) and chain-of-custody records.
- **Author/preparer:** John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG, Project Manager, Geocon Consultants, Inc. Laboratory reports prepared by Dr. Reza Karimi, Laboratory Director, Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL).
- **Date/period covered:** March 13, 2020 (tech memo); field work February 28 – March 12, 2020; laboratory analyses March 5–13, 2020.

## Key Findings
- **Stockpile 3 BCS removal met RDIP verification thresholds for barium (<1,000 mg/kg) and lead (<80 mg/kg).** The site is suitable for use as clean embankment fill without further RDIP monitoring.
- **Initial exceedances** in verification samples S3-9, S3-10, and S3-11 (barium concentrations 7,000, 5,100, and 2,200 mg/kg, respectively) required re‑excavation. Post‑re‑excavation samples (S3 9A-0, 10A-0, 11A-0) and all other verification samples passed thresholds.
- **Lead concentrations** in all samples remained well below the 80 mg/kg threshold (range 1.6–40 mg/kg).

## Analytical Results (Consolidated)
- **Contaminants:** Barium and lead in soil; methods EPA 6010B (ICP-AES) with EPA 3050B preparation.
- **Barium:** Overall range after re‑excavation: 46–970 mg/kg (threshold 1,000 mg/kg). Individual sample ranges from lab reports support this (e.g., S3 7-0: 290 mg/kg; S3 16-0: 970 mg/kg; S3 22-0 through 24-0: 140–180 mg/kg). Initial exceedances in S3 9-0 (7,000 mg/kg), S3 10-0 (5,100 mg/kg), and S3 11-0 (2,200 mg/kg) were resolved by re‑excavation.
- **Lead:** Overall range 1.6–40 mg/kg; lab report sub‑ranges (e.g., 1.6–21 mg/kg) confirm no exceedances.
- **Quality control:** Most matrix spike/LCS recoveries acceptable; noted matrix interference for barium in one sample (S3 7-0) and minor spike outlier in late samples (S3 22-0 through 24-0) attributed to matrix effects.

## Remedial Actions
- **Excavation:** Approximately 38,440 cubic yards (3,075 truckloads) of BCS removed from Stockpile 3 and placed as embankment fill in Stockpile 1.
- **Re‑excavation:** At three locations where initial verification failed, the excavation was deepened ~1 foot; subsequent re‑samples passed.
- **Future steps:** Results to be incorporated into a Removal Action Completion Report after capping of Stockpiles 1 and 2.

## Supporting Documentation
- **Laboratory reports** (ATL, March 5–13, 2020) detail results for 24 verification samples (S3 7‑0 through S3 24‑0), including initial exceedances and re‑excavation samples (S3 9A-0, 10A-0, 11A-0, and later S3 16‑0 through 24‑0). Practical quantitation limits (PQL) generally 1.0 mg/kg for both analytes.
- **Chain‑of‑custody records** document sample handling, requested analyses (metals by EPA 6010/7000, volatiles by EPA 8260/624), holding times (45 days solid/liquid), and sample condition (chilled, intact, preserved).
