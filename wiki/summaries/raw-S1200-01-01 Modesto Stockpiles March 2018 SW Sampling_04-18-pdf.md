**Document Identity & Scope**: This document comprises a Surface Water Sampling Report prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. for the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch (Mr. Richard Stewart), dated April 24, 2018, covering a March 22, 2018 sampling event (Project No. S1200-01-01). Attached as an integral part of the report is a Certificate of Analysis from ATL Global (Signal Hill, CA), reported April 2, 2018, for the same project. The site is the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (south of SR 99 & Kansas Avenue, Stanislaus County) – Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3 containing barium-contaminated fill. The media addressed is surface water (stormwater) from seven locations: PL1, PL2, PL3, PL4, PL5 (not sampled), BG1, and BG2 (not sampled). Sampling was conducted in accordance with previously approved protocols.

**Analytical Results**: Samples were analyzed for dissolved metals (22 analytes), chloride, nitrate, sulfate, sulfide, total/dissolved solids, and alkalinity. Key findings include:
- **Lead**: An isolated exceedance of the primary MCL (15 µg/L) was detected at PL3 (46 µg/L), outside Caltrans right-of-way.
- **Manganese**: Exceeded the secondary MCL (50 µg/L) at PL2 (130 µg/L), PL3 (370 µg/L), PL4 (64 µg/L), and BG1 (86 µg/L). The PL4 result is detailed in the lab report (54 µg/L, J-estimated? – note slight discrepancy between summary value 64 and lab report 54; likely either a rounding or the lab report is the precise value for the sample).
- **Barium**: All results were below the MCL of 1,000 µg/L (e.g., PL3 = 770 µg/L, PL4 = 78 µg/L).
- **Non-detects**: Beryllium, cadmium, mercury, selenium, silver, thallium were not detected above reporting limits.
- **General minerals**: All within typical historical ranges; chloride 2.0–3.4 mg/L, sulfate 4.9–11 mg/L, TDS 85–140 mg/L, TSS 2.0–740 mg/L.
- **QA/QC Flags**: The laboratory noted dilutions (D2, D6) due to high analyte concentrations. Several results (e.g., antimony, chromium, cobalt, nickel in PL4) were J-flagged as estimated values. Matrix spike recoveries for some metals (Ca, Cu, Mg, K, Na, V) exceeded limits, though the analytical batch was validated by laboratory control samples. Overall, Geocon determined no data quality limitations affecting interpretation.

**Key Claims**: Geocon concludes that stormwater runoff from the stockpiles remains within Caltrans right-of-way, with no offsite migration observed. The interceptor berm at Stockpile 2 functions as designed. Except for lead at PL3, results are consistent with historical data since 2013.

**Regulatory & Remedial Actions**: No formal regulatory decisions or physical remedial actions (excavation, capping) are reported. The report is submitted for agency review (DTSC, CVRWQCB). Current management includes maintaining vegetative cover, straw wattles, and the interceptor berm. Caltrans plans additional surface water sampling during qualifying rain events in 2018.

**Responsible Parties**: Caltrans (owner/operator), Geocon Consultants (consultant), ATL Global (analytical lab), DTSC and CVRWQCB (regulators), and historical parties (FMC/Barium Products, Shaw Environmental).
