# Document Summary

## Document Identity
- **Document type**: Approval letter for Remedial Action Plan (RAP)
- **Preparer/author organization**: Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), Site Evaluation and Remediation Unit, San Joaquin Branch, Sacramento Office, Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program
- **Recipient/addressee**: Ms. Grace Magsayo, P.E., Project Manager, California Department of Transportation (Caltrans), District 10, Stockton, California
- **Date**: March 14, 2018
- **Project number or task order**: Not stated

## Site and Scope
- **Site name**: Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project
- **Location**: Segment south of Kansas Avenue between Carpenter Avenue and just east of State Route 99, Modesto, Stanislaus County, California
- **Media addressed**: Soil (stockpiles), groundwater (monitoring referenced in future OMP), surface water (infiltration minimization referenced)
- **Specific sample locations or work areas**: Stockpile areas within the SR 132 corridor segment described above

## Key Claims
- **Claim**: The selected remedy (Alternative 4, Containment) will not have a significant effect on the environment with implementation of project controls (mitigation measures).
  - **Who is making it**: DTSC
  - **Supported by data in this document**: Supported by reference to DTSC independent analysis and Caltrans’ Final EIR/EA; no numerical data presented in this letter
- **Claim**: The RAP meets the requirements of the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA).
  - **Who is making it**: DTSC
  - **Supported by data in this document**: Supported by reference to Caltrans’ EIR/EA, Finding of No Significant Impact, and Notice of Determination
- **Claim**: Alternative 4 achieves long-term protection of human health and environment by eliminating exposure pathway to human receptors and minimizing infiltration of surface water into groundwater under the stockpiles.
  - **Who is making it**: DTSC
  - **Supported by data in this document**: Stated as basis for approval; no site-specific data presented

## Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision type**: Approval of Remedial Action Plan (RAP)
- **Deciding agency**: DTSC
- **Date of decision**: March 14, 2018
- **Conditions or requirements imposed**:
  - Draft Final RAP must be modified to read “Final RAP” and include updated information reflecting the Final RAP and Final EIR where applicable
  - Final RAP must be placed in local repositories for public viewing
  - Hard copy and electronic copy of Final RAP sent to DTSC and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB)
  - Caltrans to submit a Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP) for each phase for DTSC review and approval
  - “Start Work Notice” in English and Spanish to be prepared by DTSC and published in local newspaper(s) for each phase, 2 weeks prior to start of construction
  - Caltrans to prepare a Remedial Action Completion Report (RACR) for DTSC review and approval for each phase
  - Caltrans to enter into an Operation and Maintenance Agreement (OMA) with DTSC and prepare an Operation and Maintenance Plan (OMP) for DTSC review and approval
  - OMP to require annual inspection of pavement and other containment remedy features, plus groundwater monitoring
  - Land use covenant and soil management plan required
  - OMA and OMP may need modification following ultimate build-out phase
  - DTSC to evaluate containment remedy every five years after certification
- **Documents approved or acted on**: Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project (Alternative 4, Containment)

  - Remedy certification: Following implementation of ultimate build-out phase
