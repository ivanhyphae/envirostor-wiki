### Combined Summary

This document is a laboratory report for groundwater samples collected from ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) at the Modesto Stockpiles site in September 2012. The analysis characterizes the site's water chemistry, including major ions, trace metals, total dissolved solids (TDS), and organic compounds.

**Key Findings:**
- **Groundwater Flow and Chemistry:** Groundwater flows consistently toward the southeast at a low hydraulic gradient (0.0008 ft/ft). Stiff diagrams reveal two distinct groundwater types: calcium-dominant in most wells and sodium-dominant in wells MW-3, MW-6, and MW-8.
- **Contaminants of Concern:**
    - **No PAHs were detected** in any sample at concentrations above practical quantitation limits (PQLs).
    - Most dissolved metals (barium, lead, arsenic, chromium, vanadium, etc.) were below their respective regulatory thresholds. However, **manganese exceeded thresholds in wells MW-3 and MW-9**.
    - **Nitrate (as N) exceeded the California primary MCL (10 mg/L)** in five wells (MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, MW-9, and MW-10).
    - **TDS exceeded the secondary MCL (500 mg/L)** in three wells (MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10).
- **Background Conditions:** Upgradient well MW-1 showed elevated nitrate (12 mg/L) and TDS (460 mg/L), suggesting these constituents may originate from natural background levels in the shallow aquifer.

**Methodology:** Samples were collected on September 19-20, 2012, using standard purging and sampling techniques. Analyses were performed by an ELAP-certified laboratory (Advanced Technology Laboratories) using EPA methods (e.g., EPA 6020 for metals, EPA 300.0 for anions, EPA 8270-SIM for PAHs) and Standard Methods (e.g., SM 2540C for TDS). Quality assurance included equipment blanks and laboratory control samples, though some matrix spike recoveries were outside control limits due to matrix interference.

**Limitations:**
- This is a single sampling event (snapshot), and long-term trends are assessed by comparison to earlier events.
- Wells MW-5 and MW-6 went dry during purging; samples were collected after recovery, which may affect representativeness.
- Matrix interference affected some metal analyses, though data was validated by laboratory control samples.
- The report is an analytical data package and does not provide geological interpretation or risk assessment.
