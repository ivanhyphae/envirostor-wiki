## Final Summary

The September 2012 groundwater monitoring event at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site sampled ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) to assess water quality relative to California drinking water standards. Groundwater flow direction remained toward the southeast at an average gradient of 0.0008 ft/ft, consistent with historical data, with depth to water ranging from 32.73 to 41.19 feet.

**Key Contaminants Exceeding Thresholds:**
- **Nitrate (as N)** exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in five wells (MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, MW-9, and MW-10), with concentrations ranging from 3.0 to 22 mg/L.
- **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)** exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10 (up to 630 mg/L).
- **Manganese** exceeded its threshold in MW-3 and MW-9 (up to 5.6 µg/L in MW-6).

**Metals and PAHs:**
- Barium (47–280 µg/L), lead (all <1.0 µg/L), and strontium (220–1,100 µg/L) remained below water quality thresholds and consistent with historical levels.
- Other dissolved metals including arsenic, chromium, selenium, vanadium, and major cations (calcium, magnesium, sodium) were detected at low concentrations.
- No polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) were detected above practical quantitation limits in any sample.

**Methodology:** Wells were purged using submersible pumps and sampled with disposable bailers. Samples were analyzed by Advanced Technology Laboratories for Title 22 dissolved metals (EPA 6020/7470), general minerals (EPA 300.0, SM 2540C, SM 2320B), and PAHs (EPA 8270-SIM). Results were compared against California MCLs and secondary MCLs.

**Quality Control Issues:** Several matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate recoveries fell outside acceptance limits (70-130%), attributed to matrix interference from high native concentrations of certain analytes (e.g., barium, calcium, strontium). However, laboratory control samples (LCS) met acceptance criteria, and method blanks showed minimal contamination. Two wells (MW-5 and MW-6) went dry during purging and required recovery before sampling.

**Limitations:** Results represent a single sampling event and may not capture long-term trends. Some duplicate RPD values exceeded 20%, and several analytes required data qualification due to matrix effects and method blank detections.
