## Key claims
- Proposes a sampling plan to characterize a proposed clean fill source at SR 132 Basin 5.
- The source is assumed to be geologically contiguous with an adjacent roadway cut in native formation.
- Plan targets a 10‑foot cut depth with an estimated volume of ~20,000 cubic yards.
- Six test pit locations and six total composite soil samples are specified to assess suitability as clean fill.
- Contaminants of concern are limited to pesticides (0–2 ft depth) and metals (all depths).

## Methodology
- **Sampling design:** Six test pit locations. Two depth‑discrete, three‑part composite samples per pit? The document states “2 depth discrete 3-part composite samples” and “6 total composite soil samples,” implying each of the six test pits yields one composite (possibly across depths), but the depth intervals are: 0–2 ft (pesticides + metals), 2–5 ft (metals only), 5–10 ft (metals only).
- **Analytical methods:** Pesticides by EPA 8081A; metals by EPA 6010B (e.g., ICP‑AES) and 7471A (mercury).
- **Turnaround:** 48‑hour laboratory reporting time.

## Results
- No quantitative results are presented; this is a sampling plan, not a completed study. The document only describes the planned sampling design and analytical scope.

## Limitations
- The plan relies on the assumption that the basin material matches the adjacent roadway cut native formation; actual heterogeneity may not be captured by six test pits.
- Only pesticides and a limited set of metals are tested; other potential contaminants (e.g., volatile organics, semi‑volatiles, PCBs) are not included.
- Composite sampling may mask localized contamination hot spots.
