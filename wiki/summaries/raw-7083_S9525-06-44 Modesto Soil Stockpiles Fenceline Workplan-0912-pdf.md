## Site and Scope

- **Site name and location**: Modesto Soil Stockpiles, located south of the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue, Stanislaus County, California. The site comprises three stockpile areas (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) within Caltrans right-of-way (ROW) west and east of SR 99.
- **Areas of concern**: All three stockpiles; perimeter ROW fenceline areas; areas of elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3 identified during the 2004 Preliminary Site Investigation (PSI).
- **Media addressed**: Soil only. Groundwater is referenced for background but is not being sampled in this workplan.
- **Specific work areas referenced**: Proposed boring locations (56 total) – 29 fenceline borings (5 ft depth), 22 perimeter borings (3 ft depth), and 5 cadmium borings (20 ft depth) within Stockpiles 2 and 3.

## Key Claims

1. **Claim**: The stockpiles as currently managed by Caltrans on Caltrans ROW do not pose a risk to human health for Caltrans workers accessing the fenced site, trespassers, or residents adjacent to the stockpiles.
   - **Claimant**: DTSC (December 17, 2009 letter)
   - **Support**: Conditional; based on prior HHRA and PEA findings but requires additional data to support final RAP. This workplan is intended to address residual data gaps before project approval.

2. **Claim**: Groundwater beneath the site meets drinking water standards for analyzed constituents.
   - **Claimant**: Shaw Environmental, Inc. (referenced in background section)
   - **Support**: Unsupported in this document; no new groundwater data are presented. Cited from prior 2006 investigations.

3. **Claim**: Barium and PAHs are the primary COPCs in the soil stockpiles; cadmium was not identified as a COPC in the 2007 HHRA due to lack of elevated detections in 2006 sampling.
   - **Claimant**: Shaw Environmental, Inc. (HHRA, 2007)
   - **Support**: Partially contested by the 2004 PSI data, which showed elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3 above commercial/industrial CHHSLs. This workplan proposes new cadmium boring to resolve the discrepancy.

## Analytical Results

**No new analytical data are presented in this document.** The workplan describes proposed sampling and analysis, not completed results. The document does reference historical data:

- **2004 PSI**: Barium concentrations in stockpile samples exceeded the commercial/industrial California Human Health Screening Level (CHHSL). Elevated cadmium above the commercial/industrial CHHSL was detected in soil samples from 8 of 25 borings at Stockpile 2 and 2 of 10 borings at Stockpile 3.
- **Proposed analyses**:
  - Fenceline borings (0–6 in. and 5 ft depth): Title 22 metals and strontium by EPA Method 6010B; mercury by EPA Method 7471.
  - Perimeter borings (surface samples): Barium by EPA Method 6010B.
  - Cadmium borings (surface and 5-ft interval samples): Barium and cadmium by EPA Method 6010B.
- **Thresholds**: Commercial/industrial CHHSL for barium and cadmium are cited as screening criteria from prior assessments but not listed numerically in this document.
- **QA/QC flags**: Laboratory reports will include J-flags for estimated values between MDL and PQL. Method blanks, duplicate analyses, and matrix spikes at frequencies specified.

## Remedial Actions

**No physical remedial actions are described.** The document describes planned *investigative* activities only:

- **Activity**: Supplemental site investigation soil boring program (direct-push Geoprobe and hand-auger methods).
- **Quantities**: 56 borings total – 29 fenceline (5 ft), 22 perimeter (3 ft), 5 cadmium (20 ft).
- **Backfilling**: 20-ft direct-push borings backfilled with bentonite slurry; 3-ft and 5-ft borings backfilled with cuttings.
- **Decontamination**: Non-disposable equipment decontaminated with wash-and-double-rinse using non-phosphate detergent, deionized water, and tap water. Decontamination water to be contained, sampled, analyzed for metals, and properly disposed.
- **Phase status**: Planned. Field work scheduled for September 28, 2012, pending regulatory approval.
