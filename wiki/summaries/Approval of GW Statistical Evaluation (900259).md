## Key claims
- DTSC and the Central Valley RWQCB concur that the barium- and lead-impacted soil stockpiles encapsulated beneath State Route 132 are not currently impacting groundwater and will not impact groundwater in the future.
- The approval endorses discontinuation of groundwater monitoring and decommissioning of the four remaining monitoring wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10) as per the approved Remedial Design and Implementation Plan.
- The conclusion is supported by consistency with the earlier 2014 evaluation and the recent encapsulation of the soil beneath the Express Way.

## Methodology
- No experimental setup or quantitative evaluation metrics are described in this approval letter. The letter references an “Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data” (February 26, 2024) submitted by Geocon Consultants Inc. on behalf of Caltrans, which evaluated the potential of barium- and lead-impacted soil stockpiles to affect groundwater. The letter states that DTSC and the RWQCB reviewed this Evaluation Report, but methodological details (e.g., sampling design, statistical tests, data sources) are not provided in the present document.

## Results
- The data presented in the Evaluation Report indicate that the encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater.
- No specific numerical results (e.g., contaminant concentrations, statistical p-values, threshold exceedances) are reported in this approval letter.

## Limitations
- The letter does not discuss limitations or caveats of the evaluation. It simply states DTSC and RWQCB agreement with the conclusions and recommendations.
- The approval is contingent on the submitted Evaluation Report and its consistency with prior findings; no independent verification or additional data collection is referenced.
