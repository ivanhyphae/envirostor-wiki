## Site and Scope
- **Site:** Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles (the site), located south of the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California.
- **Areas of concern:** Stockpile 1 (south of Kansas Avenue, west of North Emerald Avenue) and Stockpile 2 (south of Kansas Avenue, between North Emerald Avenue and SR 99), both encapsulated beneath SR 132. Also referenced: former Stockpile 3 (south of Kansas Avenue, east of SR 99), which was excavated and placed atop Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- **Media addressed:** Stormwater (surface water).
- **Specific sample locations:**
    - **SW-East:** Stormwater discharge point on the north side of SR 132 west of SR 99.
    - **SW-West:** East side of Ponding Basin 5, north of SR-132 and east of N. Carpenter Road.
    - **BG-West (background):** Drop inlet along the east side of N. Carpenter Road, approximately 700 feet south of the intersection with Kansas Avenue (dry during this event and not sampled).

## Key Claims
- **Claim:** Discharge/runoff was not observed migrating away from the Caltrans right-of-way (ROW) during the February 4, 2025, sampling event.
    - **Claimant:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (based on field observations).
    - **Supported by data:** Based on field observation; the background location (BG-West) was also dry, but no visual evidence of off-site migration is stated.
- **Claim:** All detected contaminant concentrations in stormwater samples SW-East and SW-West are below their respective Primary or Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for drinking water.
    - **Claimant:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
    - **Supported by data:** Yes, supported by the laboratory analytical results presented in Tables 1 and 2 and the attached laboratory report.
- **Claim:** The laboratory QC data indicates acceptable non-detect results for method blanks and acceptable recoveries for LCS and LCSD samples.
    - **Claimant:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (based on review of lab report).
    - **Supported by data:** Yes, but with noted exceptions (see Analytical Results section regarding RPD for silver and molybdenum).

## Analytical Results
- **Media:** Stormwater.
- **Collection Date:** February 4, 2025.
- **Laboratory:** ASSET Laboratories.
- **Analytical Methods:**
    - Title 22 Dissolved Metals: EPA Method 6020/7470 (ICP/MS, CVAA).
    - Sulfate: EPA Method 9056/300.0 (Ion Chromatography).
    - Total Dissolved Solids (TDS): EPA Method 160.1 (SM2540C).
- **Summary of Results (SW-East, SW-West):**

| Parameter | SW-East | SW-West | Applicable MCL (mg/L) | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **TDS (mg/L)** | 210 | 180 | 500 (Secondary MCL) | Below MCL |
| **Sulfate (mg/L)** | 21 | 14 | 250 (Secondary MCL) | Below MCL |
| **Barium (µg/L)** | 390 | 90 | 1,000 (Primary MCL) | Below MCL |
| **Copper (µg/L)** | 16 | 14 | 1,000 (Primary MCL) | Below MCL |
| **Zinc (µg/L)** | 55 | 39 | 5,000 (Secondary MCL) | Below MCL |
| **Molybdenum (µg/L)** | 16 | 10 | *Not established* | Detected |
| **Vanadium (µg/L)** | 6.1 | 8.2 | *Not established* | Detected |
| **Strontium (µg/L)** | 170 | 190 | *Not established* | Detected |
| **Thallium (µg/L)** | 6.1 (J+) | 8.2 (J+) | 2.0 (Primary MCL) | Estimated, above MCL. *Note: Geocon states it is not addressed in this report for this event, but previous events showed exceedances.* |

- **Exceedances:** For this event, no concentrations exceeded applicable MCLs for TDS, sulfate, or the metals listed. However, thallium results were flagged as estimated and still above the 2.0 µg/L Primary MCL. The report does not claim this is a violation.
- **Background (BG-West):** Not sampled; dry conditions.
- **QA/QC Flags:**
    - **Laboratory Notes:** RPD for Matrix Spike Duplicate (MSD) is outside accepted recovery limits for silver. RPD for Sample Duplicate (DUP) is outside accepted recovery limits for molybdenum. The laboratory narrative states these are validated by acceptable LCS/LCSD results.
    - **Data Qualifiers:** J+ (estimated quantity, may be biased high) for thallium. Other J flags noted on Table 2 for various analytes (e.g., copper, zinc, molybdenum, vanadium, strontium).
