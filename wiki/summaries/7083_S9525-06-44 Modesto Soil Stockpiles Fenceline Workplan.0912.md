Based on the three section summaries, the source document "raw/7083_S9525-06-44 Modesto Soil Stockpiles Fenceline Workplan.0912.pdf" is a workplan that combines a supplemental site investigation for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles with a Health and Safety Plan (HSP) for the field activities. The investigation addresses data gaps identified by DTSC and RWQCB regarding elevated barium and PAHs from former FMC evaporation ponds, with a focus on evaluating offsite and vertical migration, defining lateral stockpile limits, and additional sampling in areas of elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3. Planned field activities include 29 fenceline borings (5 ft deep), 22 perimeter borings (3 ft deep), and 5 deeper cadmium borings (20 ft deep) using a Geoprobe or hand-auger, with laboratory analyses for Title 22 metals, strontium, barium, and cadmium via EPA methods. The HSP establishes safety controls—wet methods, PPE, utility location, and biological hazard precautions—based on prior assessments (HHRA 2007, PEA 2009) that found no unacceptable risk under current site management (institutional controls) and low airborne exposure risk for CAM 17 metals and asbestos when safe practices are used. Negative exposure assessments for lead and asbestos are cited as representative. Results indicate that no quantitative data are reported in this workplan; the anticipated schedule includes fieldwork by September 2012, with a draft report due in November 2012. Limitations include potential schedule delays, necessary field adjustments to boring locations, and reliance on prior exposure data without new industrial hygiene monitoring. The HSP has a six-month expiry and applies only to Geocon personnel. Key concepts include Title 22 metals, COPCs, RAP, PEA, CHHSLs, and various Cal/OSHA exposure limits.
