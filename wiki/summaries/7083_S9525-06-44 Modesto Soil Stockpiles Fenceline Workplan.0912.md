## Key claims
- This document is a supplemental site investigation workplan for three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1-3) located within Caltrans right-of-way near SR 99 and Kansas Avenue in Modesto, California.
- The stockpiles (~121,000 cubic yards) contain elevated barium (notably) and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) from 1960s excavations of former FMC Corporation evaporation ponds.
- Previous investigations (2004 PSI, 2006 SI, 2007 HHRA, 2009 Final PEA) concluded that stockpiles as currently managed do not pose human health risks to Caltrans workers, trespassers, or adjacent residents.
- DTSC and RWQCB identified three data gaps: 1) perimeter fenceline sampling for offsite/vertical migration, 2) lateral stockpile limit definition for construction consolidation, and 3) additional sampling in areas of elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3.
- The workplan proposes 29 fenceline borings (5 ft deep), 22 perimeter borings (3 ft deep), and 5 cadmium borings (20 ft deep) to address these data gaps and support a future Remedial Action Plan.

## Methodology
- **Experimental setup**: Direct-push Geoprobe rig (and hand-auger where inaccessible) to advance borings at pre-staked locations field-adjusted with DTSC/Caltrans/Geocon.
- **Boring types and depths**:
  - 29 Fenceline Borings: 5 ft max depth; continuous soil samples in 3-ft acetate sleeves at 0–6 in and 1-ft intervals.
  - 22 Perimeter Borings: 3 ft max depth; same sampling protocol.
  - 5 Cadmium Borings: 20 ft max depth; samples at 0–6 in and 5-ft intervals (4–5, 9–10, 14–15, 19–20 ft).
- **Sample handling**: Ends sealed with Teflon sheets and plastic end caps; hand-auger samples homogenized in resealable bags; stored chilled; chain-of-custody maintained.
- **Analytical methods** (by ATL, CA DHS-certified laboratory, 5-day turnaround):
  - Fenceline: Title 22 metals + strontium via EPA 6010B; mercury via EPA 7471.
  - Perimeter: barium only via EPA 6010B.
  - Cadmium: barium and cadmium via EPA 6010B.
- **QA/QC**: Method blank per 10 samples or batch; duplicate analysis per 10 samples or batch; spiked sample (10× detection limit) per 10 samples or batch; J-flags for values between MDL and PQL.
- **Boring backfill**: 20-ft borings with bentonite slurry; shallow borings with excess cuttings.
- **Data use**: Results to be incorporated into Supplemental Site Investigation Report, then into Remedial Action Plan for DTSC/RWQCB approval.

## Results
- No new quantitative results are presented (this is a workplan for future sampling).
- Previous 2004 PSI results: elevated barium above commercial/industrial CHHSL in stockpile samples; elevated cadmium above CHHSL in 8 of 25 borings at Stockpile 2 and 2 of 10 borings at Stockpile 3.
- Previous 2007 HHRA: metals (barium) and PAHs identified as primary COPCs; no unacceptable health risks found for any exposure scenario.
- Previous 2009 Final PEA: DTSC concurred stockpiles pose no risk to Caltrans workers, trespassers, or adjacent residents under current management.

## Limitations
- This is a workplan; results and conclusions will be presented in future reports (SSI Report, human health risk assessment, Remedial Action Plan).
- Previous HHRA did not identify cadmium as a COPC due to lack of elevated cadmium in 2006 SI samples, creating a data gap that this workplan aims to fill.
- Boring locations may require field adjustment based on utilities, buried structures, trash/debris, and access constraints.
- Schedule is subject to delays from site access, inclement weather, supplemental lab testing, and regulatory review.
- The HSP (Appendix B) is for a related but distinct project (Modesto Ramp Rehabilitation Project) and is adapted here; it expires 6 months from CIH approval date (April 2012).
