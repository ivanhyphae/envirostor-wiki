- **Specific sample locations or work areas**: The Supplemental Site Investigation (SSI) evaluated potential lateral and vertical migration of contaminants from the soil stockpiles

## Key Claims
- **Claim**: DTSC concurs with (approves) the revised March 1, 2013 SSI and HHRA Update reports.
- **Who is making it**: DTSC, in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB).
- **Whether it is supported by data**: Supported by the document's review history—the reports were revised in response to DTSC comments dated February 15, 2013, and the preparer (Geocon) provided a Response to Comments letter dated March 1, 2013.

- **Decision type**: Formal concurrence (approval) of two reports.
- **Deciding agency**: DTSC (in consultation with RWQCB).
- **Date**: April 4, 2013.
- **Documents approved**:
    1. "Supplemental Site Investigation (SSI), Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project, Stanislaus County, California" (Geocon, March 1, 2013)

## Analytical Results
- **Not stated**: No numerical monitoring data or concentration ranges are included in this letter.

## Remedial Actions
- **Activity**: The Workplan (approved September 2012) authorized additional characterization of soil stockpiles to evaluate lateral/vertical migration of contaminants and to update the HHRA. The characterization was implemented in late September 2012.
- **Phase status**: Characterization field work is completed; the resulting SSI and HHRA Update reports are now approved by DTSC.

## Responsible Parties
- **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans)**: Owner/operator; responsible party under Interagency Agreement with DTSC; recipient of this approval.
- **Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)**: Regulatory oversight agency; concurring authority on the SSI and HHRA Update.
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB)**: Co-regulatory agency, consulted during DTSC's review.
- **Geocon Consultants Inc.**: Caltrans' contractor; prepared the approved reports and the Response to Comments letter.

## Key Terms and Conditions
- The approved SSI and HHRA Update are to be used for the purpose of preparing a Remedial Action Plan for management of the soil stockpiles.
- No new deadlines or monitoring obligations are imposed by this letter.
