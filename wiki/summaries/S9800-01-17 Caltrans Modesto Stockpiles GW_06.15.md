Here is a combined coherent summary of the groundwater monitoring report "raw/S9800-01-17 Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles GW_06.15.pdf" for the April 2015 sampling event.

The report presents the results of groundwater monitoring at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, conducted on April 30, 2015. Of the ten monitoring wells on site, only four (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, and MW-7) contained sufficient groundwater for sampling; the remaining six wells were either dry or had insufficient water, limiting a complete site-wide assessment. Field procedures included water level measurement, well purging using disposable bailers until field parameters stabilized, and sample collection with in-line filtration (0.45-micron) for dissolved metals. Samples were transported to Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) for analysis.

Laboratory methods included EPA 6020/7470 for Title 22 dissolved metals (including strontium) and major cations, EPA 300.0 for anions (chloride, nitrate as N, sulfate), SM 2540C for total dissolved solids (TDS), SM 2320B for alkalinity, SM 4500 for sulfide, and SM 2540C for TDS. Results were compared to California Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) and EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories. Quality control included method blanks, LCS, MS/MSD; some MS/MSD recoveries fell outside control limits (attributed to matrix interference), but data were validated by LCS and considered usable. High dilutions were required for major cations, and some re-analyses were needed due to internal standard failures.

Key findings: Groundwater was classified as calcium-magnesium-bicarbonate dominant based on Stiff diagrams, consistent with historical data. TDS ranged from 490 mg/L (MW-7, MW-2) to 660 mg/L (MW-1), with MW-1 and MW-4 exceeding the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L. Nitrate as N ranged from 7.0 mg/L (MW-7) to 17 mg/L (MW-1); MW-1 (17 mg/L) and MW-2 (12 mg/L) exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L. The report notes that elevated nitrate in upgradient well MW-1 may reflect natural background. Dissolved metals of primary concern—barium (100–150 µg/L) and strontium (760–1,300 µg/L)—were detected in all four wells but remained well below their respective thresholds. Lead, mercury, cadmium, and selenium were not detected above PQLs. Chromium (5.5–7.1 µg/L) and vanadium (21–25 µg/L) were detected below thresholds. Arsenic was detected at low levels in MW-2 (2.2 µg/L) and MW-7 (2.5 µg/L). Major cations included calcium (61,000–100,000 µg/L), magnesium (23,000–41,000 µg/L), sodium (30,000–54,000 µg/L), and potassium (2,700–3,700 µg/L). Cation-anion balances were near neutrality (e.g., MW-7: 8.1 vs 8.1 meq/L). Depth to groundwater ranged from 36.85 to 41.26 feet below TOC, with a southerly flow direction and an average gradient of 0.004 ft/ft (historically variable from 0.0006 to 0.004). Key limitations include the dry/insufficient wells limiting spatial coverage, incomplete field purge documentation, a single sampling event precluding temporal assessment, and the possibility that elevated nitrate in MW-1 represents background rather than contamination. The data are representative only of the four sampled wells at that time.
