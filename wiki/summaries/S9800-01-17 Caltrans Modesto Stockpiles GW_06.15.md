The Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles groundwater monitoring report (S9800-01-17) summarizes an April 2015 event and the longer-term monitoring history for wells MW-1 through MW-10 at the site in Stanislaus County, California. Geocon conducted the work under Caltrans Task Order No. 17 using a DTSC-approved work plan, collecting groundwater by purging wells and sampling with disposable bailers, then analyzing the samples at Advanced Technology Laboratories. Field measurements included depth to water, pH, conductivity, temperature, and oxidation-reduction potential, while laboratory testing covered dissolved Title 22 metals, major ions and other general chemistry parameters, sulfide, TDS, alkalinity, and historically PAHs until those analyses were discontinued after 2012 with DTSC concurrence. QA/QC results were generally acceptable.

Hydrogeologically, groundwater at the site remained shallow and flowed generally southeast to south, with April 2015 groundwater depths ranging from about 36.85 to 41.26 feet below ground surface and elevations around 42.48 to 43.27 feet MSL. The chemistry was variable but overall only slightly changed since 2012. Stiff diagrams indicated bicarbonate-dominant water, with some wells showing calcium-dominant or sodium-dominant character, reflecting moderate mineralization and spatial variability across the site.

The April 2015 sampling event was limited by water availability: only MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, and MW-7 contained enough water to sample, while several wells were dry and MW-6 could not be purged. This pattern also reflects the broader monitoring record, in which some wells became too dry to sample in later years. Across the monitoring period, many regulated metals remained nondetect or at low concentrations. DTSC-identified chemicals of concern for the site are barium, lead, and strontium; in April 2015, barium and strontium were detected in all sampled wells, but lead was not detected above the practical quantitation limit in any sample. Other metals such as mercury, silver, cadmium, beryllium, selenium, and thallium were generally below reporting limits, while arsenic, chromium, nickel, manganese, and vanadium were present at low to modest µg/L levels.

The principal water-quality exceedances were not metals but nitrate and total dissolved solids. Nitrate exceeded the primary MCL in MW-1 and MW-2, and TDS exceeded the secondary MCL in MW-1 and MW-4. Sulfate and chloride were present at elevated but generally non-exceeding levels, and major ions such as calcium, magnesium, sodium, bicarbonate alkalinity, and TDS showed the strongest contribution to the groundwater’s mineralized character. Overall, the report concludes that groundwater quality beneath the soil stockpiles has remained relatively stable, with only slight changes over time and no clear indication of significant adverse impact beyond the persistent nitrate and TDS exceedances.
