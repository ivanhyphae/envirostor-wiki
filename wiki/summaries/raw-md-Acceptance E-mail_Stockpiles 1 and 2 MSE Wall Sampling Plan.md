Here is a structured summary of the source document, prepared for the case-file wiki.

## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** E-mail chain transmitting and accepting a "MSE Wall Sampling Plan." The plan describes the characterization and classification of soil from mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall footing excavations at Stockpiles 1 and 2 to determine suitability for on-site reuse.
- **Author or preparer:** The sampling plan was prepared by John Juhrend, P.E., CEG, CEM (Geocon Consultants, Inc.). The regulatory acceptance e-mail was authored by Dean Wright, P.G. (DTSC Project Manager).
- **Date or period covered:** Plan transmitted March 31, 2020; regulatory acceptance provided April 2, 2020. Fieldwork was scheduled to begin March 31, 2020.

## Key claims
- **Acceptance of plan (Dean Wright, DTSC):** "DTSC and the Water Board have reviewed the MSE Wall Sampling Plan... There are no comments... and this e-mail serves as our acceptance of the Sampling Plan." This indicates the plan meets regulatory requirements as submitted.
- **Reuse suitability approach (John Juhrend, Geocon):** Soil analytical data for barium and lead will be compared to specific site background levels and regulatory screening levels (RDIP BCS removal threshold, construction worker ESL, residential ESL, commercial ESL) to determine material reuse as clean capping material, abutment/embankment fill, or placement in BCS containment zones.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type:** Formal acceptance (no comments) of a Remedial Action work plan (Sampling Plan).
- **Agency:** California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (Water Board).
- **Date:** April 2, 2020.
- **Conditions imposed:** None stated; the acceptance is unconditional as presented.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants of concern:** Barium and lead.
- **Media:** Discrete soil samples from one-foot intervals within MSE wall footing excavations (~4 to 5 feet depth).
- **Concentration ranges & thresholds (as listed in the plan):**
    - **Barium:**
        - Maximum site-specific background: 120 mg/kg
        - RDIP BCS removal threshold (groundwater protection): 1,000 mg/kg
        - Construction worker environmental screening level (ESL): 3,000 mg/kg
        - Residential ESL: 15,000 mg/kg
        - Commercial ESL: 220,000 mg/kg
    - **Lead:**
        - Maximum site-specific background: 3.8 mg/kg
        - DTSC residential screening level: 80 mg/kg
- **Exceedances noted:** None; the document is a plan for future testing.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity described:** Soil sampling of MSE wall footing excavations for characterization and DTSC classification for on-site reuse. Sampling will be conducted as the "BCS slope zone" is removed. Sampling locations are spaced at approximate 200-foot intervals.
- **Phase status:** Planned work; sampling pending regulatory approval (which was subsequently granted).
- **Verification outcomes:** No results are reported; this is a pre-activity plan. The data will be compared to the thresholds listed in *Analytical results*.

## Responsible parties
- **Caltrans (California Department of Transportation):** Owner/operator. Richard Stewart (Environmental Scientist) requested regulatory approval and coordinated the work scope.
- **DTSC (Department of Toxic Substances Control):** Lead regulatory oversight. Dean Wright (Project Manager) provided acceptance.
- **Central Valley RWQCB (Regional Water Quality Control Board):** Co-regulator. Steven Meeks participated in review and concurrence.
- **Geocon Consultants, Inc.:** Consultant preparing the sampling plan and performing the field work (RDIP monitoring, soil sampling). Author: John Juhrend.

## Key terms and conditions
- **Approved Work:** The sampling and subsequent characterization/classification of MSE wall footing soils at Stockpiles 1 and 2 is approved.
- **Analytical Protocols:** Soil samples will be analyzed for barium and lead under a 48-hour expedited turnaround time. Data will be compared to site-specific background and RDIP/regulatory screening levels.
- **Field Conditions:** Sampling to occur after removal of the BCS (possibly "Base Course Surface" or "Borrow/Capping Soil") slope zone material in the southern slope of Stockpile 1. Sampling depth is the planned wall footing excavation depth (~4-5 ft).
- **Monitoring Obligations:** Geocon's Dave Watts is providing "RDIP monitoring including air/dust perimeter sampling" during the initial excavation work (March 31, 2020).
- **Reuse Decision Framework:** Barium and lead concentrations will dictate end-use: clean capping material, abutment/embankment fill, or placement in BCS containment zones.
