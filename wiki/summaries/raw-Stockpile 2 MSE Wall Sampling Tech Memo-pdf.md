## Combined Summary of “Stockpile 2 MSE Wall Sampling Tech Memo.pdf”

This document is a **Technical Memorandum** prepared by **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** (John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG) for **Michael Scott, Construction Manager, WSP/City of Modesto**, dated **June 18, 2020** (Project No. S9108-01-01). It addresses soil sampling along the **southerly slope of Stockpile 2** at the **State Route 132 Project in Modesto, California**, where a **mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall footing excavation** was planned.

### Key Claim
The memo asserts that the **upper three feet of excavated soil must be placed within designated Stockpile 1 and 2 fill containment zones** due to elevated barium concentrations, while material from **depths greater than three feet** is suitable for use as general embankment fill or capping material (pending regulatory approval). This claim is supported by analytical data.

### Sampling and Analytical Results
Samples were collected from **seven trenches (STK2-1 through STK2-7)** spaced at ~200‑foot intervals along the planned footing alignment. Discrete 1‑foot‑interval samples were taken to depths of 3 feet (five trenches) or 5 feet (two trenches), for a total of **29 soil samples**. All samples were analyzed by **EPA Method 6010B (ICP‑AES)** for **barium and lead** at **ATL Global** (ELAP‑certified laboratory), with chain‑of‑custody documentation.

- **Barium** – Concentrations ranged from **53 to 930 mg/kg**. The **site‑specific background maximum is 120 mg/kg**; the **BCS Removal Verification Threshold is 1,000 mg/kg**. Of the 29 samples, **14 exceeded background** (>120 mg/kg); **13 of those 14 were from the 0–3 ft depth interval** (concentrations up to 930 mg/kg). The only deeper exceedance (STK2‑7‑4 at 140 mg/kg) came from below the planned footing depth.
- **Lead** – Concentrations ranged from **3.5 to 22 mg/kg** (background maximum: 3.8 mg/kg). Most samples were near background; two elevated detections were at **STK2‑7‑0 (22 mg/kg)** and **STK2‑7‑1 (15 mg/kg)**.

**Detailed results for trenches STK2‑5, STK2‑6, and STK2‑7** (provided in the laboratory report) confirm the pattern: barium increased with depth in STK2‑7 (0 ft: 690 mg/kg; 1 ft: 580; 2 ft: 320; 3 ft: 110; 4 ft: 140 mg/kg), while STK2‑5 and STK2‑6 remained near or below background. Lead values at these locations ranged from 4.6 to 22 mg/kg.

**Quality control** was generally acceptable. Method blanks were non‑detect; LCS recoveries were within limits (barium 104–114%, lead 107–108%). Matrix spike duplicates for barium in batch B0F0375 showed high recoveries (187% and 204%) due to matrix interference (flagged M2), but the batch was validated by the LCS. All duplicate RPDs were ≤20%.

### Remedial Actions
Excavation of the BCS (barium‑affected soil) along the Stockpile 2 southerly slope was completed to facilitate MSE wall footing construction. Excavated material was transported and placed as embankment fill within the designated **Stockpile 2 fill containment zone**. Verification sampling used the 29 discrete soil samples described above. The results will be included in a future **Removal Action Completion Report** following Stockpile 1 and 2 clean fill capping.

### Regulatory Decisions
No formal regulatory decisions are documented in this memo. It references a **“Variance to Remedial Design Implementation Plan, April 9, 2020”** as context. Placement of deeper material as capping stockpile is noted as “pending regulatory agency approval.”
