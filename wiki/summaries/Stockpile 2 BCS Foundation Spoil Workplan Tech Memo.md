## Key claims
This technical memorandum outlines a workplan for handling barium-containing soil (BCS) spoils from two foundation excavations within a designated containment zone on State Route 132 in Modesto, California. The key contributions are: (1) a conservative estimate of 40 cubic yards of BCS material to be generated; (2) a protocol for stockpiling the material under plastic sheeting with erosion controls; (3) a sampling plan involving eight discrete soil samples composited into two 4-part samples for Title 22 metals analysis using EPA methods; (4) defined site-specific background thresholds for barium (120 mg/kg) and lead (3.8 mg/kg) to determine suitability for onsite reuse; (5) a disposal pathway to Forward Landfill in Manteca if reuse is not approved; and (6) a requirement for a letter summary report documenting all activities, laboratory data, manifests, and photographs to be included in the Removal Action Completion Report.

## Methodology
The workplan describes two foundation excavations: (a) a completed 4.5-foot-deep weather station foundation slab/anchor (Station 1138+60) generating ~2 cubic yards of BCS material already spread on adjacent ground; (b) a planned 5-foot-diameter by 25-foot-deep overhead sign #1 foundation (Station 1139+30) generating ~18.2 cubic yards. BCS material from both sources will be combined into a single stockpile (~40 cubic yards total). The stockpile will be pre-moistened, placed on minimum 6-milimeter-thick plastic sheeting, covered with more plastic, and surrounded by erosion control measures (straw wattles, hay bales) with cover secured by ropes and weights (sand bags, tires). Eight discrete soil samples will be collected from the stockpile on a grid pattern at various depths using labelled stainless steel sample tubes, chilled, and transported under chain-of-custody to California Laboratory Services. The lab will composite the eight samples into two 4-part composite samples. Analysis will use EPA Test Methods 6010B (metals) and 7471A (mercury) under expedited 48-hour turnaround. If total metal concentrations are elevated, additional Waste Extraction Test (WET) and Toxicity Characteristic Leaching Procedure (TCLP) will determine if the material is California or federal RCRA hazardous waste. Results are compared to site-specific background levels: barium 120 mg/kg, lead 3.8 mg/kg. If concentrations do not exceed these thresholds and Caltrans/DTSC approve the reuse location, the material may be used onsite. Otherwise, it will be profiled for acceptance at Forward Landfill in Manteca, with authorized Caltrans representative signing manifests and transport trucks having covered loads.

## Results
No quantitative analytical results are presented in this workplan, as it is a pre-activity planning document. The estimated volumes are: ~2 cubic yards from the completed weather station foundation, ~18.2 cubic yards from the planned overhead sign foundation, with a conservative total of 40 cubic yards for the stockpile. The site-specific background thresholds are specified as: barium – 120 mg/kg, lead – 3.8 mg/kg. The analytical methods (EPA 6010B, 7471A) are referenced but no numerical outcome data are provided.

## Limitations
The memorandum is a workplan, not a results report, so no empirical limitations are discussed. However, implied limitations include: (1) the estimate of 40 cubic yards is "conservative" but may not reflect actual volume; (2) the suitability for onsite reuse depends on both analytical results not exceeding background thresholds AND approval from Caltrans and DTSC, introducing regulatory uncertainty; (3) the sampling approach (eight discrete composited into two 4-part samples) may mask localized hotspots of contamination; (4) the stockpile management (plastic sheeting, erosion controls) assumes proper implementation and maintenance; (5) the document does not address potential groundwater or surface water impacts from the excavated foundations themselves; (6) the memorandum is specific to this project and may not be generalizable to other BCS management scenarios.
