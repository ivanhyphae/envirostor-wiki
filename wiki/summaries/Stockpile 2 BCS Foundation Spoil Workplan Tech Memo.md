## Key claims
This technical memorandum outlines a workplan for managing barium-containing soil (BCS) spoils from foundation excavations within the Stockpile 2 BCS Containment Zone on the State Route 132 Project in Modesto, California. The main contributions include: (1) a protocol for stockpiling approximately 40 cubic yards of BCS material from a weather station foundation (completed) and an overhead sign foundation (planned); (2) a sampling and analysis plan using composited discrete soil samples to determine barium and lead concentrations against site-specific background levels; (3) a decision framework for onsite reuse versus offsite landfill disposal; and (4) documentation requirements for inclusion in a Removal Action Completion Report.

## Methodology
- **Excavations**: 4.5-foot-deep weather station foundation (completed July/August 2021, ~2 cubic yards BCS spread on adjacent ground) and planned 5-foot-diameter by 25-foot-deep overhead sign foundation (~18.2 cubic yards BCS).
- **Stockpile Management**: BCS material (~40 cubic yards conservative estimate) placed on minimum 6-milimeter plastic sheeting, covered with same, secured with ropes/weights, surrounded by erosion control (straw wattles, hay bales).
- **Sampling**: Eight discrete soil samples collected from stockpile on grid pattern at various depths using labeled stainless steel tubes, chilled, transported under chain-of-custody to California Laboratory Services. Laboratory composites eight samples into two 4-part composites.
- **Analytical Methods**: Title 22 metals analysis via EPA Methods 6010B and 7471A (mercury) under 48-hour turnaround. Optional soluble metals testing via Waste Extraction Test (WET) and Toxicity Characteristic Leaching Procedure (TCLP) for elevated total metals.
- **Comparison Criteria**: Site-specific background levels: barium 120 mg/kg, lead 3.8 mg/kg.
- **Decision Framework**: If total barium and lead ≤ background, BCS may be suitable for onsite reuse with Caltrans/DTSC approval; otherwise, profile for landfill disposal at Forward Landfill in Manteca or other facility.

## Results
- **Weather station**: Generated ~2 cubic yards BCS material (spread on ground).
- **Overhead sign**: Expected to generate ~18.2 cubic yards BCS.
- **Total stockpile volume**: Conservative estimate of 40 cubic yards.
- **Background levels**: Barium 120 mg/kg, lead 3.8 mg/kg.
- No quantitative analytical results are reported as the workplan is prospective (sampling and analysis to be conducted per protocol).

## Limitations
- The workplan is a technical memorandum, not a completed study; no results are yet available.
- BCS material from the weather station foundation was spread on adjacent ground surface prior to this memo, potentially affecting representativeness of stockpile sampling.
- The memo notes that soluble metals testing (WET, TCLP) may be required only if total metal concentrations are elevated, implying a two-tiered approach that may delay hazardous waste determination.
- Onsite reuse is conditional upon Caltrans and DTSC approval of the proposed reuse location; no guarantee is provided.
- The memo uses a "conservative estimate" of 40 cubic yards, which may not reflect actual volumes.
