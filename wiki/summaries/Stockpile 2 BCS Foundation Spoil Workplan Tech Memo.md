## Key claims
- The memorandum proposes a workplan for managing and disposing/reusing barium-containing soil (BCS) spoils generated during foundation excavations in the Stockpile 2 BCS Containment Zone for the State Route 132 Project in Modesto, California.
- Two planned/previous excavations are identified as BCS sources: a weather station foundation slab/anchor and Overhead Sign #1.
- The excavated BCS material will be temporarily stockpiled, sampled, and characterized to determine whether it can be reused onsite or must be transported offsite for landfill disposal.
- Analytical results will be compared against site-specific background concentrations for barium and lead to inform reuse decisions.
- If analytical concentrations do not exceed background and agencies approve, the stockpiled soil may be suitable for onsite reuse; otherwise, it will be profiled and disposed of at an accepting landfill.
- A completion report will document disturbance activities, sample data, disposal documentation, and site photographs for inclusion in the Removal Action Completion Report.

## Methodology
- **Site context:** Southern portion of the Stockpile 2 BCS Containment Zone, State Route 132 Project, Modesto, California.
- **Excavation sources and estimated volumes:**
  - Weather Station Foundation Slab/Anchor at approximately Station 1138+60: 4.5-foot-deep excavation; about 2 cubic yards of BCS material already generated.
  - Overhead Sign #1 at approximately Station 1139+30: planned 5-foot-diameter by 25-foot-deep excavation; about 18.2 cubic yards of BCS material expected.
  - Conservative total stockpile estimate: 40 cubic yards.
- **Stockpile management:** Pre-moistened BCS soil will be placed on and covered with minimum 6-mil plastic sheeting, with erosion controls (e.g., straw wattles, hay bales) and secured with ropes/weights as needed.
- **Sampling plan:** Eight discrete soil samples collected from the stockpile on a grid pattern at various depths.
- **Sample handling:** Samples stored in labeled stainless steel tubes, chilled, and transported under chain-of-custody to California Laboratory Services.
- **Laboratory analysis:** The lab will composite the eight discrete samples into two 4-part composite samples.
- **Analytical methods:**
  - Title 22 metals analysis using EPA Methods 6010B and 7471A (for mercury).
  - If elevated total metals are found, soluble metals testing may be required using the Waste Extraction Test and Toxicity Characteristic Leaching Procedure (TCLP) to evaluate hazardous waste status under California or Federal RCRA.
- **Decision criteria:** Compare total barium and lead to site-specific background levels:
  - Barium: 120 mg/kg
  - Lead: 3.8 mg/kg
- **Disposition pathway:** Depending on results and regulatory approval, material may be reused onsite or disposed at Forward Landfill in Manteca (or another accepting landfill), with manifesting and licensed transport requirements.

## Results
- The memorandum is a workplan and does **not** report final analytical results.
- Quantitative information provided includes:
  - Existing weather station spoil volume: approximately 2 cubic yards.
  - Planned Overhead Sign #1 spoil volume: approximately 18.2 cubic yards.
  - Conservative total stockpile estimate: approximately 40 cubic yards.
  - Background thresholds:
    - Barium: 120 mg/kg
    - Lead: 3.8 mg/kg
- The document states that if total barium and lead do not exceed background concentrations, onsite reuse may be possible pending Caltrans and DTSC approval.
- If reuse is not approved, disposal at an accepting landfill (named example: Forward Landfill in Manteca) is proposed.

## Limitations
- No experimental outcomes or measured contaminant concentrations are presented; the document is a proposed assessment and disposal workplan.
- Onsite reuse is contingent on approvals from Caltrans and/or DTSC, so analytical compliance alone may not be sufficient.
- The plan notes that additional soluble metals testing may be required only if elevated total metals concentrations are observed, meaning final waste classification is conditional on future results.
- The memorandum relies on site-specific background values for barium and lead rather than universal thresholds.
- Disposal destination depends on landfill acceptance criteria and waste profiling results.
