## Combined Summary of "raw/Draft Interim RACR_ App D-G.pdf"

This document presents two interrelated components of a remedial action completion report (RACR) for a California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) project: characterization of borrow areas for clean cap material to cover barium‑containing soil (BCS) stockpiles, and air monitoring conducted during grading of those BCS stockpiles. Both activities were carried out under DTSC oversight as part of the SR‑132 Project in Modesto, California.

### Borrow Area Characterization for Clean Cap Material

Four potential sources of clean cover fill were evaluated: BCS Stockpiles Clean Cap Borrow Area, Basin 5 Clean Cap Borrow Area, Bent 2 Excavation Soil Stockpiles, and Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill. Sampling and analytical methods followed DTSC 2001 *Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Soil* and were approved by the agency.

- **BCS Stockpiles and Basin 5 areas:** Direct‑push borings (12) to 5 ft and trenches (6) to 10 ft were collected on a grid, with continuous core samples composited into depth‑discrete 3‑part composites. Samples were analyzed for Title 22 metals (EPA 6010B, 6461A/7471A for mercury) and organochlorine pesticides (EPA 8081A) for the 0–2 ft interval. Metal concentrations were consistent with natural background, making these areas suitable sources of clean cover fill.

- **Bent 2 Excavation Soil:** Ten discrete samples from end‑dump piles were analyzed for barium and lead (EPA 6010B, 24‑hour TAT). Barium levels (up to 230 mg/kg) exceeded the site‑specific background maximum of 120 mg/kg, so this material does **not** meet clean cap criteria for full‑depth placement. It may be considered as partial clean cap (≤ 6 inches) pending DTSC approval.

- **Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill:** Twenty‑four discrete samples from six transects at depths 2, 5, 10, and 15 ft were composited into eight 3‑part composites and analyzed for barium and lead (EPA 6010B, 48‑hour TAT). Both analytes met criteria: barium ranged from 28–96 mg/kg (below the 120 mg/kg background maximum) and lead from <1.3–3.8 mg/kg.

Overall, the BCS Stockpiles Borrow Area, Basin 5, and Carpenter Shoofly are considered suitable for generating clean cap material, while Bent 2 requires conditional approval.

### Air Monitoring During BCS Grading Activities

Air monitoring was conducted from February to June 2020 during BCS grading operations (mowing, stockpile relocation, excavations, slope grading, fill placement, trenching). The DTSC‑approved remedial approach for BCS stockpiles was containment by incorporation as highway embankment fill, covered with structural pavement and imported clean fill.

**Methodology:** Perimeter monitoring included one upwind (background) and at least two downwind sampling locations, plus personal monitoring of equipment operators. Real‑time direct‑reading particulate counters (pDR‑1200) with PM‑10 inlets recorded total dust every 60 seconds, with audible alarms set to downwind levels. Active sampling used 37‑mm filter cassettes with 0.8‑micron MCE filters and personal sampling pumps (2.0–3.0 L/min). Filters were analyzed by LA Testing for total dust (NIOSH 0500) and barium/lead (NIOSH 7300 modified).

**Action levels:** Total dust fence line = 4.0 mg/m³ (real‑time); barium = 25 µg/m³; lead = 1.5 µg/m³ (CARB 30‑day average standard).

**Results:** All airborne concentrations of total dust, barium, and lead were below their respective action levels throughout the grading period. Specific numerical data are presented in attached tables (Tables 1, 2A–G) but not reproduced in the text.

**Limitations:** The document does not explicitly discuss limitations or caveats. It notes that BCS grading activities have been largely completed; final clean fill capping is pending, and a final completion report will follow.
