## Summary of Draft Interim RACR Appendices D-G

**Source:** Draft Interim Removal Action Completion Report (RACR) for Barium Containing Soil (BCS) Stockpiles, State Route 132 Project, Modesto, California. Appendices D, E, F, G.

---

## Key claims

- **Clean cap borrow area suitability confirmed:** Two proposed borrow areas (BCS Stockpiles Clean Cap and Basin 5) contain soil with metal concentrations consistent with naturally occurring background levels, making them suitable sources for clean cover material to cap BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- **Carpenter Road Shoofly soil meets clean cap criteria:** Native soil from the Carpenter Road Shoofly excavation (approx. 5,000 cubic yards) meets the DTSC-approved clean cap criteria (barium < 120 mg/kg, lead < 3.8 mg/kg).
- **Bent 2 excavation soil fails clean cap criteria for bulk use:** Barium concentrations in 4 of 10 samples from Bent 2 end dumps exceeded the maximum site-specific background of 120 mg/kg (up to 230 mg/kg). However, the material may be suitable as a thin (≤6 inch) partial cap under future structural pavement.
- **Air monitoring confirms effective dust control:** During all BCS grading activities (Feb–Jun 2020), airborne levels of total dust, barium, and lead remained below designated project action levels and risk thresholds, validating the contractor's dust suppression measures.
- **No contamination indicators observed:** No field evidence of staining, odors, or debris was found in any borrow area or excavation soil samples.

## Methodology

**Site and Sample Design:**
- **Borrow Area Characterization (Appendix D):**
    - *BCS Stockpiles Clean Cap (Feb 2020):* 12 direct-push borings (B1–B12) in grid pattern, maximum depth 5 ft. Soil samples collected at 0–2 ft and 2–5 ft intervals. Composited into four 3-part composites per depth interval. Composites analyzed for Title 22 metals (EPA 6010B, 6461A for Hg); 0–2 ft composites also analyzed for organochlorine pesticides (OCPs, EPA 8081A).
    - *Basin 5 Clean Cap (Jun 2020):* 6 exploratory trenches (BASIN5 1–6) in grid, maximum depth 10 ft. Samples at 0–2 ft, 2–5 ft, and 5–10 ft. Field-composited into two 3-part composites per depth interval. Analyzed for Title 22 metals (EPA 6010B, 7471A for Hg); 0–2 ft composites also for OCPs (EPA 8081A).
- **Bent 2 Excavation Soil (Oct 2020):** 10 discrete samples (STK1–STK10) from two rows of end dumps, spaced ~80 ft apart. Analyzed for barium and lead only (EPA 6010B).
- **Carpenter Road Shoofly (Apr 2021):** 24 discrete samples along 6 vertical transects (CSF1–CSF6) at depths of 2, 5, 10, and 15 ft from top of slope. Laboratory-composited into eight 3-part composites per depth. Analyzed for barium and lead only (EPA 6010B).
- **Air Monitoring (Appendix F):** Performed Feb–Jun 2020 during multiple BCS grading phases. One upwind (background) and ≥2 downwind perimeter locations. Personal monitoring for equipment operators. Used real-time pDR-1200 direct-reading particulate counters with PM-10 inlets and active sampling (37 mm filter cassettes, 0.8 µ MCE filters, 2.0–3.0 L/min). Analytical methods: NIOSH 0500 (total dust), NIOSH 7300 modified (barium, lead).

**Reference Criteria:**
- Background concentrations from 2007 Shaw report (site-specific).
- Clean cap criteria: barium < 120 mg/kg; lead < 3.8 mg/kg (upperbound background from DTSC variance).
- BCS removal verification threshold: 1,000 mg/kg Ba; 80 mg/kg Pb.
- Dust action level (fence line): 4.0 mg/m³ total dust (real-time).
- Barium action level: 25 µg/m³ (contractor HASP).
- Lead action level: 1.5 µg/m³ (CARB 30-day standard).

## Results

**Borrow Area Characterization (Title 22 metals, mg/kg):**
- *BCS Stockpiles Clean Cap (0–5 ft):* As 1.0–1.5 (bg 1.2); Ba 60–110 (bg 72.8); Be 1.3–1.6 (bg 0.2); Cr 7.5–11 (bg 7.1); Co 3.2–4.9 (bg 4.4); Cu 5.3–9.9 (bg 7.5); Pb 1.7–7.0 (bg 2.0); Ni 5.6–8.2 (bg 5.3); Ag 1.6–2.5 (bg 0.2); V 16–25 (bg 31.3); Zn 21–41 (bg 26.3). Sb, Cd, Se, Tl, Hg, OCPs: not detected. *Conclusion: Consistent with background; suitable for clean cap.*
- *Basin 5 (0–10 ft):* As <1.0–1.8 (bg 1.2); Ba 66–94 (bg 72.8); Cr 8.5–11 (bg 8.6); Co 3.3–3.8 (bg 4.4); Cu 7.5–9.3 (bg 7.5); Pb 5.8–11 (bg 2.0); Ni 6.2–7.9 (bg 5.3); V 33–37 (bg 31.3); Zn 29–36 (bg 26.3). Sb, Be, Cd, Mo, Se, Ag, Tl, Hg, OCPs: not detected. *Conclusion: Consistent with background; suitable for clean cap.*

**Bent 2 Excavation Soil (Ba and Pb only, mg/kg):**
- Ba: range 31–230; max 230 (STK1). Four samples ≥ 120 mg/kg (STK1: 230; STK3: 170; STK6: 220; STK2: 120).
- Pb: range <2.5–13; max 13 (STK1). All ≤ 13 mg/kg; few exceed background maximum (3.8 mg/kg).
- *Conclusion: Does not meet bulk clean cap criteria due to elevated Ba; may be used as thin (≤6 in) partial cap under pavement.*

**Carpenter Road Shoofly (Ba and Pb only, mg/kg):**
- Ba: range 28–96 (all < 120 mg/kg max background).
- Pb: range <1.3–3.8 (all ≤ 3.8 mg/kg max background).
- *Conclusion: Meets clean cap criteria.*

**Air Monitoring:**
- *Total dust:* All downwind real-time pDR-1200 readings remained below the fence line action level of 4.0 mg/m³ (maximum recorded not explicitly stated, but all within compliance).
- *Barium:* All samples below 25 µg/m³ action level. (Specific values not provided in summary tables, but stated as "below designated project action and risk levels").
- *Lead:* All samples below 1.5 µg/m³ CARB standard.
- *Conclusion: Dust control measures effective; no off-site exceedances.*

## Limitations

- **Background comparison limitations:** Background values are site-specific from a 2007 Shaw report; methodology or potential changes in background since then are not discussed.
- **Partial thin cap for Bent 2 soil:** The recommendation to use Bent 2 soil as a ≤6-inch partial cap is contingent on "pending DTSC review and approval consideration" – not yet final.
- **Limited analyte scope for Bent 2 and Carpenter samples:** Only barium and lead were analyzed (per variance); other potential contaminants of concern (e.g., other metals, OCPs) are not addressed for these sources.
- **Compositing approach:** Composite sampling provides average concentrations but may mask localized hot spots within a borrow area or stockpile.
- **Air monitoring duration:** Sampling was conducted over a minimum of 4.5 hours per event; longer-term variability (e.g., diurnal, seasonal) is not captured.
- **Site-specific context:** Results are specific to the SR 132 right-of-way in Modesto, California; not generalizable to other sites without similar geochemical and historical conditions.
- **No formal risk assessment:** The memorandum concludes suitability based on comparison to background thresholds, not a formal human health or ecological risk assessment.
