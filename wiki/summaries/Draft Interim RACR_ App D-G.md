The source document section combines two related lines of evidence from the SR-132 West Freeway/Expressway Project in Modesto, California: soil characterization for determining where native excavated material can be reused as clean cap fill over barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles, and air monitoring during BCS grading activities to confirm dust controls were protecting workers and the surrounding community.

On the soil characterization side, samples from several borrow/excavation areas were collected and analyzed against site-specific background data and DTSC clean fill/clean cap criteria. The BCS Stockpiles Clean Cap Borrow Area and Basin 5 Clean Cap Borrow Area both showed metal concentrations generally consistent with background and were judged suitable for clean cover fill. By contrast, Bent 2 excavation stockpiles contained barium above the DTSC-approved upper-bound background in some samples, so they did not fully meet clean cap criteria, although the material may still be usable as a partial cap with additional structural pavement cover and DTSC approval. Carpenter Road Shoofly excavated soil met the clean cap criteria, with barium and lead at or below background thresholds, and was considered suitable clean cap material.

On the air monitoring side, perimeter and personal sampling were conducted during BCS grading, relocation, excavation, slope grading, and fill placement activities to verify the effectiveness of wet methods and other dust controls. Monitoring used real-time particulate instruments and laboratory analysis for total dust, barium, and lead, with results compared to project action levels and relevant health-based thresholds. The reported outcome was that airborne concentrations remained below the applicable action and risk levels, with no fence-line exceedances reported, indicating that the dust control measures were effective during the monitored work.

Overall, the section supports the project’s remedial approach: some excavated/native soils are suitable for reuse as clean cap material, some require additional restriction or cover design, and BCS handling and grading activities were conducted without exceeding air-quality action levels when proper dust controls were implemented.
