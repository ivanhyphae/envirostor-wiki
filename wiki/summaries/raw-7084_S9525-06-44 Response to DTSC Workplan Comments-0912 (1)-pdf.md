## Key terms and conditions
- Final Workplan was submitted on September 18, 2012 (same date as this response letter) addressing all DTSC/RWQCB comments from September 12, 2012.
- Specific obligations stated in responses:
  - Decontamination water must be contained, sampled, analyzed for metals, and disposed appropriately.
  - Laboratory analysis methodology (EPA 6010B/7471) applies to all boring categories; additional analysis may be required after initial results and DTSC/RWQCB direction.
  - Summary Report will be limited to Supplemental Site Investigation findings; separate reports for nature/extent (Updated Site Characterization Summary Report) and human health risk assessment (Updated Human Health Risk Assessment) will be prepared.
  - Laboratory reports must include soluble metals with method detection limits (MDL) and practical quantitation limits (PQL); concentrations between MDL and PQL shall be J‑flagged as estimates.
  - Detection limits must be suitable for human health risk assessment and comparison to water quality goals.
- No deadlines, permit conditions, land use restrictions, or ongoing monitoring obligations are established by this document.
