Here is the structured summary of the document for the case-file wiki.

## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose**: Acceptance letter from DTSC to Geocon Consultants, Inc., accepting the *Draft Interim Removal Action Completion Report (RACR)* for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Phase 1 Interim State Route 132 West Project. The letter serves as formal acknowledgment that the report is accepted as a technical status report with no comments.
- **Author or preparer**: Dean Wright, PG, Project Manager, Site Evaluation and Remediation Unit, Site Mitigation and Restoration Program, Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).
- **Date or period covered**: February 24, 2022. The document covers remediation activities completed during the Interim Phase 1 Construction effort, with a retrospective description of work, and forward-looking requirements for the Final Phase 1 RACR and Ultimate Phase 2.

## Key claims
- The Draft Phase 1 RACR indicates that remediation work was accomplished per the approved Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) and two variance requests approved on April 17, 2020 and September 21, 2020. *(Attribution: DTSC, based on review of the Draft Phase 1 RACR submitted by Geocon on behalf of Caltrans.)*
- All confirmation samples from the BCS Stockpile 3 excavation bottom, after re-excavation of three hotspots, showed barium concentrations below the cleanup goal of 1,000 mg/kg and lead below 80 mg/kg.
- All verification samples from the western and eastern ends of BCS Stockpile 2, after re-excavation of four hotspots, showed barium and lead below the verification thresholds of 1,000 mg/kg and 80 mg/kg.
- Soil removed from the southern slopes of Stockpiles 1 and 2 (for MSE wall construction) had barium and lead below cleanup goals but barium above the site-specific background concentration of 120 mg/kg; therefore, it was placed in the containment zones as embankment fill rather than used as clean cap.
- Material from the aerially deposited lead-removal effort was placed in the containment zone of BCS Stockpile 1.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type**: Acceptance of a draft report as a technical status report with no comments.
- **Agency**: DTSC (in consultation with Central Valley RWQCB).
- **Date**: February 24, 2022.
- **Conditions imposed**:
    - A Final Phase 1 RACR must be submitted (anticipated July or August 2022), including As-Built Plans, cross sections, final survey data, and an approved Operation and Maintenance (O&M) Plan.
    - The O&M Plan must include a soil management plan, annual inspection of pavement and containment features, and groundwater monitoring.
    - Following approval of the Final Phase 1 RACR, DTSC will enter into an O&M Agreement with Caltrans and complete a land use covenant for the containment areas.
    - Certification of the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) will not occur until after the Ultimate Phase 2 build-out is completed.
    - The Ultimate Phase 2 effort will require a supplemental RDIP, DTSC review and approval, field oversight, an Ultimate Phase 2 RACR, and potential revisions to the O&M Plan and Agreement.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants**: Barium, lead.
- **Media**: Soil (BCS stockpiles, native soil after excavation, aerially deposited lead soil).
- **Concentration ranges & thresholds**:
    - Barium cleanup goal: 1,000 mg/kg.
    - Lead cleanup goal: 80 mg/kg.
    - Site-specific background concentration for barium: 120 mg/kg.
- **Exceedances noted**:
    - BCS Stockpile 3 excavation base: 3 of 29 confirmation samples exceeded 1,000 mg/kg barium. All brought below goals after ~1 ft. deepening.
    - Western/eastern ends of BCS Stockpile 2 excavation: 4 areas exceeded 1,000 mg/kg barium. All brought below thresholds after ~1 ft. deepening.
    - Southern slopes of Stockpiles 1 & 2: barium above site-specific background (120 mg/kg), but below cleanup goal (1,000 mg/kg). Lead within background.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity**: Excavation, consolidation, transportation, and capping of barium-containing soils (BCS).
- **Quantities & phases**:
    - **BCS Stockpile 3**: Excavated ~27,675 yd³ of soil, transported by truck and placed as embankment fill in the BCS Stockpile 1 containment zone.
    - **BCS Stockpile 2**: Western and eastern ends excavated using scrapers, material placed directly within the BCS Stockpile 2 containment zone as embankment fill.
    - **Southern slopes of Stockpiles 1 & 2**: Soil removed to steepen slopes for MSE wall construction. Material placed in BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2 containment zones as embankment fill.
    - **Aerially deposited lead soil**: Removed from unpaved shoulder of SR 99 adjacent to Stockpile 3, placed in the containment zone in the eastern portion of BCS Stockpile 1.
- **Capping**: Containment zones for BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2 were capped with clean fill material from three approved source areas.
- **Erosion control**: Northerly slopes vegetated with hydroseed; erosion control blanket and fiber rolls installed. No controls needed on south side due to MSE wall.
- **Verification outcomes**: All final confirmation/verification samples met cleanup goals for barium and lead.

## Responsible parties
- **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans)**: Owner/operator of the site, responsible for submitting the Draft Phase 1 RACR via Geocon, and will enter into the O&M Agreement with DTSC.
- **Geocon Consultants, Inc.**: Consultant preparer of the Draft Phase 1 RACR; Project Manager John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG.
- **Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)**: Lead regulatory agency; authored the acceptance letter.
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB)**: Co-regulatory agency consulted during review.
- **City of Modesto**: Notified of the decision/acceptance.

## Key terms and conditions
- **Follow-on actions**: Submit Final Phase 1 RACR (including As-Built Plans, final survey, approved O&M Plan) by July/August 2022.
- **Monitoring obligations**: O&M Plan must include annual inspection of pavement and containment remedy features, and groundwater monitoring.
- **Restrictions & future phases**: Certification of RAP delayed until after Ultimate Phase 2. Ultimate Phase 2 will require a separate agreement, supplemental RDIP, DTSC oversight, and a new RACR.
- **Legal instruments**: Land use covenant for the containment areas required. O&M Agreement between DTSC and Caltrans to follow approval of Final Phase 1 RACR.
