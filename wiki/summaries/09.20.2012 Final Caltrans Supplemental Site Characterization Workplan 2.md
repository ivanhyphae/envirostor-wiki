## Key claims
- DTSC concurs with the “Final Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route 132 and 99, Stanislaus County, California” prepared by Geocon Consultants.
- The Workplan provides additional characterization of soil stockpiles at the proposed SR 132/99 West Expressway location to evaluate and finalize a remedy for management of hazardous substances.
- Primary chemicals of concern (COC) are barium, lead, and strontium, originating from historical chemical manufacturing by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC).
- Additional data will evaluate potential lateral and vertical migration of contaminants from soil stockpiles and update the Human Health Risk Assessment.

## Methodology
- The study is a supplemental site characterization workplan, not a completed experiment.
- Soil stockpiles consist of excess native soils and pond tailings generated during construction of a segment of SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue in the early 1960s. Excavation traversed a 4.3-acre parcel previously owned by FMC, which included an evaporation pond.
- The workplan addresses DTSC comments dated September 12, 2012, and proposes additional characterization to assess contaminant migration and support risk assessment updates.
- No specific sampling methods, analytical techniques, or quality assurance procedures are described in this letter; these are presumably detailed in the referenced Geocon workplan.

## Results
- No quantitative results are provided in this document. The letter only conveys regulatory concurrence with the proposed workplan; data collection and analysis are to follow.

## Limitations
- The letter does not explicitly discuss limitations of the workplan or previous data.
- The characterization is described as "supplemental," implying that earlier investigations were insufficient to fully define contaminant distribution or migration.
- The scope is limited to soil stockpiles at the specific project location; findings may not be generalizable to other sites.
