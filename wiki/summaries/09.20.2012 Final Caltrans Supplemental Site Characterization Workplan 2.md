## Key claims
- The document is a regulatory approval letter from the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) stating that it has reviewed the **Final Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan** for the **Modesto Soil Stockpiles** at the **State Route 132/99 West Expressway** project in Stanislaus County, California.
- DTSC concludes that the workplan provides the needed **additional site characterization** of soil stockpiles to support selection and finalization of a remedy for managing hazardous substances in the stockpiles.
- The purpose of the supplemental characterization is to assess **potential lateral and vertical migration of contaminants** from the stockpiles and to **update the Human Health Risk Assessment**.
- DTSC states that the workplan addresses its prior comments (dated 9/12/2012) and formally **concurs** with the final workplan.
- The primary contaminants of concern identified are **barium, lead, and strontium**.
