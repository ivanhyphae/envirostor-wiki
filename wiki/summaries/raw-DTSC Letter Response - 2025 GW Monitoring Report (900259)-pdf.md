## Site and scope
- **Site name**: Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles (also referenced as State Route 132 West Expressway)
- **Location**: South of intersection of State Route 99 and Kansas Avenue, Modesto, Stanislaus County, California
- **Areas of concern**: Three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) containing barium-contaminated soil; groundwater monitoring well network; storm water runoff from stockpile containment system
- **Media addressed**: Groundwater (well destruction), storm water / surface water (monitoring)
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas**: Ten groundwater monitoring wells installed in 2012; six decommissioned in 2019; remaining four wells destroyed per Well Destruction Report. Storm water sampling conducted during qualifying rain events.

## Key claims
- **Claim**: "after years of data supporting no metals were leaching into the groundwater from the stockpiles, [central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board and DTSC] agreed to their decommissioning."
    - **Who**: DTSC (in letter narrative)
    - **Supported by data in this document**: Claim is stated as established fact based on prior monitoring data, but no specific data are presented in this letter.
- **Claim**: "The results of the Storm Water Sampling Report were all within reporting limits."
    - **Who**: DTSC (citing the submitted report)
    - **Supported by data in this document**: Stated as conclusion; no numerical results or thresholds are provided in this letter.
