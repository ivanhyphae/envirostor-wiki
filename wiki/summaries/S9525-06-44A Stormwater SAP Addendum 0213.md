## Key claims
- This addendum proposes surface water sampling to evaluate whether runoff from three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1-3) at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site is migrating beyond Caltrans right-of-way and whether stormwater has been impacted by chemicals of potential concern from the stockpiles.
- The stockpiles originated from excavation of soil containing former evaporation pond residues from Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC) during construction of State Route 99 in 1961.
- Previous sampling by Shaw (March 2006) found no runoff migrating away from Caltrans right-of-way and, except for one sample, no exceedances of California Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs). The exception was sample SW03 from Stockpile 3, which had barium at 2,000 µg/l, exceeding the MCL of 1,000 µg/l. Barium in other samples ranged from 16 to 190 µg/l.
- The current scope includes collection of up to five surface water samples from locations where runoff may have migrated beyond the right-of-way, including puddles along Emerald Avenue shoulders, and at least one background rainwater sample.
- Laboratory analyses will include Title 22 total and dissolved metals (including strontium), nitrate, sulfate, sulfide, and total suspended solids.

## Methodology
- **Experimental setup**: Field collection of up to five surface water samples from puddles or runoff that has migrated beyond the Caltrans right-of-way boundary, plus one background rainwater sample. Sampling will occur during up to four separate rainfall events.
- **Sampling locations**: Puddles along east and west shoulders of Emerald Avenue between Stockpiles 1 and 2, and background sample along Emerald Avenue north or south of Stockpiles 1 and 2 (see Figure 2). Additional samples may be collected if flowing runoff is observed leaving the right-of-way.
- **Sample collection procedure**: Pre-cleaned scoop used to transfer water to laboratory-provided containers. Samples for dissolved metals will be field-filtered through a 0.45-micron filter. Field parameters (pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, turbidity) will be monitored.
- **QA/QC**: Disposable sampling scoops, chain-of-custody documentation. Laboratory QA/QC per method includes one method blank per ten samples, one duplicate per ten samples, and one spiked sample per ten samples.
- **Laboratory analyses**: 
  - Title 22 total and dissolved metals (including strontium) via EPA Method 6020/7470.
  - Nitrate as nitrogen and sulfate via EPA Method 300.0.
  - Sulfide via Standard Method 4500.
  - Total suspended solids via Standard Method 2540D.
- **Datasets and evaluation**: No new data are presented; this is a plan for future data collection and reporting. Previous data (from Shaw, 2006) are referenced: seven runoff samples analyzed for dissolved metals, PAHs, nitrate, sulfate, and sulfide.

## Results
- **Previous findings (Shaw, 2006)**: No runoff observed migrating away from Caltrans right-of-way. Of seven samples, only one (SW03 from Stockpile 3) exceeded an MCL: barium at 2,000 µg/l (MCL = 1,000 µg/l). Other six samples had barium concentrations from 16 to 190 µg/l. No other target analytes exceeded MCLs or background levels.
- **No new quantitative results are provided**; the addendum is a plan, not a results report.

## Limitations
- The planned sampling focuses on puddles/pooled water, not necessarily direct runoff from stockpiles.
- Samples may not be the direct result of runoff from Stockpile 2, but rather from soil that has migrated to the Emerald Avenue shoulder.
- The plan notes the possibility of collecting additional samples if flowing runoff is observed, but this is not guaranteed.
- Sampling is limited to up to five samples across up to four events, which may not capture all potential migration scenarios.
- The previous study (Shaw, 2006) noted an isolated elevated barium concentration, but no assessment of long-term trends or seasonal variability was provided.
