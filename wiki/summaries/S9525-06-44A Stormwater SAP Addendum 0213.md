## Key claims
- This addendum proposes a surface water sampling plan for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California, to determine whether stormwater runoff is migrating beyond Caltrans right-of-way.
- The goal is to assess whether any runoff leaving the right-of-way has been impacted by chemicals of potential concern present in the stockpiles.
- The sampling program is designed to follow protocols approved by the California Environmental Protection Agency, Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), consistent with the earlier January 2006 Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan.
- The site history indicates the stockpiles originated from excavation of contaminated soil associated with former FMC/Barium Products operations and an evaporation/disposal pond area.
- Prior sampling in 2006 found no runoff migration beyond the right-of-way, except for one elevated barium result at Stockpile 3 that exceeded the California MCL.

## Methodology
- Field sampling plan:
  - Collect up to 5 surface water samples that have migrated beyond the right-of-way boundary.
  - Sampling may occur over up to 4 rainfall events.
  - Collect rainwater puddle samples along the east and west shoulders of Emerald Avenue between Stockpiles 1 and 2, since water there may contact migrated soil from Stockpile 2.
  - Collect at least 1 background rainwater sample along Emerald Avenue north or south of Stockpiles 1 and 2.
  - If runoff is observed leaving the right-of-way during sampling, additional samples may be collected at those locations.
- Sample handling:
  - Use pre-cleaned disposable scoops.
  - Transfer samples into laboratory-provided containers.
  - For dissolved metals, filter in the field through a 0.45-micron filter.
  - Cap, label, chill, and transport samples under chain-of-custody to Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL).
- Field measurements:
  - Monitor pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, and turbidity.
- Laboratory analyses:
  - Title 22 total and dissolved metals, including strontium, using EPA Method 6020/7470.
  - Nitrate as nitrogen and sulfate using EPA Method 300.0.
  - Sulfide using Standard Method 4500.
  - Total suspended solids (TSS) using Standard Method 2540D.
- QA/QC:
  - One method blank per 10 samples/batch/matrix.
  - One duplicate per 10 samples/batch/matrix.
  - One matrix spike per 10 samples/batch/matrix, spiked at 10× detection limit or analyte level.
- Outputs:
  - A summary report documenting background, scope, field observations, and laboratory results.

## Results
- This document is a proposed sampling addendum, so it contains no new analytical results.
- It reports prior 2006 stormwater sampling results:
  - 7 runoff samples were collected from constructed impoundments during a qualifying rain event.
  - No runoff migrating away from the Caltrans right-of-way was observed.
  - Target analytes were generally below California MCLs or site background levels.
  - Barium was the only exception: sample SW03 from the northwestern side of Stockpile 3 had 2,000 µg/L, exceeding the 1,000 µg/L MCL.
  - The other six samples had barium concentrations ranging from 16 to 190 µg/L.
  - Shaw concluded the elevated barium was isolated and runoff was confined to the right-of-way.

## Limitations
- The addendum describes planned sampling only; results depend on future rainfall events and field conditions.
- Sampling may be limited to up to 5 samples over up to 4 rain events, so coverage is necessarily limited.
- The document notes that water in the Emerald Avenue shoulders may not be direct runoff from Stockpile 2, but could still be influenced by migrated soil; this introduces some interpretive ambiguity.
- Prior conclusions about runoff confinement were based on a single elevated barium result and a limited number of samples.
- No quantitative statistical analysis or trend analysis is presented in the document itself.
