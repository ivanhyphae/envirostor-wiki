## Key claims
The paper is an addendum to a Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP) that outlines proposed sampling activities to evaluate whether stormwater runoff from Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Stockpiles 1–3) is migrating beyond Caltrans’ right-of-way and, if so, whether it has been impacted by chemicals of potential concern (COPCs) from the stockpiles. A previous 2006 sampling event by Shaw Environmental collected seven runoff samples; only one sample (SW03) exceeded the California MCL for barium (2,000 µg/L vs. 1,000 µg/L), and no runoff was observed leaving the right-of-way. The current plan targets up to five samples from water that has migrated beyond the right-of-way, plus a background rainwater sample, collected during up to four separate rainfall events.

## Methodology
- **Field activities**: Up to five surface water samples will be collected from locations where runoff has migrated beyond the Caltrans right-of-way. Samples will also be collected from puddles pooling on Emerald Avenue shoulders (between Stockpiles 1 and 2). At least one background rainwater sample will be collected along Emerald Avenue north or south of Stockpiles 1 and 2. If flowing runoff is observed leaving the right-of-way during sampling events, additional samples may be taken.
- **Sample collection and handling**: Samples collected using pre-cleaned scoops, transferred to laboratory-provided containers. For dissolved metals analysis, samples will be field-filtered through a 0.45-micron filter. Samples will be capped, labeled, chilled, and transported to Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) under chain-of-custody.
- **Field measurements**: pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, and turbidity will be monitored during sampling.
- **Laboratory analyses**:
  - Title 22 total and dissolved metals (including strontium) by EPA Method 6020/7470.
  - Nitrate as nitrogen and sulfate by EPA Method 300.0.
  - Sulfide by Standard Method (SM) 4500.
  - Total suspended solids (TSS) by SM 2540D.
- **QA/QC**: Method blanks (one per 10 samples or batch), duplicate samples (one per 10 samples or batch), and spiked samples (one per 10 samples or batch, spike at 10× detection limit or at analyte level).
- **Report preparation**: A summary report will include background, scope, field observations, and laboratory results.

## Results
No new quantitative results are presented; this is a sampling plan. The only quantitative data referenced are from the 2006 Shaw event: barium in sample SW03 = 2,000 µg/L (exceeding the MCL of 1,000 µg/L); barium in the other six samples ranged from 16 to 190 µg/L. No other analytes exceeded MCLs or site background levels.

## Limitations
- The sampling is contingent on the occurrence of qualifying rainfall events (visible runoff following 72 hours of dry weather) and on migration beyond the right-of-way being observed. The 2006 event did not observe such migration, so samples may be limited.
- Sampling locations are predefined (e.g., Emerald Avenue shoulders) and may not capture all potential migration pathways.
- The addendum notes that puddle samples may not result from direct runoff from Stockpile 2.
- No explicit caveats are given regarding the limited number of samples or spatial/temporal representativeness.
