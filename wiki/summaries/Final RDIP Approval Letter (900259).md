## Key claims
- The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), formally approves the Remedial Design Implementation Plan — Interim Phase 1 (RDIP-Interim Phase 1) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles.
- The RDIP describes the removal and re-deposition of a portion of the Modesto Soil Stockpiles to support the construction of State Route 132 in Stanislaus County.
- The plan includes 100% design plans, safety measures, and sampling protocols to protect human health and the environment during and after construction.
- A companion document, “City of Modesto Book 2 of 3 100% Special Provisions for The State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Phase 1 Project,” was also reviewed and revised to address DTSC comments; the final version is incorporated into the approval.

## Methodology
- DTSC and RWQCB conducted a joint review of the RDIP-Interim Phase 1 and the related Special Provisions document.
- Minor comments on the Special Provisions were provided on March 26, 2019, and a revised version addressing those comments was received on March 28, 2019.
- The review focused on the completeness of the construction activities description, safety measures, and sampling plans for environmental and human health protection.

## Results
- The RDIP-Interim Phase 1 is approved with no additional comments.
- The Special Provisions document, after minor revision, is also considered acceptable as part of the approval.
- The formal approval letter is dated April 3, 2019, and signed by Dean Wright, PG, Project Manager at DTSC.

## Limitations
- No limitations are mentioned in the approval letter. The document is a final approval, so any caveats or limitations would have been addressed during the review process (e.g., the minor comments resolved before final approval).
