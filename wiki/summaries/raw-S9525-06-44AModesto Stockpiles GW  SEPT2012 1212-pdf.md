**Document Identity and Scope**

This document is a groundwater monitoring report prepared by **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** for the **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6**, dated December 2012. It covers a monitoring event conducted on **September 19–20, 2012**, at the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles** site in Stanislaus County, California (south of Kansas Avenue between North Emerald Avenue and State Route 99). The report includes a narrative and two laboratory certificates of analysis from **Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL)**. Samples were collected from monitoring wells **MW‑1 through MW‑10** (with MW‑9 and MW‑10 newly installed in June 2012). The project numbers are Geocon Project No. S9525-06-44A, Task Order No. 44.

**Key Analytical Results and Findings**

- **Primary contaminants of concern (COCs):** **Barium** (47–280 µg/L; threshold 1,000 µg/L), **lead** (all <1.0 µg/L), and **strontium** (220–1,100 µg/L; threshold 4,000 µg/L) were all below applicable water quality thresholds.
- **Other metals:** Concentrations of arsenic, chromium, vanadium, antimony, cobalt, copper, molybdenum, nickel, selenium, and zinc were below MCLs or health advisories. **Manganese** exceeded numeric thresholds in wells **MW‑3** and **MW‑9**.
- **General minerals:** **Nitrate (as nitrogen)** exceeded the Primary MCL of 10 mg/L in wells **MW‑1 (12 mg/L), MW‑5 (22 mg/L), MW‑6 (18 mg/L), MW‑9 (11 mg/L), and MW‑10 (14 mg/L)**. **Total dissolved solids (TDS)** exceeded the Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in **MW‑5 (590 mg/L), MW‑6 (610 mg/L), and MW‑10 (630 mg/L)**. Upgradient well MW‑1 showed 12 mg/L nitrate and 460 mg/L TDS, possibly reflecting natural background.
- **PAHs:** None were detected above practical quantitation limits in any groundwater sample.
- **Groundwater flow:** Direction is consistently southeast with an average gradient of 0.0008.

**Laboratory Data and Quality Assurance**

Laboratory analyses (by ATL) included dissolved metals (EPA 6020), mercury (EPA 7470), semivolatile organic compounds (EPA 8270/SIM), anions (EPA 300.0), alkalinity, TDS, and total sulfide. The two ATL certificates cover sampling on **September 19–20, 2012** (wells MW‑3 through MW‑9 plus equipment blank) and **September 25, 2012** (data for MW‑6 and MW‑10, plus Stiff diagrams for all 10 wells). Some data quality flags were noted (e.g., dilution required for high cation concentrations, blank contamination for strontium, matrix spike recovery outliers), but these were validated by LCS results and do not invalidate the conclusions. Overall data quality was sufficient; no qualification of the reported exceedances was necessary.

**Responsible Parties and Reporting**

- **Caltrans** is the owner/operator.
- **DTSC** and **Central Valley RWQCB** are co-regulators.
- **Geocon Consultants** performed the sampling and reporting.
- **Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL)** provided analytical services.
- **FMC Corporation** is the former owner of the property where the stockpile soils originated.

Monitoring is performed every other month. Purged groundwater was containerized and later disposed. Electronic data were uploaded to the State Water Resources Control Board GeoTracker database on October 9, 2012.

**Summary of Key Claims**

The report concludes that, for this monitoring event, barium, lead, and strontium did not exceed their respective thresholds; manganese exceeded thresholds in MW‑3 and MW‑9; nitrate exceeded the drinking water standard in five wells; and TDS exceeded the secondary standard in three wells. No other metals or PAHs were detected at levels of concern. These findings are consistent with the laboratory data provided.
