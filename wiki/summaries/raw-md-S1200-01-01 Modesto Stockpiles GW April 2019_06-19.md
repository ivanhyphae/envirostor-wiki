## Summary Report: Modesto Soil Stockpiles Groundwater Monitoring (April 2019)

**Document Identity**
This document is a Groundwater Monitoring Report prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. for the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6 Hazardous Waste Branch, with oversight from the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC). The report covers field activities and analytical results from the October 2018 and April 2019 sampling events, with a report date of June 19, 2019.

**Key Findings and Regulatory Status**
- **Groundwater Flow:** Flow direction is consistently toward the south/southeast.
- **Primary MCL Exceedances:** Nitrate (as nitrogen) consistently exceeded its Primary Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) of 10 mg/L in multiple wells, particularly MW-1, MW-2, MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10.
- **Secondary MCL Exceedances:** Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) consistently exceeded its Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in wells MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10. Manganese exceeded its Secondary MCL sporadically (e.g., well MW-5 at 59 µg/L).
- **Contaminants of Concern (DTSC-identified):** Barium, lead, and strontium were monitored. While barium and strontium concentrations remained significantly below their thresholds, barium was detected across all wells (range: 49–370 µg/L). No other dissolved metals exceeded their numeric thresholds in April 2019.
- **Regulatory Actions:** Analysis for Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons (PAHs) was discontinued after November 2012 with DTSC concurrence. Electronic depth-to-water and analytical data were uploaded to the GeoTracker database on May 7, 2019.

**Analytical Results (April 2019)**
Groundwater samples were analyzed for dissolved Title 22 metals, general minerals (including chloride, nitrate, sulfate, TDS), and field parameters. Key concentrations for targeted contaminants included:
- **Metals:** Barium (49–370 µg/L), Strontium (310–1,300 µg/L), with notable elevated levels of Calcium, Magnesium, and Sodium. Mercury and several other metals were non-detect in all samples.
- **General Minerals:** Nitrate ranged from 2.3 mg/L (MW-3) to 40 mg/L; Sulfate from 8.1 mg/L (MW-3) to 47 mg/L; TDS from 300 mg/L (MW-3) to 760 mg/L (MW-5).
- **Quality Control:** Laboratory data was deemed sufficient for the report's purposes. Quality control notes flagged matrix spike recoveries for Calcium, Copper, Magnesium, and Strontium, but the batches were validated by the Laboratory Control Sample (LCS).

**Site Conditions and Remedial Actions**
- **Well Status:** Monitoring wells MW-9 and MW-10 were dry during the April 2019 event. Well MW-9 was also found without a well plug or lid. Several other wells (MW-5, MW-6, MW-8) have been occasionally dry in recent years. Samples were successfully collected from eleven wells (MW-1 through MW-8, MW-11) during the event.
- **Sampling Method:** Wells were purged and sampled using disposable bailers. Purge water was containerized in a 55-gallon drum for offsite disposal.
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Filed with and split samples collected by the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB).
