## Document Identity

- **Document type and purpose:** Technical Memorandum – workplan for the management, sampling, and disposal/reuse of barium-containing soil (BCS) spoils generated from foundation excavations within the Stockpile 2 BCS Containment Zone at the State Route 132 Project, Modesto, California.
- **Author or preparer:** John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG, Project Manager, Geocon.
- **Date or period covered:** February 28, 2022 (memorandum date). Describes excavations completed in July/August 2021 (Weather Station foundation) and planned future excavation (Overhead Sign #1 foundation).

## Key Claims

- Excavated BCS material from the Weather Station foundation slab/anchor (approx. 2 cubic yards, completed July/August 2021) was spread on the adjacent ground surface.
- A conservative estimate of 40 cubic yards of BCS material will be generated from both foundation excavations and stockpiled prior to disposal or reuse.
- If total barium and lead concentrations do not exceed site-specific background levels (barium 120 mg/kg; lead 3.8 mg/kg), the stockpiled BCS may be suitable for onsite reuse subject to Caltrans and DTSC approval.
- If offsite disposal is required, the material will be profiled for acceptance at Forward Landfill (Manteca) or another facility; covered transport trucks and appropriate licenses/permits will be used.

## Regulatory Decisions

- No formal regulatory decisions are documented in this workplan. The memorandum references the need for Caltrans and DTSC approval for onsite reuse and notes that DTSC provides oversight, with Central Valley RWQCB as co-regulator.

## Analytical Results

- **No analytical results are reported** – this is a workplan for future sampling.
- **Proposed analytical program:**
    - 8 discrete soil samples from the stockpile (grid pattern, various depths) composited into 2 four-part composites.
    - Analysis for Title 22 metals via EPA Method 6010B and EPA Method 7471A (mercury) with 48-hour turnaround.
    - Potential soluble metals testing (WET/TCLP) if elevated total metals indicate possible hazardous waste classification under California or Federal RCRA.
- **Site-specific background concentrations referenced as thresholds:**
    - Barium: 120 mg/kg
    - Lead: 3.8 mg/kg

## Remedial Actions

- **Excavation (completed):** Weather Station Foundation Slab/Anchor – 4.5 ft deep, approx. 2 cy BCS spoils generated (July/August 2021).
- **Excavation (planned):** Overhead Sign #1 – 5 ft diameter × 25 ft deep, approx. 18.2 cy BCS spoils.
- **Stockpile management:** BCS material (total ~40 cy) will be pre-moistened, placed on and covered with minimum 6-mil plastic sheeting, surrounded by erosion controls (straw wattles, hay bales), and secured with ropes/weights.
- **Sampling and analysis:** As described under Analytical Results.
- **Offsite disposal option:** Profile material for Forward Landfill in Manteca; Caltrans representative signs non-hazardous/hazardous waste manifests; transporter licensed; covered loads required.
- **Reporting:** A letter summary report will be prepared after disposition, documenting disturbance locations, volumes, characterization data, manifests, and site photographs. This report will be included in the Removal Action Completion Report.

## Responsible Parties

- **Caltrans** – owner/operator, generator of waste, signatory for manifests, must approve reuse.
- **DTSC** – regulatory oversight, approval authority for reuse.
- **Central Valley RWQCB** – co-regulator.
- **Geocon** – workplan preparer (John E. Juhrend, Project Manager).
- **WSP/City of Modesto** – construction manager (Rick Demi).
- **California Laboratory Services** – analytical laboratory (chain-of-custody protocol).
- **Forward Landfill (Manteca)** – potential disposal facility.

## Key Terms and Conditions

- **Required follow-on actions:**
    - Collect 8 discrete soil samples from stockpile; composite into 2 samples for Title 22 metals analysis (48-hour TAT).
    - If potential hazardous waste, perform WET/TCLP.
    - Compare total barium and lead to site‑specific background (Ba 120 mg/kg, Pb 3.8 mg/kg).
    - Obtain Caltrans and DTSC approval before any onsite reuse.
    - If reuse not approved, profile material for landfill disposal; use authorized transporter and covered trucks.
    - Prepare letter summary report and include in Removal Action Completion Report.
- **Monitoring obligations:** Chain-of-custody documentation; stockpile cover maintenance (plastic sheeting, erosion controls) until final disposition.
- **Restrictions:** BCS must remain within Stockpile 2 Containment Zone until approval; no reuse outside approved location; covered loads required for transport.
