- **Claim**: Composite soil sample results are consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations and suitable as clean cover material for capping Stockpiles 1 and 2.
  - **Who**: Dean Wright (DTSC) in approval email; supported by Geocon’s comparative data analysis.

- **Claim**: All reported chromium values (3.5–11 mg/kg) are orders of magnitude below land use screening levels and not associated with contamination.
  - **Who**: John Juhrend (Geocon) in June 25 email.
  - **Supported by data in this document**: Yes – comparative data and UC Davis background (23 mg/kg) provided.

  - **Who**: Initially noted by DTSC and Water Board (Steven Meeks); then addressed by Juhrend’s footnote.
  - **Conditional**: Initially a concern; resolved after additional information and inclusion of a footnote in the revised memo.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units**: Chromium (total) in soil.
  - Basin 5 Borrow Area (0–10 ft): 6 samples, 8.5–11 mg/kg
  - SR 132 ROW Borrow Area East of Carpenter (0–10 ft): 8 samples, 7.5–11 mg/kg
  - Combined 95% UCL for the two borrow areas (14 samples): 9.9 mg/kg
  - West of Stockpile 1 (Shaw data, 0–15 ft): 24 samples, 3.5–10 mg/kg; 95% UCL all data = 7.1 mg/kg; 95% UCL for eight 5‑ft samples = 8.6 mg/kg
- **Applicable thresholds**: UC Davis background CA soil report: 23 mg/kg (low background); land use screening levels (not specified in document) – values stated as “orders of magnitude below screening levels.”
- **Which wells or locations exceeded thresholds and by how much**: All Basin 5 chromium results exceeded the 95% UCL value, but the footnote explained that they are consistent with background and not a contamination impact.
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations affecting interpretation**: None noted; the concern about all samples exceeding the 95% UCL was addressed as a statistical artifact of consistently low background concentrations.
