## Combined Summary: Groundwater Monitoring Report – Modesto Stockpiles (March 2012)

**Document Identity**  
The document is a **groundwater monitoring report** prepared by **GEOCON Consultants, Inc.** for the **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6**. It covers field activities conducted on **March 12, 2012**, and was dated **June 29, 2012**, under **Project No. S9525-06-44, Task Order No. 44, EA 10-403500**. Laboratory analyses were performed by **ATL Global** and **Excelchem Environmental Labs**, with split samples provided to the **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)**.

**Site and Scope**  
The site is the **Modesto Stockpiles** located along **SR 99 and SR 132 in Stanislaus County, California**. The report addresses **groundwater** quality at eight **2-inch diameter monitoring wells (MW‑1 through MW‑8)** placed adjacent to three soil stockpiles. A Stiff diagram appendix compares major‑ion hydrochemistry between 2006 and 2012 sampling events, showing calcium‑dominant and sodium‑dominant facies across the wells.

**Key Analytical Findings**  
- **PAHs**: None detected above the practical quantitation limit (PQL 0.20 µg/L) in any well.  
- **Dissolved Barium**: Detected in all wells (range 39–340 µg/L), all well below the MCL of 1,000 µg/L. Highest concentration (340 µg/L) at MW‑5.  
- **Dissolved Strontium**: Up to 1,200 µg/L, below the secondary MCL of 5,000 µg/L.  
- **Dissolved Mercury**: Detected in several wells (0.23–0.41 µg/L) but results considered **estimated** due to similar concentration in the equipment blank.  
- **Chromium, Nickel, Vanadium**: Detected at low levels below respective MCLs.  
- **Manganese**: Exceeded the **secondary MCL (50 µg/L)** in MW‑4 (88 µg/L).  
- **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)**: Exceeded the secondary MCL (500 mg/L) at MW‑1 (550 mg/L) and MW‑5 (700 mg/L).  
- **Nitrate (as N)**: Exceeded the MCL (10 mg/L) at MW‑5 (27 mg/L).  
- **General Minerals**: Chloride, sulfate, and alkalinity were below applicable MCLs.

**Regulatory Context and Data Quality**  
No regulatory decisions were made in this monitoring report; it serves as a data‑only submission. Laboratory data were qualified with flags such as **“U”** (non‑detect due to blank contamination), **“J”** (estimated value below reporting limit), **“B”** (potential lab blank contamination for chromium), and **“R”** (RPD outside acceptance criteria for mercury). Split samples analyzed by the CVRWQCB showed generally consistent results. A laboratory error was noted for lead in split samples.

**Responsible Parties**  
- **Owner/Operator**: Caltrans  
- **Regulators**: DTSC (oversight) and Central Valley RWQCB (co‑regulator)  
- **Consultant**: GEOCON Consultants, Inc.  
- **Laboratories**: ATL Global, Excelchem Environmental Labs (historical data from Shaw Environmental, 2007)

**Summary**  
Overall, the March 2012 groundwater monitoring event at the Modesto Stockpiles found no PAHs, barium remained well below the MCL, and most metals were below regulatory thresholds. Exceedances of secondary MCLs for TDS, manganese, and nitrate (at MW‑5) were noted, along with estimated mercury data due to blank contamination. The report documents field protocols, analytical results, and data qualifiers, serving as a routine compliance monitoring submittal.
