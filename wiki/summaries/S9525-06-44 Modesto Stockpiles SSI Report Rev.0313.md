The supplemental site investigation (SSI) for the Modesto Stockpiles project (S9525-06-44), associated with the SR132 West Freeway/Expressway Project in Modesto, was conducted to close remaining data gaps for three Caltrans soil stockpiles—especially regarding lateral and vertical migration of contaminants and previously reported cadmium hotspots. Fieldwork took place September 25–28, 2012 and included 68 borings: 35 fenceline borings to evaluate offsite/vertical migration, 28 perimeter borings to define stockpile boundaries, and 5 borings targeted at historical cadmium anomalies. Samples were collected by direct-push and hand auger where needed and analyzed by ATL Global using EPA methods for metals, mercury, GRO/DRO/ORO, and PAHs, with a full QA/QC program including blanks, duplicates, spikes, chain-of-custody, and validation. The results were compared to CHHSLs, ESLs, RSLs, and hazardous waste thresholds to support an updated human health risk assessment (HHRA) and remedial action plan (RAP).

Overall, the investigation found that the stockpiled soils are enriched in metals, most notably barium, but the data do not show offsite migration beyond the stockpile perimeters/fencelines. Historical aerial imagery supports that placement of the barium-impacted soils occurred within the Caltrans right-of-way, consistent with the project history tied to FMC evaporation pond tailings. Barium was elevated in many stockpile and fenceline samples, with some very high concentrations in solid samples (in the million-ug/kg range), and other metals such as zinc, lead, chromium, copper, nickel, vanadium, cobalt, and arsenic were also present at elevated levels in parts of the stockpiles. However, the fenceline data indicated that while some barium exceeded background, concentrations remained below the residential CHHSL and did not indicate lateral or vertical migration beyond the stockpile edges. Groundwater was not encountered during the field program, further limiting evidence for downward migration.

Cadmium was a specific concern because it had been reported in 2004, but the 2012 SSI sampling did not reproduce those detections, and the report concludes cadmium is not a constituent of concern for the site. Mercury was consistently not detected in the solid samples shown, and water samples from decontamination water showed only low dissolved metals with mercury not detected. The laboratory certificates of analysis and QC summaries support the validity of the dataset overall: most analytical results met acceptance criteria, though some matrix spike and duplicate issues were noted for certain analytes and batches. Taken together, the document supports the conclusion that the principal issue at the Modesto Stockpiles is barium-impacted soil contained within the Caltrans ROW, with limited evidence of contaminant migration and no confirmed cadmium concern, providing the analytical basis for updated HHRA and RAP development.
