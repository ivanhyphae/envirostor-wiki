Based on the six section summaries provided, here is a combined coherent summary of the source document "raw/S9525-06-44 Modesto Stockpiles SSI Report Rev.0313.pdf".

The document presents the findings of a supplemental site investigation (SSI) and associated laboratory analysis for the "Modesto Stockpiles, S9525-06-44" project. The primary conclusion of the SSI is that three soil stockpiles, containing approximately 160,000 cubic yards of material from former FMC evaporation ponds, pose no significant threat of contaminant migration. Barium and other metals present in the stockpiles did not show lateral or vertical migration exceeding California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs) for residential or industrial use at the stockpile perimeters. Consequently, the study enabled the preparation of a Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the SR132 Project.

The investigation was conducted between September 25 and October 3, 2012, using direct-push and hand-auger techniques to collect 68 soil borings from fenceline, perimeter, and deep-cadmium locations. A subsequent section of the report provides the full laboratory analytical data (a Certificate of Analysis) for these samples. All samples were analyzed for total metals using EPA methods 6020 (ICP-MS), 6010B (ICP-AES), and 7471 (Cold Vapor AA) following EPA 3050B acid digestion. Select samples were also analyzed for organic compounds (GRO, DRO, ORO, SVOCs) using EPA methods 8015B and 8270/SIM. A significant portion of the document is dedicated to detailed quality control (QC) data, validating the analytical methods. This QC data shows that most parameters (blanks, laboratory control samples, duplicates) met acceptance criteria, although matrix interference from the soil samples sometimes caused matrix spike recoveries and relative percent differences (RPDs) to fall outside acceptable limits, a known limitation in complex soil matrices.

The key analytical results confirmed that barium was the primary metal of concern, with concentrations varying by location and depth, ranging from as low as 42 mg/kg to as high as 130,000 mg/kg within the stockpile cores. Critically, all barium concentrations at the fenceline and perimeter were below the CHHSL thresholds. The 2004 finding of elevated cadmium was definitively ruled out; all 348 new samples analyzed in this study, including those from deep borings targeting the original detections, showed cadmium below 1.0 mg/kg. The organic compound analysis of one sample (CB3-9.0) showed a slight exceedance of an Environmental Screening Level (ESL) for Diesel Range Organics (DRO) at 120 mg/kg (ESL: 83 mg/kg), but all other detections were well below ESLs. The report acknowledges it is not a comprehensive site characterization and is based on a limited sampling event; the data is valid only as of the report date and is not necessarily representative of temporal variability.
