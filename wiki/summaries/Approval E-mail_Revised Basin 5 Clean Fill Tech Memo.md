## Key claims
- The Revised Technical Memorandum for Basin 5 concludes that excavated soil from the Basin 5 borrow area is suitable as clean cover/cap material for capping Stockpiles 1 and 2 at the SR 132 project.
- Chromium concentrations in Basin 5 soil are consistent with naturally occurring background levels rather than site contamination.
- DTSC and the Water Board ultimately accepted the revised memo after additional explanatory information was provided, including a chromium footnote.

## Methodology
- The memorandum compiles and compares soil analytical data from multiple borrow areas and reference datasets:
  - Basin 5 borrow area (0–10 ft depth), 6 samples
  - SR 132 ROW borrow area east of Carpenter (0–10 ft), 8 samples
  - Shaw data west of Stockpile 1 (5, 10, and 15 ft), 24 samples
- The analysis focuses on total chromium concentrations and compares them against:
  - the 95% upper confidence limit (95% UCL) for the sampled datasets
  - previously approved clean cover fill data
  - a UC Davis California soil background chromium value
  - land-use screening levels
- DTSC requested additional rationale, including consideration of DIWET (not WET) leachability testing for total and hexavalent chromium and comparison to groundwater/surface-water background values, with leaching modeling if needed.
- No formal experimental design is described beyond comparative environmental sample analysis and regulatory review.

## Results
- Basin 5 borrow area chromium concentrations: 8.5–11 mg/kg from 6 samples.
- Adjacent approved SR 132 ROW borrow area chromium concentrations: 7.5–11 mg/kg from 8 samples.
- Combined 14-sample borrow area 95% UCL: 9.9 mg/kg.
- Shaw data west of Stockpile 1: 24 samples with chromium ranging from 3.5–10 mg/kg.
- Shaw dataset 95% UCL for all data: 7.1 mg/kg.
- Shaw 95% UCL for eight 5-foot-deep samples: 8.6 mg/kg.
- All reported chromium values (3.5–11 mg/kg) were described as very low and orders of magnitude below land-use screening levels.
- The memo cites a UC Davis California soil report with a low background chromium concentration of 23 mg/kg, above the reported Basin 5 values.
- Final outcome: DTSC stated the composite sample results were consistent with naturally occurring background and suitable for clean cover material, and formally accepted the revised memo.

## Limitations
- DTSC noted concern that all chromium sample results exceeded the 95% UCL value, requiring additional explanation.
- The agencies requested stronger defensible rationale because of the project’s high public sensitivity.
- DTSC suggested DIWET leachability testing and possible leaching-model evaluation if exceedances were found.
- The source material is an email approval chain rather than a full technical report, so methods and raw datasets are summarized only indirectly.
