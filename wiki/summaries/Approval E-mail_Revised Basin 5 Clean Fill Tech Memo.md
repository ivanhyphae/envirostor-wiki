## Key claims
- Composite soil samples from the Basin 5 borrow area are consistent with naturally occurring background chromium concentrations and are suitable as clean cover material for capping Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- All measured chromium values (3.5–11 mg/kg) are orders of magnitude below land use screening levels and the widely referenced UC Davis background value of 23 mg/kg.
- The observation that every Basin 5 chromium result exceeded the 95% UCL is explained by the shallow sample depths (0–10 ft composite) compared to deeper discrete samples, and does not indicate contamination.

## Methodology
- Soil characterization based on six composite samples from Basin 5 (0–10 ft depth) and eight samples from the adjacent State Route 132 right-of-way borrow area (0–10 ft). Additional comparison data from 24 samples west of Stockpile 1 (Shaw dataset) at depths of 5, 10, and 15 ft.
- Statistical calculation of the 95% upper confidence limit (UCL) for total chromium across the combined borrow area datasets (14 samples: 9.9 mg/kg) and the Shaw dataset (7.1 mg/kg for all depths; 8.6 mg/kg for 5 ft depth samples).
- Results were compared to background soil chromium concentrations reported in the UC Davis California background soil report.
- No leachability testing (DIWET) was performed; the rationale relied on comparison to background and screening levels.

## Results
- Basin 5 chromium range: 8.5–11 mg/kg. Adjacent SR 132 ROW: 7.5–11 mg/kg. Combined 14-sample 95% UCL: 9.9 mg/kg.
- Shaw dataset (west of Stockpile 1): range 3.5–10 mg/kg; 95% UCL 7.1 mg/kg (all depths) and 8.6 mg/kg (5 ft depth samples only).
- All values are below the UC Davis background of 23 mg/kg and are considered naturally occurring. The 95% UCL for the combined dataset (9.9 mg/kg) is exceeded by individual Basin 5 samples because the composite samples are from shallower depths where chromium is naturally higher; deeper discrete samples (10–15 ft) showed the lowest values (3.5–6.6 mg/kg).

## Limitations
- All chromium results in the Basin 5 dataset exceeded the calculated 95% UCL, raising initial regulatory concern about consistency with the approved Remedial Action Plan/Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RAP/RDIP).
- The limitation was addressed by adding a footnote to the tech memo explaining that the exceedance is due to sample depth (shallow composites vs. deeper discrete samples) and that all concentrations are within natural background variability.
- No additional leachability testing (DIWET for total and hexavalent chromium) was conducted, though initially requested by the Water Board; the rationale was accepted based on comparison to background and screening levels.
