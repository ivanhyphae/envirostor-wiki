## Key claims
- The soil from the Basin 5 borrow area is suitable for use as clean cover material for capping State Route 132 stockpiles (Stockpiles 1 and 2) because the measured chromium concentrations are consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations.
- All composite soil sample results meet the criteria for clean fill as defined in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) and are acceptable to the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the California Regional Water Quality Control Board (Water Board).
- The Revised Technical Memorandum (dated July 2, 2020), which includes a footnote explaining the chromium values, receives formal acceptance from DTSC.

## Methodology
- **Sample collection:** Six composite soil samples were collected from the Basin 5 borrow area at depths of 0–10 feet. Eight composite samples were collected from the adjacent SR 132 right-of-way (ROW) borrow area (0–10 feet) as a reference. Additionally, 24 discrete or composite samples were collected from a location west of Stockpile 1 (Shaw property) at depths of 5, 10, and 15 feet.
- **Analytical measurement:** Total chromium concentration was measured in all samples.
- **Background comparison:** Results were compared to the 95% Upper Confidence Limit (UCL) of the combined data set, to the California background soil study (UC Davis report, background chromium = 23 mg/kg), and to land‑use screening levels.
- **Regulatory review:** The data were reviewed by DTSC and the Water Board following the protocols established in the RDIP. An initial concern about all chromium values exceeding the 95% UCL was addressed by providing additional explanatory context in a revised memo.

## Results
- **SR 132 ROW borrow area (0–10 ft):** 8 samples, chromium range 7.5–11 mg/kg (previously approved for clean cover fill).
- **Basin 5 borrow area (0–10 ft):** 6 samples, chromium range 8.5–11 mg/kg (essentially identical to adjacent ROW data).
- **Combined 95% UCL for both borrow areas (14 samples):** 9.9 mg/kg.
- **Shaw property (west of Stockpile 1, all depths):** 24 samples, chromium range 3.5–10 mg/kg; 95% UCL for all data = 7.1 mg/kg; 95% UCL for eight 5‑ft‑deep samples = 8.6 mg/kg.
- **Background reference (UC Davis):** 23 mg/kg.
- All reported chromium values (3.5–11 mg/kg) are orders of magnitude below applicable land‑use screening levels and are consistent with native soil background.

## Limitations
- The initial concern arose because every chromium sample from Basin 5 exceeded the 95% UCL value, which required additional justification.
- The lower chromium values in the Shaw data set (3.5–6.6 mg/kg) were from discrete samples at depths of 10 and 15 feet; the Basin 5 composites (0–2, 2–5, and 5–10 ft) do not include deeper intervals and thus show slightly higher concentrations.
- Only total chromium was reported; no leachability tests (e.g., DIWET) were conducted in the final characterization, although leachability evaluation was initially suggested by the Water Board.
- The approval is based on composite samples; discrete sample variability within the basin is not fully characterized.
- The analysis relies on a limited number of samples (6 from Basin 5) for spatial representativeness.
