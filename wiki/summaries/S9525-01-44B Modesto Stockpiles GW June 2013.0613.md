## Combined Summary of the June 2013 Groundwater Monitoring Report  
### Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Stanislaus County, CA)

**Overview and Key Claims**  
This report presents groundwater quality data from ten monitoring wells (MW‑1 through MW‑10) installed in the shallow zone of the upper aquifer (Modesto Formation) at three soil stockpiles. The primary chemicals of concern (COCs) identified by DTSC are barium, lead, and strontium. For the June 2013 sampling event, no dissolved metals exceeded California Primary or Secondary MCLs or EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories. However, nitrate‑N exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in wells MW‑1 (12 mg/L), MW‑5 (17 mg/L), MW‑6 (20 mg/L), MW‑9 (12 mg/L), and MW‑10 (20 mg/L). Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in wells MW‑1 (520 mg/L), MW‑5 (530 mg/L), MW‑6 (620 mg/L), and MW‑10 (620 mg/L). Well MW‑1 is an upgradient well; its elevated nitrate and TDS may reflect natural background levels. Groundwater flow is toward the east‑southeast at an average hydraulic gradient of 0.0005, consistent with historical data. Ionic composition is bicarbonate‑dominant; wells MW‑1, MW‑2, MW‑4, MW‑5, MW‑7, MW‑9, and MW‑10 are calcium‑dominant, while MW‑3, MW‑6, and MW‑8 are sodium‑dominant.

**Methodology**  
- **Field activities (June 5‑6, 2013):** Depth to water was measured from surveyed top‑of‑casing references. Wells were purged using submersible pumps or disposable bailers until ~3 well volumes were removed or the well went dry (MW‑5, MW‑6). Samples were collected with disposable bailers and decanted into laboratory containers; dissolved metals samples were field‑filtered (0.45 µm). Field parameters (pH, EC, temperature, turbidity, DO, ORP) were recorded during purging.  
- **Laboratory analysis:** Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) performed analyses under project S9525‑01‑44B. Methods included EPA 6020/7470 for dissolved metals (including mercury), EPA 300.0 for anions (chloride, nitrate‑N, sulfate), SM 2320B for alkalinity, SM 2540C for TDS, and SM 4500‑S²⁻ for sulfide. Stiff diagrams were generated for each well.  
- **Quality control:** Batch‑specific blanks, LCS, MS/MSD, and duplicates were employed. Acceptance criteria: LCS 85–115% (metals) / 80–120% (others); MS 70–130%; RPD ≤ 20%. A field equipment blank detected nickel at 1.8 µg/L but did not significantly affect results. Some matrix spike recoveries fell outside control limits due to high native analyte concentrations; data were validated by LCS.

**Results**  
- **Dissolved metals (range across wells):** Barium 45–230 µg/L (MCL 1,000), strontium 250–1,000 µg/L (health advisory 4,000), arsenic 2.0–4.8 µg/L (MCL 10), chromium 0.77–7.5 µg/L (MCL 50), vanadium 17–35 µg/L (notification level 50).  
- **General minerals:** Nitrate‑N ranged from 3.3 mg/L (MW‑3) to 20 mg/L (MW‑6,‑10); TDS ranged from 340 mg/L (MW‑7) to 620 mg/L (MW‑6,‑10).  
- **Example well data (MW‑1, MW‑2):**  
  - MW‑1: TDS 280 mg/L; major cations (Ca 82, Mg 48, Na 110, K 3.7 mg/L); anions (Cl 21, SO₄ 57, HCO₃ 220 mg/L); dissolved metals included As 1.5, Ba 55, Sr 660 µg/L.  
  - MW‑2: TDS 350 mg/L; cations (Ca 48, Mg 18, Na 38, K 2.3 mg/L); anions (Cl 11, SO₄ 11, HCO₃ 230 mg/L); dissolved metals included As 2.5, Ba 84, Sr 620 µg/L.  
  Cation‑anion balance for both wells was equal (5.6 meq/L), confirming good ion balance.  
- **Quality control performance:** LCS recoveries were within limits (metals 96–102%). Mercury LCS was slightly below the lower bound (82.6% vs. 80%) but still considered acceptable. Duplicate RPDs were generally ≤ 10%.

**Limitations**  
- **Field:** Low‑yielding wells MW‑5 and MW‑6 went dry during purging, requiring multiple recovery cycles and modified protocols (including surge‑block redevelopment for MW‑5), which may affect sample representativeness.  
- **Laboratory:** Several major cations (Ca, Mg, Na, K) and strontium in MW‑1 and MW‑2 required dilution (D6), raising practical quantitation limits. Matrix spike recoveries for Ca, Mg, Na, K, Sr, V, and Zn exceeded 70–130% due to high native concentrations (noted M1), but were validated by LCS. Mercury batch showed slightly low LCS recovery. PAH analysis was discontinued after November 2012 due to consistent non‑detects (DTSC concurrence).
