The document is a Stantec-prepared surface water inspection and site documentation package for Caltrans’ Modesto Soil Stockpiles project in Stanislaus County under Contract 06A2542, Task Order 97. It summarizes a February 28, 2023 field event conducted in general accordance with the DTSC-approved Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan and later addendum, with a focus on visually assessing drainage features, stockpiles, runoff areas, and erosion-control measures after a minor rain event.

Because there was no standing surface water at the time of inspection, no surface water samples were collected. The fieldwork instead documented site conditions through inspection and photographs at locations including BG1, BG3, PL6, PL7, Basin 5, and Stockpiles 1 and 2. The sampling network had been revised from earlier configurations following construction changes associated with SR 132 completion, including removal of BG2, addition of BG3, and use of PL6 and PL7 as newer runoff points while older PL1–PL5 locations became obsolete.

Observed conditions showed no visible discharge or runoff beyond the project area or BMP controls. However, the inspection identified minor issues such as trash and debris blocking one discharge pipe at PL7, localized erosion on the east side of Basin 5 and along the north side of Stockpile 1, and some hydroseeded or bare areas on Stockpile 2 lacking vegetation. BMPs such as straw waddles and jute netting were present in improved areas. Overall, the report concludes that no active surface water discharge was observed, and the appendix provides a photographic record of these conditions on 2/28/2023.
