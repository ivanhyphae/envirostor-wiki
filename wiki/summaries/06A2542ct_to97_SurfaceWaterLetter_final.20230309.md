## Key claims
This report documents the February 28, 2023 surface water inspection at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project. The main finding is that due to limited rainfall (0.09 inches) on the morning of the inspection, no surface water samples were collected—there was no standing surface water, active runoff, or discharge observed at any of the designated sampling locations. This represents a null event for surface water monitoring. The report also documents updated sampling locations (addition of PL6, PL7, and BG3; removal of BG2) following site construction completion (SR 132 opened to traffic November 2022). No surface water migration beyond Best Management Practices or the Caltrans right-of-way was observed. Minor soil erosion was noted at the east side of stormwater basin 5 and on the north slope of Stockpile 1; some hydroseeded areas on Stockpile 2 lacked vegetation.

## Methodology
The inspection followed protocols from the Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (Shaw Environmental, Inc., January 2006) and the Addendum to Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (Geocon Consultants, Inc., February 2013). A site walk was performed November 2, 2022 to revise sampling locations: BG2 was removed, BG3 added (east side of North Carpenter Road), and surface runoff locations PL6 (north side of SR 132 near SR 99) and PL7 (north side of SR 132 at stormwater basin 5 discharge pipe) were added. The inspection was conducted February 28, 2023 at approximately 8:45 am. Precipitation measured 0.09 inches. Activities included visual inspection of background locations (BG1, BG3), surface water sampling locations (PL6, PL7), stormwater basin 5, road embankment along north side of SR 132, and Stockpile 1 and 2 slopes. A photographic record was compiled. No samples were collected; the inspection was limited to site observation due to insufficient rainfall.

## Results
- Precipitation measured: 0.09 inches on February 28, 2023.
- No surface water samples collected.
- BG1 and BG3: no visible water ponding.
- PL6: no active runoff from stormwater pipe.
- PL7: trash/debris blocking discharge pipe; no active runoff.
- No visible discharges or runoff of surface water throughout the project area.
- Soil erosion observed on east side of stormwater basin 5.
- Minor erosion observed at several locations on north side of Stockpile 1.
- Some hydroseeded areas on north side of Stockpile 2 lacked vegetation.
- Straw waddles and jute mesh used as Best Management Practices observed in areas where improvements occurred.

## Limitations
- No surface water samples were collected due to insufficient rainfall (only 0.09 inches), so no analytical data were obtained.
- The report notes that opinions are based on conditions existing at the time of the document and do not account for subsequent changes.
