## Combined Summary of Source Document

This summary integrates information from two separate section summaries of the document titled "raw/06A2542ct_to97_SurfaceWaterLetter_final.20230309.pdf", which describes a surface water inspection conducted on February 28, 2023, at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project (Contract #06A2542, Task Order 97) in Stanislaus County, California.

**Methodology:** The inspection followed protocols approved by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) as established in the Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (January 2006) and its Addendum (February 2013). Due to insufficient rainfall for sample collection, the survey consisted of a qualitative visual assessment and photographic documentation of designated background stations (BG1, BG3), stormwater Basin 5, two soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1 and 2), and discharge points PL6 and PL7. Twelve photographs were taken with directional orientation and location identifiers. No quantitative evaluation metrics were applicable because no water samples were collected.

**Key Findings and Results:**  
- Only 0.09 inches of precipitation was measured on February 28, 2023. No standing surface water or active runoff was observed at any sampled location, including BG1, BG3, PL6, and PL7.  
- Erosion was noted on the east side of Basin 5 and minor erosion on the northern slope of Stockpile 1.  
- Hydroseeded areas on Stockpile 2 lacked vegetation, while jute netting was observed on its northern slope.  
- Trash and debris blocked the discharge pipe near PL7 in Basin 5.  
- No visible discharges or runoff beyond best management practices (BMPs) were observed.
