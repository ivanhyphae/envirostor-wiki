## Site and scope
- **Site name & location:** Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route (SR) 99 and 132, Modesto, California (Stanislaus County)
- **Areas of concern:** Soil stockpiles consisting of excess native soils and pond tailings generated during 1960s SR 99 construction north of Kansas Avenue; the soils originated from a 4.3-acre parcel formerly owned by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC), which included an evaporation pond. Primary chemicals of concern (COCs): barium, lead, and strontium.
- **Media addressed:** Groundwater (monitoring wells sampled March 12, 2012; reference to previous sampling events October 5, 2006 and June 14, 2006; wells installed in 2006)
- **Specific monitoring wells / sample locations:** Not identified by well ID in this letter; only referenced as "monitoring wells" from which split water samples were collected during the March 2012 event.

## Key claims
- **Claim:** DTSC concurs with the Groundwater Monitoring Report dated May 2012 (prepared by Geocon Consultants).
  - **Who is making it:** DTSC (Randy S. Adams, C.E.G.)
  - **Supported by data in this document?** Yes – DTSC states they reviewed the report in consultation with RWQCB. The letter does not present the underlying monitoring data, but indicates acceptance of the report findings.
- **No other claims about site conditions, compliance, or remedial adequacy are made in this letter.**

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type:** Conditional acceptance (concurrence with report plus requirements for future reports).
- **Deciding agency and date:** DTSC, in consultation with Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region (RWQCB); June 27, 2012.
- **Conditions or requirements imposed:**
  1. Future groundwater monitoring reports must include a discussion comparing groundwater monitoring results and concentration trends from previous sampling events as more data become available.
  2. Graphs and maps (including “Stiff” diagrams) must be prepared depicting concentration trends of primary COCs over time; all graphics must include a legend defining symbols/colors.
  3. At a minimum, groundwater monitoring should continue on a quarterly schedule.
- **Documents approved or acted on:** "Groundwater Monitoring Report, California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) Modesto Soil Stockpiles – State Route 99 and 132, Modesto, California", prepared by Geocon Consultants, dated May 2012 (covering March 12, 2012 sampling event, with reference to 2006 baseline data).
