This technical memorandum, prepared by John E. Juhrend of Geocon Consultants for the Caltrans State Route 132 Project, summarizes barium-containing soil (BCS) removal verification testing at Stockpile 2 in Modesto, CA, conducted from February 21 to March 6, 2020. The removal activities were performed under the January 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP). Verification testing was conducted by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) using EPA Method 6010B.

At the west end of Stockpile 2, initial excavation to approximately 82 feet MSL was deepened to approximately 81 feet MSL at two locations based on results. Final verification samples showed barium concentrations ranging from 54 to 140 mg/kg and lead from 1.5 to 4.2 mg/kg. At the east end, excavation progressed from approximately 83 feet MSL down to approximately 81 feet MSL in stages. Final verification samples showed barium ranging from 63 to 760 mg/kg and lead from 1.5 to 35 mg/kg. A sample from the east end (S2E 8A-0) analyzed on March 6, 2020, reported barium at 63 mg/kg and lead at 1.6 mg/kg. All final excavation samples met the RDIP verification thresholds (barium ≤ 1,000 mg/kg for groundwater protection and lead ≤ 80 mg/kg for residential screening levels), confirming the west and east ends of Stockpile 2 are suitable for placement of clean embankment fill. The removed BCS was placed as embankment fill within designated containment zones in Stockpile 2.

The analytical data is supported by quality control documentation. Blank and Laboratory Control Sample (LCS) recoveries were within acceptable limits for all batches. While a matrix spike for barium in one east-end batch showed a recovery outside acceptable limits (212%–318%), this was attributed to matrix interference, and the batch was validated based on the acceptable LCS. A subsequent batch for the final west-end sample (B0C0152, analyzed March 9, 2020) showed acceptable QC results, including a matrix spike recovery of 112% for barium and 76.8% for lead, with RPD values of 2.39% and 2.08% respectively.

The memorandum concludes that a comprehensive Removal Action Completion Report will be submitted after the stockpile is capped with clean fill. The document also includes blank chain of custody forms used for sample tracking during the removal action.
