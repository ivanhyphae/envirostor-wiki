## Key claims
- Four groundwater monitoring wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10) at the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles site were successfully decommissioned.
- An updated comparative evaluation (2024) confirmed that the encapsulated barium- and lead-impacted soil stockpiles do not impact groundwater and will not do so in the future.
- Based on data consistency with a 2014 evaluation, a declining water table, and planned final encapsulation under SR 132, further groundwater monitoring was deemed unnecessary.
- The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) approved the discontinuation of monitoring and the destruction of the four wells.

## Methodology
- Pre-field activities: Obtained well destruction permits from Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Resources; marked work areas and delineated subsurface utilities via Underground Service Alert; retained a utility locating company; notified Caltrans and the county; hired a C‑57 licensed drilling contractor (PeneCore).
- Field activities (March 18, 2025): Each well was filled to the surface with cement grout via a tremie pipe, and approximately 20 psi of air pressure was applied for 5 minutes to force grout through the well screen into the filter pack. Well boxes and concrete aprons were removed, and each well was overdrilled to 5 ft with a hollow‑stem auger. The overdrilled annulus was filled with cement grout and capped with native soil.
- Well construction details: All wells used 2‑inch‑diameter SCH 40 PVC casing, with screen intervals and filter pack (No. 2/12 sand) specified for each well (e.g., MW4: total depth 42 ft, screen interval 30–40 ft, filter pack interval 26–42 ft).
- Waste materials (casings, pipes, concrete debris) were transported for disposal; California DWR Well Completion Reports were submitted.

## Results
- Wells destroyed: MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10.
- Grout volume used vs. anticipated:
  - MW4: 10 g used vs. 6.0 g anticipated
  - MW6: 15 g used vs. 6.8 g anticipated
  - MW9: 10 g used vs. 6.2 g anticipated
  - MW10: 17 g used vs. 5.7 g anticipated
- Regulatory outcome: DTSC and RWQCB formally agreed that “encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater” and approved well destruction.

## Limitations
- The report is a procedural well‑destruction document and does not discuss statistical or methodological limitations of the underlying groundwater evaluation.
- The updated comparative evaluation (2024) relies on consistency with a 2014 evaluation and a declining water table; no new quantitative groundwater data are presented in this report.
