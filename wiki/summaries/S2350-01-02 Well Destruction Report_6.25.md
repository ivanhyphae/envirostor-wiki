## Key claims
- Geocon destroyed the four remaining groundwater monitoring wells at the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles site in Modesto, California: MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10.
- The rationale for well destruction is that updated groundwater evaluations showed the encapsulated soil stockpiles do not impact groundwater and future monitoring would not provide useful additional information.
- The decision to decommission the wells was supported by consistency with a prior 2014 comparative evaluation, declining groundwater levels, and upcoming full encapsulation of the remaining stockpiles during SR 132 Phase II construction.
- DTSC and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board formally agreed that the encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater and approved destruction of the four wells.

## Methodology
- This is an engineering/environmental field report documenting well decommissioning activities, not a hypothesis-driven experimental study.
- Pre-field work included:
  - obtaining Stanislaus County well destruction permits (MW25-13 and MW25-14),
  - marking the work area,
  - USA utility notifications and utility mapping,
  - hiring a utility locating contractor,
  - notifying Caltrans and SCDER,
  - retaining a licensed drilling contractor (PeneCore Drilling, C-57 license #906899).
- Field methodology on March 18, 2025:
  - each well was filled to the surface with grout via tremie pipe,
  - approximately 20 psi air pressure was applied for 5 minutes to force grout through the screen into the filter pack,
  - well boxes and concrete aprons were removed,
  - each well was overdrilled to 5 ft using a hollow-stem auger rig,
  - the overdrilled annulus was filled with cement grout,
  - the surface was capped with native soil.
- Data sources referenced for the decommissioning decision:
  - a 2024 updated comparative evaluation of groundwater data,
  - a 2014 comparative evaluation,
  - correspondence from DTSC/RWQCB dated May 3, 2024.
- Reported well construction details included casing material, depth, diameter, screen interval, slot size, and filter pack material for each well.
- No quantitative statistical evaluation metrics are used in the report; the relevant operational measures are grout volumes and well construction parameters.

## Results
- Four wells were successfully destroyed: MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10.
- Well depths:
  - MW4: 42 ft
  - MW6: 46.5 ft
  - MW9: 40 ft
  - MW10: 40 ft
- Casing details for all four wells:
  - casing material: SCH 40 PVC
  - casing diameter: 2 inches
- Screen intervals:
  - MW4: 30–40 ft
  - MW6: 33–43 ft
  - MW9: 29.5–39.5 ft
  - MW10: 29.5–39.5 ft
- Slot size for all wells: 0.010 inches
- Filter pack material:
  - MW4: #2/12 sand, 26–42 ft
  - MW6: #2/12 sand, 30–46.5 ft
  - MW9: #2/12 sand, 27.5–40 ft
  - MW10: #2/12 sand, 27.5–40 ft
- Approximate grout volumes anticipated vs. used:
  - MW4: 6.0 gal anticipated, 10 gal used
  - MW6: 6.8 gal anticipated, 15 gal used
  - MW9: 6.2 gal anticipated, 10 gal used
  - MW10: 5.7 gal anticipated, 17 gal used
- All well destruction waste materials were transported for disposal.
- California DWR Well Completion Reports were completed and submitted for each destroyed well.

## Limitations
- The report does not present raw groundwater concentration data, trend plots, or detailed results from the 2024 comparative evaluation; it relies on that prior evaluation and agency approval.
- The conclusion that monitoring can be discontinued is site-specific and tied to current and planned site conditions, including future SR 132 Phase II encapsulation.
- Only four wells were destroyed in 2025; six other wells had already been destroyed in 2019 due to highway construction conflicts, so the monitoring network was partially unavailable before this action.
- No independent performance verification of the grout seal or long-term post-destruction monitoring is reported.
- The report is operational/documentary in nature and does not include a formal uncertainty analysis.
