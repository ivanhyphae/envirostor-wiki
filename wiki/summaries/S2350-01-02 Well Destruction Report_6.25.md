## Key claims
- The encapsulated soil stockpiles (barium and lead impacted) at the Caltrans site in Modesto, California, do not currently impact groundwater and are not expected to do so in the future.
- A 2024 comparative evaluation of groundwater data, consistent with a 2014 evaluation, and the recent complete encapsulation of stockpiles beneath State Route 132 support this conclusion.
- Based on the above, the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) approved discontinuation of all groundwater monitoring and destruction of the four remaining monitoring wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10).
- The four wells were successfully decommissioned in March 2025 by filling with grout via tremie pipe, overdrilling to 5 feet, and sealing with cement grout and native soil.

## Methodology
- **Pre-field activities**: Obtained well destruction permits (MW25-13, MW25-14) from Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Resources; marked work area and contacted utilities via Underground Service Alert (USA tickets); retained a utility locating company to survey existing utilities; notified Caltrans and the county; retained a C-57-licensed drilling contractor (PeneCore).
- **Field activities**: On March 18, 2025, PeneCore destroyed wells MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10 by:
  - Filling each PVC casing (2-inch SCH 40, depths 40–46.5 ft) to surface with cement grout via a tremie pipe.
  - Applying approximately 20 psi of air pressure to the grout column for 5 minutes to force grout through the well screen (0.010-inch slots) and into the filter pack (#2/12 sand).
  - Removing the well box and concrete apron.
  - Overdrilling each well to 5 ft depth using a hollow-stem auger rig.
  - Filling the overdrilled annulus with cement grout and capping with native soil.
- **Evaluation data**: The 2024 updated comparative evaluation of groundwater data from the site was used to support the decision, building on a 2014 evaluation. No new groundwater samples were collected for this report.

## Results
- **Wells decommissioned**: Four wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10) were destroyed on March 18, 2025.
- **Grout volume used vs. anticipated**:
  - MW4: Anticipated 6.0 gallons, Used 10 gallons
  - MW6: Anticipated 6.8 gallons, Used 15 gallons
  - MW9: Anticipated 6.2 gallons, Used 10 gallons
  - MW10: Anticipated 5.7 gallons, Used 17 gallons
  - (Anticipated volumes are based on well depth minus 5 ft for overdrilling.)
- **Waste materials**: Well casings, pipes, and concrete debris were transported for disposal by PeneCore.
- **Regulatory approval**: DTSC and RWQCB formally approved the well destruction and discontinuation of monitoring in a May 3, 2024 letter (attached to the report).

## Limitations
- The report itself does not explicitly discuss limitations, but the decision to discontinue monitoring is based on:
  - The consistency of recent groundwater data with the 2014 evaluation.
  - A declining water table.
  - The final encapsulation of remaining stockpiles scheduled for Phase II construction in 2026.
- The evaluation was a comparative analysis of existing data, not new sampling or long-term predictive modeling.
- Grout volumes used exceeded anticipated volumes in all wells, indicating possible voids or differences between calculated and actual annular volumes.

## Concepts
