The combined summary integrates all information as follows:

### Document Identity and Scope
- **Main report:** Groundwater Monitoring Report prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. (Project No. S9525-06-44A, Task Order 44), dated November 28, 2012, for fieldwork conducted May 17, 2012. Recipient: Richard Stewart, PG, Caltrans District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch, Fresno, CA.
- **Laboratory report:** Analytical data report from ATL Global (Signal Hill, CA), dated June 7, 2012, addressed to Geocon (Rebecca Silva). Samples were analyzed May–June 2012.
- **Site:** Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, 3), adjacent to the former FMC Corporation facility, Stanislaus County, CA. Media: groundwater (shallow zone of upper aquifer).
- **Monitoring locations:** Wells MW‑1 through MW‑8, plus a surface sample and an equipment blank (distilled water over decontaminated pump).

### Key Findings and Claims (from Geocon report)
- **Chemicals of concern (COCs):** Barium and strontium are primary; lead not detected above PQL. Barium ranged 55–310 µg/L (MCL 1,000 µg/L); strontium 270–1,400 µg/L (Health Advisory 4,000 µg/L). All well below thresholds.
- **No PAHs detected** (PQL 0.20 µg/L) in any groundwater sample.
- **Nitrate as N** exceeded the California primary MCL of 10 mg/L in MW‑1 (12 mg/L), MW‑4 (10 mg/L), MW‑5 (26 mg/L), and MW‑6 (18 mg/L). MW‑1 upgradient, may reflect background.
- **Total dissolved solids (TDS)** exceeded secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in MW‑4 (540 mg/L), MW‑5 (690 mg/L), and MW‑6 (630 mg/L).
- **Groundwater flow** is southeast at average gradient 0.0006. Two water types: calcium-dominant (MW‑1,‑2,‑4,‑5,‑7) and sodium-dominant (MW‑3,‑6,‑8), the latter coinciding with downgradient locations from the former FMC facility.
- **Data quality** deemed sufficient for report purposes despite equipment blank contamination (antimony, barium, calcium, magnesium, sodium) and some matrix spike recoveries outside control limits; qualified as estimated but not near thresholds.

### Analytical Results (combining Geocon and ATL lab data)
- **Dissolved metals (Table 2, Geocon; also ATL data):** Barium: 55 (MW‑8) to 310 µg/L (MW‑5). Strontium: 270 (MW‑8) to 1,400 µg/L (MW‑5). Arsenic: 2.1–3.9 µg/L (all below MCL 10). Chromium: 1.6–12 µg/L (MCL 50). Vanadium: 14–32 µg/L (Notification Level 50). Manganese detected only in MW‑1 (35 µg/L; secondary MCL 50). Antimony detected in MW‑5 (0.59 µg/L) and MW‑7 (0.74 µg/L). Other metals (cobalt, copper, molybdenum, nickel, selenium) sporadic but below thresholds.
- **General minerals (Table 3, Geocon):** Nitrate as N: 2.5–26 mg/L (exceedances noted). TDS: 300–690 mg/L (exceedances in MW‑4,‑5,‑6). Sulfate 14–66 mg/L, chloride 6.3–35 mg/L, calcium 55–100 mg/L, sodium 20–81 mg/L.
- **PAHs:** All 17 target compounds non‑detect in all samples.
- **QA/QC flags from lab report:** Equipment blank contained antimony (1.3 µg/L), barium (1.4 µg/L), calcium (200 µg/L), magnesium (72 µg/L), sodium (610 µg/L) – qualifying related results as estimated (J). Surrogate recoveries for SVOC batch B2E0767 above acceptance limits (flags S8, S1) but no target analytes detected. Matrix spike recoveries outside limits for some metals (e.g., barium, chromium, arsenic, strontium); validated by LCS. Other flags: RPD outside criteria (R), dilution required (D6), holding time issues (H3, H1). All analytical methods: EPA 6020 (dissolved metals), EPA 7470 (mercury), EPA 8270/SIM (SVOCs), EPA 300.0, SM 2320B, SM 2540C, SM 4500‑S=D.
- **Specific well results from ATL (excerpt):** MW-3: Ba 64 µg/L, As <1.0; MW-6: Ba 93 µg/L, As 3.9, Se 2.1, TDS 630; MW-7: Ba 63 µg/L, TDS 280; MW-8: Ba 55 µg/L, TDS 390; MW-5: Ba 310 µg/L (highest), Se 2.6, TDS 690.
