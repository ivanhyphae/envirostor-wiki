## Key claims
- Groundwater monitoring conducted bimonthly from ten wells (MW-1 through MW-10) in Stanislaus County.
- Groundwater flow direction is toward the southeast.
- Dissolved metals and general minerals are predominantly below water quality thresholds.
- Barium ranged 60–300 µg/L (MCL: 1,000 µg/L), strontium 430–1,100 µg/L (HA: 4,000 µg/L), arsenic 2.1–5.1 µg/L (MCL: 10 µg/L).
- Exceedances: manganese at MW-8; nitrate up to 24 mg/L in MW-5 (MCL: 10 mg/L); TDS up to 640 mg/L in MW-5 and MW-10.

## Methodology
- Depth-to-water measurements were taken; wells were purged and samples collected using disposable bailers.
- Dissolved metals analyzed by ICP-MS (EPA 6020) and cold vapor atomic absorption (EPA 7470); anions by ion chromatography (EPA 300.0); total dissolved solids (TDS) by SM 2540C.
- Quality assurance/quality control (QA/QC) included blanks, laboratory control samples, duplicates, and matrix spikes. Some matrix spike recoveries fell outside limits (flagged M1, M2, M3).

## Results
- Primary chemicals of concern (barium, lead, strontium) were below MCLs (maximum contaminant levels) and HAs (health advisories).
- Nitrate exceeded MCL in multiple wells, with MW-5 at 24 mg/L vs. 10 mg/L MCL.
- All 17 target semi-volatile organic compounds (SVOCs) were not detected.
