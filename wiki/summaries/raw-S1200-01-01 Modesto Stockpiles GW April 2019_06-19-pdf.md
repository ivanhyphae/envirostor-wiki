## Combined Summary: Groundwater Monitoring Report (April 2019)

### Document Identity
- **Document type**: Groundwater Monitoring Report, including tables, field logs, Stiff diagrams, and a Laboratory Certificate of Analysis.
- **Preparer / author organization**: Geocon Consultants, Inc. (report prepared by Geocon; laboratory analysis by ATL Global).
- **Recipient / addressee**: California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch; copied to DTSC (Dean Wright) and CVRWQCB (Steve Meeks).
- **Date / reporting period**: Report dated June 19, 2019; covers the April 2019 sampling event (fieldwork April 8–11, 2019) and depth-to-water measurements from October 2018.
- **Project number**: Geocon Project No. S1200-01-01, Task Order No. 1, EA No. 10-0X2700, Contract No. 06A2184.

### Site and Scope
- **Site**: Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Stanislaus County, California (south of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue).
- **Areas of concern**: Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3 (barium-contaminated fill from former FMC evaporation pond).
- **Media addressed**: Groundwater.
- **Monitoring wells**: MW-1 through MW-11 (with MW-11 as a duplicate). Wells are 2-inch Schedule 40 PVC, screened ~29–45 ft bgs.
- **Field issues**: MW-9 and MW-10 were dry and not sampled in April 2019. MW-5 had low recovery (purged 0.5 gal). Filter malfunctions at MW-1 and MW-3 on April 8 required resampling on April 9.

### Key Claims and Findings
- **Barium and strontium**: Concentrations in April 2019 (barium 49–370 µg/L; strontium 310–1,300 µg/L) are similar to historical levels and well below regulatory thresholds (barium MCL 1,000 µg/L; strontium Health Advisory 4,000 µg/L).
- **Exceedances (April 2019)**:
  - **Manganese**: MW-5 at 59 µg/L exceeded the Secondary MCL of 50 µg/L.
  - **Nitrate (as N)**: MW-1 (15 mg/L), MW-2 (16 mg/L), MW-5 (40 mg/L), and MW-6 (16 mg/L) exceeded the Primary MCL of 10 mg/L.
  - **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)**: MW-1 (630 mg/L), MW-2 (530 mg/L), MW-4 (500 mg/L), MW-5 (760 mg/L), and MW-6 (630 mg/L) met or exceeded the Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L.
- **Other metals**: Lead was not detected above the MDL. Other dissolved metals (calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium, chromium, vanadium, etc.) were all below applicable thresholds.
- **Groundwater flow**: Southward direction; hydraulic gradient 0.008 in October 2018 and 0.01 in April 2019, consistent with historical southeast flow.
- **Groundwater chemistry**: Two distinct water types (calcium-dominant and sodium-dominant) persist based on Stiff diagrams, consistent since 2012.
- **PAHs**: Not detected (<0.20 µg/L) in any historically tested wells.

### Regulatory Decisions
- No new regulatory decisions are contained in this monitoring report; it is a routine deliverable submitted for agency review.
- The report references DTSC’s approval of the Final Work Plan (Shaw Environmental, 2006) and DTSC’s concurrence to discontinue PAH analysis after November 2012.

### Analytical Results (April 2019)
- **Laboratory**: Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) analyzed samples using EPA 6020/7470A (metals), EPA 300.0 (anions), SM 2540C (TDS), SM 2320B (alkalinity), and SM 4500 (sulfide). Dissolved metals were field-filtered through 0.45-micron filters.
- **Data quality**: Several results were flagged “J” (estimated between MDL and PQL). Matrix spike recoveries for some metals were outside limits (Flag M1) but validated by LCS. Data are considered sufficient for assessment.
- **Key results for selected wells** (including MW-4, MW-8, MW-11 from the lab certificate):
  - MW-4: Barium 140 µg/L, TDS 500 mg/L, nitrate 7.0 mg/L (below MCL).
  - MW-8: Barium 130 µg/L (April 2019), historical max 240 µg/L. TDS 300 mg/L, nitrate 5.1 mg/L.
  - MW-11 (duplicate): Barium 60 µg/L.
- **Historical data** (MW-8, MW-9, MW-10): Barium up to 356 µg/L; nitrate up to 18 mg/L; TDS consistently >500 mg/L in MW-9 and MW-10.

### Remedial Actions
- No active remediation (excavation, capping, etc.) is reported. Only monitoring (depth-to-water measurement, purging, sampling).
- Purge water (approximately 55 gallons) was transported offsite to Geocon’s office pending disposal at Inviro-Tec Disposal (scheduled May 16, 2019).
