## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered**: Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Site); southerly of intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue, Stanislaus County, California. Three stockpiles present: Stockpile 1 (south of Kansas Ave., west of N. Emerald Ave.), Stockpile 2 (south of Kansas Ave., between N. Emerald Ave. and SR 99), Stockpile 3 (south of Kansas Ave., east of SR 99). Stockpiles generated during 1961 construction of SR 99 from soil excavated from former Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC) property that contained evaporation ponds formerly used by Barium Products Ltd.
- **Media addressed**: Groundwater (shallow zone of upper aquifer, Modesto Formation)
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced**: MW-1 through MW-10. MW-1 through MW-8 installed 2006; MW-9 and MW-10 installed June 2012. All wells completed in shallow zone (screened intervals ~30–45 ft bgs). Locations shown on Figure 2.

## Key claims
1. **Barium and strontium concentrations were similar to historical levels and remained significantly less than their numeric water quality thresholds.**
   - **Maker**: Geocon Consultants, Inc. (report author)
   - **Support**: April 2017 data: barium 33–180 µg/l (threshold: 1,000 µg/l primary MCL / 700 µg/l health advisory); strontium 190–1,200 µg/l (threshold: 4,000 µg/l health advisory)
2. **None of the reported dissolved metals concentrations (except manganese in MW-1) exceeded their respective numeric water quality threshold values.**
   - **Maker**: Geocon
   - **Support**: See Table 2; all metals below MCLs/health advisories. Manganese in MW-1 = 71 µg/l, exceeding secondary MCL of 50 µg/l.
3. **Nitrate (as N) in MW-1, MW-2, and MW-4 exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/l. TDS in MW-1, MW-2, and MW-4 exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/l.**
   - **Maker**: Geocon
   - **Support**: Table 3: nitrate ranged 2.6–24 mg/l; TDS ranged 230–550 mg/l.
4. **Groundwater flow direction in October 2016 was toward the south (gradient ~0.007); in April 2017 toward the east-southeast (gradient ~0.0007); consistent with historical flow toward south to southeast.**
   - **Maker**: Geocon
   - **Support**: Depth-to-water data Table 1, groundwater elevation maps Figures 3 and 4.
5. **The duplicate sample (MW-11) for MW-7 showed RPDs >25% for sulfide (151%) and carbonate (68%), qualifying those results as estimated.**
   - **Maker**: Geocon
   - **Support**: Reported in QA/QC discussion (p. 7-8 of report).
6. **PAH analysis was discontinued after November 2012 with DTSC concurrence.**
   - **Maker**: Geocon (historical summary)
   - **Support**: Background section states no PAHs detected and DTSC concurred.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units**:
  - **Dissolved metals (Table 2)** – groundwater, April 12, 2017 (5 samples: MW-1, MW-2, MW-3, MW-4, MW-7):
    - Barium: 33–180 µg/l
    - Strontium: 190–1,200 µg/l
    - Lead: all < MDL (method detection limit)
    - Manganese: 71 µg/l (only detected in MW-1)
    - Antimony: 0.08–0.15 µg/l
    - Arsenic: 1.6–4.8 µg/l
    - Chromium: 2.8–7.4 µg/l
    - Copper: 0.54–2.9 µg/l
    - Molybdenum: 0.18–1.3 µg/l
    - Nickel: 0.49–3.4 µg/l
    - Selenium: 0.19–0.73 µg/l
    - Vanadium: 21–34 µg/l
    - Cobalt: 0.07–1.8 µg/l
    - Cadmium: 0.03 µg/l (2 samples)
    - Zinc: 2.7–9.0 µg/l
    - Beryllium, silver, thallium, mercury: all < MDL
  - **General minerals (Table 3)**:
    - Nitrate (as N): 2.6–24 mg/l
    - TDS: 230–550 mg/l
    - Sulfide: 0.016–0.18 mg/l
    - Chloride: 26–54 mg/l
    - Sulfate: 34–55 mg/l

    - Calcium: 13–75 mg/l
    - Magnesium: 3.3–26 mg/l
- **Applicable thresholds**:
  - Primary MCLs (CDPH): barium 1,000 µg/l; arsenic 10 µg/l; chromium 50 µg/l; nickel 100 µg/l; selenium 50 µg/l; antimony 6 µg/l; cadmium 5 µg/l; nitrate (as N) 10 mg/l.
  - Secondary MCLs (CDPH): manganese 50 µg/l; zinc 5,000 µg/l; TDS 500 mg/l; copper 1,000 µg/l.
  - EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories: barium 700 µg/l; strontium 4,000 µg/l; molybdenum 40 µg/l; zinc 2,000 µg/l.
  - CDPH Notification Level: vanadium 50 µg/l.
  - CDPH Regulatory Action Level: copper 1,300 µg/l.
- **Which wells or locations exceeded thresholds and by how much**:
  - Nitrate (as N) primary MCL (10 mg/l): MW-1 (15 mg/l), MW-2 (12 mg/l), MW-4 (23 mg/l) – exceedance.
  - TDS secondary MCL (500 mg/l): MW-1 (550 mg/l), MW-2 (550 mg/l), MW-4 (550 mg/l) – exceedance.
  - Manganese secondary MCL (50 µg/l): MW-1 (71 µg/l) – exceedance.
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations affecting interpretation**:
  - Duplicate sample MW-11 (for MW-7): RPD of 151% for sulfide and 68% for carbonate – results qualified as estimated (J-flag).
  - Laboratory MS/MSD recoveries outside control limits for some analytes, but sample results validated by LCS; no data qualification deemed necessary.
  - Dilution performed due to high target analyte concentrations/failing internal standards; method blanks acceptable.
