## Key claims
- This document is an errata/transmittal letter for the **January 30, 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) – Interim Phase 1** for the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles**.
- It states that the **prescribed remedy** established by the June 2014 Feasibility Study, June 2014 Remedial Action Plan (RAP), and accepted RDIP is the **“containment alternative”** during construction of the **SR 132 West Expressway project**.
- The main purpose of the errata is to **update roles and responsibilities** in the RDIP because **Geocon, Inc. is no longer under contract** with the state.
- The letter emphasizes that **Caltrans remains the responsible party** and must demonstrate that remediation work was performed according to the RDIP, regardless of which consultant performs the work.

## Methodology
- This is an **administrative/technical correspondence**, not an experimental research paper.
- It references prior decision documents:
  - **June 2014 Feasibility Study**
  - **June 2014 Remedial Action Plan (RAP)**
  - **January 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP)**
- The attached document is a **Post Construction Contract Award Errata** that revises the RDIP to reflect changes in consultant responsibility.
- No datasets, experimental setup, or quantitative evaluation metrics are presented in the document.

## Results
- No experimental results or numerical performance metrics are reported.
- The substantive outcome is procedural:
  - The **RDIP remains the governing implementation document** for the containment remedy.
  - **Geocon is removed as the state’s agent** in the updated roles/responsibilities language.
  - **Caltrans retains responsibility** for ensuring and documenting remedy implementation.

## Limitations
- The letter does not provide technical data, monitoring outcomes, or remediation effectiveness results.
- It does not describe the actual construction or cleanup performance, only an administrative update to responsibility assignments.
- The errata depends on the earlier feasibility study, RAP, and RDIP, but does not restate their full technical basis.
