## Key claims
- The document is a transmittal letter from Caltrans to the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) accompanying a Post Construction Contract Award Errata.
- It updates the January 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles to reflect that Geocon, Inc. is no longer under contract and is removed as Caltrans’ sole agent.
- Caltrans remains the responsible party for demonstrating that remediation (via the “containment alternative” for the SR 132 West Expressway project) is performed in accordance with the RDIP.

## Methodology
- The letter does not describe an experimental or quantitative methodology. It is an administrative revision process: DTSC concurred with the 2014 Feasibility Study and Remedial Action Plan (RAP), the RDIP was accepted in January 2019, and this errata updates roles and responsibilities after Geocon’s contract ended.

## Results
- No quantitative results are presented. The outcome is an approved change in contractor responsibilities while retaining Caltrans as the responsible party.

## Limitations
- The document is a procedural update rather than a research study. It does not report any empirical findings, data, or evaluation of remediation effectiveness.
