## Key claims
- The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) concurred with the June 2014 Feasibility Study and June 2014 Remedial Action Plan for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles.
- A Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) was subsequently prepared and accepted by DTSC in January 2019, establishing the “containment alternative” as the prescribed remedy during construction of the SR 132 West Expressway project.
- Geocon, Inc. was originally identified as Caltrans’ sole agent to ensure containment in accordance with the RAP and RDIP, but is no longer under contract with the state.
- The enclosed Post Construction Contract Award Errata updates the RDIP to strike Geocon as the agent employed by the state.
- Caltrans remains the responsible party that must demonstrate the work was performed as described in the RDIP, regardless of which consultant performs the work.
