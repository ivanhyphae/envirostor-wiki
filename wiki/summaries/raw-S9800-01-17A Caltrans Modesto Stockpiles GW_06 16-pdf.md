The document is a Groundwater Monitoring Report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. for Caltrans District 6 and dated June 22, 2016. It covers the May 2016 annual groundwater sampling event at the site located south of the intersection of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue in Stanislaus County, California. The report is accompanied by a combined field/lab data package (field data sheets and analytical report from Advanced Technology Laboratories) that provides raw field measurements and laboratory results for the same event.

During the May 2, 2016 sampling event, only two of the ten monitoring wells (MW-1 and MW-7) contained sufficient groundwater for sampling; the remaining wells (MW-2 through MW-6 and MW-8 through MW-10) were dry or had insufficient water. A field duplicate (MW-11) was also collected from MW-7. Field parameters for MW-11 showed a temperature of 22.5–24.1°C, conductivity of 712–724 µmhos/cm, pH of 7.03–7.12, and ORP of –2 mV. All wells were sampled using disposable bailers.

Laboratory analysis focused on dissolved Title 22 metals (including barium, lead, arsenic, chromium, vanadium, manganese, and strontium) and general minerals (chloride, sulfate, nitrate, TDS, alkalinity, sulfide). Results showed that none of the reported dissolved metals exceeded their respective numeric water quality thresholds (e.g., barium at 100–110 µg/L remained well below the 1,000 µg/L MCL; strontium at 810–1,100 µg/L was below the 4,000 µg/L EPA Health Advisory). However, TDS in MW-7 measured 510 mg/L, exceeding the California secondary MCL of 500 mg/L. Nitrate as nitrogen ranged from 8.6 to 9.2 mg/L, below the 10 mg/L primary MCL. A QA/QC issue was noted for sulfide: the relative percent difference between MW-7 and the duplicate MW-11 exceeded the 25% criterion, so all sulfide results were qualified as estimated. No other data qualifications were required.

The report also documents a remedial action: on May 12, 2016, Geocon extended the well casing for MW-5 and installed a stove pipe and bollards after the well box was found damaged. The report does not contain any formal regulatory decisions or approvals; it is submitted for agency review. Electronic data were uploaded to GeoTracker on June 1, 2016. Future monitoring obligations include semi-annual depth-to-water measurements (next event October 2016) and annual groundwater sampling (next event April 2017). Responsible parties include Caltrans (owner/operator), Geocon Consultants (consultant), and oversight agencies DTSC and CVRWQCB.
