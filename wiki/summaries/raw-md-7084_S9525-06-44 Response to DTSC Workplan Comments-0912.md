Here is the structured summary extracted from the source document.

## Document Identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Response letter addressing comments from the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) on the Draft Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles.
- **Author or preparer:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.; prepared for the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans), District 6.
- **Date or period covered:** Prepared September 18, 2012; addresses comments dated September 12, 2012.

## Key Claims
- Geocon asserts that all DTSC and RWQCB comments have been addressed in the Final Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan (Final Workplan) dated September 18, 2012. This includes revisions to boring locations, sampling depths, and laboratory analysis protocols.

## Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision type:** Conditional approval of the Draft Workplan, with specific revisions required in the Final Workplan.
- **Agency:** DTSC, in cooperation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB).
- **Date:** September 12, 2012.
- **Conditions imposed:** The Final Workplan must include: additional discretionary boring locations for erosion features; clarified rationale for varying boring depths; containment and analysis of decontamination rinse water; a specific summary report limited to the supplemental investigation; inclusion of soluble metals analysis with MDL and PQL; reporting of "J-flag" values; and an appended Health and Safety Plan (HSP) with dust control measures.

## Analytical Results
- **No new analytical results are presented in this document.** It is a response to comments on a workplan, not a report of findings.
- **Planned analytical methods:**
    - **Fenceline Borings:** Title 22 metals and strontium (EPA Method 6010B and 7471 for mercury).
    - **Perimeter Borings:** Barium (EPA Method 6010B) for surface samples.
    - **Cadmium Borings:** Cadmium and barium (EPA Method 6010B).
- **Reporting requirements:** DTSC requires laboratory reporting for soluble metals with method detection limits (MDL) and practical quantitation limits (PQL). All concentrations between MDL and PQL must be reported with a "J-flag" as an estimate.

## Remedial Actions
- **No remedial actions are described.** This document details planning for characterization activities (soil borings) intended to evaluate and finalize a remedy.

## Responsible Parties
- **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans):** Owner/operator of the stockpiles; named as responsible for the project and recipient of the response.
- **Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC):** Identified as the previous owner of a parcel from which contaminated soils were generated; not named as a responsible party for the current remediation.
- **Geocon Consultants, Inc.:** Author of the workplan and preparer of the response.

## Key Terms and Conditions
- The Final Workplan must be revised to clarify that a comprehensive "Updated Site Characterization Summary Report" and "Updated Human Health Risk Assessment" will be prepared as separate reports.
- An April 2012 Health and Safety Plan (HSP) must be appended to the Final Workplan, with added dust control measures (e.g., wet sampling methods).
- All decontamination rinse water must be contained, sampled, analyzed for metals, and disposed of properly.
