## Key claims
- The proposed Basin 5 clean cap borrow area (~750 ft long, 100–200 ft wide, up to 10 ft deep) is suitable for generating clean cover fill materials to cap barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles (Stockpiles 1 and 2) on State Route 132.
- All measured Title 22 metal concentrations in the borrow area soil are consistent with site-specific naturally occurring background concentrations.
- Antimony, beryllium, cadmium, molybdenum, selenium, silver, thallium, mercury, and organochlorine pesticides (OCPs) were not detected above laboratory reporting limits in any composite sample.
- No field indicators of contamination (staining, odors, debris) were observed during trenching.

## Methodology
- **Site:** The borrow area is flat, vacant land historically used for agriculture, acquired by the State in the 1960s, located adjacent and easterly of Carpenter Road within the SR 132 right-of-way.
- **Sampling strategy:** Developed in accordance with DTSC 2001 Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Soil and approved by DTSC (Dean Wright).
- **Field sampling:** Six exploratory trenches (BASIN5 1–6) on a grid pattern, excavated to 10 ft depth by the highway contractor on June 16, 2020.
- **Sample depth intervals:** 0–2 ft, 2–5 ft, and 5–10 ft.
- **Compositing:** Field composited into depth-discrete 3-part composites, yielding two 3-part composite samples per depth interval per trench set.
- **Analytical methods:**
  - Title 22 metals via EPA Method 6010B (ICP-AES).
  - Mercury via EPA Method 7471A (Cold Vapor AA).
  - Organochlorine pesticides (OCPs) via EPA Method 8081A (only on 0–2 ft composites).
- **Laboratory:** Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) under chain of custody.
- **Background comparison:** 2007 Shaw background data summary provided site-specific background concentrations for metals.

## Results
Summary of reported concentration ranges (mg/kg) with site-specific background concentrations in parentheses:
- **Arsenic:** <1.0–1.8 (1.2 mg/kg)
- **Barium:** 66–94 (72.8 mg/kg)
- **Chromium:** 8.5–11 (8.6 mg/kg; based on 5-ft-deep sample data)
- **Cobalt:** 3.3–3.8 (4.4 mg/kg)
- **Copper:** 7.5–9.3 (7.5 mg/kg)
- **Lead:** 5.8–11 (2.0 mg/kg)
- **Nickel:** 6.2–7.9 (5.3 mg/kg)
- **Vanadium:** 33–37 (31.3 mg/kg)
- **Zinc:** 29–36 (26.3 mg/kg)
- **Antimony, beryllium, cadmium, molybdenum, selenium, silver, thallium, mercury:** Not detected above PQL in any sample.
- **OCPs (0–2 ft):** All analytes (e.g., 4,4′-DDD, 4,4′-DDE, 4,4′-DDT, aldrin, chlordane, dieldrin, endrin, heptachlor, etc.) not detected above PQL. Surrogate recoveries within acceptance limits.
- **Native soil:** Brown to tan, damp, clayey to fine sandy silt.

## Limitations
- The memo is a characterization testing summary for a specific borrow area (Basin 5) and is not a generalizable study.
- Background concentrations for chromium are based on 5-ft-deep sample data only (not all depth intervals).
- The sampling was conducted on a single day (June 16, 2020); temporal variability is not addressed.
- OCP analysis was only performed on the 0–2 ft depth interval composites, not on deeper samples.
- No explicit statistical comparison to background was performed; the assessment is based on qualitative comparison of concentration ranges.
- The memo notes one matrix spike recovery outside acceptance limits for thallium, but the batch was validated by the LCS.
- The memo does not discuss potential variability in soil composition across the full 750 ft × 100–200 ft area beyond the six trench locations.
