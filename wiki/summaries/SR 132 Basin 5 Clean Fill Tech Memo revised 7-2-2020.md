The study characterized the Basin 5 clean cap borrow area as suitable for generating clean fill to cap barium-containing soil stockpiles. On June 16, 2020, six exploratory trenches (BASIN5 1–6) were sampled on a grid pattern to a maximum depth of 10 feet, with samples collected at 0–2 ft, 2–5 ft, and 5–10 ft intervals and field-composited into three-part composites per depth. Composites were analyzed for Title 22 metals via EPA 6010B and mercury via EPA 7471A; 0–2 ft composites were also analyzed for organochlorine pesticides (OCPs) via EPA 8081A, following DTSC 2001 guidelines. Quality control data for mercury (EPA 7471A) showed acceptable performance, with a post-spike recovery of 101%. For OCPs (EPA 8081A), the laboratory control sample (LCS) validated the batch, though a matrix spike and duplicate showed some individual pesticide recoveries outside acceptance criteria. Reported metal concentrations were consistent with naturally occurring background levels: arsenic <1.0–1.8 mg/kg (background 1.2), barium 66–94 (72.8), chromium 8.5–11 (8.6), cobalt 3.3–3.8 (4.4), copper 7.5–9.3 (7.5), lead 5.8–11 (2.0), nickel 6.2–7.9 (5.3), vanadium 33–37 (31.3), zinc 29–36 (26.3). Antimony, beryllium, cadmium, molybdenum, selenium, silver, thallium, mercury, and OCPs were not detected above laboratory reporting limits. The borrow area was historically agricultural land acquired in the 1960s; background data from a 2007 Shaw study was used for comparison, and no field indicators of contamination were observed.
