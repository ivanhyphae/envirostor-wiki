The source document is a groundwater-monitoring and laboratory-report package for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California, covering the March 18, 2013 sampling event and related analytical/QC results. Ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) were measured, purged, and sampled, with groundwater levels used to confirm a generally east-southeast flow direction and a very low hydraulic gradient consistent with historical conditions. Samples were analyzed under chain-of-custody and QA/QC controls for dissolved Title 22 metals, major ions, nitrate, sulfate, sulfide, alkalinity, total dissolved solids, and—historically—PAHs, with results compared to California and EPA drinking-water benchmarks.

Overall, the groundwater is mineralized, alkaline, and geochemically heterogeneous. Bicarbonate is the dominant alkalinity species, but the Stiff diagrams and chemistry data show more than one groundwater type across the site, including calcium-dominant and sodium-dominant waters. Total dissolved solids and nitrate are the most common exceedances, with occasional manganese exceedances; these elevations may reflect natural background conditions, particularly in the upgradient area near MW-1. Most dissolved metals were at low concentrations or below numeric thresholds, and mercury was non-detect in the reported samples. Representative detections included low-level arsenic, chromium, nickel, molybdenum, vanadium, barium, and strontium, but none were identified as widespread exceedances. PAHs were generally non-detect or very low in frequency and concentration, and because of the low detection frequency and historical non-detections, PAH monitoring was discontinued after November 2012 with DTSC concurrence.

The laboratory appendix and QC tables show that the analytical batches were generally valid, with blanks clean and control recoveries/precision within acceptance limits aside from some noted, accepted matrix effects or dilution-related exceptions. Overall, the document concludes that site groundwater quality is stable, naturally mineralized, and only sporadically impacted by parameters such as nitrate, TDS, and manganese.
