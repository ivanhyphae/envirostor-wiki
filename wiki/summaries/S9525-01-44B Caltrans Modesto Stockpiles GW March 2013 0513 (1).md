The March 2013 groundwater monitoring report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (GEOCON Project No. S9525-01-44B) presents a comprehensive water quality assessment of ten monitoring wells (MW‑1 through MW‑10) sampled on March 18, 2013. The study evaluated Title 22 metals, general minerals, and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) using calibrated field measurements (depth‑to‑water, dissolved oxygen, ORP) and standard laboratory methods. Wells were purged by three well volumes or until dry, then sampled with disposable bailers. Analytical methods included EPA 300.0 (anions), EPA 6020/7470 (dissolved metals by ICP‑MS and cold‑vapor AA), SM 2320B (alkalinity), SM 2540C (TDS), and SM 4500‑S²⁻=D (sulfide). Quality control was validated through blanks, LCS, MS, MSD, and duplicates.

Key findings show that most dissolved metals (barium, lead, strontium, arsenic, chromium, vanadium) remained below their respective primary or secondary maximum contaminant levels (MCLs) or notification levels. However, manganese exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L in MW‑1 and MW‑7 (up to 260 µg/L). Nitrate‑nitrogen exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in four wells (MW‑1, MW‑5, MW‑6, MW‑10, with values up to 30 mg/L). Total dissolved solids met or exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in the same wells (up to 700 mg/L). Major cation concentrations included elevated sodium (up to 150 mg/L in MW‑6), calcium (up to 91 mg/L in MW‑5), and magnesium (up to 35 mg/L).

Groundwater flow was east‑southeast with an average gradient of 0.0006 ft/ft, consistent with historical data. Stiff diagrams indicate bicarbonate‑dominant water with two hydrochemical facies: calcium‑dominant (MW‑1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 9, 10) and sodium‑dominant (MW‑3, 6, 8). PAH analysis was discontinued for this monitoring event.

A notable limitation is that wells MW‑5 and MW‑6 went dry during purging; MW‑5 consistently yields low volumes, potentially affecting representativeness. Overall, data quality was deemed sufficient for the monitoring objectives.
