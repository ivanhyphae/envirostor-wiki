## Combined Summary: Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project Surface Water Monitoring Report (March 28, 2023)

### Document Identity & Scope
- **Document type**: Surface water monitoring report (cover letter, inspection results, and supporting Data Validation Report) prepared by Stantec Consulting Services Inc. for the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans). Contract No. 06A2542, Task Order 97 (TO97). Report date: March 28, 2023.
- **Site**: Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA) – former FMC Corporation barium processing facility area, south of Kansas Avenue/SR 99.
- **Media**: Surface water.
- **Sample locations (active)**: PL6 (north side SR 132 near SR 99), PL7 (north side SR 132 at discharge pipe to stormwater basin 5), BG1 (background – Loletta Ave), BG3 (background – catch basin at N. Carpenter Road/Kansas Ave). Previous locations (PL1–PL5, BG2) were removed after construction.
- **Analytical work**: Four surface water samples collected on March 10, 2023, analyzed for dissolved metals (EPA 6020/7470A), nitrate-N (EPA 300.0), total suspended solids (TSS, SM 2540D), and dissolved sulfide (SM 4500-S-D). A separate Data Validation Report (Appendix D) assessed data quality.

### Key Claims & Findings
- **All detected dissolved metals were below Primary and Secondary MCLs** – Claim by Stantec, supported by analytical data. Detected metals included arsenic, barium, chromium, copper, lead, molybdenum, selenium, vanadium, zinc, strontium, and mercury. Antimony, beryllium, cadmium, cobalt, nickel, silver, and thallium were not detected above MDLs. Notable concentrations: barium up to 300 µg/L (PL7), zinc up to 130 µg/L (PL7), strontium up to 77 µg/L.
- **No surface water discharge/runoff beyond BMPs or the Site was observed** – Based on field inspection on March 10, 2023; northern fill slopes of Stockpiles 1 and 2 were vegetated and showed no erosion concerns.
- **Background sample BG1 results were generally consistent with historical results** – Supported by comparison with previous data.
- **All data are considered usable as qualified; no data were rejected** – Claim by Stantec (validated by S. Von Raesfeld), despite several data quality limitations (see below). This conclusion applies to both the main report and the Data Validation Report.
- **Data Quality Limitations** (from Data Validation Report):
  - **Nitrate-N holding time exceedance**: All four samples were analyzed outside the recommended 48-hour holding time; results qualified as estimated with potential low bias (J-).
  - **Method blank contamination**: Nine metals (cadmium, chromium, copper, molybdenum, vanadium, zinc, beryllium) were detected in the method blank for batch B3C0693. Results below PQL were raised to non-detect (U); results above PQL but <10× blank were qualified as estimated with potential high bias (J+).
  - **Antimony MDL exceeded its Primary MCL** (8.8 µg/L MDL vs. 6.0 µg/L MCL) – noted as a limitation, but antimony was not detected above the MDL in any sample.
  - **No field duplicate or field blank** was submitted.
- **Background sample BG3** had elevated TSS (310 mg/L) compared to other samples (1.6–9.5 mg/L), but no MCL applies.

### Regulatory Context & Responsible Parties
- **No regulatory decisions were issued** in this document. The report is for review by Caltrans, DTSC, and CVRWQCB.
- **Responsible parties**: Caltrans (owner/operator), Stantec (consultant/preparer), Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) (analytical lab), DTSC and CVRWQCB (regulatory oversight). Historical SAP authors: Shaw Environmental, Inc. (2006) and Geocon Consultants, Inc. (2013).

### Inspection and Remedial Status
- **Inspection**: Visual assessment of northern fill slopes of Stockpiles 1 and 2 found vegetation cover, no erosion, and functioning BMPs (straw waddles, jute mesh, hydroseeding). No trash/debris concerns.
- **Remedial actions**: None described in this document; the project is in a post-encapsulation monitoring phase (Stockpile 3 removed; Stockpiles 1 and 2 encapsulated under SR 132 since October 2021; road opened November 2022).
