# Document Summary: Errata to January 2019 RDIP - SR 132 West Expressway Phase 1

## Document Identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Post-Construction Contract Award Errata to the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) – Interim Phase 1, dated January 30, 2019. Updates roles and responsibilities following award of the construction contract, specifically striking Geocon Consultants, Inc. as the state-employed consultant.
- **Author or preparer:** Prepared for Caltrans by Geocon Consultants, Inc.; Caltrans identified as responsible party.
- **Date or period covered:** January 30, 2019 (original RDIP); April 3, 2019 (DTSC approval); errata issued after construction contract award.

## Key Claims
- Geocon was originally identified as Caltrans' agent to ensure containment of stockpiles in accordance with the RAP and RDIP; however, regardless of the consultant performing the work, Caltrans remains the responsible party to ensure work is performed per the RDIP.
- Most constituents of potential concern (COPCs) in the stockpiles are present at concentrations generally less than residential health risk screening levels; however, barium and lead are present at elevated concentrations.

## Regulatory Decisions
- **DTSC Conditional Approval (August 20, 2018):** Conditional approval of Draft RDIP pending administrative text revisions and review of 95% and 100% design plans.
- **DTSC Conditional Approval (October 12, 2018):** Conditional approval of revised RDIP incorporating text revisions.
- **DTSC Approval (April 3, 2019):** Final approval of RDIP for "containment alternative" remediation of Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles via SR 132 West project.

## Analytical Results
- **Contaminants:** Barium and lead (primary COPCs). Verification samples analyzed for barium and lead.
- **Media:** Soil (stockpile removal verification sampling; clean imported fill source sampling).
- **Analytical method:** EPA Test Method 6010B with targeted reporting limit of 1.0 mg/kg.
- **Thresholds:** 
  - Barium: 1,000 mg/kg
  - Lead: 80 mg/kg
- **Verification criteria:** Stockpile removal confirmed complete when verification sample results (total values or calculated 95% UCLs) are less than threshold levels.

## Remedial Actions
- **Activity:** Excavation, consolidation, and containment of barium-containing soil from Stockpiles 1–3 for Interim Phase 1 of SR-132 West highway construction.
- **Quantities:** 
  - Stockpile 1: SR-132 stations 1125+60 through 1131+95
  - Stockpile 2: stations 1135+90 through 1148+30
- **Phase status:** Interim Phase 1 construction post-contract award.
- **Verification:** Removal confirmation via survey data, field observations, and verification soil sampling at rate of one sample per 5,000 sq ft removal area and one sample per 100 lineal feet of activity area.
- **Capping:** Completed Stockpile 1 and 2 barium-containing soil consolidation zones capped with minimum 12-inch-thick clean imported fill and/or minimum 4-inch-thick AB materials, plus structural pavement, hardscape, or additional clean embankment fill.
- **Groundwater monitoring:** Annual monitoring (typically April) per DTSC-approved Final Work Plan, Groundwater Assessment (Shaw, 2006), continuing during construction.
- **BMPs:** Dust control (water spray), erosion control, stormwater BMPs per SWPPP; grading suspended during high winds when visible dust occurs or runoff/soil trackout observed.
- **Well abandonment:** Caltrans to supervise proper abandonment of site-related groundwater monitoring wells impacted by highway construction.

## Key Terms and Conditions
- Land use covenant (LUC)/deed restriction required for completed barium-containing soil containment zones within Stockpiles 1 and 2; not required for completed removal areas (western and eastern ends of Stockpile 2 and Stockpile 3).
- Clean imported fill source (vacant/agricultural land along SR-132 ROW between Carpenter Road and Rosemore Avenue overcrossings) to be sampled per DTSC's Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Material and pre-approved by DTSC before placement.
- Caltrans representative onsite fulltime during site preparation, excavation, fill placement, and clean fill containment.
- California Professional Geologist (PG) or field geologist/engineer under PG supervision to oversee stockpile removal and collect verification samples.
- Daily field logs required documenting personnel, activities, air monitoring, dust observations, excavation quantities, and removal area confirmation.
- Stop work authority held by Caltrans' environmental consultant in coordination with construction manager for dust, runoff, or soil trackout issues.
- Monthly status reports to be provided.
- Remedial Action Completion Report to be prepared.
