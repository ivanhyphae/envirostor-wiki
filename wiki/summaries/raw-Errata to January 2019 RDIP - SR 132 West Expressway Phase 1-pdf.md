## Document Identity

- **Document type:** Errata to Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP)
- **Preparer / author organization:** Caltrans (updating prior RDIP prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc.)
- **Recipient / addressee agency or party:** DTSC and CVRWQCB (regulatory oversight agencies)
- **Date or reporting period covered:** January 30, 2019
- **Project number or task order:** SR-132 West Freeway/Expressway Project, Interim Phase 1

## Site and Scope

- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) along SR-132 West Freeway/Expressway alignment, Stanislaus County, CA
- **Media addressed:** Soil (barium-containing fill material), groundwater, stormwater, air (fugitive dust)
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** 
  - Stockpile 1: approximate SR-132 stations 1125+60 through 1131+95
  - Stockpile 2: approximate SR-132 stations 1135+90 through 1148+30 (including eastern and western bridge abutment areas)
  - Stockpile 3: entire stockpile to be removed
  - ADL (apparently delineated limits) consolidation zones within Stockpiles 1 and 2
  - Clean fill source areas: between Carpenter Road and Rosemore Avenue overcrossings along SR-132 ROW

  - **Who is making it:** Caltrans (citing prior site data, via HSP reference)
  - **Supported by data in this document?** Yes — referenced from prior site characterization

## Analytical Results

- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units:**
  - Barium in soil — threshold: 1,000 mg/kg (action level)
  - Lead in soil — threshold: 80 mg/kg (action level)
  - Targeted reporting limit: 1.0 mg/kg (using EPA Method 6010B)
- **Applicable thresholds:**
  - Barium: 1,000 mg/kg (based on DTSC-approved action level)
  - Lead: 80 mg/kg (based on DTSC-approved action level)
- **Verification sampling protocol:**
  - One sample per 5,000 sq ft of removal area
  - One sample per 100 lineal ft of activity area (loading, unpaved haul roads, track-out prevention areas outside Stockpiles 1 and 2)
- **Which wells or locations exceeded thresholds and by how much:** Not specified in this document (verification sampling is planned future activity)
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations:** None specified — samples to be collected using pre-cleaned stainless trowels, placed in laboratory-provided glass containers, chain-of-custody protocol to California-certified analytical laboratory

## Remedial Actions

- **Activity:** 
  - Barium-containing soil excavation and removal (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3)
  - Fill placement and consolidation within designated areas (Stockpiles 1 and 2 ADL zones)
  - Capping with minimum 12-inch clean fill material and/or minimum 4-inch AB materials
  - Site grading and retaining wall/bridge structure construction
  - Removal and offsite disposal of remnant homeless encampments, debris, and perimeter fencing
  - Mowing to minimum 2-inch vegetation thickness prior to excavation/fill placement
  - Discing/ripping, moisture conditioning, and recompaaction of fill placement area surfaces per geotechnical specifications
  - Proper abandonment of site-related groundwater monitoring wells impacted by construction
- **Quantities:** Not specified in this document (quantities to be documented in daily field logs)
- **Verification sampling outcomes:** Pending — will confirm removal completion when results are <1,000 mg/kg barium and <80 mg/kg lead (total values or 95% UCLs)
- **Phase status:** Planned/In-progress — Interim Phase 1 construction awarded to Bay Cities Paving and Grading, Inc.

## Key Terms and Conditions

- **Deadlines:**
  - Submission of 100% design plans and final project specifications to DTSC and CVRWQCB: early 2019
  - Monthly status reports to DTSC and CVRWQCB (ongoing)
- **Required follow-on deliverables:**
  - Survey data of final barium-containing soil containment zones (including ADL soil and clean cover materials) with latitude, longitude, elevation (NAVD 88), and SR-132 postmile limits
  - Regulatory-required Land Use Covenant (LUC)/deed restriction recorded with Stanislaus County Clerk-Recorder Office for completed containment zones in Stockpiles 1 and 2 (not required for Stockpile 3 removal areas or ends of Stockpile 2)
  - Remedial Action Completion Report (RACR)
  - Daily field logs documenting personnel, activities, air monitoring locations, visible dust observations, excavation quantities, removal area limits confirmation, and photographs
  - Pre-approval from DTSC for clean imported fill source areas (sampled per DTSC Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Material)
- **Permit conditions/monitoring obligations:**
  - Annual groundwater monitoring (typically April) per DTSC-approved Final Work Plan, Groundwater Assessment (Shaw, 2006)
  - Stormwater monitoring during construction per approved SWPPP
  - Air monitoring for fugitive dust during site preparation, excavation, fill placement, and clean fill containment
  - Dust control (water spray); grading suspended during high winds or inadequate dust control, uncontrolled runoff, or soil trackout
  - Worker health and safety per HSP (Appendix F)
  - Caltrans representative onsite full-time during barium-containing soil activities
  - California Professional Geologist (PG) or field geologist/engineer under PG supervision for verification sampling oversight
- **Land use restrictions:**
  - LUC required for Stockpile 1 and 2 containment zones only
  - Capping requirements: minimum 12-inch clean fill (imported) and/or minimum 4-inch AB materials (with structural pavement, hardscape, or additional embankment fill)
