### Summary of Groundwater Monitoring Report: Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (June 2014)

This document is a groundwater monitoring report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California. The report presents the results of a June 2014 sampling event from 10 monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) installed in the shallow upper aquifer (Modesto Formation) at depths of 40–48 feet.

**Key Findings on Contaminants of Concern (COCs):**

- **Primary COCs (Barium and Strontium):** No dissolved metals exceeded regulatory thresholds. Barium concentrations (51–280 µg/l) were well below its MCL of 1,000 µg/l, and strontium (250–1,100 µg/l) was below its EPA Health Advisory of 4,000 µg/l. These levels are consistent with historical data.
- **Nitrate and Total Dissolved Solids (TDS):** Nitrate as nitrogen (2.6–29 mg/l) exceeded the primary MCL (10 mg/l) in five wells (MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, MW-9, and MW-10). TDS (320–680 mg/l) exceeded the secondary MCL (500 mg/l) in five wells (MW-1, MW-4, MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10). Notably, the elevated nitrate in upgradient well MW-1 (15 mg/l) may represent natural background conditions rather than site-related impacts.
- **Other Analytes:** Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon (PAH) analysis was discontinued after November 2012 due to consistent non-detects. Other metals (e.g., arsenic, chromium, vanadium) were detected sporadically but remained below applicable standards.

**Groundwater Flow and Chemistry:**

Groundwater flow direction is south-southeast with an average gradient of 0.0008 ft/ft, and depth to water ranged from 35.09 ft (MW-1) to 42.86 ft (MW-5) below top of casing. Stiff diagrams reveal two distinct water types: calcium-dominant (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-7, MW-9, MW-10) and sodium-dominant (MW-3, MW-5, MW-6, MW-8). Well MW-5 shifted from calcium- to sodium-dominant in 2013, suggesting mixing of different water types.

**Methodology:**

Groundwater samples were collected on June 5, 2014, using standardized field procedures (purge of 1–3 well volumes, monitoring of field parameters, and collection with disposable bailers). Samples for dissolved metals were field-filtered through 0.45-micron filters. Analyses were performed by a California-certified laboratory using EPA methods (6020 for metals, 7470A for mercury, 300.0 for anions) and Standard Methods (2320B for alkalinity, 2540C for TDS, 4500-S=D for sulfide). Results were compared to California MCLs, EPA Health Advisories, and California Notification Levels.

**Study Limitations & Quality Control:**

The report acknowledges several limitations. Several wells (MW-5, MW-6, MW-10) went dry during purging due to low recharge and required sampling after recharge, potentially affecting representativeness. Analytical quality control issues were noted, including matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate (MS/MSD) recoveries outside control limits for several analytes; these were attributed to matrix interference and validated by laboratory control samples. Mercury was not detected in any sample. The report does not provide statistical trend analysis or quantitative uncertainty bounds.
