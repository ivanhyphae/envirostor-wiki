## Synthesized Summary

A groundwater monitoring event was conducted at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (S9800-01-17) on June 4–5, 2014, with ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) sampled to assess water quality and contaminant concentrations. Field measurements—including temperature (20.9–24.6°C), conductivity (542–1,092 µmhos/cm), pH (6.71–8.82), dissolved oxygen (3.44–6.36 mg/L), oxidation-reduction potential (–26 to 153 mV), and turbidity (10–611 NTU)—were recorded during purging. Wells were purged of 0.5–4.5 gallons (1.0–3.8 well volumes) using submersible pumps or disposable bailers; samples were filtered for dissolved metals and analyzed by Advanced Technology Laboratories for Title 22 dissolved metals (EPA 6020/7470A), major cations (EPA 6020), anions (EPA 300.0), sulfide (SM 4500), total dissolved solids (SM 2540C), and alkalinity (SM 2320B).

Laboratory results showed that no dissolved metals exceeded their respective numeric thresholds. Barium ranged from 51–280 µg/L (threshold 1,000 µg/L primary MCL, 700 µg/L secondary MCL) and strontium from 250–1,100 µg/L (health advisory 4,000 µg/L), both consistent with historical levels. Lead was not detected above the practical quantitation limit. However, nitrate as nitrogen exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in wells MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, MW-9, and MW-10 (range 2.6–29 mg/L), and TDS exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in wells MW-1, MW-4, MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10 (range 320–680 mg/L). Sulfide was detected in MW-1, MW-8, and MW-10 at 0.017–0.12 mg/L. Groundwater flow direction was south-southeast with an average gradient of 0.0008 ft/ft.

Quality assurance/quality control issues included several matrix spike recoveries outside 70–130% acceptance limits for calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium, and barium, attributed to matrix interference. Some samples (MW-5, MW-6, MW-10) went dry during purging due to slow recharge, requiring next-day sampling. Turbidity reached 611 NTU in some samples. Laboratory control samples and field duplicates were generally within limits, with a few RPD exceedances. Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon (PAH) analysis had been discontinued since November 2012 per DTSC concurrence. The groundwater ionic composition was predominantly bicarbonate, with calcium-dominant or sodium-dominant water types observed across the wells.
