### Site and Scope
- **Site name**: Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project  
- **Location**: South of the intersection of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue, Modesto, Stanislaus County, California  
- **Areas of concern**: Three barium‑contaminated soil stockpiles: Stockpile #1 (2.5 acres), Stockpile #2 (7.5 acres), and Stockpile #3 (2.5 acres) – approximately 160,000 cubic yards of fill embankment  
- **Media addressed**: Soil (surface soil 0–1 ft, subsurface soil 0–20 ft, construction zone soil), groundwater, and air  
- **Specific monitoring wells and sample locations**:  
  - Groundwater wells: MW‑1 through MW‑10 (MW‑9 and MW‑10 installed in 2012)  
  - Soil borings: 35 Fenceline Borings, 28 Perimeter Borings, Cadmium Borings in Stockpiles 2 and 3; specific boring IDs include SR99/132‑N01 through SR99/132‑N24, SR99/132‑S01 through SR99/132‑S11, and background locations SR99/132‑BG01 through BG08  

### Key Claims
The document makes the following assertions about site conditions and risk assessment methodologies:

1. **Validity of 2007 HHRA**: The 2007 HHRA remains valid for all exposure scenarios.  
   - **Who**: Geocon (preparer)  
   - **Supported?** Yes, supported by comparison of 2012 analytical data to 2007 HHRA exposure‑point concentrations.

2. **No significant increase in risk**: 2012 soil and groundwater data do not significantly increase conservative cancer risk and noncancer hazard estimates.  
   - **Who**: Geocon  
   - **Supported?** Yes – analytical comparisons show same order of magnitude concentrations.

3. **No risk to human health**: Soil stockpiles do not pose a risk to human health for current trespassers, Caltrans workers, or adjacent residents (per DTSC December 17, 2009 letter).  
   - **Who**: DTSC (cited)  
   - **Supported?** Yes, referenced as previous determination; current data supports continued validity.

4. **Conservative methodology in 2007 HHRA**: The use of maximum detected concentrations for all metals (except lead) likely overestimates actual risk and hazard for current off‑site residents/trespassers and future off‑site residents and construction workers.  
   - **Who**: Preparer of the HHRA  
   - **Supported?** This is stated as a known conservative assumption, not a data‑based finding.

5. **Chronic toxicity for short‑term construction scenario**: Applying chronic toxicity criteria to evaluate risk from the short‑term future construction scenario is “highly uncertain and conservative.”  
   - **Who**: Preparer of the HHRA  
   - **Supported?** No, this is a methodological assertion.

6. **Exclusion of mowing exposure**: Mowing, which occurs once a year, was not included in the HHRA because the exposure duration is too short to be chronic.  
   - **Who**: Preparer of the HHRA  
   - **Supported?** No, this is a rationale for excluding a pathway.

- **Regulatory oversight**: DTSC and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB).
