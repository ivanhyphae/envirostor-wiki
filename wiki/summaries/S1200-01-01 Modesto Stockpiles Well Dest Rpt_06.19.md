The source document is a procedural compliance report set, including California DWR Well Completion Reports (Form 188), documenting the destruction and abandonment of six former groundwater monitoring wells at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California. The wells identified across the report include MW1, MW2, MW3, MW5, MW7, and MW8. The work was performed in advance of highway construction and was intended to comply with California Well Standards Bulletin 7490, Monitoring Well Standards, Part III, as well as City of Modesto and county permit requirements.

Before fieldwork, Geocon obtained the City of Modesto well destruction permit, marked the well locations, provided required underground utility and city notices, and retained Gregg Drilling and Testing to carry out the destruction. On April 29 and 30, 2019, the wells were destroyed under Geocon supervision, with Caltrans personnel present. The standard destruction procedure consisted of measuring groundwater and total well depth, pressure-grouting each casing with neat cement grout through a tremie pipe, applying about 20–25 psi air pressure for roughly five minutes, overdrilling the upper 5 feet of casing with an 8-inch hollow-stem auger, and backfilling the upper interval with cuttings, native material, clean fill, or topsoil as appropriate. The report also notes that well boxes were removed and that completion/destruction records were prepared for each well.

The report provides well-by-well completion details, including grout volumes, pressure, duration, and depth data. Minimum calculated grout volumes ranged from about 6.4 to 7.4 gallons per well, while actual grout usage ranged from roughly 13 to 20 gallons. Example entries show MW1 and MW2 with 15 gallons of grout and groundwater depths around 39–42 feet, while MW3 and the later reported wells MW5, MW7, and MW8 show similar destruction documentation with grout amounts of 15–20 gallons and about 20–24 psi applied. All six wells were reported destroyed and the completion reports were electronically submitted to the California Department of Water Resources on June 12, 2019, with copies also forwarded to the relevant local environmental health agency.

The document is limited to describing the destruction activity and permit compliance for this specific site and does not present broader hydrogeologic analysis, performance evaluation, or long-term monitoring results.
