## Key claims
This report documents the successful destruction of six groundwater monitoring wells (MW1, MW2, MW3, MW5, MW7, MW8) at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California, in preparation for highway construction activities. The work was performed in general accordance with California Well Standards Bulletin 74-90, Monitoring Well Standards, Part III, and under a City of Modesto permit (No. ENC2019-50174). Two wells were located within future highway travel lanes; four were in areas of future drainage improvements. Four wells were left in place at the site.

## Methodology
- **Pre-field activities**: Obtained a well destruction permit from the City of Modesto; marked well locations with white paint (April 8, 2019); provided 72-hour notification to Underground Service Alert; notified the City of Modesto at least 48 hours prior; retained Gregg Drilling and Testing (Caltrans-approved, California-certified subcontractor).
- **Field activities**: On April 29, 2019, wells MW1, MW2, and MW5 were destroyed; on April 30, 2019, wells MW3, MW7, and MW8 were destroyed. Geocon staff member Lauren Short supervised; Caltrans personnel were onsite.
- **Destruction procedure**:  
  1. Measured depth to groundwater and total well depth.  
  2. Filled each well with grout via a tremie pipe installed to approximate total depth.  
  3. Applied air pressure (20–25 psi) for approximately 5 minutes.  
  4. Prepared grout using 3 gallons of water per 47-pound sack of neat cement, mixed in 5-gallon buckets.  
  5. Used grout volumes: minimum calculated 6.4–7.4 gallons; actual ranged from 13 to 20 gallons per well until grout reached the top of the casing.  
  6. After grout stabilized, removed well boxes and over-drilled the top 5 feet of each casing with an 8-inch-diameter hollow-stem auger.  
  7. Backfilled the top 5 feet with a combination of cuttings, native material, and bagged topsoil.  
- **Debris disposal**: Well box materials and concrete were deposited in Caltrans’ onsite construction debris pile.
- **Reporting**: Completed Well Completion Reports (DWR Form 188) for each destroyed well and forwarded them to the Department of Water Resources and Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Health.
