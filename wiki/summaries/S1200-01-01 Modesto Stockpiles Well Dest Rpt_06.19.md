## Combined Summary of "raw/S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles Well Dest Rpt_06.19.pdf"

This document reports the successful destruction of six groundwater monitoring wells (MW1, MW2, MW3, MW5, MW7, MW8) associated with the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California. The work was performed on April 29–30, 2019, in accordance with California Well Standards Bulletin 74-90 and a City of Modesto permit (ENC2019-50174). Pre-field activities included notifying Underground Service Alert and the city, marking wells, and retaining certified subcontractor Gregg Drilling and Testing.

Each well was abandoned using pressure grouting via a tremie pipe at approximately 20–25 psi for five minutes, using neat cement grout (3 gallons water per 47-pound sack). Grout volumes ranged from 13 to 20 gallons per well, exceeding calculated minimums of 6.4–7.4 gallons. After grout stabilization, well boxes were removed and the top five feet of casing was over-drilled with an 8-inch hollow-stem auger, then backfilled with cuttings, native material, and topsoil. Depth to groundwater and well depth were measured prior to destruction.

Detailed well destruction reports (Form DWR 188) were submitted to the California Department of Water Resources and Stanislaus County Environmental Health for four wells: MW5 (20 gallons grout, 30 ft depth, water at 44 ft), MW7 (15 gallons, 30 ft depth, water at 38.4 ft), MW8 (15 gallons, 38.5 ft depth, water at 38.5 ft), and an additional well on Page 1 (15 gallons, water at 45 ft). Post-grouting air pressure caused grout drops of 6–12 inches. The remaining two wells (MW1, MW2) were also abandoned per the same protocol. Well debris and concrete were placed in Caltrans’ onsite construction debris pile.

Limitations include that the report is valid only as of June 19, 2019, no warranty is implied, and services followed local standard of care. Some well location accuracy is approximate (GPS, ±50 ft), and no long-term yield or drawdown data are available. Key concepts include well destruction, pressure grouting, tremie pipe, neat cement grout, hollow-stem auger, over-drilling, groundwater monitoring well abandonment, and adherence to California Well Standards Bulletin 74-90 and DWR Form 188 protocols.
