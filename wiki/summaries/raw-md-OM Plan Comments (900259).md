## Document Identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Regulatory comment letter from the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) on the Draft Operation and Maintenance Plan (O&M Plan) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Interim Phase 1 State Route 132 West Project. The plan covers inspection, maintenance, and repair of the clean cap cover over two barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles.
- **Author/preparer:** Dean Wright, P.G., Project Manager, DTSC Site Mitigation and Restoration Program.
- **Date:** August 1, 2022. The reviewed Draft O&M Plan is dated March 2022.

## Key Claims (DTSC Assertions on Plan Adequacy)
- The subject line of the Draft O&M Plan misspells “Maintenance.”
- Section 2.1 lacks a minimum advance notification period for BCS disturbance. DTSC recommends **10 working days** advance notice to allow prior written authorization.
- Section 2.4 does not explicitly reference **Health and Safety Code (HSC) 25123.3(b)(4)(A)** requirements. The stated 6 mm minimum thickness meets HSC requirements, but HSC uses mils, creating ambiguity; the word “millimeter” is also misspelled.
- Section 3.1 should require inspections after specific events (fires, ≥1 inch rainfall in 24 hours, significant seismic events, traffic accidents causing cap damage), not only annual inspections. This is critical for the north vegetated slope, which remains unfinished.
- Section 2.7 periodic patrols should note impacts from unauthorized access (e.g., homeless encampments), especially on the north vegetated slope.
- The O&M Site Inspection Form needs expanded subheadings for each stockpile feature: MSE Wall (bulging, out-of-plane); Highway Pavement (cracks, potholes); Median and Shoulders (cracks, potholes); North Slope (vegetation, rills, slumping); Storm Drains (debris/obstructions, erosion around inlets/outlets).

## Regulatory Decisions
- Formal agency action: DTSC (in consultation with Central Valley RWQCB) reviewed the Draft O&M Plan and issued written comments. The letter directs Geocon Consultants to incorporate all recommended changes and submit the revised O&M Plan as **Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report**.
- Decision type: Conditional – approval of the O&M Plan is pending satisfactory revision.
- Agencies: DTSC (lead), Central Valley RWQCB (co-regulator).
- Date: August 1, 2022.

## Analytical Results
- *No analytical results presented in this document.*

## Remedial Actions
- The remedial action described is a **clean cap cover** over two barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles, integrated with the SR 132 freeway.
- Cap components referenced: MSE Wall, highway pavement, median and shoulders, north vegetated slope, storm drains.
- The O&M Plan covers inspection, maintenance, and repair of these components. The north side of the freeway remains a vegetated slope until the final phase of the freeway is complete.

## Key Terms and Conditions
- Revise the Draft O&M Plan per all six comments.
- Submit revised plan as Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report.
- Advance notification: minimum **10 working days** to DTSC prior to any BCS disturbance; prior written authorization required.
- Post-event inspections: after fires, ≥1 inch rainfall in 24 hours, significant seismic events, traffic accidents that may damage the cap.
- Periodic patrols must check for unauthorized access impacts (e.g., homeless encampments).
- Inspection form must include subheadings for MSE Wall, Highway Pavement, Median and Shoulders, North Slope, and Storm Drains.
- HSC 25123.3(b)(4)(A) requirements for liner thickness must be explicitly referenced; minimum thickness 6 mm (note: HSC uses mils, so clarify conversion).
- The north vegetated slope requires specific attention because it is not yet the final freeway surface.
