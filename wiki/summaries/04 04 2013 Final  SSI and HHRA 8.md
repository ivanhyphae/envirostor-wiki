## Key claims
- The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), reviewed and concurred with the revised *Supplemental Site Investigation (SSI)* and *Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) Update* reports for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (State Routes 99 and 132, Stanislaus County).  
- The approved Final Workplan (September 2012) provided for additional characterization of soil stockpiles to evaluate lateral/vertical migration of contaminants and to update the HHRA, supporting preparation of a Remedial Action Plan.  
- DTSC’s February 15, 2013 comments were addressed in a Response to Comments letter, leading to revision of the SSI and HHRA Update (both dated March 1, 2013), which DTSC now accepts.

## Methodology
- **Experimental setup/design:** The letter does not describe a controlled experiment. It references an Interagency Agreement and Task Order for DTSC oversight of soil stockpile management, including implementation of a Final Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan. Additional characterization was performed in late September 2012.  
- **Dataset:** The underlying reports (SSI and HHRA Update) were generated by Geocon Consultants Inc. and submitted to DTSC. The letter indicates the reports contain site-specific soil characterization data and risk calculations, but no numerical data are provided in the letter itself.  
- **Evaluation metrics:** DTSC reviewed the reports for compliance with regulatory requirements (e.g., characterization of contaminant migration, updated risk estimates). No specific metrics (e.g., confidence intervals, threshold exceedances) are stated in the letter.
