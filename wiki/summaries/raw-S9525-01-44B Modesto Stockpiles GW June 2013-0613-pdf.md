### Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units:**
  - **Dissolved metals (Title 22) in groundwater (µg/l):**
    - Barium: 45 (MW-3) to 230 (MW-5) — all below Primary MCL (1,000 µg/l)
    - Strontium: 250 (MW-8) to 1,000 (MW-1, MW-10) — all below EPA Health Advisory (4,000 µg/l)
    - Arsenic: 2.0 (MW-4) to 4.8 (MW-3) — all below Primary MCL (10 µg/l)
    - Chromium: 0.77 (MW-10) to 7.5 (MW-6) — all below Primary MCL (50 µg/l)
    - Vanadium: 17 (MW-5) to 35 (MW-6) — all below Notification Level (50 µg/l)
    - Lead: Not detected > PQL (1.0 µg/l) in any well
    - Appendix C also reports results for calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium, and other constituents.
  - **General minerals in groundwater (mg/l):**
    - Nitrate as N: 3.3 (MW-3) to 20 (MW-6, MW-10) — exceeded Primary MCL (10 mg/l) in MW-1 (12), MW-5 (17), MW-6 (20), MW-9 (12), MW-10 (20)
    - TDS: 340 (MW-7) to 620 (MW-6, MW-10) — exceeded Secondary MCL (500 mg/l) in MW-1 (520), MW-5 (530), MW-6 (620), MW-10 (620)
    - Chloride, sulfate, bicarbonate alkalinity also reported.
- **Applicable thresholds:** California Primary MCLs, Secondary MCLs, EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories, CDPH Notification/Regulatory Action Levels.
- **Wells exceeding thresholds (June 2013):** Nitrate > 10 mg/l: MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, MW-9, MW-10; TDS > 500 mg/l: MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, MW-10.
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations:**
  - Equipment blank: Nickel detected at 1.8 µg/l; sample results for nickel considered unaffected.
  - Matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate (MS/MSD) recoveries outside control limits for some analytes; data validated by laboratory control samples — "no qualification of the data is necessary."
  - Several wells required multiple purging/recovery cycles due to going dry (MW-5, MW-6).
  - Appendix C: Sample D6 (dilution) applied to calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium, and strontium in MW-1 and MW-2 due to high concentrations; M1 flag for matrix spike recovery outside limits but validated by LCS.
  - *Note:* The summary data for MW-1 and MW-2 in the lab report appear nearly identical, which may be a data entry or extraction error; however, the main report shows distinct results for each well.
