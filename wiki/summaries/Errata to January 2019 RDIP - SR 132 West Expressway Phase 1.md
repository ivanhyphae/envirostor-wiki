## Key claims
- This document is an **errata/update to the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP)** for the State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project, specifically **Interim Phase 1**.
- Its main purpose is to **revise project roles and responsibilities** after construction contract award, including **removing Geocon as the state’s consultant/agent** and clarifying that **Caltrans remains the responsible party** for ensuring compliance with the RAP/RDIP.
- The errata confirms the **project organization**, identifies responsible agencies and contractors, and clarifies oversight relationships among **Caltrans, DTSC, CVRWQCB, the City of Modesto, and the construction contractor**.
- It specifies the operational requirements for managing **barium-containing soil stockpiles** during highway construction, including excavation, containment, verification sampling, dust control, stormwater controls, and final capping.
- It establishes the conditions under which **land use covenants (LUCs)/deed restrictions** are required for completed containment zones.

## Methodology
- This is a **project implementation / regulatory compliance document**, not an experimental study.
- The “methods” are construction and remediation procedures for managing barium-containing soil stockpiles at the SR-132 corridor:
  - Site preparation: mowing vegetation to **2 inches**, discing/ripping, moisture conditioning, and recompaction.
  - Excavation oversight by a **California Professional Geologist (PG)** or supervised field geologist/engineer.
  - Documentation through **daily field logs**, photographs, chain-of-custody records, and monthly status reports.
  - **Verification sampling** of excavated areas and activity areas.
  - **Air monitoring** for fugitive dust, plus **groundwater and stormwater monitoring** consistent with existing protocols.
  - **Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)** and best management practices for erosion/runoff control.
- Sampling approach:
  - Verification samples collected from completed excavation areas at **1 sample per 5,000 sq ft** of removal area and **1 sample per 100 linear feet** of activity area.
  - Samples collected with **new/precleaned stainless trowels**, placed in lab containers, and submitted via chain of custody to a **California-certified laboratory**.
- Analytical methods:
  - Testing for **barium and lead** using **EPA Method 6010B**.
  - Target reporting limit: **1.0 mg/kg**.
  - Results compared against threshold levels of **1,000 mg/kg for barium** and **80 mg/kg for lead**.
- Decision criterion:
  - Removal is considered complete when analytical results (total values or **95% UCLs**) are below the threshold levels.
- The document also references regulatory review/approval of prior RDIP drafts by **DTSC**.

## Results
- The errata itself does not report experimental results; it reports **administrative and procedural changes** plus implementation requirements.
- It states that DTSC issued:
  - a **Conditional Approval Letter dated August 20, 2018** for the July 18, 2018 draft RDIP, and
  - a **Conditional Approval Letter dated October 12, 2018** for the September 14, 2018 revised RDIP.
- The final RDIP includes excerpts of the **100% design plans**, with full plans and final specifications to be submitted in early 2019.
- Sampling and threshold criteria specified:
  - **Barium threshold: 1,000 mg/kg**
  - **Lead threshold: 80 mg/kg**
  - **Reporting limit: 1.0 mg/kg**
- Spatial limits identified for containment zones:
  - **Stockpile 1:** SR-132 stations approximately **1125+60 to 1131+95**
  - **Stockpile 2:** SR-132 stations approximately **1135+90 to 1148+30**
- Capping requirements:
  - Minimum **12-inch clean fill cover** for completed containment zones, or
  - Minimum **4-inch AB material** where structural pavement/hardscape/additional embankment fill will be placed.
- Soil stockpile removal areas at the **western and eastern ends of Stockpile 2** and **all of Stockpile 3** are stated to **not require a LUC** once completed.

## Limitations
- This is an **errata document**, so it mainly provides corrections and clarifications rather than new technical findings.
- It does not present outcome data on remediation effectiveness, contamination levels, or construction performance.
- Several details are described as **anticipated** or subject to change, including:
  - personnel assigned to project roles,
  - final clean-fill source areas,
  - some construction specifications and timing,
  - final design plan submission timing.
- The document notes that **Caltrans, not the consultant, is ultimately responsible** for compliance, indicating that responsibility is shared across parties but accountability rests with the agency.
- Groundwater monitoring is described as occurring **annually, usually in April**, but the document does not provide monitoring results.
- The HSP notes that most COPCs are below residential screening levels, but **barium and lead are elevated**; however, no detailed risk calculations are included in this errata.
