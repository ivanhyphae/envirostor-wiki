## Key claims
- This document is a post-construction contract award errata to the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) for the SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project (Interim Phase 1).
- It updates roles and responsibilities, removing Geocon Consultants, Inc. as the designated consultant and clarifying that Caltrans remains the responsible party for implementing the RDIP.
- The project team is reorganized to include Caltrans (site owner/ responsible party), DTSC and CVRWQCB (regulatory agencies), City of Modesto/Stanislaus Council of Governments (project proponent), Dokken Engineering (civil engineer), WSP, USA (construction manager), and Bay Cities Paving and Grading, Inc. (contractor).
- Caltrans will supervise barium-containing soil excavation, fill placement, capping, verification sampling, air monitoring, stormwater monitoring, and preparation of a Remedial Action Completion Report.
- The City’s team will execute physical work: stockpile clearing, grading, barium-containing soil removal/consolidation, dust/stormwater management, and placement of clean cover.
- Verification sampling is performed at one sample per 5,000 ft² of removal area and one per 100 lineal ft of activity area, with analysis for barium and lead by EPA Method 6010B (reporting limit 1 mg/kg). Threshold levels: barium < 1,000 mg/kg, lead < 80 mg/kg.
- Completed consolidation zones within Stockpiles 1 and 2 will be capped with ≥12 in of clean imported fill (or ≥4 in AB material under pavement) and require a land use covenant (LUC). The fully removed western/eastern ends of Stockpile 2 and all of Stockpile 3 do not require a LUC.
- The contaminated soil is barium- and lead-containing with COPCs generally below residential screening levels except for barium and lead; health and safety measures (water spray, air monitoring) are described in an HSP (Appendix F).

## Methodology
- **Setup**: The errata references an already-approved RDIP (DTSC conditional approvals August 20, 2018 and October 12, 2018). It updates organizational roles for Interim Phase 1 construction of SR 132 West.
- **Datasets**: Not a research dataset; the paper describes planned field procedures: clearing, mowing, ripping/discing, moisture conditioning, recompaction, and verification sampling.
- **Evaluation metrics**: No experimental evaluation. Verification sampling uses threshold concentrations for barium (1,000 mg/kg) and lead (80 mg/kg). Compliance is confirmed when verification soil sample results (or 95% UCLs) are below thresholds.
- **Quality control**: Sampling uses new/precleaned stainless trowels, chain-of-custody, California-certified laboratory, and EPA Method 6010B. Air monitoring for fugitive dust and stop‑work authority for visible dust or runoff are specified.

## Results
- No new quantitative results are presented; the document is a procedural update.
- The key numerical specifications are:
  - Verification sampling density: 1 per 5,000 ft² (removal area) and 1 per 100 lineal ft (activity area).
  - Barium threshold: < 1,000 mg/kg.
  - Lead threshold: < 80 mg/kg.
  - Reporting limit for barium/lead: 1.0 mg/kg.
  - Clean cover thickness: ≥12 in of imported clean fill or ≥4 in of AB material under pavement/hardscape.
  - Mowing height: minimum 2 in remaining vegetation.
- The consolidation zones correspond to SR 132 stations 1125+60–1131+95 (Stockpile 1) and 1135+90–1148+30 (Stockpile 2).

## Limitations
- The errata addresses only the **Interim Phase 1** of construction; ultimate Phase 2 is not covered.
- Personnel assignments are anticipated to change over the project duration.
- The document acknowledges that health risk screening levels for COPCs generally fall below residential thresholds, but barium and lead remain elevated, requiring continued dust control and air monitoring.
- A LUC/deed restriction is required only for the completed containment zones, not for fully removed stockpile areas.
- The clean fill source areas are not yet finalized; they will be sampled and pre‑approved by DTSC prior to placement.
