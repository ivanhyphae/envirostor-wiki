## Key claims
- Updates the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) for Interim Phase 1 of the SR-132 West Project to reflect roles and responsibilities after a post-construction contract award, specifically removing Geocon Consultants as the implementing agent and assigning Caltrans as the responsible party.
- Clarifies the project team structure including Caltrans (site owner/responsible party), DTSC and CVRWQCB (regulatory agencies), City of Modesto (project proponent), Dokken Engineering (civil engineer), WSP USA (construction manager), and Bay Cities Paving and Grading (contractor).
- Specifies detailed procedures for excavation, verification sampling, fill placement, capping, and monitoring of barium‑containing soil stockpiles to comply with the approved Remedial Action Plan (RAP) and DTSC conditional approval letters.

## Methodology
- **Excavation and verification sampling:** Barium‑containing soil removal is verified by targeted excavation depths (based on original ground surface elevations), field observations by a California Professional Geologist, and soil sampling at a rate of one sample per 5,000 ft² of removal area and one sample per 100 lineal feet of activity area. Samples are collected using hand‑sampling methods with clean stainless trowels, placed in laboratory‑provided glass containers, and delivered to a California‑certified laboratory under chain‑of‑custody.
- **Analytical methods:** Verification samples are analyzed for barium and lead using EPA Test Method 6010B with a targeted reporting limit of 1.0 mg/kg.
- **Thresholds:** Removal is confirmed when verification results (total values or 95% UCLs) are below 1,000 mg/kg for barium and 80 mg/kg for lead.
- **Capping:** Completed consolidation zones (Stockpiles 1 and 2) are capped with minimum 12‑inch‑thick clean imported fill or minimum 4‑inch‑thick aggregate base (AB) material pending pavement or hardscape. Clean fill sources are sampled per DTSC’s Clean Imported Fill Material advisory.
- **Monitoring:** Full‑time Caltrans oversight during barium‑containing soil activities; air monitoring for fugitive dust (Section 5.3); periodic stormwater monitoring; and annual groundwater monitoring per an existing DTSC‑approved plan (Shaw, 2006).

## Results
- No quantitative results (e.g., measured concentrations, volumes removed) are reported in this errata document. The text is a procedural update and does not include data or outcomes from implemented remediation.

## Limitations
- Roles and contact persons may change over the project duration (Section 2.2).
- The document applies only to Interim Phase 1 of the SR‑132 West project; Ultimate Phase 2 is referenced but not detailed.
- The errata supersedes Geocon’s role but notes that regardless of consultant, Caltrans remains ultimately responsible.
- Dust control measures (water truck/spray) may be inadequate during high winds; work is suspended under such conditions.
- The RDIP and this errata are specific to the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles and the containment alternative; generalizability beyond this site is not addressed.
