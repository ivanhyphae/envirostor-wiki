The source document is an interim removal-action completion report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles associated with the SR 132 West Project in Stanislaus County, California, prepared under DTSC and Central Valley RWQCB oversight. It describes a containment-based remedy for barium-containing soil (BCS): stockpiled contaminated soil was excavated, consolidated into designated containment zones, reused as highway embankment fill where appropriate, and then capped with clean fill, aggregate base, pavement, and/or other structural cover. The report ties this work to the Final RAP, the 2019 RDIP, and approved RDIP variances, and it also lays out long-term operations and maintenance requirements for the capped areas.

Field work focused on interim Phase 1 cleanup and verification at Stockpiles 1–3, including excavation of Stockpile 3, removal of the west and east ends of Stockpile 2, work at the southern slopes and MSE footing areas at Stockpiles 1 and 2, relocation of ADL-contaminated soil, and placement of clean cap materials. Confirmation and verification samples were collected from excavation bottoms, berms, footing areas, and other cut surfaces and analyzed primarily for barium and lead by EPA Method 6010B, with clean-fill suitability evaluated against DTSC guidance and site-specific background concentrations. Air, groundwater, and stormwater monitoring were also performed during the work, using perimeter and worker air sampling, remaining groundwater wells, and stormwater perimeter sampling.

Overall, the verification results showed that where initial samples exceeded project thresholds, re-excavation and deeper cuts brought the soils into compliance. For Stockpile 3, about 27,675 cubic yards of soil were removed; a few initial confirmation samples exceeded the 1,000 mg/kg barium cleanup goal, but after deeper excavation all remaining samples met the barium and lead thresholds. Stockpile 2 west and east end areas similarly had some initial exceedances of the barium criterion, followed by additional excavation and resampling that brought the final conditions below cleanup goals. Southern slope and footing-area samples for Stockpiles 1 and 2 were generally below cleanup thresholds, and statistical evaluation of Stockpile 1 footing data produced a 95% upper confidence limit for barium of 171.9 mg/kg, supporting compliance.

The report also includes characterization of potential clean cover-fill sources. The Basin 5 clean cap borrow area showed background-level metals and no detectable mercury or organochlorine pesticides, supporting its use as clean fill. By contrast, Bent 2 excavation stockpiles did not fully meet clean-cap criteria because barium exceeded the threshold in part of the dataset, while Carpenter Road Shoofly material met clean cap criteria with barium and lead within site-specific background levels. Monitoring during the interim work found no exceedances of air action levels for dust, barium, or lead, and the report states there was no documented offsite migration during the interim operations.
