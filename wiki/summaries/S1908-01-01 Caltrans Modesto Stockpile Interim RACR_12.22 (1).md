This Interim Removal Action Completion Report (RACR) documents the successful Phase 1 remediation of barium-containing soil (BCS) from Stockpiles 1–3 on the State Route 132 West Project in Modesto, California. The selected remedy, approved by the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), consolidated BCS within designated containment zones (Stockpiles 1 and 2) and covered it with clean fill and structural pavement. All verification sampling demonstrated that barium (threshold 1,000 mg/kg) and lead (threshold 80 mg/kg) met required levels, with final barium concentrations ranging from 46 to 970 mg/kg and lead from 1.5 to 40 mg/kg. A final RACR will follow Ultimate Phase 2 construction, planned for 2028.

Work was performed under the January 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) and two variance requests. BCS was excavated from Stockpile 3 and the west and east ends of Stockpile 2, then transported and compacted as embankment fill within containment zones. Verification soil samples were collected at the base of excavations—29 from Stockpile 3 (26 locations), 10 from Stockpile 2 west (8 locations), and 14 from Stockpile 2 east (12 locations)—using EPA Method 6010B for total metals. Where initial barium exceeded 1,000 mg/kg, excavations were deepened approximately 1 foot and resampled. Characterization of clean cap borrow areas (Basin 5, Carpenter Shoofly, Bent 2, and the Stockpile 1/2 MSE wall footing excavations) involved discrete and composite samples analyzed for Title 22 metals via EPA Methods 6010B, 7471A, and 8081A. Air monitoring during BCS grading (February–June 2020) employed real-time particulate monitors (pDR-1200) and active sampling for total dust (NIOSH 0500), barium, and lead (NIOSH 7300 modified), with fence-line action levels of 4.0 mg/m³ (dust), 25 µg/m³ (barium), and 1.5 µg/m³ (lead).

All final verification samples met thresholds. Stockpile 3: barium 46–970 mg/kg, lead 1.6–40 mg/kg; Stockpile 2 west: barium 54–430 mg/kg, lead 1.5–2.8 mg/kg; Stockpile 2 east: barium 73–760 mg/kg, lead 1.6–35 mg/kg. Stockpile 1 MSE wall footing excavations (0–5 ft depth) had barium 42–420 mg/kg (95% UCL = 113.1 mg/kg) and lead <1.0–6.7 mg/kg. Stockpile 2 MSE wall excavations showed barium 53–930 mg/kg (upper 3 ft above site-specific background of 120 mg/kg; deeper material acceptable for wider reuse). Clean cap borrow areas—Basin 5 and Carpenter Shoofly—yielded metal concentrations consistent with natural background; Bent 2 stockpiles (barium up to 230 mg/kg) exceeded background and were unsuitable for clean cap. Air monitoring results were below all action levels: total dust <0.041–0.52 mg/m³, barium mostly <0.86–2.5 µg/m³ (maximum 6.8 µg/m³), and lead <0.41–0.82 µg/m³.

Several limitations apply. This is an interim report; a final RACR will be submitted after Ultimate Phase 2 construction (scheduled for 2028). The clean cap along northerly slopes remains interim pending Phase 2 retaining walls and highway pavement. A land use covenant is required before DTSC certification. During construction, aerially deposited lead (ADL) soil (>80 mg/kg lead) from SR 99 was also placed within containment zones per a 2016 DTSC/Caltrans agreement. A record rain event in January 2021 caused erosion that was subsequently repaired with native hydroseed and erosion control blankets (September–October 2021). Some matrix spike recoveries fell outside acceptance limits due to matrix interference, but laboratory control samples validated the batches; composite sampling may obscure localized variability. The draft RACR was accepted as a technical status report, with as-built plans, survey data, and an Operations & Maintenance Plan pending completion for the final Phase 1 RACR.
