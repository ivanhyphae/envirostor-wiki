# Summary of "Caltrans Modesto Stockpile Interim RACR"

This interim Removal Action Completion Report (RACR) documents the successful interim containment of barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles along State Route 132 in Modesto, California, as part of Phase 1 construction. The primary remediation strategy involved consolidating BCS from Stockpiles 1–3 and SR 99 aerially deposited lead (ADL) zones into designated containment zones within Stockpiles 1 and 2, compacting the material as highway embankment fill, and capping it with clean fill and structural pavement.

**Methodology**  
Remediation activities included excavating BCS, transporting it to containment zones, and constructing clean covers sourced from approved borrow areas—including Carpenter Road Shoofly soil and mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall footing excavations. Verification sampling targeted thresholds of ≤1,000 mg/kg total barium and ≤80 mg/kg total lead. Air monitoring during grading operations (February–June 2020) used real-time particulate counters and filter analysis for total dust (NIOSH 0500), barium, and lead (NIOSH 7300 modified), with action levels set at 4.0 mg/m³ (dust), 25 µg/m³ (barium), and 1.5 µg/m³ (lead).

**Results**  
All verification samples met thresholds: Stockpile 3 relocated approximately 38,440 yd³ of BCS with barium ranging 46–970 mg/kg and lead 1.6–40 mg/kg; Stockpile 2 end areas showed barium 54–760 mg/kg and lead 1.5–35 mg/kg. Carpenter Road Shoofly soil (5,000 yd³) was suitable for clean cap (barium 28–96 mg/kg), while Bent 2 excavation soil partially exceeded the site-specific background of 120 mg/kg (barium up to 230 mg/kg), limiting its use. The proposed Basin 5 borrow area yielded barium 66–94 mg/kg and other Title 22 metals consistent with background. Air monitoring found no exceedances: total dust ≤0.076 mg/m³ (8-hour TWA), barium <0.95 µg/m³, lead <0.50 µg/m³, with maximum downwind barium reaching 6.8 µg/m³—still below action levels.

**Limitations and Future Steps**  
This interim RACR covers only Phase 1; a Final RACR will follow Ultimate Phase 2 construction (scheduled approximately 2028). A record rain event caused cap erosion on northern slopes, requiring regrading and revegetation. The Land Use Covenant (LUC) and five-year reviews remain pending. An Operations and Maintenance (O&M) Plan specifies annual inspections, DTSC notification for any BCS disturbance, and documentation requirements. Groundwater monitoring wells have been destroyed or protected, and stormwater monitoring will be suspended permanently. The Draft Phase 1 RACR was accepted by DTSC as a technical status report, with as-built plans, final survey data, and the O&M Plan still to be completed.
