## Key claims
- The document is a regulatory concurrence letter from the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) regarding three groundwater monitoring reports submitted by the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site.
- DTSC, in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), confirms receipt and review of the May, June, and July 2012 monitoring reports prepared by Geocon Consultants.
- The soil stockpiles originated from excess native soils and pond tailings generated during construction of State Route 99 (SR-99) in the early 1960s.
- The construction site included a portion of a former 4.3-acre parcel owned by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC), which operated a chemical manufacturing facility processing barium and strontium sulfate ores.
- The primary chemicals of concern (COCs) identified are barium, lead, and strontium.
- DTSC formally concurs with the findings and conclusions presented in the submitted groundwater monitoring reports.
