## Key claims
- DTSC, in consultation with RWQCB, reviewed three groundwater monitoring reports (May, June, July 2012) for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site at State Routes 99 and 132 in Stanislaus County, California.
- The site contains stockpiles of excess native soils and pond tailings from 1960s SR 99 construction on a former FMC chemical manufacturing property (barium sulfate, strontium sulfate processing).
- Two additional monitoring wells were installed in June 2012: MW-9 (south of Kansas Ave, west of SR 99) and MW-10 (south of Kansas Ave, east of SR 99).
- The primary chemicals of concern (COC) are barium, lead, and strontium.
- DTSC concurred with the subject reports and requested submission of future reports as data becomes available.

## Methodology
- Three separate groundwater monitoring events (May, June, July 2012) were conducted by Geocon Consultants.
- The June 2012 report additionally documented the installation of two new monitoring wells (MW-9, MW-10).
- Monitoring wells were sampled and analytical results were presented in each report.
- The study area is the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site along SR 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California, on a 4.3-acre parcel formerly owned by FMC.

## Results
- No specific numerical results (concentrations of COCs, detection limits, or exceedances) are provided in this review letter.
- DTSC did not identify any deficiencies or non-compliance with the submitted reports; concurrence was granted.

## Limitations
- This document is a regulatory concurrence/approval letter, not a full study. No limitations or caveats regarding data quality, sample representativeness, or analytical methods are discussed.
- Future monitoring reports were requested "as groundwater data becomes available," implying ongoing monitoring needs are not yet complete.
