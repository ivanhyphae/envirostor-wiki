## Key claims
- DTSC, in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), reviewed three groundwater monitoring reports for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site near SR 99 and SR 132.
- The reports cover groundwater monitoring sampling conducted in May, June, and July 2012, and a June 2012 update on installation of two new monitoring wells.
- DTSC states it concurs with the submitted groundwater monitoring reports.
- DTSC requests continued future groundwater monitoring reports as additional groundwater data become available.
- The site’s primary chemicals of concern (COCs) are barium, lead, and strontium.

## Methodology
- This is a regulatory review letter, not an experimental research paper.
- Reviewed documents:
  1. “Groundwater Monitoring Report - May 2012, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route (SR) 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California”
  2. “Additional Well Installation and Groundwater Monitoring Report - June 2012, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California”
  3. “Groundwater Monitoring Report - July 2012, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California”
- The underlying reports present analytical results from groundwater monitoring well sampling performed in May, June, and July 2012.
- The June 2012 report additionally documents installation of monitoring wells MW-9 and MW-10:
  - MW-9: south of Kansas Avenue and west of SR 99
  - MW-10: south of Kansas Avenue and east of SR 99
- No explicit performance metrics or statistical evaluation framework is described in the letter itself.

## Results
- The letter does not report numerical contaminant concentrations or trend statistics.
- The main result is administrative/technical concurrence: DTSC agrees with the groundwater monitoring reports.
- Monitoring infrastructure expansion in June 2012 included two new wells, MW-9 and MW-10, improving site monitoring coverage.
- The site context indicates the stockpiles are composed of excess native soils and pond tailings generated during early-1960s SR 99 construction over part of a 4.3-acre parcel formerly associated with FMC.
- The contamination concern is linked to historical chemical manufacturing activities involving barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores and other minerals.

## Limitations
- The document is a brief review letter and does not include the full analytical dataset, laboratory values, or detailed interpretation of groundwater quality.
- No numerical results, exceedances, or plume characterization are provided in the available text.
- The letter only covers three monthly monitoring reports and requests continued reporting as more groundwater data are collected, indicating the assessment is ongoing.
