## Document identity

- **Document type and purpose**: Updated comparative evaluation of groundwater data assessing whether barium-contaminated soil stockpiles under Caltrans right-of-way have impacted groundwater; recommendations to decommission remaining monitoring wells and discontinue monitoring
- **Author or preparer**: Geocon Consultants, Inc. (Rebecca L. Silva, Project Manager; Dr. Andrew Kopania, PG, CHG, Senior Hydrogeologist)
- **Date or period covered**: Report dated February 26, 2024; data evaluated from 2014 through February 2023

## Key claims

- Encapsulated soil stockpiles do not have the potential to impact groundwater (Geocon, p. 5)
- Future monitoring data from the four remaining Caltrans wells will not provide useful data for further analysis (Geocon, p. 5)
- Groundwater impacts in wells MW5, MW6, and MW10 did not originate from Caltrans soil stockpiles; decreasing trends attributed to reduction of source mass by the FMC groundwater extraction and treatment system (EMKO 2014 Evaluation, cited in Geocon report, p. 3)
- Barium concentrations in remaining Caltrans wells are well below the U.S. EPA drinking-water health advisory (DWHA) concentration of 700 µg/L and about 40 times less than the health-based site-specific target level for barium in groundwater at the FMC facility of 6,210 µg/L approved by the Central Valley RWQCB (Geocon, p. 4)
- Strontium concentrations in remaining Caltrans wells are, on average, about four times less than the U.S. EPA DWHA concentration of 4,000 µg/L and more than 20 times less than the site-specific target level of 61,900 µg/L (Geocon, p. 4)
- Since 2019, sulfate concentration in remaining Caltrans monitoring wells has been less than 50 mg/L (Geocon, p. 4)
- Due to decreasing regional groundwater levels, the four remaining Caltrans wells have been dry on numerous occasions since 2014 (Geocon, pp. 4-5)

## Regulatory decisions

- Central Valley RWQCB Cleanup and Abatement Order No. 98-724 and Monitoring and Reporting Program No. 98-805 (June 26, 1998) — addressed FMC Corporation Modesto facility groundwater impacts (cited, p. 7)
- Central Valley RWQCB approved health-based site-specific target levels for barium (6,210 µg/L) and strontium (61,900 µg/L) in groundwater at the FMC facility in the Remedial Action Plan (ERM, 2020) (Geocon, p. 4)
- Groundwater monitoring frequency reduced from quarterly to annually in 2015 (Geocon, p. 3)
- Six of ten Caltrans monitoring wells decommissioned in 2019 (Geocon, p. 3)

**Well dryness frequency (2014–2023)**:
- MW4: dry during 4 of 11 sampling events (Sept 2014, May 2016, Mar 2022, Feb 2023)
- MW6: dry during 6 of 11 events (Sept 2014–Apr 2017, Mar 2022, Feb 2023)
- MW9: dry during 8 of 11 events (Sept 2014–Apr 2017, Apr 2019, Mar 2021–Feb 2023)
- MW10: dry during 9 of 11 events (Sept 2014–Apr 2017, Apr 2019–Feb 2023)

## Remedial actions

- Stockpile 3 removed; material placed on Stockpiles 1 and 2 (Phase I construction, began January 2020)
- Stockpiles 1 and 2 encapsulated beneath SR 132 roadway with layer of clean fill and vegetated soil on northern slope (completed by October 2021)
- SR 132 opened to traffic in November 2022
- Phase II construction for SR 132 scheduled to begin in 2026 — will fully encapsulate soil stockpiles beneath highway pavement and perimeter retaining walls
- Phase II work anticipated to require removal of well MW9 (retaining wall construction) and MW10 (new pump station construction)
- FMC groundwater extraction and treatment system operating since 1996 — approximately 1 billion gallons treated (Parsons, 2013, cited p. 2)
