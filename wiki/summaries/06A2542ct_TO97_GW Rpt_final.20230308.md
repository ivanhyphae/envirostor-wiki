## Key claims
This is a regulatory groundwater monitoring report documenting that all four monitoring wells (MW-4, MW-6, MW-9, and MW-10) at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project site were found to be dry during both the November/December 2022 gauging event and the February 21, 2023 monitoring/sampling event. No groundwater samples were collected or analyzed. The site is associated with former barium processing operations by Barium Products Ltd. (later FMC Corporation), where liquid residue was discharged to unlined ponds. Three soil stockpiles totaling over 970,000 cubic yards were constructed in the 1960s and subsequently encapsulated beneath the structural section of SR 132. Six of the original ten monitoring wells were decommissioned in 2019.

## Methodology
- **Monitoring well network**: Four wells (MW-4, MW-6, MW-9, and MW-10) constructed of SCH 40 PVC, 2-inch diameter, with screens set between approximately 29.5-43 feet below ground surface (sand filter pack #2/12). Six additional wells (MW-1, MW-2, MW-3, MW-5, MW-7, MW-8) were decommissioned in 2019.
- **Gauging procedure**: Water levels measured using GeoTech water level meter to 0.01 ft BTOC from surveyed reference points. Meter decontaminated with Liquinox™ and de-ionized water between wells.
- **Event dates**: November 2, 2022 (MW-4, MW-6, MW-9); December 13, 2022 (MW-10, after locating with metal detector); February 21, 2023 (all four wells).
- **Monitoring frequency**: Groundwater sampling/monitoring annually, depth to water measurements semi-annually.
- **No laboratory analysis** was performed as all wells were dry.

## Results
- All four wells (MW-4, MW-6, MW-9, MW-10) were dry on all measurement dates (November 2, 2022; December 13, 2022; February 21, 2023).
- Groundwater flow directions and gradients were not calculated due to dry conditions.
- Historic groundwater flow direction has generally been toward the south to southeast with hydraulic gradient varying from 0.0006 to 0.004.
- TOC elevations (AMSL): MW-4 = 82.47 ft, MW-6 = 84.52 ft, MW-9 = 82.53 ft, MW-10 = 83.97 ft.
- Well depths (ft): MW-4 = 40.00, MW-6 = 43.80, MW-9 = 39.65, MW-10 = 39.57.
- Historic groundwater elevations at these wells have ranged from approximately 41.22 ft to 49.76 ft AMSL (MW-4), 41.22 to 49.19 ft (MW-6), 43.31 to 48.86 ft (MW-9), and 44.73 to 48.79 ft (MW-10).
- Well MW-10 box was destroyed during site work; PVC casing undamaged; box replaced by contractor. Not located on November 2, 2022 due to thick layer of wood mulch.

## Limitations
- Wells were dry; therefore, groundwater samples were not collected for laboratory analysis.
- Well MW-10 could not be located on November 2, 2022; its well box had been destroyed during site work, though the PVC casing was undamaged.
- The report states that Stantec did not verify information supplied to it by others.
- The document reflects professional judgment based on conditions at the time and does not account for subsequent changes.
- Data completeness is limited by the dry well conditions; no statistical evaluation possible.
- Historic monitoring data exists but represents conditions prior to site encapsulation and construction activities.
