This report presents long-term (2006–2023) groundwater monitoring results for the Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project, focusing on monitoring wells MW-1 through MW-10. A significant finding during the current reporting period (2022–2023) is that all four remaining active wells (MW-4, MW-6, MW-9, and MW-10) were dry during both the November/December 2022 gauging event and the February 2023 annual sampling event. As a result, no groundwater samples could be collected, groundwater flow direction and gradient could not be calculated, and no laboratory analyses were performed for these wellsordingly.

Historically, groundwater samples from the other wells (MW-1 through MW-8) were analyzed for dissolved Title 22 metals, general minerals, and PAHs. Most metal concentrations remained below detection limits, with some consistent detections of barium, chromium, nickel, manganese, and strontium. General minerals analysis showed total dissolved solids ranging from 130–660 mg/L and chloride from 140–1,100 mg/L across wells. PAH concentrations were typically below detection limits.

The report documents several limitations: many wells experienced insufficient water for sampling from 2014–2023, resulting in substantial data gaps. Well MW-10 was initially inaccessible due to mulch burial with a destroyed well box alerting. Additionally, some results were flagged as estimated concentrations (J flag) or non-detects due to equipment blank contamination, and laboratory error was noted for one sample. The report notes that six wells were decommissioned to accommodate highway construction, reducing the network to the four currently dry wells.
