## Document Identity

- **Document type and purpose:** DTSC approval letter for the "Final Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California." The workplan provides additional characterization of soil stockpiles to evaluate lateral and vertical migration of contaminants and to update the Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) for remedy finalization.
- **Author or preparer:** Randy S. Adams, C.E.G., Senior Engineering Geologist, DTSC Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program.
- **Date or period covered:** Letter dated September 20, 2012; workplan prepared September 2012.

## Key Claims

- Soil stockpiles consist of excess native soils and pond tailings generated during construction of a segment of SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue in the early 1960s.
- Excavation traversed a portion of a 4.3-acre parcel purchased from Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation Inc. (FMC), which contained an evaporation pond in its southernmost corner.
- FMC (and predecessors) was a chemical manufacturer that processed barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores and other minerals.
- Primary Chemicals of Concern (COCs) are barium, lead, and strontium.
- The workplan is intended to evaluate potential lateral and vertical migration of contaminants from the soil stockpiles and to update the HHRA. (Attribution: DTSC letter summarizing workplan content.)

## Regulatory Decisions

- **Decision type:** Concurrence (approval) with the Final Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan.
- **Agency:** DTSC, in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region (RWQCB).
- **Date:** September 20, 2012.
- **Conditions imposed:** No explicit conditions beyond concurrence; the workplan was revised to address DTSC comments dated September 12, 2012.

## Analytical Results

- **Contaminants identified:** Barium, lead, strontium (listed as primary COCs).
- **Media:** Soil stockpiles (excess native soils and pond tailings).
- **Concentration ranges, thresholds, exceedances:** Not provided in this document.

## Remedial Actions

- **Physical work described:** No remedial actions are described. The document approves a characterization workplan intended to support future remedy evaluation; no construction, removal, or treatment activities are reported.
