The Carpenter Road Shoofly Clean Fill Tech Memo, prepared by Geocon project manager John E. Juhrend, P.E., CEG, documents the characterization of native soil from the shoofly area for use as clean capping material over barium-contaminated soil (BCS) stockpiles at the SR 132 site. The memorandum, supported by a chain of custody (COC) and laboratory analytical reports from Certified Laboratory Services (CLS) and American Technologies Laboratory (ATL), confirms that the 5,000 cubic yards of soil meet clean fill criteria.

The investigation, conducted under a 48-hour expedited turnaround on April 7, 2021, involved collecting 24 discrete samples from six vertical transects (CSF1–CSF6) at depths of 2, 5, 10, and 15 feet. These were combined into eight composite samples for analysis of total barium and total lead by EPA Method 6010B.

Results showed that barium concentrations in all composites (28–96 mg/kg) were below the site-specific upperbound background threshold of 120 mg/kg established by a DTSC-approved variance. Lead concentrations (<1.3–3.8 mg/kg) were at or below the corresponding background threshold of 3.8 mg/kg. Quality control data (laboratory control samples, matrix spikes, and blanks) met all acceptance criteria.

Key responsible parties include Geocon (consultant/sampler), CLS and ATL (analytical labs), Caltrans (owner/operator), and the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC, approval authority). The document concludes that the soil is suitable for use as clean capping material over BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2, pending DTSC approval. Final results will be included in the Removal Action Completion Report following completion of capping activities.
