### Site and Scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Modesto Soil Stockpiles, located southerly of the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue in Stanislaus County, California (also referenced as SR 132 area). Three soil stockpiles (Stockpile 1, 2, 3) are present within Caltrans right-of-way, adjacent to the former FMC property and former evaporation/disposal ponds.
- **Media addressed:** Soil (borings for well installation), groundwater (monitoring wells).
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** Existing wells MW-1 through MW-8; newly installed wells MW-9 and MW-10. Groundwater sampling locations for June 2012 event: MW-9 and MW-10 only. Stiff Diagrams prepared for MW-9 and MW-10.

### Key Claims / Findings
1. **Dissolved metals (barium, lead, strontium, etc.) in June 2012 samples from MW-9 and MW-10 did not exceed numeric water quality thresholds, except manganese in MW-10 (290 µg/L) which exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L for taste/odor.**  
   *Supported by analytical data (Table 2).*

2. **No PAHs were detected at concentrations ≥ PQL in any June 2012 groundwater sample.**  
   *Supported by data (Table 3, lab results).*

3. **General minerals:** Nitrate in MW-9 (13 mg/L) exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L; TDS in both MW-9 (510 mg/L) and MW-10 (710 mg/L) exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L.  
   *Supported by data (Table 3).*

4. **Groundwater flow direction** in the shallow upper aquifer is generally toward the southeast with hydraulic gradients varying from 0.001 to 0.0008 (interpretive, based on water level data).

5. **Soil conditions** encountered for MW-9 and MW-10 are consistent with previous descriptions (alluvial soils of Modesto Formation), per boring logs.

6. **QA/QC findings:** Several matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate (MS/MSD) recoveries for dissolved metals (especially barium, strontium, and major ions like calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium) were outside laboratory control limits, attributed to high source concentrations. Geocon concluded no additional qualification was needed as dissolved metals concentrations were not near thresholds. The laboratory QC report also flagged "E" (above quantitation range), "M1" (matrix spike outside limits, batch validated by LCS), and "R" (RPD above limits) for major ions.

### Analytical Results
**Dissolved Metals (Title 22) – June 2012 (MW-9, MW-10):**  
- Barium: 67 µg/L (MW-9), 160 µg/L (MW-10) – below thresholds.  
- Lead: <1.0 µg/L (MW-9), 2.2 µg/L (MW-10) – below primary MCL of 15 µg/L.  
- Strontium: 840 µg/L (MW-9), 990 µg/L (MW-10) – below EPA Health Advisory of 4,000 µg/L.  
- Manganese: 43 µg/L (MW-9), **290 µg/L (MW-10)** – exceeds secondary MCL of 50 µg/L (5.8×).  
- Other metals (arsenic, chromium, molybdenum, nickel, selenium, vanadium, zinc) all below applicable thresholds.  
- Antimony, beryllium, cadmium, silver, thallium, mercury not detected above PQLs.

**General Minerals – June 2012:**  
- Nitrate as N: **13 mg/L (MW-9)** exceeds primary MCL of 10 mg/L (1.3×); 9.2 mg/L (MW-10) below.  
- TDS: **510 mg/L (MW-9)** and **710 mg/L (MW-10)** both exceed secondary MCL of 500 mg/L.  
- Chloride, sulfate: below secondary MCLs.  
- Bicarbonate alkalinity: 293 mg/L (MW-9), 356 mg/L (MW-10).

**PAHs (SIM):** All compounds non-detect in both wells (PQL = 0.20 µg/L).

### Remedial Actions
