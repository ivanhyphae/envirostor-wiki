## Key Claims
1. **Clean cap borrow area (Feb 2020 memo)**: "The proposed clean cap borrow area appears to be suitable to generate clean cover fill materials for capping BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2" – Claim made by Geocon (Juhrend) to WSP/City of Modesto. Supported by analytical data (12 direct-push borings, 3-part composites, Title 22 metals, OCPs). Metal concentrations reported consistent with site-specific background concentrations.

2. **Basin 5 clean cap borrow area (July 2020 memo)**: "The proposed Basin 5 clean cap borrow area appears to be suitable to generate clean cover fill materials for capping BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2" – Claim made by Geocon (Juhrend) to WSP/City of Modesto. Supported by analytical data (6 exploratory trenches, 3-part composites, Title 22 metals, OCPs). Metal concentrations consistent with natural background.

3. **Bent 2 excavation soil stockpiles (Oct 2020 memo)**: "The Bent 2 soil stockpiles do not meet the clean cap criteria as established in the referenced variance document as approved by DTSC (upperbound background threshold for barium of 120 mg/kg)" – Claim made by Geocon (Juhrend) to WSP/City of Modesto. Supported by analytical data (10 discrete samples, barium range 31–230 mg/kg). However, conditional option offered: "pending DTSC review and approval consideration, the material may be suitable for use as partial clean cap (6 inches or less) on the upper surface of Stockpile 2 since additional clean cap consisting of the planned structural pavement section will be placed over the entire upper surface."

4. **Carpenter Road Shoofly (Apr 2021 memo)**: "Soil material generated from excavation of the Carpenter Road Shoofly meet the BCS clean cap criteria as established in the referenced variance document as approved by DTSC (upperbound background threshold for barium of 120 mg/kg)" – Claim made by Geocon (Juhrend) to WSP/City of Modesto. Supported by analytical data (24 discrete samples, 8 composite samples; barium range 28–96 mg/kg; all below 120 mg/kg maximum background).

5. **Air monitoring (Sep 2020 memo)**: "The results of air monitoring performed during BCS grading activities for the Project indicate airborne levels of total dust, barium, and lead below designated project action and risk levels, confirming effective dust control measures" – Claim made by Geocon (Watts, Juhrend) to WSP/City of Modesto. Supported by data (real-time pDR-1200 measurements, laboratory analysis of filter cassettes). All results below action levels.
