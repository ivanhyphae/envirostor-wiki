## Combined Summary of "Stockpile 1 MSE Wall Sampling Tech Memo.pdf"

### Key Claims
- Soil from the Stockpile 1 MSE wall footing excavation, after removal of barium-containing soil (BCS), is suitable for placement within designated fill containment zones, general bridge embankment fill, or as capping material.
- Barium concentrations in all 15 samples exceeded the maximum site-specific background of 120 mg/kg but were below the RDIP removal threshold of 1,000 mg/kg.
- Lead concentrations were within the range of site-specific background (max 3.8 mg/kg) and below the RDIP removal threshold of 80 mg/kg.
- Quality control (blank, LCS, MS, MSD) for barium and lead met acceptance criteria (80–120% recovery; RPD ≤20), except for lead MS recovery at 84.7% (still within wide limits) and barium MS at 110%.
- ProUCL statistical analysis recommends a 95% H-UCL of 171.9 mg/kg for barium based on the data appearing lognormal after failing normal and gamma distribution tests.

### Methodology
- Soil samples were collected from three trenches (STK1-1, STK1-2, STK1-3) spaced at approximately 200-foot intervals along the MSE wall footing excavation midpoint.
- Discrete samples were taken from 1-foot intervals to a maximum depth of 5 feet, yielding 15 soil samples total.
- All samples were analyzed for total barium and lead using EPA Test Method 6010B (ICP-AES) after acid digestion (EPA 3050B) under 24-hour turnaround.
- Quality control procedures included method blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), matrix spikes (MS), and matrix spike duplicates (MSD). Acceptance criteria were 80–120% recovery for LCS and MS, and RPD ≤20% for duplicates.
- Statistical analysis was performed using ProUCL 5.1 on 13 barium observations (data set reduced from 15 due to potential outliers or QC issues). Goodness-of-fit tests (Shapiro-Wilk, Lilliefors, Anderson-Darling, Kolmogorov-Smirnov) were applied to determine distribution. Nonparametric and parametric upper confidence limits (UCLs) were generated; the recommended UCL is the 95% H-UCL for lognormal distributions.

### Results
- **Barium concentrations**: Ranged from 42 to 420 mg/kg (mean 128.3 mg/kg, 95% UCL 171.9 mg/kg). All values were ≤420 mg/kg, well below the 1,000 mg/kg removal threshold. Site-specific background maximum was 120 mg/kg, which was exceeded in all samples.
- **Lead concentrations**: Detected in 14 of 15 samples, ranging from 1.1 to 6.7 mg/kg. All were ≤6.7 mg/kg, below the 80 mg/kg removal threshold and mostly ≤3.8 mg/kg background. The practical quantitation limit (PQL) for both analytes was 1.0 mg/kg.
- **Quality control**: Method blank was non-detect. LCS recoveries: 87.6% (barium) and 90.7% (lead). MS/MSD recoveries: barium MS 110%, lead MS 84.7% (within wide limits 24–123% and 33–121% respectively). RPD values: 2.53% (barium) and 2.18% (lead).
- **Statistical summary for barium (n=13)**: Mean 128.3 mg/kg, SD 92.63, minimum 42, maximum 420, skewness 2.578. Data failed normality and gamma distribution tests at the 5% significance level and appeared lognormal. The recommended 95% UCL was the H-UCL of 171.9 mg/kg.
- **Regulatory Environmental Screening Levels (ESLs)** for barium: 3,000 mg/kg (construction workers), 15,000 mg/kg (residential), 220,000 mg/kg (commercial).

### Limitations
- Sampling was limited to three trenches at the midpoint of the wall footing excavation; spatial variability across the entire excavation is not fully characterized.
- Data derived from discrete 1-foot interval samples to a maximum depth of 5 feet; deeper or more laterally extensive sampling was not performed.
- Threshold comparisons rely on site-specific background and RDIP values; regulatory approval is still pending for some proposed uses.
- The data set is small (n=13 for barium statistics) and highly skewed (skewness >2.5), failing normality and gamma distribution tests. The recommended H-UCL may be unstable; ProUCL notes that the H-statistic can produce high or low values and nonparametric methods are often preferred for skewed data not following a gamma distribution.
- Quality control: The MS for lead showed recovery of 84.7%, which is within very wide acceptance limits but lower than ideal; barium MS recovery at 110% was at the upper bound.
