The memo documents characterization testing of soil excavated from the MSE wall footing area on the southerly slope of Stockpile 1 for the State Route 132 project in Modesto, California, after removal of barium-containing soil. The purpose was to determine whether the excavated footing material could be reused within site containment or embankment areas, such as fill or capping material.

Fifteen discrete soil samples were collected from three trenches spaced along the wall footing excavation midpoint and sampled at one-foot intervals to a planned depth of 5 feet. The samples were analyzed under chain of custody by Advanced Technology Laboratories using EPA Method 6010B (ICP-AES) for total metals, with reporting based on a 24-hour turnaround. The analytical focus in the provided sections was barium and lead, with results compared to site background concentrations, RDIP removal thresholds, and applicable ESLs.

The results show barium in all samples and lead in 14 of 15 samples. Barium ranged from 42 to 420 mg/kg, with a mean of 128.3 mg/kg and a 95% UCL of 171.9 mg/kg. Lead ranged from 1.1 to 6.7 mg/kg. Although some results exceeded site background values, both barium and lead were well below their respective RDIP removal thresholds of 1,000 mg/kg and 80 mg/kg. For barium, the statistical evaluation indicated the data were non-normal and non-gamma but consistent with a lognormal distribution, supporting the use of the 95% UCL of 171.9 mg/kg.

Quality control results for the analytical batch were acceptable overall, including a clean method blank and acceptable LCS, MS, and MSD recoveries, supporting the validity of the reported concentrations. Based on the data, the memo concludes that the excavated footing material is suitable for reuse as fill or capping material, pending regulatory approval.

The scope is limited to barium and lead in the sampled excavation footprint and does not address other contaminants or broader site variability outside the three trenches and 5-foot sampling depth.
