## Key claims
- Barium concentrations in MSE wall footing excavation at Stockpile 1 (42–420 mg/kg) exceeded site-specific background (120 mg/kg) but remained below the RDIP removal threshold (1,000 mg/kg).
- Lead concentrations (1.1–6.7 mg/kg) were within or below site-specific background (3.8 mg/kg) and below the RDIP removal threshold (80 mg/kg).
- The excavated material is deemed suitable for placement within Stockpiles 1 and 2 fill containment zones, as general bridge embankment fill, or as capping material pending regulatory approval.

## Methodology
- **Location**: Stockpile 1 southerly slope, State Route 132, Modesto, California.
- **Sampling**: Soil samples collected from three trenches (STK1-1, STK1-2, STK1-3) spaced at ~200-foot intervals, with discrete 1-foot intervals from 0 to 5 feet depth (15 samples total).
- **Analysis**: Total barium and lead by EPA Method 6010B (ICP-AES) at Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) under chain of custody.
- **Statistical analysis**: 95% upper confidence limit (UCL) computed using ProUCL (H-UCL, lognormal-based).
- **Metrics**: Comparison against maximum site-specific background values (barium: 120 mg/kg; lead: 3.8 mg/kg) and RDIP removal thresholds (barium: 1,000 mg/kg; lead: 80 mg/kg).

## Results
- **Barium**: Detected in 15/15 samples; range 42–420 mg/kg; 95% UCL = 171.9 mg/kg; mean = 128.3 mg/kg.
- **Lead**: Detected in 14/15 samples; range <1.0–6.7 mg/kg.
- **Quality control**: LCS recoveries 87.6–90.7%; matrix spike recoveries 84.7–110% (barium) and 87.3–90.7% (lead); all within acceptance limits.

## Limitations
- Small sample size (n = 15) limits statistical power.
- Data distribution was not normal or gamma-distributed at 5% significance; lognormal assumption used for UCL.
- 95% H-UCL (171.9 mg/kg) recommended but noted as potentially unstable for skewed datasets.
- Results are specific to the Stockpile 1 MSE wall footing excavation and may not generalize to other areas.
- Regulatory approval for use as capping material is pending.
