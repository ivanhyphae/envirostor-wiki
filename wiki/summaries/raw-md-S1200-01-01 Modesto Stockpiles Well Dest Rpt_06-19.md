# Combined Summary: Groundwater Monitoring Well Destruction Report – Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles

## Overview and Purpose

This document, dated June 19, 2019, is a Groundwater Monitoring Well Destruction Report prepared by Geocon Consultants Inc. under Caltrans Contract No. 06A2184, Task Order No. 1. The report documents the abandonment of six groundwater monitoring wells at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (located off SR 132 in Stanislaus County, California) prior to highway construction. Two wells were within future travel lanes and four were in areas of future drainage improvements; four additional wells were left in place.

## Work Performed

Field activities occurred on April 29–30, 2019. Wells MW1, MW2, and MW5 were destroyed on April 29; wells MW3, MW7, and MW8 on April 30. Destruction was supervised by Geocon with Caltrans personnel onsite. The work was performed by Gregg Drilling and Testing, a Caltrans-approved, California-certified subcontractor.

All six wells were destroyed by pressure grouting via tremie pipe, using neat cement grout (3 gallons water per 47 lb sack). Grout was placed to the maximum depth of each well, followed by application of 20–25 psi air pressure for approximately 5 minutes. Calculated minimum grout volume ranged from 6.4 to 7.4 gallons per well; actual usage was 13–20 gallons per well. The top 5 feet of each casing was over-drilled with an 8-inch hollow-stem auger and backfilled with cuttings, native material, and bagged topsoil.

Specific well details: MW1 (42 ft depth, 34 ft water level), MW2 (39 ft, 35 ft), MW3 (grout dropped 12 inches after 21 psi), MW5 (45 ft, 44 ft), MW7 (40.5 ft, 38.4 ft), MW8 (38.5 ft water level). All wells showed grout drop of 6–12 inches after pressure application.

## Containment and Disposal

Well box materials and concrete were deposited daily in Caltrans' onsite construction debris pile by Gregg Drilling. No analytical testing was required or performed as part of this well destruction scope.

## Regulatory Compliance

Well destruction was performed in accordance with California Well Standards Bulletin 74-90 and Monitoring Well Standards Part III. An encroachment permit (ENC2019-50174) was obtained from the City of Modesto, with secondary coordination with Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Resources. The permit was issued April 26, 2019, valid through October 26, 2019.

Conditions included: 72-hour notification to Underground Service Alert (ticket numbers provided for each well), 48-hour pre-mobilization notification to City of Modesto, inspection by City staff, compliance with City Standard Specifications and Municipal Code, erosion/sediment control, stormwater management (SWPPP for projects >1 acre), traffic control per MUTCD California Supplement (no lane closures during peak hours without permit), and landscaping approval if needed.

## Permits and Reporting

Well Completion Reports (Form DWR 188) for all six destroyed wells were completed and forwarded to the California Department of Water Resources and Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Health. These reports include detailed well construction drawings, GPS coordinates (WGS84; Mount Diablo baseline), and destruction procedures. The reports were signed June 12, 2019, and reference permit ENC2019-50174.
