### Summary of "raw/S1200-01-01 Caltrans Modesto Stockpile RDIP_01.19 (1).pdf"

This document is a Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) – Interim Phase 1 for the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles** in Stanislaus County, California. It was prepared by **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** for **Caltrans** and addresses the management of soil contaminated with **barium** and **aerially deposited lead (ADL)** from a historical FMC facility. The plan was conditionally approved by the **California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)** in 2018 and is governed by a broader DTSC-Caltrans agreement for managing ADL soils statewide.

**Regulatory Framework and Risk Assessment**

The document rests on a foundation of prior approvals, including a 2009 DTSC Variance and a 2018 Final Remedial Action Plan (RAP). Key findings state that the soil stockpiles, as managed, do not pose an unacceptable risk to human health. To be protective of groundwater, DTSC has set stringent **post-excavation verification thresholds**: **1,000 mg/kg for barium** and **80 mg/kg for lead**, which is the residential screening level. A corresponding **Health and Safety Plan (HSP)** mandates strict protocols, including a buddy system, designated work zones, and Level D or C personal protective equipment.

**Remedial Action Plan**

The planned action is the **containment of contaminated soil as highway embankment fill**. Specifically, it involves the excavation of **Stockpile 3** (24,000 yd³) and portions of **Stockpile 2** (3,850 yd³) for a total removal of approximately **27,850 yd³**. This material will be consolidated onto the upper surfaces and southern slopes of Stockpiles 1 and 2, which have a combined capacity of up to 54,500 yd³. The consolidated zones will be capped with a minimum of 12 inches of clean soil or a pavement section. ADL-contaminated soil must be placed at least 5 feet above the highest groundwater elevation.

**Key Compliance and Management Requirements**

Long-term management will require recording a **Land Use Covenant** for the barium containment zones, conducting a **Five-Year Review** of the containment's effectiveness, and establishing an **Operation and Maintenance Agreement**. Excavation must be suspended during high winds or when dust control is inadequate. The plan also calls for abandoning certain monitoring wells (MW1, MW2, MW3, MW5, MW7, MW8) while protecting others (MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10). Key responsible parties include **Geocon**, **Caltrans**, **DTSC**, and co-regulator **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)**.
