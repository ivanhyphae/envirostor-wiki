## Key claims (findings)
- **Primary chemicals of concern (COCs)**: Barium, lead, and strontium were designated by DTSC. In the July 2012 event, barium and strontium were detected in all ten samples; lead was below the practical quantitation limit (PQL) of 1.0 µg/L. None of these three COCs exceeded numeric thresholds (barium MCL 1,000 µg/L; strontium Health Advisory 4,000 µg/L).
- **Other exceedances**:
  - Vanadium in MW-6 (51 µg/L) slightly exceeded the California Notification Level of 50 µg/L.
  - Nitrate (as N) exceeded the Primary MCL of 10 mg/L in MW-1 (12 mg/L), MW-5 (20 mg/L), and MW-6 (19 mg/L).
  - Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) exceeded the Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in MW-1 (540 mg/L), MW-5 (620 mg/L), MW-6 (590 mg/L), and MW-10 (710 mg/L).
  - The report notes that MW-1 is an upgradient well and elevated nitrate/TDS may reflect natural background.
- **No PAHs were detected** in any groundwater sample at or above the PQL of 0.20 µg/L.
- **Water types** (from Stiff diagrams in Appendix C): Wells MW-1 through MW-5 show calcium-dominant facies; wells MW-6 through MW-10 show sodium-dominant facies.

## Analytical results

### Dissolved metals (µg/L, by EPA 6020 ICP-MS)
- **Barium**: 51–280 µg/L (PQL 1.0–20). Highest in MW-5 (280). Threshold: MCL 1,000.
- **Strontium**: 210–1,100 µg/L (PQL 10). Highest in MW-1, MW-5, MW-6. Threshold: Health Advisory 4,000.
- **Lead**: Not detected above PQL 1.0 in any well.
- **Arsenic**: 2.2–6.6 µg/L (PQL 1.0). All below MCL 10.
- **Chromium**: 1.3–14 µg/L (PQL 0.50). All below MCL 50.
- **Vanadium**: 14–51 µg/L (PQL 1.0). Exceedance at MW-6 (51; Notification Level 50).
- **Selenium**: ND–4.4 µg/L (PQL 0.50). Detected in MW-5, MW-6, MW-8, MW-9, MW-10.
- **Other metals (antimony, copper, molybdenum, nickel, zinc)**: Detected below thresholds. Zinc up to 48 µg/L (MW-4).
- **Dissolved mercury** (EPA 7470): ND in all wells (<0.20 µg/L).

### General minerals (mg/L)
- **Nitrate as N**: 2.8–20 mg/L. Exceedances: MW-1 (12), MW-5 (20), MW-6 (19). MCL = 10.
- **TDS**: 300–710 mg/L. Exceedances: MW-1 (540), MW-5 (620), MW-6 (590), MW-10 (710). Secondary MCL = 500.
- **Chloride**: 7.5–39 mg/L (MW-10 highest).
- **Sulfate**: 20–110 mg/L.
- **Bicarbonate alkalinity (as CaCO3)**: 160–400 mg/L.
- **Total sulfide**: ND–0.18 mg/L (detected in MW-7, MW-8, MW-9, MW-10).

### Semivolatile organic compounds (EPA 8270/SIM)
All 16–17 target PAHs (including naphthalene, phenanthrene, benzo(a)pyrene, etc.) were **non-detect** at PQL 0.20 µg/L in all samples.

### QA/QC flags and data quality limitations
- **Equipment blank**: Contained barium (1.1 µg/L), copper (1.0 µg/L), and sodium (100 µg/L). Data for these constituents in field samples are qualified as estimated, with potential to be less than reported.
- **Matrix spike (MS/MSD) exceedances**: Lab replicate recoveries outside control limits for barium, antimony, selenium, calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium, and others. Batch validated by LCS; report states no additional qualification needed for metals far from thresholds.
- **RPD flags (R)**: Poor precision in duplicate analyses for several dissolved metals (arsenic, barium, chromium, copper, molybdenum, vanadium) in batch B2G0390.
- **E flags**: Results above quantitation range for calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium – values reported as estimated.
- **Surrogate recoveries** for SVOCs were generally within limits, except in MW-8 where 4-Terphenyl-d14 (49.4%) and 2-Fluorobiphenyl (39.8%) were outside acceptance ranges.
- **No thresholds (MCLs, etc.) are referenced in the lab-only documents**, as they are raw data reports.
