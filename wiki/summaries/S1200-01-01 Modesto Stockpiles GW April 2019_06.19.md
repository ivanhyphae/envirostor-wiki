The source document is a groundwater monitoring report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, covering monitoring activities from 2006 through 2019, with detailed results for the October 2018 and April 2019 sampling events. The report concludes that groundwater beneath the site occurs in the shallow upper aquifer of the Modesto Formation and generally flows south to southeast, with measured gradients around 0.008 to 0.01. Across the monitoring history, groundwater chemistry is characterized by two recurring water types—calcium-dominant and sodium-dominant—with bicarbonate as the major anion, indicating elevated mineralization and salinity.

The 2018–2019 monitoring program included semiannual water-level measurements and annual groundwater sampling from monitoring wells MW-1 through MW-10, though several wells were dry or unavailable during specific events (for example, MW-8 was dry in October 2018, MW-9 and MW-10 were dry in April 2019, and MW-10 could not be located in October 2018). Sampling was performed using disposable bailers after purging roughly 2 to 3.5 well volumes, with field measurements of pH, conductivity, temperature, dissolved oxygen, and ORP. Laboratory analyses included Title 22 dissolved metals, major ions/general minerals, TDS, alkalinity, sulfide, dissolved mercury, and in some historical contexts PAHs. Results were evaluated against California primary and secondary drinking-water standards, EPA health advisories, and related notification/action levels, with QA/QC checks showing generally acceptable data quality despite some flagged matrix spike recoveries.

Analytical results show that most dissolved metals are at low concentrations or nondetect, while dissolved solids and major ions are consistently elevated. In April 2019, examples of high major-ion chemistry included very large concentrations of calcium, sodium, magnesium, and potassium in some wells, along with elevated bicarbonate alkalinity and TDS. Specific wells showed TDS commonly in the 300–760 mg/L range, and some wells exhibited especially high sodium and calcium concentrations. The report identifies nitrate and TDS as the most recurring exceedances relative to drinking-water benchmarks, with occasional manganese exceedance, while other trace metals generally remain below applicable thresholds. Overall, the site’s groundwater quality is described as mineralized but stable, with major-ion chemistry dominating over trace-metal concerns.
