## Combined Summary: Modesto Stockpiles Groundwater Monitoring Report (April 2019)

This report details the April 2019 groundwater monitoring event at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (Project S1200-01-01), part of a long-term monitoring program (2006–2019) for Title 22 metals, general minerals, and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs). Ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10, installed in 2006 and 2012) were sampled, though wells MW-8, MW-9, and MW-10 were dry during several events, preventing data collection. Analytical methods included EPA 6020 (ICP-MS) for dissolved metals, EPA 7470A for mercury, EPA 300 for anions, SM 2320B for alkalinity, and SM 2540C for total dissolved solids (TDS). Quality control measures involved field duplicates, equipment blanks, and laboratory qualifiers (e.g., J for estimated values, M1 for matrix spike exceedances). Notable sampling issues included a filter malfunction for MW-1 and MW-3 on April 8, 2019 (requiring re-sampling the next day) and a laboratory preparation error for one split sample.

Key results from this event show that most dissolved metal concentrations were below California Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), but several thresholds were exceeded. Nitrate exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in wells MW-1, MW-2, MW-5, and MW-6. Barium and strontium remained below numeric thresholds of 1,000 µg/L and 4,000 µg/L, respectively, across all wells. However, TDS exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in certain wells, with values ranging from 180 to 1,100 mg/L across the site. Elevated concentrations of calcium, magnesium, and sodium were detected; for example, MW-1 showed calcium at 110,000 µg/L, MW-6 showed sodium at 120,000 µg/L, and TDS varied from 300 mg/L (MW-8, MW-11) to 500 mg/L (MW-4) to 630 mg/L (MW-1). PAHs were not detected (<0.20–<0.50 µg/L) in any analyzed samples. Cation-anion balances were provided for all samples, and Stiff diagrams indicated both calcium-dominant and sodium-dominant water types across the site. Overall, the report confirms ongoing exceedances of nitrate and TDS at multiple wells, while most dissolved metals remain below regulatory limits, with site conditions affected by variable groundwater levels.
