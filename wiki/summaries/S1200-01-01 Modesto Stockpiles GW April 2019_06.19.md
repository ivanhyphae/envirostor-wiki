### Combined Summary

This report documents the results of a groundwater monitoring event at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California, conducted in April 2019 (with additional depth-to-water measurements from October 2018). The primary objective was to assess groundwater quality by analyzing samples from eight monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-8) for a suite of dissolved metals, general minerals, and physical parameters. The data were compared to California primary and secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) and EPA health advisories.

**Methodology and Sampling**
Groundwater samples were collected in April 2019 from wells MW-1 through MW-8 using disposable bailers after purging 2-3 well volumes. Samples for dissolved metals were field-filtered (0.45-µm) and analyzed by a third-party laboratory (Advanced Technology Laboratories) using standard EPA methods (e.g., EPA 6020 for metals, EPA 300.0 for anions, SM 2540C for TDS). A duplicate sample from MW-8 (labeled MW-11) was collected for QA/QC purposes. Data quality was supported by laboratory control samples, though some matrix spike recoveries for major cations fell outside acceptance limits, suggesting a potential matrix effect. Data collection was limited by dry conditions; wells MW-9 and MW-10 were dry and could not be sampled in April 2019, and MW-8 had a history of insufficient water in prior sampling events (2014-2016). MW-10 could not be located in October 2018 due to vegetation, and MW-9 was found missing a well plug and lid.

**Key Analytical Results**
The analysis yielded several findings regarding groundwater quality:

- **Primary Contaminants:** Groundwater met drinking water thresholds for the primary chemicals of concern. **Lead** was not detected above method detection limits in any sample. Concentrations of **barium** (49–370 µg/L) and **strontium** (310–1,300 µg/L) were well below their respective thresholds of 1,000 µg/L and 4,000 µg/L. Other metals like chromium and vanadium were also below their respective MCLs and notification levels.
- **Contaminant Exceedances:** The primary MCL of 10 mg/L for **nitrate (as nitrogen)** was exceeded in four wells (MW-1, MW-2, MW-5, MW-6), with concentrations ranging from 2.3 to 40 mg/L. The secondary MCL of 500 mg/L for **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)** was exceeded in five wells (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-5, MW-6), with concentrations ranging from 300 to 760 mg/L. **Manganese** exceeded its secondary MCL of 50 µg/L in well MW-5 (59 µg/L).
- **Groundwater Chemistry:** Stiff diagrams revealed bicarbonate-dominant groundwater with two distinct water types: a calcium-dominant type in most wells (MW-1, 2, 4, 5, 7) and a sodium-dominant type in wells MW-3, MW-6, and MW-8. The groundwater flow direction remained generally toward the south-southeast, consistent with historical trends.

**Data Limitations**
The report acknowledges several limitations. The primary limitation is the incomplete spatial coverage due to the inability to sample from wells MW-9 and MW-10 (and sometimes MW-8) during dry periods. Additionally, quality control issues with matrix spike recoveries for some major cations (Ca, Mg, Na) were noted, though the batch was validated by laboratory control samples. Some metal results were reported as estimated (J-qualified) due to detection near the method detection limit. The data from this April 2019 event represent a single point in time and lack interpretive discussion of trends.
