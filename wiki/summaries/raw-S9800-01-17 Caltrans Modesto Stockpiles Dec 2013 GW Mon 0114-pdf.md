## Summary: Groundwater Monitoring Report for Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (December 2013)

**Document Identity:** This is a groundwater monitoring report prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. for the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans), District 6. The report covers the December 2013 sampling event (fieldwork December 11–12, 2013) and is dated January 2014. Analytical testing was performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL).

**Site and Scope:** The report addresses groundwater quality at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (southerly of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue, Stanislaus County). Ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) were sampled, associated with Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3 and the former FMC evaporation/disposal ponds.

**Key Findings and Claims:**
- **Groundwater flow** is toward the southeast at an average gradient of 0.001, consistent with historical data.
- **Dissolved metals:** With the exception of manganese in MW-6 (140 µg/L, exceeding the 50 µg/L secondary MCL), no dissolved metals (barium, strontium, arsenic, vanadium, chromium, lead, etc.) exceeded their respective water quality thresholds. Barium and strontium concentrations remained significantly below thresholds.
- **General minerals:** Nitrate (as N) exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in five wells: MW-1 (13 mg/L), MW-5 (25 mg/L), MW-6 (16 mg/L), MW-9 (11 mg/L), and MW-10 (18 mg/L). Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) equaled or exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in six wells: MW-1 (500 mg/L), MW-4 (510 mg/L), MW-5 (630 mg/L), MW-6 (580 mg/L), MW-9 (520 mg/L), and MW-10 (610 mg/L).
- **Groundwater types:** Stiff diagrams identified two distinct groundwater types: calcium-dominant (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-7, MW-10) and sodium-dominant (MW-3, MW-5, MW-6, MW-8, MW-9). Wells MW-5 and MW-9 shifted from calcium-dominant to sodium-dominant in this event, suggesting water mixing.

**Regulatory and QA/QC Context:** The report is submitted for review; no formal regulatory decisions are recorded. A holding time violation for nitrate analysis (MW-1 through MW-4) was noted but did not affect data usability. Several matrix spike outliers occurred, but sample results were validated by laboratory control samples.

**Phase and Responsibility:**
- **Responsible Parties:** Caltrans (owner), Geocon Consultants (monitoring), ATL (laboratory), DTSC (oversight), CVRWQCB (co-regulator).
- **Status:** A routine quarterly monitoring event was completed; no physical remediation was reported. The next event is scheduled for March 2014. Purge water was disposed of by Inviro-Tec Disposal.
