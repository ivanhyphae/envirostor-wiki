# Summary of: Approval of Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data, Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles, SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA (Site Code: 900259)

## Document identity
- **Document type**: Approval letter (regulatory correspondence)
- **Preparer / author organization**: Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), Site Mitigation and Restoration Program
- **Recipient / addressee agency or party**: Rebecca L. Silva, Project Manager, Geocon Consultants, Inc.
- **Date**: May 3, 2024
- **Project number or task order**: Site Code 900259

## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered**: Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles, State Route (SR) 132, Stanislaus County, California. Barium- and lead-impacted soil stockpiles beneath the newly constructed SR 132 Express Way.
- **Media addressed**: Groundwater (beneath the soil stockpiles)
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced**: MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10 (four remaining monitoring wells)

## Key claims
- **Claim**: The encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater and will not impact groundwater in the future.
  - **Who is making it**: DTSC and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), concurring with the conclusions in the Geocon-prepared Evaluation Report.
  - **Whether supported by data in this document, conditional, or contested**: Supported by reference to the Evaluation Report data and noted consistency with an earlier 2014 evaluation and recent encapsulation. No numerical data presented in this letter.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type**: Approval
- **Deciding agency and date**: DTSC, in consultation with RWQCB; letter dated May 3, 2024.
- **Conditions or requirements imposed**:
  - Groundwater monitoring beneath the soil stockpiles shall be discontinued.
  - The four remaining monitoring wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10) shall be decommissioned per the approved Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP).
- **Documents approved or acted on**: Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data, Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles (Evaluation Report) dated February 26, 2024, prepared by Geocon on behalf of Caltrans.

## Responsible parties
- **Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)**: Approving agency, project management (Dean Wright, PG, Project Manager; Lora Jameson, PG, Chief).
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB)**: Co-regulator, consulted on approval (Kyle Cockerham, PG).
- **Geocon Consultants, Inc.**: Preparer of the Evaluation Report; recipient of approval.
- **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans)**: Owner/operator; represented by Adam Inman, PG, Engineering Geologist, Caltrans D-6.

## Key terms and conditions
- **Deadlines**: None specified in this letter.
- **Required follow-on deliverables**: Decommissioning of wells MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10 per the approved RDIP.
- **Permit conditions, land use restrictions, or monitoring obligations**: Monitoring discontinued; no new monitoring obligations imposed by this letter. Decommissioning must follow the approved RDIP.

- Groundwater monitoring discontinuation, well decommissioning, barium, lead, encapsulated soil stockpiles, DTSC approval letter, RWQCB concurrence, MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10, SR 132 Express Way, Geocon, Caltrans, site code 900259, Stanislaus County, 2014 evaluation, Remedial Design and Implementation Plan
