## Document Identity
- **Document type and purpose**: Formal approval letter (from DTSC) of the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles project. The letter authorizes implementation of Alternative 4 (Containment) as the selected remedy.
- **Author or preparer**: California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)
- **Date or period covered**: Approval issued for phased implementation: Phase 1 (2019–2020), Phase 2 (2028–2029). Exact date of letter not provided in source.

## Key Claims
- DTSC approves Alternative 4 (Containment) as the selected remedy for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles.
- Containment is achieved by constructing retaining walls, bridge abutments, and roadway directly over the stockpiles; unpaved areas receive a clean fill cover.
- The remedy eliminates the exposure pathway to human receptors and minimizes surface water infiltration into stockpiled material.
- The RAP satisfies CEQA requirements; an EIR/EA has been certified with a Finding of No Significant Impact under NEPA.
- DTSC independently determined that no significant environmental effect will result from implementation of the institutional and engineering controls.

## Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision type**: Formal approval of the Remedial Action Plan (RAP)
- **Agency**: DTSC (lead regulatory agency), with Central Valley RWQCB as co-regulatory agency
- **Conditions imposed**:
  - Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) required before each construction phase.
  - Start-work notice (in English and Spanish) must be issued two weeks before construction.
  - Operation, Maintenance, and Monitoring Plan (OMA/OMP) required after Phase 1.
  - Land use covenant must be recorded to restrict future land use.
  - Annual pavement inspection and groundwater monitoring required.
  - Five-year remedy evaluation required.

## Analytical Results
No contaminant concentrations, media, or threshold exceedances were provided in the approval letter. Barium contamination in fill material is the underlying concern but quantitative data are not included in this document.

## Remedial Actions
- **Activity**: Construction of retaining walls, bridge abutments, and roadway over the stockpiled barium-contaminated fill material; placement of clean fill cover on all unpaved areas.
- **Quantities**: Not specified.
- **Phase status**:
  - Phase 1: Planned for 2019–2020.
  - Phase 2: Planned for 2028–2029.
- **Verification outcomes**: No verification data are described in the approval letter.

## Responsible Parties
- **Caltrans**: Owner/operator of the site; responsible for implementing the remedy, O&M, and land use covenant.
- **DTSC**: Lead regulatory agency; approval and oversight authority.
- **Central Valley RWQCB**: Co-regulatory agency.

## Key Terms and Conditions
- Submit a Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) before each phase of construction.
- Provide a start-work notice (English and Spanish) to the public two weeks before construction.
- Prepare and implement an Operation, Maintenance, and Monitoring Plan (OMA/OMP) after completion of Phase 1.
- Record a land use covenant restricting future land use to prevent exposure to residual contamination.
- Conduct annual pavement inspections and groundwater monitoring.
- Perform a five-year remedy evaluation to assess long-term effectiveness.
- The OMA/OMP may require revision after completion of Phase 2.
