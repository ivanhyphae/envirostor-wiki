The source document is a surface-water monitoring and data-validation report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project in Stanislaus County, California (Task Order 97 / EA 10-1E7003). Stantec conducted a March 10, 2023 inspection and sampling event during a qualifying rain event to evaluate stormwater conditions around the encapsulated stockpiles near SR 132. The inspection found no runoff or discharge beyond implemented BMPs or beyond the site boundary.

The report compiles both current and historical surface-water results from locations including BG1, BG3, PL6, and PL7, with older stations (PL1–PL5 and BG2) largely removed from the network due to construction. Field measurements were collected with a Horiba U-50 meter, and laboratory analyses included dissolved metals, nitrate as nitrogen, sulfide, and total suspended solids. QA/QC and data validation were performed using chain-of-custody review, holding-time checks, blanks, duplicates, matrix spikes, and EPA National Functional Guidelines/Region 9 criteria. The validation concluded that the dataset was usable, with some qualified results but no rejected data.

Analytically, the March 10, 2023 samples showed mostly non-detects or low-level detections for dissolved metals such as arsenic, barium, chromium, copper, lead, molybdenum, selenium, vanadium, zinc, strontium, and mercury. Reported concentrations were generally below California Primary and Secondary MCLs where applicable. One notable caveat was antimony, where the method detection limit exceeded the Primary MCL, limiting the ability to fully evaluate compliance for that analyte. General mineral results also remained low overall, though total suspended solids varied substantially by location, with one background sample notably elevated. Historical tables indicate similar patterns over time: mostly low or nondetect results, occasional estimated detections near reporting limits, and some dry or unavailable samples due to site changes and construction.
