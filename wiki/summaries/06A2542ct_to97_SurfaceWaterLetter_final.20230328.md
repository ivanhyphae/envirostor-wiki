Surface water inspection and sampling were conducted on March 10, 2023, at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project in Stanislaus County, California. Following DTSC-approved protocols, samples were collected from four locations (BG1, BG3, PL6, PL7) – including two newly established after construction (PL6, PL7, BG3) – after 0.29 inches of precipitation. Field parameters were measured using a Horiba U-50 meter, and samples were analyzed for dissolved metals (EPA 6020/7470A), nitrate-N, sulfide, and total suspended solids. Data validation was performed per EPA National Functional Guidelines, with all data deemed usable as qualified.

Most dissolved metals and general minerals were below Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs). Key exceptions include antimony, where the method detection limit (8.8 µg/L) exceeded the Primary MCL (6.0 µg/L), and vanadium, which showed elevated estimated concentrations (up to 25 J+ µg/L) but has no established MCL. Nitrate-N results in all four samples were qualified as estimated with potential low bias (J-) due to holding time exceedance (analyzed beyond the 48-hour limit). Additionally, nine metals detected in a method blank led to qualification of associated sample results as either non-detect (U) or estimated with positive bias (J+). Historical data (2013–2023) indicated occasional higher concentrations, particularly for total dissolved solids, sulfate, and chloride, and some sampling events were precluded by dry conditions. No runoff beyond best management practices was observed.

Limitations include that the document was prepared solely for Caltrans, with no third-party reliance permitted; conditions reflect only the time of publishing. Nitrate-N data may be biased low; method blank contamination introduces uncertainty for low-level metal detections; and no field duplicates were collected, limiting precision assessment. Several results were flagged as estimated (J, J+, or UJ) due to detection between the MDL and PQL, and one strontium result was not reported due to PQL exceedance.
