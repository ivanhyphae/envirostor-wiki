### Combined Summary

**Overview:**  
On March 10, 2023, a surface water inspection and sampling event was conducted at the Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project for Caltrans. The inspection confirmed that no runoff migrated beyond the Caltrans right-of-way; all stormwater was contained by best management practices (BMPs). Four water samples were collected – two background samples (BG1, BG3) and two discharge locations (PL6, PL7) – following the approved Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan. New sampling locations (BG3, PL6, PL7) were added after encapsulation of Stockpiles 1 and 2 and the opening of SR 132; these have no prior historical results for trend comparison.

**Methodology:**  
Field measurements (temperature, pH, electrical conductivity, turbidity, ORP, dissolved oxygen) were taken using a Horiba U‑50 meter. Samples were field‑filtered (0.45 µm) for dissolved metals and transported under chain of custody to Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL). Analytical methods included EPA 6020/7470A (dissolved metals), EPA 300.0 (nitrate‑N), SM 4500‑S‑D (sulfide), and SM 2540D (total suspended solids). Data validation followed USEPA National Functional Guidelines and Region 9 requirements, evaluating holding times, preservation, blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate (MS/MSD) recoveries, and duplicate precision.

**Results:**

- **Metals (dissolved, µg/L):** All detected metals remained below Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) where MCLs exist. Key results: barium (17–300), arsenic (ND to 9.2 J), chromium (ND to 12 J+), zinc (18 J to 130), strontium (52–77), and mercury (0.06–0.10 J). Antimony, beryllium, cadmium, cobalt, nickel, silver, and thallium were not detected above method detection limits (MDLs).
- **General minerals (mg/L):** Nitrate‑N results (0.11–0.20) were all qualified as estimated with potential low bias (J‑) due to a holding time exceedance. Sulfide was <0.010 in all samples. Total suspended solids (TSS) were 1.6 (PL6), 9.5 (PL7), 5.3 (BG3), but BG1 was 310 mg/L, exceeding the secondary MCL of 250 mg/L.
- **Data completeness:** The project completeness goal of 90% was achieved; all data are considered usable as qualified. No data were rejected.

**Data Validation and Quality Control:**  
- **Holding time violation:** Nitrate‑N samples were analyzed outside the recommended 48‑hour holding time, causing all nitrate results to be qualified as J‑ (estimated, potential low bias).  
- **Method blank contamination:** Nine metals were detected in the method blank of batch B3C0693. For associated samples, results between MDL and practical quantitation limit (PQL) were raised to PQL and flagged as not detected (U); results above PQL but less than ten times the blank were qualified as J+ (estimated, potential high bias). Examples: cadmium in BG1 raised to 0.0030 U; zinc in BG3 raised to 0.025 U; chromium and copper in BG1 flagged as J+.  
- **Other QC:** LCS recoveries, MS/MSD recoveries (e.g., mercury at 107–108%), and laboratory duplicate RPDs were all within acceptance criteria. No field blanks or field duplicate samples were submitted, limiting assessment of field contamination and representativeness.

**Limitations:**  
- Holding time exceedance for nitrate‑N and method blank contamination are the primary data limitations.  
- New locations (BG3, PL6, PL7) lack historical data, preventing trend analysis.  
- The BG1 background sample had unusually high turbidity and TSS (310 mg/L), which was noted but not explained.  
- The report reflects professional judgment under contract; third‑party reliance is not permitted.  
- Sensitivity is limited by PQLs; values below PQL but above MDL are flagged as estimated (J) or not detected (U).  
- Validation covers only one sample delivery group (SDG 2300466) from this single event, so results may not generalize to other batches or sites.
