The document combines field soil characterization and laboratory metals testing for the Stockpile 2 southerly slope MSE wall footing excavation area associated with barium-containing soil removal on State Route 132 in Modesto, California. Soil was sampled along the planned footing excavation alignment in discrete 1-foot intervals from trenches STK2-1 through STK2-7, and 29 samples were submitted under chain of custody for total metals analysis by EPA Method 6010B (ICP-AES) after EPA 3050B_S digestion/prep. The focus was on barium and lead, with results compared to site-specific background concentrations and BCS removal verification thresholds.

Analytical results showed barium in every sample, ranging from about 53 to 930 mg/kg in the field memo and 69 to 690 mg/kg in the lab certificate excerpt; lead was also detected in all samples, generally at much lower levels, ranging roughly from 3.5 to 22 mg/kg. Several samples, especially those from the upper 3 feet, exceeded the site-specific background value for barium, while lead was typically near background. Quality control in the laboratory data was generally acceptable, although some barium matrix spike recoveries were elevated and flagged as likely due to matrix interference; blanks and laboratory control samples were acceptable.

Based on the combined field and lab findings, the memo concludes that material excavated from the upper 3 feet of the footing excavation should be placed in the designated Stockpiles 1 and 2 fill containment zones, while material excavated below 3 feet is considered potentially suitable for reuse as embankment fill or capping material, subject to regulatory approval. The report is limited to this excavation area, this sampling event, and the two analyzed metals.
