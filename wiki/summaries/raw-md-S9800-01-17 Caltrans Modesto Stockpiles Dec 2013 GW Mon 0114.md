## Combined Summary of Groundwater Monitoring Report – December 2013  
### Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Site  

**Document Identity and Purpose**  
This report, prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. (Project No. S9800-01-17) and dated January 29, 2014, documents the semiannual groundwater monitoring event conducted December 11–12, 2013 at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (Stanislaus County). The report summarizes sampling results, compliance with water quality thresholds, and quality assurance/quality control (QA/QC) data, and is submitted to the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), and the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch).  

**Groundwater Flow and Hydrogeology**  
Groundwater flow direction is toward the southeast at an average gradient of 0.001, consistent with historical flow patterns. The monitoring network comprises ten wells (MW‑1 through MW‑10) screened in the Modesto and Riverbank Formations.  

**Analytical Results and Exceedances**  
Primary chemicals of concern (COCs) are barium, lead, and strontium, all detected in all ten wells. Barium ranged from 34 to 230 µg/L (MW‑5), well below the primary MCL of 1,000 µg/L and health advisory of 700 µg/L. Lead was detected only in MW‑6 at 1.3 µg/L (action level 15 µg/L). Strontium ranged from 240 to 1,200 µg/L (MW‑1), below the notification level of 4,000 µg/L.  

Other dissolved metals (arsenic, vanadium, chromium) were detected at levels below their respective MCLs or notification levels, except manganese in MW‑6 (140 µg/L), which exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L.  

Nitrate as nitrogen exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in five wells: MW‑1 (13 mg/L), MW‑5 (25 mg/L), MW‑6 (17 mg/L), MW‑9 (14 mg/L), and MW‑10 (16 mg/L). Total dissolved solids (TDS) equaled or exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in MW‑1, MW‑4, MW‑5, MW‑6, MW‑9, and MW‑10 (maximum 630 mg/L in MW‑5). Notably, upgradient well MW‑1 also exceeded TDS and nitrate standards, suggesting natural background contributions. Minor shifts in ionic composition (sodium vs. calcium dominance) were observed in MW‑5 and MW‑9.  

**Regulatory Context**  
Monitoring follows the DTSC-approved *Final Work Plan, Groundwater Assessment* (Shaw Environmental, Inc., January 2006). DTSC concurred with discontinuation of polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon (PAH) analysis after November 2012.  

**Quality Assurance/Quality Control**  
QA/QC data from Geocon’s in‑house laboratory and Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) indicate acceptable method performance overall. Nitrate analyses for MW‑1 through MW‑4 were run outside holding time but deemed valid based on similarity to draft in‑hold‑time results. Matrix spike recoveries for major cations (calcium, magnesium, sodium) and sulfide occasionally exceeded control limits due to high native matrix concentrations; these flags (M1, M2, D6) do not invalidate field data.  

**Remedial and Monitoring Actions**  
No physical remedial actions were conducted; the event comprised only monitoring. Purged groundwater (0.75–5.5 gallons per well) was containerized in a 55‑gallon drum and disposed offsite at Inviro‑Tec Disposal (Lincoln, CA) on January 7, 2014.  

**Responsible Parties**  
- **Caltrans (District 6)** – site owner/operator, contracting entity.  
- **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** – consultant performing monitoring, analysis, and reporting (Project Manager: Rebecca L. Silva).  
- **Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL)** – analytical laboratory (ELAP 1838).  
- **DTSC** – primary regulatory oversight.  
- **Central Valley RWQCB** – co‑regulator.  
- **FMC Corporation** – original owner of the evaporation pond source area.  

**Monitoring Obligations**  
Obligations include semiannual groundwater sampling from ten wells, analyzing for Title 22 dissolved metals (including strontium), major cations/anions, TDS, sulfide, and alkalinity. Electronic data files were uploaded to GeoTracker on January 29, 2014 (confirmation numbers provided).
