## Summary: Hwy 132 Clean Fill Source Testing Exhibit

The document is an exhibit from an Initial Site Assessment for the SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project (Alternative 1) in Modesto, California. It presents a site map and table identifying properties along the project corridor with Recognized Environmental Conditions (RECs) based on a Phase I environmental site assessment methodology. The goal is to document potential contamination sources that could affect the use of clean fill or construction activities.

### Key Claims
- Twelve locations (parcels and existing right-of-way) were identified with RECs.
- Common contaminants include petroleum hydrocarbons from underground storage tanks (USTs), heavy metals, solvents, waste oil, and drug lab waste.
- Risk classifications (High, Medium, Low) are assigned based on contaminant type and regulatory database records.
- The existing SR-132 right-of-way south of Kansas Avenue contains heavy metals in soil stockpiles and miscellaneous debris.

### Methodology
- **Data Collection:** Review of regulatory databases (SLIC, CA FID UST, HIST UST, SWEEPS, LUST, HIST CORTESE, CDL, ENVIROSTORE, HAZNET, US HIST AUTO) and historical aerial photos (1979–present).
- **Site Inspection:** Visual observations supported by photographs and reference to project figures (Figs. 2D/3D, 2E/3E, etc.).
- **Risk Classification:** Based on the presence of RECs, type of contaminant, and database hits. Categories: High (e.g., confirmed LUST or multiple UST records), Medium (e.g., solvents, waste oil), Low (e.g., isolated non-hazardous waste).

### Limitations
- Data sources are limited to publicly available regulatory databases and historical records; no subsurface or soil sampling was performed.
- Some parcel boundaries or exact property addresses were not determined or were inferred.
- The assessment excludes non‑recorded releases or contaminants that may not appear in the reviewed databases.
- Risk classifications are relative and based solely on documented regulatory history, not on quantitative health risk assessment.
