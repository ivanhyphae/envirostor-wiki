## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Approval letter from the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) to the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) approving the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project, Stanislaus County, California.
- **Author or preparer:** Steven R. Becker, P.G., Chief, Site Evaluation and Remediation Unit, San Joaquin Branch, Sacramento Office, Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program, DTSC.
- **Date or period covered:** March 14, 2018. The RAP addresses the segment south of Kansas Avenue between Carpenter Avenue and just east of State Route 99 in Modesto, CA. Implementation is scheduled in two phases: Phase 1 (2019–2020) and Phase 2 (2028–2029).

## Key claims
- The selected remedy (Alternative 4, Containment) will protect human health and the environment over the long term by eliminating exposure pathways to human receptors and minimizing surface water infiltration into groundwater beneath the stockpiles.
- The RAP meets California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) requirements. DTSC, after independent analysis, determined the selected remedy will not have a significant effect on the environment with implementation of project controls (mitigation measures).
- Caltrans’ Final Environmental Impact Report/Environmental Assessment (EIR/EA) contains all necessary mitigation measures to maintain less-than-significant impact.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision:** DTSC approves the RAP for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 132 project.
- **Agency:** DTSC (with Central Valley RWQCB as co-regulator and responsible reviewing agency under CEQA).
- **Date:** March 14, 2018.
- **Conditions imposed:** The remedy must be implemented in two phases; Caltrans must submit a Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP) for each phase for DTSC review and approval; a "Start Work Notice" (English and Spanish) to be published 2 weeks prior to each construction phase; Remedial Action Completion Report required for each phase; Operation and Maintenance Agreement (OMA) and Operation and Maintenance Plan (OMP) required after Phase 1; land use covenant and soil management plan required; DTSC to evaluate containment remedy every five years; certification of containment will occur after Phase 2.

## Analytical results
- *No specific contaminant data, media, concentrations, or thresholds are provided in this document.* The overarching contaminant of concern (barium-contaminated fill material) is known from project context but not quantified here.

## Remedial actions
- **Selected remedy:** Alternative 4, Containment: containment of stockpiles by construction of the SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project. Stockpiles will be contained behind retaining walls, bridge abutments, and beneath the roadway. Unpaved portions will have clean fill cover.
- **Phase 1 (interim):** 2019–2020; **Phase 2 (ultimate build-out):** 2028–2029.
- **Post-remedy requirements:** OMP to include annual inspection of pavement and other containment features, plus groundwater monitoring. OMA and OMP may need modification after Phase 2.

## Responsible parties
- **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans):** Owner/operator; lead agency under CEQA/NEPA; preparer of RAP, EIR/EA, RDIPs, Remedial Action Completion Reports, OMA, OMP, land use covenant, and soil management plan.
- **DTSC:** Approving agency; will prepare Statement of Findings and Notice of Determination; responsible for five-year remedy evaluations.
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB):** Co-regulator; responsible reviewing agency under CEQA; recipient of final RAP copy.
- **Stanislaus Council of Governments (StanCOG):** Notified as interested party via Elizabeth Hahn.

## Key terms and conditions
- Draft Final RAP must be modified to read "Final RAP," include updated information reflecting the Final RAP and Final EIR, and be placed in local repositories; hard and electronic copies sent to DTSC and RWQCB.
- RDIP for each phase must be submitted for DTSC review and approval.
- DTSC will publish a "Start Work Notice" in English and Spanish in local newspapers two weeks prior to construction for each phase.
- Remedial Action Completion Report required for each phase.
- OMA must be entered into with DTSC; OMP must include annual inspection, groundwater monitoring, land use covenant, and soil management plan.
- Certification of containment remedy will occur after Phase 2 implementation; DTSC evaluates remedy every five years.
