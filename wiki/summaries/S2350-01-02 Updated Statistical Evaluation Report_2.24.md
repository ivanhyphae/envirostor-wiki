Here is a structured summary of the academic paper.

## Key claims
- The encapsulated soil stockpiles within the Caltrans right-of-way in Modesto have not impacted groundwater quality.
- Groundwater data from 2014 onward confirm that constituent concentrations (TDS, sulfate, barium, strontium) have remained stable and are consistent with the 2014 evaluation.
- The remaining four monitoring wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10) are frequently dry due to declining regional groundwater levels and their shallow construction.
- The report recommends decommissioning the four remaining wells and discontinuing all groundwater monitoring related to the soil stockpiles.

## Methodology
- **Experimental setup:** A comparative evaluation of groundwater data from Caltrans monitoring wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10) at the Modesto Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California.
- **Datasets:** Laboratory analysis results for total dissolved solids (TDS), sulfate, barium, and strontium collected from the four wells since 2014, compared to historical data and data from the nearby FMC facility.
- **Evaluation metrics:** Comparison of concentrations to background values (e.g., 95% upper confidence limit, mean concentrations from the 2014 evaluation), regulatory standards (e.g., EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories), and concentration trends over time.
- **Analysis:** Visual assessment of concentration vs. time plots (Figures 3-6) and comparison to site-specific target levels from the FMC Remedial Action Plan.

## Results
- **TDS & Sulfate:** Concentrations in the remaining wells are in the same range as previously reported. Since 2019, sulfate concentrations were less than 50 mg/L.
- **Barium:** Concentrations are consistent with the FMC upgradient background concentration (151 µg/L), well below the EPA drinking-water health advisory (DWHA) of 700 µg/L, and about 40 times less than the FMC site-specific target level (6,210 µg/L).
- **Strontium:** Concentrations are consistent with historical data, on average about four times less than the EPA DWHA (4,000 µg/L) and more than 20 times less than the FMC site-specific target level (61,900 µg/L).
- **Well Status:** All four wells were dry on many sampling occasions since 2014 due to declining groundwater levels. The last samplable dates were March 2021 (MW4, MW6), February 2022 (MW9), and April 2018 (MW10).
- **Statistical Parameters:** The 2014 evaluation showed that concentrations in Caltrans wells were between those measured in nearby FMC wells, indicating that the stockpiles were not the source of groundwater impacts.

## Limitations
- **Aging well infrastructure:** The Caltrans wells were designed to screen across the water table at installation and are now often dry due to declining regional groundwater levels, preventing further data collection.
- **Data gaps:** Several sampling events since 2014 were "DRY," resulting in an incomplete dataset for trend analysis beyond the existing stable patterns.
- **Different well construction:** Caltrans wells are shallow and designed for a different purpose than FMC wells, which are deeper and have longer screened intervals, limiting direct comparison of absolute concentrations.
- **Source material change:** Stockpile 3 was removed and placed onto Stockpiles 1 and 2, and the stockpiles were encapsulated beneath a roadway, altering the physical conditions at the site.
