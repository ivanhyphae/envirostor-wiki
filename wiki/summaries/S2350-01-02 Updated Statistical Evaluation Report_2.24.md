## Key Claims
- The updated evaluation confirms the 2014 conclusion that groundwater impacts near Caltrans encapsulated soil stockpiles (which contain FMC constituents) did not originate from the stockpiles but from the nearby FMC facility.
- Constituent concentrations (TDS, sulfate, barium, strontium) in the remaining monitoring wells have remained stable since 2014 and are well below health-based thresholds. No increasing trends were observed.
- The encapsulated soil stockpiles do not have the potential to impact groundwater.
- Due to declining regional groundwater levels, the four remaining wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10) are increasingly dry, limiting data collection and rendering future monitoring uninformative.
- The authors recommend decommissioning all four remaining Caltrans monitoring wells and discontinuing groundwater monitoring for the stockpiles.

## Methodology
- **Data**: Groundwater data collected from four Caltrans monitoring wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, MW10) sampled periodically between 2006 and 2023, compared to the 2014 baseline and data from the FMC facility.
- **Analytes**: Total dissolved solids (TDS), sulfate, barium, and strontium.
- **Metrics**: Time-series trend analysis; comparison to background concentrations (e.g., 95% upper confidence limit), U.S. EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories (DWHA), Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), and FMC site-specific target levels.
- **Context**: Caltrans wells were screened near the water table (shallow), while FMC wells are deeper (20–30 ft screens at ~45–55 ft bgs). Monitoring frequency was reduced from quarterly to annual after 2015.

## Results
- **Well Dryness**: Wells have frequently been dry during annual sampling since 2014, with many missed events (e.g., MW10 dry for 9 of 11 measurements after 2014; MW4 dry in 2022 and 2023; MW6 dry in 2014–2016, 2022, 2023; MW9 dry in many years after 2014).
- **TDS**: Ranged from approximately 340–700 mg/L, with most values below the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L. Concentrations are in the same range as pre-2014.
- **Sulfate**: Ranged from 11–120 mg/L, all below the secondary MCL of 250 mg/L. Since 2019, concentrations have been below 50 mg/L.
- **Barium**: Ranged from 51–180 µg/L, comparable to the FMC upgradient background of 151 µg/L and far below the DWHA of 700 µg/L and the site-specific target of 6,210 µg/L.
- **Strontium**: Ranged from 550–1,200 µg/L, on average ~4× lower than the DWHA of 4,000 µg/L and >20× lower than the site-specific target of 61,900 µg/L.
- No increasing trends were observed; concentrations remained stable.

## Limitations
- Declining regional groundwater levels have caused the shallow Caltrans wells to go dry frequently, limiting the ability to collect sufficient data.
- Six of the original ten wells were decommissioned in 2019.
- The Caltrans and FMC wells were installed for different purposes, with different screen depths and intervals, complicating direct comparison.
- Future monitoring data will be uninformative due to the encapsulation of soil stockpiles beneath highway pavement and retaining walls during Phase II construction (scheduled 2026).
- Two wells (MW9 and MW10) will require removal during Phase II construction.
