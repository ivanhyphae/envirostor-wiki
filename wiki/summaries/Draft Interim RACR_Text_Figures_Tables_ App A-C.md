The document is an interim Removal Action Completion Report package for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles in the SR 132 West Project area in Modesto, California, covering Interim Phase 1. It explains that the selected remedy for the site is containment: barium-containing soil (BCS) was consolidated into designated highway embankment fill areas and capped with DTSC-approved clean fill and structural pavement to isolate remaining contamination. The work was completed under DTSC and CVRWQCB oversight by Caltrans and its contractors, and the report states that implementation was in substantial conformance with the 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) and the approved RAP. A final RACR will be prepared after later construction phases are finished.

Field activities included excavation and grading of stockpiles, transport and direct placement of BCS into containment zones, placement of clean cover fill, protection or abandonment of monitoring wells, stormwater and dust controls, compaction testing, and as-built surveying. Verification relied on soil sampling and laboratory analysis for barium and lead, groundwater and stormwater monitoring, and air monitoring for fugitive dust. The main RDIP cleanup thresholds used for excavation verification were 1,000 mg/kg for barium and 80 mg/kg for lead, with site-specific background comparison values of 120 mg/kg for barium and 3.8 mg/kg for lead.

Results show that Stockpile 3 was completely excavated and incorporated as embankment fill, with about 3,075 truckloads totaling roughly 27,675 cubic yards of BCS moved. Verification sampling found that remaining excavation surfaces met the cleanup thresholds after limited re-excavation where elevated barium had initially been detected. Final Stockpile 3 results ranged from 46–970 mg/kg barium and 1.6–40 mg/kg lead; Stockpile 2 results ranged from 54–760 mg/kg barium and 1.5–35 mg/kg lead. In Stockpile 1/MSE wall footing areas, soils were characterized at depth and found to be below removal thresholds, though barium exceeded background in some samples. A Carpenter Road borrow source was approved as clean imported fill for capping, and Class 3 aggregate subbase was accepted as an interim equivalent for clean cover over Stockpile 2 until the full pavement/hardscape cap is installed.

The report also includes statistical and technical evaluation of soil data, including ProUCL guidance for selecting 95% upper confidence limits and cautions against relying on unstable H-statistic UCLs. The Stockpile 2 analytical data show barium elevated above site background but below the removal threshold, while lead remained low relative to the action level. Overall, the report concludes that the interim remedial work successfully contained, capped, and in some cases removed the contaminated soils, with verification sampling, monitoring, and construction controls indicating compliance and no significant offsite migration or regulatory exceedances.
