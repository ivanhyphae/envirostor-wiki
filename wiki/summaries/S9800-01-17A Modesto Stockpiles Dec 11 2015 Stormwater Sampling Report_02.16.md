The report summarizes stormwater/surface-water sampling at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, south of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue, with the main focus on the December 11, 2015 rain event. Geocon collected runoff and puddle water from four accessible locations: PL1 and PL2 along North Emerald Avenue, PL3 near Bennett Street/Loletta Street outside the Caltrans right-of-way, and PL4 at a drainage basin outfall adjacent to Stockpile 3. Other designated locations were dry and not sampled. Samples were field-filtered for dissolved metals, preserved, and shipped under chain of custody to ATL for analysis.

The analytical program included dissolved metals, mercury, chloride, nitrate as nitrogen, sulfate, sulfide, alkalinity, total dissolved solids, and total suspended solids using EPA and Standard Methods procedures. Field measurements showed neutral to slightly alkaline water with moderate conductivity and turbidity. The 2015 results were generally low-level and consistent with earlier sampling events. Reported dissolved metal concentrations did not exceed primary drinking-water MCLs, and the main exceedances noted were manganese values above secondary MCLs at some locations. Most other constituents were at low concentrations or nondetect.

Across the broader project history, the report compares results from multiple sampling events between 2013 and 2015 and finds no indication of widespread contamination migration beyond the Caltrans right-of-way. The document also notes that surface water controls at the site, including vegetative/fiber cover, straw wattles, and a berm at the Bennett Street ramp, are being used to manage runoff. Laboratory quality-control results were acceptable overall, with blanks generally nondetect and recoveries within method limits, supporting the validity of the reported data. Additional sampling was planned for the 2016 rainy season.
