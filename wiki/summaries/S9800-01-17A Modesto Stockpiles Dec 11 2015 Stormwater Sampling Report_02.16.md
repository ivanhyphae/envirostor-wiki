## Key claims

*   This report documents surface water sampling at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Stockpiles 1–3) on December 11, 2015, following a qualifying rain event (approximately 0.18 inch).
*   Four surface water samples (PL1–PL4) were collected and analyzed for dissolved metals, general minerals, and field parameters.
*   No dissolved metals concentrations exceeded primary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs). Manganese concentrations in samples PL2, PL3, and PL4 exceeded secondary (taste/odor) MCLs.
*   No general minerals concentrations exceeded primary or secondary MCLs.
*   No surface water runoff was observed migrating beyond the Caltrans right-of-way.
*   Sample locations PL5, BG1, and BG2 were dry and could not be sampled.

## Methodology

*   **Site:** Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Stanislaus County, California.
*   **Sampling Protocol:** Followed the Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP) (Shaw Environmental, January 2006) and an Addendum (February 2013), approved by DTSC.
*   **Sampling Event:** December 11, 2015. A qualifying rain event (0.18 inch) followed 72 hours of dry weather.
*   **Sample Collection:** Four surface water samples (PL1–PL4) were collected using disposable bailers with a suction pump. Samples for dissolved metals were field-filtered through a 0.45-micron filter.
*   **Field Parameters:** pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, turbidity, oxygen-reduction potential (ORP), and dissolved oxygen (DO) were measured at the time of collection.
*   **Laboratory Analyses:** Performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories, Inc. (ATL), a California-certified laboratory. Analyses included:
    *   Dissolved metals (EPA Method 6020/7470A)
    *   Chloride, nitrate as nitrogen, sulfate (EPA Method 300.0)
    *   Sulfide (Standard Method 4500-S-D)
    *   Total, bicarbonate, and carbonate alkalinity (SM 2320B)
    *   Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) (SM 2540C)
    *   Total Suspended Solids (TSS) (SM 2540D)
*   **Quality Control (QA/QC):** Included field decontamination procedures, chain-of-custody documentation, and laboratory QA/QC (blanks, LCS, matrix spikes, duplicates).
*   **Evaluation Metrics:** Results were compared to California primary and secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for drinking water.

## Results

*   **Dissolved Metals (Table 2):** Antimony, barium, calcium, chromium, cobalt, copper, manganese, magnesium, molybdenum, nickel, potassium, sodium, strontium, vanadium, and zinc were detected in samples PL1–PL4. Arsenic was detected in PL3 (1.2 µg/L). Lead was detected in PL4 (1.0 µg/L).
    *   No dissolved metals exceeded primary MCLs.
    *   Manganese exceeded the secondary MCL (50 µg/L) in PL2 (52 µg/L), PL3 (290 µg/L), and PL4 (110 µg/L).
    *   Beryllium, cadmium, mercury, selenium, silver, and thallium were not detected at or above practical quantitation limits (PQLs).
*   **General Minerals (Table 3):** Sulfate, chloride, nitrate, sulfide, alkalinity, TDS, and TSS concentrations were reported for PL1–PL4.
    *   No general minerals concentrations exceeded primary or secondary MCLs.
*   **Field Parameters (Table 1):** pH ranged from 7.30–7.74. Specific conductivity ranged from 108–293 µmhos/cm. Dissolved oxygen was low (1.02–2.11 mg/L).
*   **Laboratory QA/QC Issues:**
    *   Nitrate reanalysis for some samples was past holding time.
    *   Matrix spike recoveries for several metals were outside acceptance limits, attributed to possible matrix interference.

## Limitations

*   The sample size is small (n=4) and represents a single storm event (0.18 inch).
*   Three designated sampling locations (PL5, BG1, BG2) were dry and could not be sampled.
*   The report notes potential matrix interference affecting the accuracy of metal matrix spike recoveries for some samples.
*   The laboratory report states that the initial analysis for nitrate as nitrogen was within holding time, but reanalysis was past holding time.
*   This is a compliance monitoring report, not a scientific study; it does not test a hypothesis or establish long-term trends beyond comparing results to regulatory thresholds.
