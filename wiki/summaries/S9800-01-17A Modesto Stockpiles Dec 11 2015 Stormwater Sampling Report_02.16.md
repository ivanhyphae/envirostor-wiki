**Summary**

This report documents stormwater sampling and analysis from Caltrans Modesto soil stockpiles, covering multiple events between 2013 and 2015, with a focus on the December 11, 2015, sampling event. The primary objective was to assess compliance with drinking water standards for dissolved metals and general minerals. Samples were collected from six stockpile locations (PL1–PL5, BG1–BG2) using bailers, following protocols approved by DTSC and the Central Valley Water Quality Control Board. The December 11, 2015, event occurred during a rain event of 0.18 inches, with samples collected at PL1, PL2, PL3, and PL4 (PL5, BG1, BG2 were dry). Analysis was performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ELAP No. 1838) using standard EPA methods for dissolved metals (ICP-MS, EPA 6020), dissolved mercury (cold vapor AA, EPA 7470A), anions (EPA 300.0), alkalinity (SM 2320B), total dissolved solids (SM 2540C), total suspended solids (SM 2540D), and sulfide (SM 4500-S²⁻). MCLs from California EPA SWRCB were used as benchmarks.

**Results** showed that no primary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for dissolved metals were exceeded. However, manganese exceeded secondary (aesthetic) MCLs in samples PL2, PL3, and PL4. Barium and other metal concentrations were within the same general range as previous sampling events. For the December 2015 event, total dissolved solids ranged from 70–120 mg/L, and bicarbonate alkalinity from 31–99 mg/L. Dissolved metals concentrations included antimony (0.74–1.9 µg/L), barium (20–190 µg/L), calcium (8,100–26,000 µg/L), copper (8.9–18 µg/L), manganese (36–290 µg/L), and zinc (24–60 µg/L); mercury, arsenic, cadmium, lead, selenium, silver, and thallium were mostly non-detect. No runoff was observed migrating beyond the Caltrans right-of-way. At background locations BG1 and BG2, nitrate (as N) up to 0.81 mg/L and TDS up to 670 mg/L were measured in earlier events.

Quality control (QC) results indicated that most blanks showed no detection, and laboratory control samples (LCS) generally achieved acceptable recoveries (80–120%). However, matrix spikes (MS) and matrix spike duplicates (MSD) for dissolved metals exhibited variable recoveries, with several analytes (e.g., calcium, magnesium, zinc, sulfide) showing recoveries outside acceptance limits due to matrix interference. Relative percent differences (RPD) exceeded 20% for some duplicates (e.g., antimony 74.9%). These issues were flagged as limitations.

**Limitations** include that some locations were dry on scheduled sampling dates, resulting in no data for those periods. Several analytes were reported as less than the laboratory reporting limit, limiting quantitation. QC flags noted matrix spike recoveries outside limits (M1, M2), RPD values outside criteria (R), samples requiring dilution (D5, D6), results above quantitation range (E), and reanalysis past holding time (H3). These indicate potential matrix effects on accuracy and precision for some metals and sulfide.
