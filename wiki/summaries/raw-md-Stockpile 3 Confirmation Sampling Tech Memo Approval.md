## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Email chain and attachment notification. This serves as formal documentation of DTSC’s acceptance of a Technical Memorandum (Memo) dated March 13, 2020, which describes the removal of Barium Containing Soil (BCS) from Stockpile 3 and the results of confirmation soil sampling.
- **Author or preparer:**
    - **DTSC Acceptance:** Dean Wright, P.G. (Project Manager, DTSC).
    - **Memo Author (not included in text):** John Juhrend, P.E., CEG, CEM (Geocon Consultants, Inc.).
    - **Submitter:** Richard C. Stewart, P.G. (Engineering Geologist, Caltrans Central Region).
- **Date or period covered:** March 13, 2020 (Memo preparation) to March 18, 2020 (DTSC acceptance email). The period of remedial work is referenced as "completed" prior to March 16, 2020.

## Key claims
- The Technical Memorandum demonstrates that all BCS material from Stockpile 3 has been excavated to depths where concentrations of barium and lead in the native soil are below the threshold values established in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP).
- Based on sampling results, the area is now suitable for clean embankment fill.
- The removal and relocation of all BCS stockpile material (Stocks 1, 2, and 3) is now complete, with the exception of Regulated ADL soil along northbound State Route 99.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type:** Regulatory acceptance/approval.
- **Agency:** California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).
- **Date:** March 18, 2020.
- **Decision:** DTSC accepts the Technical Memorandum and agrees that the Stockpile 3 excavation area meets RDIP verification thresholds.
- **Condition imposed:** DTSC agrees that no further BCS RDIP monitoring requirements are necessary for this area.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants:** Barium and lead.
- **Media:** Native soil (in-situ, after removal of BCS stockpile material).
- **Outcome:** Post-excavation concentrations of barium and lead are below the threshold values established in the RDIP. No specific concentration values or exceedances are provided in this email chain.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** Excavation and removal of Barium Containing Soil (BCS) from Stockpile 3.
- **Phase status:** Complete. Verified.
- **Verification outcome:** Confirmation soil sampling demonstrates that cleanup levels have been achieved.
- **Follow-up action (conditional):** Pending DTSC approval (for Stockpiles 1 and 2) to place a minimum 12-inch clean soil cover and/or a minimum 4-inch thick layer of aggregate base (AB) material on the BCS consolidation zones (including temporary slopes) of Stockpiles 1 and 2.

## Responsible parties
- **Caltrans (Owner/Operator):** Richard C. Stewart submits the Memo; Caltrans is responsible for the removal and placement of clean cover.
- **DTSC (Regulator):** Dean Wright provides regulatory acceptance of the work and cleanup approval.

## Key terms and conditions
- The removal and relocation of all BCS material does **not** include the regulated ADL soil along the northbound State Route 99.
- DTSC approval of the Stockpile 2 BCS Removal is requested by Caltrans (a copy of the DTSC approval is to be provided).
- Placement of clean cover/aggregate base on Stockpiles 1 and 2 is conditional upon DTSC approval of this memo (and presumably the Stockpile 2 approval).
