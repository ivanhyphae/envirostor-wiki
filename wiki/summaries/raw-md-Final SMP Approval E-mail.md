Here is a structured summary of the provided email chain, prepared for your case-file wiki.

## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Final regulatory approval of a Soil Management Plan (SMP) via email correspondence. The primary purpose is to document the official acceptance of the revised SMP by two regulatory agencies and to transmit a submittal from the responsible party (City of Modesto).
- **Author or preparer:** Dean Wright, PG (DTSC Project Manager) is the author of the final approval email. The submittal email was prepared by Ryan Thacher, PhD, PE (Rincon Consultants, Inc.) on behalf of the City of Modesto.
- **Date or period covered:** The email chain spans **February 8–11, 2021**. The approved SMP is dated **February 8, 2021**.

## Key claims
- **Claim (City of Modesto / Rincon):** The revised SMP has been updated to address all previous DTSC requests, including lowering the TPH-d screening level to 500 mg/kg for commercial/industrial soil. (Source: Ryan Thacher email, Feb 8, 2021)
- **Claim (City of Modesto / Rincon):** Two proposed excavation activities (targeted TPH-d removal at borings RB-10 & RB-11; bulk excavation in Basin 6B) should not be impacted by a pending discussion with the RWQCB regarding groundwater protection screening levels. (Source: Ryan Thacher email, Feb 8, 2021)
- **Assertion (Caltrans / Richard Stewart):** The City of Modesto appeared ready to proceed with excavation without first receiving regulatory acceptance of the SMP. Caltrans informed the City that regulatory acceptance was required first. (Source: Richard Stewart email, Feb 9, 2021)

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type:** Official Acceptance of a Revised Soil Management Plan (SMP).
- **Agency:** Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (Water Board / RWQCB).
- **Date:** February 11, 2021.
- **Conditions imposed:**
    1.  Soil containing Total Petroleum Hydrocarbons (TPH) above screening levels must be segregated, covered, **and labeled** until transported offsite.
    2.  The SMP must include a contingency plan for the scenario where TPH-impacted soil remains present after reaching the maximum design depth of the basins. In this event, Caltrans must be alerted, and consultation with the WB and DTSC must occur to determine next steps.
    - **Note:** The agencies allowed these two conditions to be incorporated "in the field" without requiring a further formal revision to the SMP document.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants:**
    - Total Petroleum Hydrocarbons as diesel (TPH-d).
    - Barium (The subject of the larger remediation project; the screening level is not explicitly stated in this email, but it is the primary COC).
- **Media:** Soil.
- **Concentration ranges / Thresholds:**
    - The revised SMP lowered the screening level for **TPH-d to 500 mg/kg**, based on the DTSC commercial/industrial soil screening level.
    - This threshold applies to soils left onsite or reused at finished grade, pending a final determination from the RWQCB regarding protection of groundwater.
- **Exceedances noted:** TPH-d impacted soil was identified at boring locations **RB-10 and RB-11** within Basin 6A.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** Targeted excavation and confirmation soil sampling of TPH-d impacted soils at borings RB-10 and RB-11 within Basin 6A.
- **Activity:** Excavation of an estimated **500 cubic yards (+/-)** of soil from Basin 6B (area south of 611 Franklin), where no TPH-d impacts are known.
- **Phase status:** Proposed and pending approval (as of Feb 8, 2021). The approval was granted on Feb 11, 2021, with minor field-implemented conditions.
- **Verification outcomes:** Confirmation sampling is part of the planned work for Basin 6A.

## Responsible parties
- **City of Modesto:** Owner/operator of the construction project and submitter of the SMP. They were directed by Caltrans to obtain regulatory acceptance before proceeding.
- **Rincon Consultants, Inc.:** Environmental consultant preparing the SMP on behalf of the City of Modesto (Ryan Thacher).
- **Caltrans (California Department of Transportation):** Owner/operator of the overall SR 132 remediation site; liaison between the City and regulators (Richard Stewart).
- **DTSC (Department of Toxic Substances Control):** Lead regulatory agency providing final approval (Dean Wright).
- **Central Valley RWQCB (California Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region):** Co-regulatory agency providing final approval (Chris Flower, Steven Meeks, Kyle Cockerham).

## Key terms and conditions
- **Follow-on actions required:**
    1.  Label all segregated TPH-impacted soil stockpiles until offsite transport.
    2.  Develop a contingency for residual TPH impacts at maximum basin depth, involving Caltrans and regulatory consultation.
- **Deadline / Status:** The approval is immediate (Feb 11, 2021). The conditions do not require a revised SMP document.
- **Monitoring obligations:** Confirmation soil sampling is required following targeted excavation at RB-10 and RB-11.
- **Restrictions:** The RWQCB has not yet agreed on the final TPH-d screening level for protection of groundwater; this remains an open item that may require future SMP revisions.
