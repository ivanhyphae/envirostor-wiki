## Document Identity
- **Document Type and Purpose:** Regulatory concurrence letter from DTSC to Caltrans, approving three groundwater monitoring reports for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 99 and SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA). The letter also confirms regulatory review and acceptance of monitoring well installation activities.
- **Author or Preparer:** Randy S. Adams, C.E.G. (Senior Engineering Geologist, Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program, DTSC).
- **Date or Period Covered:** November 29, 2012. The referenced reports cover groundwater monitoring events for May, June, and July 2012.

## Key Claims
- **Site Conditions:** The soil stockpiles consist of excess native soils and pond tailings generated during early 1960s construction of a segment of SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue.
- **Source of Contamination:** Excavation traversed a portion of a 4.3-acre parcel purchased from Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC), which contained an evaporation pond in its southern corner. FMC processed barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores.
- **Chemicals of Concern (COC):** Barium, lead, and strontium are identified as the primary COCs.
- **Regulatory Concurrence:** The letter states that DTSC “concurs with the subject groundwater monitoring reports” and requests future reports as data becomes available. This constitutes a formal approval of the monitoring program and reporting.

## Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision Type:** Formal, written concurrence with three submitted groundwater monitoring reports.
- **Agency:** California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).
- **Co-Regulator:** Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) was consulted in the review.
- **Date:** November 29, 2012.
- **Conditions Imposed:** None explicitly stated in this letter, but the standard condition is that future monitoring reports must be submitted promptly as data becomes available.

## Analytical Results
- **Media:** Groundwater.
- **Analytical Results:** The letter does not provide specific concentration data or ranges. It refers to the three referenced reports (prepared by Geocon Consultants, November 2012) for the analytical details of the May, June, and July 2012 sampling events.
- **Thresholds:** No numerical thresholds or exceedances are cited in this body text. The document defers to the referenced reports for specific analytical results.

## Remedial Actions
- **Activity:** Installation of two new monitoring wells.
- **Well IDs:** MW-9 (located south of Kansas Avenue and west of SR 99) and MW-10 (located south of Kansas Avenue and east of SR 99).
- **Phase Status:** Installation was completed and documented in the “Additional Well Installation and Groundwater Monitoring Report – June 2012.”
- **Verification Outcomes:** The installation and subsequent monitoring were approved via DTSC’s concurrence.
