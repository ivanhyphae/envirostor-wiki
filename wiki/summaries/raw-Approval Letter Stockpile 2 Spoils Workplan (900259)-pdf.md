## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered**: State Route 132 West Project, Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. Specifically addresses Stockpile 2 – Barium Containing Soil (BCS) Foundation Spoil.  
- **Media addressed**: Soil (spoil material from foundation installation).  
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced**: Stockpile 2; spoils generated from installation of the Weather Station and Overhead Sign #1.  

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type**: Approval (concurrence) with conditions.  
- **Deciding agency and date**: DTSC, March 4, 2022.  
- **Conditions or requirements imposed**:  
  - If sampling results indicate barium and lead concentrations are below established site‑specific background, the material may be reused on‑site at locations mutually agreed upon by DTSC and Caltrans.  
  - If sampling results show spoils material is above background concentrations, the material shall be profiled for disposal at Forward Landfill in Manteca, California, or another acceptable landfill facility.  
- **Documents approved or acted on**: *Technical Memorandum for Stockpile 2 BCS Foundation Spoil Assessment Workplan* dated February 28, 2022, prepared by Geocon Consultants Inc. on behalf of Caltrans.  

## Remedial actions
- **Activity**: Sampling and management of BCS spoils generated from Weather Station and Overhead Sign #1 installation.  
- **Phase status**: Planned (workplan has been approved; sampling and management actions are to be executed following the approved procedures).

## Key terms and conditions
- The approved workplan describes stockpiling and sampling procedures for the BCS spoil.  
- Reuse on‑site is permitted only if concentrations of barium and lead are below site‑specific background, and only at locations mutually agreed by DTSC and Caltrans.  
- If concentrations exceed background, the spoils must be profiled for disposal at Forward Landfill (Manteca) or another acceptable facility.
