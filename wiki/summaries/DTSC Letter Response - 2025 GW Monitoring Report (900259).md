## Key claims
- The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) conditionally approves the Well Destruction Report and approves the Storm Water and Surface Water Monitoring Report (FY24/25) for the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles site (State Route 132, Modesto).
- Three stockpiles contain soil contaminated with barium since the 1960s; Phase I of the Remedial Action Plan moved soil from Stockpile 3 into Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- Ten groundwater monitoring wells installed in 2012 showed no metals leaching into groundwater over years of sampling, leading to decommissioning (six in 2019, remaining four in current report).
- Storm water sampling results were all within reporting limits, indicating containment integrity.
- Conditional approval of the Well Destruction Report requires inclusion of a table comparing estimated versus actual grout volume used for each decommissioned well to provide a performance metric.

## Methodology
- Groundwater monitoring: Ten wells installed on site to monitor potential barium migration to groundwater; sampling occurred periodically over several years.
- Well decommissioning: Six wells decommissioned in 2019; the current report documents destruction of the remaining four wells using grouting.
- Surface water monitoring: Runoff collected during qualifying rain events to evaluate integrity of stockpile containment; sampling conducted per storm water sampling protocol.
- Review process: Reports reviewed by DTSC’s Geological Services Branch (GSB) and Human and Ecological Risk Office (HERO); approval based on compliance with regulatory standards.

## Results
- Groundwater monitoring over multiple years: no metals detected leaching from stockpiles.
- Storm water sampling: all analyte concentrations were within reporting limits (specific numerical values not provided in the letter).
- No quantitative exceedances or contamination events reported.

## Limitations
- The Well Destruction Report is only conditionally approved pending revision: a table must be added presenting estimated versus actual grout volume for each well, needed as a performance metric for well abandonment.
- No other limitations or caveats explicitly stated; the site appears to be progressing through remediation without major setbacks.
