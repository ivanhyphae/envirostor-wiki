## Key claims
- DTSC conditionally approves the **Well Destruction Completion Report** and approves the **Storm Water and Surface Water Monitoring Report (FY24/25)** for the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles site (Site Code 900259) in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California.
- The site contains **three encapsulated stockpiles of barium-contaminated soil** dating to the 1960s.
- Monitoring history indicates **no evidence of metals leaching into groundwater** from the stockpiles over years of groundwater data, supporting decommissioning of monitoring wells.
- Stormwater monitoring during qualifying rain events found results **within reporting limits**, indicating the stockpile containment system is performing as intended.
- DTSC requests one revision to the well destruction report: include **actual grout volumes used** alongside estimated volumes as a performance metric for well abandonment.

## Methodology
- This is a **regulatory review letter**, not a primary experimental paper.
- Data sources reviewed:
  - **Well Destruction Report** dated June 18, 2025, prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. for Caltrans.
  - **Storm Water Sampling/Monitoring Report** dated June 18, 2025, also prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc.
- Monitoring context:
  - **Groundwater monitoring wells** were installed in 2012 to track potential contaminant migration from the encapsulated stockpiles.
  - **Stormwater/surface water runoff sampling** was conducted during qualifying rain events to assess containment integrity.
- Review process:
  - DTSC technical staff reviewed the reports, including **geologists from the Geological Services Branch (GSB)** and **toxicologists from the Human and Ecological Risk Office (HERO)** for the stormwater report.
- Evaluation criteria:
  - Groundwater trend evidence for **no metal leaching**.
  - Stormwater results relative to **reporting limits**.
  - Well destruction documentation quality, specifically whether the report includes **performance metrics** for grout use.

## Results
- The site originally had **10 groundwater monitoring wells** installed in 2012.
- **6 of 10 wells** were decommissioned in **2019**.
- The remaining **4 wells** are addressed by the current well destruction report.
- DTSC states that **years of groundwater data** support the conclusion that **no metals were leaching into groundwater** from the stockpiles.
- Stormwater sampling results were **all within reporting limits**.
- Final disposition:
  - **Storm Water and Surface Water Monitoring Report: approved**
  - **Well Destruction Completion Report: conditionally approved**
- Required revision: add a table comparing **estimated grout volume vs. actual grout volume used** for each well.

## Limitations
- The document is a **letter of regulatory approval**, so it provides **limited methodological detail** beyond the referenced reports.
- No raw numerical groundwater or stormwater concentrations are included in the letter.
- “Years of data” are referenced, but the letter does not provide the full time series, analyte list, or statistical analysis.
- The approval of the well destruction report is **conditional** pending incorporation of the requested grout-volume reporting table.
- The stormwater report is said to be within reporting limits, but the letter does not specify which parameters were measured or the exact limits.
