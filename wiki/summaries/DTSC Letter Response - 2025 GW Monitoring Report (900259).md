## Key claims
This document is a regulatory letter from the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) conditionally approving a Well Destruction Completion Report and approving a Storm Water and Surface Water Monitoring Report (FY24/25) for the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles site (Site Code: 900259) on State Route 132, Modesto, California. The main findings are:
- The site contains three stockpiles of soil contaminated with barium since the 1960s. As part of Phase I of the Remedial Action Plan (2020), soil from Stockpile 3 was moved to Stockpiles 1 and 2. Phase II will utilize the remaining two stockpiles for a 4-lane highway (State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway).
- Ten groundwater monitoring wells were installed in 2012. Six were decommissioned in 2019 after years of data showed no metals leaching into groundwater. The Well Destruction Report covers destruction of the remaining four wells.
- Storm water sampling results were all within reporting limits, indicating no metals are being displaced into surface water from the stockpiles.
- DTSC conditionally approves the Well Destruction Report, requiring a table comparing estimated versus actual grout volume used for each well as a performance metric.

## Methodology
- **Groundwater monitoring**: Ten wells were installed in 2012 to monitor potential contamination migration. Years of sampling data were reviewed by DTSC and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board.
- **Well decommissioning**: Six wells were decommissioned in 2019. The current report details procedures for destroying the remaining four wells. No specific technical methods for well destruction are described in this letter.
- **Surface water monitoring**: Storm water runoff samples were collected during qualifying rain events to evaluate stockpile containment system integrity. Sampling results were compared to reporting limits.

## Results
- No quantitative results (concentration values) are provided in this letter. The storm water sampling results were described as "all within reporting limits," and groundwater monitoring data over years supported that "no metals were leaching into the groundwater from the stockpiles."

## Limitations
- The Well Destruction Report is conditionally approved; it must be revised to include a table presenting estimated versus actual volume of grout used for each well decommissioned.
- This is a regulatory approval letter, not a standalone research paper. No formal experimental limitations or caveats are discussed. The document notes that years of data supported no leaching, but does not present the underlying data.
