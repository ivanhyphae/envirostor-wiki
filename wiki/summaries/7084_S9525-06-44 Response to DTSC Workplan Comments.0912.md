## Key claims
- The document responds to DTSC and RWQCB comments on a Draft Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan for Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (State Routes 99 and 132, Stanislaus County).
- Field boring locations were added or adjusted based on a site survey (September 17, 2012) to address erosion and sediment migration features.
- Sampling depths and intervals are specified for three boring categories: Fenceline borings (continuous core to 5 ft deep, sampled at 0–6 in and every 1‑ft interval), Perimeter borings (continuous core to 3 ft, same intervals), and Cadmium borings (to 20 ft, sampled at 0–6 in and approximate 5‑ft intervals).
- Equipment decontamination rinse water will be contained, sampled for metals, and appropriately disposed.
- Laboratory analysis uses EPA Method 6010B (metals) and 7471 (mercury) for all boring categories; additional analysis may be performed after review of initial results and at DTSC/RWQCB direction.
- Soluble metals analysis will include method detection limits (MDL) and practical quantitation limits (PQL); concentrations between MDL and PQL will be flagged with a “J-flag” as estimates.
- The Summary Report will be limited to findings of the Supplemental Site Investigation; separate reports will address nature/extent of contamination and human health risk assessment.

## Methodology
- **Field activities:** Site survey by DTSC, Caltrans, and Geocon to review and adjust staked boring locations. Continuous coring performed at specified depths and intervals: Fenceline (5 ft), Perimeter (3 ft), Cadmium (20 ft). Sampling intervals: 0–6 inches and subsequent 1‑ft intervals for Fenceline/Perimeter; 0–6 inches and 5‑ft intervals for Cadmium borings.
- **Laboratory analysis:** EPA Test Method 6010B (metals) and 7471 (mercury) applied to all boring categories. Soluble metals analysis with reported MDL and PQL; estimated concentrations between MDL and PQL designated with “J-flag.”
- **Quality control:** Decontamination water sampled and analyzed for metals before disposal. Additional analyses initiated after review of initial results and per DTSC/RWQCB direction.
- **Reporting:** Investigation findings, nature/extent, and human health risk assessment presented in separate documents.

## Results
- Specific boring depths and intervals finalized: Fenceline to 5 ft (0–6 in, 1–2 ft, 2–3 ft, 3–4 ft, 4–5 ft); Perimeter to 3 ft (same intervals); Cadmium to 20 ft (0–6 in, 4–5 ft, 9–10 ft, 14–15 ft, 19–20 ft).
- Laboratory reporting format includes soluble metals analysis with MDL and PQL values; “J-flag” applied to estimated concentrations.
- All detection limits are set at concentrations suitable for human health risk assessment and comparison to water quality goals.
- No quantitative analytical results are presented; the document is a workplan response.

## Limitations
- Boring locations were adjusted based on field observations during a single site survey; additional discretionary boring locations may be needed if field conditions change.
- Initial laboratory analysis may be followed by further analysis as directed by DTSC and RWQCB pending review of results.
- The workplan does not provide actual contamination data or risk estimates—these are deferred to separate future reports.
