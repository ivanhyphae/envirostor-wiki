## Key claims
- The Final Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan incorporates responses to comments from the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) regarding the Draft Workplan for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (State Routes 99 and 132, Stanislaus County).
- Additional discretionary boring locations were added or adjusted based on a September 17, 2012 site survey to address erosion and soil/sediment migration features.
- Detailed rationale for differences in sampling depths among Fenceline Borings (5 feet), Perimeter Borings (3 feet), and Cadmium Borings (20 feet) is provided.
- All rinse water from equipment decontamination will be contained, sampled, analyzed for metals, and appropriately disposed of.
- Laboratory analysis methodology (EPA Test Methods 6010B and 7471) applies to all boring categories, with additional analyses to follow initial results as directed by DTSC/RWQCB.
- The Summary Report will be limited to findings of the Supplemental Site Investigation; nature/extent of contamination and human health risk assessment will be presented in separate reports.
- Laboratory reports will include soluble metals analysis with method detection limits (MDL) and practical quantitation limits (PQL); concentrations between MDL and PQL will be reported with a “J-flag” as estimates.

## Methodology
- **Field activities:** Site survey conducted on September 17, 2012, by representatives of DTSC, Caltrans, and Geocon to review and adjust staked boring locations based on field conditions.
- **Boring depths and sampling intervals:**
  - Fenceline borings: continuous core to 5 feet; samples collected from 0–6 inches, then from 1-foot intervals (1–2 ft, 2–3 ft, 3–4 ft, 4–5 ft).
  - Perimeter borings: continuous core to 3 feet; same 0–6 inch and 1-foot interval sampling as above.
  - Cadmium borings: continuous core to 20 feet; samples from 0–6 inches and approximate 5-foot intervals (4–5 ft, 9–10 ft, 14–15 ft, 19–20 ft).
- **Laboratory analysis:** EPA Test Methods 6010B (metals) and 7471 (mercury) applied to all boring categories. Additional analyses to be performed after review of initial results and as directed by DTSC/RWQCB.
- **Decontamination:** Rinse water from equipment decontamination contained, sampled, analyzed for metals, and disposed of appropriately.
- **Reporting:** Laboratory reports include soluble metals analysis with MDL and PQL values; estimated concentrations between MDL and PQL flagged with “J-flag.”

## Results
- No quantitative results (e.g., contaminant concentrations) are reported in this response letter; it only details modifications to the workplan based on DTSC/RWQCB comments.
