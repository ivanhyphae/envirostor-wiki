## Key claims
- This document is a response letter from Caltrans/Geocon addressing DTSC and RWQCB comments on the Draft Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles (State Routes 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California).
- The main contribution is revision and clarification of the Final Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan (Final Workplan), incorporating requested changes to field sampling, decontamination handling, laboratory analysis, reporting scope, and laboratory reporting format.
- The response emphasizes that the workplan was updated based on a September 17, 2012 site survey with DTSC, Caltrans, and Geocon.

## Methodology
- The paper is not an experimental study; it is a regulatory/technical response documenting modifications to a site investigation workplan.
- Field investigation methodology:
  - Additional discretionary boring locations were added or adjusted based on field observations, erosion, and soil/sediment migration features.
  - Boring categories include:
    - Fenceline borings: continuous coring to 5 feet.
    - Perimeter borings: continuous coring to 3 feet.
    - Cadmium borings: continuous coring to 20 feet.
- Sampling intervals:
  - Fenceline and Perimeter borings: samples from 0–6 inches, then 1-foot intervals (1–2, 2–3, 3–4, 4–5 feet).
  - Cadmium borings: samples from 0–6 inches, then approximately 5-foot intervals (4–5, 9–10, 14–15, 19–20 feet).
- Decontamination handling:
  - Rinse water from equipment decontamination will be contained, sampled, analyzed for metals, and properly disposed of.
- Laboratory analysis:
  - EPA Test Method 6010B and 7471 are referenced for metals analysis.
  - Laboratory analysis applies to all boring location categories, with additional analyses to be performed after review of initial results and as directed by DTSC/RWQCB.
- Reporting:
  - Summary report will be limited to findings from the Supplemental Site Investigation.
  - Separate reports will address nature and extent of contamination and human health risk assessment.
  - Laboratory reports will include soluble metals, MDL/PQL values, and J-flagging for estimated concentrations between MDL and PQL.

## Results
- No site investigation results are reported in this document.
- The only concrete outcomes are procedural/workplan revisions:
  - New/revised boring locations were added following the September 17, 2012 site survey.
  - Decontamination water management procedures were added.
  - Laboratory reporting was expanded to include soluble metals with MDL and PQL values and J-flag notation.
- No quantitative contamination measurements, risk results, or analytical findings are provided.

## Limitations
- This is a comment-response letter, not a results paper; it contains no empirical findings or final contamination data.
- The document only summarizes changes to the Final Workplan and does not present the outcomes of the supplemental site investigation.
- Nature and extent of contamination and human health risk assessment are explicitly deferred to separate future reports.
- Some details depend on subsequent DTSC/RWQCB review and direction, especially for additional analyses after initial laboratory results.
