## Key claims
- This report presents the results of a surface water sampling event conducted on March 5, 2016, at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles in Stanislaus County, California.
- The primary finding is that dissolved metal concentrations in surface water samples from the site generally did not exceed primary or secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), with one exception: manganese in sample PL2 (60 µg/l) exceeded its secondary MCL (50 µg/l).
- Stormwater runoff was observed to be retained within the Caltrans right-of-way, with no evidence of migration offsite.
- The analytical results for dissolved metals and general minerals were within the same general range of concentrations as previously reported for each sample location, indicating no significant change in water quality.

## Methodology
- **Site and Background**: The study was conducted at three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1-3), generated during construction of SR 99 in 1961 from soil excavated from a former Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC) evaporation pond. The stockpiles are located in Caltrans right-of-way.
- **Sampling Locations**: Surface water samples were collected from six designated locations: PL1 (west side of North Emerald Ave), PL2 (east side of North Emerald Ave), PL3 (south of Stockpile 2 at the alley behind Loletta Street, outside Caltrans right-of-way), PL4 (drainage basin outfall adjacent to Stockpile 3), PL5 (within 3 feet of a gate valve in the drainage basin south of Stockpile 3), and BG1 (background sample from a drop inlet on Loletta Avenue). Location BG2 (North Emerald Avenue) was dry and not sampled.
- **Sampling Event**: The sampling occurred on March 5, 2016, during a rain event that began on March 3 and ended on March 7, with a total rainfall of approximately 2.3 inches.
- **Sample Collection**: Samples were collected into disposable bailers using a suction pump and transferred to laboratory-provided containers. Samples for dissolved metals analysis were field-filtered through a 0.45-micron filter. Samples were capped, labeled, chilled, and transported to Advanced Technology Laboratories, Inc. (ATL) under chain-of-custody.
- **Field Measurements**: During sampling, field parameters were monitored: pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, turbidity, oxygen-reduction potential (ORP), and dissolved oxygen (DO). Data are presented in Table 1.
- **Laboratory Analyses**: Samples were analyzed for:
    - Dissolved metals: EPA Method 6020/7470A
    - Chloride, nitrate as nitrogen, sulfate: EPA Method 300.0
    - Sulfide: SM 4500-S-D
    - Total, bicarbonate, and carbonate alkalinity: SM 2320B
    - Total dissolved solids (TDS): SM 2540C
    - Total suspended solids (TSS): SM 2540D
- **QA/QC**: Field QA/QC included use of disposable bailers, decontamination of the sample pump, and chain-of-custody documentation. Laboratory QA/QC was reviewed and is discussed in the report.

## Results
- **Dissolved Metals (Table 2)**:
    - Antimony, barium, calcium, chromium, copper, magnesium, molybdenum, nickel, potassium, sodium, strontium, and vanadium were reported for samples PL1 through PL5.
    - Zinc was reported for samples PL2, PL4, and PL5.
    - Manganese was reported for samples PL2 and PL4.
    - Arsenic was reported only for sample PL2 (1.1 µg/l, below MCL of 10 µg/l).
    - **Manganese in sample PL2 (60 µg/l) exceeded its secondary MCL (50 µg/l).** All other reported concentrations were below their respective primary or secondary MCLs.
    - Beryllium, cadmium, cobalt, lead, mercury, selenium, silver, and thallium were not detected above PQLs in samples PL1-PL5.
    - Background sample BG1 contained antimony, barium, calcium, chromium, copper, magnesium, manganese, molybdenum, nickel, potassium, sodium, strontium, vanadium, and zinc, all below MCLs.
    - Specific numerical results for March 5, 2016 (key analytes, in µg/l unless noted):
        - Barium: PL1 24, PL2 35, PL3 210, PL4 59, PL5 65, BG1 53.
        - Calcium (mg/l): PL1 19, PL2 12, PL3 20, PL4 8.1, PL5 7.4, BG1 9.6.
        - Manganese: PL2 60, PL4 10 (at PQL), not detected in others.
        - Zinc: PL2 16, PL4 43, PL5 34, not detected in PL1, PL3, BG1.
- **General Minerals (Table 3)**:
    - None of the reported general minerals concentrations exceeded their respective primary or secondary MCLs.
    - For March 5, 2016: Chloride 0.24-3.6 mg/l, Sulfate 2.6-17 mg/l, TDS 61-130 mg/l, TSS 9-67 mg/l.
- **Field Parameters (Table 1, March 5, 2016)**:
    - pH: 7.20-7.86
    - Conductivity: 96-332 μmhos/cm
    - Temperature: 16.9-17.8 °C
    - Turbidity: 28.2-198 ntu
    - ORP: 126-182 mV
    - DO: 5.88-7.26 mg/l
- **Stormwater Management**: Observations indicated that stormwater runoff was retained within the site boundary.

## Limitations
- **Holding Time**: All samples were outside holding time for nitrate analysis because samples were collected on a Saturday.
- **Sample Dilution**: Samples PL1-PL5 and BG1 required dilution due to high concentration of target analytes (calcium). PL3 was diluted for barium and potassium.
- **Matrix Spike Recoveries**: Matrix spike recoveries for calcium, magnesium, nitrate, and sulfate were outside their acceptance limits. The laboratory noted that the analytical batch was validated by the laboratory control sample.
- **Missing Sample**: Sample location BG2 was dry and could not be sampled.
- **Single Event**: The report covers only one sampling event (March 5, 2016), though it references previous events for context.
