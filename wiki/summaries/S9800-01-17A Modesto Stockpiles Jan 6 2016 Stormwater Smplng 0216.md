Based on the four provided section summaries, the source document "raw/S9800-01-17A Modesto Stockpiles Jan 6 2016 Stormwater Smplng 0216.pdf" is a comprehensive stormwater sampling report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site. The document presents results from a surface water sampling event conducted on January 6, 2016, as part of a long-term monitoring program (2013–2016) that also includes historical data. The central finding is that runoff from the stockpiles remains within the Caltrans right-of-way and does not migrate offsite. No dissolved metals or general mineral concentrations in any sample exceeded their respective primary or secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs). The data are consistent with previous sampling events (2013–2015), with an isolated elevated barium level from 2006 noted but not repeated. Runoff management measures (vegetative cover, straw wattles, regraded berm, gravel on the access ramp) appear effective.

**Methodology**  
Field activities occurred during a rain event starting January 4 (total ~1.64 inches). Seven surface water samples were collected: five from stockpile drainage locations (PL1–PL5) and two background sites (BG1–BG2). Samples were taken from puddles, ponded areas, and drainage inlets using disposable bailers and a suction pump. Field parameters (pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, ORP, dissolved oxygen) were recorded. Laboratory analyses included dissolved metals (EPA Method 6020/7470A), anions (EPA 300.0), sulfide (SM 4500-S-D), alkalinity (SM 2320B), total dissolved solids (SM 2540C), and total suspended solids (SM 2540D). All analyses were performed by a California-certified laboratory (ATL) with chain-of-custody documentation.

**Results**  
- **Dissolved metals:** All reported concentrations (antimony, barium, calcium, chromium, copper, magnesium, manganese, molybdenum, nickel, potassium, sodium, strontium, vanadium, zinc) were below respective MCLs. For example, barium ranged from <0.50 to 100 µg/L in stockpile samples (PL1–PL5). Background samples showed similar ranges. Historical peaks (e.g., nickel up to 87 µg/L in 2014, barium up to 320 µg/L in 2014) remained below MCLs.  
- **General minerals:** Chloride, nitrate, sulfate, sulfide, alkalinity, TDS, and TSS were all below MCLs. One historical event (BG2 on 12/2/2014) showed TDS of 670 mg/L, exceeding the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L, but no such exceedance occurred in the January 2016 event.  
- **Field parameters (selected, from Table 1):** pH 7.73–8.92, conductivity 36–89 µmhos/cm, temperature 9.4–11.2 °C, turbidity 58.4–161 ntu, ORP 83–128 mV, DO 8.09–13.40 mg/L.  
- **Observations:** No runoff was observed migrating beyond the Caltrans right-of-way during sampling.

**Limitations and QA/QC**  
- **Laboratory QA/QC issues:** Several samples required dilution due to high concentrations of target analytes (e.g., calcium, barium, potassium, strontium, zinc). Matrix spike recoveries for total sulfide and calcium were outside acceptance limits, attributed to possible matrix interference; however, the analytical batch was validated by the laboratory control sample (LCS).  
- **Sampling constraints:** Samples PL1 and PL2 were taken from puddles (not flowing runoff); PL3 was collected outside the right-of-way beyond a chain-link fence (no photographs taken). The gate valve at PL5 was not opened during the event, so that sample reflects only standing basin water.  
- **Temporal scope:** This is a single event; long-term trends require continued monitoring.  
- **General limitations:** Stormwater matrix may not be directly comparable to drinking water MCLs; only dissolved metals were reported (not total metals); some sampling dates historically were dry (no sample).
