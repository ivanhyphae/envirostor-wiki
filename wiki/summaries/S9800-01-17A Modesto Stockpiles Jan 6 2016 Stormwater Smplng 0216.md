## Key claims
This report documents the results of a surface water sampling event conducted on January 6, 2016, at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles in Stanislaus County, California. The primary contributions are:
- No dissolved metals or general minerals exceeded state or federal Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for drinking water during this event.
- Concentrations of all monitored analytes were within the same general range as those reported in six prior sampling events since 2013, confirming no new or worsening contamination.
- Surface water runoff remained within the Caltrans right-of-way; no off-site migration was observed.
- Berm regrading and gravel placement on the Stockpile 2 access ramp have been implemented to further prevent off-site runoff.

## Methodology
- **Site and sampling locations**: Seven surface water samples were collected: PL1–PL5 (near stockpiles) and BG1–BG2 (background). PL1–PL2 were from puddles on North Emerald Avenue, PL3 from a puddle outside the right-of-way, PL4 from the drainage basin outfall, PL5 near a gate valve, BG1 from a drop inlet on Loletta Avenue, and BG2 from a drop inlet on North Emerald Avenue.
- **Sampling protocol**: Samples were collected using a suction pump and disposable bailers during active rainfall. Field-filtered (0.45 µm) for dissolved metals. Field parameters (pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, ORP, DO) were measured in situ.
- **Analyses**: Performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL), a California-certified lab. Methods included: EPA 6020/7470A for dissolved metals; EPA 300.0 for chloride, nitrate, sulfate; SM 4500-S-D for sulfide; SM 2320B for alkalinity; SM 2540C for TDS; SM 2540D for TSS.
- **Quality control**: QA/QC included decontamination procedures, chain-of-custody, laboratory blanks, LCS, matrix spikes, and duplicates. All QC acceptance criteria were met except for matrix spike recoveries for total sulfide and calcium, which were flagged as possibly due to matrix interference.
- **Comparative data**: Results were compared to historical data from events in April 2013, January 2014, February 2014 (two events), December 2014 (two events), and December 2015.

## Results
- **Rainfall**: Total precipitation for the event (January 4–6, 2016) was approximately 1.64 inches.
- **Field parameters**: pH ranged from 7.73 to 8.92; conductivity 36–89 µmhos/cm; temperature 9.4–11.2 °C; turbidity 58.4–261 ntu; ORP 65–128 mV; DO 3.74–13.40 mg/L.
- **Dissolved metals**: Barium was detected in all samples (19–100 µg/L in PL samples, 25–30 µg/L in background). No analyte exceeded MCLs. Arsenic, beryllium, cadmium, cobalt, mercury, selenium, silver, and thallium were below practical quantitation limits (PQLs) in all samples.
- **General minerals**: Nitrate (as N) was ≤0.47 mg/L; sulfate ≤5.6 mg/L; chloride ≤2.6 mg/L; TDS ≤63 mg/L; TSS up to 180 mg/L; sulfide up to 0.061 mg/L. No MCL exceedances.
- **Historical comparison**: Results were consistent with previous events.

## Limitations
- **Single event**: The report covers only one rain event; conclusions about long-term trends are limited.
- **Matrix interference**: QA/QC notes indicated matrix spike recoveries for total sulfide and calcium were outside acceptance limits, attributed to possible matrix interference. The analytical batch was validated by the laboratory control sample.
- **Dilution required**: Samples PL1–PL5 and BG2 required dilution due to high target analyte concentrations.
- **No photo for PL3**: A photograph was not taken at sample location PL3 due to oversight.
- **Gate valve not opened**: The drainage basin gate valve (sample PL5) was not opened during the event; thus, data may not represent worst-case discharge conditions.
- **Runoff not observed migrating off-site**: The observation of no off-site migration is based on visual inspection during this event.
