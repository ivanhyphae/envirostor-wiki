The document is a monitoring and laboratory reporting package for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles project (S9800-01-17A) in Stanislaus County, California. Geocon collected stormwater/surface-water samples during a rain event on January 6, 2016, from stockpile runoff locations PL1–PL5 and background/inlet locations BG1–BG2, using procedures consistent with a DTSC-approved Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan. The purpose was to evaluate whether runoff from the stockpiles contained elevated contaminants and whether it migrated beyond the Caltrans right-of-way.

Sampling and analysis followed standard field and laboratory QA/QC procedures: water was collected with bailers and a suction pump, dissolved-metal samples were filtered in the field, chain-of-custody was maintained, and field measurements included pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, ORP, and dissolved oxygen. Laboratory testing included dissolved metals by EPA 6020 and mercury by EPA 7470A, along with chloride, nitrate, sulfate, sulfide, alkalinity, total dissolved solids, and total suspended solids. The report also includes QC documentation showing the analytical batches were generally valid, with only a few minor QC flags such as some matrix spike recoveries outside limits or results requiring dilution.

Overall, results showed low concentrations for most analytes, with many results below detection limits and no reported exceedances of California Title 22 primary or secondary drinking-water standards. Detected dissolved metals included common constituents such as barium, calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium, strontium, and zinc, along with some trace metals like chromium, copper, nickel, molybdenum, antimony, manganese, vanadium, and lead at some locations. General mineral parameters were measurable but modest, and the document’s comparison across current and prior events indicates runoff has generally remained contained within the right-of-way under existing site controls.
