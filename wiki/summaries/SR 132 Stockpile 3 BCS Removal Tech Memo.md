The memorandum for SR 132 Stockpile 3 documents verification testing and certified analytical results for soil removed from the former Stockpile 3 area in the State Route 132 right-of-way in Modesto, California. The project involved excavation and offsite/onsite transfer of about 38,440 cubic yards of barium-containing soil, and the purpose of the memo was to confirm that the remaining excavation met the RDIP cleanup criteria so the stockpile area could be reused as clean embankment fill without additional monitoring.

Sampling and analysis were conducted on the excavation base and berms using stainless-steel sampling tubes under chain of custody, with soils analyzed by Advanced Technology Laboratories for total barium and lead using EPA Method 6010B ICP-AES after EPA 3050B preparation. The verification program required 24 soil samples, and 24 excavation-base samples (S3 1-0 through S3 24-0) were collected between Feb. 28 and Mar. 12, 2020, along with two berm samples. Where elevated barium was first encountered around sample locations S3 9 through S3 11, excavation was deepened by about one foot and resampled. The report also describes the laboratory QA/QC program, including blanks, laboratory control samples, matrix spikes, and matrix spike duplicates; one barium matrix spike recovery fell outside acceptance limits due to suspected matrix interference, but the batch was still validated based on the LCS and other QC checks.

Overall results show that final verification samples met the applicable cleanup thresholds of 1,000 mg/kg for barium and 80 mg/kg for lead. Reported barium concentrations across the final excavation samples ranged from about 46 to 970 mg/kg, and lead ranged from about 1.6 to 40 mg/kg. The highest final barium result was 970 mg/kg at S3 16-0, and the highest lead result was 40 mg/kg at S3 4-0. Some earlier or pre-correction samples showed much higher barium values, such as 7,000 mg/kg at S3 9-0 and 5,100 mg/kg at S3 10-0, but these were addressed by re-excavation and were not the final compliant condition. The later certificate-of-analysis sections for samples such as S3 22-0 through S3 24-0 likewise showed low-level barium and lead, with QC results generally within limits. The document’s conclusion is that Stockpile 3 passed verification and requires no further BCS RDIP monitoring.
