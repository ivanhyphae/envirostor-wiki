## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Email transmittal of the “Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan” (attached as PDF) from Geocon to Caltrans, forwarded to DTSC and Central Valley RWQCB for review and approval. The plan covers sampling at a proposed clean cover mining location (Basin 5) for the SR 132 remediation project.
- **Author or preparer:** *Original preparer:* John Juhrend, P.E., CEG, CEM (Geocon Consultants, Inc.). *Forwarder:* Richard C. Stewart, Engineering Geologist (Caltrans Central Region Environmental Division).
- **Date or period covered:** June 11, 2020 (email date; sampling plan attached not separately dated in email).

## Key claims
- **Geocon’s assertion:** The sampling frequency for Basin 5 is based on its location “adjacent and north of the previously tested SR 132 roadway cut section east of Carpenter Road that exposed non‑impacted native formation.” Similar geological conditions are anticipated at Basin 5.
- **Geocon’s assumption:** A previously submitted technical memorandum (for the area east of Carpenter Avenue) — containing sampling methodology and comparative background metal concentration tables — is applicable to Basin 5 and was not resubmitted.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Status:** Pending. The plan is submitted to DTSC (Dean Wright, Perry Myers) and Central Valley RWQCB (Steven Meeks) for “expedited approval.” No decision or conditions are recorded in this email.
