## Key claims

- This report presents groundwater monitoring results for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (Stanislaus County, California) from the October 2016 and April 2017 monitoring events, conducted in accordance with an approved DTSC work plan.
- Depth-to-groundwater measurements were collected from up to 10 monitoring wells. In October 2016, three wells (MW-3, MW-4, MW-8) were dry. In April 2017, five wells (MW-5, MW-6, MW-8, MW-9, MW-10) were dry or had insufficient water for sampling.
- Groundwater samples were collected from five monitoring wells (MW-1, MW-2, MW-3, MW-4, MW-7) in April 2017 and analyzed for dissolved metals (including barium, lead, strontium) and general minerals.
- Barium and strontium concentrations (33–180 µg/L and 190–1,200 µg/L, respectively) remained significantly below their numeric water quality thresholds (1,000/700 µg/L and 4,000 µg/L). Lead was not detected above method detection limits in any sample.
- Manganese in MW-1 (71 µg/L) exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L. Nitrate exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in three wells (MW-1: 15 mg/L, MW-2: 24 mg/L, MW-4: 12 mg/L). Total dissolved solids (TDS) exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in three wells (MW-1: 550 mg/L, MW-2: 550 mg/L, MW-4: 520 mg/L).
- Groundwater flow direction was toward the south (October 2016, gradient 0.007) and toward the east-southeast (April 2017, gradient 0.0007), consistent with historical flow.
- Stiff diagrams show two distinct groundwater types: calcium-dominant (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-7) and sodium-dominant (MW-3). These compositional patterns have been consistent since 2012.

## Methodology

- **Study site**: Three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1–3) generated during construction of SR 99 in 1961 from soil excavated from a former FMC evaporation pond. Site is underlain by the Modesto Formation (interbedded sands, silts, clays). Groundwater is encountered at ~35 ft bgs under unconfined to semi-confined conditions in the shallow upper aquifer zone.
- **Monitoring network**: Ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) installed to assess groundwater quality adjacent to the stockpiles. Wells were constructed with 2-inch diameter schedule 40 PVC casing, 10-ft screened intervals, and #2/12 sand filter packs.
- **Depth-to-water measurements**: Collected using a battery-operated water level meter from surveyed reference points at the top of well casings (TOC) on October 28, 2016 and April 12, 2017.
- **Well purging and sampling (April 2017)**: Approximately 3–4 well volumes (2–4.5 gallons) were purged from five wells (MW-1 through MW-4, MW-7) using disposable bailers. During purging, pH, electrical conductivity, and temperature were monitored. Groundwater samples were collected using disposable bailers and decanted through slow-emptying devices. Samples for dissolved metals were field-filtered through a 0.45-micron filter using a hand-pressure pump.
- **Analytical methods**: Title 22 dissolved metals (including strontium) by EPA Method 6020/7470; dissolved calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium by EPA 6020; chloride, nitrate as nitrogen, sulfate by EPA 300.0; sulfide by SM 4500; TDS by SM 2540C; total/bicarbonate/carbonate alkalinity by SM 2320B. Analyses performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL), a California-certified laboratory.
- **Quality assurance/quality control**: One duplicate sample (MW-11) was collected from MW-7. Laboratory QA/QC included method blanks, laboratory control samples, and matrix spike/matrix spike duplicates (MS/MSD).
- **Evaluation metrics**: Dissolved metal concentrations were compared to California Department of Public Health (CDPH) Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories, and CDPH Notification Levels. General minerals were compared to primary and secondary MCLs.
- **Data interpretation**: Groundwater elevation contours and gradients were calculated from depth-to-water data. Stiff diagrams were constructed from major cation (Na⁺, K⁺, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺) and anion (Cl⁻, HCO₃⁻, SO₄²⁻) concentrations to characterize groundwater types.

## Results

- **Depth to groundwater (October 2016)**: Ranged from 38.77 to 47.10 ft below TOC in 7 wells with water; groundwater flow toward south, average gradient of 0.007.
- **Depth to groundwater (April 2017)**: Ranged from 35.52 to 40.73 ft below TOC in 5 wells with water; groundwater flow toward east-southeast, average gradient of 0.0007.
- **Dissolved barium**: Range 33 µg/L (MW-3) to 180 µg/L (MW-1). Threshold: 1,000 µg/L (Primary MCL) / 700 µg/L (Health Advisory). All below thresholds.
- **Dissolved strontium**: Range 190 µg/L (MW-3) to 1,200 µg/L (MW-1). Threshold: 4,000 µg/L (Health Advisory). All below threshold.
- **Dissolved lead**: Not detected above MDL in any of the five samples.
- **Other dissolved metals**: Antimony (0.08–0.15 µg/L), arsenic (1.6–4.8 µg/L), chromium (2.8–7.4 µg/L), copper (0.54–2.9 µg/L), molybdenum (0.18–1.3 µg/L), nickel (0.49–3.4 µg/L), selenium (0.19–0.73 µg/L), vanadium (21–34 µg/L) — all below respective thresholds. Manganese: 71 µg/L in MW-1 (exceeds secondary MCL of 50 µg/L). Cadmium, cobalt, zinc detected at low concentrations.
- **General minerals**: Nitrate as nitrogen: 2.6–24 mg/L (MCL = 10 mg/L; exceeded in MW-1: 15 mg/L, MW-2: 24 mg/L, MW-4: 12 mg/L). TDS: 230–550 mg/L (Secondary MCL = 500 mg/L; exceeded in MW-1: 550 mg/L, MW-2: 550 mg/L, MW-4: 520 mg/L). Sulfide: 0.016–0.18 mg/L. Dissolved calcium: 13–73 mg/L. Dissolved sodium: 15–54 mg/L. Total alkalinity: 130–340 mg/L as CaCO₃.
- **Stiff diagrams**: Wells MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-7 show calcium-dominant composition; well MW-3 shows sodium-dominant composition — consistent with historical data since 2012.
- **QA/QC**: Duplicate sample (MW-11 vs. MW-7) showed elevated relative percent differences (RPDs) for sulfide (151%) and carbonate (68%), qualifying those results as estimated. Laboratory MS/MSD had some recoveries/RPDs outside control limits, but data were validated by laboratory control samples and considered of sufficient quality.

## Limitations

- Several monitoring wells were dry or had insufficient water for sampling during both events, limiting the spatial coverage of groundwater quality data: MW-3, MW-4, MW-8 were dry in October 2016; MW-5, MW-6, MW-8, MW-9, MW-10 were dry or had insufficient water in April 2017.
- Duplicate sample analysis revealed high relative percent differences for sulfide (151%) and carbonate (68%), indicating potential analytical variability for these parameters. Results for sulfide and carbonate are qualified as estimated values.
- Laboratory MS/MSD analyses showed several analyte recoveries or RPDs outside control limits; however, sample results were validated by laboratory control samples, and no data qualification was deemed necessary.
- Laboratory samples required dilution due to high target analyte concentrations or internal standard failure, which may affect detection limits and data comparability.
- Historical data (prior to March 2012) were reproduced from a previous report (Shaw Environmental, 2007), introducing potential inconsistencies in methodology and reporting.
- Groundwater monitoring is performed annually with semi-annual depth-to-water measurements, providing limited temporal resolution for detecting trends or seasonal variability.
