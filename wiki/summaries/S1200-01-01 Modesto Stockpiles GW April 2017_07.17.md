Based on the four provided section summaries, the source document is a groundwater monitoring report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, covering data from 2006 to 2017 with a focus on the April 2017 sampling event. The primary objectives were to characterize groundwater quality, track long-term trends for dissolved metals (Title 22) and general minerals, and assess compliance with regulatory thresholds.

**Methodology** involved semi-annual depth-to-water measurements and annual groundwater sampling from up to ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10). Samples were collected using disposable bailers and analyzed by certified laboratories for dissolved metals, general minerals, physicochemical parameters, and occasionally PAHs. Stiff diagrams were used to characterize hydrogeochemical facies.

**Key results** include: (1) groundwater flow direction remains generally south to southeast, with gradients ranging from 0.007 (October 2016) to 0.0007 (April 2017); (2) barium (33–180 µg/L) and strontium (190–1,200 µg/L) concentrations were consistently below respective thresholds (MCL 1,000 µg/L; Health Advisory 4,000 µg/L); (3) nitrate as nitrogen exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in three wells (up to 24 mg/L), and total dissolved solids (TDS) exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in three wells (up to 550 mg/L); (4) most PAH results were below detection limits; and (5) Stiff diagrams from April 2017 indicate calcium-bicarbonate and sodium-bicarbonate water types.

**Limitations** include: several wells (MW-5, MW-6, MW-8, MW-9, MW-10) were dry or had insufficient water for sampling during the April 2017 event, reducing spatial coverage. Additionally, high relative percent differences in field duplicate samples (e.g., sulfide 151%, carbonate 68%) required certain results to be considered estimated. Some historical data were reproduced from a previous report, and certain results were qualified as estimated (J-flag) or non-detect due to equipment blank contamination (U-flag).
