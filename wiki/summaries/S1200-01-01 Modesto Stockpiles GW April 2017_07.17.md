The report summarizes groundwater monitoring at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California, with emphasis on sampling events in October 2016 and April 2017 and historical data from 2006–2017. The site is monitored by ten wells (MW-1 through MW-10), though several wells were dry or had insufficient water for sampling during the April 2017 event. Groundwater occurs in the shallow zone of the upper aquifer and generally flows south to southeast/east-southeast under a low hydraulic gradient.

Field work in April 2017 included depth-to-water measurements, purging of roughly 3–4 well volumes, and collection of groundwater samples from MW-1, MW-2, MW-3, MW-4, and MW-7. Field parameters such as pH, conductivity, dissolved oxygen, and ORP indicated neutral to slightly alkaline, low-oxygen groundwater with no free product observed. Laboratory analyses covered Title 22 dissolved metals, major ions, nitrate, sulfate, TDS, alkalinity, chloride, and related water-quality constituents, with QA/QC supported by duplicate samples, blanks, and chain-of-custody review.

Across the monitoring network and sampling history, most dissolved metals and general mineral concentrations were at or below reporting limits or applicable drinking-water thresholds. The main recurring exceedances or elevated constituents were nitrate and total dissolved solids, with an isolated manganese exceedance. Barium and strontium, identified as primary chemicals of concern, remained well below their numeric thresholds and were generally consistent with historical levels. Reported lead and other metals were mostly low or non-detect, with a few isolated detections in specific wells such as MW-5.

Groundwater elevations measured on 4/12/2017 ranged from about 43.01 to 44.87 ft MSL, and the contour map indicates southward groundwater flow. Stiff diagrams and ionic chemistry data show multiple groundwater types, with overall bicarbonate-dominant water but persistent differences between calcium-dominant wells (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-7) and sodium-dominant well MW-3. Overall, the report concludes that groundwater quality is generally stable, with localized chemical differences and a few parameters of concern, but no evidence of widespread dissolved-metal contamination above regulatory thresholds.
