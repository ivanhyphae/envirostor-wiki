## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Approval letter from DTSC to Caltrans conveying DTSC’s concurrence with the revised Supplemental Site Investigation (SSI) and Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) Update reports for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (State Route 132/99, Stanislaus County, CA). The letter documents regulatory acceptance of these reports as part of the remedial action planning process.
- **Author or preparer:** Randy S. Adams, C.E.G., Senior Engineering Geologist, Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) – Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program.
- **Date or period covered:** Letter dated April 4, 2013. Reports under review were finalized March 1, 2013; the workplan that led to the reports was approved September 20, 2012; fieldwork occurred in late September 2012; draft reports submitted December 2012; DTSC comments provided February 15, 2013.

## Key claims
- DTSC, in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), reviewed the following reports prepared by Caltrans’ contractor Geocon Consultants Inc. (Geocon):
  - "Response to DTSC Comments on the Supplemental Site Investigation and Human Health Risk Assessment Update" (March 1, 2013)
  - "Supplemental Site Investigation (SSI), Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles…" (March 1, 2013)
  - "Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) Update, Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles…" (March 1, 2013)
- DTSC concurs with the March 1, 2013 SSI and HHRA Update reports.
- The additional characterization data (from the September 2012 workplan) were intended to evaluate potential lateral and vertical migration of contaminants from the soil stockpiles and to update the HHRA.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision:** DTSC (with RWQCB concurrence) formally approves (concurs with) the Final SSI and HHRA Update reports dated March 1, 2013.
- **Agency:** Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB).
- **Date of decision:** April 4, 2013 (letter date).
- **Conditions imposed:** None explicitly stated in this letter. The approval is unconditional; the reports are accepted as submitted.
- **Related prior decision:** DTSC approved the "Final Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan" (Geocon, September 18, 2012) on September 20, 2012.

## Analytical results
- No analytical results are presented in this letter. The letter references that the SSI and HHRA Update reports contain characterization data (including evaluation of lateral and vertical migration of contaminants). The known contaminant of concern from the overall project is barium in fill material, but no concentrations or thresholds are cited in this document.

## Remedial actions
- No remedial actions are described in this letter. The SSI and HHRA Update are investigatory and risk-assessment steps, not remediation implementation documents.

## Key terms and conditions
- No specific follow-on actions, deadlines, monitoring obligations, or restrictions are imposed in this approval letter. The letter serves as acceptance of the submitted reports and closes the review cycle for the SSI and HHRA Update.
- The work is conducted under an Interagency Agreement and Task Order between Caltrans and DTSC for Remedial Action Plan preparation.
- The SSI and HHRA Update are considered final and approved for use in the remedial action planning process.
