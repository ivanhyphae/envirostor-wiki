### Summary of "raw/S9525-06-44AModesto Stockpiles GW. SEPT2012.1212.pdf"

This document is a laboratory report from Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) for a routine groundwater monitoring event conducted at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California, in September 2012. The investigation involved collecting samples from ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) to assess groundwater quality.

**Key Findings on Groundwater Quality**

- **Metals**: With the exception of manganese, concentrations of dissolved metals were below regulatory thresholds. Barium (47–280 µg/L), lead (all <1.0 µg/L), and strontium (220–1,100 µg/L) were present at levels consistent with historical data. Manganese exceeded various thresholds in wells MW-3 and MW-9.
- **Inorganics**: Nitrate as nitrogen exceeded the primary drinking water standard (MCL) of 10 mg/L in five wells (MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, MW-9, and MW-10). Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in three wells (MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10). Notably, the report suggests that elevated nitrate and TDS in upgradient well MW-1 may reflect natural background conditions.
- **Organics**: No polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) were detected at or above practical quantitation limits (PQLs) in any sample.

**Hydrogeological Context**

Groundwater flow direction was consistent with historical data, moving toward the southeast with a low average gradient of 0.0008 ft/ft. Stiff diagrams indicate the groundwater is bicarbonate-dominant, with a calcium-dominant composition in most wells and a sodium-dominant composition in wells MW-3, MW-6, and MW-8.

**Methodology and Data Quality**

Samples were collected on September 19–20, 2012, and analyzed by ATL. The laboratory used a standard suite of EPA and Standard Methods (e.g., EPA 6020 for metals on ICP-MS, EPA 8270-SIM for PAHs, EPA 300.0 for anions). Quality control data (method blanks, laboratory control samples, matrix spikes) were generally acceptable. Some minor issues were noted, including matrix spike recoveries and duplicate relative percent differences (RPDs) outside control limits for certain metals, likely due to high native concentrations. An equipment blank confirmed no significant contamination from sampling equipment. A key limitation is that the wells sample the shallow upper aquifer, and deeper aquifers were not evaluated.

**Overall Conclusion**

The September 2012 monitoring event indicated that, aside from naturally elevated manganese and site-related exceedances of nitrate and TDS standards, the groundwater at the Modesto Stockpiles site met applicable water quality thresholds. No PAH contamination was detected.
