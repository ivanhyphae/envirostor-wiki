The document is a Certificate of Analysis from Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) for the Modesto Stockpiles project (S9525-06-44), reporting analytical results for six groundwater monitoring well samples (MW-5 through MW-10) and an equipment blank collected on September 20, 2012. Analyses included dissolved and total metals (EPA 6020), mercury (EPA 7470), anions (EPA 300.0), alkalinity, total dissolved solids, sulfide, and semivolatile organic compounds (SVOCs; EPA 8270/SIM). Key findings indicate that no dissolved metals exceeded drinking water standards; SVOCs and mercury were not detected in any sample. Specific results include elevated concentrations of calcium, magnesium, sodium, and other major ions in wells MW-7, MW-6, and MW-10, with total dissolved solids reaching up to 630 mg/L in MW-10. Several quality control flags (e.g., matrix spike recoveries outside limits, high relative percent differences, dilutions required) suggest potential matrix interferences, particularly for metals in MW-6. Overall, the report provides a comprehensive groundwater chemistry characterization for the site with documented QC limitations.
