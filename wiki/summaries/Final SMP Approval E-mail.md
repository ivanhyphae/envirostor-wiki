## Key claims
- The document is an email approval from DTSC and the Water Board confirming official acceptance of a revised Soil Management Plan (SMP) for the SR 132 Modesto project.
- The SMP governs excavation, confirmation sampling, handling, and offsite disposal of impacted soil for two proposed retention basins associated with new construction along State Route 132.
- The revised SMP was updated to address prior comments from DTSC and the Water Board.
- A key technical update was lowering the diesel-range total petroleum hydrocarbons (TPH-d) screening level to 500 mg/kg, aligning with the DTSC screening level for commercial/industrial soil.
- The City intended to proceed with certain excavation activities before final resolution of groundwater-protection screening levels with the Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), with the understanding that any needed revisions could follow.

## Methodology
- This is not an experimental research paper; it is a regulatory review and approval correspondence.
- The reviewed document is a revised Soil Management Plan dated February 8, 2021.
- The SMP covers:
  - excavation activities,
  - confirmation soil sampling,
  - segregation and covering of impacted soil,
  - labeling and transport of impacted material offsite,
  - proper disposal of impacted soil.
- The plan applies to two retention basins:
  - Basin 6A, including targeted excavation at borings RB-10 and RB-11,
  - Basin 6B, including excavation of approximately 500 cubic yards of soil south of 611 Franklin.
- The correspondence references prior regulatory comments from January 31, 2021 and an unresolved discussion with RWQCB regarding the appropriate screening level for protection of groundwater.

## Results
- DTSC and the Water Board concluded that the revised SMP addressed their January 31, 2021 comments.
- The SMP received official acceptance via email on February 11, 2021.
- Two minor additional comments were noted:
  - soil with TPH above screening levels should be segregated, covered, and labeled until offsite transport,
  - if TPH-impacted soil remains when basin maximum depth is reached, Caltrans should be alerted and DTSC/WB consulted for next steps.
- Quantitative values mentioned:
  - TPH-d screening level lowered to 500 mg/kg,
  - excavation of approximately 500 cubic yards +/- at Basin 6B.
- No performance metrics or statistical evaluation results are provided.

## Limitations
- The document is an approval email, not a full technical report, so it provides limited detail on underlying site characterization data, sampling results, or risk calculations.
- RWQCB had not yet responded to requests to discuss the groundwater-protection screening level, so the groundwater-related aspect of the SMP remained unresolved at the time of approval.
- The approval is conditional in practice: field incorporation of the two noted comments is allowed without further formal SMP revision.
- The correspondence does not provide final outcomes for excavation or disposal activities, only approval of the management plan.
