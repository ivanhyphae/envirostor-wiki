## Key claims
- DTSC and Water Board (WB) officially accept the revised Soil Management Plan (SMP) for the SR 132 Modesto project, dated February 8, 2021.
- Two minor comments require field incorporation: (1) soil with TPH above screening levels must be segregated, covered, and labeled until offsite transport; (2) if TPH-impacted soil persists at maximum basin depth, Caltrans must be alerted and consultation with WB and DTSC is needed for next steps.
- The screening level for diesel-range total petroleum hydrocarbons (TPH-d) was lowered to 500 mg/kg to align with DTSC's commercial/industrial soil screening level.
- Excavation activities can proceed under the SMP, but a separate discussion with RWQCB regarding appropriate screening levels for groundwater protection is anticipated; its outcome will apply to soils left onsite or reused at finished grade.

## Methodology
- Review process: DTSC and WB reviewed the revised SMP submitted via email on February 8, 2021, which incorporated previous comments from January 31, 2021.
- Regulatory context: The SMP governs excavation, confirmation sampling, handling, and disposal of impacted soil for two retention basins along State Route 132.
- Proposed excavation activities: Targeted excavation of TPH-d impacted soils at borings RB-10 and RB-11 within Basin 6A with confirmation soil sampling; excavation of ~500 cubic yards from an area with no known TPH-d impacts in Basin 6B.
- Approval mechanism: Formal acceptance conveyed via email, allowing field incorporation of two minor comments without SMP revision.

## Results
- Official acceptance of SMP granted via email on February 11, 2021, by DTSC Project Manager Dean Wright.
- Soil with TPH above screening levels must be segregated, covered, and labeled; unresolved TPH at maximum depth triggers consultation.
- Screening level for TPH-d set at 500 mg/kg (commercial/industrial standard).
- No quantitative results (e.g., contaminant concentrations, volumes of excavated soil) are provided; only process approvals are documented.

## Limitations
- The screening level for TPH-d is based on commercial/industrial soil criteria, but protection of groundwater remains unresolved pending separate discussion with RWQCB.
- If TPH-impacted soil remains after reaching maximum basin depth, no predefined action is specified—only a requirement for consultation.
- The approval is conditional on field incorporation of two comments; no re-review of the written SMP is required, which may introduce ambiguity if field interpretations differ.
