## Key claims
- The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Water Board (WB) officially accepted the revised Soil Management Plan (SMP) for the SR‑132 Modesto project, dated February 8, 2021.
- The revised SMP incorporates comments from a January 31, 2021 review, including lowering the screening level for diesel‑range total petroleum hydrocarbons (TPH‑d) to 500 mg/kg, consistent with the DTSC commercial/industrial soil screening level.
- Two minor additional conditions were required: (1) soil with TPH above screening levels must be labeled until transported offsite, and (2) if TPH‑impacted soil remains at the maximum basin depth, Caltrans must be alerted and consultation with WB and DTSC shall determine next steps.
- The City of Modesto originally planned to begin excavation before regulatory acceptance; Caltrans reminded them that acceptance was required first.

## Methodology
- The “study” consisted of a regulatory review process: DTSC and WB reviewed a revised SMP submitted by Rincon Consultants (on behalf of the City of Modesto) and issued acceptance via email.
- No experimental setup, datasets, or evaluation metrics are applicable; this is a project management and regulatory compliance communication.
- The screening level for TPH‑d (500 mg/kg) was selected based on DTSC’s commercial/industrial soil screening criteria, with the understanding that a separate discussion with the RWQCB would determine the appropriate level for protection of groundwater.

## Results
- Quantitative outcome: TPH‑d screening level set to 500 mg/kg.
- Approval granted with two field‑implementable conditions; no further revision of the SMP was required.
- Excavation activities proposed: (1) targeted excavation of TPH‑d impacted soils at borings RB‑10 and RB‑11 within Basin 6A, and (2) excavation of approximately 500 cubic yards from south of 611 Franklin within Basin 6B (no known TPH‑d impacts).

## Limitations
- The SMP does not address the scenario where TPH‑impacted soil persists after reaching the maximum basin depth; the approval condition requires alerting Caltrans and consulting WB/DTSC for next steps.
- The appropriate screening level for protection of groundwater was still unresolved at the time of acceptance (RWQCB had not responded to meeting requests); the outcome of that discussion would apply to soil left onsite or reused at finished grade.
- The email‑based acceptance mechanism implies acceptance is contingent on the two noted conditions being incorporated in the field, not in a formal revised document.
