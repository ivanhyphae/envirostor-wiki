## Key claims
- The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) approves Alternative 4 (Containment) as the selected remedy for Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles under the State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project.
- Containment is achieved by constructing retaining walls, bridge abutments, and roadway over stockpiles; unpaved areas receive clean fill cover.
- The remedy eliminates the exposure pathway to human receptors and minimizes surface water infiltration into groundwater beneath the stockpiles.
- The Remedial Action Plan (RAP) satisfies California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) requirements; the associated Environmental Impact Report/Environmental Assessment (EIR/EA) was certified with a Finding of No Significant Impact under NEPA.
- DTSC independently determined that the selected remedy will have no significant environmental effect with implementation of project controls and mitigation measures.

## Methodology
- **Regulatory framework**: CEQA and National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) compliance; DTSC and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) as reviewing agencies.
- **Phased implementation**: Phase 1 (interim, 2019–2020) and Phase 2 (ultimate build-out, 2028–2029).
- **Required deliverables per phase**: Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP) for DTSC review/approval; start work notice (English/Spanish) published locally two weeks before construction; Remedial Action Completion Report; Operation and Maintenance Agreement (OMA) and Operation and Maintenance Plan (OMP) after Phase 1; land use covenant and soil management plan; annual pavement inspection and groundwater monitoring; DTSC five-year remedy evaluation.
- **Certification**: Containment remedy certified only after ultimate build-out phase; OMA/OMP may be modified after Phase 2.

## Results
- DTSC formally **approves** the RAP.
- No quantitative performance metrics (e.g., contaminant concentrations, infiltration rates) are provided in the letter.
- Implementation schedule fixed: Phase 1 (2019–2020), Phase 2 (2028–2029).
- Remedy is deemed protective of human health and environment under specified conditions.

## Limitations
- Contamination remains in place indefinitely; remedy relies on containment rather than removal or treatment.
- Long-term effectiveness depends on continued operation, maintenance, and monitoring (annual inspections, five-year evaluations).
- Land use covenant restricts future land use to prevent exposure.
- Final RAP must be updated, placed in local repositories, and submitted to DTSC/RWQCB before proceeding.
- OMA and OMP may need revision after Phase 2 to reflect ultimate build-out.
