## Key claims
- The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) approves the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles associated with the State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project in Stanislaus County, California.
- The selected remedy is **Alternative 4: Containment**.
- The containment remedy is achieved by incorporating the stockpiles into project construction features, specifically:
  - retaining walls,
  - bridge abutments,
  - beneath the roadway,
  - and clean fill cover on unpaved portions.
- DTSC claims the remedy provides **long-term protection of human health and the environment** by:
  - eliminating exposure pathways to human receptors,
  - minimizing surface water infiltration into groundwater beneath the stockpiles.
- DTSC concludes the RAP meets **CEQA** requirements and, with project controls/mitigation measures, will not have a significant environmental effect.

## Methodology
- This is an **approval letter** summarizing DTSC’s review of Caltrans’ RAP and related environmental documentation, rather than a primary experimental study.
- Caltrans served as the lead agency under **CEQA** and **NEPA**.
- Caltrans prepared an **Environmental Impact Report/Environmental Assessment (EIR/EA)** for the State Route 132 project.
- DTSC and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board served as reviewing agencies under CEQA.
- Caltrans also prepared:
  - a **Finding of No Significant Impact (FONSI)** under NEPA,
  - and a **Notice of Determination** under CEQA.
- DTSC performed an **independent analysis** of the selected remedy and associated mitigation measures.
- Planned implementation and oversight elements include:
  - two project phases,
  - Remedial Design and Implementation Plans (RDIPs) for each phase,
  - a Start Work Notice in English and Spanish,
  - Remedial Action Completion Reports,
  - an Operation and Maintenance Agreement (OMA),
  - an Operation and Maintenance Plan (OMP),
  - annual inspections,
  - groundwater monitoring,
  - a land use covenant,
  - a soil management plan,
  - and five-year remedy reviews.

## Results
- DTSC **approves** the RAP for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles project.
- DTSC determined that **Alternative 4 (Containment)** will **not have a significant effect on the environment** when implemented with project controls/mitigation measures.
- DTSC found that Caltrans’ Final EIR/EA contains the necessary mitigation measures to maintain a **less than significant impact**.
- The project is scheduled in two phases:
  - **Phase 1 (interim project phase): 2019–2020**
  - **Phase 2 (ultimate build-out phase): 2028–2029**
- Post-implementation requirements include:
  - annual inspection of pavement and containment features,
  - groundwater monitoring,
  - remedy certification after final build-out,
  - and five-year evaluations thereafter.

## Limitations
- The letter does not provide quantitative contaminant concentration data, sampling results, or comparative performance metrics for alternative remedies.
- The document is an approval and regulatory determination, so it contains **no experimental dataset** or formal statistical evaluation.
- The final remedy depends on future implementation details, including phase-specific RDIPs, O&M plans, and ongoing monitoring.
- The final containment remedy may require later modification of the OMA/OMP after the ultimate build-out phase.
