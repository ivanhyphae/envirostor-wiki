Here is a structured summary of the provided document.

## Key claims
- The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) formally approves the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles.
- The selected remedy, "Alternative 4, Containment," involves containing the contaminated soil stockpiles by constructing the State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project over them.
- The containment remedy will achieve long-term protection of human health and the environment by eliminating exposure pathways and minimizing surface water infiltration into groundwater.
- The RAP meets the requirements of the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA).
- DTSC has independently determined that the selected remedy will not have a significant effect on the environment, given the implementation of identified mitigation measures.

## Methodology
- The RAP was prepared by the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles associated with the State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project.
- Caltrans, as the lead agency, prepared an Environmental Impact Report/Environmental Assessment (EIR/EA) under CEQA and NEPA. DTSC and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) were responsible reviewing agencies.
- DTSC performed an independent analysis of the Final EIR/EA to determine the environmental impact of the selected remedy.

## Results
- **Selected Remedy:** Alternative 4, Containment.
- **Implementation Schedule:** Two phases: Phase 1 (interim) from 2019 to 2020; Phase 2 (ultimate build-out) from 2028 to 2029.
- **Regulatory Outcome:** DTSC will prepare a Statement of Findings and a Notice of Determination.
- **Post-implementation Requirements:**
    - An Operation and Maintenance Agreement (OMA) and Operation and Maintenance Plan (OMP) are required after Phase 1.
    - A land use covenant and soil management plan are required.
    - DTSC will evaluate the containment remedy every five years.
    - Final certification of the remedy will occur after the ultimate build-out phase.

## Limitations
- The letter notes that the Draft Final RAP needs to be modified to read "Final RAP" and include updated information reflecting the Final EIR.
- The remedy's ultimate certification is contingent upon completion of both implementation phases, with the second phase not scheduled until 2028-2029.
- The OMA and OMP may require modification following the ultimate build-out phase.
