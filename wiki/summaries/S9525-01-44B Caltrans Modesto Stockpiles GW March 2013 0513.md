# Combined Summary of "raw/S9525-01-44B Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles GW March 2013 0513.pdf"

This document presents results of the March 18, 2013, groundwater monitoring event at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California. The report provides field measurements, analytical data from ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) and one equipment blank, and an evaluation of groundwater quality relative to regulatory thresholds.

## Key Claims
Barium, lead, and strontium are confirmed as the primary chemicals of concern (COCs) in groundwater at the site. With the exception of manganese in MW-1 and MW-7, no dissolved metals exceeded their respective numeric water quality thresholds. Nitrate (as nitrogen) and total dissolved solids (TDS) were the only general minerals exceeding primary or secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), observed in wells MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10. Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon (PAH) analysis was discontinued after November 2012 with concurrence from DTSC. The groundwater flow direction is toward the east-southeast at an average gradient of 0.0006 ft/ft, consistent with historical flow. Two distinct groundwater types are present: calcium-dominant (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-5, MW-7, MW-9, MW-10) and sodium-dominant (MW-3, MW-6, MW-8), as illustrated by Stiff diagrams. The monitoring schedule will be modified from bi-monthly to quarterly, with the next event scheduled for June 2013. Well MW-5 requires redevelopment due to consistent purging dry, high turbidity, and potential sediment clogging.

## Methodology
Field activities were conducted on March 18, 2013, and included depth-to-groundwater measurements, dissolved oxygen (DO), and oxygen-reduction potential (ORP) measurements from all ten wells. Three well volumes (2–6.5 gallons) were purged using a submersible pump or disposable bailer; wells MW-5 and MW-6 went dry during purging and were allowed to recover. Groundwater samples were collected using disposable bailers, with dissolved metals samples filtered through a 0.45-micron filter in the field.

Laboratory analyses were performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) using the following methods:
- Title 22 dissolved metals (including strontium): EPA Methods 6020 (ICP-MS) and 7470 (cold vapor AA for mercury)
- Dissolved calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium: EPA Method 6020
- Chloride, nitrate as nitrogen, sulfate: EPA Method 300.0 (ion chromatography)
- Sulfide: SM 4500-S=D
- Total dissolved solids (TDS): SM 2540C
- Total alkalinity, bicarbonate alkalinity, carbonate alkalinity: SM 2320B

Comparison thresholds included primary and secondary MCLs as promulgated by the California Department of Public Health (CDPH). Quality assurance/quality control (QA/QC) included an equipment blank, evaluation of method blank surrogate recoveries and laboratory control samples (LCS), and matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate (MS/MSD) analyses. Some MS/MSD recoveries fell outside control limits (flagged M1 or M2), but data were validated by LCS results; no data qualification was deemed necessary.

## Results
**Depth to Groundwater**: Ranged from 31.15 ft (MW-1) to 39.62 ft (MW-5) below top of casing. Groundwater flow direction is east-southeast with an average gradient of 0.0006 ft/ft.

**Primary COCs – March 2013 Concentrations**:
- Barium: 43 µg/L (MW-3) to 300 µg/L (MW-5); threshold: 1,000 µg/L (Primary MCL)/700 µg/L (Health Advisory)
- Lead: 2.2 µg/L (MW-1) to 2.5 µg/L (MW-7); threshold: 15 µg/L (Regulatory Action Level)
- Strontium: 250 µg/L (MW-8) to 1,100 µg/L (MW-5); threshold: 4,000 µg/L (Health Advisory)

**Other Dissolved Metals (all below thresholds except where noted)**:
- Arsenic: 2.0 µg/L (MW-4) to 5.2 µg/L (MW-3); threshold: 10 µg/L
- Chromium: 1.5 µg/L (MW-10) to 12 µg/L (MW-1); threshold: 50 µg/L
- Vanadium: 17 µg/L (MW-4, MW-5) to 35 µg/L (MW-7); threshold: 50 µg/L
- Manganese: 14 µg/L (MW-10) to 260 µg/L (MW-7); exceeded Secondary MCL of 50 µg/L in MW-1 and MW-7
- Mercury: Not detected in any sample (reporting limit 0.20 µg/L)

**General Minerals (exceeding thresholds)**:
- Nitrate as nitrogen: 2.7 mg/L (MW-3) to 30 mg/L (MW-5); exceeded Primary MCL (10 mg/L) in MW-1 (13 mg/L), MW-5 (30 mg/L), MW-6 (17 mg/L), and MW-10 (17 mg/L)
- TDS: 290 mg/L (MW-7) to 700 mg/L (MW-5); exceeded Secondary MCL (500 mg/L) in MW-1 (500 mg/L), MW-5 (700 mg/L), MW-6 (600 mg/L), and MW-10 (620 mg/L)

**Sulfide**: Detected in 7 of 10 samples, ranging from 0.010 mg/L (MW-8) to 0.18 mg/L (MW-1). Not detected in MW-5.

**Equipment Blank**: Chromium was detected at 0.91 µg/L in the equipment blank, but the reviewer concluded this did not significantly influence sample results since chromium concentrations were similar to historical levels.

**Cation-Anion Balance**: Calculated balances (in meq/L) showed general agreement across samples (e.g., MW-1: 8.1 vs. 8.1; MW-10: 9.3 vs. 9.8).

**Stiff Diagrams**: Graphical representations confirmed two dominant water types: calcium-dominant (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-5, MW-7, MW-9, MW-10) and sodium-dominant (MW-3, MW-6, MW-8).

## Limitations
- **Well MW-5 Performance Issue**: This well consistently purges dry before recovery and produces samples with higher turbidity. Sediment adjacent to the screen may be preventing flow, potentially impacting representativeness of samples. Redevelopment is recommended for June 2013.
- **Equipment Blank Contamination**: Low-level chromium (0.91 µg/L) was detected in the equipment blank but did not significantly affect sample interpretation.
- **MS/MSD Outliers**: Several matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate analytes had recoveries or relative percent differences outside laboratory control limits (flagged M1 or M2). These were attributed to matrix interference (e.g., barium, magnesium, sodium, strontium, zinc in batch B3C0307). Sample results were validated by LCS and no data qualification was necessary.
- **Dilution Requirements**: Many samples required dilution (flagged D6) due to high concentrations of target analytes (e.g., calcium, magnesium, sodium), which increased method detection limits. Some dilutions were also performed due to failing internal standards (flagged D5).
- **Well MW-1 Upgradient Nitrate/TDS**: MW-1 is an upgradient monitoring well; reported nitrate (13 mg/L) and TDS (500 mg/L) concentrations may be indicative of natural background levels for shallow groundwater in the vicinity of the site.
