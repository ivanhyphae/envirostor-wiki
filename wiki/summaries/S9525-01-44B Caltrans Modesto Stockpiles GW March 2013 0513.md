### Comprehensive Summary

This document details the results of groundwater monitoring conducted at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles in Stanislaus County, California, in March 2013. Samples were collected from ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) and analyzed for Title 22 dissolved metals, general minerals, PAHs, major anions, alkalinity, total dissolved solids (TDS), total sulfide, and mercury. The analytical methods employed included EPA 300.0, SM 2320B, SM 2540C, EPA 6020 (ICP-MS), and EPA 7470 (cold vapor AA).

The groundwater flow direction is east-southeast with a gradient of 0.0006. Stiff diagrams from the data indicate two distinct water types: calcium-dominant and sodium-dominant. The most significant findings show that nitrate and TDS exceeded primary and secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) in several wells, although data from the upgradient well MW-1 suggests these levels may be attributable to background conditions. Dissolved barium, lead, and strontium remained below threshold levels, while manganese exceeded its secondary MCL in MW-1 and MW-7.

Other detected metals included arsenic (up to 5.2 µg/L in MW-3 and 4.6 µg/L in MW-6), barium (up to 300 µg/L in MW-5), chromium (up to 12 µg/L in MW-1), and strontium (up to 1100 µg/L in MW-5). Mercury was not detected in any sample. The quality control assessment noted that matrix spike recoveries were outside acceptance limits for several analytes, attributed to matrix interference, and some Relative Percent Difference (RPD) values exceeded acceptance criteria. However, the equipment blank showed no significant contamination, and laboratory control sample recoveries for mercury were acceptable at 113% and 116%.
