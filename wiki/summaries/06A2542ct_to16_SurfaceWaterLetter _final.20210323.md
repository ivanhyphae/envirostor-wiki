## Key claims
- No surface water samples were collected during the December 17, 2020 inspection because the rainfall (0.04–0.05 inches) did not produce visible discharge or runoff from the site.
- Best Management Practices (BMPs) such as straw waddles and grading changes prevented any offsite discharge during the inspection.
- Construction activities (concrete abutments, regrading, excavation of a retaining wall footing, enlargement of the stormwater basin to Basin 6B) have altered or eliminated several former surface water sampling locations (PL1, PL2, PL3, PL4, PL5) and removed the former gate valve, making future sampling at those locations uncertain.

## Methodology
- **Experimental setup**: Field inspection conducted on December 17, 2020 at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (Stanislaus County, California). No sample collection occurred due to insufficient precipitation.
- **Datasets**: Rainfall data from CIMIS Modesto weather station (0.04 in on Dec 16, 0.05 in on Dec 17) and UCANR Modesto station (0.0 in on Dec 17). Visual observations recorded via photographs.
- **Evaluation metrics**: Qualitative inspection for visible ponding, discharge/runoff, and effectiveness of installed BMPs; comparison to historical sampling locations approved by DTSC and CVRWQCB.

## Results
- No quantitative chemical results are reported because no surface water samples were collected.
- Precipitation was minor, with conflicting station reports (CIMIS recorded 0.04 in and 0.05 in; UCANR recorded 0.0 in).
- No ponding or runoff was observed at background locations BG1/BG2, former PL1/PL2 (now covered by concrete abutments and straw waddles), PL3 (regraded with excavation for a retaining wall footing), or PL4/PL5 (now part of larger Basin 6B; isolated puddles present but no discharge from the pipe).
- All BMPs (straw waddles, grading) appeared to contain stormwater on‑site.

## Limitations
- The report is prepared solely for Caltrans (the Client); reliance by any third party is strictly prohibited.
- Findings reflect conditions and information available at the time of inspection (December 17, 2020) and do not account for subsequent changes.
- Stantec did not independently verify information supplied by others.
- Because no runoff occurred, the report provides no data on dissolved metals, PAHs, nitrate, sulfate, or sulfide, which were the target analytes in prior sampling events.
- Construction activities have altered the site topography, and the ability to collect future samples at the established locations is uncertain.
