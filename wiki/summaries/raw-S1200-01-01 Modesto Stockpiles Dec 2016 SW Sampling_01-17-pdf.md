## Document Identity
- **Main report:** Surface Water Sampling Report prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc., dated January 16, 2017, covering a sampling event on December 15, 2016. The report is addressed to Mr. Richard Stewart, PG, of the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch, under Project No. S1200-01-01, Contract No. 06A2184, Task Order No. 1, EA No. 10-0X2700.
- **Laboratory certificate:** The report includes a Laboratory Certificate of Analysis from ATL Global (Signal Hill, CA), dated January 9, 2017, addressed to Rebecca Silva of Geocon Consultants, Inc. Samples were prepared/analyzed between 12/17/2016 and 1/5/2017.

## Site and Scope
- **Site name and location:** Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Stanislaus County, California, south of the intersection of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue. Three stockpiles are present: Stockpile 1 (south of Kansas Ave, west of N. Emerald Ave), Stockpile 2 (south of Kansas Ave, between N. Emerald Ave and SR 99), and Stockpile 3 (south of Kansas Ave, east of SR 99).
- **Media addressed:** Surface water (stormwater).
- **Sample locations:** PL1, PL2, PL3, PL4, PL5, BG1, BG2.
  - PL1 and PL2 along N. Emerald Avenue.
  - PL3 at Bennett Street south of Stockpile 2.
  - PL4 at drainage basin outfall adjacent to Stockpile 3.
  - PL5 at gate valve within drainage basin south of Stockpile 3.
  - BG1 at drop inlet on Loletta Avenue.
  - BG2 at drop inlet on N. Emerald Avenue.

## Key Claims
- Geocon claims that stormwater runoff is retained within the Caltrans right-of-way and does not migrate offsite. This is supported by field observations during this and previous events (qualitative, not analytical).
- No stormwater runoff was observed migrating offsite during the December 15, 2016 sampling event.
- The interceptor berm at the base of the gravel-covered south side access ramp to Stockpile 2 functioned as designed, directing runoff within the stockpile boundary.
- Analytical results are generally within the same range as previously reported, except that manganese exceeded the secondary MCL at PL3, PL4, and BG1.

## Regulatory Decisions
- No formal regulatory decisions are contained in this document; it is a reporting deliverable.
- Sampling was conducted in accordance with protocols approved by DTSC as established in the Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (Shaw Environmental, January 2006) and its Addendum (February 20, 2013).

## Analytical Results
- **Dissolved metals (EPA 6020/7470A):** Antimony (ND–1.6 µg/L), Arsenic (ND–1.3 µg/L), Barium (20–170 µg/L), Beryllium (ND), Cadmium (ND), Calcium (4,300–30,000 µg/L), Chromium (0.64–2.6 µg/L), Cobalt (ND–1.7 µg/L), Copper (7.2–22 µg/L), Lead (ND–3.0 µg/L), Magnesium (870–7,700 µg/L), Manganese (14–220 µg/L), Molybdenum (1.2–7.4 µg/L), Nickel (2.0–4.8 µg/L), Potassium (2,900–47,000 µg/L), Selenium (ND), Silver (ND), Sodium (1,100–10,000 µg/L), Strontium (38–330 µg/L), Thallium (ND), Vanadium (2.6–6.5 µg/L), Zinc (ND–54 µg/L). Mercury was not detected in any sample.
- **General minerals:** Chloride (1.4–7.6 mg/L), Nitrate as N (0.21–1.9 mg/L), Sulfate (2.0–19 mg/L), Sulfide (ND–0.10 mg/L), Alkalinity as CaCO3 (22–77 mg/L), Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) (67–420 mg/L), Total Suspended Solids (TSS) (14–1,100 mg/L).
- **Thresholds exceeded:** Manganese exceeded the California secondary MCL of 50 µg/L at PL3 (220 µg/L), PL4 (53 µg/L), and BG1 (140 µg/L). All other metals and general minerals were below respective primary or secondary MCLs.
- **Laboratory certificate details (selected samples):** The ATL Global certificate provides specific concentrations for certain samples (e.g., laboratory IDs 1604687-01 through -06), including: Barium 66–70 µg/L, Calcium ~11,000 µg/L, Manganese 53–58 µg/L, TDS 420–445 mg/L, TSS 1,135–1,165 mg/L. These values are consistent with the overall ranges reported by Geocon.
- **QA/QC flags and limitations:**
  - Samples PL1-PL4, BG1, BG2 were diluted due to high concentrations of target analytes (calcium, sodium, magnesium, barium, potassium).
  - Dissolved oxygen meter was not functioning; DO not measured.
  - "J" flags indicate estimated concentrations below the PQL but ≥ MDL.
  - Laboratory QA/QC issues: Chloride and sulfate duplicate RPD values exceeded acceptance criteria. Matrix spike recoveries for total sulfide and several metals (e.g., calcium, sodium, potassium) were outside acceptance limits, though the batch was validated by the LCS. Some barium results were above the quantitation range (estimated).

## Remedial Actions
- No active remedial construction is described. Management measures include maintaining vegetative/fiber cover and straw wattles on Stockpile 2 to intercept and redirect surface water flow. The interceptor berm at the Stockpile 2 access ramp functioned as designed.
- Caltrans plans to conduct additional surface water sampling during qualifying rain events in 2017.
