The document is a groundwater monitoring report prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. for the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch. The report covers the April 30, 2015 monitoring event at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, located south of the intersection of State Route 99 and Kansas Avenue in Stanislaus County, California. Ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) are installed on the property, which is associated with the State Route 99/132 Project. The site is under oversight from the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB). The former owner of an adjacent property, FMC Corporation, is noted as the source of barium-contaminated material.

During the April 30, 2015 sampling event, wells MW‑3, MW‑5, MW‑8, MW‑9, and MW‑10 were dry and could not be sampled; MW‑6 had insufficient water. Samples were collected from MW‑1, MW‑2, MW‑4, and MW‑7. Analytical results for dissolved Title 22 metals (including barium, strontium, chromium, vanadium, arsenic, manganese, molybdenum, and nickel) were all below their respective primary MCLs, secondary MCLs, health advisories, or notification levels for that event. Barium concentrations ranged from 100 to 150 µg/L, well below the 1,000 µg/L primary MCL. However, historical data from earlier sampling events (2012–2014) showed that manganese exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L in several wells (e.g., MW‑9, MW‑10, MW‑7, MW‑4), and barium was occasionally detected at higher levels (up to 400 µg/L) in wells that were dry in 2015.

Two constituents exceeded regulatory thresholds in the April 2015 samples: nitrate as nitrogen (17 mg/L in MW‑1, and implied >10 mg/L in MW‑2) exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L, and total dissolved solids (TDS) (660 mg/L in MW‑1, 550 mg/L in MW‑4) exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L. Geocon noted that MW‑1 is upgradient and its nitrate level may reflect natural background. PAH analysis was discontinued after November 2012 with DTSC concurrence. QA/QC review showed that method blanks were clean and LCS recoveries were acceptable; some matrix spike recoveries fell outside control limits for chloride, sulfate, alkalinity, and metals such as calcium and magnesium, but the data were validated by the LCS and considered sufficient for use. Field notes indicated MW‑1 was turbid, which could affect dissolved metal results. A laboratory certificate from Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) documented dilutions and duplicate precision for the April 2015 samples.

No regulatory decisions (e.g., approvals or remedial actions) are contained in this report; it is a monitoring data summary. The only physical work described was depth-to-water measurements, well purging (1.25–4 gallons per well), and sampling. Purge water was collected in a 55‑gallon drum and transported to Inviro‑Tec Disposal in Lincoln, California on June 1, 2015. The next annual groundwater sampling event is scheduled for April 2016, with semi‑annual depth‑to‑water measurements in October 2015. Electronic data were uploaded to GeoTracker on April 8, 2014. The document serves as documentation of the ongoing, long‑term groundwater monitoring program at the site.
