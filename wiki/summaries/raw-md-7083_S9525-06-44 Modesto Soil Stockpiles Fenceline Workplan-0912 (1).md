## Summary of "raw-md/7083_S9525-06-44 Modesto Soil Stockpiles Fenceline Workplan.0912 (1).md"

### Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan (including a Health and Safety Plan and analytical data appendices) to address data gaps (fenceline/perimeter sampling and cadmium impacts) needed to finalize the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles. The workplan presents the sampling scope, supporting analytical data from 2004 and 2012, and site-specific health and safety procedures.
- **Author or preparer:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG; Rebecca L. Silva; Bob Kimball; Douglas S. Krause, CIH). Shaw Environmental, Inc. prepared the 2009 Preliminary Endangerment Assessment (PEA) and some data tables. Analytical laboratory: Advanced Technology Laboratories.
- **Date:** Workplan dated September 18, 2012. Health and Safety Plan (HSP) effective April 2012. Analytical data covers samples collected in February 2004, May 2004, and June–July 2012.

### Key claims
- The stockpiles (~121,000 yd³) contain barium-impacted fill from former Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC) evaporation ponds.
- A 2009 DTSC letter concluded the stockpiles, as currently managed, pose no human health risk to Caltrans workers, trespassers, or adjacent residents.
- A 2007 Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) concluded no unacceptable risk to future users provided institutional controls are maintained.
- Negative Exposure Assessments (within 12 months) document that asbestos exposures are consistently below 0.1 f/cc (PEL-TWA) and airborne lead below 30 µg/m³ (Action Level), representing low exposure risk for other toxic metals during sampling.

### Regulatory decisions
- **DTSC (April 8, 2005):** Recommended groundwater evaluation and a Preliminary Endangerment Assessment (PEA).
- **DTSC (December 17, 2009):** Concurred with PEA findings; directed continued stockpile management and groundwater monitoring.
- **DTSC (March 1, 2012):** Required quarterly groundwater monitoring, well inspection/repair, and addition of strontium to the analyte list.
- **HSP:** Approved by Certified Industrial Hygienist Douglas S. Krause, CIH, and Geocon Project Manager in April 2012. Regulated areas for lead sampling are not required under the plan.

### Analytical results
- **2004 PSI:** Elevated barium (above commercial/industrial CHHSL) in stockpiles; elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3. Barium total concentrations reached up to 187,000 mg/kg (Stockpile 2), exceeding the California TTLC of 10,000 mg/kg. Barium soluble (WET) in Stockpile 3 reached 5,940 mg/l, exceeding the STLC (100 mg/l) and the federal RCRA TCLP limit (100 mg/l). TCLP barium concentrations up to 1,230 mg/l were also recorded.
- **2012 Sampling:** Total barium in soil at 300 mg/kg; other metals (antimony, arsenic, chromium, cobalt, copper, lead, nickel, vanadium, mercury) detected at low levels. STLC (DI) metals: barium at 0.05 mg/L (J-flagged), strontium at 0.25 mg/L.
- **Proposed analyses (workplan):** Title 22 metals + strontium (Fenceline Borings); barium (Perimeter Borings); barium and cadmium (Cadmium Borings) via EPA Method 6010B/7471.
- **Background/native soil:** Barium concentrations ranged from 52–134 mg/kg.

### Remedial actions
- **Proposed scope:** 29 fenceline borings (5 ft depth), 22 perimeter borings (3 ft depth), and 5 cadmium borings (20 ft depth) using a Geoprobe direct-push rig. Scheduled for completion by September 28, 2012. This is a pre-remedial investigation phase; soil and groundwater sampling tasks.
- **Institutional controls (required):** Maintain land use covenant, soil management plan, fence maintenance, current grade and vegetative cover, and restricted access to Caltrans authorized personnel.

### Responsible parties
- **Owner/operator:** California Department of Transportation (Caltrans).
- **Regulatory oversight:** Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).
- **Co-regulator:** Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB).
- **Preparer/consultant:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (HSP and workplan); Shaw Environmental, Inc. (2009 PEA and data tables).

### Key terms and conditions
- **Background contamination source:** FMC Corporation operations (1930s–1970s) and former Barium Products Ltd. operations. Barium and strontium sulfate are the primary chemicals of potential concern.
- **Stockpile locations:** Stockpile 1 (west of Emerald Ave), Stockpile 2 (between Emerald Ave and SR99), Stockpile 3 (east of SR99).
- **Construction timeline:** SR132 Project first phase scheduled to start in 2015.
- **HSP protocols:** Level D PPE (hard hat, safety glasses, hearing protection, ANSI vest); Level C (APR with P100 filters) if dust concerns arise. Buddy system required. Hand-wash facilities with clean water must be provided; eating, drinking, smoking prohibited in potentially impacted areas.
- **Emergency medical facility:** Doctor's Medical Center, 1441 Florida Ave, Modesto, CA 95350.
- **HSP expiration:** 6 months from CIH approval date (April 2012), per T8 CCR §1532.1(e)(2)(E). Pre-job conference required.
- **Data reporting:** Results below the Practical Quantitation Limit (PQL) are flagged as estimated values (J-flagged). For mean calculations, non-detects are set to half the detection limit when >50% of samples have detectable concentrations.
