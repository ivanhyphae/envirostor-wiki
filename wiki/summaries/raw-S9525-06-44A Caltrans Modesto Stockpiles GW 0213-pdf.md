This document is a **Groundwater Monitoring Report** prepared by **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** for the **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6** (Project No. S9525-06-44A, Task Order 44). It reports on a groundwater sampling event conducted in **January 2013** at the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (SR 132)** in Stanislaus County, California. The monitoring network includes wells **MW-1 through MW-10**.

**Key findings** include:
- **Barium and strontium** concentrations remained similar to historical levels and below regulatory thresholds. Barium ranged from 55–310 µg/L (MCL of 1,000 µg/L) and strontium from 260–1,200 µg/L (EPA health advisory of 4,000 µg/L).
- **Lead** was not detected above the practical quantitation limit (PQL) of 1.0 µg/L in any sample.
- **Nitrate as N** exceeded the MCL (10 mg/L) in wells MW-1 (12 mg/L), MW-5 (up to 30 mg/L), MW-6 (18 mg/L), and MW-10. **Total dissolved solids (TDS)** exceeded the secondary MCL (500 mg/L) in MW-5 (680 mg/L), MW-6 (600 mg/L), and MW-10. The report notes that nitrate in upgradient well MW-1 may represent background conditions.
- **Manganese** in MW-6 (100 µg/L) exceeded its secondary MCL (50 µg/L).
- Historical data (2006–2013) show prior exceedances of the barium MCL in **MW-4** (up to 1,300 µg/L), though the January 2013 result for MW-4 was 100 µg/L.
- **Stiff diagrams** classify most wells (MW-3, MW-5 through MW-9) as calcium-dominant, while MW-4 and MW-10 are sodium-dominant.

**Laboratory analyses** were performed by **ATL Global** (Signal Hill, CA) for dissolved metals (ICP-MS EPA 6020), mercury (EPA 7470), anions (EPA 300.0), alkalinity, and TDS. Several quality control flags were noted, including matrix spike recovery exceedances for sulfate, chloride, and some metals, and blank detections for calcium and zinc; however, the batches were validated by laboratory control samples.

**Regulatory oversight** is provided by the **California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)** and the **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)**. The report does not describe any remedial actions; it is a monitoring-only report. The document concludes that no primary MCL exceedances occurred for the target barium/strontium list in the January 2013 event, but that exceedances for nitrate, TDS, and manganese are noted in specific wells.
