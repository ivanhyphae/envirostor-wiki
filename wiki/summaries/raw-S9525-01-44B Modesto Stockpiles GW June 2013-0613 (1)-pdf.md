## Combined Summary of Groundwater Monitoring Report  
**Source Document:** "raw/S9525-01-44B Modesto Stockpiles GW June 2013.0613 (1).pdf"  

### Document Identity  
The document is a **groundwater monitoring report** prepared by **Geocon Consultants, Inc.**, for the **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch**. Dated **June 27, 2013**, it covers a sampling event on **June 5–6, 2013**, under **Project No. S9525-01-44B, Task Order No. 44, Contract No. 06A1580**. Additional supporting materials include laboratory certificates from **Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL Global)** and **Stiff diagrams** illustrating groundwater geochemistry.

### Site and Scope  
The site is the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles** in **Stanislaus County, California**, located south of the SR 99 and Kansas Avenue intersection (also referenced as SR 132/SR 99). The investigation focuses on **groundwater quality** beneath three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) that contain barium-contaminated fill from SR 99 construction dating to approximately 1961. **Ten monitoring wells (MW‑1 through MW‑10)** are used for assessment; MW‑9 and MW‑10 were installed in 2012. Well construction details (PVC casing, screen intervals ~29–45 ft bgs, sand filter pack) are provided.

### Key Findings and Claims  
Geocon’s analysis yields the following principal interpretations:  
- **No dissolved metals** exceeded numeric water quality thresholds.  
- **Barium and strontium** concentrations remained similar to historical levels and below applicable limits.  
- **Nitrate** (as N) exceeded the **primary MCL of 10 mg/L** in wells **MW‑1, MW‑5, MW‑6, MW‑9, and MW‑10** (maximum 30 mg/L at MW‑5).  
- **Total dissolved solids (TDS)** exceeded the **secondary MCL of 500 mg/L** in wells **MW‑1, MW‑5, MW‑6, and MW‑10** (maximum 730 mg/L at MW‑5).  
- **Groundwater flow** direction is **east-southeast** with a gradient of approximately **0.0005**, based on depth‑to‑water measurements.
