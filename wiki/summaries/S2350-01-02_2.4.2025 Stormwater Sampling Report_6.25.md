The report documents stormwater/surface-water sampling and laboratory QC for the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles project in Modesto, California, conducted under Caltrans Contract No. 06A2767, Task Order No. 2. Geocon sampled stormwater on February 4, 2025, following the approved Storm Water Sampling and Analysis Plan and DTSC/CVRWQCB protocols. Sampling focused on SW-East and SW-West; BG-West was dry and could not be sampled. The report is part of an ongoing monitoring program and also references earlier sampling events from 2023 and 2024 for comparison.

Field collection used disposable bailers/pressure bailer with suction pump, with dissolved metals field-filtered through 0.45-micron filters. Samples were documented under chain-of-custody and transported chilled to ASSET Laboratories. Analyses included total dissolved solids (SM 2540C / Method 160.1), sulfate (EPA 300.0 / 9056), dissolved mercury (EPA 7470A), and dissolved Title 22 metals by EPA 3010A/6010B and related methods. Laboratory QC included method blanks, LCS/LCSD, MS/MSD, and duplicates.

For the February 2025 event, rainfall was about 0.52 inch. No runoff migration beyond the Caltrans right-of-way was observed. Water quality results were generally low: TDS was 210 mg/L at SW-East and 180 mg/L at SW-West; sulfate was 21 mg/L and 14 mg/L, respectively. Mercury was non-detect in both samples. Detected metals included barium, copper, zinc, molybdenum, vanadium, and strontium, with barium, copper, and zinc remaining below applicable MCLs; molybdenum, vanadium, and strontium were detected at low levels but do not have established MCLs. The report states that TDS and sulfate did not exceed Secondary MCLs and that most measured constituents were below drinking-water screening levels.

The QC appendix shows generally acceptable analytical performance for sulfate, metals, and mercury methods, with blanks mostly non-detect and LCS/MS/MSD recoveries largely within limits. A few QC anomalies were flagged, including some duplicate/RPD issues and recoveries outside acceptance limits for selected analytes, but these were limited and did not change the overall conclusion that the data are usable for site monitoring.
