## Combined Summary

A stormwater sampling event was conducted on February 4, 2025, at a Caltrans encapsulated soil stockpile site in Modesto, California, to assess water quality. Surface water grab samples were collected from three locations (SW-East, SW-West, and BG-West) and analyzed for total dissolved solids (TDS), sulfate, and dissolved Title 22 metals. Analytical methods included SM2540C (TDS), EPA 300.0 (sulfate), EPA 3010A/6010B (metals via ICP), and EPA 7470A (mercury via cold vapor).

### Key Findings

- **TDS and Sulfate:** Concentrations at all sampled locations were below secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) of 500 mg/L and 250 mg/L, respectively. For example, TDS ranged from 42 to 210 mg/L and sulfate from <2.0 to 38 mg/L across sampling events.
- **Dissolved Metals (February 2025):** Barium (90–390 µg/L), copper (14–16 µg/L), and zinc (39–55 µg/L) remained below both Primary and Secondary MCLs. However, previous sampling events (2023–2024) had shown exceedances for barium, strontium, copper, and zinc.
- **Thallium:** A thallium exceedance of the Primary MCL was noted in a prior December 2023 event, but no such exceedance was reported for the February 2025 sampling.
- **Runoff:** No runoff migration from the Caltrans right-of-way was observed during the event.

### Quality Control

- **Method Blanks and Laboratory Control Samples (LCS):** Method blanks showed no detection for sulfate or metals, and LCS recoveries met acceptance criteria (85–115%).
- **Matrix Spike (MS) and Duplicate (MSD):** Recoveries were generally within limits, but relative percent differences (RPDs) for molybdenum (28.1%) and silver (25.7%) exceeded the 20% control limit, resulting in "R" qualifier flags.
- **Strontium and Mercury:** QC results for strontium (EPA 6010B) and mercury (EPA 7470A) were within acceptable accuracy and precision limits, validating method performance for these analytes.

### Limitations

Some analytical results were flagged as estimated (J, J+) due to concentrations between detection and quantitation limits, or as estimated non-detects (UJ). Additionally, QC qualifiers indicate that RPDs for molybdenum and silver fell outside recovery limits.
