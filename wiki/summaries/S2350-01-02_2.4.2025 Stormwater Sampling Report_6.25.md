## Key claims
- Storm water samples collected on February 4, 2025, from two locations (SW-East and SW-West) at Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles in Modesto, California.
- All measured total dissolved solids (TDS) and sulfate concentrations were below Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for drinking water.
- Dissolved barium, copper, and zinc were detected but remained below their respective Primary or Secondary MCLs.
- Molybdenum, vanadium, and strontium were detected but have no established MCLs.
- No discharge or runoff migration was observed beyond the Caltrans right-of-way during the sampling event.
- The background location (BG-West) was dry and could not be sampled.

## Methodology
- Sampling conducted on February 4, 2025, following the Storm Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP) approved by DTSC and CVRWQCB.
- Two storm water sample locations (SW-East at the discharge point north of SR 132 west of SR 99; SW-West at Ponding Basin 5 east of N. Carpenter Road). Background location (BG-West) was dry.
- Total rainfall for the event: approximately 0.52 inch.
- Samples collected using a pressure bailer with suction pump. For dissolved metals analysis, samples were field-filtered through 0.45-micron filters.
- Laboratory analyses performed by ASSET Laboratories (California-certified) under chain-of-custody:
  - Title 22 dissolved metals (including barium and strontium) by EPA Method 6020/7470.
  - Sulfate by EPA Method 9056/300.0.
  - Total dissolved solids by EPA Method 160.1.
- QA/QC procedures included method blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), LCS duplicates (LCSD), matrix spikes (MS), and matrix spike duplicates (MSD).

## Results
- **TDS**: SW-East = 210 mg/L; SW-West = 180 mg/L (Secondary MCL = 500 mg/L).
- **Sulfate**: SW-East = 21 mg/L; SW-West = 14 mg/L (Secondary MCL = 250 mg/L).
- **Dissolved metals (µg/L)**:
  - Barium: SW-East = 390; SW-West = 90 (MCL = 1,000 µg/L).
  - Copper: SW-East = 16; SW-West = 14 (Secondary MCL = 1,000 µg/L).
  - Zinc: SW-East = 55; SW-West = 39 (Secondary MCL = 5,000 µg/L).
  - Molybdenum: SW-East = 16; SW-West = 10 (no MCL).
  - Vanadium: SW-East = 6.1; SW-West = 8.2 (no MCL).
  - Strontium: SW-East = 170; SW-West = 190 (no MCL).
  - All other Title 22 metals (antimony, arsenic, beryllium, cadmium, chromium, cobalt, lead, mercury, nickel, selenium, silver, thallium) were below detection limits or reporting limits.
- Laboratory QC: Method blanks acceptable; LCS/LCSD recoveries within limits. RPD for silver in MSD and for molybdenum in DUP exceeded acceptance limits, but the LCS validated the analytical batch.

## Limitations
- Background sample (BG-West) could not be collected due to dry conditions, limiting comparison to ambient water quality.
- QC issues noted: RPD outside accepted recovery limits for silver in matrix spike duplicate and for molybdenum in sample duplicate; however, laboratory control samples validated the batch.
- Strontium, molybdenum, and vanadium lack established MCLs, making risk interpretation incomplete.
- Sampling is limited to a single rain event (0.52 inch) and may not represent all weather conditions.
- Historical thallium exceedances (from December 2023) were not observed in this event, indicating temporal variability.
