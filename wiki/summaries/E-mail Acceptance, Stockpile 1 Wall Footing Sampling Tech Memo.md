## Key claims
- The technical memorandum characterizes soil excavated from the mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall footing area associated with Stockpile 1.
- Based on sampling and laboratory analysis, the authors seek to classify whether the excavated wall footing soil can be reused in Stockpiles 1 and 2 as embankment fill and/or clean cover material.
- DTSC and the Water Board accepted the memo, but with an important restriction: the material cannot be used as Stockpiles 1 and 2 capping material.
- Because the soil’s 95% Upper Confidence Limit (UCL) exceeds the maximum background threshold of 120 mg/kg and the wall footing area lies outside the designated borrow area in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan, the material must be placed within the Stockpiles 1 and 2 fill containment zones or used as general bridge embankment fill.

## Methodology
- Field sampling was conducted along the MSE wall footing excavation area.
- Laboratory analysis was performed on the collected soil samples to characterize chemical concentrations.
- The key statistical decision metric reported was the 95% Upper Confidence Limit (UCL) for the sampled soil concentration, compared against a maximum background criterion of 120 mg/kg.
- The memo’s purpose was to support material classification for reuse in Stockpiles 1 and 2, specifically for embankment fill and/or clean cover material.

## Results
- The 95% UCL of the sampled soil was above the maximum background concentration of 120 mg/kg.
- No additional numeric sample concentration values are provided in the source text beyond the 120 mg/kg background threshold.
- Approval outcome:
  - DTSC officially accepted the memo on April 7, 2020.
  - Acceptance was conditional on excluding the material from use as Stockpiles 1 and 2 capping material.
- Permitted disposition:
  - Place material within Stockpiles 1 and 2 fill containment zones, or
  - Use as general bridge embankment fill.

## Limitations
- The source text is an approval email chain rather than the full technical memorandum, so detailed sampling design, analyte list, and full laboratory dataset are not included.
- The reuse recommendation is limited by the exceedance of the 95% UCL above the 120 mg/kg background threshold.
- The material is also constrained by project-specific land-use/designation boundaries: the wall footing area is outside the designated borrow area in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan.
- The sample data discussed are for a specific wall footing excavation area and may not generalize beyond that location.
