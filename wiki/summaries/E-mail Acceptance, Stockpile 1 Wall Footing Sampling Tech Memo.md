## Key claims
- The Technical Memorandum (Memo) dated April 6, 2020 describes the sampling and laboratory analysis of soil along the mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall footing excavation area for Stockpile 1.
- DTSC and the Water Board accept the Memo with one exception: the MSE wall footing soil **cannot be used as capping material** for Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- The 95% Upper Confidence Limit (UCL) of the sampled soil exceeds the maximum background concentration of 120 mg/kg, and the footing area lies outside the designated borrow area in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan.
- Therefore, the material must be placed within the Stockpile 1 and 2 fill containment zones or used as general bridge embankment fill.

## Methodology
- **Experimental setup / sampling**: Soil characterization was performed on excavated material from the MSE wall footing area associated with Stockpile 1. Sampling followed the technical memo protocol (details not fully provided in the email chain).
- **Laboratory analysis**: Soil samples were analyzed for relevant contaminants (likely metals or other constituents; not specified in the email).
- **Evaluation metrics**: The primary metric is the **95% UCL** of the contaminant concentration compared to the **maximum background threshold** of 120 mg/kg. The decision also considers whether the sample area falls within the designated borrow area.

## Results
- The **95% UCL** of the sampled soil is **above 120 mg/kg** (the maximum background level).
- The MSE wall footing area is **outside the designated borrow area** defined in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan.
- Consequently, the material is **not approved for use as capping material** but is **acceptable as fill within Stockpile 1 and 2 containment zones or as general bridge embankment fill**.
