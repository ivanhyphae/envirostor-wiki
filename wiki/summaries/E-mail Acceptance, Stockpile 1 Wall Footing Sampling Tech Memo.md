## Key claims
- The Technical Memorandum (April 6, 2020) characterized soil from the mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall footing excavation area for potential use as capping material for Stockpiles 1 and 2.  
- DTSC and the Water Board accept the Memo except for one provision: the soil cannot be used as capping material because the 95% Upper Confidence Limit (UCL) of arsenic in the sampled soil exceeds the maximum background concentration of 120 mg/kg, and the excavation area falls outside the designated borrow area in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan.  
- Accordingly, the material must be placed within the Stockpile 1 and 2 fill containment zones or used as general bridge embankment fill, not as clean cover/capping material.

## Methodology
- Soil samples were collected along the MSE wall footing excavation area.  
- Laboratory analysis was performed on the samples.  
- The 95% Upper Confidence Limit (UCL) of arsenic concentrations was computed and compared to the established maximum background arsenic level of 120 mg/kg.  
- The material’s location relative to the designated borrow area defined in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan was also assessed.

## Results
- The 95% UCL of arsenic in the sampled soil is above the maximum background concentration of 120 mg/kg.  
- No other numerical results (e.g., sample count, individual concentrations) are provided in the email.

## Limitations
- The Memo’s original recommendation to use the MSE wall footing soil as capping material for Stockpiles 1 and 2 was rejected due to the arsenic exceedance and the area lying outside the designated borrow area.  
- The email does not discuss other potential limitations such as sample size, spatial coverage, or statistical assumptions behind the 95% UCL calculation.
