## Key claims
- The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Water Board formally accepted the MSE Wall Sampling Plan submitted via email on March 31, 2020.
- The plan proposes soil sampling of Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE wall footing excavations to characterize and classify soils for on-site reuse options (clean capping material, abutment/embankment fill, or BCS containment zones).
- Sampling will target 10 locations at ~200-foot intervals along the midpoint of the planned wall footing, collecting discrete samples from 1-foot intervals to the excavation depth (~4–5 feet).

## Methodology
- **Experimental setup**: Soil sampling will occur after removal of the BCS slope zone on Stockpile 1 southern slope. Borings or trenches will be collected at 10 locations spaced approximately 200 feet apart along the midpoint of the MSE wall footing excavation.
- **Sampling procedure**: Discrete soil samples from 1-foot intervals down to the planned excavation depth (~4–5 feet).
- **Analytes**: Barium and lead.
- **Analytical turnaround**: Expedited 48-hour turnaround time.
- **Evaluation metrics**: Analytical results compared to site-specific background levels and regulatory screening levels (RDIP thresholds, Environmental Screening Levels [ESLs] for construction worker, residential, and commercial scenarios).
- **Regulatory framework**: RDIP (Removal Design Implementation Plan) BCS removal threshold for groundwater protection, DTSC residential standards, and California ESLs.

## Results
- **No quantitative results reported** – the document is a sampling plan acceptance, not a data report.
- Relevant screening levels provided for comparison:
  - **Barium**: maximum site-specific background = 120 mg/kg; RDIP BCS removal threshold (groundwater protection) = 1,000 mg/kg; construction worker ESL = 3,000 mg/kg; residential ESL = 15,000 mg/kg; commercial ESL = 220,000 mg/kg.
  - **Lead**: maximum site-specific background = 3.8 mg/kg; DTSC residential standard = 80 mg/kg.

## Limitations
- No limitations or caveats are explicitly discussed in the email chain; the plan was accepted without comment.
