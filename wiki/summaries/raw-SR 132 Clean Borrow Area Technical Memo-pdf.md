## Document identity
- **Document type:** Technical Memorandum (Clean Cap Borrow Area Characterization Testing)
- **Preparer / author organization:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG, Project Manager)
- **Recipient / addressee agency or party:** Rick Demi, PE, Construction Manager, WSP/City of Modesto
- **Date or reporting period covered:** February 27, 2020 (fieldwork on December 21, 2020)
- **Project number or task order (if stated):** Geocon Project No. S1908-01-01

## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Proposed clean cap borrow area adjacent and easterly of Carpenter Road within the State Route 132 right-of-way, Modesto, California. The borrow area is approximately 1,250 ft long, 200 ft wide, with an excavation depth of 5 ft.
- **Media addressed:** Soil (clean cover fill material).
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:**
    - Direct-push borings B1 through B12, located on a grid pattern within the proposed borrow area.
    - BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2 (located easterly of the proposed borrow area), which are the target for the clean cap.

## Key claims
- **The claim:** "The reported metal concentrations in the composite samples are consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations. Based on the data presented herein, the proposed clean cap borrow area appears to be suitable to generate clean cover fill materials for capping BCS Stockpiles 1 and 2."
- **Who is making it:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (consultant for project).
- **Whether it is supported by data in this document, conditional, or contested:** Supported. The claim is based on direct analytical results from composite soil samples compared against site-specific background concentrations. The analytical laboratory report is attached to the memo. The claim is made in anticipation of inclusion in a future Removal Action Completion Report (RACR).

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type:** No formal agency decision is documented within this memo. The sampling strategy was *approved* by Mr. Dean Wright with DTSC.
- **Deciding agency and date:** DTSC (Dean Wright); date not specified.
- **Conditions or requirements imposed:** The sampling strategy was developed "in general accordance with the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) 2001 Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Soil".
- **Documents approved or acted on:** The soil sampling strategy (not a standalone document) was approved.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units, thresholds:**
    - **Media:** Soil (composite samples from 0-2 ft and 2-5 ft intervals).
    - **Analytes:** Title 22 metals and Organochlorine Pesticides (OCPs).
    - **Concentrations (mg/kg) and comparison to site-specific background (in parentheses):**
        - Arsenic: 1.0 – 1.5 (background 1.2)
        - Barium: 60 – 110 (background 72.8)
        - Beryllium: 1.3 – 1.6 (background 0.2)
        - Chromium: 7.5 – 11 (background 7.1)
        - Cobalt: 3.2 – 4.9 (background 4.4)
        - Copper: 5.3 – 9.9 (background 7.5)
        - Lead: 1.7 – 7.0 (background 2.0)
        - Nickel: 5.6 – 8.2 (background 5.3)
        - Silver: 1.6 – 2.5 (background 0.2)
        - Vanadium: 16 – 25 (background 31.3)
        - Zinc: 21 – 41 (background 26.3)
    - **Non-detects:** Antimony, cadmium, selenium, thallium, mercury, and all OCPs (including 4,4'-DDD, 4,4'-DDE, 4,4'-DDT, Aldrin, Chlordane, etc.) were not detected above reporting limits.
    - **Applicable thresholds:** Site-specific background concentrations (from a 2007 Shaw background data summary).
- **Which wells or locations exceeded thresholds and by how much:** None. All reported results were stated to be "consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations." No thresholds were exceeded.
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations affecting interpretation:** The analytical report notes one quality control issue: "M1 - Matrix spike recovery outside of acceptance limit. The analytical batch was validated by the laboratory control sample." This limitation applies to a specific mercury post-spike analysis in batch B0B0410. All other QC data (blanks, LCS, duplicates) were within acceptable limits.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** Soil characterization for a proposed clean cap borrow area.
- **Quantities:** 12 direct-push borings to a depth of 5 ft. 8 composite soil samples generated (4 from 0-2 ft, 4 from 2-5 ft).
- **Verification sampling outcomes:** Metal concentrations in all composite samples are consistent with natural background. All OCPs and several metals were not detected.
- **Phase status:** This is a characterization study for a **planned** borrow area. The findings are to be included in the **future** Removal Action Completion Report (RACR) for stockpile capping activities.
