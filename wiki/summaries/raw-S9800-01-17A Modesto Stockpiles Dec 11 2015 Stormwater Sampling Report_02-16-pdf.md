## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Site), located south of the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue in Stanislaus County, California. Three stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) are present. The site was generated during construction of SR 99 around 1961 from soil excavated from the former Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC) property.
- **Media addressed:** Surface water (stormwater)
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** Sample locations PL1 (west side of North Emerald Avenue between Stockpiles 1 and 2), PL2 (east side of North Emerald Avenue between Stockpiles 1 and 2), PL3 (south of Stockpile 2 at Bennett Street/Loletta Avenue, outside Caltrans right-of-way), PL4 (drainage basin outfall adjacent to Stockpile 3). Locations PL5, BG1, and BG2 were sampled but were dry on December 11, 2015.

## Key claims
- **Claim:** No dissolved metals concentrations exceeded applicable primary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs). Manganese concentrations at PL2, PL3, and PL4 exceeded secondary (taste/odor/welfare-based) MCLs. Beryllium, cadmium, mercury, selenium, silver, and thallium were not detected at concentrations ≥ practical quantitation limits (PQLs).
    - **Who is making it:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (the consultant/preparer).
    - **Supported by data:** Yes. Analytical results in Table 2 (ATL laboratory report, Appendix A) support the claim.
- **Claim:** The analytical results are within the same general range of concentrations as previously reported at each sample location.
    - **Who is making it:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
    - **Supported by data:** Yes, by comparison with historical results presented in Tables 2 and 3.
- **Claim:** No surface water migration beyond the Caltrans right-of-way was observed during the December 11, 2015 event.
    - **Who is making it:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
    - **Supported by data:** Implicitly supported (field observations; no contrary data presented). The claim is consistent with prior events.

- **General minerals (Table 3, mg/L unless noted):**
    - No primary or secondary MCL exceedances reported.
    - Noteworthy concentrations: Sulfide (0.044 to 0.066 mg/L), TDS (70 to 120 mg/L), TSS (22 to 190 mg/L), chloride (5.4 to 11 mg/L), sulfate (3.2 to 11 mg/L), nitrate as N (0.28 to 0.48 mg/L, except PL3 where N.D.).
- **QA/QC flags/data quality limitations:**
    - Laboratory notes: Initial analysis for nitrate as nitrogen was within holding time; reanalysis was past holding time.
    - Matrix spike recoveries for several metals in samples PL1–PL4 were outside acceptance limits due to possible matrix interference. The laboratory stated the "analytical batch was validated by the laboratory control sample."
    - Several dissolved metals results required dilution due to high target analyte concentrations (D6 flag) or failing internal standards (D5 flag).
    - RPD values outside acceptance criteria noted for some duplicate analyses (e.g., Barium in DUP2, Manganese, Molybdenum, Nickel – flagged as R).
    - Matrix spike recoveries for some analytes outside acceptance range (e.g., Calcium, Manganese, Zinc – flagged M1/M2). Laboratory notes attribute outliers to matrix interference and note batch validation by LCS.
