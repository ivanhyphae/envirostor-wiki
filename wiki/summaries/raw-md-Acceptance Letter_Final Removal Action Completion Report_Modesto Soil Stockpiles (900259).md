## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose**: Acceptance letter from DTSC to Geocon Consultants, Inc., approving the Interim Removal Action Completion Report (RACR) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Phase 1 Interim State Route 132 West Project. The letter also outlines next steps (O&M Agreement, Land Use Covenant) and describes the transition to Ultimate Phase 2.
- **Author or preparer**: Steven Becker, P.G., Chief, Santa Susana Field Laboratory Site Mitigation and Restoration Program, Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).
- **Date or period covered**: January 18, 2023 (letter date). RACR submitted December 1, 2022. Phase 1 work completed per earlier approvals (RAP, RDIP, variance requests dated April 17, 2020 and September 21, 2020). Ultimate Phase 2 anticipated to begin in 8–10 years.

## Key claims
- The RACR describes consolidation and capping of barium‑containing soils (BCS) that were completed as part of the Interim Phase 1 construction effort.
- Soil removal, consolidation, and capping were performed in accordance with the approved Remedial Action Plan (RAP), Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP), and two variance requests (April 17, 2020 and September 21, 2020).
- The As‑Built Plans, cross‑section figures, final survey data, and final Operation and Maintenance (O&M) Plan were included in the final RACR.
- DTSC has no comments and accepts the RACR as final.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Action**: DTSC acceptance of the RACR (formal approval of the interim removal action completion).
- **Agency**: Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in consultation with Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB).
- **Date**: January 18, 2023.
- **Conditions imposed**:
  - DTSC will prepare a draft O&M Agreement within 30 days of the letter and submit it for Caltrans review.
  - A Land Use Covenant (LUC) for the remediated area will be completed and recorded with the Stanislaus County Assessor’s office.
  - Final certification of the RAP for the project will not occur until after Ultimate Phase 2 (State Route 132 build‑out) is completed.
  - Ultimate Phase 2 will require a separate agreement between DTSC and Caltrans, a supplemental RDIP, field oversight, a Phase 2 RACR, and possible revisions to the O&M Plan and O&M Agreement.

## Analytical results
- **No analytical data presented in this letter.** The document does not include contaminant concentrations, media tested, thresholds, or exceedances. (Analytical results presumably contained in the approved RACR, but not restated here.)

## Remedial actions
- **Activity**: Consolidation and capping of barium‑containing soils (BCS) as part of the Interim Phase 1 effort.
- **Quantities**: Not specified in the letter.
- **Phase status**: Interim Phase 1 completed. Ultimate Phase 2 anticipated in 8–10 years (will include adding lanes and final build‑out of State Route 132).
- **Verification outcomes**: As‑Built Plans, cross‑section figures, and final survey data were included in the RACR; DTSC accepted the work as compliant with approved plans.

## Key terms and conditions
- DTSC will prepare a **draft O&M Agreement** within 30 days of January 18, 2023 (i.e., by approximately February 17, 2023).
- A **Land Use Covenant (LUC)** for the remediated area will be completed, signed, and recorded with the Stanislaus County Assessor’s office.
- The acceptance letter and completion of the O&M Agreement/LUC mark the **conclusion of Interim Phase 1**.
- **Final certification** of the RAP will occur only after **Ultimate Phase 2** is completed.
- Ultimate Phase 2 is anticipated to begin in **8 to 10 years** and includes adding lanes and final build‑out of SR 132.
- Ultimate Phase 2 requires a **separate agreement** between DTSC and Caltrans, a **supplemental RDIP**, field oversight, an **Ultimate Phase 2 RACR**, and possible **revisions to the O&M Plan and O&M Agreement**.
