The source document is a laboratory analytical report (ATL Global, Project S9525-06-44) for groundwater samples collected from the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in May 2012. Samples were taken from eight monitoring wells (MW‑1 to MW‑8) plus surface samples, with depth to groundwater measured and wells purged (1–7 gal) before collection. Dissolved metals were filtered (0.45 µm) and analyzed via EPA 6020 (ICP‑MS), mercury via EPA 7470 (cold‑vapor AA), semivolatile organic compounds via EPA 8270/SIM, anions via EPA 300.0, and total dissolved solids/alkalinity via SM 2540C/2320B. Quality control included method blanks, LCS, matrix spikes, and duplicates.

Key results include: no PAHs detected above PQLs; dissolved metals generally below water‑quality thresholds (barium 55–310 µg/L, strontium 270–1,400 µg/L, arsenic 2.1–3.9 µg/L, chromium 1.6–12 µg/L, vanadium 14–32 µg/L); manganese (35 µg/L) detected only in MW‑1 but exceeded the secondary MCL in MW‑4. Nitrate exceeded the primary MCL (10 mg/L) in MW‑1 (12 mg/L), MW‑4 (10 mg/L), MW‑5 (26 mg/L), and MW‑6 (18 mg/L). TDS exceeded the secondary MCL in MW‑4, MW‑5, and MW‑6 (up to 690 mg/L). Major cations (calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium) were present at high levels (e.g., total calcium up to 110,000 µg/L in MW‑5). Groundwater flow was southeast at a gradient of ~0.0006.

Limitations include matrix‑spike recoveries for calcium, magnesium, potassium, and sodium that frequently exceeded control limits (70–130%) due to high native concentrations, requiring sample dilutions (noted as D6). Some duplicate RPD values exceeded 20% for certain metals. Surrogate recoveries for SVOCs (e.g., 1,2‑dichlorobenzene‑d4 at 115%) slightly exceeded acceptance limits (36–107%). Overall, the report provides routine groundwater chemistry data with acceptable performance for most analytes, but matrix effects for major cations and minor analytical outliers should be considered.
