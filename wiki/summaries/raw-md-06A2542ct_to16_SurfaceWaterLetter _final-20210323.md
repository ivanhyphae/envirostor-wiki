Here is a structured summary of the document, organized for a remediation project case-file wiki.

## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Surface Water Report / Inspection Summary. Documents the results of a periodic surface water discharge inspection at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, conducted in lieu of sampling because no qualifying runoff occurred.
- **Author or preparer:** Stantec Consulting Services Inc. (Paul Stoppelmann, PG, CEG; Ralph Carson, PG; Kevin Miskin, PE).
- **Date or period covered:** Inspection conducted December 17, 2020. Report dated March 24, 2021.

## Key claims
- **No surface water discharge occurred.** Stantec asserts that due to limited rainfall (0.04–0.05 inches recorded), no surface water runoff or discharge was observed from the Site or its Best Management Practices (BMPs) during the December 17, 2020 inspection. (Source: Stantec field observations)
- **Past sampling locations destroyed or modified.** Stantec notes that former sampling locations PL1 and PL2 no longer exist due to construction of concrete overpass abutments. The area near PL3 has been regraded, and the stormwater basin (Basin 6B) near PL4/PL5 has been enlarged; the former gate valve at PL5 has been removed. (Source: Stantec field observations)
- **BMPs are effective.** Straw waddles and grading at the site are functioning to prevent offsite runoff during minor rain events. (Source: Stantec field observations)
- **No prior qualifying runoff since May 2020.** Based on UCANR and CIMIS weather station data, no appreciable rain fell between the last sampling event (March 2020) and the December 2020 inspection. (Source: Stantec, referencing UCANR/CIMIS data)

## Regulatory decisions
- No formal regulatory decisions (e.g., approvals, enforcement actions, or orders) are documented in this report.
- The inspection was conducted under previously approved protocols: *Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP)* (Shaw, 2006) and *Addendum to Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan* (Geocon, 2013), as approved by DTSC and CVRWQCB.

## Analytical results
- **No samples collected.** No analytical results are reported. Inspection-only event.

## Remedial actions
- **No direct remedial action described.** The report documents ongoing construction activities (SR 99/132 expressway project) including:
    - Regrading and sloping near former Stockpile 2.
    - Construction of concrete overpass abutments near former Stockpiles 1 and 2.
    - Enlargement of the stormwater basin (Basin 6B) and removal of the former gate valve.
    - Installation of straw waddles as BMPs around construction areas.

## Responsible parties
- **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans):** Owner/operator of the site; recipient of the report; contract holder.
- **Stantec Consulting Services Inc.:** Consultant/preparer performing the inspection under Task Order 16.
- **California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC):** Co-regulator; approved sampling plan.
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB):** Co-regulator; approved sampling plan.

## Key terms and conditions
- **Inspection frequency:** On-call basis following qualifying rain events (≥72 hours dry, visible runoff). No sampling event was triggered due to insufficient rainfall.
- **Follow-on actions:** None specified. A photographic record of the inspection was provided as Appendix A.
- **Monitoring obligations:** The report implies continued adherence to the approved SAP and SAP Addendum for future sampling events.
- **Limitations:** Report is prepared solely for Caltrans; third-party reliance prohibited.
