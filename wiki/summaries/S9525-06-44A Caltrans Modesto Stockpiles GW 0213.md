The January 2013 groundwater monitoring event for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Project S9525-06-44A) was conducted under DTSC-approved protocols as part of an ongoing every-other-month monitoring program, with a planned shift to quarterly sampling after March 2013. Ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) were sampled on January 22–23, 2013 using standard field and laboratory procedures: groundwater levels and field parameters such as pH, conductivity, dissolved oxygen, ORP, turbidity, and purge volume were measured; wells were purged with submersible pumps or bailers; and filtered groundwater samples were sent under chain of custody to Advanced Technology Laboratories for analysis.

The laboratory program included Title 22 dissolved metals, mercury, major ions, alkalinity, TDS, chloride, nitrate as N, sulfate, and sulfide, with PAH analysis discontinued after November 2012 because earlier results were nondetect. QA/QC results generally showed acceptable method performance, with blanks mostly nondetect, control sample recoveries within limits, and only some matrix-spike or duplicate precision flags noted; overall, the batch was considered valid.

Hydrogeologically, the shallow groundwater continued to flow generally southeast, consistent with historic site conditions, with an average gradient of about 0.001 ft/ft. Depth to groundwater ranged from about 31 to 40 ft below ground surface, and groundwater elevations ranged from about 46.5 to 49.35 ft MSL. Field conditions showed circumneutral pH and moderate to high conductivity, while turbidity often dropped substantially after purging, indicating clearer water before sample collection.

Chemically, the site groundwater reflected at least two water types: bicarbonate-dominant groundwater paired with either calcium-dominant or sodium-dominant cation chemistry, as illustrated by Stiff diagrams. Most dissolved metals and general mineral constituents were at or below applicable water-quality thresholds. Exceedances were limited mainly to nitrate and TDS, with one manganese exceedance in MW-6. Barium and strontium were detected at low to moderate concentrations but remained below their respective thresholds. No free product was observed in any sampled well.
