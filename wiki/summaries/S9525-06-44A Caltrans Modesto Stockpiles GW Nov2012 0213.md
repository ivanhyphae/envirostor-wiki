The document is a November 2012 groundwater monitoring and laboratory report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, prepared under Caltrans Task Order 44. It combines field sampling of 10 monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) with certified laboratory analysis of groundwater chemistry, including dissolved metals, major ions/anions, total dissolved solids (TDS), alkalinity, sulfide, mercury, and semivolatile organic compounds (SVOCs), especially PAHs. Field work on November 28–29, 2012 included inspecting well seals, measuring depth to groundwater and field parameters, purging wells where possible, and collecting groundwater samples; some wells went dry during purging and were allowed to recover before sampling. QA/QC included blanks, duplicates, laboratory control samples, matrix spikes, and surrogate recovery checks, and the results were generally considered analytically defensible.

Hydrogeologically, groundwater was encountered in the shallow upper aquifer, with depths to water ranging from about 32.28 ft bgs in MW-1 to 41.18 ft bgs in MW-5. The groundwater gradient averaged about 0.0008 to the southeast, consistent with previous monitoring. The chemistry indicates moderately to strongly mineralized groundwater dominated by sodium, calcium, magnesium, bicarbonate alkalinity, nitrate, chloride, and sulfate, with elevated dissolved solids across the site. TDS ranged roughly from 380 to 640 mg/L, alkalinity from 270 to 390 mg/L as CaCO3, chloride from 5.7 to 42 mg/L, nitrate as N from 2.8 to 24 mg/L, and sulfate from 12 to 66 mg/L.

With respect to contaminants of concern, barium, lead, and strontium were the principal metals of interest for the site. Barium was detected in the range of about 60 to 300 µg/L and strontium from about 430 to 1,100 µg/L, both below their applicable drinking-water thresholds; lead was below detection limits in the reported results. Most dissolved metals were not detected or were present at low concentrations, though some metals such as arsenic, chromium, and vanadium were detected at low µg/L levels. Mercury was not detected in any analyzed sample. General water quality was mostly below California drinking-water standards, but nitrate and TDS exceeded standards in several wells, and manganese exceeded its standard in one well.

The organic analyses were reassuring: all targeted SVOCs, including PAHs, were nondetect in the analyzed wells at the reporting limits. Because PAHs were not detected in any samples, the report supports discontinuing PAH analysis in future sampling events. Overall, the report concludes that the groundwater beneath the Modesto Stockpiles site is mineralized but shows no significant PAH contamination, with only limited inorganic exceedances and no evidence that barium or strontium are above regulatory thresholds.
