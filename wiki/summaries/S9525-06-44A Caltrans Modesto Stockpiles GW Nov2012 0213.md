## Combined Summary of "raw/S9525-06-44A Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles GW Nov2012 0213.pdf"

This document reports on the November 2012 groundwater monitoring event at the Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles site (S9525-06-44A). It integrates field measurements, a geochemical interpretive report, and two sets of laboratory analytical certificates from Advanced Technology Laboratories (ELAP‑certified). The primary objective was to characterize groundwater chemistry, evaluate compliance with California drinking water standards, and document spatial trends.

### Methods

**Field procedures** (interpretive report section): Water levels were measured with a water‑level meter; dissolved oxygen and ORP were recorded. Wells were purged using a submersible pump (except low‑yield wells MW‑5 and MW‑6, which were bailed and allowed to recover). Approximately three well volumes (1.5–6 gal) were removed while monitoring pH, conductivity, temperature, and turbidity. Samples were collected with disposable bailers; dissolved metals were filtered through a 0.45‑µm filter.

**Laboratory analyses** (all three sections):  
- **Anions** (Cl⁻, NO₃⁻, SO₄²⁻): EPA 300.0 (ion chromatography).  
- **Alkalinity**: SM 2320B (speciated).  
- **Total dissolved solids (TDS)** : SM 2540C.  
- **Sulfide**: SM 4500‑S²⁻.  
- **Dissolved metals** (including Title 22 metals, Ca, Mg, K, Na, Sr, V, Co, Mo, etc.): EPA 6020 (ICP‑MS).  
- **Dissolved mercury**: EPA 7470 (cold‑vapor AA).  
- **Semivolatile organic compounds (SVOCs)** : EPA 8270 by selected‑ion monitoring (SIM); PQL = 0.20 µg/L.  
- **Quality control**: Method blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), LCS duplicates, matrix spikes (MS), matrix spike duplicates (MSD), sample duplicates, and a field (equipment) blank. Cation‑anion balances were calculated for all samples.

### Key Results

- **Groundwater hydraulics**: Depth to water ranged from 32.28 ft (MW‑1) to 41.18 ft (MW‑5) below TOC; elevations varied from 45.48 to 48.11 ft MSL. Flow direction is consistently southeast with an average gradient of 0.0008 ft/ft. Two distinct water types persist: calcium‑dominant (MW‑1, MW‑2, MW‑4, MW‑5, MW‑7, MW‑9, MW‑10) and sodium‑dominant (MW‑3, MW‑6, MW‑8); MW‑10 shifted to calcium‑dominant during 2012.

- **Metals (µg/L)** :  
  - Barium: 60–300 (MCL 1,000/health advisory 700).  
  - Strontium: 430–1,100 (health advisory 4,000).  
  - Arsenic: 2.1–5.1 (MCL 10).  
  - Chromium: 0.60–8.0 (MCL 50).  
  - Vanadium: 18–38 (notification level 50).  
  - Manganese: 11–160 (secondary MCL 50); MW‑8 exceeded the standard at 160.  
  - Nickel: 1.3–2.3 (MCL 100).  
  - Copper: 1.0–2.1 (MCL 1,300/1,000).  
  - Lead, antimony, beryllium, cadmium, silver, thallium, zinc, mercury: all below practical quantitation limits (PQL).  
  - Calcium, sodium, magnesium, and potassium were reported at high levels (up to 82,000, 120,000, 31,000, and 12,000 µg/L, respectively), requiring significant dilutions.

- **General minerals (mg/L)** :  
  - Nitrate as nitrogen: 2.8–24; exceeded the primary MCL (10 mg/L) in wells MW‑1, MW‑5, MW‑6, MW‑8, and MW‑10.  
  - TDS: 340–640; exceeded the secondary MCL (500 mg/L) in MW‑4, MW‑5, MW‑6, and MW‑10.  
  - Chloride: 5.7–30; sulfate: 12–98.  
  - Sulfide: detected in 8 of 10 wells (0.06–0.12 mg/L).  
  - Cation‑anion balances ranged from 4.9 to 9.8 meq/L (cations) and 6.0 to 11 meq/L (anions).

- **Organic compounds**: No PAHs (by EPA 8270‑SIM) were detected above the PQL in any well. PAH analysis was discontinued for future events. All 17 SVOC target analytes were not detected.

### Limitations and QA/QC Notes

- **Low‑yield wells**: MW‑5 and MW‑6 went dry during purging and required recovery time, potentially affecting sample representativeness.  
- **Matrix spike outliers**: Several MS recoveries for dissolved metals (e.g., Ca, Ba, Mg, Na, Cr, Cu, V) fell outside the 70–130% acceptance limits; however, the laboratory attributed these to matrix interference (M2) or high native analyte‑to‑spike ratios (M3). The analytical batches were validated by satisfactory LCS recoveries. Similarly, some relative percent differences (RPDs) for duplicate analyses exceeded 20% (e.g., antimony, beryllium, cadmium).  
- **Dilution effects**: Many high‑concentration targets (Ca, Na, Mg) required up to 100‑fold dilutions (flag D6), with results marked above the quantitation range (flag E).  
- **Equipment blank**: The field blank contained sodium at 64 µg/L, but the report concluded this did not significantly influence sample results because sodium concentrations were consistent with historical data.  
- **Historical data**: Data prior to March 2012 were reproduced from an earlier report (Shaw Environmental, 2007) and were not independently verified.  
- **Scope**: SVOC analysis was limited to 17 PAH‑like compounds; other organic contaminants were not tested.
