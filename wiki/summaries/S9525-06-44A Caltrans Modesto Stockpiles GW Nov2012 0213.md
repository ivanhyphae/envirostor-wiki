This document is a Certificate of Analysis from Advanced Technology Laboratories for the groundwater monitoring report of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles project (November 2012). The study involved bimonthly groundwater monitoring from ten wells (MW-1 through MW-10) in Stanislaus County, California. Groundwater flow was determined to be toward the southeast.

The primary methodology included depth-to-water measurements, well purging, and sample collection using disposable bailers. Samples were analyzed by a California-certified lab using various EPA and Standard Methods: dissolved metals via ICP-MS (EPA 6020) and cold vapor atomic absorption (EPA 7470), anions via ion chromatography (EPA 300.0), alkalinity (SM 2320B), total dissolved solids (SM 2540C), sulfide (SM 4500-S²⁻), and semivolatile organic compounds (SVOCs) via EPA 8270/SIM. Quality control measures included blanks, LCS, duplicates, and matrix spikes. Several data quality limitations were noted, including matrix spike recoveries outside acceptance limits (flagged M1/M2/M3) due to interference, and the need for sample dilution (D6 flag) due to high analyte concentrations.

Key results indicate that dissolved metals and general minerals were predominantly below water quality thresholds. The primary chemicals of concern—barium, lead, and strontium—remained below their respective Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) and Health Advisories. Specifically, barium ranged from 60-300 µg/L (MCL: 1,000 µg/L), strontium from 430-1,100 µg/L (Health Advisory: 4,000 µg/L), and arsenic from 2.1-5.1 µg/L (MCL: 10 µg/L). Exceedances were limited to manganese (MW-8), nitrate (up to 24 mg/L in MW-5, exceeding the 10 mg/L MCL in multiple wells), and total dissolved solids (TDS, up to 640 mg/L in MW-5 and MW-10). All 17 target SVOCs were not detected in the sampled wells. Major cation concentrations included calcium (up to 82,000 µg/L), sodium (up to 120,000 µg/L in MW-6), and magnesium, while mercury was not detected in any sample. Stiff diagrams in the appendix illustrate the major ion composition of the groundwater.
