## Site and Scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered**: Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles – two barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles located under a portion of State Route 132 (SR 132) in Stanislaus County, California. The clean cap cover over the stockpiles consists of the SR 132 freeway pavement, median/shoulders, north vegetated slope, MSE wall, and storm drains.
- **Media addressed**: Soil (BCS stockpiles), cap/cover materials (pavement, slope, drains). No groundwater, surface water, or air addressed in this document.
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced**: Not applicable (no sampling locations; only O&M plan elements: north slope, MSE wall, highway pavement, median/shoulders, storm drains).

## Key Claims
- **No claims about site conditions, compliance, or remedial adequacy are made in this document.** The letter contains only regulatory comments and recommendations on the draft O&M Plan. DTSC asserts that the Plan requires revisions to meet regulatory expectations, but these are not data-supported claims about the site itself.

## Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision type**: Comment letter with required revisions; no formal approval or denial of the draft O&M Plan at this time. The Plan is to be revised and resubmitted as Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report (RACR).
- **Deciding agency and date**: DTSC (in consultation with Central Valley RWQCB); August 1, 2022.
- **Conditions or requirements imposed**:
  1. Correct misspelling of “Maintenance” in the subject line.
  2. Section 2.1: Include a minimum of 10 working days advance notification to DTSC before any BCS disturbance, with prior written authorization required.
  3. Section 2.4: Add text stating compliance with Health and Safety Code (HSC) 25123.3(b)(4)(A). Note that 6 millimeters thickness meets HSC requirements (but HSC uses mils); correct misspelling of “millimeter.”
  4. Section 3.1: Revise to require inspections after fires, substantial rainfall (≥1 inch in 24 hours), significant seismic events, and traffic accidents that may damage the cap.
  5. Section 2.7: Periodic patrols must also note unauthorized access (e.g., homeless encampments) that may impact the cap, especially on the north vegetated slope.
  6. O&M Site Inspection Form: Expand line items with subheadings for specific elements under each stockpile: MSE Wall (bulging, out-of-plane), Highway Pavement (cracks, potholes), Median and Shoulders (cracks, potholes), North Slope (vegetation, rills, slumping), Storm Drains (debris/obstructions, erosion around inlets/outlets).
- **Documents approved or acted on**: Draft Operation and Maintenance Plan (March 2022) and accompanying blank O&M Site Inspection Form.

## Key Terms and Conditions
- Advance notification: ≥10 working days to DTSC before any BCS disturbance.
- HSC 25123.3(b)(4)(A) compliance to be explicitly stated; cap thickness of 6 mm (equivalent to 6 mils) accepted.
- Inspection triggers: annual plus events (fire, ≥1 inch rain in 24 hours, significant seismic event, traffic accident).
- Patrols must check for unauthorized access/homeless encampments.
- Inspection form must include subheadings for specific cap elements (MSE wall, pavement, median/shoulders, north slope, storm drains).
- Revised O&M Plan to be submitted as Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report.
