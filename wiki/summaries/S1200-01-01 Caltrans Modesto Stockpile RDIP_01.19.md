# Final Summary: Caltrans Modesto Stockpile RDIP

## Document Overview

This Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) and associated regulatory agreements govern the management of barium-contaminated and aerially deposited lead (ADL)-contaminated soil stockpiles at the Caltrans Modesto site, located within State right-of-way south of the SR-99/Kansas Avenue interchange. The project addresses soil stockpiles generated during prior highway construction activities and establishes procedures for their containment and reuse as highway embankment fill.

## Key Claims and Regulatory Framework

The DTSC-approved remedial alternative for barium-containing soil stockpiles is **containment by encapsulation** – incorporating the stockpiles as highway embankment fill covered with structural pavement sections and imported clean fill during construction of the SR-132/SR-99 interchange. The project is divided into two phases: **Interim Phase 1** (planned completion 2020), addressed by this RDIP for Stockpiles 1 through 3, and **Ultimate Phase 2** (planned 2028), which will be covered by a separate RDIP.

A formal agreement between DTSC and Caltrans replaces a previous Variance (V09HQSCD006) for managing ADL-contaminated soil from highway projects. This agreement defines thresholds (80–3200 mg/kg total lead or 5–150 mg/L extractable lead) that allow reuse within State highway rights-of-way without full hazardous waste permitting. A transition plan allows projects that passed the Project Approval & Environmental Document milestone before July 1, 2016, to continue under earlier Variance conditions until July 1, 2020.

Verification thresholds for stockpile removal are **barium ≤ 1,000 mg/kg** and **lead ≤ 80 mg/kg** (DTSC residential screening level). The post-excavation barium confirmation level of 1,000 mg/kg, based on an attenuation factor of 1, is described as overly protective since existing data show no measurable groundwater impact from existing stockpiles. For lead, the 80 mg/kg confirmation level is a screening level derived from limited data exhibiting a "nugget effect" (only 2 of 254 samples exceeded 20 mg/kg). A **Fence Line Action Level of 4 mg/m³** total dust above upwind background will be used for real-time perimeter air monitoring, based on a conservative lead concentration of 190 mg/kg in stockpile soil.

## Methodology

**Site characterization** identified three soil stockpiles within Caltrans ROW with estimated volumes of approximately 34,000 yd³ (Stockpile 1), 102,000 yd³ (Stockpile 2), and 24,000 yd³ (Stockpile 3). **Remedial design** involves excavating barium-containing soil from Stockpile 2 bridge abutment areas and the entire Stockpile 3, then placing it within designated consolidation zones on Stockpiles 1 and 2, and adjacent vacant State ROW. Total estimated removal volume is 27,850 yd³ (Stockpile 2 abutments: 2,200 + 1,650 yd³; Stockpile 3: 24,000 yd³), with total consolidation zone volume of 54,500 yd³, leaving approximately 26,650 yd³ capacity for ADL soil.

**Verification sampling** requires one sample per 5,000 ft² of removal area and one per 100 lineal feet of activity area, analyzed for barium and lead via EPA Method 6010B (reporting limit 1.0 mg/kg), with minimum samples of 8 (West End Stockpile 2), 12 (East End Stockpile 2), and 24 (Stockpile 3).

The **regulatory framework** for ADL management defines thresholds: ADL-contaminated soil has total lead ≥80 mg/kg (95% UCL) or extractable lead ≥5 mg/L (CA-WET). Soils exceeding 150 mg/L DI-WET or 3200 mg/kg total lead must be handled as hazardous waste. Cover requirements depend on lead concentration: soils with extractable lead >5 mg/L CA-WET or total lead >320 mg/kg require minimum 1 ft clean soil cover (provided DI-WET ≤1.5 mg/L and total lead ≤1600 mg/kg); soils with DI-WET >1.5 mg/L or total lead >1600 mg/kg require pavement structure (provided DI-WET ≤150 mg/L and total lead ≤3200 mg/kg). Soils with pH ≤5.0 are ineligible for management under this agreement.

**Technical analysis** (Appendix D) compares total barium vs. DI-WET concentrations and native soil barium concentrations beneath stockpiles with background levels. Evaluation uses California MCL (1.0 mg/L for barium, 0.15 mg/L for lead), attenuation factors (1 to 10), and the 95% upper confidence limit of background data.

**Air monitoring** employs MIE pDR-1200 real-time particulate counters with PM-10 inlet and 5µ PVC filters, with one upwind and two downwind monitoring locations; filters analyzed for inorganic lead and barium (NIOSH 7300-MS or equivalent). **Groundwater monitoring** involves annual sampling (April) per 2006 DTSC-approved Work Plan; stormwater sampling during qualified precipitation events per 2006 SAP and 2013 Addendum.

**Health and safety** procedures are outlined in two HSPs. The project HSP identifies primary chemical hazards as T22 (CAM 17) toxic metals (barium and inorganic lead), with low exposure risk based on a 2012 negative exposure assessment. Level D Protection may be upgraded to Level C without justification, but Level C may only be downgraded upon approval. The HSP lists permissible exposure limits: Inorganic Arsenic (PEL = 0.01 mg/m³, Action Level = 0.005 mg/m³), Cadmium (PEL = 5 µg/m³), Inorganic Lead (PEL = 50 µg/m³, Action Level = 30 µg/m³), and Barium Sulfate (PEL-TWA = 10 mg/m³). Grading does not require OSHA 40-hour trained personnel or Class A-HAZ licenses, only awareness training.

## Results

Background barium 95% UCL is approximately 73-75 mg/kg. For total barium concentrations ≤12,000 mg/kg, DI-WET concentration is ≤20 mg/L. Native soil barium beneath Stockpile #2 has maximum 780 mg/kg, with 55 of 59 samples <200 mg/kg. A post-excavation confirmation level of **1,000 mg/kg** is recommended for barium.

Of 254 lead samples, 252 have lead concentrations ≤20 mg/kg; two outliers at 150 mg/kg and 1,500 mg/kg had DI-WET results of 0.07 mg/L and 0.1 mg/L (below MCL of 0.15 mg/L). A post-excavation confirmation level of **80 mg/kg** is recommended for lead.

Total estimated barium-containing soil removal volume: **27,850 yd³**. Total consolidation zone volume: **54,500 yd³**, with approximately **26,650 yd³** capacity available for ADL soil. The calculated Fence Line Action Level for total dust is **4 mg/m³** (using 190 mg/kg lead, ARB-OEHHA unit risk 0.0015 mg/m³, safety factor 0.5). Clean cover requirements are **minimum 12-inch-thick** imported clean fill or **minimum 4-inch-thick** aggregate base beneath pavement/hardscape on consolidation zones. Groundwater depths are **35–46 ft** below natural grade (April 2018), flowing generally south, with no dewatering anticipated.

## Limitations

This RDIP covers **only Interim Phase 1** (completion 2020); Ultimate Phase 2 containment (retaining walls along northern ROW of Stockpiles 1 and 2) is required for complete encapsulation by 2028. **Northern slope areas** of Stockpiles 1 and 2 will only be capped with vegetated clean soil (12-inch) during Interim Phase 1, not fully contained behind retaining walls until Phase 2.

The DTSC-Caltrans 2016 Agreement has several constraints: It applies only to ADL-contaminated soil where lead is the sole hazardous constituent; soils with other hazardous constituents are excluded. Soils with pH ≤5.0 cannot be managed under this agreement. No land use covenants can be recorded for most State highway rights-of-way (lack of assessor parcel numbers); instead, Caltrans must maintain an internal list and revise relinquishment policies. Joint projects with local government entities retain joint and several liability.

The lead analysis is based on only two elevated samples (outliers), making statistical analysis very limited. The barium confirmation level of 1,000 mg/kg uses an attenuation factor of 1, described as overly protective. The regulatory agreement does not release Caltrans from liability for past, current, or future operations. Annual DTSC oversight cost is capped at $150,000 (≤1760 hours plus contract support). Stockpile duration is limited to maximum 90 days (extendable to 180 days with DTSC notification). The HSP related to lead exposure expires six months from the date of Project CIH approval unless updated.

## Key Concepts

- **Containment by encapsulation**: Barium-containing soil incorporated as highway embankment fill covered with structural pavement and clean imported fill
- **ADL management thresholds**: Total lead 80–3200 mg/kg, extractable lead 5–150 mg/L (CA-WET, DI-WET)
- **Verification sampling**: One sample per 5,000 ft² (removal area), one per 100 lineal feet (activity area)
- **Fence line action level**: 4 mg/m³ total dust above upwind background
- **Confirmation levels**: Barium ≤1,000 mg/kg, lead ≤80 mg/kg
- **Cover requirements**: Minimum 12-inch clean soil or 4-inch aggregate base beneath pavement
- **Real-time air monitoring**: MIE pDR-1200 with PM-10 inlet, NIOSH 7300-MS analysis
- **Regulatory instruments**: DTSC-Caltrans 2016 Agreement, Variance V09HQSCD006 (transition plan), Land Use Covenant (LUC), Five-Year Review, Operation and Maintenance Plan (OMP)
- **Testing methods**: Waste Extraction Test (WET), Deionized Water Waste Extraction Test (DI-WET), Toxicity Characteristic Leaching Procedure (TCLP), Designated Level Methodology, Environmental Screening Level (ESL)
- **Health and safety**: Level D/C PPE, P100 filters, PELs (Lead 50 µg/m³, Cadmium 5 µg/m³, Arsenic 0.01 mg/m³, Barium Sulfate 10 mg/m³), negative exposure assessment
