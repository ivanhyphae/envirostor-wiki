## Summary

This document outlines the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) for managing barium- and lead-contaminated soil stockpiles associated with the SR-132 West Project in Modesto, California. The plan integrates a site-specific remedial approach with a broader regulatory framework for Aerially Deposited Lead (ADL) soil and includes detailed health and safety protocols.

**Key Claims**
The selected remedial alternative is **containment**. Barium- and lead-contaminated soil from Stockpiles 2 and 3 will be excavated and incorporated as permanent highway embankment fill within consolidation zones on Stockpiles 1 and 2. These areas will be capped with structural pavement and imported clean fill, eliminating direct exposure and protecting groundwater and surface water. The plan operates under a legally binding agreement between Caltrans and the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), allowing ADL-contaminated soil to be managed within state highway rights-of-way under specific conditions, rather than as full hazardous waste. For post-excavation soil, confirmation levels are proposed: **1,000 mg/kg for barium** (based on attenuation factors and waste extraction test data) and **80 mg/kg for lead** (based on DTSC residential screening levels and limited leachability data). The plan mandates a buddy system and comprehensive safety protocols for all field personnel.

**Methodology**
The plan describes a multi-faceted methodology:
- **Excavation & Placement:** Barium-containing soil is excavated from Stockpiles 2 and 3, transported under covered tarps, and placed in designated consolidation zones as permanent fill.
- **Verification Sampling:** Post-excavation soil confirmation sampling (one sample per 5,000 sq ft for removal areas, one per 100 linear feet for activity areas) will be analyzed for barium and lead using EPA Method 6010B.
- **ADL Soil Management:** The plan adopts procedures from a Caltrans-DTSC agreement, including sampling via the California Waste Extraction Test (CA-WET) and a modified DI water extraction test (DI-WET), with concentration-based management tiers (e.g., ≤80 mg/kg total lead = clean; >3,200 mg/kg = full hazardous waste). ADL soil must be placed at least 5 feet above the highest groundwater elevation.
- **Air & Water Monitoring:** Air monitoring uses MIE pDR-1200 real-time particulate counters and pre-weighed filters for gravimetric and lead/barium analysis, with a Fence Line Action Level of 4 mg/m³ above upwind background. Groundwater and stormwater monitoring continue during construction.
- **Safety Protocols:** The Health and Safety Plan (HSP) defines task-specific Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) levels (Level D baseline, upgradable to Level C with P100 respirators), decontamination procedures (TSP/Alconox/Liquinox wash plus DI water rinse), and an emergency response chain.

**Results**
- **Containment Feasibility:** The containment design is deemed feasible and protective. Key soil volumes include removal of 27,850 yd³ (2,200 yd³ from Stockpile 2, 1,650 yd³ from SR-99 abutment, and 24,000 yd³ from Stockpile 3), with 26,650 yd³ of capacity available for ADL soil.
- **Verification Thresholds:** Barium confirmation level of 1,000 mg/kg is supported by native soil data (maximum native soil concentration 780 mg/kg) and protects groundwater (DI-WET equivalent of 1.0 mg/L using an attenuation factor of 1). For lead, only 2 of 254 samples showed elevated concentrations (150 and 1,500 mg/kg), with DI-WET results below the MCL of 0.15 mg/L. The 80 mg/kg confirmation level is considered protective given the very small affected area.
- **Air Monitoring:** Prior partial removal of Stockpile 3 showed airborne barium and lead levels "well below regulatory thresholds." The Fence Line Action Level was calculated using a conservative lead concentration of 190 mg/kg.
- **ADL Management Tiers:** Specific quantitative thresholds are established, e.g., leachable lead thresholds (CA-WET <5 mg/l, DI-WET <1.5 mg/l or <150 mg/l), total lead thresholds (80 mg/kg clean fill, 3,200 mg/kg for pavement cover, >3,200 mg/kg as hazardous waste), and burial depth minimum (5 feet above maximum historical water table).
- **Permissible Exposure Limits (PELs):** Referenced limits include lead (50 µg/m³ PEL, action level 30 µg/m³), cadmium (5 µg/m³), inorganic arsenic (0.01 mg/m³), and barium sulfate (10 mg/m³).

**Limitations**
- **Phased Implementation:** The plan is specific to Interim Phase 1 only; a separate RDIP will be prepared for Ultimate Phase 2 (scheduled for 2028). Northern slopes of Stockpiles 1 and 2 will not be fully contained behind retaining walls until Phase 2.
- **Excluded Soils:** The ADL agreement excludes soils that are RCRA hazardous waste, those whose sole hazardous constituent is not lead, and soils with pH ≤5.0. Such materials must be disposed of as hazardous waste. ADL soil cannot be moved outside a defined 150-mile corridor without DTSC approval.
- **Data Constraints:** For lead, data are very limited (only two elevated samples had DI-WET analysis). Barium analysis relies on the 2007 dataset as more comprehensive. An attenuation factor of 1 is acknowledged as "overly-protective."
- **Liability & Oversight:** The agreement is not a release from liability; DTSC retains the right to require removal for public health protection. Records must be preserved for six years. Project team roles may change over time.
- **Safety Caveats:** PPE is described as secondary protection; engineering controls are preferred. The plan acknowledges that medications require clearance before operating equipment. Air monitoring procedures are included only as a blank template.
