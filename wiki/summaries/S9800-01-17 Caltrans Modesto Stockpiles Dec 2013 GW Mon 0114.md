**Summary of "raw/S9800-01-17 Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles Dec 2013 GW Mon 0114.pdf"**

This document presents the results of groundwater monitoring for December 2013 at the Caltrans Modesto soil stockpiles site, based on sampling from 10 monitoring wells (MW-1 to MW-10) installed in the shallow upper aquifer (Modesto Formation). The primary objective was to assess water quality against California primary and secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs).

**Findings:** Long-term monitoring (2006–2013) indicates that general minerals and dissolved metals are largely below MCLs. Specifically, dissolved metals like barium (34–230 µg/L), lead (1.3 µg/L), and strontium (240–1,200 µg/L) remained below numeric thresholds. However, several exceedances were documented: manganese in well MW-6 (140 µg/L) exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L; nitrate (as N) exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in five wells (MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, MW-9, MW-10), with concentrations ranging from 2.4 to 25 mg/L; and TDS exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in six wells (MW-1, MW-4, MW-5, MW-6, MW-9, MW-10), with a range of 270–630 mg/L. Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) were consistently below detection limits. Groundwater flow is to the southeast with an average gradient of 0.001, and two distinct groundwater types persist: calcium-dominant (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-7, MW-10) and sodium-dominant (MW-3, MW-5, MW-6, MW-8, MW-9).

**Methodology:** Field methods included measuring depth-to-water and water quality parameters, purging 2-3 well volumes, and sampling with disposable bailers. Dissolved metals were filtered using a 0.45-µm filter. Laboratory analysis employed Title 22 methods: dissolved metals via EPA 6020 (ICP-MS) and mercury via EPA 7470A (cold vapor AA), with general minerals (anions, alkalinity, TDS, sulfide) analyzed using EPA 300.0 and Standard Methods. Quality control (QC) performance was acceptable for most analytes, with LCS recoveries within limits. However, matrix spike recoveries for several cations (e.g., calcium, magnesium, sodium) and some anions (e.g., sulfate, sulfide) occasionally fell outside acceptance limits (flagged M1, M2) due to high native concentrations requiring dilution; the batch was validated by LCS. Most metals showed RPDs below 20% for duplicates. This report represents a single sampling event and does not assess temporal trends.
