## Combined Summary of "raw/S9800-01-17 Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles Dec 2013 GW Mon 0114.pdf"

This document reports the results of the December 2013 groundwater monitoring event at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California. The site contains three soil stockpiles generated during 1961 highway construction from a former FMC evaporation pond. The report includes a narrative summary of field activities, analytical results, and two Certificates of Analysis (COAs) from Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) for samples collected from ten monitoring wells (MW‑1 through MW‑10) in the shallow upper aquifer (Modesto Formation).

### Methodology

Field work was conducted on December 11–12, 2013. Depth‑to‑groundwater was measured, and dissolved oxygen and oxygen‑reduction potential were recorded. Wells were purged (2–3 well volumes, 0.75–5.5 gallons) using a submersible pump or disposable bailer until field parameters (pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, turbidity) stabilized. Samples were collected with disposable bailers; dissolved metals samples were field‑filtered through a 0.45‑µm filter. Analytical work was performed by ATL, a California‑certified laboratory. Methods included:

- **Dissolved metals** (Title 22 metals including strontium) by EPA Method 6020 (ICP‑MS); mercury by EPA Method 7470A (Cold Vapor AA).
- **Major cations** (calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium) by EPA 6020.
- **Anions** (chloride, nitrate‑N, sulfate) by EPA Method 300 (ion chromatography).
- **Alkalinity** (total, bicarbonate, carbonate) by SM 2320B.
- **Total dissolved solids (TDS)** by SM 2540C.
- **Sulfide** by SM 4500‑S=D.

Quality control included method blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), matrix spikes (MS/MSD), and duplicates. Results were compared to California Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) and EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories.

### Results

**Groundwater elevations** ranged from 44.08 ft (MW‑8) to 46.93 ft (MW‑1) above mean sea level. Flow direction was generally southeast with an average gradient of 0.001 ft/ft, consistent with historical data.

**Dissolved metals** were mostly below regulatory thresholds. The only exceedance among metals was **manganese in MW‑6** (140 µg/L, exceeding the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L). Other metals (barium, lead, strontium, arsenic, vanadium, chromium, cobalt, copper, molybdenum, nickel, selenium, zinc) were below applicable MCLs or health advisories. Mercury was not detected in any well (PQL 0.20 µg/L).

**General minerals and inorganics** showed several exceedances:
- **Nitrate as nitrogen** exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in five wells: MW‑1 (13 mg/L), MW‑5 (25 mg/L), MW‑6 (16 mg/L), MW‑9 (11 mg/L), MW‑10 (18 mg/L). Upgradient well MW‑1 had elevated nitrate, suggesting natural background influences.
- **Total dissolved solids (TDS)** exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in six wells: MW‑1 (500 mg/L), MW‑4 (510 mg/L), MW‑5 (630 mg/L), MW‑6 (580 mg/L), MW‑9 (520 mg/L), MW‑10 (610 mg/L).
- **Sulfide** was detected in six wells at concentrations between 0.011 and 0.19 mg/L.

**Groundwater chemistry** was bicarbonate‑dominant. Two water types persisted: calcium‑dominant (MW‑1, MW‑2, MW‑4, MW‑7, MW‑10) and sodium‑dominant (MW‑3, MW‑5, MW‑6, MW‑8, MW‑9). Stiff diagrams were prepared for all wells. Slight shifts were noted, with MW‑5 and MW‑9 changing from calcium‑ to sodium‑dominant in December 2013. Cation‑anion balances (e.g., MW‑10: 8.5 meq/L cations, 9.5 meq/L anions) were generally acceptable.

**PAH analysis** had been discontinued after November 2012 with DTSC concurrence because no PAHs were detected in any prior samples; this event relied on that conclusion.

### Limitations

Several data quality issues were identified:
- **Holding time exceedance:** Nitrate analyses for MW‑1 through MW‑4 were performed outside holding time due to a lab error. However, draft data from an in‑time re‑analysis showed comparable results, so no data qualification was required. TDS analyses for MW‑5 through MW‑10 also exceeded holding time (flagged H2).
- **Matrix spike anomalies:** Multiple MS/MSD recoveries fell outside acceptance limits (e.g., sulfide recoveries of 163–192%; some metals like Ca, Mg, Na due to high native concentrations). These results were validated by corresponding LCS recoveries, but potential matrix interference is noted.
- **Dilution effects:** Several samples required dilution (flags D5, D6) due to high target analyte concentrations or internal standard failures, which may affect quantitation limits.
- **Delta checks:** Some Stiff diagrams note that delta checks (repeat analyses) were not performed for certain wells.
- **Representativeness:** Results are specific to the December 2013 sampling event and may not reflect long‑term conditions.

### Key Conclusions

- The only dissolved‑metal exceedance was manganese in MW‑6 (140 µg/L, secondary MCL 50 µg/L).
- Nitrate and TDS exceedances are widespread, affecting five and six wells respectively. Upgradient well MW‑1 indicates background sources may contribute to elevated nitrate and TDS.
- Two distinct water types (calcium‑dominant and sodium‑dominant) persist, with minor shifts observed.
- Groundwater flow remains southeastward with a shallow gradient.
- PAHs are not a concern at this site.
- Data quality is considered sufficient for reporting purposes despite the noted limitations.
