## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route 132 and 99, Stanislaus County, California. The site is the location of a proposed State Route 132/99 West Expressway. The specific areas are soil stockpiles consisting of excess native soils and pond tailings.
- **Media addressed:** Soil (soil stockpiles).
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** None specified.

## Key claims
- **Claim:** The Final Workplan provides additional characterization of soil stockpiles to evaluate and finalize a remedy for the management of hazardous substances.
    - **Who is making it:** DTSC (Randy S. Adams).
    - **Supported by data in this document, conditional, or contested:** This is DTSC’s stated purpose for the document, not a claim supported by data presented here.
- **Claim:** The additional characterization data is needed to evaluate potential lateral and vertical migration of contaminants from the soil stockpiles and to update the Human Health Risk Assessment.
    - **Who is making it:** DTSC.
    - **Supported by data in this document, conditional, or contested:** Stated as the objective of the Workplan.
- **Claim:** The soil stockpiles were generated when Caltrans constructed a segment of SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue in the early 1960’s, traversing a portion of a 4.3-acre parcel purchased from Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation Inc. (FMC) that contained an evaporation pond.
    - **Who is making it:** DTSC.
    - **Supported by data in this document, conditional, or contested:** This is background information provided by DTSC in the letter.
- **Claim:** The primary Chemicals of Concern (COC) are barium, lead, and strontium.
    - **Who is making it:** DTSC.
    - **Supported by data in this document, conditional, or contested:** This is a statement of known site conditions.
