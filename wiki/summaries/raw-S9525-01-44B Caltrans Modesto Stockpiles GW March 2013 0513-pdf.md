# Combined Summary: Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles Groundwater Monitoring Report (March 2013)

## Document Identity
- **Document type**: Groundwater Monitoring Report (including Certificate of Analysis from ATL Global as an appendix)
- **Preparer/author organization**: Geocon Consultants, Inc. (report); ATL Global, Signal Hill, CA (laboratory analytical report)
- **Recipient/addressee**: California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch, Fresno, CA (Mr. Richard Stewart, PG); laboratory report addressed to Geocon Consultants, Inc. (Rebecca Silva)
- **Date/reporting period**: Samples collected March 18, 2013; report dated May 2013; Certificate of Analysis dated April 1, 2013
- **Project numbers**: Geocon Project No. S9525-01-44B, Task Order No. 44, EA 10-403500, Caltrans Contract No. 06A1580

## Site and Scope
- **Site name and location**: Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, southerly of the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue, Stanislaus County, California (also referred to as “Modesto Stockpiles” near SR 132)
- **Areas of concern**: Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3 (barium-contaminated fill material from former FMC evaporation pond excavation)
- **Media addressed**: Groundwater
- **Monitoring wells**: MW-1 through MW-10 (MW-1–MW-8 installed 2006; MW-9 and MW-10 installed June 2012). All 2-inch diameter, Schedule 40 PVC, screened intervals 29–42 ft bgs. Construction details in Table 4.
- **Appendices**: Certificate of Analysis, Stiff diagrams (Appendix C), cation-anion balance data, QA/QC documentation

## Key Claims (Geocon Consultants, Inc.)

- **Primary chemicals of concern (COCs)**: Barium, lead, strontium (identified by DTSC). All remained below respective water quality thresholds in March 2013.
- **No exceedances of drinking water standards for dissolved COCs**:
  - Barium: 43–300 µg/L (threshold 700–1,000 µg/L)
  - Lead: 2.2–2.5 µg/L (threshold 15 µg/L)
  - Strontium: 250–1,100 µg/L (threshold 4,000 µg/L)
- **Manganese exceedance**: MW-1 (34 µg/L) and MW-7 (260 µg/L) exceeded secondary MCL of 50 µg/L.
- **Nitrate and TDS exceedances**: Nitrate as N exceeded primary MCL (10 mg/L) in MW-1 (13 mg/L), MW-5 (30 mg/L), MW-6 (17 mg/L), MW-10 (17 mg/L). TDS met or exceeded secondary MCL (500 mg/L) in MW-1 (500), MW-5 (700), MW-6 (600), MW-10 (620). MW-1 (upgradient) may represent background.
- **Groundwater flow**: Toward east-southeast at average gradient 0.0006 ft/ft, consistent with historical data (supported by water level measurements and elevation contours, Figure 3).
- **Hydrogeology**: Shallow zone of upper aquifer (unconfined to semi-confined) at ~35 ft bgs; similar to adjacent FMC site.
- **PAH analysis discontinued**: No PAHs detected previously; DTSC concurred to discontinue after November 2012.
- **MW-5 performance**: Consistently purges dry; planned redevelopment in June 2013.

## Regulatory Decisions

- **DTSC concurrence** to discontinue PAH analysis (referenced; not a formal decision in this report)
- **DTSC observation** of sample collection during March 2012 event (split samples collected)
- **No formal regulatory decision** made in this report; submitted for compliance with DTSC-approved work plan (Shaw Environmental, January 2006)
- **Sampling frequency recommendation**: Geocon recommends change from bi-monthly to quarterly; next event June 2013

### QA/QC & Data Quality

- Equipment blank: chromium at 0.91 µg/L (considered insignificant)
- Laboratory method blanks, LCS acceptable
- **QA/QC flags from lab report**:
  - **D6**: Diluted due to high analyte concentration (barium in MW-1, MW-5; also major cations)
  - **D5**: Diluted due to failing internal standard (beryllium in MW-1, MW-5; MW-9 batch)
  - **M1/M2**: Matrix spike recovery outside limits, batch validated by LCS
  - **R**: RPD outside acceptance criteria (MSD2 for dissolved metals)
- **Cation-anion balances**: Provided for each well; calculated values 4.6–12 meq/L; imbalances noted in MW-5, MW-6, MW-8 (see Stiff diagrams, Appendix C)
- All data considered valid; no qualification necessary
