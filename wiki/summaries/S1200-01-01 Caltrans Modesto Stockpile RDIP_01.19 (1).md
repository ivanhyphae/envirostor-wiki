## Combined Summary of the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) – Interim Phase 1, Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles

This document is a **Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP)** for the **Interim Phase 1** management of three barium‑laden soil stockpiles (totaling ~160,000 yd³) located south of the SR‑99/Kansas Avenue interchange in Modesto, California. The stockpiles originated from former FMC chemical processing facilities and are being addressed through a containment remedy that incorporates the soil as highway embankment fill for the SR‑132 West Project. The RDIP also addresses **aerially deposited lead (ADL)**‑contaminated soil generated during highway widening excavations, which may be placed in designated consolidation zones. The selected remedy (encapsulation) eliminates direct exposure and protects groundwater/surface water by covering the stockpiles with a structural pavement section and imported clean fill.

### Regulatory Context
The RDIP operates under a broader **Imminent and Substantial Endangerment Order (ISE Order) and Administrative Order on Consent (AOC)** between the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and Caltrans. This statewide agreement defines ADL‑contaminated soil (total lead >80 mg/kg based on 95% UCL, or extractable lead >5 mg/L by CA‑WET) and establishes management criteria: soils below 320 mg/kg total lead require no cover; soils up to 1,600 mg/kg need 1 ft of clean soil cover; soils up to 3,200 mg/kg need pavement structure cover; soils exceeding 3,200 mg/kg are subject to full hazardous waste regulation. Soils with pH ≤5.0 are excluded from the agreement. The RDIP references this framework to guide handling of ADL soils encountered during construction.

### Remedial Strategy and Quantified Thresholds
- **Barium confirmation level**: 1,000 mg/kg post‑excavation total barium, derived from statistical analysis of DI‑WET (de‑ionized water waste extraction test) data and attenuation factors (1–10). All total barium concentrations ≤12,000 mg/kg yield DI‑WET ≤20 mg/L; an attenuation factor of 10 gives a maximum DI‑WET of 10 mg/L, corresponding to ~6,000 mg/kg soil. A factor of 1 (no aquifer dilution) supports the 1,000 mg/kg threshold.
- **Lead confirmation level**: 80 mg/kg (equivalent to the residential soil screening level). Only two of 254 samples exceeded background (150 and 1,500 mg/kg); DI‑WET on those two gave 0.1 and 0.07 mg/L, well below the MCL.
- Stockpile 3 (~24,000 yd³) will be completely removed and placed over Stockpile 1 and adjacent vacant State right‑of‑way. Approximately 27,850 yd³ of barium soil from Stockpile 3 and Stockpile 2 bridge abutment areas will be relocated to consolidation zones of ~54,500 yd³ capacity.

### Methodology
- **Soil characterization**: Sampling at one per 5,000 ft² of removal area and one per 100 lineal feet of activity area, analyzed by EPA Method 6010B for lead and barium. Verification thresholds: 1,000 mg/kg barium, 80 mg/kg lead.
- **Air monitoring**: Perimeter real‑time particulate counters (MIE pDR‑1200 with PM‑10 inlets) and 5‑µm PVC filter cassettes for metals analysis (NIOSH 7300). Fence Line Action Level for total dust is 4 mg/m³ above upwind background (based on conservative lead concentration of 190 mg/kg).
- **Groundwater monitoring wells**: Abandoned via pressure‑grouting per California Well Standards (Bulletin 74‑90). Groundwater extraction is not anticipated.
- **Health and Safety Plan (HSP)** : The RDIP includes a detailed HSP mandating a buddy system, Level D PPE (hard hat, safety glasses, steel‑toed boots, Tyvek coveralls, etc.) with possible upgrade to Level C (air‑purifying respirator with P100 filter) when handling dry, dusty materials. Decontamination uses TSP, Alconox®, or Liquinox® followed by double deionized water rinse. Emergency procedures include immediate 911 activation and Cal/OSHA notification within 24 hours of serious injury or death.

### Results and Schedule
- The **Final Remedial Action Plan (RAP)** for the site was approved by DTSC on March 14, 2018.
- **Interim Phase 1 construction**: mid‑2019 to late 2020.
- Stockpile 2 excavation depths: 10–20 ft to native ground (elevations ~80–82 ft above MSL). Stockpile 3 removal depths up to ~13 ft.
- Minimum verification samples: West end Stockpile 2 (8), East end Stockpile 2 (12), Stockpile 3 (24).
- 2014 groundwater data showed no measurable impact from stockpiles. Air monitoring from September 2012 found airborne lead and barium well below ARB‑OEHHA unit risk levels.
- Statistical analysis of background and stockpile data: 2007 data set (254 samples) gave mean barium background ~75 mg/kg; maximum concentration in native soils beneath stockpiles was 780 mg/kg (Stockpile 2). Lead: 252 of 254 samples had max 20 mg/kg (mean 3.9, SD 3.3).

### Limitations
- **Phase 1 only**: This RDIP covers only Interim Phase 1 (completion by 2020); a separate RDIP will be prepared for Ultimate Phase 2 (completion ~2028). Final containment of northern slopes of Stockpiles 1 and 2 (retaining walls and pavement) will not occur until Phase 2.
- **Statewide ADL restrictions**: ADL soil cannot be stockpiled/buried outside the project corridor (>150 miles without DTSC approval). Soils with pH ≤5.0 or RCRA‑hazardous levels are excluded. For highway rights‑of‑way lacking assessor parcel numbers, land use covenants cannot be recorded, requiring alternative tracking mechanisms.
- **Data limitations**: Lead data are very limited (only two elevated samples); the small data set makes the confirmation level (32–225 mg/kg range) not meaningfully distinguishable. Barium attenuation factors assume worst‑case (factor=1, no mixing) and may be overly protective.
- **Land use covenant**: Unrestricted uses (residential, schools, daycare) are prohibited. Five‑year reviews will assess containment effectiveness until final DTSC certification after Phase 2.
- **Transition Plan**: Projects that completed PA&ED milestone before July 1, 2016 may operate under previous Variance requirements until July 1, 2020; shelved projects requiring redesign revert to full Agreement requirements.
