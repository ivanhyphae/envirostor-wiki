## Key claims
- The removal of Barium Containing Soil (BCS) from Stockpile 3 has been completed.
- Post-excavation confirmation soil sampling shows that remaining soil meets the cleanup levels established in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP).
- The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) agrees that no further BCS RDIP monitoring requirements are necessary.
- The area is now suitable for use as clean embankment fill.
- Excavation reached native soil depths where barium and lead concentrations are below RDIP threshold values.

## Methodology
- Removal of BCS Stockpile 3 was performed, followed by confirmation soil sampling to verify cleanup levels.
- Sampling targeted the excavation floor and sidewalls to confirm that concentrations of barium and lead in native soil are below established RDIP threshold values.
- Analytical verification was conducted to compare measured concentrations against the RDIP verification thresholds.
- The process followed the protocol described in the Technical Memorandum dated March 13, 2020.

## Results
- All BCS material from Stockpile 3 was excavated from the area.
- Barium and lead concentrations in the remaining native soil are below the threshold values specified in the RDIP.
- The confirmation sampling results demonstrate achievement of cleanup levels.

## Limitations
- The approval applies only to Stockpile 3 BCS removal; it does not include the regulated ADL soil along northbound State Route 99.
- The memo does not discuss detection limits, sampling density, or potential heterogeneity of soil contamination.
- No statistical uncertainty or confidence intervals are reported for the verification sampling.
