## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Email acceptance of a Technical Memorandum (Tech Memo) regarding the characterization of excavated Bent 2 soils for use as clean cap material at the SR 132 site. Also accepts the Revised Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) Amendment dated October 22, 2020.
- **Author or preparer:** Dean Wright, PG — DTSC Project Manager (author of acceptance email); Richard C. Stewart, PG — Caltrans Engineering Geologist (sender of original Tech Memo).
- **Date or period covered:** Tech Memo dated October 30, 2020; acceptance email sent November 6, 2020; original email transmitting Tech Memo sent November 2, 2020. Winter period referenced (potential temporary exposure of soils).

## Key claims
- Soil sample results from the Bent 2 column excavation **do not meet the clean cap criteria** established in the April 9, 2020 Variance Request approved by DTSC.
- Despite the exceedance, DTSC and the Water Board (WB) agree the soil **may be placed on top of Stockpile 2** at a thickness of 6 inches or less, provided it is covered with clean base material and the asphalt/concrete used to construct the roadway.
- This approach allows efficient use of Bent 2 soil while not jeopardizing the integrity of the remedy.
- Due to the current project schedule, the Bent 2 soils **may be temporarily exposed to the elements during the winter months**.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Agency:** DTSC (Department of Toxic Substances Control) and Central Valley RWQCB (Water Board).
- **Decision type:** Acceptance of the Technical Memorandum and Revised SWPPP Amendment.
- **Date:** November 6, 2020 (acceptance email).
- **Conditions imposed:**
    - Bent 2 soil may be placed on top of Stockpile 2 only at ≤6 inches thickness.
    - Must be covered with clean base material and the roadway asphalt/concrete.
    - Rainy season surface water sampling effort and erosion control measures outlined in the Revised SWPPP Amendment (October 22, 2020) must be followed strictly.
    - Purpose of conditions: to ensure contaminants remain on site and do not impact surrounding areas.
