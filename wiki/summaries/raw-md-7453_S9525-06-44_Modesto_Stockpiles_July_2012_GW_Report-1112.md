The document is a **groundwater monitoring report for July 2012** at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. (dated November 28, 2012). Field activities occurred July 16–17, 2012, with laboratory analysis performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) through July 27, 2012.

**Key Findings:**  
The primary chemicals of concern identified by DTSC (barium, lead, strontium) were detected below applicable thresholds in all samples. However, **vanadium at MW-6 (51 µg/L) exceeded the notification level of 50 µg/L.** Additionally, **nitrate exceeded the primary MCL (10 mg/L)** in wells MW‑1, MW‑5, and MW‑6, and **total dissolved solids (TDS) exceeded the secondary MCL (500 mg/L)** in wells MW‑1, MW‑5, MW‑6, and MW‑10. All 18 target PAHs were non-detect (<0.20 µg/L) in all samples.

**Hydrogeology and Water Chemistry:**  
Groundwater flow direction remained southeast (gradient ~0.0006), consistent with historical data. Two distinct water types persisted: calcium‑dominant and sodium‑dominant.

**Quality Control Issues:**  
An equipment blank showed contamination of barium, copper, and sodium (results qualified as estimated). Numerous matrix spike (MS/MSD) recoveries for metals (calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium, strontium) and anions exceeded control limits due to apparent matrix interference; these results were flagged accordingly. Mercury and SVOC QC was acceptable.

**Regulatory Context:**  
The work followed DTSC‑approved protocols from the January 2006 Final Work Plan. No remedial actions were described in this report—it solely presents monitoring data and laboratory certifications.

**Methods:**  
Analyses included EPA 6020 (dissolved metals), EPA 7470 (mercury), EPA 8270/SIM (PAHs), and standard methods for general minerals (nitrate, chloride, sulfate, TDS, sulfide).
