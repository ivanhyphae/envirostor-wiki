Based on the three summaries, the source document "raw/S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles Oct 2016 SW Sampling_12.16.pdf" reports on surface water quality monitoring at Caltrans Modesto soil stockpiles from 2013 to 2016, with a focus on a rain event on October 28, 2016. The study, conducted per a DTSC-approved SAP, involved collecting field-filtered and grab samples from seven locations (stockpile points PL1–PL5 and background sites BG1, BG2).

All measured dissolved metals (analyzed via EPA 6020/7470A) and general minerals (chloride, sulfate, nitrate, TDS, TSS, alkalinity) were below primary and secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs). Key findings include that no stormwater runoff migrated offsite and that historical elevated barium levels from 2006 were not repeated, with barium ranging from 6.5–91 µg/L in recent sampling. Some results were estimated (J-flagged) as they fell between the method detection limit (MDL) and practical quantitation limit (PQL). Sampling limitations include potential matrix interference requiring laboratory dilutions, J-flagged data, dry conditions on some dates, missing background data, and the fact that sampling was conducted during a single rain event, limiting generalizability.
