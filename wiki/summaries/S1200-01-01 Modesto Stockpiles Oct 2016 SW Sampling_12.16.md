## Combined Summary of Source Document: "raw/S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles Oct 2016 SW Sampling_12.16.pdf"

### Key Findings
Surface water sampling at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site was conducted on October 28, 2016, during a rain event of approximately 1.5 inches. Stormwater was collected from seven locations (puddles and flowing water within and near stockpiles, plus two background inlets). None of the reported dissolved metals or general minerals exceeded their respective primary or secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs). The sole exception was manganese in sample PL3 (south of Stockpile 2), which equaled the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L. Field observations confirmed that stormwater falling on the stockpiles remained within the site boundary, with no runoff migration beyond Caltrans right-of-way; the interceptor berm at the base of Stockpile 2's access ramp functioned as designed.

### Methodology
Samples were collected from puddles or flowing water using disposable bailers or suction pumps, field-filtered for dissolved metals (0.45‑micron filters), and analyzed by Advanced Technology Laboratories, Inc. (ATL). Laboratory methods included:
- Dissolved metals: EPA Method 6020 (ICP-MS) and 7470A (cold vapor AA for mercury)
- Chloride, nitrate, sulfate: EPA Method 300.0 (ion chromatography)
- Alkalinity speciation: SM 2320B
- Total dissolved solids (TDS): SM 2540C
- Total suspended solids (TSS): SM 2540D
- Total sulfide: SM 4500‑S‑D

Field parameters (pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, ORP, DO) were measured in situ. Quality assurance/quality control included chain-of-custody, duplicate/replicate samples, blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), and matrix spikes (MS/MSD).

### Results
**Overall (samples PL1–PL5, BG1, BG2):** Dissolved metals (antimony, barium, calcium, copper, magnesium, molybdenum, nickel, potassium, sodium, strontium, zinc) were detected but below MCLs. Beryllium, chromium, mercury, silver, thallium, and vanadium were not detected above method detection limits (MDLs) in stockpile samples. Arsenic (0.42–0.46 µg/L) and lead (1.8–9.2 µg/L) were detected only in PL3 and PL5. General minerals (chloride, sulfate, nitrate, alkalinity, TDS, TSS, sulfide) were all within MCLs. Field parameters: pH 7.2–8.6, conductivity 23–81 µmhos/cm, temperature 16.0–17.1°C, turbidity 22.3–135 ntu.

**Sample PL4 (illustrative detailed results):** Concentrations included chloride 1.3 mg/L, nitrate as N 0.63 mg/L, sulfate 2.1 mg/L, bicarbonate alkalinity 19 mg/L as CaCO₃, TDS 69 mg/L, TSS 330 mg/L, and sulfide not detected. Dissolved metals (µg/L): antimony 0.64 (J), barium 48, calcium 3500, copper 8.5 (J), magnesium 590, manganese 14 (J), molybdenum 2.2, nickel 1.2 (J), potassium 3200, sodium 1400, strontium 90, zinc 32; lead, arsenic, and mercury not detected. Several metals were J‑flagged (estimated between MDL and practical quantitation limit, PQL). Quality control results were generally acceptable for LCS and duplicates, but many MS/MSD recoveries for metals fell outside limits (e.g., barium 268%, calcium 675%) due to matrix interference, resulting in flags (M1, R, D1/D5). The TDS duplicate RPD (10.7%) slightly exceeded the 10% criterion.

### Limitations
- Many analytical results were J‑flagged, indicating estimated concentrations between MDL and PQL.
- Multiple samples required dilution for various analytes (nitrate, sulfide, chloride, sulfate, and several dissolved metals) due to matrix interference or failing internal standards, potentially affecting accuracy.
- The high suspended solids content (e.g., 330 mg/L in PL4) contributed to matrix effects; several metals required additional dilution (flags D1 or D5).
- Some MS/MSD recoveries for dissolved metals were outside acceptance limits, indicating potential matrix effects in the specific sample.
- Sampling was limited to a single rain event; results may not represent all storm conditions.
- The gate valve at PL5 (drainage basin next to Stockpile 3) was not opened, so water quality at the point of release to the SR 99 collection system was not directly sampled.
- No statistical or long-term trend analysis was performed; historical comparisons are qualitative.
- The TDS duplicate RPD (10.7%) slightly exceeded the 10% limit.
