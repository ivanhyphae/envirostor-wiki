This document is a groundwater monitoring report for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles project, conducted in July 2012. Its purpose was to assess potential groundwater impacts from three soil stockpiles (originating from 1960s highway construction) located south of Kansas Avenue, near SR 99 and SR 132 in Stanislaus County, California.

**Monitoring Network and Methods**

The monitoring network consisted of ten wells (MW-1 through MW-10) installed into the shallow upper aquifer (Modesto Formation), with wells MW-9 and MW-10 newly installed in June 2012. Field measurements (depth to water, dissolved oxygen, oxygen-reduction potential) were recorded on July 16, 2012. Samples were collected after purging three well volumes (wells MW-5 and MW-6 went dry and were allowed to recover) and analyzed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL). Analytical methods included EPA 6020/7470 for metals, EPA 300.0 for anions, SM 2540C for total dissolved solids (TDS), SM 2320B for alkalinity, SM 4500 for sulfide, and EPA 8270-SIM for semivolatile organic compounds (SVOCs). Results were compared to California Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), Public Health Goals, Notification Levels, and agricultural thresholds.

**Key Results**

Groundwater flow was toward the southeast at an average gradient of 0.0006 ft/ft. Stiff diagrams identified two water types: calcium-dominant (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-5, MW-7, MW-9, MW-10) and sodium-dominant (MW-3, MW-6, MW-8); MW-10 shifted from sodium- to calcium-dominant between June and July 2012, suggesting mixing.

- **Primary Chemicals of Concern (barium, lead, strontium):** All significantly below thresholds. Barium ranged 51–280 µg/L (MCL 1,000 µg/L), lead was <1.0 µg/L (PQL), and strontium ranged 210–1,100 µg/L (health advisory 4,000 µg/L).
- **Other Metals:** Vanadium in MW-6 (51 µg/L) slightly exceeded its Notification Level of 50 µg/L. Arsenic (2.2–6.6 µg/L), chromium (1.3–14 µg/L), and molybdenum (0.56–8.2 µg/L) were all below respective thresholds.
- **General Minerals:** Nitrate exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in MW-1 (12 mg/L), MW-5 (20 mg/L), and MW-6 (19 mg/L). TDS exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in MW-1 (540 mg/L), MW-5 (730 mg/L), MW-6 (590 mg/L), and MW-10 (710 mg/L). Note: nitrates and TDS in upgradient MW-1 may reflect natural background.
- **SVOCs:** All target compounds (e.g., naphthalene, benzo(a)pyrene) were not detected above 0.20 µg/L PQL.
- **Major Ions:** Concentrations varied widely, e.g., total calcium up to 99,000 µg/L (MW-8), sodium up to 130,000 µg/L dissolved (MW-6), and bicarbonate alkalinity up to 330 mg/L (MW-8).

**Quality Control and Limitations**

Several QC issues were flagged:
- Equipment blank contained trace barium (1.1 µg/L), copper (1.0 µg/L), and sodium (100 µg/L), qualifying these results as estimated.
- Matrix spike recoveries fell outside acceptance limits for barium, antimony, selenium, calcium, magnesium, and sodium—attributed to matrix interference or disproportionate native concentrations (flags M1/M2/M3/M6).
- Duplicate RPD values exceeded 20% for some metals in batch B2G0390 (flag R).
- Wells MW-5 and MW-6 went dry during purging, requiring recovery before sampling, potentially affecting representativeness.

Despite these issues, the analytical batch was validated by laboratory control samples, and the overall conclusions—that the soil stockpiles have not caused significant groundwater degradation beyond minor exceedances of vanadium, nitrate, and TDS—remain consistent with historical data. The report includes detailed cation-anion balances, Stiff diagrams, and reference to the GeoTracker database.
