The document is a laboratory and field report for the Caltrans Modesto soil stockpiles stormwater project (S1200-01-01) in Stanislaus County, California, focused on surface water sampling and analysis conducted under DTSC-approved SAP protocols, with a key event on December 15, 2016 during a rainfall of about 0.45 inch. The purpose was to determine whether stormwater runoff from Stockpiles 1–3 was migrating offsite and to characterize water chemistry, including dissolved metals and general mineral parameters, in samples collected from PL1–PL5 and background locations BG1–BG2. Field observations indicated that runoff remained within the Caltrans right-of-way, and no offsite migration was observed.

Sampling was performed using disposable bailers and a suction pump, with 0.45-micron field filtration for dissolved metals and chain-of-custody transfer to the laboratory. Field measurements included pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, turbidity, and oxidation-reduction potential, though dissolved oxygen was not measured because of instrument malfunction. Laboratory analyses included dissolved metals by EPA 6020/7470A, anions by EPA 300.0, sulfide by SM 4500-S-D, alkalinity by SM 2320B, TDS by SM 2540C, and TSS by SM 2540D. The report also includes detailed QA/QC documentation, showing that the methods were generally within acceptance criteria, with a few noted exceptions such as some dilution-related issues and isolated QC flags.

Overall, the analytical results were consistent across the sampling events and generally showed low concentrations for many regulated toxic metals, with several analytes such as arsenic, cadmium, lead, and selenium often at or below reporting limits. Measurable common ions and minerals included calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium, strontium, and vanadium, along with dissolved metals such as barium, chromium, copper, manganese, molybdenum, nickel, and zinc. Manganese was the only analyte reported to exceed a secondary MCL in some samples, specifically in PL3, PL4, and BG1, while no other reported dissolved metal or general mineral concentrations exceeded applicable primary or secondary MCLs. Example chemistry from the December 15, 2016 event showed low chloride, nitrate, sulfate, TDS, and sulfide values relative to drinking-water standards, reinforcing the conclusion that the stormwater chemistry was generally stable and not indicative of significant offsite impact.
