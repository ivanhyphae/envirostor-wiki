## Combined Summary of "raw/S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles Dec 2016 SW Sampling_01.17.pdf"

This report presents surface water analytical results from stormwater sampling at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles in Stanislaus County, California, conducted during a qualifying rain event (0.45 inch) on December 15, 2016. Samples were collected from six locations (PL1–PL4, and background sites BG1 and BG2); location PL5 was dry and not sampled. The primary objective was to assess whether stormwater runoff migrated offsite, with results indicating no evidence of such migration.

Concentrations of dissolved metals (Title 22 metals analyzed by EPA Method 6020/7470A) and general minerals (chloride, nitrate, sulfate by EPA 300.0; alkalinity by SM 2320B; total dissolved solids by SM 2540C; total suspended solids by SM 2540D; total sulfide by SM 4500-S²⁻ D) were measured. Field parameters included pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, and oxidation-reduction potential (ORP); dissolved oxygen was not measured due to a malfunctioning meter.

Most analytical results fell within historical ranges and below California primary and secondary maximum contaminant levels (MCLs). Notable exceptions were manganese at locations PL3, PL4, and background BG1, which exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L. Other metals detected included antimony, arsenic, barium, chromium, copper, lead, zinc, calcium, magnesium, sodium, strontium, potassium, and several others, all generally at low concentrations. General minerals such as chloride (1.4–7.6 mg/L), sulfate (2.0–19 mg/L), nitrate as N (0.21–1.9 mg/L), total alkalinity (22–77 mg/L as CaCO₃), TDS (67–420 mg/L), and TSS (76–1100 mg/L) were reported. Mercury was not detected.

Quality control (QC) procedures included blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), duplicates, matrix spikes (MS), and matrix spike duplicates (MSD). Most QC metrics met acceptance criteria (80–120% recovery; RPD ≤20%). Exceedances were flagged, including some matrix spike recoveries outside limits (e.g., calcium MS recovery of 13.2%) and RPD values exceeding 20% (e.g., sulfate RPD 26.5%). These anomalies were attributed to high analyte concentrations requiring dilutions, and LCS performance validated overall data quality. Certain results were flagged as estimated concentrations (J-flag) when detected below the practical quantitation limit but above the method detection limit. Reporting limits varied by analyte and batch, and not all parameters were analyzed in every sampling round due to dry conditions at some locations.

Key concepts addressed include dissolved metals, secondary MCLs, general minerals, field parameters, QA/QC, stormwater management, and Caltrans right-of-way compliance.
