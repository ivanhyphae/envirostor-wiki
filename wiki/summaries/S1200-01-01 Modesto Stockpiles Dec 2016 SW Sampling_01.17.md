## Key Claims

This document reports on a routine surface water sampling event conducted on December 15, 2016, at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Project S1200-01-01) in Stanislaus County, California. The primary finding is that dissolved manganese concentrations in samples PL3 (220 µg/L), PL4 (53 µg/L), and background sample BG1 (140 µg/L) exceeded the secondary Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) of 50 µg/L. All other reported dissolved metals and general minerals were below their respective primary or secondary MCLs. The report also claims that no stormwater runoff was observed migrating off the Caltrans right-of-way during this event. QC data from the same sampling event indicate that most analytical methods met acceptance criteria, thus validating the reported results. Notable exceptions include matrix spike (MS) recoveries for total sulfide (68%) falling below the lower acceptance limit (70%) and relative percent differences (RPD) for chloride (42.4%) and sulfate (26.5%) exceeding the 20% limit, suggesting potential matrix interferences or sample heterogeneity for those analyses.

## Methodology

Surface water samples were collected from six designated locations (PL1, PL2, PL3, PL4, BG1, BG2) during a qualifying rain event (0.45 inches of rainfall). A fifth location (PL5) was dry and not sampled. Samples were collected using disposable bailers and a suction pump. Field parameters (pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, ORP) were measured in situ; dissolved oxygen (DO) was not measured due to equipment malfunction. Samples were field-filtered (0.45-micron) for dissolved metals analysis. All samples were delivered under chain-of-custody to Advanced Technology Laboratories, Inc., a California-certified laboratory.

The following analytical methods were used:
- **Dissolved metals and mercury**: EPA Method 6020 (ICP-MS) and EPA Method 7470A (cold vapor AA), prepared using EPA 3010A.
- **General minerals**: Chloride, nitrate as N, and sulfate by EPA Method 300.0 (ion chromatography); total sulfide by SM 4500-S²⁻-D; alkalinity (total, bicarbonate, carbonate) by SM 2320B; total dissolved solids (TDS) by SM 2540C; total suspended solids (TSS) by SM 2540D.

QC elements per batch included blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), duplicates, matrix spikes (MS), and matrix spike duplicates (MSD). Percent recoveries and RPDs were evaluated against laboratory acceptance criteria.

## Results

**Analytical Results (Main Report):**
- **Manganese**: Exceeded the secondary MCL (50 µg/L) in PL3 (220 µg/L), PL4 (53 µg/L), and BG1 (140 µg/L). BG2 (38 µg/L), PL1 (14 µg/L), and PL2 (32 µg/L) were below the MCL.
- **Barium**: Concentrations ranged from 20 µg/L (PL2, BG2) to 170 µg/L (PL3), all below the primary MCL of 1,000 µg/L.
- **Other dissolved metals**: Beryllium, cadmium, mercury, selenium, silver, and thallium were not detected above method detection limits (MDLs). Cobalt and lead were detected in PL3 (1.7 and 3.0 µg/L) and BG1 (1.2 and 1.2 µg/L).
- **General minerals**: None (chloride, sulfate, TDS, TSS, etc.) exceeded primary or secondary MCLs.
- **Field parameters**: High turbidity was recorded (PL1: 601 NTU; PL2: 429 NTU). pH was near neutral (7.1–8.1). ORP values were positive (195–230 mV) except PL2 (201 mV).

**Quality Control Results:**
- **LCS recoveries** fell within acceptance limits for all analytes tested (e.g., alkalinity 90.9%, TDS 102%, TSS 103%, chloride 95.0%, nitrate 96.6%, sulfate 93.5%, sulfide 114%, dissolved metals 86.9–100%, mercury 106%).
- **MS/MSD recoveries** were acceptable for most analytes, except:
  - Total sulfide: MS and MSD recoveries of 68.0% (limit 70–120%) – flagged as matrix interference (M1).
  - Dissolved metals (batch B7A0015): Several MS recoveries were outside limits (e.g., calcium MS1: 13.2%, calcium MS2: 199%, potassium MS2: 136%, sodium MS3: 67.1%) due to high native concentrations requiring dilution or exceeding quantitation ranges.
- **Duplicate precision (RPD)**: Acceptable for most analytes, except chloride (42.4%) and sulfate (26.5%) which exceeded the 20% limit – flagged for sample heterogeneity or analytical issues.

## Limitations

- **Missing DO data**: The dissolved oxygen meter malfunctioned; DO was not measured at any location.
- **Dry location**: No sample was collected from PL5.
- **QA/QC dilutions**: Laboratory notes indicate that samples were diluted for several target analytes (e.g., calcium, sodium, magnesium, potassium, barium) due to high concentrations, potentially affecting precision of those results.
- **QC outliers**: Sulfide MS recoveries (68%) and chloride/sulfate RPDs (42.4% and 26.5%) indicate possible matrix effects or sample heterogeneity, which may reduce confidence in the corresponding sample results.
- **Scope**: The report notes this is one of a series of sampling events; Caltrans planned additional sampling in 2017.
