**Combined Summary**

The document reports on surface water sampling conducted at Caltrans Modesto soil stockpiles (Project S1200-01-01) in Stanislaus County, California, with focus on a March 24, 2017 rain event and long-term trends (2013–2017). Samples were collected from stockpile puddles (PL1–PL3), a drainage basin outfall (PL4), and a gate valve area (PL5) after 1.21 inches of rain; background samples could not be taken due to insufficient water. Field parameters (pH, conductivity, turbidity, ORP, DO) were measured, and samples were field-filtered (0.45‑µm) for dissolved metals analysis. Laboratory methods included EPA 6020/7470A for metals, EPA 300.0 for anions, SM 2320B for alkalinity, SM 2540C/D for TDS/TSS, and SM 4500‑S⁻²⁻=D for sulfide. Quality control for the analytical batch showed acceptable LCS recoveries (e.g., alkalinity 96.5%) but some matrix spike recoveries (calcium, barium, selenium) and duplicate RPD values fell outside criteria; several results were J‑flagged (estimated between MDL and PQL) due to low-level detections or dilution requirements for matrix interference.

Key findings: all dissolved metal concentrations were below primary and secondary MCLs except manganese (elevated at PL2 and PL4). Barium decreased from historical levels (2,000 µg/L in 2006) to low µg/L in 2017. Representative PL1 results (µg/L): antimony 2.4, arsenic 1.4, barium 63, copper 19, zinc 9.1 (J); common anions were low (chloride 3.8 mg/L, sulfate 12 mg/L). No stormwater runoff migrated beyond Caltrans right‑of‑way. Limitations included reliance on ponded water rather than direct runoff, a closed gate valve in the drainage basin, seasonal dry sampling events, and estimated values for some analytes. Overall, the data align with previous sampling rounds and indicate no exceedances of drinking water standards except for manganese at two locations.
