The document is a laboratory and field report for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles project in Stanislaus County, California, covering stormwater/surface water sampling and QC analyses associated with the March 24, 2017 rain event. Sampling at the stockpiles was conducted under DTSC-approved protocols to evaluate whether runoff was leaving the Caltrans right-of-way and whether dissolved metals or other inorganic constituents were present at concentrations exceeding California drinking-water standards. The event occurred during a qualifying storm totaling about 1.21 inches of rainfall over March 20–26, 2017.

Field sampling was completed at five monitoring points, PL1–PL5; background locations BG1 and BG2 were dry and could not be sampled. Samples were collected using disposable bailers and a suction pump, field-filtered for dissolved metals through 0.45-micron filters, and submitted under chain-of-custody to Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL). Analyses included dissolved metals by EPA 6020/7470A, chloride/nitrate/sulfate by EPA 300.0, sulfide by SM 4500-S-D, alkalinity by SM 2320B, TDS by SM 2540C, and TSS by SM 2540D, with QC checks using blanks, LCSs, duplicates, matrix spikes, and MSDs.

Overall, the results showed low to moderate mineralization, with the highest dissolved solids and major-ion concentrations generally in PL1 and lower values at the other stations. A range of dissolved metals was detected, including antimony, barium, calcium, chromium, cobalt, copper, magnesium, molybdenum, nickel, potassium, sodium, strontium, vanadium, arsenic, cadmium, lead, manganese, selenium, and zinc, though most concentrations were low and many were non-detect or estimated near detection limits. Aside from manganese, no reported concentrations exceeded applicable MCLs/secondary MCLs in the summarized results. The report concludes that stormwater stayed within the Caltrans right-of-way during the sampling event and that runoff control features were functioning as designed. Analytical patterns were generally consistent with prior sampling events dating back to 2013, and QC results indicated the laboratory data met acceptance criteria overall with only minor flagged exceptions handled appropriately.
