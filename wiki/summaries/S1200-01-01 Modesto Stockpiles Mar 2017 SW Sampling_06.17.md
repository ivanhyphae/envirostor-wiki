The document reports on surface water samples collected on March 24, 2017, during a qualifying rain event from Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (originating from 1960s SR 99 excavation) in Stanislaus County, California. The primary objective was to assess water quality against primary and secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs). Samples were collected from five locations (PL1–PL5) using disposable bailers and a suction pump; background sites could not be sampled due to insufficient water. Field parameters (pH, conductivity, turbidity, DO, ORP) were measured on-site.

Analytical results, performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ELAP 1838), included dissolved metals (EPA 6020/7470A), anions (EPA 300.0), sulfide (SM 4500-S-D), alkalinity (SM 2320B), TDS (SM 2540C), and TSS (SM 2540D). A separate quality control (QC) section validated these methods, showing most QC parameters (blanks, LCS, matrix spikes, duplicates) met acceptance criteria. Notable exceptions included some matrix spike recoveries outside limits (flagged M1) for certain metals (e.g., calcium, magnesium, manganese, strontium) and occasional RPD exceedances (flagged R). Several analyte detections below the Practical Quantitation Limit but above the Method Detection Limit were reported as estimated concentrations (flagged J). Some samples required dilution due to high analyte concentrations or matrix interferences.

Key findings: Dissolved metals and general minerals did not exceed primary or secondary MCLs, except manganese which exceeded its secondary MCL (50 µg/L) at locations PL2 (79 µg/L) and PL4 (110 µg/L). No stormwater runoff was observed migrating offsite; runoff appeared retained within the site boundaries. Results were consistent with previous sampling events (2013–2016). Limitations included the inability to collect background samples, reliance on standing water rather than flowing runoff for some locations, and QC flags indicating matrix-specific effects and estimated concentrations.
