The document is the **Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) – Interim Phase 1** for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. (January 30, 2019). Its purpose is to detail the technical approach for implementing the Final Remedial Action Plan to support SR-132 West construction, primarily addressing barium-contaminated soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1–3) and secondary aerially deposited lead (ADL)-contaminated soil from highway shoulder excavations.

**Regulatory Background:** The RDIP received **conditional approval from DTSC** (August and October 2018) following approval of the Final RAP (March 2018). DTSC had previously determined (December 2009) that the stockpiles pose no unacceptable risk under current management, requiring continued monitoring, access restrictions, and institutional controls. The Central Valley RWQCB serves as co-regulator, and DTSC’s **ADL Soil Management Agreement** (June 2016) governs handling of lead-contaminated soil.

**Key Claims:** Barium is the primary contaminant of concern, with two distinct solubility forms present. Containment is deemed protective of human health and the environment, technically feasible, and cost-effective. A 2007/2013 HHRA concluded stockpiles pose no unacceptable risk to offsite residents, trespassers, or workers. Air monitoring during prior Stockpile 3 excavation showed airborne barium and lead levels well below regulatory thresholds, and groundwater data (2014) indicate no measurable impact from the stockpiles.

**Analytical Results:** Verification thresholds are set at **1,000 mg/kg for barium** (based on SFRWQCB Tier 1 ESL adjusted via a 2018 Tech Memo) and **80 mg/kg for lead** (DTSC HERO Note 3 residential). Background barium averaged 75 mg/kg. For ADL soil, low-risk fill criteria include ≤1.5 mg/L WET-soluble lead and ≤1,411 mg/kg total lead. A **Fence Line Action Level** for total dust is 4 mg/m³. DI-WET barium data show distinct groupings (<50 mg/L and ~200 mg/L), with Stockpile 2 exhibiting elevated values.

**Remedial Actions:** **Interim Phase 1** involves removal and re-deposition of portions of the stockpiles (barium-containing soil) and placement of ADL-contaminated soil in consolidation zones within the state right-of-way. Stockpile 1 will receive consolidation fill on its upper surface and southern slope (15,000 yd³) and State ROW (12,500 yd³) without removal. Verification through ongoing groundwater and stormwater monitoring is required, along with five-year reviews of interim and final containment effectiveness.

**Health and Safety:** A **negative exposure assessment** based on 2012 air monitoring (Stockpile 3 excavation) documented airborne lead and barium below Cal/OSHA PELs and Action Levels; thus, medical surveillance is not required. Dust control via wet methods is the primary control. The Health and Safety Plan specifies Level D protection with possible upgrade to Level C, decontamination procedures (TSP/Alconox wash plus deionized water rinse), and emergency response protocols. The plan provides substance-specific safety data for cadmium, inorganic lead, and barium sulfate (nuisance dust, PEL 10 mg/m³).

**Responsible Parties:** **Caltrans** is the owner/operator; **DTSC** is the primary regulator; **Central Valley RWQCB** is co-regulator; **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** is the implementing consultant.
