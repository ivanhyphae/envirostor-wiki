**Document Summary: Groundwater Monitoring Report – Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project (March 2023)**

**Document Identity and Purpose**
This document is a Groundwater Monitoring Report prepared by Stantec Consulting Services Inc. under Caltrans Contract No. 06A2542, Task Order No. 97. Dated March 8, 2023, it summarizes semi-annual gauging events (November/December 2022) and the annual groundwater sampling event (February 21, 2023) for the Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project in Stanislaus County, California, along SR 132.

**Key Findings and Analytical Results**
No measurable groundwater was present in any of the four active monitoring wells (MW-4, MW-6, MW-9, MW-10) during the gauging events, and all wells were dry on the sampling date. Consequently, no groundwater samples were collected, and no analytical results are reported for this period. The persistent dry conditions (noted as "insufficient water in well" for many wells on prior dates) are a recurring theme. Historical data (2006–2023) from earlier reports shows that the primary contaminant of concern is dissolved barium, with concentrations ranging from approximately 34 µg/L to 410 µg/L, and one exceedance of 3.1 mg/L in MW-10 (June 2013). Other monitored parameters include Title 22 metals, general minerals, and PAHs.

**Remedial Actions and Network Changes**
The project involves the encapsulation of Stockpiles 1 and 2 beneath the SR 132 structural section, completed in October 2021. SR 132 opened to traffic in November 2022. Prior to this reporting period, six monitoring wells (MW-1, MW-2, MW-3, MW-5, MW-7, MW-8) were decommissioned to accommodate highway construction, reducing the monitoring network to the four wells listed above. Well MW-10’s box was destroyed during site work and subsequently replaced by the contractor.

**Responsible Parties and Regulatory Oversight**
- **Owner/Operator:** California Department of Transportation (Caltrans)
- **Consultant/Preparer:** Stantec Consulting Services Inc. (with historical data from Geocon Consultants, Inc., Shaw Environmental, Inc., and GeoTrans Inc.)
- **Regulatory Oversight:** California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)

**Key Terms and Conditions**
- **Monitoring Obligations:** Annual groundwater sampling and semi-annual depth-to-water measurements are required.
- **Next Events:** The next gauging event is scheduled for Fall 2023; next sampling event for Spring 2024.
- **Data Management:** The report is to be uploaded to the GeoTracker database (State Water Resources Control Board).
- **Well Specifications:** The active wells have 2-inch PVC casing, with total depths ranging from approximately 39.6 to 43.8 feet. Historical hydraulic gradient is 0.0006 to 0.004, with flow direction to the south-southeast.
