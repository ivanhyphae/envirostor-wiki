**Document Identity and Scope**
This document is a **Groundwater Monitoring Report** for the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles** site, prepared by **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** for the **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6**. The report covers the March 2013 monitoring event at a site located south of the SR 99 and Kansas Avenue intersection in Stanislaus County, California. The monitoring program, overseen by the **Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)** , focuses on ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10).

**Key Findings and Analytical Results**
The March 2013 sampling indicates that groundwater quality is largely stable. The primary chemicals of concern (COCs), **barium** and **strontium**, remain at levels consistent with historical data and well below regulatory thresholds.

- **Dissolved Metals:** No dissolved metals exceeded their respective Primary or Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), with two exceptions: **manganese** exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L in wells MW-1 (160 µg/L) and MW-7 (260 µg/L). Other key metals like arsenic, chromium, and lead were all detected below their MCLs.
- **General Minerals:** Several wells show elevated levels of **nitrate as nitrogen** and **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)** . Nitrate exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10, and TDS met or exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in the same four wells. The report notes that the concentrations in upgradient well MW-1 (13 mg/L nitrate, 500 mg/L TDS) may represent natural background conditions.
- **Groundwater Flow:** The flow direction is toward the east-southeast at an average gradient of 0.0006, consistent with historical patterns.

**Regulatory Status and Remedial Actions**
This report is a compliance monitoring document and contains no formal regulatory decisions, such as site closure or new permit conditions.

- **Completed Actions:** With DTSC concurrence, analysis for polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) was discontinued after the November 2012 sampling event.
- **Planned Actions:** Well MW-5 is scheduled for redevelopment during the June 2013 monitoring event due to consistent issues with dry-purging, high turbidity, and potential impacts on sample representativeness.

**Data Quality and Validation**
Analytical data were provided by **Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL)** . The laboratory QA/QC was deemed acceptable, with no data qualification required. Some minor laboratory anomalies were noted and addressed; for instance, low-level chromium detected in an equipment blank was determined not to have significantly influenced sample results. A split sample analysis performed by the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB) was also included. Electronic data was uploaded to the GeoTracker database on May 16, 2013. The next quarterly monitoring event was scheduled for June 2013.
