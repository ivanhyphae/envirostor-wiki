## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project (the "Site"). Located south of the intersection of Kansas Avenue with State Route (SR) 99 in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. The report covers the entire project area, including Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3, and the SR 132 roadway embankment.
- **Media addressed:** Surface water (stormwater runoff). Inspection included the road embankment, BMPs, and drainage infrastructure.
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:**
    - New surface water sampling locations: PL6, PL7
    - New background sampling location: BG3
    - Legacy locations (not sampled in this event): PL1–PL5, BG1, BG2
    - Areas of erosion: East side of stormwater basin 5, and north side of Stockpile 1.

## Key claims
- **Claim:** No discharge or runoff of surface water beyond the Best Management Practices (BMPs) and/or the Site was observed.
    - **Who:** Stantec (as the inspector).
    - **Support:** The claim is supported by field observations on the day of inspection (February 28, 2023). It is a site-specific observation from a single event.
- **Claim:** The rain event that occurred overnight (Feb 27-28) was limited, resulting in no standing surface water from which to collect samples.
    - **Who:** Stantec.
    - **Support:** Supported by a measurement of 0.09 inches of precipitation on the day of the inspection.

## Regulatory decisions
No formal agency decisions (e.g., approvals, denials) are contained in this document. The report is a deliverable under an existing contract and approved Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP). It notes that prior sampling locations (PL1-PL5, BG1, BG2) were approved by DTSC and CVRWQCB.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured:** None. The report explicitly states, "no surface water samples were collected" due to insufficient rainfall and the absence of standing surface water. Therefore, no analytical results are presented.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** The report summarizes past and completed work:
    - **Excavation and consolidation:** Soil from former Stockpile 3 was excavated, transported, and placed on Stockpiles 1 and 2 as part of Phase I of the SR 99/132 expressway earthwork (begun January 2020).
    - **Capping/Encapsulation:** The majority of Stockpiles 1 and 2 were completely encapsulated beneath the structural section of SR 132 and with clean soil and vegetation on the northern side slopes (completed October 2021).
    - **Construction:** SR 132 was opened to traffic (November 2022).
- **Phase status:** These remedial actions are described as completed.

## Key terms and conditions
- **Deadlines/Required Follow-on:** The report is a scheduled deliverable under Task Order 97. It does not establish new deadlines. It implies that future surface water inspections and sampling will be triggered by qualifying rain events.
- **Monitoring Obligations:** The inspection was performed in accordance with protocols approved by DTSC as established in the *Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP)* (Shaw, Jan 2006) and the *Addendum to Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP Addendum)* (Geocon, Feb 2013).
- **Permit Conditions:** The report references a gate valve on the stormwater drainage basin. The valve could only be opened if the basin was near capacity, noting that releasing water would go to the Tuolumne River.
