Hereis a structured summary of the document suitable for a case-file wiki.

## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Comment response letter from Geocon Consultants to Caltrans, prepared to address and incorporate comments from DTSC (and RWQCB) on the Draft Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles. The letter serves as a transmittal and summary of revisions leading to the Final Workplan dated September 18, 2012.
- **Author or preparer:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG, Project Manager; Rebecca L. Silva, Senior Scientist).
- **Date or period covered:** September 18, 2012 (letter date). References review of DTSC comments dated September 12, 2012, and a site survey on September 17, 2012.

## Key claims
- **Site origin and primary contaminants (attributed to DTSC September 12, 2012 letter):** The soil stockpiles are excess native soils and pond tailings from 1960s SR 99 construction. The source property was purchased from Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation Inc. (FMC), which processed barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores. The primary chemicals of concern (COCs) are barium, lead, and strontium.
- **Rationale for boring depths (Geocon response):** Fenceline borings (5 ft) and Perimeter borings (3 ft) target near-surface migration at stockpile boundaries; Cadmium borings (20 ft) target deeper contamination in areas of elevated historical cadmium detections.
- **Sampling methodology (Geocon clarification, affirmed by DTSC):** Continuous coring with acetate sleeves will be used for all boring categories. Surface samples (0-6 inches) will be collected from all borings. Samples from deeper intervals are collected per boring type (e.g., 1-ft intervals to 5 ft, or approximate 5-ft intervals for Cadmium borings).

## Regulatory decisions
- **Action:** DTSC (with RWQCB consultation) issued comments on the Draft Workplan on September 12, 2012.
- **Agency:** DTSC (Randy S. Adams, C.E.G.); co-regulator RWQCB Central Valley Region.
- **Conditions imposed/Addressed:** All DTSC requests have been addressed in the Final Workplan. Key conditions include containing and analyzing decontamination rinse water, including discretionary borings for erosion features, and separating the Summary Report from other reports (Nature & Extent, Human Health Risk Assessment).

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants of Interest:** Barium, lead, strontium (primary COCs); cadmium, mercury, Title 22 metals (for specific boring categories).
- **Media:** Soil (soil stockpiles and subsurface soil).
- **Laboratory Methods:** EPA Method 6010B (metals) and Method 7471 (mercury).
- **Reporting Requirements:** DTSC required soluble metals analysis with Method Detection Limit (MDL) and Practical Quantitation Limit (PQL). All concentrations between MDL and PQL must be reported with a "J-flag" as an estimated value. Detection limits must be suitable for human health risk assessment and water quality goal comparison.

## Remedial actions
- **Physical work described (planned in Final Workplan):** Supplemental site investigation. Activities include advancing direct-push borings (Fenceline, Perimeter, Cadmium categories) for soil sampling; potential hand-auger borings in inaccessible areas; and analyzing soil samples for metals. A site Health and Safety Plan (HSP) is appended.
- **Phase status:** Planned; workplan approved. The investigation is to provide additional characterization to evaluate and finalize a remedy.

## Responsible parties
- **Owner/Operator:** California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) — District 6 (Fresno) and District 10 (Stockton).
- **Operator (Contractor):** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (preparing workplans and leading investigation).
- **Regulatory Agencies:**
    - Lead: DTSC (Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program).
    - Co-regulator: Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB).
- **Historical Property Owner:** Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation Inc. (FMC) — source of contamination on original parcel.

## Key terms and conditions
- **Rinse Water Management:** All equipment decontamination rinse water must be contained, sampled/analyzed for metals, and appropriately disposed of (not discharged to stockpile surface).
- **Report Separation:** The Summary Report must be limited to the findings of the Supplemental Site Investigation. Separate reports are required for an "Updated Site Characterization Summary Report" and an "Updated Human Health Risk Assessment."
- **Data Quality:** Laboratory reports must include both MDL and PQL. Detection limits must be suitable for risk assessment. Estimated values (between MDL and PQL) must be flagged with "J."
- **Dust Control:** A site-specific Health and Safety Plan (HSP) must be included as an appendix, including measures to control dust from drilling activities (e.g., wet sampling methods).
