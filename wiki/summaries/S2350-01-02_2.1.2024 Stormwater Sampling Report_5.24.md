## Key claims
- Storm water samples collected on February 1, 2024 from two storm water discharge locations (SW-East, SW-West) and one background location (BG-West) at the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles site in Modesto, California.
- All measured constituents (total dissolved solids, sulfate, and dissolved Title 22 metals) were below their respective Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for drinking water, except for thallium which exceeded the primary MCL in prior sampling (December 2023) but was not detected in the February 2024 event.
- No discharge/runoff from the Caltrans right-of-way was observed during the February 1, 2024 sampling event.
- The encapsulated stockpiles (Stockpiles 1 and 2) are effectively containing contaminants, as storm water concentrations are low and do not exceed regulatory thresholds.

## Methodology
- **Experimental setup**: Storm water samples were collected from three designated locations (SW-East, SW-West, and BG-West) on February 1, 2024, during a qualifying rain event (3.02 inches total rainfall, starting January 31, 2024). Sampling followed protocols approved by DTSC and CVRWQCB as established in the Storm Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP) dated December 13, 2023.
- **Sample collection**: Samples were collected using a pressure bailer with suction pump, transferred to laboratory‑provided containers. Samples for dissolved metals analysis were field‑filtered through 0.45‑micron filters. QA/QC included disposable bailers, chain‑of‑custody documentation, and laboratory QC (method blanks, LCS, LCSD, MS, MSD).
- **Analytical methods**:  
  - Total dissolved solids (TDS): EPA Method 160.1 (SM2540C).  
  - Sulfate: EPA Method 300.0/9056.  
  - Dissolved Title 22 metals (including barium, strontium, zinc, copper, thallium, etc.): EPA Method 6010B/6020 (ICP) and EPA 7470A (mercury by cold vapor).
- **Dataset**: Three surface water samples (SW-East, SW-West, BG-West) collected on February 1, 2024, plus comparative data from previous events (March 2023, December 2023).
- **Evaluation metrics**: Concentrations compared to federal and California primary and secondary MCLs for drinking water.

## Results
- **TDS**: SW-East = 88 mg/L, SW-West = 63 mg/L, BG-West = 42 mg/L (all below secondary MCL of 500 mg/L).
- **Sulfate**: SW-East = <2.0 mg/L (non‑detect), SW-West = 3.6 mg/L, BG-West = 6.5 mg/L (all below secondary MCL of 250 mg/L).
- **Dissolved metals (selected)**:
  - *Barium*: SW-East = 0.90 mg/L, SW-West = 0.52 mg/L, BG-West = 0.010 mg/L (MCL = 1.0 mg/L).
  - *Zinc*: SW-East = 0.025 mg/L, SW-West = 0.24 mg/L, BG-West = 0.018 mg/L (secondary MCL = 5.0 mg/L).
  - *Copper*: SW-East = 0.013 mg/L (MCL = 1.3 mg/L), not detected in other samples.
  - *Strontium*: SW-East = 0.18 mg/L, SW-West = 0.078 mg/L (no MCL established).
  - *Thallium*: Not detected in any sample (reporting limit 0.015 mg/L; MCL = 0.002 mg/L, note: previous December 2023 samples exceeded MCL).
  - *Mercury*: Not detected in all samples.
  - All other Title 22 metals (antimony, arsenic, beryllium, cadmium, chromium, cobalt, lead, molybdenum, nickel, selenium, silver, vanadium) were below detection limits.
- **QA/QC**: Laboratory QC showed acceptable surrogate recoveries, non‑detect method blanks, and acceptable LCS/LCSD recoveries.

## Limitations
- The report notes that MCLs are drinking water standards and are used as screening benchmarks; storm water is not intended for potable use.
- Strontium was detected but no MCL or screening level has been established for this analyte.
- Some results were reported with J‑flags (estimated concentrations between MDL and PQL) or J+ (biased high estimates).
- The sampling event represents a single point in time; results may not be representative of all storm events.
- No runoff migration from the Caltrans right-of-way was observed, but this does not guarantee long‑term containment.
- Historical thallium exceedances were observed in December 2023 but not in February 2024; variability may require continued monitoring.
