## Combined Summary: Stormwater Sampling and Analytical QC Report (February 1, 2024)

### Key Claims

- Stormwater samples collected on February 1, 2024, from three locations (SW‑East, SW‑West, BG‑West) at the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles site (Modesto) showed concentrations of total dissolved solids (TDS), sulfate, and all detected dissolved metals (barium, zinc, copper, strontium) below primary or secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for drinking water.
- Thallium, which had exceeded the primary MCL in December 2023 samples, was not detected in the February 2024 samples.
- No discharge or runoff migrated beyond the Caltrans right‑of‑way, indicating effective containment.
- Analytical QC results confirmed that all method blanks were non‑detect, laboratory control samples (LCS) met acceptance criteria for TDS, sulfate, dissolved metals, and dissolved mercury, and matrix spike (MS/MSD) recoveries and precision (RPD) were within acceptable limits for all analytes, validating the accuracy of the reported data.

### Methodology

**Sampling:** Samples were collected on February 1, 2024, during a rain event (total ~3.02 inches, Jan 31–Feb 8). Locations: SW‑East (stormwater discharge north of SR 132 west of SR 99), SW‑West (Ponding Basin 5, north of SR 132 east of N. Carpenter Road), BG‑West (background drop inlet along N. Carpenter Road, ~700 ft south of Kansas Avenue). Samples were collected using a pressure bailer with suction pump and field‑filtered through 0.45‑micron filters for dissolved metals analysis.

**Laboratory Analyses (ASSET Laboratories, California‑certified):**
- TDS: EPA Method 160.1 / SM 2540C  
- Sulfate: EPA Method 9056 / 300.0 (ion chromatography)  
- Dissolved metals (Title 22 suite, including barium, strontium, zinc, copper, thallium): EPA Method 6020/6010B (ICP) after EPA 3010A preparation  
- Dissolved mercury: EPA Method 7470A (cold vapor technique)

**QA/QC Procedures:** Disposable bailers, chain‑of‑custody documentation, temperature verification (2.4 °C at receipt). QC samples per batch included method blanks (MBLK), laboratory control samples (LCS), LCS duplicates, matrix spikes (MS), matrix spike duplicates (MSD), and sample duplicates (DUP). Instrumentation: IR gun, ICP, cold vapor unit.

### Results

**Field Results (Stormwater Samples):**
- **TDS:** SW‑East 88 mg/L, SW‑West 63 mg/L, BG‑West 42 mg/L (secondary MCL = 500 mg/L).  
- **Sulfate:** SW‑West 3.6 mg/L, BG‑West 6.5 mg/L (secondary MCL = 250 mg/L); not detected at SW‑East.  
- **Barium:** Detected in all (0.90 mg/L SW‑East, 0.52 mg/L SW‑West, 0.010 mg/L BG‑West; MCL = 1.0 mg/L).  
- **Zinc:** Detected in all (0.025, 0.24, 0.018 mg/L; secondary MCL = 5 mg/L).  
- **Copper:** Detected only at SW‑East (0.013 mg/L; MCL = 1.3 mg/L).  
- **Strontium:** Detected at SW‑East (0.18 mg/L) and SW‑West (0.078 mg/L); no MCL established.  
- **Thallium:** Not detected (≤0.015 mg/L) at any location.  
- All other Title 22 metals were either not detected or below MCLs.

**Analytical QC Results:**
- **Method blanks:** All analytes ND across all batches.  
- **TDS:** LCS recovery 97.9% (limits 80–120%); duplicate RPD 3.11% (limit 10%).  
- **Sulfate:** LCS recovery 102% (limits 90–110%); MS recovery 97.0%, MSD recovery 95.4%; MS/MSD RPD 0.916% (limit 15%).  
- **Dissolved metals (6010B):** LCS recoveries 89.7% (Pb) to 110% (Ag) (limits 85–115%); MS recoveries 85.6% (Pb) to 125% (Ag) (limits 75–125%); MS/MSD RPDs all ≤2.03% (limit 20%).  
- **Dissolved mercury (7470A):** LCS recovery 101% (limits 85–115%); MS recovery 96.8%, MSD recovery 96.0%; MS/MSD RPD 0.830% (limit 20%); sample duplicate RPD 0%.

### Limitations

- Some dissolved metal results carry J‑flags (estimated concentrations between MDL and PQL) or UJ flags (estimated non‑detects), indicating potential uncertainty.  
- A single event cannot capture temporal variability; past events (Dec 2023) showed thallium exceedances not observed here.  
- Strontium has no MCL, limiting risk assessment.  
- Matrix interference was noted for some analytes in previous samples; QA/QC data for this event showed acceptable surrogate recoveries and blanks, but a note flagged “Spike/Surrogate outside of limits due to matrix interference” and “Surrogate Diluted Out” (no specific non‑conformance in detailed data).  
- The sample receipt checklist indicated custody seals were not present on the cooler or sample bottles, though chain of custody was signed.  
- No runoff migration was observed, but long‑term monitoring is required to confirm continued containment.
