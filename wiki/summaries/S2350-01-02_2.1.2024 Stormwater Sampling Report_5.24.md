Geocon, on behalf of Caltrans, conducted stormwater sampling for the Modesto Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles / Stockpiles Stormwater project (S2350-01-02, Work Order N062854) on February 1, 2024, during a rain event that began January 31 and produced about 3.02 inches of rainfall. Sampling followed the approved Storm Water Sampling and Analysis Plan and included three locations: SW-East, SW-West, and BG-West. No runoff or discharge from the Caltrans right-of-way was observed during the event. Samples were collected with a pressure bailer and suction pump, field-filtered for dissolved metals, preserved and shipped under chain of custody to ASSET Laboratories, and analyzed using standard methods for dissolved metals, mercury, sulfate, and total dissolved solids. The report also includes prior sampling results from December 2023 and March 2023 for comparison.

Overall, the analytical results showed relatively low dissolved solids and sulfate, with most constituents at non-detect or low concentrations and generally below applicable drinking-water screening levels. For the February 1, 2024 event, total dissolved solids ranged from 42 mg/L at BG-West to 88 mg/L at SW-East, and sulfate ranged from non-detect at SW-East to 3.6 mg/L at SW-West and 6.5 mg/L at BG-West; none exceeded secondary MCLs. Metals were mostly non-detect or low: barium and zinc were detected in all samples but did not exceed MCLs, copper was detected at SW-East below its primary MCL, and strontium was detected at SW-East and SW-West but has no MCL. The report notes that a prior December 2023 sample had thallium above the primary MCL, but no comparable exceedance is reported for the February 2024 sampling event.

A substantial portion of the document is dedicated to laboratory QA/QC. Method blanks were generally clean, laboratory control samples met acceptance criteria, and matrix spike, matrix spike duplicate, and duplicate results were largely within control limits. Reported qualifiers and minor QC issues did not indicate a widespread analytical problem. In short, the report presents a quality-controlled stormwater dataset showing that conditions at the three sampling locations were similar in overall pattern, with no major exceedances in the February 2024 event and laboratory performance that supports the reported results.
