# Combined Summary of Source Document "raw/S9525-06-44A Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles GW Nov2012 0213.pdf"

## Document identity
- **Document type:** Groundwater Monitoring Report (November 2012), including a Certificate of Analysis (laboratory report from Advanced Technology Laboratories), quality control documentation, and stiff diagrams (Appendix C).
- **Preparer / author organization:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (prepared the main report and coordinated sampling); Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL), Signal Hill, CA, performed all chemical analyses (ELAP 1838).
- **Recipient / addressee agency or party:** Mr. Richard Stewart, PG, California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch.
- **Date or reporting period covered:** November 28–29, 2012 (sample collection); report dated February 2013; laboratory report issued December 11, 2012.
- **Project number or task order:** Geocon Project No. S9525-06-44A, Task Order No. 44, EA 10-403500.

## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Stanislaus County, California, southerly of the intersection of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue. Three stockpiles (1, 2, and 3) are present. Also referenced as SR 132 area.
- **Media addressed:** Groundwater.
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** Monitoring wells MW-1 through MW-10 (ten wells), all sampled via groundwater. One equipment blank (Eq. Blank) was also collected.

## Key claims
- **Claim 1 – No PAHs detected:** No polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) were reported at concentrations equal to or greater than their practical quantitation limits (PQLs) in any sample. This is supported by analytical results for all 17 target SVOCs (PQL = 0.20 µg/L) showing non-detect in all ten wells. Surrogate recoveries were within acceptance limits. This finding led to a request to discontinue PAH analysis.
- **Claim 2 – Dissolved metals generally below thresholds:** With the exception of manganese in MW-8 (160 µg/L, exceeding the Secondary MCL of 50 µg/L), no reported dissolved metals concentrations exceeded their respective numeric water quality threshold values. Specific data: Barium ranged from 60 (MW-3) to 300 (MW-5) µg/L (MCL 1,000 µg/L); arsenic ranged 2.1–5.1 µg/L (MCL 10 µg/L); chromium ranged 0.60–8.0 µg/L (MCL 50 µg/L); lead not detected above 1.0 µg/L; vanadium ranged 18–38 µg/L (Notification Level 50 µg/L); strontium ranged 430–1,100 µg/L (EPA HA 4,000 µg/L); mercury was not detected in any well (PQL 0.20 µg/L). The equipment blank showed only sodium at 64 µg/L, which was not considered to have significantly influenced sample results.
- **Claim 3 – Nitrate and TDS exceedances:** Nitrate (as N) consistently exceeded the Primary MCL of 10 mg/L in five wells: MW-1 (12 mg/L), MW-5 (24 mg/L), MW-6 (18 mg/L), MW-8 (11 mg/L), and MW-10 (16 mg/L). Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) met or exceeded the Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in MW-4 (500 mg/L), MW-5 (640 mg/L), MW-6 (610 mg/L), and MW-10 (640 mg/L). The report notes that MW-1 is upgradient, suggesting background levels are partly responsible for these exceedances.
- **Claim 4 – Discontinuation of PAH analysis approved:** Based on review of historical data showing a lack of PAHs, Geocon used 2012 monitoring data to request discontinuation of PAH analysis. This request was approved by DTSC in November 2012. (PAHs were analyzed for this event but will be discontinued going forward.)

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type:** Approval of a request to modify monitoring obligations.
- **Deciding agency and date:** California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), November 2012.
- **Conditions or requirements imposed:** DTSC approved Geocon’s request to discontinue PAH analysis for future monitoring events. Groundwater monitoring is to continue every other month with the reduced analytical suite.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units:**
  - *SVOCs (PAHs) by EPA 8270/SIM:* All 17 target analytes non-detect in all wells (PQL 0.20 µg/L).
  - *Dissolved Metals by EPA 6020 (ICP-MS):*
    - Barium: 60 (MW-3) – 300 (MW-5) µg/L
    - Arsenic: 2.1 (MW-4) – 5.1 (MW-6) µg/L
    - Chromium: 0.60 (MW-10) – 8.0 (MW-6) µg/L
    - Lead: ND (PQL 1.0 µg/L) in all samples
    - Vanadium: 18 (MW-1, MW-4, MW-10) – 38 (MW-6) µg/L
    - Strontium: 430 (MW-3) – 1,100 (MW-1) µg/L
    - Manganese: detected in MW-4 (11 µg/L) and MW-8 (160 µg/L)
    - Mercury: ND (PQL 0.20 µg/L) in all wells
    - Additional cations (calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium, etc.) reported; some samples required dilution (D6 flag) due to high concentrations.
  - *Anions and general chemistry:*
    - Chloride: 5.7 – 30 mg/L
    - Nitrate as N: 2.8 (MW-3) – 24 (MW-5) mg/L
    - Sulfate: 12 – 98 mg/L
    - TDS: 340 (MW-7) – 640 (MW-5, MW-10) mg/L
    - Total Alkalinity (as CaCO₃): 220 – 390 mg/L
    - Sulfide: detected in MW-5 (0.090 mg/L), MW-6 (0.061), MW-7 (0.069), MW-10 (0.089); ND in MW-3, MW-8, MW-9.
- **Applicable thresholds:** California Primary MCLs, Secondary MCLs (including SMCL for TDS 500 mg/L, manganese 50 µg/L), EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories, and California Notification Levels (vanadium 50 µg/L) as identified in the main report.
- **Exceedances:** Manganese in MW-8 (160 µg/L) exceeded the Secondary MCL (50 µg/L). Nitrate exceeded the Primary MCL (10 mg/L) in five wells. TDS met or exceeded the Secondary MCL (500 mg/L) in four wells.
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations:**
  - Equipment blank: sodium detected at 64 µg/L, considered negligible.
  - Matrix spike (MS/MSD) recoveries: Several analytes (e.g., calcium, magnesium, sodium, vanadium, zinc, strontium) had recoveries outside 70–130% limits, attributed to matrix interference or high native concentrations. Laboratory control sample (LCS) recoveries were within 85–115% for all metals, supporting overall batch validity.
  - Duplicate analyses: RPD exceeded 20% for numerous analytes (antimony, barium, beryllium, chromium, etc.), primarily in high-concentration samples.
  - Dilution required (D6) and high-concentration flags (E) applied to several samples for major cations and barium.
  - Mercury QC (LCS, MS, MSD) all within acceptance limits (97–98% recovery).
  - No qualification of the data was considered necessary by the preparer; the analytical batch was validated by the LCS.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** Well purging and sampling (routine groundwater monitoring event).
- **Quantities:** Purged 1.5 to 6 gallons (3 well volumes) from most wells. MW-5 and MW-6 went dry after purging 0.75 and 1 gallon, respectively. Purged groundwater was transported to Geocon’s Rancho Cordova office and subsequently disposed at Inviro-tec Disposal facility.
- **Verification sampling outcomes:** Not applicable (routine monitoring, not verification).
- **Phase status:** Completed (routine monitoring event).
