### Document Identity
- **Document Type and Purpose:** Formal regulatory approval letter from the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) for an “Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data.” The letter documents DTSC and RWQCB concurrence with the report’s findings and authorizes the discontinuation of groundwater monitoring and decommissioning of monitoring wells.
- **Author or Preparer:** Dean Wright, P.G., Project Manager, Site Mitigation and Restoration Program, DTSC. The letter is addressed to Rebecca L. Silva of Geocon Consultants Inc.
- **Date or Period Covered:** Letter dated May 3, 2024. The evaluation report reviewed is dated February 26, 2024.

### Key Claims
- **Source:** Evaluation Report (Geocon Consultants Inc. for Caltrans, Feb 26, 2024) and this DTSC/RWQCB concurrence letter.
- **Claim (Primary):** The barium- and lead-impacted soil stockpiles beneath the State Route 132 Expressway are **not impacting** groundwater and will **not impact** groundwater in the future.
- **Basis for Claim:** The conclusion is supported by: (1) consistency with an earlier 2014 evaluation; (2) the recent encapsulation of the soil beneath the Expressway; and (3) the data presented in the February 2024 Evaluation Report.

### Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision Type:** Formal approval and concurrence with a recommendation to discontinue an active monitoring program.
- **Agency:** Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB).
- **Date:** May 3, 2024.
- **Decision:** DTSC and RWQCB **approve** the recommendation to discontinue groundwater monitoring beneath the soil stockpiles.
- **Condition(s):** The four remaining monitoring wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10) must be **decommissioned** according to the approved Remedial Design and Implementation Plan.

### Analytical Results
- **Contaminants of Concern:** Barium and Lead.
- **Media:** Groundwater (beneath the encapsulated soil stockpiles).
- **Concentration Ranges:** Not specified in this approval letter, but the letter states the data indicates **no impact** to groundwater. The report is a *comparative evaluation* (presumably comparing pre- and post-encapsulation data or against groundwater thresholds).
- **Thresholds/Standards:** Not explicitly stated in the letter, but the evaluation is for a “barium and lead impacted soil stockpile” site, indicating a cleanup or risk-based threshold was used. The evaluation confirms *no impact*, implying concentrations are below applicable thresholds (likely MCLs or site-specific risk levels).

### Remedial Actions
- **Activity:** Encapsulation of soil stockpiles beneath the State Route 132 Expressway.
- **Phase Status:** Post-construction (the evaluation is described as “the recent encapsulation of the soil beneath the Express Way,” indicating it is complete).
- **Verification Outcomes:** The evaluation confirms that the encapsulation is effective—the encapsulated soil is not impacting or expected to impact groundwater.
