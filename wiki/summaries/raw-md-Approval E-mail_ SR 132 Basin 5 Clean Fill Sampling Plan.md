Here is the structured summary of the document for the case-file wiki.

## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose:** E-mail chain serving as formal agency approval of the "Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan" (Plan). The Plan proposes sampling and analysis for soil to be used as cap material on the State Route 132 soil stockpiles.
- **Author or preparer:**
    - *Approval:* Dean Wright (DTSC).
    - *Requestor to DTSC:* Richard Stewart (Caltrans).
    - *Sampling Plan preparer:* John Juhrend (Geocon Consultants, Inc.).
- **Date or period covered:** June 11–12, 2020.

## Key claims
- The Basin 5 sampling plan is consistent with previous borrow area sampling conducted for the project.
- The DTSC and the Central Valley RWQCB (Waterboard) have reviewed the Plan and have no comments.
- Basin 5 is located adjacent to and north of a previously tested SR 132 roadway cut section east of Carpenter Road.
- The previously tested cut section exposed non-impacted native formation, and similar conditions are anticipated at Basin 5. A previous technical memorandum on sampling methodology for the Carpenter Avenue area is being relied upon as applicable.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type:** Approval of a Clean Fill Sampling Plan.
- **Agency:** DTSC (Department of Toxic Substances Control).
- **Co-signatory body:** Central Valley RWQCB (California Regional Water Quality Control Board) (noted as having reviewed and concurred).
- **Date of decision:** June 12, 2020.
- **Conditions:** None imposed; the Plan was approved as submitted without comments.

## Analytical results
*No analytical results are presented in this document. The document is an approval of a plan for *future* sampling.*

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** Approval of a plan to sample soil from a proposed borrow area (Basin 5) to be used as a "clean cover" or cap for the SR 132 soil stockpiles.
- **Phase status:** Planning/Pre-remedial. The sampling activities were authorized but not yet executed at the time of the document.
- **Verification outcomes:** None. The plan was approved without comments; however, Ritchie Stewart (Caltrans) noted a deviation from prior submittals (the technical memo with background concentrations was not included) and requested approval regardless, relying on a prior memo for the Carpenter Avenue area.

## Responsible parties
- **Caltrans (California Department of Transportation) (Richard Stewart, Ranbir Bhullar, Juergen Vespermann, Jennifer Lugo, Michael Leongson, Kai Pavel, Paul Bauldry):** Owner/operator of the site; party requesting approval of the sampling plan and responsible for implementation of the remedial cap.
- **DTSC (Dean Wright, Perry Myers):** Lead regulatory agency; provided formal approval of the Plan.
- **Central Valley RWQCB (Steven Meeks):** Co-regulator; reviewed and concurred with the approval.
- **Geocon Consultants, Inc. (John Juhrend):** Contractor preparing the sampling plan for the clean borrow source.

## Key terms and conditions
- The approved Plan (dated June 11, 2020) must be implemented as written, defining the sampling frequency and methodology for the Basin 5 borrow material.
- Sampling and analysis results must demonstrate that the Basin 5 soil is "clean fill" suitable for capping the barium-contaminated stockpiles.
- Reliance on the previously approved technical memorandum for the "area east of Carpenter Avenue" for background metal concentration comparisons is acceptable to the regulators despite the lack of a site-specific technical memo for Basin 5.
