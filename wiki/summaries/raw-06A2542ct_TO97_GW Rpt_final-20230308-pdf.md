## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project (SR 132/99, Modesto, Stanislaus County, CA). Site is south of Kansas Avenue and west of North Emerald Avenue, near SR 99. Three stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, 3) originally containing barium-contaminated fill material from former Barium Products Ltd./FMC facility.
- **Media addressed:** Groundwater
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** Active monitoring wells: MW-4, MW-6, MW-9, MW-10. Decommissioned wells (previously removed): MW-1, MW-2, MW-3, MW-5, MW-7, MW-8. Areas: Stockpiles 1, 2, and former Stockpile 3 (removed during Phase I construction). Phase I encapsulation completed October 2021; SR 132 opened to traffic November 2022.

## Key claims
- **Claim:** All four monitoring wells were dry during the November 2, 2022, December 13, 2022, and February 21, 2023 gauging events; no groundwater was present.
  - **Who is making it:** Stantec (consultant)
  - **Supported by data in this document:** Yes – field gauging data (Table 2, Figures 3 and 4) show "dry" for all wells on those dates.
- **Claim:** No groundwater samples were collected because all wells were dry.
  - **Who is making it:** Stantec
  - **Supported by data:** Yes – field notes and narrative confirm no water in wells.
- **Claim:** Historic groundwater flow direction is generally toward the south to southeast with hydraulic gradient varying from 0.0006 to 0.004.
  - **Who is making it:** Stantec (citing historic data)
  - **Conditional:** Not calculated for this event due to dry wells; based on earlier monitoring.

## Analytical results

- Media: groundwater

  - Barium: 33 µg/L (MW-3, 4/12/2017) to 410 µg/L (MW-5, 10/5/2006)
  - Arsenic: ND to 6.6 µg/L (MW-4, 7/16/2012)
  - Lead: ND to 14.5 µg/L (MW-5, 3/12/2012 S)
  - Chromium: ND to 160 µg/L (MW-1, 3/18/2013)
  - Manganese: ND to 410 µg/L (MW-5, 12/12/2013)
  - Mercury: ND to 0.29 µg/L (MW-4, 3/12/2012)
  - Total Dissolved Solids: 130 µg/L (MW-8, 3/12/2012) to 750 µg/L (MW-5, 9/5/2013)
- Applicable thresholds: MCLs (Title 22) noted in Table 3 footnotes (e.g., barium MCL 1,000 µg/L; arsenic MCL 10 µg/L; lead action level 15 µg/L; chromium MCL 50 µg/L; mercury MCL 2 µg/L). Secondary MCLs for TDS, chloride, sulfate, etc.
- Exceedances: Not explicitly summarized for current event; historical data show occasional exceedances (e.g., manganese exceeded secondary MCL of 50 µg/L in many samples; TDS above 500 mg/L in some; chromium above MCL in MW-1 on 3/18/2013 at 160 µg/L). No exceedances are discussed as current.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** Phase I earthwork began January 2020; soil from Stockpile 3 was excavated and placed on Stockpiles 1 and 2. Majority of Stockpiles 1 and 2 were encapsulated beneath the structural section of SR 132 and with clean soil and vegetation on northern side slopes, completed October 2021. SR 132 opened to traffic November 2022.
- **Quantities:** Total volume of three stockpiles estimated >970,000 cubic yards. No new quantities for this period.
- **Verification sampling outcomes:** Not reported in this document.
- **Phase status:** Completed (Phase I encapsulation and road construction accepted; active monitoring continues).
