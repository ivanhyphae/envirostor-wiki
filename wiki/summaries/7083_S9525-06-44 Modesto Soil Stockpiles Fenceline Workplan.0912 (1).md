Based on the three section summaries, the source document ("raw/7083_S9525-06-44 Modesto Soil Stockpiles Fenceline Workplan.0912 (1).pdf") presents a comprehensive workplan and health and safety plan for a supplemental site investigation of barium‑impacted soil stockpiles (~121,000 cubic yards) located in Caltrans right‑of‑way near the SR99/132 interchange in Modesto, California. The investigation addresses requirements from DTSC and RWQCB, including fenceline sampling to evaluate offsite and vertical migration, perimeter sampling to define lateral stockpile limits, and additional sampling in areas of elevated cadmium identified in 2004 studies. The data will support a Remedial Action Plan for future SR132 highway construction.

**Key Claims**  
Historical barium processing (1930s–1970s) left elevated barium concentrations in the stockpiles, and prior investigations also found elevated cadmium in a subset of borings. However, a 2007 Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) concluded that current management poses no unacceptable risk to workers, trespassers, or residents, provided institutional controls (e.g., land use covenant, soil management plan, fence maintenance) are maintained. The workplan further claims that airborne exposures to regulated metals (arsenic, lead, barium sulfate, asbestos) are below Action Levels, so medical surveillance and respirator use are not required for most staff.

**Methodology**  
The document proposes two overlapping scopes: (1) 56 total direct‑push and hand‑auger borings—29 fenceline borings to 5 ft depth, 22 perimeter borings to 3 ft depth, and 5 cadmium borings to 20 ft depth within Stockpiles 2 and 3; and (2) 19 additional borings at Stockpile 3 (three 21‑ft direct‑push, eleven 3‑ft hand‑auger on the slope face, and five 3‑ft direct‑push along the toe). Fenceline samples are to be analyzed for Title 22 metals and strontium (EPA 6010B/7471); perimeter surface samples for barium (EPA 6010B); and cadmium boring samples for barium and cadmium (EPA 6010B). QA/QC includes method blanks, duplicates, and spiked samples. Boring logs follow the Unified Soil Classification System. Air monitoring per Cal/OSHA (T8 CCR §§1529, 5214, 1532, 1532.1, 1532.2) and protection levels (Level D or C) are prescribed. The HSP also references prior investigations (2004 Preliminary Site Investigation with 50 borings; 2009 Preliminary Endangerment Assessment).

**Results**  
No quantitative results are present because the document is a workplan and HSP, not a data report. Prior investigations found elevated barium above commercial/industrial CHHSL and elevated cadmium (8 of 25 borings at Stockpile 2, 2 of 10 at Stockpile 3). Groundwater is at ~30 ft bgs, flowing southeast at 0.002 ft/ft; no active supply wells were found in the flow direction. The HHRA determined no unacceptable risk for current/future construction workers, offsite residents, or trespassers. Negative exposure assessments from similar past tasks document that airborne lead, arsenic, and asbestos are consistently below regulatory limits (e.g., lead Action Level 30 µg/m³; asbestos PEL‑TWA 0.1 f/cc).

**Limitations**  
Boring depths may need modification to penetrate native soil; locations may be adjusted for utilities, debris, or access. Schedule is subject to weather, access, and regulatory review delays. Cadmium was not identified as a COPC in the HHRA but remains a data gap. The HSP is based on pre‑field conditions, expires 6 months after CIH approval, and covers only Geocon employees/subcontractors—other site personnel follow their own safety rules. Institutional controls must remain in place for risk conclusions to hold.
