## Key claims
- This document is a Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Stockpiles 1-3), located south of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue in Stanislaus County, California, comprising approximately 121,000 cubic yards of fill embankment.
- The stockpiles were placed in the early 1960s and partially generated from excavations in evaporation ponds containing elevated heavy metals (notably barium) and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) from former Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC Corporation) operations.
- Previous investigations (2003-2009) identified elevated barium above commercial/industrial California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs) and elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3, but a 2007 HHRA concluded no significant health risks for workers, trespassers, or offsite residents under current management.
- DTSC directed Caltrans to manage stockpiles until the SR99/132 Interchange construction (scheduled to start 2015), when stockpiles will be incorporated as roadway fill and bridge abutments.
- Three regulatory data gaps were identified: (1) perimeter ROW fenceline sampling for offsite/vertical migration, (2) perimeter sampling to define lateral stockpile limits, and (3) additional sampling in areas with elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3.

## Methodology
- **Boring locations**: 56 borings total — 29 Fenceline Borings (5 feet deep), 22 Perimeter Borings (3 feet deep), and 5 Cadmium Borings (20 feet deep) within Stockpiles 2 and 3 near elevated cadmium detections from the 2004 PSI.
- **Drilling**: Geoprobe direct-push rig; hand-auger in inaccessible areas.
- **Soil sampling**: Continuous soil samples in 3-foot acetate sleeves. Fenceline and Perimeter Borings sampled at surface (0-6 inches) and at 1-foot intervals. Cadmium Borings sampled at surface and ~5-foot intervals.
- **Laboratory analyses**: Advanced Technologies Laboratories (ATL), California DOH-certified. Fenceline Borings analyzed for Title 22 metals + strontium via EPA 6010B and EPA 7471 (mercury). Perimeter Borings analyzed for barium via EPA 6010B. Cadmium Borings analyzed for barium and cadmium via EPA 6010B.
- **QA/QC**: Method blanks, duplicates, and spiked samples every 10 samples; chain-of-custody; J-flagged estimated values between MDL and PQL.
- **Field procedures**: Dust control via wet methods; equipment decontamination (wash-double rinse with non-phosphate detergent, deionized water, tap water); backfill of borings with bentonite slurry or excess soil cuttings.

## Results
- Previous 2004 PSI: Elevated barium above commercial/industrial CHHSLs; elevated cadmium in 8 of 25 borings at Stockpile 2 and 2 of 10 borings at Stockpile 3.
- Average maximum stockpile fill thickness: approximately 25 feet.
- Groundwater depth: 30-40 feet below natural grade; groundwater meets drinking water standards for constituents analyzed.
- DTSC (December 2009) found stockpiles pose no risk to Caltrans workers, trespassers, or adjacent residents under current management.
- No human health risk was identified for construction workers, offsite residents, or trespassers in the 2007 HHRA for chemicals of potential concern (COPCs: metals, notably barium, and PAHs).

## Limitations
- The workplan describes *proposed* activities; results are pending field implementation and laboratory analysis.
- Boring depths may require modification to penetrate native soil deposits; locations may be adjusted based on field conditions, utilities, buried structures, and access.
- Borings will not be performed in close vicinity to dumped trash and debris.
- Schedule delays possible due to site access, inclement weather, supplemental lab testing, or regulatory review.
- Initial analysis covers Title 22 metals, strontium, barium, and cadmium; additional total/soluble metal analysis will be assigned after review by Caltrans, DTSC, and RWQCB.
