Here is a combined summary of the "Final Caltrans Modesto Stockpile RAP_03.18.pdf," synthesized from the three section summaries.

### Document Identity and Parties
This document is a **Final Remedial Action Plan (RAP)** prepared by **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** for the **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans)**. It is submitted to and overseen by the **Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)** and the **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)**. The document is dated **March 2018** and is associated with Project No. S1200-01-01.

### Site and Scope
The site, known as the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles**, consists of three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles #1, #2, and #3) located near the SR-99/Kansas Avenue interchange in Modesto, Stanislaus County. The RAP addresses **soil, groundwater, stormwater, surface water, and air**. Specific monitoring locations include groundwater wells **MW-1 through MW-10** and stormwater sample location **SW03**.

### Key Claims and Regulatory Decisions
The core claim is that the soil stockpiles do not pose an unacceptable risk to human health or the environment under current management conditions. This claim is supported by a 2007 Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) and its 2013 update, with which the **DTSC concurred**.

- **Claim 1:** The stockpiles pose no unacceptable cancer risk or non-cancer hazard to current or future offsite residents, trespassers, construction workers, or hypothetical future shallow groundwater users. DTSC concurrence was conditioned on proper management (fencing, signage, vegetative cover, no disturbance of deeper soil, and continued monitoring).

- **Claim 2:** Previously reported elevated cadmium concentrations in Stockpiles 2 and 3 are considered false positives, as they were not reproducible in 348 subsequent samples.

- **Regulatory Decisions:** DTSC approved the HHRA Update, the Feasibility Study (FS), and the alternative selection. The agreed-upon remedy is **containment via the SR-132 Project**.

### Analytical Results
The primary contaminant of potential concern (COPC) is **barium**.
- **Soil:** Barium levels were significantly elevated, especially in Stockpile #2 (up to 130,000 mg/kg). Other detected compounds include lead, strontium, and minor diesel-range organics.
- **Groundwater:** All monitored constituents met drinking water standards (MCLs).
- **Stormwater:** A single sample (**SW03**) exceeded the MCL for barium (2,000 µg/L vs. 1,000 µg/L).

Risk assessments concluded that these concentrations do not translate into unacceptable risk under current management, though barium levels in Stockpiles 2 and 3 exceed residential screening levels, requiring land use restrictions.

### Remedial Actions
The selected remedy is **Containment via Capping (Alternative 4)**.
- **Phase 1 (Interim Progress):** A portion of Stockpile 3 (approx. 2,800 yd³) was previously removed and disposed of offsite. The primary interim action involves consolidating soil and placing a clean soil cap over the northern portions of Stockpiles 1 and 2. This phase was scheduled for completion by 2020.
- **Phase 2 (Ultimate Build-Out):** The stockpile soil will be permanently contained as embankment fill within the **SR-132/SR-99 interchange**, behind retaining walls and beneath road pavement. This phase is scheduled for completion by 2028.
- **Alternative 3 (Removal)** of all soil was evaluated but not selected.

### Responsible Parties and Conditions
- **Caltrans** is the owner/operator responsible for implementing the remedy.
- **DTSC** and **CVRWQCB** are co-regulators providing oversight.
- **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** prepared the RAP.
- **Key Conditions** include: a **Land Use Covenant (LUC)** restricting use to transportation, annual groundwater monitoring, periodic five-year reviews by DTSC, and ongoing maintenance of fencing, signage, and vegetative cover.
- **A 59-day public review period** and a public hearing were held in 2017.
