## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose**: Regulatory correspondence letter from DTSC to Caltrans conveying review and concurrence with the “Groundwater Monitoring Report, California Department of Transportation Modesto Soil Stockpiles – State Route 99 and 132, March 2012”. The letter also establishes follow-on reporting requirements and affirms ongoing monitoring obligations.
- **Author or preparer**: Randy S. Adams, C.E.G., Senior Engineering Geologist, Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program, Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).
- **Date or period covered**: Letter dated June 27, 2012. The referenced monitoring report covers sampling events on March 12, 2012 (primary), as well as June 14, 2006 and October 5, 2006 (comparison data).

## Key claims
- DTSC, in consultation with the Central Valley RWQCB, concurs with the May 2012 Groundwater Monitoring Report prepared by Geocon Consultants.
- The soil stockpiles consist of excess native soils and pond tailings generated during the early 1960s construction of SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue. The excavation traversed a 4.3-acre parcel formerly owned by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC), which included an evaporation pond used by a chemical manufacturing process involving barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores.
- The primary chemicals of concern (COC) are barium, lead, and strontium.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Concurrence**: DTSC formally concurs with the submitted Groundwater Monitoring Report (May 2012, Geocon Consultants). No additional corrective action or modification of the monitoring plan was directed beyond the conditions listed below.
- **Conditions imposed**:
  - Future groundwater monitoring reports must include a discussion comparing groundwater monitoring results and concentration trends from previous sampling events as more data become available.
  - Graphs and maps depicting concentration trends of primary COC over time must be prepared and included.
  - All graphics, including existing “stiff” diagrams, must include a legend defining symbols and colors used.
  - Groundwater monitoring must continue on a **quarterly schedule**, at a minimum.
- **Agency coordination**: Decision made in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region (RWQCB). The RWQCB collected split water samples during the March 12, 2012 sampling event.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants/media**: Groundwater; primary COC – barium, lead, strontium.
- **Sampling dates**: March 12, 2012 (primary event); also June 14, 2006 and October 5, 2006.
- **Concentration ranges / exceedances**: Not provided in this letter. The analytical data are contained within the May 2012 Geocon report and are not summarized in this DTSC response. No specific thresholds or exceedances are cited.
- **Additional data**: RWQCB split samples collected during March 12, 2012 event were analyzed and presumably compared.

## Remedial actions
- **Physical work described**: None. This document addresses groundwater monitoring only, not remedial construction or removal activities.
- **Phase status**: Monitoring is ongoing; no remediation actions are described in this letter.

## Responsible parties
- **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans)**: Owner/operator of the soil stockpiles; preparer/distributor of the monitoring report via their engineering geologist (Mr. Richard Stewart, P.G.). Obligated to comply with DTSC’s requests and maintain quarterly monitoring.
- **Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)**: Lead regulatory oversight agency (Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program).
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB)**: Co-regulator; collected split samples and provided consultation on the report review.
- **Geocon Consultants**: Preparer of the subject Groundwater Monitoring Report (May 2012).
- **Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC)**: Former owner of the 4.3-acre parcel where the stockpile source materials originated; historical operator of the evaporation pond and chemical processing facility.
- **Stanislaus County Health Agency**: Notified party (cc list).

## Key terms and conditions
- **Quarterly monitoring**: Minimum frequency for future groundwater monitoring.
- **Reporting enhancements**: All subsequent monitoring reports must include trend analysis (comparison to prior events), concentration trend graphs/maps, and defined legends for all graphics (including stiff diagrams).
- **No deviation from approved plan**: Concurrence is granted without changes to the sampling protocol, but the additional data presentation requirements are mandatory for future submissions.
