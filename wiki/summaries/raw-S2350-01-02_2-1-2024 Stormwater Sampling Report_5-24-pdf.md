## Document identity
- **Document type:** Stormwater Sampling Report
- **Preparer / author organization:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
- **Recipient / addressee agency or party:** California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) - District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch; Attn: Adam Inman, PG
- **Date or reporting period covered:** Report dated May 14, 2024; sampling event February 1, 2024
- **Project number or task order:** Geocon Project No. S2350-01-02; Caltrans Contract No. 06A2767, Task Order No. 2, EA No. 10-1E7003

## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles, located south of the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. The site comprises Stockpiles 1 and 2, which were encapsulated beneath SR 132.
- **Media addressed:** Stormwater (surface water)
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** Stormwater sample locations SW-East, SW-West, and BG-West (background). SW-East collected from the storm water discharge point on the north side of SR 132 west of SR 99. SW-West collected within the east side of Ponding Basin 5, north of SR-132 and east of N. Carpenter Road. BG-West collected from water flowing into a drop inlet along the east side of North Carpenter Road, approximately 700 feet south of the intersection with Kansas Avenue.

## Key claims
- **Claim:** Total dissolved solids (TDS), sulfate, and metal concentrations in storm water samples were less than their respective Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) except for thallium, which exceeded the primary MCL in storm water samples collected in December 2023. For the February 1, 2024 event, TDS and sulfate were below secondary MCLs; detected metals (barium, zinc, copper, strontium) did not exceed applicable MCLs.
- **Claim:** No discharge/runoff from the Caltrans ROW was observed during the February 1, 2024, sampling event.
- **Who is making it:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
- **Whether it is supported by data in this document, conditional, or contested:** Supported by analytical results (Tables 1 and 2) and field observations reported in this document.

## Regulatory decisions
- No formal regulatory decisions (approvals, denials, etc.) are contained in this document. It is a data summary report for a sampling event.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units:**
    - **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS):** Surface water; SW-East: 88 mg/L; SW-West: 63 mg/L; BG-West: 42 mg/L.
    - **Sulfate:** Surface water; SW-East: <2.0 mg/L; SW-West: 3.6 mg/L; BG-West: 6.5 mg/L.
    - **Title 22 Dissolved Metals (surface water):**
        - **Barium:** SW-East: 900 µg/L (0.90 mg/L); SW-West: 520 µg/L (0.52 mg/L); BG-West: 10 µg/L (0.010 mg/L).
        - **Zinc:** SW-East: 25 µg/L; SW-West: 240 µg/L; BG-West: 18 µg/L.
        - **Copper:** SW-East: 13 µg/L; SW-West: <5.0 µg/L; BG-West: <5.0 µg/L.
        - **Strontium:** SW-East: 180 µg/L; SW-West: 78 µg/L; BG-West: <50 µg/L.
        - **Mercury:** All locations: <0.20 µg/L.
        - **Thallium:** All locations: <0.015 mg/L (15 µg/L).
        - Other metals (antimony, arsenic, beryllium, cadmium, chromium, cobalt, lead, molybdenum, nickel, selenium, silver, vanadium) were non-detect at all locations.
- **Applicable thresholds:**
    - Primary MCL for Thallium: 2.0 µg/L (not exceeded in Feb 2024).
    - Secondary MCL for TDS: 500 mg/L (not exceeded).
    - Secondary MCL for Sulfate: 250 mg/L (not exceeded).
    - Primary MCL for Barium: 1000 µg/L (not exceeded).
    - Primary MCL for Copper: 1300 µg/L (not exceeded).
    - Primary MCL for Zinc: 5,000 µg/L (secondary MCL) (not exceeded).
    - No MCL established for strontium.
- **Which wells or locations exceeded thresholds and by how much:** No exceedances of applicable MCLs were reported for the February 1, 2024 event.
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations affecting interpretation:** Laboratory QA/QC data showed acceptable surrogate recoveries, non-detect method blanks, and acceptable LCS/LCSD recoveries. Several historical results (Table 2) were qualified with "J" (estimated concentration) or "J+" (estimated, possibly biased high) flags.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** Encapsulation of Stockpiles 1 and 2 beneath the structural section of SR 132 and with clean soil and vegetation on the northern side slopes.
- **Quantities:** Not quantified in this document (referenced as completed in October 2021).
- **Verification sampling outcomes:** Not applicable for this stormwater report; prior surface water sampling events are referenced but no verification outcomes are reported here.
- **Phase status:** Completed (Stockpile 3 soil was excavated and placed on Stockpiles 1 and 2; encapsulation completed October 2021; SR 132 opened November 2022). Current phase: Post-encapsulation stormwater monitoring.
