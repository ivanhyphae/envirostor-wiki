## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose**: Remedial Action Completion Report (RACR) – documents consolidation and capping of barium-contaminated soil stockpiles on SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA, under DTSC oversight.
- **Author or preparer**: Not explicitly stated; referenced entities include Caltrans (owner), Geocon (environmental consultant), WSP (construction manager), BCPG (contractor), Dokken (design), Kleinfelder (compaction testing).
- **Date or period covered**: Phase 1 work described with monitoring data from November 2019 through March 2021; a record rain event in January 2021; repairs by September 2021. Phase 2 scheduled for 2028.

## Key claims
- Consolidation of barium-containing soil (BCS) from Stockpiles 1–3 and ADL soil as highway embankment fill, capped with DTSC-approved clean fill.
- Stockpile 3: ~27,675 yd³ (3,075 truckloads) fully excavated and placed on Stockpile 1.
- Stockpile 2: ~102,000 yd³ graded and consolidated within BCS Containment Zones.
- Final verification after re‑excavation of hotspots: barium 54–760 mg/kg; lead 1.5–35 mg/kg.
- Verification thresholds: barium ≤ 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection); lead ≤ 80 mg/kg (DTSC residential screening).
- Statistical evaluation (ProUCL v5.14): 95% UCL for MSE wall footing data = 171.9 mg/kg barium (exceeds site-specific background of 120 mg/kg but deemed suitable as capping material). H‑statistic UCL unstable; nonparametric methods preferred.

## Regulatory decisions
- **DTSC variances**: Two variances approved clean cover material (Class 3 aggregate subbase, minimum 4 inches).
- **Land use covenant**: Required to restrict future land use with five‑year reviews.
- **Supplemental RDIP**: Required before Phase 2 (scheduled 2028).
- **Final closure certification**: Awaits completeness of Phase 2.

## Remedial actions
- **Phase 1 (interim)**: BCS consolidation from Stockpiles 1–3 and ADL soil onto designated BCS Containment Zones; placement of clean fill cap; construction of MSE wall.
- **Clean cover**: Class 3 aggregate subbase (min 4 inches) approved under two DTSC variances.
- **Erosion repair**: Record rain event January 2021 caused erosion on unvegetated slopes; repaired by September 2021.
- **Phase 2**: Scheduled 2028 (requires supplemental RDIP approval prior to initiation).
