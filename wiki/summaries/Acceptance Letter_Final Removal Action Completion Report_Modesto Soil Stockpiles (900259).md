## Key claims
- The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Board, **accepted the Removal Action Completion Report (RACR)** for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Phase 1 Interim State Route 132 West Project.
- The RACR documents the **consolidation and capping of barium-containing soils (BCS)** as part of the Interim Phase 1 construction effort.
- All soil removal, consolidation, and capping activities were completed in accordance with the approved **Remedial Action Plan (RAP)**, **Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP)**, and two approved **variance requests**.
- The accepted RACR includes **As-Built Plans**, cross‑section figures, final survey data, and a final **Operation and Maintenance (O&M) Plan**.
- DTSC will prepare a draft **O&M Agreement** within 30 days and complete a **Land Use Covenant (LUC)** for the remediated area.
- **Final certification of the RAP will not occur** until the **Ultimate Phase 2** build‑out (adding lanes and completing State Route 132) is finished, anticipated in 8–10 years. Phase 2 will require a separate agreement and supplemental RDIP.

## Methodology
*No experimental or quantitative methodology is described.* The document is a regulatory acceptance letter. The process involved:
- Review of the submitted RACR (dated December 1, 2022) by DTSC and the Central Valley RWQCB.
- Verification of compliance with the approved RAP, RDIP, and two variance requests (approved April 17, 2020 and September 21, 2020).
- Inclusion of As‑Built Plans, cross sections, final survey data, and the O&M Plan in the final RACR.
- DTSC prepared a draft O&M Agreement for Caltrans review and will complete a Land Use Covenant for recording with the county assessor.

## Results
*No quantitative results (e.g., contaminant concentrations, volumes of soil removed, dimensions of capped area) are provided.* The key outcome is the **formal acceptance** of the Interim Phase 1 removal action, marking its conclusion.

## Limitations
- **Final certification of the RAP is deferred** until after the Ultimate Phase 2 construction, which is projected to begin in 8–10 years.
- The Remedial Action Completion Report (RACR) covers **only the Interim Phase 1** effort; the project is not fully closed.
- Ultimate Phase 2 work will be conducted under a **separate agreement** between DTSC and Caltrans and will require its own supplemental RDIP, RACR, and possible revisions to the O&M Plan and Agreement.
