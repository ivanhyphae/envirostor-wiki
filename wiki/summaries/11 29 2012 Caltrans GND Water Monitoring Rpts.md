## Key claims
- DTSC, in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board, reviewed three groundwater monitoring reports for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site at SR 99 and 132, dated for sampling events in May, June, and July 2012.
- The reports document groundwater monitoring well sampling results and, in the June 2012 report, the installation of two new monitoring wells:
  - MW-9: south of Kansas Avenue and west of SR 99
  - MW-10: south of Kansas Avenue and east of SR 99
- The site consists of excess native soils and pond tailings from construction of SR 99 in the early 1960s on land formerly owned by FMC (Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation), whose operations involved barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores and other minerals.
- The main chemicals of concern at the site are barium, lead, and strontium.
- DTSC states that it concurs with the groundwater monitoring reports and requests continued reporting as more groundwater data become available.

## Methodology
- The document is a regulatory review letter, not a primary experimental study.
- Reviewed materials:
  1. “Groundwater Monitoring Report - May 2012, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California”
  2. “Additional Well Installation and Groundwater Monitoring Report - June 2012, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California”
  3. “Groundwater Monitoring Report - July 2012, Modesto Soil Stockpiles, SR 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California”
- The underlying monitoring program involved groundwater well sampling in May, June, and July 2012 and additional well installation in June 2012.
- No detailed analytical methods, sampling frequencies beyond those months, or formal performance metrics are provided in the letter itself.

## Results
- The letter does not report specific contaminant concentration values or statistical summaries.
- It indicates that the submitted groundwater monitoring reports contained analytical results for the May, June, and July 2012 sampling events.
- DTSC’s qualitative assessment was favorable: it “concur[s] with the subject groundwater monitoring reports.”
- Two new wells were installed in June 2012 (MW-9 and MW-10), expanding the monitoring network.

## Limitations
- This document is a brief agency review letter and does not include the underlying numerical groundwater chemistry results.
- No quantitative comparison to standards, trends, or exceedances is given in the letter.
- The conclusions are contingent on future groundwater data availability; DTSC explicitly requests continued monitoring reports as more data are collected.
