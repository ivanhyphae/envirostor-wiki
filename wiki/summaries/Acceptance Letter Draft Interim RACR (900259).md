## Key claims
- The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) accepts the Draft Interim Removal Action Completion Report (Draft Phase 1 RACR) as a technical status report with no comments.
- Remediation of barium‑containing soils (BCS) from three stockpiles along State Route 132 in Modesto was completed as per the approved Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) and two approved variance requests.
- Soil removal and consolidation activities: fully excavated Stockpile 3 (~27,675 yd³), removed ends of Stockpile 2, steepened southern slopes of Stockpiles 1 and 2 for a mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall, and removed aerially deposited lead from a shoulder of State Route 99.
- Confirmation/verification sampling showed that all remaining in‑situ soil met cleanup goals for barium (1,000 mg/kg) and lead (80 mg/kg), with residual barium above site‑specific background (120 mg/kg) only in removed material that was placed as embankment fill within containment zones.
- Containment zones of Stockpiles 1 and 2 were capped with clean fill from three approved local sources; northern slopes were vegetated and erosion‑controlled; southern slopes protected by the new MSE wall.
- Pending items for Final Phase 1 RACR: as‑built plans, final survey data, and an Operation & Maintenance (O&M) Plan. Ultimate Phase 2 (final build‑out of State Route 132) is anticipated in 8–10 years.

## Methodology
- **Field activities** followed the approved Remedial Action Plan (RAP) and Interim Phase 1 RDIP.
- **Soil removal and sampling**:  
  – Stockpile 3 fully excavated; 29 confirmation samples collected from the excavation bottom (analyzed for barium and lead). Three areas with barium >1,000 mg/kg were deepened ~1 ft and resampled.  
  – Western and eastern ends of Stockpile 2 removed; 8 verification samples from the west end and 12 from the east end (native soil after removal). Four areas >1,000 mg/kg Ba were deepened 1 ft and re‑tested.  
  – Southern slopes of Stockpiles 1 and 2: soil sampled at 200‑ft intervals to a depth of 5 ft to assess usability as clean cap material; all results below cleanup goals for Ba and Pb, but barium above site‑specific background (120 mg/kg), so removed material was placed as embankment fill.
- **Clean fill sourcing**: four potential sources tested per DTSC’s *Information Advisory Clean Imported Fill Material* (2001); three accepted.
- **Capping and erosion control**: clean fill placed on containment zones; northern slopes seeded with hydroseed mix and covered with erosion control blanket and fiber rolls.
- **Acceptance criteria**: DTSC and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) reviewed the Draft Phase 1 RACR for compliance with the RDIP and approved variance requests.

## Results
- **Stockpile volumes** (estimated fill): Stockpile 1 = 34,000 yd³; Stockpile 2 = 102,000 yd³; Stockpile 3 = 24,000 yd³.
- **Excavation and placement**: ~27,675 yd³ from Stockpile 3 and material from the ends of Stockpile 2 were placed as embankment fill inside containment zones of Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- **Confirmation sampling (Stockpile 3)**: initial 29 samples; 3 showed barium >1,000 mg/kg; after 1‑ft deepening, all final samples met cleanup goals (barium ≤1,000 mg/kg, lead ≤80 mg/kg).
- **Verification sampling (Stockpile 2 ends)**: 20 total locations; 4 areas initially exceeded barium goal; after 1‑ft deepening, all met goals.
- **Southern slope sampling**: all barium and lead concentrations below cleanup goals; barium above site‑specific background (120 mg/kg) in all removed material.
- **Clean fill sources**: 3 of 4 sourced areas acceptable; acceptance deferred for the fourth.
- **Aerially deposited lead**: soil from State Route 99 shoulder placed in the eastern containment zone of Stockpile 1.

## Limitations
- The Draft Phase 1 RACR is accepted as a technical status report only; final deliverables (as‑built plans, final survey data, O&M Plan) are still pending.
- The O&M Plan (including a soil management plan, annual pavement/feature inspections, and groundwater monitoring) must be submitted and approved before the Final Phase 1 RACR (estimated July/August 2022).
- Approval of the Final Phase 1 RACR will initiate an O&M Agreement and a land use covenant, but certification of the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) will not occur until after the Ultimate Phase 2 build‑out is completed (8–10 years later).
- Ultimate Phase 2 will require a separate supplemental RDIP, field oversight, a new RACR, and potential revisions to the O&M Plan and Agreement.
- No mention of long‑term performance monitoring beyond the planned O&M inspections or of potential residual contamination beneath the caps.
