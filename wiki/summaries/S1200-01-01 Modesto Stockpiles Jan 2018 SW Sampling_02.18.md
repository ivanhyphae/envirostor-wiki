## Summary

This document presents analytical results from surface water sampling conducted at the Caltrans Modesto soil stockpiles in Stanislaus County, California. The primary sampling event occurred on January 8, 2018, following an approved Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP), with additional historical data from 2013–2017 included for comparison.

**Methodology:** Stormwater samples were collected from four stockpile locations (PL1–PL4) and two background locations (BG1, BG2) during approximately 2.2 inches of rain. (Sample location PL5 was dry and could not be sampled.) Samples were collected using disposable bailers and a suction pump, filtered through 0.45-micron filters for dissolved metals analysis, and analyzed using EPA methods 6020 (ICP-MS for metals), 7470A (cold vapor AA for mercury), and 300.0 (ion chromatography for anions), along with Standard Methods for alkalinity (SM 2320B), solids (SM 2540C/D), and sulfide (SM 4500-S²⁻). Field parameters including pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, ORP, and DO were also monitored. Results were compared to California primary and secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for drinking water.

**Key Results:** Numerous dissolved metals and general minerals were detected. Notably:
- **Metals:** Antimony, arsenic, barium, calcium, chromium, cobalt, copper, lead, manganese, magnesium, molybdenum, nickel, potassium, sodium, strontium, vanadium, and zinc were detected. Mercury and several other metals (beryllium, cadmium, selenium, silver, thallium) were not detected above reporting limits. Manganese in sample BG2 exceeded its secondary MCL; all other dissolved metals were below MCLs. For historical context, arsenic ranged from <0.50 to 3.5 µg/L (MCL: 10 µg/L), chromium from <0.50 to 7.8 µg/L (MCL: 50 µg/L), and lead from <0.50 to 4.6 µg/L (MCL: 15 µg/L).
- **General Minerals:** Chloride ranged from 0.57 to 7.1 mg/L, nitrate as N from 0.31 to 2.9 mg/L, sulfate from 1.1 to 6.9 mg/L, total alkalinity from 15 to 33 mg/L, TDS from 24 to 82 mg/L, and TSS from 16 to 98 mg/L. Total sulfide was detected in some samples (up to 0.098 mg/L). No general mineral concentrations exceeded MCLs, and results were within the same range as previous sampling events.

**Quality Control and Limitations:** Quality control data demonstrated acceptable method performance, with most recoveries within 80–120% limits. Notable QC exceptions included matrix spike recoveries for sulfate (147%) and sulfide (63%) that exceeded control limits, and one RPD value flagged as outside limits. Dilutions were applied for barium, calcium, potassium, zinc, and sulfide due to high analyte concentrations. Several results were J-flagged as estimated concentrations below practical quantitation limits. A key limitation is that sample PL5 could not be collected due to insufficient water, and its gate valve was not opened for sampling. Additionally, observations of no offsite stormwater migration were based on qualitative field observations rather than quantitative measurement.
