The document is a January 2018 surface-water/stormwater monitoring report and accompanying laboratory certificates for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California. It covers a continuing sampling program conducted from 2013 through early 2018 at drainage locations PL1–PL5 and background points BG1–BG2, with the January 8, 2018 event sampled during a rainstorm (about 2.2 inches total rainfall over Jan. 4–10, 2018). The purpose of the program was to evaluate whether runoff from stockpiles 1–3 was migrating offsite and to characterize surface-water chemistry under DTSC-approved protocols. PL5 was dry and not sampled in January 2018.

Sampling was conducted using disposable bailers and suction pumps, with field measurements taken for pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, dissolved oxygen, and ORP. Samples were analyzed by ATL/ATL Global using standard methods for dissolved metals (EPA 6020 and mercury by EPA 7470A), chloride/nitrate/sulfate (EPA 300.0), alkalinity (SM 2320B), TDS (SM 2540C), TSS (SM 2540D), and sulfide (SM 4500-S-D). Results were reported with detection limits, estimated values where applicable, and full QA/QC documentation including chain-of-custody, blanks, spikes, and duplicate checks.

Overall, the monitoring results indicate that stormwater generally remained within the Caltrans right-of-way and no clear offsite migration was observed, consistent with earlier sampling since 2013. Most analytes were below or near drinking-water standards, and comparisons were made against California primary and secondary MCLs to provide context. Dissolved metals detected across the dataset included antimony, arsenic, barium, calcium, chromium, cobalt, copper, lead, manganese, magnesium, molybdenum, nickel, potassium, sodium, strontium, vanadium, and zinc. The main exceedance highlighted in the summary was manganese in background sample BG2; otherwise, reported dissolved-metal concentrations generally did not exceed applicable standards. Historical results show intermittent elevated values at certain locations and dates, such as arsenic in PL3, chromium in PL4, and lead in BG2, but the January 2018 event itself was broadly consistent with prior observations.

The January 8, 2018 field/lab results show modestly mineralized stormwater with low to moderate solids and variable metals. For example, field data at PL1 included pH 7.18, conductivity 42 µmhos/cm, turbidity 138 ntu, and dissolved oxygen 8.50 mg/L. Laboratory certificates for BG1, BG2, PL1–PL4 show low concentrations of chloride, nitrate, sulfate, alkalinity, TDS, and TSS in background samples, with higher and more variable dissolved metals in some stockpile drainage samples. For PL4 in particular, the certificate shows measurable alkalinity, TDS, TSS, sulfide, major anions, and elevated dissolved metals such as calcium, sodium, potassium, magnesium, strontium, zinc, and barium, while QC results generally indicate the analyses were valid and within acceptance limits.
