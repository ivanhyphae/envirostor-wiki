## Key claims
This report documents a surface water sampling event (January 8, 2018) at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, which contains soil excavated from a former chemical manufacturing facility (FMC) that operated unlined evaporation ponds. The main findings are: (1) Stormwater runoff from the stockpiles was retained within the Caltrans right-of-way, with no evidence of offsite migration. (2) Dissolved metals concentrations in all samples were below primary and secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), except for manganese in background sample BG2. (3) Previously elevated barium (2,000 µg/L in 2006 sample SW03) was not observed in this event; barium in the January 2018 samples ranged from 18 to 200 µg/L. (4) Analytical results for the 2018 event were consistent with results from previous monitoring events (2013-2017), showing no significant changes in water quality.

## Methodology
**Sampling event:** January 8, 2018, during a rain event (total ~2.2 inches from Jan 4-10). Six surface water samples were collected from four locations designated by previous studies (PL1-PL4) and two background locations (BG1, BG2). Location PL5 had insufficient water and was not sampled. Sample PL3 was collected outside the Caltrans right-of-way. Samples were collected using disposable bailers and a suction pump, field-filtered through 0.45-micron filters for dissolved metals, and transported on ice to Advanced Technology Laboratories, Inc. (ATL).

**Field parameters measured:** pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, turbidity, oxygen-reduction potential (ORP), and dissolved oxygen (DO).

**Laboratory analyses:** (1) Dissolved metals by EPA Method 6020/7470A. (2) Chloride, nitrate (as N), and sulfate by EPA Method 300.0. (3) Sulfide by Standard Method (SM) 4500-S-D. (4) Total alkalinity, bicarbonate alkalinity, carbonate alkalinity by SM 2320B. (5) Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) by SM 2540C. (6) Total Suspended Solids (TSS) by SM 2540D.

**Quality Assurance/Quality Control:** Chain-of-custody documentation, laboratory QA/QC review, noted dilutions for high-concentration analytes (barium, calcium, potassium, zinc in all samples; sulfide in BG1 and BG2 due to possible matrix interference).

**Comparison data:** Results from seven previous surface water sampling events (April 2013 through March 2017) at the same locations are presented for context.

## Results
**Field parameters (January 8, 2018):**
- pH: 6.88-7.39 across all locations.
- Conductivity: 36-117 µmhos/cm.
- Turbidity: 56.4-174 ntu.
- ORP: -119 to -3 millivolts.
- DO: 8.34-9.59 mg/L.

**Dissolved metals (all µg/L, January 8, 2018):**
- PL1: Barium 18, Calcium 12,000, Strontium 80, Zinc 60, Copper 8.4.
- PL2: Barium 25, Calcium 5,500, Strontium 53, Zinc 57, Molybdenum 2.6.
- PL3 (outside right-of-way): Barium 200, Calcium 8,600, Strontium 130, Zinc 48, Lead 11, Copper 10.
- PL4: Barium 130, Calcium 9,600, Strontium 210, Zinc 160, Copper 24, Lead 5.1, Arsenic 1.2, Antimony 2.8.
- BG1: Barium 31, Calcium 2,500, Strontium 22, Zinc 37.
- BG2: Barium 100, Calcium 5,900, Strontium 43, Zinc 230, Copper 45, Lead 13, Cobalt 1.1, Chromium 6.4.

All dissolved metals were below their respective primary or secondary MCLs, except manganese in BG2 (no numerical value given, but stated as exceeding secondary MCL). Beryllium, cadmium, mercury, selenium, silver, and thallium were not detected.

**General minerals (January 8, 2018):**
- TDS: 24-94 mg/L across all locations.
- TSS: 16-98 mg/L.
- Chloride: 0.57-7.1 mg/L.
- Sulfate: 1.1-6.9 mg/L.
- Nitrate (as N): 0.31-2.9 mg/L.
- Total alkalinity (as CaCO3): 15-33 mg/L.
- Sulfide: 0.098 mg/L (BG1), 0.030 mg/L (PL2), 0.086 mg/L (PL4); not detected in others.
All general minerals were below their respective secondary MCLs.

## Limitations
- The sample PL5 was not collected due to insufficient water, limiting the assessment of that location.
- Laboratory noted sample dilutions for barium, calcium, potassium, and/or zinc due to high concentrations in all samples, and for sulfide in BG1 and BG2 due to possible matrix interference.
- Quality control for sulfate (matrix spike) and sulfide (matrix spike) showed recovery outside acceptance limits, though the lab validated the batch via the laboratory control sample.
- The report does not include statistical analysis or trend testing; the conclusion of consistency with prior events is qualitative.
- The 2018 event represents a single point in time; the site continues to be monitored.
