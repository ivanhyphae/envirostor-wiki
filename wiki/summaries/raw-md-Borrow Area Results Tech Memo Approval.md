### **Document Identity**
- **Document type**: Email correspondence and attached Technical Memorandum (Tech Memo) titled "Clean Cap Borrow Area Tech Memo".
- **Purpose**: To formally submit for regulatory review and approval a Technical Memorandum characterizing the suitability of backfill soil for use as clean cap material over barium-contaminated stockpiles at the State Route 132 (SR 132) site.
- **Author/Preparer**: John Juhrend (Geocon Consultants, Inc.) prepared the Tech Memo. Richard C. Stewart (Caltrans) and Dean Wright (DTSC) authored the email chain.
- **Date/Period covered**: The Tech Memo is dated February 27, 2020. The email chain covers correspondence from February 28, 2020, to March 4, 2020.

### **Key Claims**
- **Claim by Caltrans (Richard Stewart)**: The composite soil sample results from the clean cap borrow area are consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations and are therefore suitable as clean cover material.
- **Claim submitted by Geocon (John Juhrend)**: The data supports the proposal that the borrow area material is suitable for use as a clean cap.
- **Claim approved by DTSC (Dean Wright)**:
    - DTSC formally agrees with the conclusion in the Tech Memo.
    - DTSC states the composite sample results are *“consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations and suitable as clean cover material for capping Stockpiles 1 and 2.”*

### **Regulatory Decisions**
- **Decision type**: Formal acceptance/approval of a Technical Memorandum.
- **Agency**: California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).
- **Date**: March 4, 2020.
- **Conditions**: The approval was given via email, serving as DTSC’s acceptance of the Tech Memo. The approval is for the specific material characterized in the memo to be used for capping Stockpiles 1 and 2.
