## Key claims
- Consolidation of barium-containing soil (BCS) Stockpiles 1–3 and ADL soil used as highway embankment fill, capped with DTSC-approved clean fill.
- Stockpile 3 fully excavated (~27,675 yd³, 3,075 truckloads) and placed on Stockpile 1.
- Stockpile 2 graded and consolidated (~102,000 yd³) within BCS Containment Zones.
- Final verification results: barium 54–760 mg/kg, lead 1.5–35 mg/kg after re-excavation of hotspots.
- Verification thresholds: barium ≤ 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection); lead ≤ 80 mg/kg (DTSC residential screening).

## Methodology
- Phase 1 (interim) remedial actions: BCS consolidation, clean fill capping, MSE wall construction.
- Clean cover: Class 3 aggregate subbase (minimum 4 inches) approved via two DTSC variances.
- Record rain event in January 2021 caused erosion on unvegetated slopes; repaired by September 2021.
- Statistical evaluation using ProUCL v5.14 for 95% UCL calculations.
- MSE wall footing data: 95% UCL = 171.9 mg/kg barium (exceeds site-specific background of 120 mg/kg, but considered suitable as capping material).
- H-statistic UCL unstable; nonparametric methods preferred.

## Results
- Air monitoring: PM-10 below action levels (total dust < 4.0 mg/m³, barium < 25 µg/m³, lead < 1.5 µg/m³).
- Groundwater monitoring (four events, Nov 2019–Mar 2021) below regulatory thresholds; no offsite migration detected.
- Stormwater monitoring (three events, Dec 2019–Dec 2020) within limits.
- Final closure certification pending Phase 2 completion.

## Concepts
Barium-containing soil (BCS), Stockpile 3, Stockpile 2, Stockpile 1, ADL soil, highway embankment fill, DTSC-approved clean fill, MSE wall (mechanically stabilized earth wall), Class 3 aggregate subbase, DTSC variance, ProUCL v5.14, 95% UCL, H-statistic, nonparametric methods, site-specific background, groundwater protection threshold, DTSC residential screening level, land use covenant, five-year reviews, supplemental RDIP (Remedial Design Implementation Plan), Phase 2 (scheduled 2028)
