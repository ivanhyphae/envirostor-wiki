## Document Identity

- **Document type and purpose:** Surface Water Report – Inspection report documenting the February 28, 2023 surface water discharge inspection at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project. No samples were collected due to insufficient rainfall.
- **Author or preparer:** Stantec Consulting Services Inc. (Michael Myers, Associate Environmental Scientist; Ralph Carson, PG; Kevin Miskin, PE)
- **Date or period covered:** Inspection performed February 28, 2023; report dated March 9, 2023

## Key Claims

- No standing surface water or runoff was present at any designated sampling location (BG1, BG3, PL6, PL7) during the February 28, 2023 inspection.
- No discharge/runoff of surface water beyond BMPs and/or the Site was observed (Stantec).
- Precipitation measured as 0.09 inches on February 28, 2023 — insufficient for a qualifying rain event.
- Minor soil erosion observed on the east side of stormwater basin 5 and at several locations on the north side of Stockpile 1.
- Some hydroseeded areas on the north side of Stockpile 2 lacked vegetation.
- General BMPs (straw waddles, jute mesh) were present and functioning across improved areas.

## Regulatory Decisions

- No regulatory decisions are documented in this report. The letter is a compliance inspection report submitted under existing DTSC-approved protocols (*Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan*, Shaw, Jan 2006; *Addendum to Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan*, Geocon, Feb 20, 2013).

## Analytical Results

- **No samples collected.** Insufficient rainfall prevented any surface water sampling during this event. No analytical data are reported.

## Remedial Actions

- **Phase I earthwork** (begun Jan 2020): Stockpile 3 soil excavated, transported, and placed on Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- **Encapsulation completion** (Oct 2021): Majority of Stockpiles 1 and 2 encapsulated beneath structural section of SR 132, with clean soil and vegetation on northern side slopes.
- **SR 132 opening** (Nov 2022): Roadway opened to traffic.
- **BMP maintenance:** Straw waddles and jute mesh present as stormwater control measures.
- **Status:** Remedial construction (encapsulation) is complete; current activities are post-construction compliance inspections.

## Key Terms and Conditions

- **Surface water sampling** is to be conducted during qualifying rain events per DTSC-approved SAP (Shaw 2006) and SAP Addendum (Geocon 2013), requiring visible runoff and 72 hours of prior dry weather.
- **Five former surface runoff locations (PL1–PL5)** eliminated due to road construction (Phase I, Jan 2020) that covered stockpile areas; replaced with new locations PL6 and PL7.
- **Background locations revised:** BG3 added; BG2 removed from network.
- **Gate valve** on stormwater basin at PL5 would only be opened if basin filled near capacity, threatening SR 99 northbound lanes; discharge would then go to Tuolumne River. This condition did not arise during this inspection.
- **No discharge/runoff beyond BMPs observed** — no further action triggered.
- **Minor erosion and sparse vegetation noted** on stockpile slopes; no follow-on obligations explicitly stated beyond observation.
