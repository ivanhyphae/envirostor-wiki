### Combined Summary of "raw/S9525-01-44C Modesto Stockpiles GW September 2013 1013.pdf"

This document is a laboratory report and associated analysis of groundwater quality for the Modesto Stockpiles project, based on samples collected from 10 monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) on September 4–5, 2013.

**Key Findings**
No dissolved metal concentrations exceeded any numeric water quality thresholds. However, nitrate (as N) exceeded the primary Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) of 10 mg/L in five wells (MW-1, MW-4, MW-5, MW-6, MW-10), with concentrations ranging from 2.5 to 36 mg/L. Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in the same five wells, ranging from 300 to 750 mg/L. Two distinct groundwater types were identified: calcium-dominant (MW-1, MW-2, MW-4, MW-7, MW-9, MW-10) and sodium-dominant (MW-3, MW-6, MW-8). Well MW-5 appeared to be shifting from calcium- to sodium-dominant, suggesting mixing of different water types. Groundwater flow direction remained toward the southeast.

**Methodology and Analytical Data**
Field activities included measuring depth-to-groundwater, dissolved oxygen, and ORP, followed by purging and sampling. Samples were analyzed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (a CA-certified lab) for a comprehensive suite of parameters including dissolved metals (EPA 6020/7470), major anions (EPA 300.0), alkalinity (SM 2320B), TDS (SM 2540C), and sulfide (SM 4500). Stiff diagrams in an appendix graphically summarize the major ion chemistry for each well.

**Quality Control and Limitations**
The analytical data were validated through a rigorous QA/QC process including method blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), and matrix spikes (MS/MSD). While some MS/MSD recoveries fell outside control limits—attributed to sample matrix effects—the analytical batches were validated by passing LCS results. Key limitations include: the data represent a single sampling event, well MW-5 shows evidence of water mixing, and PAH analysis was discontinued after November 2012 as no PAHs had been detected previously.
