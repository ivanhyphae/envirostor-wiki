- **Document type**: Storm Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP)
- **Preparer / author organization**: Geocon Consultants, Inc.
- **Recipient / addressee agency or party**: California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) - District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch; Attn: Richard Stewart, PG
- **Date**: December 13, 2023
- **Project number or task order**: Project No. S2350-01-02; Contract No. 06A2767, Task Order No. 2; EA No. 10-1E7003

## Site and Scope
- **Site name**: Former Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Modesto Soil Stockpiles)
- **Location**: South of the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue, Stanislaus County, California
- **Areas of concern**: Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2, encapsulated beneath SR 132 West Expressway; storm water discharge points from drainage system capturing runoff from segment of SR 132 where stockpile containment system was constructed
- **Media addressed**: Stormwater runoff
- **Specific sample locations**:
  - SW-East: Storm water discharge point on north side of SR 132 west of SR 99
  - SW-West: Storm water discharge pipe adjacent and east of Ponding Basin 5 along north side of SR 132
  - BG-West (background): East side of North Carpenter Road, ~700 feet south of intersection with Kansas Avenue
  - BG-East (background): Near northeast corner of intersection of North Emerald Avenue and Loletta Avenue

## Key Claims
- **Claim**: Storm water samples will help gauge effectiveness of the containment system and determine if storm water has been impacted by chemicals of potential concern from encapsulated stockpiles (barium and lead-contaminated soil).
  - **Who**: Geocon Consultants, Inc. (preparer)
  - **Support**: Conditional – this is stated as the purpose of the planned sampling; no results are presented in this document as this is a plan for future sampling.
- **Claim**: During most recent sampling event (Stantec, March 2023), reported metal concentrations in surface water samples were below their Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs).
  - **Who**: Geocon (citing prior work by Stantec)
  - **Support**: Supported by data referenced from March 2023 sampling event.

## Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision type**: Not a regulatory decision document; this is a SAP submitted for Caltrans approval.
  - **Agency**: Prepared for Caltrans; sampling protocols were approved by DTSC as established in prior documents (2006 Shaw SAP and 2013 Geocon SAP Addendum).
  - **Documents referenced as previously approved**: Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (Shaw, January 2006); Addendum to Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (Geocon, February 20, 2013)

## Remedial Actions
- **Activity**: Encapsulation of Stockpiles 1 and 2 beneath structural section of SR 132 and with clean soil and vegetation on northern side slopes.
  - **Quantities**: Not specified.
  - **Verification sampling**: Not reported in this document (referenced March 2023 Stantec sampling showed metals below MCLs).
  - **Phase status**: Completed – SR 132 opened to traffic November 2022; Stockpiles 1 and 2 completely encapsulated by October 2021; Stockpile 3 excavated and placed on top of Stockpiles 1 and 2 (earthwork began January 2020).
- **Planned activities**: Collection of up to two storm water runoff samples per event, up to four rainfall events per year; field filtration for dissolved metals through 0.45-micron filter; monitoring of pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, and turbidity.

## Key Terms and Conditions
- **Analytes to be tested**: Title 22 dissolved metals (including barium and strontium) by EPA Method 6020/7470; sulfate by EPA Method 9056/300.0; Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) by EPA Method 160.1
- **Qualifying rain event criteria**: Visible runoff and 72 hours of prior dry weather (per prior SAP protocols)
- **QA/QC requirements**:
  - One method blank per 10 samples, batch, or matrix type (whichever more frequent)
  - One duplicate sample per 10 samples, batch, or matrix type (whichever more frequent)
  - One spiked sample per 10 samples, batch, or matrix type (whichever more frequent); spike at 10x detection limit or analyte level
- **Deliverable**: Draft summary report after each sampling event; final report after incorporation of Caltrans comments
- **Additional sampling**: If runoff observed leaving Caltrans ROW, additional samples may be collected at those locations
- **References cited**: Final Surface Water SAP (Shaw, January 2006); SAP Addendum (Geocon, February 2013); Surface Water Report (Stantec, March 2023)
