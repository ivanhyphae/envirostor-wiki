## Key claims
- This document is a response letter addressing DTSC and RWQCB comments on the Draft Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles (State Routes 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California).
- The authors claim the Final Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan has been revised to incorporate requested clarifications and field/lab procedures.
- Main revisions include:
  - adjusting/adding boring locations based on a September 17, 2012 site survey,
  - clarifying boring depths and sampling intervals for different boring categories,
  - specifying containment, sampling, and analysis of decontamination rinse water,
  - clarifying that laboratory analytical methods apply across boring categories, with additional analyses as needed after agency review,
  - limiting the summary report to supplemental site investigation findings and separating contamination extent and human health risk assessment into other reports,
  - adding soluble metals reporting with MDL/PQL values and “J-flag” reporting for estimated detections.

## Methodology
- This is not an experimental research paper; it is a regulatory/workplan response describing planned site investigation methods.
- Field setup:
  - Representatives of DTSC, Caltrans, and Geocon conducted a site survey on September 17, 2012 to review proposed boring locations.
  - Boring locations were added or adjusted at each stockpile based on field conditions.
- Sampling design:
  - **Fenceline borings**: continuous cored to 5 ft.
  - **Perimeter borings**: continuous cored to 3 ft.
  - **Cadmium borings**: continuous cored to 20 ft.
- Sampling intervals:
  - Fenceline and Perimeter borings: 0–6 inches, then 1-foot intervals (1–2, 2–3, 3–4, 4–5 ft).
  - Cadmium borings: 0–6 inches, then approximately 5-foot intervals (4–5, 9–10, 14–15, 19–20 ft).
- Decontamination water:
  - equipment rinse water will be contained, sampled, analyzed for metals, and disposed of appropriately.
- Laboratory analysis:
  - Methods referenced include EPA Test Method 6010B and 7471.
  - Additional analysis may be performed after initial results are reviewed and in consultation with DTSC and RWQCB.
- Reporting:
  - Summary report limited to supplemental investigation findings.
  - Separate reporting planned for contamination extent and human health risk assessment.
  - Laboratory reports include soluble metals plus MDL/PQL values and “J-flag” estimates.

## Results
- No quantitative site contamination results are reported in this document.
- The only numerical information concerns planned investigation parameters:
  - Fenceline boring depth: 5 ft
  - Perimeter boring depth: 3 ft
  - Cadmium boring depth: 20 ft
  - Sampling intervals: 0–6 inches; then 1-ft intervals for shallow borings; approximately 5-ft intervals for cadmium borings
- The outcome of the response is procedural: DTSC/RWQCB comments were addressed through revisions in the Final Workplan, but no analytical findings are presented here.

## Limitations
- This document does not present investigation data, contamination concentrations, or risk assessment results.
- It is a comment-response letter rather than a completed study report, so there are no experimental findings to evaluate.
- Some analysis is contingent on initial results and further direction from DTSC and RWQCB.
- The response indicates that contamination extent and human health risk assessment will be handled in separate reports, limiting the scope of this document.
