## Key claims
The document is a response letter from Geocon (on behalf of Caltrans) to DTSC and RWQCB comments on a Draft Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan for soil stockpiles at Modesto (State Routes 99 and 132). The final workplan incorporates field adjustments, clarified sampling depths (fenceline at 5 ft, perimeter at 3 ft, cadmium borings at 20 ft), continuous coring protocols, containment and analysis of decontamination rinse water, and laboratory reporting with MDL, PQL, and J‑flags. Separate reports are planned for site characterization summary and human health risk assessment.

## Methodology
- **Field activities**: Site survey (Sept 17, 2012) to review staked boring locations; fenceline and perimeter borings added or adjusted based on erosion/sediment migration features.
- **Sampling depths**: Fenceline borings continuous cored to 5 ft, perimeter to 3 ft, cadmium borings to 20 ft. Sample intervals: 0–6 inches, then 1‑ft intervals (1–2, 2–3, 3–4, 4–5 ft) for fenceline/perimeter; 0–6 inches and 5‑ft intervals (4–5, 9–10, 14–15, 19–20 ft) for cadmium borings.
- **Decontamination**: Rinse water contained, sampled for metals, and disposed of appropriately.
- **Laboratory analysis**: EPA Method 6010B (metals) and 7471 (mercury) for all boring categories; additional analysis after initial results per DTSC/RWQCB direction. Soluble metals reporting includes MDL and PQL; concentrations between MDL and PQL flagged as “J‑flag” (estimated).
- **Reporting**: Summary report limited to Supplemental Site Investigation findings; nature/extent and human health risk assessment in separate documents.

## Results
No quantitative results are reported; this is a workplan response addressing regulatory comments. The final workplan only outlines planned activities and methodologies.

## Limitations
- Detection limits must be suitable for human health risk assessment and comparison to water quality goals.
- Additional laboratory analysis may be performed after review of initial results, as directed by DTSC and RWQCB.
- Separate reports are needed for full site characterization and human health risk assessment; the current workplan does not integrate previous data.
