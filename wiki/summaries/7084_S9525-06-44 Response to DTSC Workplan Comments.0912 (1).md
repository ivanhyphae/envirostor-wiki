## Key claims
- This is a response letter (Geocon, Project S9525‑06‑44) to DTSC and RWQCB comments on a Draft Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan for Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (State Routes 99 and 132, Stanislaus County).
- The response states that a September 17, 2012 site survey with DTSC, Caltrans, and Geocon resulted in added or adjusted “Fenceline” and “Perimeter” boring locations at each stockpile based on field conditions (erosion, soil/sediment migration).
- Clarifies that Fenceline borings are 5‑ft deep continuous cores, Perimeter borings are 3‑ft continuous cores, and Cadmium borings are 20‑ft deep. Sampling intervals are specified for each category.
- All equipment decontamination rinse water will be contained, sampled for metals, and disposed of appropriately.
- Laboratory analysis methodology (EPA Methods 6010B and 7471) applies to all boring categories; additional analyses may be performed after initial results and at DTSC/RWQCB direction.
- The Summary Report will be limited to findings of the Supplemental Site Investigation; separate reports will cover nature/extent of contamination and human health risk assessment.
- Laboratory reporting will include soluble metals with MDL and PQL, and all concentrations between MDL and PQL will be reported with a “J‑flag” as an estimate.

## Methodology
- **Field activities**: Site survey on September 17, 2012; staked proposed boring locations; added/adjusted Fenceline and Perimeter borings based on field observations.
- **Boring categories and depths**:
  - Fenceline borings: continuous core to 5 ft; samples from 0–6 in, then 1‑ft intervals (1–2 ft, 2–3 ft, 3–4 ft, 4–5 ft).
  - Perimeter borings: continuous core to 3 ft; same 0–6 in and 1‑ft intervals.
  - Cadmium borings: to 20 ft; samples from 0–6 in and approximate 5‑ft intervals (4–5 ft, 9–10 ft, 14–15 ft, 19–20 ft).
- **Decontamination**: Rinse water will be contained, sampled, and analyzed for metals; appropriate disposal.
- **Laboratory analysis**: EPA Method 6010B (metals) and 7471 (mercury) applied to all boring categories. Reporting includes soluble metals, MDL, PQL, and J‑flag for estimated concentrations.
- **Reporting structure**: A separate Supplemental Site Investigation Summary Report; updated Site Characterization Summary Report and Human Health Risk Assessment to be prepared separately.

## Results
- No quantitative results are presented; the document only describes planned procedural modifications in response to regulatory comments. Results (chemical concentrations, risk assessment) are to be presented in future reports.

## Limitations
- Not explicitly stated, but the workplan remains contingent on DTSC and RWQCB approval and direction for additional analyses.
- The response acknowledges that the initial analytical methodology may be supplemented after review of preliminary data and at the direction of regulatory agencies.
- The scope is limited to the Supplemental Site Investigation; longer‑term characterization and risk assessment require separate reports.

Fenceline borings, Perimeter borings, Cadmium borings, continuous coring, EPA Method 6010B, EPA Method 7471, method detection limit (MDL), practical quantitation limit (PQL), J‑flag, soluble metals, rinse water containment, site characterization, human health risk assessment
