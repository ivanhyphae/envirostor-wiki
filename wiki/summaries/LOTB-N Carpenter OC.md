## Key claims
- The document is a **Log of Test Borings (LOTB)** for the **North Carpenter Road Overcrossing (Bridge No. 2636)** in Modesto, California.
- It documents **subsurface soil conditions** at multiple boring locations along the proposed bridge alignment (“C” line).
- The boring logs support geotechnical characterization of near-surface and deeper foundation soils, including identification of **alluvial/formation units** such as the **Modesto Formation**.
- The sheet explicitly notes that the logs represent the **professional engineer/geologist’s opinion** at the sampled locations and are intended for design reference.
- Groundwater was encountered at shallow depths in some borings, and the logs indicate that conditions vary between borings and over time.

## Methodology
- **Field exploration** consisted of test borings at mapped/staked locations along the proposed bridge site.
- **Sampling methods**:
  - Relatively undisturbed samples were obtained using a **3-inch outside diameter / 2.4-inch inside diameter California Modified split-spoon sampler**.
  - Some sampling used a **2-inch outside diameter split-spoon sampler** for **Standard Penetration Test (SPT)** methods.
- **Driving equipment**: a **140-pound automatic hammer with a 30-inch drop** was used.
- **Soil classification** was based on **visual field inspection** and then **confirmed/revised with laboratory test results**.
- **Blow counts** shown are **field blow counts**; for California Modified sampler relative density interpretation, a **correction factor of 0.7** was applied.
- **Location control** was based on staking and pacing from mapped features; accuracy is limited to the measurement methods used.
- The sheet states it follows the **Caltrans Soil & Rock Logging, Classification, and Presentation Manual (2010)**.
- The log includes boring profiles with:
  - soil descriptions,
  - depths/elevations,
  - blow counts,
  - groundwater strikes,
  - termination depths,
  - benchmark/control point information,
  - and plan/profile scales.
- No formal performance metric is reported; the document is a subsurface characterization report rather than an experimental model evaluation.

## Results
- Multiple borings were logged, including **A-17-084, A-17-085, and A-17-086**.
- Example soil units encountered include:
  - **Silty sand (SM)**, often **medium dense to very dense**,
  - **Sandy silt (ML)**, often **hard/very stiff** and sometimes **moderately cemented**,
  - **Poorly graded sand (SP)**, commonly **dense to very dense**,
  - **Lean clay (CL)** and **silty clay (CL-ML)**, generally **hard to very stiff**,
  - **Clayey sand (SC)** and **silty/clayey sand (SC-SM)**.
- Several units are described as having **low plasticity** and/or **moderate cementation**.
- The logs record **groundwater** on at least some borings, with groundwater dates noted as **11-07-17** and **11-09-17**.
- Reported groundwater/reference entries include **ERi = 69%** for some borings.
- Termination depths/elevations shown in the log include:
  - **Terminated at Elev. 3.0 ft**,
  - **Terminated at Elev. 2.2 ft**,
  - **Terminated at Elev. -51.3 ft**.
- The site profile shows a sequence of **fill/asphalt/base layer near the surface** over **interbedded sands, silts, and clays**, with both dense granular strata and stiff cohesive horizons.
- The borings indicate **significant heterogeneity** in lithology and density across short horizontal distances, which is important for foundation design.

## Limitations
- The authors note that boring logs represent conditions **only at the sampled locations**; conditions **between borings may differ**.
- **Groundwater conditions may change over time**.
- Boring location accuracy is limited by **staking and pacing** from mapped features.
- Soil descriptions are partly based on **visual inspection**, which may be revised by laboratory results.
- Blow counts are **field counts** and require **sampler correction** for interpretation with the California Modified sampler.
- The document is a log sheet, so it does **not provide a broader statistical subsurface model** or site-wide uncertainty analysis.
