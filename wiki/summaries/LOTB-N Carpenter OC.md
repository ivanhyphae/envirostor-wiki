## Summary: Log of Test Borings – North Carpenter Road Overcrossing

**Source**: A-17-085, A-17-086 – Geotechnical boring log for proposed bridge No. 2636, City of Modesto, CA.  
**Format**: Single-sheet Log of Test Borings (LOTB) per Caltrans *Soil & Rock Logging, Classification, and Presentation Manual* (2010).

---

## Key claims

- Subsurface conditions for the proposed bridge consist of interbedded layers of Silty Sand (SM), Clayey Silt (ML/CL), Sandy Silt (ML), Poorly Graded Sand (SP), and Lean Clay (CL) to a termination elevation of approximately -51.3 ft (NAVD 88).
- Groundwater was encountered during drilling (noted as “GWS” on 11‑09‑17 and 11‑07‑17) at variable depths.
- Soil densities range from “loose” to “very dense” (for granular soils) and “very stiff” to “hard” (for cohesive soils), with blow counts (N‑values) from 10 to >99 per foot.
- Standard Penetration Test (SPT) and Modified California sampling methods were used; sampler correction factor of 0.7 was applied for relative density classification of the California Modified sampler.
- Visual field classification was confirmed or revised by laboratory testing. The log represent the opinion of the professional engineer/geologist at the specific boring locations.

---

## Methodology

- **Boring Layout**: Three borings located at Sta 19+21, Sta 16+60, and Sta 18+01, each 56.0 ft left of the “C” line. Locations determined by staking and pacing from mapped features; accuracy limited by the measuring method.
- **Sampling**: Relatively undisturbed samples obtained using:
  - 3‑in. O.D. / 2.4‑in. I.D. split‑spoon **California Modified** sampler (Modified California method).
  - 2‑in. O.D. split‑spoon **Standard Penetration Test** sampler.
- **Driving Equipment**: 140‑lb automatic hammer with 30‑in. drop.
- **Classification**: Visual field classification per Caltrans manual, confirmed/revised by laboratory tests.
- **Blow Counts**: Field blow counts reported; relative density determined using a sampler correction factor of 0.7 for the California Modified sampler.
- **Vertical Datum**: NAVD 88 (Benchmark Control Point #40, Elev. 84.360 ft).

## Limitations

- **Spatial variability**: Conditions between adjacent test holes and at other locations may differ from those shown.
- **Temporal change**: Groundwater conditions may change with time (the logs record conditions only at the time of drilling).
- **Location accuracy**: Boring coordinates were determined by staking and pacing from mapped features; accuracy is limited by the measuring methods used.
- **Interpretation**: The logs represent the opinion of the professional engineer/geologist; laboratory tests may have revised field classifications.
- **Sampler correction**: The relative density scale for California Modified samples uses a factor of 0.7; direct comparison with SPT N‑values requires adjustment.
- **Single sheet**: This is one log sheet; it does not include detailed laboratory data, strength parameters, or recommendations.
