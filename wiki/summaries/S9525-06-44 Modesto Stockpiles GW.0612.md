### Combined Summary

This document reports on the quarterly groundwater monitoring conducted at the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (also associated with the Caltrans SR132 project) on March 12, 2012. Samples were collected from eight monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-8) to assess water quality.

**Methodology and Laboratory Analysis**
The groundwater samples were analyzed by multiple laboratories (ATL Global and Excelchem Environmental Labs) using EPA methods for dissolved metals (EPA 6020/200.7) and total metals (EPA 200.7), mercury (EPA 7470), semivolatile organic compounds (EPA 8270-SIM), anions (EPA 300.0), and standard methods for alkalinity, sulfide, and total dissolved solids. The CVRWQCB also collected split samples for validation. Quality control was assessed through blanks, laboratory control samples, duplicates, and matrix spikes.

**Key Results**
The monitoring revealed several key findings:
- **Flow and Geochemistry:** Groundwater flows toward the southeast at a gradient of 0.0008. Stiff diagrams indicate a shift from calcium-dominant water upgradient to sodium-dominant water downgradient of the FMC site.
- **Metals:** No PAHs were detected. Dissolved metals like barium (39–340 µg/l), vanadium (18–37 µg/l), and strontium (390–1,800 µg/l) were present but below thresholds. However, manganese in well MW-4 (88 µg/l) exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/l. Total and dissolved calcium, sodium, and magnesium were found at elevated concentrations across most wells.
- **Inorganics:** Nitrate levels exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/l in wells MW-1 (12 mg/l), MW-5 (27 mg/l), and MW-6 (18 mg/l). Total dissolved solids (TDS) exceeded the 500 mg/l secondary MCL in several wells (MW-1, MW-4, MW-5, MW-6).
- **Other Compounds:** Mercury was detected at low levels (0.23–0.41 µg/L) in several wells, but results are considered questionable due to contamination in the equipment blank.

**Quality Control Limitations**
Several data quality issues were noted, which qualify the results:
- **Equipment Blank:** Mercury (0.31 µg/l) and sodium were detected in the equipment blank, rendering mercury results as estimated and likely over-represented.
- **Matrix Interference:** Spike recoveries for dissolved calcium (up to 272%), sodium (up to 763%), and manganese were outside acceptable limits, indicating possible matrix interference in some samples.
- **Other Issues:** The CVRWQCB split sample for MW-7 was broken during transport, and a result for molybdenum in MW-6 was estimated due to method blank contamination. Some analytes were reported below the practical quantitation limit (J-flagged).
