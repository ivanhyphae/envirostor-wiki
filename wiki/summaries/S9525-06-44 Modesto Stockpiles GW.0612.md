## Combined Summary of "raw/S9525-06-44 Modesto Stockpiles GW.0612.pdf"

This document reports the March 2012 quarterly groundwater monitoring event at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Site. The monitoring was conducted under DTSC-approved protocols and involved eight monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-8) installed in 2006 adjacent to three soil stockpiles. Samples were collected on March 12 and 14, 2012, and analyzed by two laboratories: Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) and Excelchem Environmental Laboratories.

### Key Claims
- No polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) were detected above practical quantitation limits (PQLs) in any sample.
- Dissolved metals (barium, vanadium, strontium, chromium, mercury) were detected but did not exceed drinking water thresholds. Manganese in well MW-4 (88 µg/l) exceeded the California Secondary MCL for taste and odor (50 µg/l).
- Nitrate as nitrogen exceeded the Primary MCL of 10 mg/l in wells MW-1 (12 mg/l), MW-5 (27 mg/l), and MW-6 (18 mg/l). The upgradient well MW-1 may represent natural background.
- Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) exceeded the Secondary MCL of 500 mg/l in wells MW-1, MW-4, MW-5, and MW-6.
- Stiff diagrams from 2006 and 2012 reveal two water types: calcium-dominant beneath the stockpiles and upgradient (MW-1), and sodium-dominant downgradient of the neighboring FMC facility (MW-3, MW-6, MW-8), indicating a hydrogeochemical influence.
- Groundwater flow in the shallow upper aquifer is generally southeast, with a hydraulic gradient of approximately 0.0008 for this event.

### Methodology
- **Experimental Setup:** Quarterly monitoring of eight wells screened in the shallow zone of the upper aquifer (Modesto Formation) at ~35 feet below ground surface.
- **Field Activities (March 12, 2012):** Depth to groundwater, dissolved oxygen (DO), and oxygen-reduction potential (ORP) measured. Wells were purged with a submersible pump (~3 well volumes; MW-5 went dry after 1 gallon). Samples collected with disposable bailers; dissolved metals field-filtered (0.45 µm). Purge water monitored for pH, conductivity, temperature, and turbidity. An equipment blank was collected.
- **Analytical Methods:**
  - Dissolved metals: EPA Method 6020/7470 (ICP-MS/Cold Vapor AA) – Title 22 metals plus strontium, calcium, magnesium, potassium, sodium.
  - General minerals: EPA 300.0 (chloride, nitrate, sulfate); SM 4500 (sulfide); SM 2540C (TDS); SM 2320B (alkalinity).
  - PAHs: EPA Method 8270-SIM (GC/MS).
  - Additional metals analysis by Excelchem via EPA 200.7 (ICP-OES).
- **Quality Control:** Blanks, laboratory control samples (LCS), matrix spikes (MS/MSD), duplicates (DUP). Flags applied for estimated values (J), method blank detections (B), and out-of-range recoveries (M1, R, S1).

### Results
- **PAHs:** Not detected in any sample.
- **Dissolved Metals (key ranges):**
  - Barium: 39–340 µg/l (MCL 1,000 µg/l)
  - Vanadium: 18–37 µg/l (Notification Level 50 µg/l)
  - Strontium: 390–1,800 µg/l (EPA Health Advisory 4,000 µg/l)
  - Chromium: 4.4–9.5 µg/l (MCL 50 µg/l)
  - Mercury: 0.23–0.41 µg/l (MCL 2 µg/l; results estimated due to blank contamination)
  - Manganese: 88 µg/l in MW-4 only (Secondary MCL 50 µg/l)
  - Other Title 22 metals not detected.
- **General Minerals:**
  - Nitrate: MW-1 (12 mg/l), MW-5 (27 mg/l), MW-6 (18 mg/l) – all above MCL of 10 mg/l.
  - TDS: MW-1 (550 mg/l), MW-4 (530 mg/l), MW-5 (700 mg/l), MW-6 (680 mg/l) – above Secondary MCL of 500 mg/l; other wells ranged 273–460 mg/l.
  - Sulfide detected in some samples (e.g., MW-4: 0.172 mg/L; MW-5: 0.0778 mg/L).
- **Hydraulic Gradient:** 0.0008 to the southeast; groundwater elevations from 47.98 ft (MW-8) to 50.14 ft (MW-1).
- **Data Quality:** Mercury and sodium results qualified as estimated due to equipment blank contamination; molybdenum in MW-6 estimated due to method blank; nitrobenzene-d5 surrogate recoveries >130% (no target SVOCs detected, minimal impact); some matrix spike recoveries out of limits (e.g., dissolved calcium and sodium) attributed to high native concentrations.

### Limitations
- **Mercury Results:** Detected in the equipment blank at 0.31 µg/l, similar to sample values; all results considered estimated and potentially over-representative.
- **Sodium Results:** Also detected in equipment blank; all sodium concentrations considered estimated and potentially less than reported.
- **Molybdenum (MW-6):** Estimated due to method blank contamination.
- **MW-5 Purging:** Went dry after 1 gallon; less than 3 well volumes purged, potentially affecting sample representativeness.
- **Matrix Spike Issues:** Some recoveries and RPDs outside limits; no data qualification deemed necessary for metals not analyzed on the affected date.
- **Reporting Limits:** Dilutions required for some samples (e.g., MW-5, MW-6) due to matrix interference, raising detection limits for trace constituents.
