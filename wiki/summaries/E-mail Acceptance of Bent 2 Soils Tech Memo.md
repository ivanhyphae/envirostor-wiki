## Key claims
- The Technical Memorandum evaluated whether excavated Bent 2 soils from the SR 132 project could be reused as clean cap material.
- DTSC and the Water Board concluded the sampled soils **do not meet the clean cap criteria** from the April 9, 2020 Variance Request.
- Despite failing clean cap criteria, the agencies agreed the soil **may be placed on top of Stockpile 2 at a thickness of 6 inches or less**, provided it is covered with clean base material and the roadway’s asphalt/concrete.
- DTSC’s acceptance was contingent on strict implementation of stormwater and erosion control measures, especially if soils are temporarily exposed during winter.

## Methodology
- The memo appears to be based on **analytical results** from Bent 2 column excavation soils.
- The review process involved **agency evaluation** by DTSC and the Water Board of the Technical Memorandum dated October 30, 2020.
- The acceptance decision also referenced:
  - the **April 9, 2020 Variance Request** approved by DTSC,
  - the **Revised Stormwater Prevention Plan (SWPPP) Amendment** dated October 22, 2020.
- No explicit numerical sampling methods, dataset size, or laboratory analytical details are provided in the email text.

## Results
- The soils from the Bent 2 column excavation **exceeded background criteria** for use as clean cap cover material.
- Consequently, the soils **did not meet clean cap criteria**.
- Allowed reuse condition: placement on Stockpile 2 at **≤ 6 inches thickness**, if covered by clean base material and asphalt/concrete.
- DTSC issued this email as **official acceptance** of the Technical Memorandum and Revised SWPPP Amendment.

## Limitations
- The soils are **not suitable as clean cap material** under the established criteria.
- Temporary winter exposure was acknowledged as a risk, requiring strict adherence to rainfall surface water sampling and erosion controls.
- The approval is conditional and does **not** indicate unrestricted reuse; it is limited to a capped placement scenario.
- The source text does not include detailed analytical tables, contaminant concentrations, or full sampling methodology.
