## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Caltrans Modesto Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles (the site), located near the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. The document addresses three historical soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) that contain barium-contaminated fill material from a former FMC Corporation processing plant. The document provides a comparative evaluation of groundwater data for these stockpiles.
- **Media addressed:** Groundwater.
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** Caltrans monitoring wells MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10 (the four remaining active wells). Also references the six previously decommissioned wells (MW1, MW2, MW3, MW5, MW7, MW8) and FMC facility-related wells (M-101, M-113, M-154, M-157).

## Key claims
- **Claim 1:** The encapsulated soil stockpiles do not have the potential to impact groundwater, and future monitoring data from the four remaining Caltrans wells will not provide any useful data.
    - **Claimant:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (on behalf of Caltrans)
    - **Supported by data:** Yes. The report states that post-2014 data confirms constituent concentrations have remained stable and consistent with the 2014 evaluation.
- **Claim 2:** The groundwater impacts observed in Caltrans wells (MW5, MW6, MW10) did not originate from the Caltrans soil stockpiles.
    - **Claimant:** 2014 Evaluation (EMKO Environmental) – cited and affirmed by Geocon in this update.
    - **Supported by data:** Yes. The report states that decreasing trends in these wells, despite no changes to the stockpiles, shows the reductions are caused by the FMC groundwater extraction and treatment system.
- **Claim 3:** The Caltrans monitoring wells are often dry due to decreasing regional groundwater levels.
    - **Claimant:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
    - **Supported by data:** Yes. Table 1 documents numerous occasions where wells had insufficient water to sample, including MW10 (dry for 9 of 11 measurements), MW9 (dry for 8 of 11), MW6 (dry for 6 of 11), and MW4 (dry for 4 of 11) since 2014.
- **Claim 4:** Barium concentrations in the remaining Caltrans wells are well below the EPA drinking-water health advisory.
    - **Claimant:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
    - **Supported by data:** Yes. Figure 5 shows all barium concentrations are below 200 µg/L, compared to the EPA DWHA of 700 µg/L and the FMC-specific target of 6,210 µg/L.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units:** Groundwater. Total dissolved solids (TDS) in mg/L, sulfate (SO4) in mg/L, barium (Ba) in µg/L, strontium (Sr) in µg/L. The most recent non-dry sampling data (from 2018-2021) is as follows:
    - **MW4 (3/11/2021):** TDS=530 mg/L, SO4=20 mg/L, Ba=160 µg/L, Sr=930 µg/L.
    - **MW6 (3/11/2021):** TDS=620 mg/L, SO4=48 mg/L, Ba=130 µg/L, Sr=720 µg/L.
    - **MW9 (2/28/2020):** TDS=560 mg/L, SO4=42 mg/L, Ba=180 µg/L, Sr=1100 µg/L.
    - **MW10 (4/11/2018):** TDS=630 mg/L, SO4=67 mg/L, Ba=140 µg/L, Sr=1100 µg/L.
- **Applicable thresholds (MCLs, screening levels, action levels, background):**
    - **TDS:** Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L.
    - **Sulfate:** Secondary MCL of 250 mg/L.
    - **Barium:** EPA Drinking Water Health Advisory (DWHA) of 700 µg/L. Site-specific FMC level: 6,210 µg/L. FMC background concentration: 151 µg/L.
    - **Strontium:** EPA DWHA of 4,000 µg/L. Site-specific FMC level: 61,900 µg/L.
- **Which wells or locations exceeded thresholds and by how much:** No wells exceeded the primary action levels (EPA DWHAs or FMC site-specific targets). TDS in several wells (e.g., MW6 at 620 mg/L, MW10 at 630 mg/L) exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L.
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations affecting interpretation:** A significant, stated limitation is that wells were "DRY" for multiple sampling events, preventing the collection of data since 2021 for MW4 and MW6, and as far back as 2018 for MW10. No other QA/QC flags are noted.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** Consolidation and encapsulation of soil stockpiles.
- **Quantities:** Stockpile 3 was removed, and its material was placed on Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- **Verification sampling outcomes:** None reported; the report focuses on groundwater monitoring, not verification of the encapsulation.
- **Phase status:**
    - **Completed:** Stockpile 3 removal; encapsulation of Stockpiles 1 and 2 beneath the SR 132 roadway with clean fill and vegetation (Phase I construction, completed October 2021).
    - **Planned:** Phase II construction (scheduled to begin 2026) will fully encapsulate stockpiles under pavement and retaining walls. This will require removal of well MW9 and MW10.
    - **Recommended:** Decommissioning of wells MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10.
