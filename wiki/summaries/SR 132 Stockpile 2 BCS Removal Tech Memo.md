This technical memorandum details the removal of barium-containing soil (BCS) from two areas (west and east ends) of Stockpile 2 at the SR132 site, with verification against thresholds of 1,000 mg/kg for barium and 80 mg/kg for lead. Initial excavation samples exceeded these thresholds at several locations (e.g., S2W 1-0: 3,200 mg/kg Ba, S2E 8-0: 4,100 mg/kg Ba), requiring deepened excavation to achieve compliance. Final verification samples from the base of the excavations showed barium concentrations ranging from 54 to 760 mg/kg and lead from 1.5 to 35 mg/kg, all below the respective thresholds.

Methodology involved excavating the west end from an initial elevation of 82 feet to 81 feet MSL, and the east end from 83 feet to 81 feet in spots. Verification soil samples were collected using stainless steel tubes, transported under chain of custody to Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL), and analyzed for total barium and lead by EPA Method 6010B (ICP-AES) after acid digestion (EPA 3050B). Quality control included blank, LCS, matrix spike, and matrix spike duplicate analyses; practical quantitation limits (PQL) were 1.0 mg/kg for both analytes, with method detection limits (MDL) of 0.12 mg/kg (barium) and 0.18 mg/kg (lead).

Results show that while initial samples contained elevated barium (up to 4,100 mg/kg) and lead (up to 35 mg/kg), re-excavation and re-sampling brought all concentrations below the verification thresholds. One sample (S2E 8-0) required a dilution factor of 2 due to high barium. LCS recoveries were within 80–120%, though a matrix spike recovery for one analysis fell outside acceptance limits (attributed to matrix interference, with the batch validated by the LCS). Results are reported on a wet weight basis unless otherwise specified.

Key concepts include barium-containing soil (BCS), verification thresholds for groundwater protection, EPA Method 6010B, chain of custody protocols, and laboratory qualifiers such as ND (not detected), PQL, and MDL. The memo does not explicitly state limitations beyond the noted matrix interference and dilution requirements, and it emphasizes that all final samples met the residential screening levels for the project.
