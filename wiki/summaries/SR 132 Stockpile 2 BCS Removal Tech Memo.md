# Summary: Stockpile 2 BCS Removal Verification Testing, State Route 132 Project

## Key claims

- Barium-containing soil (BCS) removal verification testing was completed at the west and east ends of Stockpile 2 within the State Route 132 right-of-way.
- Excavation was conducted to facilitate construction of bridge overcrossing structures; removed BCS was placed as embankment fill within designated Stockpile 2 fill containment zones.
- Final verification soil samples from both excavation areas all contained barium and lead concentrations below the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) verification thresholds (barium: 1,000 mg/kg; lead: 80 mg/kg).
- Barium concentrations in final samples ranged from 54 to 760 mg/kg; lead concentrations ranged from 1.5 to 35 mg/kg.
- The BCS removal at both ends of Stockpile 2 has met the verification thresholds and is suitable for placement of clean embankment fill.

## Methodology

- **Experimental setup**: Excavation of BCS from two areas: west end of Stockpile 2 (adjacent to Emerald Avenue) and east end of Stockpile 2 (adjacent to State Route 99).
- **Excavation depths and sample counts**:
  - West end: initially excavated to ~82 ft above MSL (8 samples); after elevated barium in two samples, deepened to ~81 ft above MSL (2 additional samples).
  - East end: eastern half excavated to ~83 ft above MSL (6 samples); after elevated barium in one sample, western half excavated to ~82 ft above MSL (7 additional samples); after another elevated result, deepened area to ~81 ft above MSL (1 additional sample).
- **Sample collection**: Verification soil samples collected from base of excavation using new stainless steel sample tubes, transported to Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) under chain of custody protocol.
- **Analytical method**: Each soil sample analyzed for total barium and lead using EPA Test Method 6010B (Inductively Coupled Plasma Atomic Emission Spectroscopy).
- **Verification thresholds**: Barium ≤ 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection); Lead ≤ 80 mg/kg (residential screening level).
- **Minimum number of verification samples**: West end – 8; East end – 12 (per RDIP).
- **Location documentation**: Sample locations depicted on attached plan sheets (not included in summary).

## Results

- **West end Stockpile 2**:
  - Initial samples S2W 1-0 (barium = 3,200 mg/kg) and S2W 3-0 (barium = 1,100 mg/kg) exceeded the barium threshold.
  - After deepening to ~81 ft MSL, re-samples S2W 1A-0 (barium = 140 mg/kg) and S2W 3A-0 (barium = 64 mg/kg) were below threshold.
  - All final samples: barium range 54–430 mg/kg; lead range 1.5–2.8 mg/kg.
- **East end Stockpile 2**:
  - Initial sample S2E 3-0 (barium = 1,400 mg/kg) exceeded barium threshold; area deepened.
  - Second round: S2E 8-0 (barium = 4,100 mg/kg) exceeded threshold; area deepened further.
  - After deepening to ~81 ft MSL, re-sample S2E 8A-0 (barium = 63 mg/kg) passed.
  - All final samples: barium range 63–760 mg/kg; lead range 1.6–35 mg/kg.
- **Overall compliance**: All final excavation soil samples contained barium below 1,000 mg/kg and lead below 80 mg/kg.

## Limitations

- Matrix spike recoveries for barium in two analytical batches (B0B0533 and B0B0535) were outside acceptance limits (15700% and 212%, respectively) due to possible matrix interference; however, the laboratory control sample validated each batch.
- One sample (S2E 3-0) required dilution (2×) due to high target analyte concentration; another sample (S2W 1-0) was run at 10× dilution.
- The memo is a project-specific technical memorandum for a construction/ remediation project, not a controlled scientific study; generalizability is limited to this site and excavation.
- Verification is based on discrete point samples from excavation base; spatial variability between sample points is not addressed.
