The memo documents verification sampling and laboratory analysis for the removal of barium-containing soil (BCS) from the west and east ends of Stockpile 2 in the SR 132 right-of-way project in Modesto, California. In accordance with the January 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP), excavation areas were resampled at the native-soil interface/base of excavation to confirm that remaining soil met cleanup thresholds for barium and lead before the areas could be approved for placement of clean embankment fill.

Two excavation zones were evaluated: the west end of Stockpile 2 near Emerald Avenue and the east end near SR 99. Samples were collected using new stainless steel sample tubes, documented under chain of custody, and analyzed by Advanced Technology Laboratories for total metals using EPA Method 6010B (ICP-AES), with preparation by EPA 3050B_S. The RDIP verification thresholds were 1,000 mg/kg for barium and 80 mg/kg for lead. Planned minimum verification sampling called for 8 samples at the west end and 12 at the east end.

Initial sampling showed elevated barium in both areas, requiring additional excavation and resampling. At the west end, early samples included barium exceedances such as S2W 1-0 at 3,200 mg/kg and S2W 3-0 at 1,100 mg/kg; after re-excavation, follow-up samples like S2W 1A-0 (140 mg/kg) and S2W 3A-0 (64 mg/kg) were below the threshold. At the east end, initial exceedances included S2E 3-0 at 1,400 mg/kg and S2E 8-0 at 4,100 mg/kg; after additional excavation, follow-up samples such as S2E 3A-0 (570 mg/kg) and S2E 8A-0 (63 mg/kg) showed reduced concentrations. The laboratory certificates of analysis for the submitted samples reported generally acceptable QC performance, with blanks clean and control recoveries within acceptance criteria overall, supporting the validity of the data.

Overall, the final excavation samples from both the west and east ends of Stockpile 2 were below the RDIP verification thresholds for barium and lead, indicating that the excavated areas met the criteria for approval and could be placed back with clean embankment fill.
