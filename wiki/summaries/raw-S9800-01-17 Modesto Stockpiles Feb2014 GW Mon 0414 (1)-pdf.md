Here is a combined summary of the source document "raw/S9800-01-17 Modesto Stockpiles Feb2014 GW Mon 0414 (1).pdf".

This document is a **Groundwater Monitoring Report** prepared by **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** for the **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6**. It covers the **February 25-26, 2014** quarterly monitoring event at the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles** site, located south of the SR 99 and Kansas Avenue intersection in Stanislaus County, California. The report (Project No. S9800-01-17) documents groundwater monitoring across ten wells (MW-1 through MW-10) at the site, which contains three stockpiles of barium-contaminated fill material.

The monitoring event found that **groundwater flow direction was generally toward the southeast**, consistent with historical data. **Dissolved metals concentrations were generally below their respective water quality thresholds**, with the exception of **manganese in well MW-10**, which exceeded the Secondary MCL (73 µg/l vs. 50 µg/l). **Barium** (the primary contaminant of concern) and **strontium** concentrations were similar to historical levels and remained well below their thresholds (maximum barium: 310 µg/l vs. 1,000 µg/l MCL). However, **nitrate as nitrogen exceeded the Primary MCL of 10 mg/l in four wells** (MW-1, MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10, with MW-5 reaching 34 mg/l), and **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) exceeded the Secondary MCL of 500 mg/l in three wells** (MW-5, MW-6, and MW-10, with MW-5 reaching 750 mg/l). The report notes that nitrate in the upgradient well MW-1 (10 mg/l) may reflect natural background levels.

The laboratory analytical data, provided by **Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL)**, includes results for dissolved metals (via EPA 6020), mercury (EPA 7470A), and general chemistry parameters. Some matrix spike/matrix spike duplicate (MS/MSD) recoveries fell outside control limits, but the report concludes these were validated by laboratory control samples and the data are of sufficient quality without qualification.

The document is a monitoring report submitted to regulatory agencies (**DTSC**, **CVRWQCB**) for review; it contains no formal regulatory decisions. The next quarterly monitoring event was scheduled for **May 2014**. The report also notes that PAH analysis was discontinued after November 2012 with DTSC concurrence due to a lack of detections.
