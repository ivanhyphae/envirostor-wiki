This report, prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. for Caltrans and dated April 25, 2014, summarizes groundwater monitoring results from the February 2014 sampling event at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California (Project No. S9800-01-17). The document, which is a monitoring-only report with no remedial actions described, consists of a main report and supporting laboratory analytical data from Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL).

The groundwater sampling of ten monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) revealed that most dissolved metals and general minerals were below regulatory thresholds. Barium (48–310 µg/L), strontium (260–970 µg/L), arsenic, chromium, and nickel were all below their respective MCLs or health advisories, and lead was not detected. However, several exceedances were noted: manganese exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L in MW-10 (73 µg/L); nitrate (as N) exceeded the primary MCL of 10 mg/L in wells MW-1 (10 mg/L), MW-5 (34 mg/L), MW-6 (15 mg/L), and MW-10 (18 mg/L); and Total Dissolved Solids (TDS) exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L in wells MW-5 (750 mg/L), MW-6 (570 mg/L), and MW-10 (630 mg/L). The data also confirmed a southeast groundwater flow direction and the persistence of two distinct hydrochemical facies (calcium-dominant and sodium-dominant), as illustrated by Stiff diagrams in the appendix. While some laboratory matrix spike outliers were noted, the data were validated by acceptable laboratory control samples and deemed sufficient without qualification. The next quarterly monitoring event was scheduled for May 2014.
