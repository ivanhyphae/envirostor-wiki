## Key claims
- The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Waterboard approved the revised "Carpenter Shoofly Area Clean Fill Sampling Plan" (the Plan), received April 5, 2021.
- The Plan outlines the proposed sampling and analysis for soil at the Carpenter Shoofly location that will be used as clean fill.
- The approval states the Plan is consistent with previous borrow area sampling efforts and that DTSC has no comments.

## Methodology
The Plan (not included in the source) is described through email correspondence:
- Six boring locations were proposed at the Carpenter Shoofly area.
- At each boring, four depth‑discrete samples were to be collected from identified horizons (depths of 2 ft, 5 ft, 10 ft, and 15 ft).
- The excavation depth is 20 ft, and the total fill volume is 5,000 cubic yards.
- Sampling is to be conducted after DTSC approval; no use of the soil will occur until approval is granted.
- The Plan was prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. and forwarded by Caltrans to DTSC for review.
