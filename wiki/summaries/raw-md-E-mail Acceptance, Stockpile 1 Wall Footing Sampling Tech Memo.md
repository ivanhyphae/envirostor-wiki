## Document identity
- **Document type and purpose**: Email chain providing DTSC and Water Board acceptance of the "Stockpile 1 MSE Wall Footing Soil Characterization" Technical Memo (dated April 6, 2020). The memo characterized excavation soil from the MSE wall footing area at Stockpile 1 for potential reuse as fill or cover material at Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- **Author or preparer**: Dean Wright, P.G. (DTSC Project Manager) – acceptance email. Original memo prepared by John Juhrend, P.E., CEG, CEM (Geocon Consultants, Inc.). Forwarded by Richard Stewart (Caltrans).
- **Date or period covered**: Acceptance email dated April 7, 2020. Technical Memo dated April 6, 2020. Sampling and analysis period not specified but likely prior to April 6, 2020.

## Key claims
- The 95% Upper Confidence Limit (UCL) of barium in the sampled MSE wall footing soil exceeds the maximum background concentration of 120 mg/kg (attributed to DTSC/Water Board review; stated by Dean Wright).
- The MSE wall footing excavation area falls outside the designated borrow area described in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) (attributed to DTSC).
- Geocon (John Juhrend) originally proposed the material for use as "embankment fill and/or clean cover material" within Stockpiles 1 and 2. DTSC rejected the "clean cover material" use due to the elevated barium UCL.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Agency**: DTSC (Dean Wright) and Central Valley RWQCB (Steve Meeks – Water Board reviewed via cc).
- **Date**: April 7, 2020 (acceptance communicated by email).
- **Decision**: DTSC and the Water Board accept the Technical Memo with one condition: the MSE wall footing soil **cannot** be used as capping material for Stockpiles 1 and 2 (i.e., as clean cover). It must be placed within the Stockpile 1 and 2 fill containment zones or used as general bridge embankment fill.
- **Condition execution**: Acceptance is contingent upon field implementation of the above restriction.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminant**: Barium (primary contaminant of concern for this project).
- **Media**: Soil from MSE wall footing excavation area (Stockpile 1).
- **Concentration**: Not explicitly stated in the email, but the 95% UCL of sampled soil is above the maximum background threshold of 120 mg/kg.
- **Applicable thresholds**: Maximum background barium concentration = 120 mg/kg (site-specific).
- **Exceedances**: The 95% UCL exceeds 120 mg/kg, therefore the material is not suitable for unrestricted clean cover/ capping use.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity**: Characterization sampling along the MSE wall footing excavation area at Stockpile 1.
- **Phase**: Sampling completed; results reviewed and accepted by regulators.
- **Verification outcomes**: Material determined to be barium-impacted above background; restricted reuse options.
- **Pending work**: Once Stockpile 2 BCS (presumably "Buttress/Cap/Soil" or similar) southerly slope material is removed, additional wall footing excavation sampling will be completed in that area (stated by Geocon).

## Key terms and conditions
- The MSE wall footing excavation soil **shall not** be used as capping material (clean cover) for Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- Acceptable placement locations: within the **Stockpile 1 and 2 fill containment zones** or as **general bridge embankment fill**.
- Additional sampling of the wall footing excavation area near Stockpile 2 is required after removal of Stockpile 2 BCS southerly slope material.
- The material does not qualify as "borrow area" material per the RDIP.
