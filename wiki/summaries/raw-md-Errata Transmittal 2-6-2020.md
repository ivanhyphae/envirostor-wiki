## Document Identity
- **Document type and purpose:** Transmittal letter (Errata) — updates the January 30, 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan — Interim Phase 1 (RDIP) to reflect post-construction contract award changes in roles and responsibilities, specifically removing Geocon, Inc. as Caltrans' agent for environmental services.
- **Author or preparer:** Carl Hartmann, Senior Environmental Planner, California Department of Transportation (Caltrans), District 6 Office, Fresno, CA.
- **Date or period covered:** February 6, 2020 (issuance date); references decision documents from June 2014 (Feasibility Study, Remedial Action Plan) and the January 2019 RDIP.

## Key Claims
- DTSC concurred with the June 2014 Feasibility Study and June 2014 Remedial Action Plan for mitigation of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles.
- The RDIP, accepted by DTSC in January 2019, established the "containment alternative" as the prescribed remedy during construction of the SR 132 West Expressway project.
- Geocon, Inc. was originally identified as Caltrans' sole agent to ensure containment was accomplished per the RAP and RDIP, but is no longer under contract with the state.
- Caltrans remains the responsible party required to demonstrate the work was performed as described in the RDIP, regardless of the consultant performing the work.

## Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision type:** Concurrence (DTSC) with June 2014 Feasibility Study and June 2014 Remedial Action Plan.
- **Decision type:** Acceptance (DTSC) of the January 2019 Remedial Design Implementation Plan — Interim Phase 1.
- **Date:** June 2014 (Feasibility Study and RAP); January 2019 (RDIP acceptance).
- **Agency:** Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).
- **Conditions imposed:** Adherence to the "containment alternative" remedy as described in the RDIP.

## Analytical Results
- No analytical results presented in this document.

## Remedial Actions
- **Activity:** Implementation of the "containment alternative" for Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles during SR 132 West Expressway construction.
- **Phase status:** Interim Phase 1 (per RDIP title); post-construction contract award stage.
- **Quantities/verification outcomes:** Not specified in this transmittal.

## Responsible Parties
- **California Department of Transportation (Caltrans):** Owner/operator; responsible party required to demonstrate work was performed per the RDIP.
- **Geocon, Inc.:** Formerly identified as Caltrans' sole agent for environmental services; no longer under contract with the state as of this transmittal.
- **Dept. of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC):** Regulator; provided concurrence/acceptance of decision documents.
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB):** Co-regulator (implied by corpus context but not explicitly named in this document).

## Key Terms and Conditions
- The Errata updates pertinent sections of the RDIP to reflect changes to previously identified roles and responsibilities, striking Geocon as the agent employed by the state.
- Caltrans retains ultimate responsibility for demonstrating compliance with the RDIP "containment alternative" regardless of the consultant performing the work.
- No deadlines, monitoring obligations, or site restrictions specified in this transmittal.
