## Analytical Results (March 24, 2017 Event)

### Dissolved Metals (µg/L) – EPA Method 6020/7470A
Results from the main report show concentrations at PL1–PL5 for antimony, arsenic, barium, cadmium, chromium, cobalt, copper, lead, manganese, molybdenum, nickel, selenium, strontium, vanadium, zinc, calcium, magnesium, potassium, and sodium. Manganese exceeded the secondary MCL (50 µg/L) at PL2 (79 µg/L) and PL4 (110 µg/L). Metals not detected above MDL included beryllium, mercury, silver, and thallium.

### General Minerals (mg/L) – EPA Method 300.0 / SM 2320B / SM 2540C/D
Chloride, sulfate, nitrate, sulfide, alkalinity, TDS, and TSS were reported. No threshold exceedances.

### Field Parameters (March 24, 2017)
pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, ORP, and DO measured at each location.

### Laboratory Report Details (ATL Global)
The attached Certificate of Analysis provides quality control results for the same analyte suites, including:
- **Quantitation limits**: PQLs and MDLs for alkalinity species (5.0/1.5 mg/L), TDS (10/10 mg/L), TSS (1.0/1.0 mg/L), chloride (0.50/0.12 mg/L), nitrate as N (0.10/0.03 mg/L), sulfate (0.50/0.11 mg/L), total sulfide (0.010/0.004 mg/L), dissolved metals (varying PQLs/MDLs), and dissolved mercury (0.20/0.13 µg/L)
- **QA/QC flags**:
    - **R**: RPD outside acceptance criteria
    - **M1**: Matrix spike recovery outside limit; batch validated by LCS
    - **J**: Analyte detected between MDL and PQL (estimated concentration)
    - **D6, D5, D2, D1**: Dilution due to high target analyte, failing internal standard, high non-target concentration, or matrix interference, respectively
- **Dilution notes for field samples**: PL1–PL5 diluted for barium, calcium, magnesium, manganese, potassium, sodium, and nitrate (matrix interference); PL4/PL5 diluted for lead and thallium (failing internal standard); PL3 diluted for chloride and sulfate (matrix interference)
- **Matrix spike issues**: Some recoveries outside 75–125% (e.g., calcium at -167%, barium at 64.6%); batch validated by LCS which met 85–115% for most metals
