## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered**: Modesto Soil Stockpiles – State Route 99 and 132, Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. The soil stockpiles consist of excess native soils and pond tailings generated during construction of a segment of SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue in the early 1960s, which traversed a portion of a 4.3‑acre parcel purchased from Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC). An evaporation pond was located at the southernmost corner of the parcel. Primary chemicals of concern (COC) are barium, lead, and strontium.
- **Media addressed**: Groundwater
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced**: Monitoring wells MW-9 (south of Kansas Avenue, west of SR 99) and MW-10 (south of Kansas Avenue, east of SR 99) were installed in June 2012 and reported in the June 2012 report. All three reports (May, June, July 2012) contain analytical results of groundwater monitoring well sampling.
