## Document identity
- **Document type:** Well Destruction Report
- **Preparer / author organization:** Geocon Consultants, Inc.
- **Recipient / addressee agency or party:** California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch, attn: Adam Inman, PG
- **Date or reporting period covered:** June 18, 2025 (report date); well destruction performed March 18, 2025
- **Project number or task order:** Geocon Project No. S2350-01-02; Caltrans Contract No. 06A2767, Task Order No. 2; EA No. 10-1E7003; GeoTracker Case # SL0609924194

## Site and scope
- **Site name, location, areas of concern covered:** Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles; south of the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue, Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. The site encompasses Stockpiles 1 and 2 (encapsulated beneath SR 132) and the former location of Stockpile 3 (removed and placed at Stockpiles 1 and 2).
- **Media addressed:** Groundwater (via monitoring well destruction)
- **Specific monitoring wells, sample locations, or work areas referenced:** Monitoring wells MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10 (destroyed in this event). Six additional wells (MW1, MW2, MW3, MW5, MW7, and MW8) were destroyed in 2019.

## Key claims
- **Claim:** The encapsulated soil stockpiles do not have the potential to impact groundwater and future groundwater monitoring data from the four wells would not provide useful data for further analysis.
    - **Who is making it:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (on behalf of Caltrans), based on a 2024 update of a 2014 comparative evaluation of groundwater data.
    - **Supported by data in this document:** Yes — the claim is based on the consistency of recent data with the 2014 evaluation, the declining water table, and the planned full encapsulation of remaining stockpiles during Phase II construction. The claim is explicitly "demonstrated" in the 2024 evaluation.
- **Claim:** The encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater and will not impact groundwater in the future.
    - **Who is making it:** Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) in a May 3, 2024 approval letter, concurring with the Geocon evaluation.
    - **Supported by data in this document:** Yes — the agencies cite the consistency of the evaluation with the earlier 2014 study and the recent encapsulation of soil beneath the Express Way as supporting this conclusion.

## Regulatory decisions
- **Decision type:** Approval of the Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data and concurrence with recommendations for well decommissioning and discontinuation of groundwater monitoring.
- **Deciding agency and date:** DTSC (in consultation with RWQCB), May 3, 2024.
- **Conditions or requirements imposed:** Destruction of the four remaining monitoring wells (MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10) is to be performed "as per the approved Remedial Design and Implementation Plan."
- **Documents approved or acted on:** "Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data, Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles" dated February 26, 2024, prepared by Geocon on behalf of Caltrans. Well destruction was subsequently performed in accordance with the approved RDIP.

## Analytical results
- **Contaminants measured, media, concentration ranges with units:** The report does not present new analytical results. It references a 2024 "comparative evaluation of groundwater data" that used data demonstrating the stockpiles do not impact groundwater. The document references barium and lead as the contaminants of concern (from the DTSC approval letter, stating "barium and lead impacted soil stockpiles").
- **Applicable thresholds:** Not discussed in this document.
- **Which wells or locations exceeded thresholds:** Not discussed in this document.
- **QA/QC flags or data quality limitations affecting interpretation:** None stated.

## Remedial actions
- **Activity:** Well destruction (decommissioning) of four groundwater monitoring wells: MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10.
- **Quantities:**
    - Well depths: MW4 = 42 ft, MW6 = 46.5 ft, MW9 = 40 ft, MW10 = 40 ft
    - All wells: 2-inch SCH 40 PVC casing; 0.010-inch slot size; #2/12 sand filter pack.
    - Grout volumes: Anticipated vs. Used: MW4 = 6.0 gal vs. 10 gal; MW6 = 6.8 gal vs. 15 gal; MW9 = 6.2 gal vs. 10 gal; MW10 = 5.7 gal vs. 17 gal.
- **Verification sampling outcomes:** No verification sampling reported.
- **Phase status:** Completed — wells were destroyed on March 18, 2025. The well destruction is part of preparations for Phase II construction of SR 132 (scheduled to begin in 2026), which will fully encapsulate the soil stockpiles beneath highway pavement and perimeter retaining walls.
