## Key claims
- The document is a Storm Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP) for evaluating the integrity of a containment system encapsulating barium‑ and lead‑contaminated soil stockpiles beneath SR 132 in Stanislaus County, California.
- Sampling will determine whether storm water runoff from the encapsulated area has been impacted by chemicals of potential concern (COPCs), thereby gauging containment effectiveness.
- The plan updates prior sampling protocols (Shaw 2006, Geocon 2013) to reflect post‑construction conditions, with new discharge and background locations.

## Methodology
- **Field activities**: Up to two storm water runoff samples per event, collected from two containment‑system discharge points (SW‑East, SW‑West) and at least one background location (BG‑West or BG‑East). Sampling occurs during qualifying rain events (≥72 hours dry prior, visible runoff) for up to four events per year.
- **Sample collection**: Use pre‑cleaned/disposable scoops; dissolved metals samples filtered in the field through 0.45‑µm filters. Samples are capped, labeled, chilled, and transported via chain‑of‑custody to Asset Laboratories.
- **Field measurements**: pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, turbidity.
- **Laboratory analyses**:
  - Title 22 dissolved metals (including barium, strontium) by EPA Method 6020/7470.
  - Sulfate by EPA Method 9056/300.0.
  - Total dissolved solids (TDS) by EPA Method 160.1.
- **QA/QC**: Method blank, duplicate, and spiked sample per every ten samples/batch/matrix; spike at ten times detection limit or analyte level.
- **Report deliverables**: Background summary, field observations, lab results, conclusions/recommendations, maps, tabular data, photographs, chain‑of‑custody records.

## Results
- No new sampling results are presented; the document is a plan only.
- Prior sampling by Stantec (March 2023) reported metal concentrations below Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) at the four post‑construction locations.

## Limitations
- The plan does not explicitly state limitations, but inherent caveats include:
  - Sampling only during qualifying rain events, which may not capture all hydrologic conditions.
  - Only two discharge points and two background locations; spatial coverage is limited.
  - No monitoring of groundwater or soil‑gas, only surface water runoff.
  - The containment system’s integrity is assessed indirectly via storm water quality; direct inspection of the encapsulation is not part of this scope.
