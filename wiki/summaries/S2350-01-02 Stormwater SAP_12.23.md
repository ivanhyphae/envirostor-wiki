## Key claims
- Presents a Storm Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP) to evaluate the integrity of the containment system encapsulating barium and lead-contaminated soil stockpiles beneath SR 132 in Stanislaus County, California.
- Storm water sample analysis will help gauge containment effectiveness and determine whether storm water has been impacted by chemicals of potential concern from the encapsulated stockpiles.
- The SAP updates prior sampling plans (2006 Shaw SAP and 2013 Geocon Addendum) to reflect post-construction conditions after SR 132 opened to traffic in November 2022.
- Prior sampling events (2006, 2013–2019, and March 2023) reported no runoff migration beyond Caltrans right-of-way (ROW) and metal concentrations below Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs).

## Methodology
- **Field activities**: Collection of up to two storm water runoff samples from two discharge points (SW-East and SW-West) that capture runoff from the SR 132 segment where stockpile containment was constructed. Samples collected during up to four separate qualifying rainfall events per year. At least one background sample collected per event from BG-West or BG-East.
- **Sample collection and handling**: Pre-cleaned or disposable scoop; samples for dissolved metals filtered through 0.45-micron filter in the field. Samples capped, labeled, chilled, and transported to Asset Laboratories under chain-of-custody. Field monitoring of pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, and turbidity.
- **Laboratory analyses** (standard turnaround):
  - Title 22 dissolved metals (including barium and strontium) by EPA Method 6020/7470.
  - Sulfate by EPA Method 9056/300.0.
  - Total dissolved solids (TDS) by EPA Method 160.1.
- **QA/QC**: Method blank, sample duplicate, and spiked sample (spike at 10× detection limit or analyte level) per ten samples/batch/matrix.

## Results
- Prior sampling results are referenced from earlier reports:
  - March 2006 (Shaw): 7 runoff samples from constructed impoundments; analyzed for dissolved metals, PAHs, nitrate, sulfate, sulfide.
  - April 2013 to April 2019 (Geocon): 5 surface runoff and 2 background locations; no runoff migration from Caltrans ROW observed.
  - March 2023 (Stantec, first post-construction event): 4 water samples from 2 discharge and 2 background locations. All reported metal concentrations in surface water samples were **below their Primary and Secondary MCLs**.
- This SAP describes a plan for future sampling; no new quantitative results are presented in this document.

## Limitations
- The SAP is a plan document; it does not contain results from the proposed sampling activities.
- Sampling is limited to storm water runoff from specific drainage discharge points; groundwater or soil sampling is not included.
- The plan only addresses the two encapsulated stockpiles (Stockpiles 1 and 2); Stockpile 3 was excavated and placed atop Stockpiles 1 and 2 prior to encapsulation.
- Sampling is contingent on qualifying rain events (visible runoff after 72 hours of dry weather), which may introduce scheduling constraints.
- The plan acknowledges that additional samples may be collected if runoff is observed leaving the Caltrans ROW, indicating a reactive rather than fully proactive monitoring design.
