## Key claims
- The paper proposes a Storm Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP) for the former Caltrans Modesto soil stockpiles in Stanislaus County, California, now encapsulated beneath the SR 132 West Expressway project.
- The purpose of the sampling is to evaluate the integrity and effectiveness of the DTSC-approved stockpile containment system and determine whether stormwater has been impacted by chemicals of potential concern from the encapsulated barium- and lead-contaminated soils.
- The SAP follows prior DTSC-approved protocols from the 2006 Surface Water SAP and the 2013 addendum, but updates the sampling design to reflect post-construction site conditions.
- The planned work includes stormwater runoff sampling, laboratory analysis by a California-certified lab, and reporting of observations, analytical data, and conclusions.

## Methodology
- **Site context:** Former FMC/Barium Products industrial property in Modesto; contaminated soils were excavated historically and stockpiled by Caltrans, then encapsulated during SR 132 construction.
- **Sampling design:**  
  - Up to **2 stormwater runoff samples** per qualifying rain event from:
    - **SW-East:** discharge point on north side of SR 132 west of SR 99
    - **SW-West:** discharge pipe north side of SR 132 east of Ponding Basin 5
  - At least **1 background sample** per event from:
    - **BG-West:** east side of North Carpenter Road, ~700 ft south of Kansas Ave
    - **BG-East:** near northeast corner of North Emerald Ave and Loletta Ave
  - Sampling may occur during up to **4 rainfall events per year** if needed.
- **Field procedures:** Samples collected during qualifying rain events using pre-cleaned or disposable scoops, transferred to lab containers, filtered in the field for dissolved metals using **0.45-micron filtration**, capped, labeled, chilled, and transported under chain-of-custody.
- **Field measurements:** pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, and turbidity.
- **Laboratory analyses:** Asset Laboratories to analyze for:
  - **Title 22 dissolved metals** (including barium and strontium) via **EPA 6020/7470**
  - **Sulfate** via **EPA 9056/300.0**
  - **Total dissolved solids (TDS)** via **EPA 160.1**
- **QA/QC:** Method blanks, duplicate samples, and matrix spikes at a frequency of **1 per 10 samples/batches/type of matrix**, whichever is more frequent.
- **Reporting:** A summary report after each sampling event including background, field observations, analytical results, conclusions, maps, tables, photos, lab reports, and chain-of-custody documentation.

## Results
- This document is a **sampling and analysis plan**, so it does **not present new analytical results** from the proposed sampling program.
- It summarizes prior findings:
  - **2006 Shaw sampling:** **7 runoff samples** collected during a qualifying rain event; analyzed for dissolved metals, PAHs, nitrate, sulfate, and sulfide.
  - **2013–2019 Geocon sampling:** samples from **5 runoff locations** and **2 background locations**; no observed runoff migration from the Caltrans ROW during inspections.
  - **March 2023 Stantec sampling:** **4 water samples** from **2 stormwater discharge locations** and **2 background locations**; reported metal concentrations were **below Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs)**.

## Limitations
- The SAP is a plan, not a results paper; it provides **no new measured stormwater data** from the proposed sampling campaign.
- Sampling is **event-dependent** and limited to qualifying rain events, so actual sample collection frequency may vary and may not occur during every year as planned.
- The report notes that sampling will be based on current drainage conditions and may need to adapt if **runoff is observed leaving the Caltrans ROW**, implying site hydrology could change.
- Prior sampling locations became unusable after roadway construction, requiring relocation of sampling points, which may limit direct comparability with earlier data.
