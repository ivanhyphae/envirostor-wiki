## Key claims
- DTSC approves Alternative 4 (Containment) as the selected remedy for Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles.
- Containment is achieved by constructing retaining walls, bridge abutments, and roadway over stockpiles; unpaved areas receive clean fill cover.
- The remedy eliminates the exposure pathway to human receptors and minimizes surface water infiltration.
- The RAP satisfies CEQA requirements; the EIR/EA is certified with a Finding of No Significant Impact under NEPA.
- DTSC independently determined no significant environmental effect with implementation of controls.

## Methodology
- The document is a formal approval letter from DTSC for a Remedial Action Plan (RAP).
- The remedy involves physical containment through construction and capping.
- Regulatory compliance is assessed under CEQA and NEPA.

## Results
- Formal approval of the Remedial Action Plan with specific conditions, including a RDIP before each phase, start work notices (English/Spanish) two weeks before construction, OMA/OMP after Phase 1, a land use covenant, annual pavement inspection and groundwater monitoring, and five-year remedy evaluations.
- Phased implementation: Phase 1 (2019–2020) and Phase 2 (2028–2029).
