## Document identity
- **Document type:** Storm Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP), submitted under Contract No. 06A2767, Task Order No. 2, EA No. 10-1E7003.
- **Purpose:** Outline storm water sampling activities to evaluate the integrity of the barium/lead-contaminated soil containment system encapsulated beneath SR 132 in Stanislaus County, California, and determine whether storm water has been impacted by chemicals of potential concern.
- **Author/preparer:** Geocon Consultants, Inc. (Rebecca L. Silva, Project Manager; Josh Ewert, PG, Senior Geologist).
- **Date:** December 13, 2023. Period covered: ongoing sampling events (up to four per year) following the November 2022 opening of SR 132.

## Key claims
- **Geocon asserts** that the containment system (approved by DTSC) isolates barium- and lead-contaminated soil behind retaining walls, bridge abutments, beneath roadway pavements, and under clean soil.
- **Geocon states** that prior surface water sampling (Stantec, March 2023) reported metal concentrations below Primary and Secondary Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs).
- **Geocon claims** that during inspections from 2013 to 2019, no runoff migration was observed from the Caltrans right-of-way (ROW).

## Regulatory decisions
- **DTSC** approved the original containment system design and the 2006 *Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan* (Shaw Environmental) and the 2013 *Addendum to Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan* (Geocon). The current SAP follows those approved protocols.
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)** approved the sample locations used in prior events.
- No new regulatory decisions are documented in this SAP; it is a monitoring plan under existing approvals.

## Analytical results
- **Prior results (Stantec, March 2023):** Dissolved metal concentrations in storm water at four locations (two discharge points, two background) were below Primary and Secondary MCLs. Specific concentrations not provided in this document.
- **Proposed analytes for future events:**
  - Title 22 dissolved metals (including barium and strontium) by EPA Method 6020/7470
  - Sulfate by EPA Method 9056/300.0
  - Total dissolved solids (TDS) by EPA Method 160.1
- **Field measurements:** pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, turbidity.
- **No exceedances noted** in this document; prior results were compliant.

## Remedial actions
- **Historical:** During the 1930s–1970s, FMC (formerly Barium Products Ltd. and Westvaco) discharged liquid residue to unlined ponds. In 1961, Caltrans purchased a 4.3-acre parcel for SR 99 ROW. Soil from those ponds was stockpiled at three locations (Stockpile 1, 2, 3).
- **Earthwork (Phase I):** January 2020 – October 2021. Soil from Stockpile 3 was excavated and placed atop Stockpiles 1 and 2. Stockpiles 1 and 2 were completely encapsulated beneath the structural section of SR 132 and with clean soil/vegetation on northern side slopes.
- **Construction completion:** SR 132 opened to traffic in November 2022. No new remedial actions are described in this SAP; it is a post-construction monitoring plan.

## Key terms and conditions
- **Sampling frequency:** Up to two samples per event from two designated storm water discharge points (SW-East, SW-West) plus one background sample (BG-West or BG-East) during up to four qualifying rain events per year. A qualifying rain event requires visible runoff after 72 hours of dry weather.
- **Field procedures:** Samples collected with pre-cleaned/disposable scoops into laboratory-provided containers. Samples for dissolved metals field-filtered (0.45-micron filter). Field monitoring of pH, electrical conductivity, temperature, turbidity. Additional samples if runoff observed leaving Caltrans ROW.
- **QA/QC:** One method blank per ten samples/batch; one duplicate per ten samples/batch; one spike per ten samples/batch (spike at 10× detection limit or analyte level). Chain-of-custody documentation required.
- **Reporting:** Draft report submitted after each sampling event; final report after Caltrans review and incorporation of comments. Report to include background, scope, observations, analytical results, conclusions/recommendations, maps, tables, photos, and lab documentation.
