**Combined Summary of “raw/S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles March 2018 SW Sampling_04.18.pdf”**

Surface water sampling was conducted at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles in Stanislaus County on March 22, 2018, during a light rain event (0.53 inch total). Stormwater runoff was retained within the Caltrans right-of-way with no observed offsite migration. Samples were collected from five locations (PL1–PL4 and background BG1) using disposable bailers and suction pumps; stockpile locations PL5 and BG2 could not be sampled due to insufficient water. Field parameters (pH, conductivity, temperature, turbidity, ORP, DO) were measured, and samples were field‑filtered (0.45‑micron) for dissolved metals analysis. Laboratory analyses for dissolved metals (EPA 6020/7470A) and general minerals (EPA 300.0, SM 4500‑S‑D, SM 2320B, SM 2540C/D) were performed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL), following protocols approved by DTSC and CVRWQCB. Results were compared to California Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs).

Key results showed lead exceeded the primary MCL (15 µg/L) at location PL3 (46 µg/L). Manganese exceeded the secondary MCL (50 µg/L) at four locations (PL2–PL4 and BG1), with concentrations ranging from 54 to 370 µg/L. Dissolved metals such as calcium (15,000–24,000 µg/L), magnesium, and sodium were detected, but no other metals exceeded respective MCLs. General minerals, including total dissolved solids (120–140 mg/L), total suspended solids (up to 740 mg/L at PL2), and nitrate‑nitrogen (0.19–1.7 mg/L), were within previously reported ranges. Mercury was not detected, and sulfide was found only in PL2 and PL3 (0.085–0.10 mg/L).

Limitations include the single March 23, 2018 sampling event (temporal variability unassessed), several results flagged “J” (estimated concentrations between the method detection limit and practical quantitation limit), and samples diluted for metals and sulfide due to matrix interference. The gate valve at PL5 was not opened, preventing assessment of potential discharge. Not all analytes were measured at every event, and some stockpile locations were dry. A background location (e.g., BG1) was included but some early data were not collected.

Concepts central to the document include dissolved metals, Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), surface water sampling, stormwater runoff management, field filtration, chain‑of‑custody, and the GeoTracker database.
