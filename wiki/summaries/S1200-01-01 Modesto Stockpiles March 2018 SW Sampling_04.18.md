The document reports on surface water sampling conducted at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles on March 22, 2018, during a rain event. Samples were collected from five pre-approved locations (PL1-PL4 and BG1) using bailers and field filtration for dissolved metals. PL5 and BG2 could not be sampled due to insufficient water. Laboratory analyses for dissolved metals, general minerals, and field parameters were performed by a California-certified lab.

Key results show that lead was detected at PL3 (46 µg/L), exceeding the primary drinking water MCL of 15 µg/L. Manganese exceeded the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L at four locations (PL2-PL4 and BG1), with concentrations ranging from 54 to 370 µg/L. Other metals like beryllium, cadmium, mercury, and thallium were not detected. General mineral results (chloride, sulfate, TDS) did not exceed MCLs. Field parameter results were also reported. The analytical results are generally consistent with previous sampling events from 2013–2018.

A detailed Certificate of Analysis for sample PL4 confirms these findings specific to that location, reporting a manganese concentration of 54 µg/L and noting that lead and several other metals were not detected. For PL4, TDS was 85 mg/L and TSS was 4.1 mg/L. The report notes limitations for PL4, including estimated concentrations for some metals (J flag) and matrix spike recoveries that fell outside acceptance limits (M1 flag), though the analytical batch was validated by the laboratory control sample. Overall, stormwater runoff from the stockpiles appeared to be retained within the Caltrans right-of-way, with no observed offsite migration.
