## Site and Scope

- **Site name, location, areas of concern**: Modesto Soil Stockpiles, at the intersection of State Routes 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California. The site consists of three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) placed in the early 1960s as fill for a future SR132 highway alignment; these stockpiles contain barium-impacted fill material, with additional elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3.
- **Media addressed**: Soil (stockpile soil and underlying native soil).
- **Specific sample locations / work areas referenced**:
  - Twenty-nine (29) 5-foot-deep Fenceline Borings at stockpile perimeter/fenceline locations adjacent to residential and commercial/industrial development.
  - Twenty-two (22) 3-foot-deep Perimeter Borings at perimeter and stockpile end locations.
  - Five (5) 20-foot-deep Cadmium Borings within Stockpiles 2 and 3 in areas with elevated cadmium from the 2004 PSI.

## Key Claims

- **Claim**: The stockpiles as currently managed by Caltrans on Caltrans ROW do not pose a risk to human health for: 1) Caltrans workers who access the fenced site; 2) trespassers; and 3) residents adjacent to the stockpiles.
  - **Who is making it**: DTSC, in a letter dated December 17, 2009 (responding to the Final PEA).
  - **Supported by data**: Yes, based on prior investigations and the HHRA.
- **Claim**: Groundwater beneath the site meets drinking water standards for those constituents analyzed.
  - **Who is making it**: The document (Geocon, summarizing previous investigations).
  - **Supported by data**: Yes, based on 2006 sampling results.
- **Claim**: Additional data are needed to address perimeter fenceline soil sampling for offsite/vertical migration, define lateral stockpile limits for consolidation, and investigate elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3. This data will be incorporated into the Remedial Action Plan (RAP).
  - **Who is making it**: Caltrans, DTSC, and RWQCB (identified in the July 26, 2012 meeting).
  - **Conditional**: This is a stated need, not a conclusion about site conditions.

## Analytical Results

**No new analytical data are presented in this workplan.** The document references historical results:

- **2004 Preliminary Site Investigation (PSI)** (51 borings):
  - Stockpile soil samples showed elevated barium concentrations above the commercial/industrial California Human Health Screening Level (CHHSL).
  - Elevated cadmium above the commercial/industrial CHHSL was detected in soil samples from 8 of 25 borings at Stockpile 2 and 2 of 10 borings at Stockpile 3.
- **2006 Site Investigation** (monitoring wells MW-1 through MW-8):
  - Groundwater analytical results indicated that groundwater meets drinking water standards for the constituents analyzed.
  - First encountered groundwater is present at depths between 30 and 40 feet below natural grade.
  - The average maximum stockpile fill thickness is approximately 25 feet.
- **2007 Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA)**:
  - Metals (notably barium) and PAHs identified as primary COPCs in soil.
  - None of the COPCs were deemed to pose potential health risks or hazards to current or future construction workers, offsite residents, or trespassers.
  - Cadmium was not identified as a COPC due to lack of elevated cadmium in the 2006 investigation samples.

## Remedial Actions

**No physical remediation is described or planned in this workplan.** The document is a *planning* document for an *investigation* that will provide data to support a future Remedial Action Plan (RAP).

- **Planned field activities** (investigation, not remediation):
  - 29 Fenceline Borings to 5 ft depth.
  - 22 Perimeter Borings to 3 ft depth.
  - 5 Cadmium Borings to 20 ft depth.
  - Borings will be advanced using Geoprobe direct-push rig; hand-auger may be used in inaccessible areas.
  - Backfill: 20-ft-deep borings will be backfilled with bentonite slurry; 3- and 5-ft-deep borings and hand-auger borings will be backfilled with excess soil cuttings.
- **Planned laboratory analyses**:
  - Fenceline Borings (surface and 5-ft samples): Title 22 metals and strontium (EPA Method 6010B; EPA Method 7471 for mercury).
  - Perimeter Borings (surface samples): barium (EPA Method 6010B).
  - Cadmium Borings (surface and 5-ft interval samples): barium and cadmium (EPA Method 6010B).
  - Additional total and/or soluble metals analysis may be assigned after initial data review by Caltrans, DTSC, and RWQCB.
- **Phase status**: Planned.
