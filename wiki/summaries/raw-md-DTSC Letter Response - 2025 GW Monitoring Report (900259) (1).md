## Document Identity
- **Document Type and Purpose:** DTSC Conditional Approval Letter. The document serves to formally approve two monitoring reports (Well Destruction Report and Storm Water/Surface Water Monitoring Report) for the Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles site, while imposing a condition for revision on one of the reports.
- **Author/Preparer:** Mr. William Martinez, PG, Chief, Site Evaluation and Remediation Unit, Site Mitigation and Restoration Program, DTSC. Signed with a reviewer notation from Ms. Dawn Bascomb, PG, Geological Services Branch, DTSC.
- **Date:** January 30, 2026. The period covered includes the submission dates of the reviewed reports (June 18, 2025) and monitoring activities for FY 2024/2025.

## Key Claims
- **Assertion of Remedial Adequacy (Source: DTSC/Geocon/Caltrans):** The document claims that after years of groundwater monitoring data, there is no evidence that metals (specifically barium) are leaching into groundwater from the soil stockpiles. This finding supported the decision to decommission the remaining groundwater monitoring wells.
- **Assertion of Containment Integrity (Source: Storm Water Sampling Report):** The storm water sampling results showed that all detectable metals were within reporting limits, indicating that the stockpile containment system is effectively preventing the displacement of contaminated soil into surface water.
- **Claim of Regulatory Compliance:** The submission and subsequent approval of the reports by DTSC (with conditions) confirms the ongoing compliance of Caltrans with the monitoring requirements of the site’s Remedial Action Plan.

## Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision Type:** Conditional Approval.
- **Agency:** California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), acting in coordination with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWBQCB).
- **Date of Decision:** January 30, 2026.
- **Conditions Imposed:** DTSC conditionally approves the Well Destruction Report contingent on a revision. The report must be updated to include a table comparing the estimated volume of grout to the actual volume of grout used for each of the four decommissioned wells. This serves as a performance metric for well abandonment.

- **Concentration:** Results were all "within reporting limits," meaning no concentrations of concern were detected above the lab's minimum detectable levels.
- **Exceedances:** None identified.
