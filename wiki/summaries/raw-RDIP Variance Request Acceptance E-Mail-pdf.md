## Document Identity
- **Document type:** Regulatory approval email (acceptance of RDIP Variance Request)
- **Preparer / author organization:** Dean Wright, P.G. — California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), Site Evaluation and Remediation Unit
- **Recipient / addressee:** Richard C. Stewart (California Department of Transportation, Caltrans); cc: Perry Myers (DTSC), Steven Meeks (Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board), and other Caltrans and consultant (Geocon) personnel
- **Date:** April 17, 2020 (response); original VR letter dated April 9, 2020; Caltrans submittal email dated April 13, 2020
- **Project number / task order:** SR 132 (State Route 132, Stanislaus County)

## Site and Scope
- **Site name / location:** SR 132 Modesto Soil Stockpiles remedial project, Stanislaus County, California
- **Area of concern:** Southern boundary of Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2 — mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall footing excavation area
- **Media addressed:** Soil (elevated barium in shallow fill; deeper soil within background range)
- **Specific locations referenced:**
  - Southern boundary of Stockpile 1 (0–1 ft and 1–5 ft below existing grade)
  - Southern boundary of Stockpile 2 (to be evaluated after data submission)

## Key Claims
- **Claim:** Soil from 1 to 5 feet below existing grade along the southern boundary of Stockpile 1 has a calculated 95% upper confidence limit (UCL) for barium of **113.1 mg/kg**, which falls within the range of site-specific naturally occurring background concentrations.
- **Maker:** Caltrans (through the RDIP Variance Request letter; confirmed by DTSC via email)
- **Support:** Supported by data referenced in the VR (UCL calculation); accepted by both DTSC and the Regional Water Board.
- **Secondary claim:** The upper foot (0–1 ft) of that same excavation area is not suitable as clean fill due to elevated barium and will be placed in Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2 permanent fill areas.
- **Maker:** Caltrans/VR; not contested.

## Regulatory Decisions
- **Decision type:** **Acceptance** (official DTSC acceptance of the Variance Request for Stockpile 1 soil)
- **Deciding agency:** DTSC (Dean Wright), with concurrence from the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board
- **Date of decision:** April 17, 2020 (email)
- **Conditions / requirements imposed:**
  - Soil from 1 to 5 ft depth along the southern boundary of Stockpile 1 **may be used as clean cap soil** on the project.
  - The use is explicitly limited: soil excavated for the MSE footing shall only be used as clean cover or embankment fill **within the footprints of the stockpiles** and **not at other areas of the project or hauled off-site** (condition stated in the Caltrans submittal and implicitly accepted by DTSC).
  - For Stockpile 2 southern boundary: official approval or rejection will be provided once analytical data is submitted and reviewed (conditional deferred decision).
- **Document acted on:** “Variance Request to the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (VR) dated April 9, 2020” prepared by Caltrans (attached to the April 13 email).

## Analytical Results
- **Contaminant:** Barium in soil
- **Media:** Soil (1–5 ft depth interval, southern boundary of Stockpile 1)
- **Concentration:** 95% Upper Confidence Limit (UCL) = **113.1 mg/kg**
- **Applicable threshold:** Site-specific naturally occurring background range (value not stated explicitly but the 113.1 mg/kg is stated to be within that range)
- **Exceedances:** None (within background)
- **Data quality limitations:** None noted; UCL calculation is referenced as the basis of the decision.
- **Stockpile 2:** No numerical data provided in this document; data pending.

## Remedial Actions
- **Activity described (planned):** Excavation of MSE wall footing along the southern boundaries of Stockpiles 1 and 2; segregation of soil by depth:
  - Upper 1 ft (0–1 ft) — unsuitable due to elevated barium → placed in permanent fill areas of Stockpiles 1 and 2.
  - Soil from 1–5 ft — suitable as clean cover/embankment fill within stockpile footprints.
- **Quantities:** Not specified (volumes, truckloads, dimensions not provided).
- **Verification sampling outcomes:** The UCL of 113.1 mg/kg (barium) supports the classification for Stockpile 1.
- **Phase status:** **Planned** (approved; can proceed subject to conditions for Stockpile 2 after data submission).
