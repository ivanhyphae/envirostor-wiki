## California Modified Split Spoon Sampler

### Definition

The California Modified Split Spoon Sampler is a specialized soil sampling device used in geotechnical and environmental site investigations. It is a variation of the standard split-spoon sampler (used in the Standard Penetration Test, SPT) that includes a larger-diameter, modified barrel and a replaceable liner system. The "California Modified" nomenclature refers to its design refinement, first popularized in California for obtaining relatively undisturbed soil samples in cohesionless and cohesive soils, particularly where high-quality samples are required for laboratory testing. Compared to a standard SPT sampler, the California Modified version typically has an inner diameter of 2.0 inches (50.8 mm) and an outer diameter of 2.5 inches (63.5 mm), allowing for a larger sample volume and reduced side friction during recovery.

### How It Works

The California Modified Split Spoon Sampler operates on the same principle as the conventional SPT sampler but with key design improvements:

1. **Assembly**: The sampler consists of a drive shoe (hardened steel) at the bottom, a split-barrel section (typically 18 to 24 inches long), and a head assembly (with a check valve and vent ports) at the top. The barrel is lined with a thin-walled, removable liner (often brass or stainless steel) that captures the soil sample.

2. **Penetration**: The sampler is attached to a drill rod and driven into the ground using a 140-lb (63.5 kg) hammer dropped from a height of 30 inches (0.76 m). The number of blows required for each 6-inch increment of penetration is recorded (standard N-value), though the primary purpose is sample collection, not blow count.

3. **Sample Recovery**: After driving a desired depth (typically 18 to 24 inches), the sampler is extracted. The split barrel is unscrewed, and the liner containing the soil sample is removed. The sample is then sealed (e.g., with wax caps or plastic bags) and transported to the laboratory.

4. **Key Differences from Standard SPT**: The larger bore (2.0 in vs. 1.375 in) reduces side-wall friction, minimizing disturbance during extraction. The replaceable liner facilitates handling and preserves the sample’s natural structure by preventing contact with the barrel halves.

### Variants

While the "California Modified" designation is specific, several related or alternative samplers exist:

- **Standard Split Spoon Sampler**: The original SPT sampler with a 1.375-in (35 mm) inner diameter and no liner. Less suited for high-quality samples.
- **Modified California Sampler (with Liners)**: The version described above; available in sizes such as 2.0-in or 3.0-in inner diameter for larger samples.
- **Thin-Walled Tube Sampler (Shelby Tube)**: A thin-walled, seamless tube pushed into soil (not driven) to obtain "undisturbed" cohesive samples. Offers less disturbance than driven samplers but is limited to cohesive soils.
- **Osterberg Sampler**: A piston-type sampler that reduces soil disturbance by preventing soil expansion during penetration; similar sample quality to the California Modified but more complex.
- **Denison Sampler**: A double-tube rotary sampler that provides high-quality samples in granular soils, often used for in-situ testing of gravels.

### Trade-offs

| Advantage | Limitation |
|-----------|------------|
| Larger sample volume (2.0 in diameter) allows for more representative testing (grain size, density, shear strength). | Heavier and bulkier than standard SPT sampler; may require larger rigs. |
| Replaceable liner preserves sample structure and reduces contamination risk during transport. | Liner insertion and removal can be time-consuming in the field. |
| Can be driven using standard SPT hammer and rods, minimizing additional equipment. | The driving process still causes some soil disturbance, especially in loose sands or sensitive clays. |
| Suitable for a wide range of soils (sands, silts, clays). | Not suitable for very soft soils where push-in shelby tubes are preferred. |

- **Cost**: The modified barrel and disposable liners add cost compared to a standard SPT sampler.
- **Data Use**: Blow counts (N-values) are often not reported or are unreliable due to the larger diameter; the primary focus is sample recovery.
- **Regulatory Acceptance**: Widely accepted in North America for geotechnical investigations, but some standards (e.g., ASTM D1586 for SPT) specify standard sampler dimensions. California Modified samples may be used as "disturbed" or "relatively undisturbed" depending on the liner system.
