## Definition

**Work Notice Mailing Approval** is an approval step in a document or project workflow in which a work notice, sampling plan, or similar field notice is formally reviewed and authorized to be sent to recipients by email. In practice, it confirms that the content is ready for distribution and that the mailing should proceed as an official communication.

In the provided source, the concept appears as an email-based approval for a **Borrow Area Sampling Plan**, indicating that the document was reviewed and approved for circulation.

## How it works

Work Notice Mailing Approval typically follows this pattern:

1. **Draft preparation**  
   A work notice, plan, or notice document is prepared by the responsible party.

2. **Review for correctness**  
   The draft is checked for technical accuracy, completeness, formatting, and appropriateness for distribution.

3. **Approval decision**  
   An authorized reviewer confirms that the notice may be mailed. This approval may be explicit in an email reply or part of a larger approval record.

4. **Mailing/distribution**  
   Once approved, the notice is sent to the intended recipients, usually by email, though other distribution methods may also be used.

5. **Record retention**  
   The approval message or email becomes part of the project record, providing evidence that the notice was authorized before release.

This approval step is important because it separates internal review from external communication. It helps ensure that recipients receive only finalized, authorized information.

## Variants

Common variants and implementations include:

- **Email approval**: The most direct form, where approval is granted by replying to an email or sending a standalone approval message.
- **Document workflow approval**: Approval is captured in a document management system before the notice is mailed.
- **Two-step review and release**: One person reviews technical content, and another authorizes mailing.
- **Conditional approval**: Mailing is approved only after required edits or attachments are added.
- **Template-based approval**: Standardized language is used to approve recurring notices, such as sampling plans or work notices.

Related alternatives include informal sign-off, routing slips, or electronic workflow buttons in document management systems.

## Trade-offs

Key considerations include:

- **Traceability vs. speed**  
  Formal approval creates a clear audit trail, but it can slow down urgent communications.

- **Control vs. flexibility**  
  Requiring approval reduces the risk of incorrect mailing, but may add procedural overhead.

- **Email simplicity vs. record management**  
  Email approvals are easy to use, but can be harder to manage than structured workflow systems when searching for records later.

- **Single approver vs. multi-approver workflows**  
  One approver is faster, while multiple approvals improve oversight but increase turnaround time.

- **Operational risk**  
  Mailing without approval can lead to dissemination of incorrect or premature information, especially for technical or field-related notices.
