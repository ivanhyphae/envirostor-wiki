## Definition

**Metals Major Ions And Pahs Analysis** refers to a laboratory analytical package used in environmental monitoring to measure three broad classes of constituents in soil, groundwater, or other environmental samples:

- **Metals**: trace and major inorganic elements such as arsenic, lead, cadmium, chromium, copper, zinc, and others
- **Major ions**: common dissolved inorganic species that define water chemistry, such as calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium, chloride, sulfate, bicarbonate/alkalinity, and sometimes nitrate
- **PAHs**: **polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons**, a group of organic contaminants commonly associated with petroleum products, combustion residues, asphalt, creosote, and other hydrocarbon sources

In practice, this analysis package is used to characterize whether a site has inorganic contamination, hydrocarbon-related contamination, or changes in water chemistry that may indicate impacts from construction, industrial activity, stockpiles, or other land uses.

## How it works

This is not a single chemical test but a **combined monitoring suite**. Samples are collected in the field and sent to an accredited laboratory, where each analyte class is measured using methods suited to its chemistry.

### 1. Sample collection
Typical sample types include:

- **Groundwater**
- **Surface water**
- **Soil**
- **Sediment**

Field personnel collect samples using contamination-control procedures appropriate to the matrix and analyte list. Preservation, filtration, cooling, and holding times matter, especially for dissolved metals and PAHs.

### 2. Metals analysis
Metals are usually analyzed by instrumental methods such as:

- **ICP-MS** (inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry)
- **ICP-OES** / **ICP-AES** (inductively coupled plasma optical emission spectroscopy)
- **GFAAS** (graphite furnace atomic absorption spectroscopy) for some trace metals
- **Cold vapor methods** for mercury, when included

For groundwater, metals may be reported as:

- **Total recoverable metals**
- **Dissolved metals** after field or laboratory filtration

Because metals behavior depends strongly on pH, oxidation state, and suspended solids, the sample preparation and reporting basis are important.

### 3. Major ions analysis
Major ions are commonly measured by techniques such as:

- **Ion chromatography** for anions like chloride, sulfate, nitrate, fluoride
- **ICP or atomic methods** for cations like calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium
- **Titration** or other wet-chemistry methods for **alkalinity**
- Sometimes **conductivity** and **total dissolved solids (TDS)** are reported alongside major ions

Major ions help define the water’s geochemical signature. They are often used to:

- Compare upgradient and downgradient water quality
- Distinguish natural groundwater chemistry from anthropogenic effects
- Evaluate mixing, salinity intrusion, evaporation, or leaching

### 4. PAHs analysis
PAHs are hydrophobic organic compounds composed of fused aromatic rings. They are typically measured by:

- **GC-MS** (gas chromatography-mass spectrometry)
- Sometimes **GC-MS/MS** or other confirmatory methods

PAH suites often include compounds such as:

- Naphthalene
- Acenaphthylene
- Acenaphthene
- Fluorene
- Phenanthrene
- Anthracene
- Fluoranthene
- Pyrene
- Benzo[a]anthracene
- Chrysene
- Benzo[b]fluoranthene
- Benzo[k]fluoranthene
- Benzo[a]pyrene
- Indeno[1,2,3-cd]pyrene
- Dibenzo[a,h]anthracene
- Benzo[g,h,i]perylene

PAHs are often reported individually and sometimes as summed concentrations, depending on the program.

### 5. Interpretation
Results are compared against:

- Detection limits and reporting limits
- Background or upgradient conditions
- Regulatory screening levels or cleanup criteria
- Temporal trends from repeated monitoring rounds

The combined package is especially useful because it captures both:

- **Inorganic geochemistry** via metals and major ions
- **Combustion/petroleum-related organic contamination** via PAHs

## Variants

Common variants of this analysis package include:

- **Metals only**
- **Major ions only**
- **PAHs only**
- **Metals + major ions**
- **Metals + PAHs**
- **Metals + major ions + PAHs** as a comprehensive screening suite

Additional variants may include:

- **Total vs. dissolved metals**
- **Filtered vs. unfiltered groundwater samples**
- **Priority pollutant metals**
- **EPA 16 priority PAHs**
- Expanded PAH lists used by state or project-specific programs
- Inclusion of **field parameters** such as pH, specific conductance, temperature, oxidation-reduction potential, and dissolved oxygen

Analytical methods may vary by laboratory, matrix, and regulatory program, but the overall purpose is the same: to characterize contamination and geochemical conditions.

## Trade-offs

### Advantages
- Provides a broad picture of environmental quality in one sampling program
- Links inorganic chemistry with organic contaminant screening
- Useful for trend analysis and source identification
- Supports regulatory compliance, risk assessment, and remedial monitoring

### Limitations
- **Cost**: Combined metals, major ions, and PAHs panels can be expensive
- **Complexity**: Different analyte classes require different preservation and methods
- **Detection limits**: Some PAHs or trace metals may be below reporting limits, limiting interpretation
- **Matrix effects**: Turbidity, organic matter, and salinity can interfere with analysis or interpretation
- **Speciation issues**: Metals may be present in multiple chemical forms with different mobility and toxicity
- **Representativeness**: A single sample may not capture seasonal or spatial variability
- **Comparability**: Results can differ depending on whether metals are reported as total recoverable or dissolved, and whether PAH suites are standardized

### Practical considerations
- Ensure the lab methods match the project’s regulatory objectives
- Use consistent sampling procedures across monitoring events
- Interpret major ions together with pH, conductivity, and alkalinity
- Treat non-detects carefully, especially for PAHs, where reporting limits can affect summed values
- Compare results to appropriate background conditions rather than relying only on generic thresholds
