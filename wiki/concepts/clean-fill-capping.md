## Overview

Clean Fill Capping is the remedial action of placing a certified clean soil cover over contaminated stockpiles to isolate hazardous constituents, prevent direct contact, and limit infiltration. At the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA), this technique is applied to multiple stockpiles following source material removal, as part of the site closure strategy approved by the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).

## Regulatory status

DTSC has oversight authority. The Clean Fill Sampling Plan received formal written approval via e‑mail (see *DTSC Approval E‑mail for Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Sampling Plan.pdf*). The cap must maintain physical isolation of impacted soils. Long‑term compliance is tracked through periodic groundwater monitoring and cap integrity inspections. No violations or non‑compliance findings are noted in the provided extracts.
