# Clean Fill Capping

## Overview

Clean fill capping is the remedial action of placing certified clean soil cover over contaminated stockpiles to isolate hazardous constituents, prevent direct contact, and limit infiltration. At the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA), this technique is applied to multiple barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles as part of the interim Phase 1 remedy for the State Route 132 West Project. The cap design incorporates a clean soil layer overlain by roadway pavement (asphalt/concrete) and/or vegetated slope cover. The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) oversees the remedy, with concurrence from the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB). Clean fill material is sourced from certified borrow areas and must meet background-based criteria established in the April 9, 2020 Variance Request.

## Site Record

### Cap Design and Coverage

- **Stockpiles 1 and 2 (BCS):** The primary cap covers these two stockpiles, which are located beneath the SR 132 freeway alignment. The cap design includes a minimum 6 mil (0.006 inch) synthetic liner (meeting Health and Safety Code §25123.3(b)(4)(A) requirements) overlaid by clean fill and roadway pavement. On the north side of the freeway, a vegetated slope remains until the final freeway phase is completed. (Source: *OM Plan Comments (900259).md*, August 1, 2022, DTSC Comment 3 and Comment 4)
- **Stockpile 3 (post-BCS removal):** BCS was excavated from Stockpile 3 prior to capping; verification sampling confirmed removal volumes (specific values not extractable from available excerpts). (Source: existing article, referencing *SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo*)
- **Stockpile 2 – Bent 2 soils placement:** Excavated soils from the Bent 2 column (adjacent construction) were accepted for reuse on Stockpile 2, but only if placed at **6 inches or less** thickness and immediately covered with clean base material and asphalt/concrete roadway. This conditional approval was granted to enable efficient use of material while preserving cap integrity. (Source: *E-mail Acceptance of Bent 2 Soils Tech Memo*, November 6, 2020)
- **Basin 5 borrow area:** A new clean fill source at Basin 5 (adjacent to the previously tested SR 132 roadway cut east of Carpenter Road) was approved via a sampling plan dated June 11, 2020. The sampling frequency and methodology relied on the previously accepted Carpenter Shoofly technical memo. (Source: *Approval E-mail for SR 132 Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan*, June 12, 2020)
- **Borrow Area (east of Carpenter Ave.)** : Composite soil samples from a borrow area characterized in February 2020 were found consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations and suitable as clean cover for Stockpiles 1 and 2. DTSC accepted the Technical Memorandum on March 4, 2020. (Source: *Borrow Area Results Tech Memo Approval*, March 4, 2020)

### O&M Plan Requirements

The Draft Operation and Maintenance Plan (March 2022) outlines inspection, maintenance, and repair activities for the cap/SR 132 freeway. DTSC provided comments on August 1, 2022, requiring:

- **Advance notification:** Minimum 10 working days advance notice to DTSC before any BCS disturbance.
- **Post‑event inspections:** In addition to annual inspections, inspections must occur after fires, substantial rainfall (≥1 inch in 24 hours), significant seismic events, and traffic accidents that may damage the cap.
- **Unauthorized access:** Periodic patrols must document impacts from homeless encampments or other unauthorized access, especially on the north vegetated slope.
- **Inspection form refinement:** The O&M Site Inspection Form should include sub‑headings for each stockpile feature: MSE wall (bulging/out‑of‑plane), highway pavement (cracks, potholes), median/shoulders (cracks, potholes), north slope (vegetation, rills, slumping), storm drains (debris/obstruction, erosion around inlets/outlets).
- **Thickness specification:** The minimum synthetic liner thickness of 6 millimeters is misspelled in the plan; DTSC recommended clarifying that 6 mils (0.006 inch) meet Health and Safety Code requirements and to reference HSC §25123.3(b)(4)(A).

Revised O&M Plan is to be incorporated as Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report.

### Groundwater Monitoring

Post‑capping groundwater monitoring is ongoing. Wells such as MW‑5 are sampled per the approved monitoring plan. The March 8, 2023 groundwater report (06A2542ct_TO97_GW Rpt) includes data collected pre‑ and post‑capping; individual concentrations are not extractable from available excerpts. (Source: existing article, referencing GW report)

## Regulatory Status

- **Oversight agencies:** DTSC (Site Code 900259) with RWQCB concurrence.
- **Clean fill criteria:** Established in the April 9, 2020 Variance Request. Materials must meet naturally occurring background concentrations. Bent 2 soils exceeded background criteria but were conditionally accepted for thin‑layer placement (≤6 inches) on Stockpile 2, provided immediate covering and strict adherence to the Revised Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP Amendment, October 22, 2020).
- **Current compliance:** No violations or non‑compliance findings are documented in the available records. DTSC approved the borrow area memo (March 2020), Basin 5 sampling plan (June 2020), and Bent 2 soils tech memo (November 2020). The Draft O&M Plan is undergoing revision to address DTSC comments; final approval is pending upon submittal of the Remedial Action Completion Report.

---

## Open Items

- **Final O&M Plan:** Revisions must incorporate all DTSC comments (advance notice, post‑event inspections, unauthorized access monitoring, inspection form details, corrected thickness unit). The revised plan is to be submitted as Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report.
- **Winter exposure of Bent 2 soils:** If Bent 2 soils remain temporarily exposed during winter months, surface water sampling and erosion control measures per the Revised SWPPP Amendment (October 22, 2020) must be strictly followed to prevent off‑site migration. DTSC emphasized this condition in its November 6, 2020 approval.
- **Cap integrity after Phase 1:** The north vegetated slope will remain uncovered until the final freeway phase. Long‑term performance monitoring and maintenance of the vegetated slope are pending definition in future phases.
