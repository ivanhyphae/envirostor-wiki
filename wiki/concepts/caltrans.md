## Overview

**Caltrans** (California Department of Transportation, District 6, Hazardous Waste Branch) is the responsible party for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County. The stockpiles were generated in 1961 during SR 99 construction when Caltrans excavated soil from property formerly occupied by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC), which had operated an evaporation pond. Caltrans placed the excavated material in three stockpiles within its right‑of‑way, anticipating future freeway construction. Under DTSC oversight, Caltrans has since completed a Remedial Action Plan (RAP), a Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP), and ongoing operation and maintenance (O&M) of the encapsulated stockpiles.

## Regulatory status

- **Oversight agencies**: DTSC (lead) and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB, consultative).
- **Applicable standards**: Primary and Secondary MCLs (CDPH) for groundwater; site‑specific background: barium 120 mg/kg, lead 3.8 mg/kg; RDIP BCS removal threshold 1,000 mg/kg barium.
- **Compliance**: The 2024 Comparative Evaluation concluded that the encapsulated stockpiles pose no future threat to groundwater. Annual O&M inspections (e.g., March 2025) indicate containment structures remain intact.

## Open items

- **Monitoring well decommissioning**: The May 3 2024 DTSC approval requires decommissioning of wells MW‑4, MW‑6, MW‑9, and MW‑10 in accordance with the approved RDIP. A well destruction report has not yet been submitted.
- **Long‑term O&M**: Annual inspections and maintenance of the cap and stormwater controls will continue per the RDIP.
