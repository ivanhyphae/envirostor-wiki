### Overview

The **Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan (SSI Workplan)** for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (State Routes 99 and 132, Stanislaus County, California) is a planning document developed by Geocon on behalf of the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans, District 6) to guide additional subsurface investigation at the project site. The workplan was prepared under Contract No. 06A1580, Task Order No. 44, EA No. 10-403500, and was submitted in draft form for regulatory review. The final workplan, dated September 18, 2012, was issued in response to comments from the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in cooperation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), following their September 12, 2012 comment letter. The workplan’s purpose is to define field activities, laboratory analysis protocols, and reporting requirements for characterizing contamination in soil stockpiles at the site.

### Site Record

- **Document History**: Geocon transmitted the final SSI Workplan on September 18, 2012 (Project No. S9525-06-44) via electronic mail to Mr. Richard Stewart, PG, Caltrans District 6. The letter included a response to DTSC’s September 12, 2012 comments on the draft workplan.
- **Regulatory Comments**: DTSC and RWQCB requested several revisions to the draft workplan, including: (1) addition of discretionary boring locations to address erosion and soil/sediment migration features; (2) rationale for sampling depth differences among boring categories; (3) containment, analysis, and disposal of equipment decontamination rinse water for metals; (4) confirmation that laboratory methods (EPA Test Method 6010B and 7471) apply to all boring categories, with potential additional analysis after initial results and DTSC/RWQCB review; and (5) a separate summary report limited to characterization findings, with nature/extent and risk assessment to be presented in separate reports.
- **Field Activities**: A site survey was performed on September 17, 2012, with representatives of DTSC, Caltrans, and Geocon, to review staked proposed boring locations. Based on field conditions, Fenceline and Perimeter borings were added or adjusted at each stockpile location.
- **Boring Depths and Sampling Intervals**:
    - *Fenceline Borings*: Continuous coring to 5 feet deep; samples from 0–6 inches, then 1–2 ft, 2–3 ft, 3–4 ft, and 4–5 ft.
    - *Perimeter Borings*: Continuous coring to 3 feet deep; samples from 0–6 inches, then 1–2 ft and 2–3 ft.
    - *Cadmium Borings*: Continuous coring to 20 feet deep; samples from 0–6 inches, then 4–5 ft, 9–10 ft, 14–15 ft, and 19–20 ft.
- **Laboratory Analysis**: Text added to the final workplan specifies that decontamination water will be contained, sampled, and analyzed for metals. All boring location categories are subject to EPA Method 6010B (metals) and 7471 (mercury) analysis; additional analysis may be performed after initial results, as directed by DTSC and RWQCB.
- **Reporting**: The workplan references a summary report limited to supplemental site characterization findings; separate reports are required for nature and extent of contamination and human health risk assessment.

### Regulatory Status

- **Agencies Involved**: DTSC (lead oversight) and Central Valley RWQCB (cooperating agency), in coordination with Caltrans District 6.
- **Standards Referenced**: Not explicitly cited in the summarized source but implied through DTSC/RWQCB direction on analytical methods (e.g., EPA 6010B for metals, 7471 for mercury) and disposal requirements. Decontamination water was required to be sampled and analyzed for metals prior to disposal.
- **Compliance**: The final SSI Workplan was approved by DTSC and RWQCB via the September 18, 2012 response letter. No further regulatory actions are documented in this source.

### Open Items

- The source does not indicate whether the proposed field activities (boring adjustments, sampling) were completed or if subsequent reports (nature/extent, risk assessment) were submitted. These remain pending deliverables under the workplan.
- No data on contaminant concentrations, spatial distribution, or exceedances are provided in the source listing; these would be generated by the investigation and addressed in later reports.
