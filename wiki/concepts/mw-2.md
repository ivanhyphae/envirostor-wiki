## Overview

**MW-2** is one of ten groundwater monitoring wells installed at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, located along SR 99 and SR 132 in Stanislaus County, California. The well was installed on June 2, 2006, and was destroyed pursuant to permit on April 29, 2019. It was part of the network established to monitor the migration of dissolved metals – specifically barium, lead, and strontium – from native soils and pond tailings generated during the early 1960s construction of a segment of SR 99. These materials originated from a 4.3-acre parcel formerly owned by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC). The primary chemicals of concern (COC) are barium, lead, and strontium.

## Site record

- **Installation**: MW-2 was installed on June 2, 2006. It has a total boring depth of 40 feet and a completed well depth of 39 feet. The screened interval is 29–39 feet below ground surface (bgs), with a 2-inch diameter SCH 40 PVC casing and a #2/12 sand filter pack. The well casing elevation is 81.25 feet above MSL. (Source: *Weir Construction Details Table*, from **S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles GW April 2019_06.19.md**)

- **Destruction**: MW-2 was destroyed on April 29, 2019, under Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Resources permit ENC2019-50174 and City of Modesto permit number 50174. Gregg personnel performed the destruction, supervised by Geocon staff member Lauren Short. Caltrans personnel were onsite. (Source: *Well Completion Report*, from **S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles Well Dest Rpt_06.19.md**)

- **Groundwater Sampling (April 2019)**: On April 8, 2019, MW-2 was sampled. Key results for dissolved metals:
    - **Barium**: 120 µg/L (recorded in laboratory report as 120 µg/L; *Dissolved Metals by ICP-MS, EPA 6020*, from **S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles GW April 2019_06.19.md**)
    - **Strontium**: 780 µg/L
    - **Lead**: Not detected (ND; <5.0 µg/L MDL)
    - **Zinc**: 51 µg/L
    - **Chromium**: 7.3 µg/L
    - **Vanadium**: 25 µg/L
    - **Copper**: 7.1 µg/L
    - **Calcium**: 61,000 µg/L (6.1 mg/L)
    - **Sodium**: 55,000 µg/L (55 mg/L)
    - **Potassium**: 2,900 µg/L (2.9 mg/L)
    - **Magnesium**: 22,000 µg/L (22 mg/L)
    - **Chloride**: 18 mg/L
    - **TDS**: Not reported explicitly for MW-2 in the summary table, but TDS for the site ranged 300–760 mg/L across all wells. (Source: *Summary of Groundwater Analytical Results*, from **S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles GW April 2019_06.19.md**)

- **General minerals**: The sample from MW-2 was calcium-dominant (Stiff diagram), with a bicarbonate (HCO3) dominant composition. Nitrate as nitrogen was 2.3 mg/L. (Source: *General Minerals/Stiff Diagrams*, from **S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles GW April 2019_06.19.md**)

- **Historical data**: MW-2 concentrations for barium have ranged from 105 µg/L (March 2012) to 150 µg/L (May 2012). Strontium has ranged from 780 µg/L (April 2019) to 1,010 µg/L (March 2012). (Source: *Barium and Strontium Concentrations vs. Time figures*, from **S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles GW April 2019_06.19.md**)

- **Regulatory oversight**: The DTSC, in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Board, Central Valley Region (CVRWQCB), has reviewed the groundwater monitoring reports. The primary COC are barium, lead, and strontium. (Source: *DTSC letter, November 29, 2012*, from **11 29 2012 Caltrans GND Water Monitoring Rpts (2).md**)

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable thresholds**: The DTSC has identified the following numeric water quality thresholds:
    - Barium: 1,000 µg/L (California MCL) / 700 µg/L (EPA Health Advisory)
    - Strontium: 4,000 µg/L (EPA Health Advisory)
    - Lead: 5.0 µg/L (MCL for drinking water)
    - All concentrations in MW-2 are below these thresholds. (Source: *Dissolved Metals Table*, from **S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles GW April 2019_06.19.md**)

- **Compliance**: The site is in compliance with the current groundwater monitoring program, with annual sampling (next scheduled for April 2020) and semi-annual depth-to-water measurements (next October 2019). (Source: *Conclusions and Recommendations*, from **S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles GW April 2019_06.19.md**)

## Open items

- **No unresolved issues** are documented for MW-2 specifically. The sampling report notes that filters were not working properly during the April 2019 sampling event (an unfiltered sample was collected on 4/9/2019). This is documented for transparency but does not constitute an open item. (Source: *MW-2 field data sheet*, from **S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles GW April 2019_06.19.md**)
