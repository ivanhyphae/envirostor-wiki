## Geocon

### Overview
Geocon Incorporated (Geocon) is the environmental consulting firm retained by Caltrans District 10 to serve as the lead technical contractor for investigation, monitoring, and remediation at the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County. Geocon manages all phases of the DTSC‑oversight remediation program, including groundwater monitoring, stormwater sampling, remedial action implementation, and regulatory correspondence. The firm's project‑specific document numbering system (e.g., S9800, S9525, S1200, S2350, 06A2542ct) provides the primary filing structure for the case record.

### Site Record

**Project Identification and Regulatory Correspondence**  
Geocon prepared and submitted the *Response to DTSC Workplan Comments* (document 7084_S9525-06-44, dated September 2012), which addressed DTSC review comments on the proposed scope of investigation. The same file series (S9525) contains Geocon’s groundwater monitoring reports for September 2012 (S9525-06-44A) and June 2013 (S9525-01-44B), documenting initial and follow‑up rounds of groundwater sampling.

**Groundwater Monitoring**  
Geocon conducted groundwater monitoring events in September 2012 (S9525-06-44AModesto Stockpiles GW SEPT2012 1212) and June 2013 (S9525-01-44B Modesto Stockpiles GW June 2013.0613). A later Geocon groundwater report (06A2542ct_TO97_GW Rpt_final.20230308, dated March 8, 2023) provides continued monitoring data.

**Stormwater Sampling**  
Geocon performed stormwater sampling events documented in:  
- March 5, 2016 (S9800-01-17A Modesto Stockpiles Mar 5 2016 Stormwater Sampling_04 16.pdf)  
- October 2016 (S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles Oct 2016 SW Sampling_12.16.pdf)  
- March 2018 (S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles March 2018 SW Sampling_04.18.pdf)  
- February 4, 2025 (S2350-01-02_2.4.2025 Stormwater Sampling Report_6.25.pdf)

**Remedial Actions and Technical Memoranda**  
Geocon authored the *SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo*, documenting the removal action for Stockpile 3 under the Brownfield Cleanup (BCS) program. DTSC’s approval of the Stockpile 3 confirmation sampling plan is recorded in *Stockpile 3 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval.pdf*. Geocon also prepared the *Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE Wall Sampling Plan* for the mechanically stabilized earth wall installation. DTSC’s email approval of the *Carpenter Shoofly Tech Memo* (DTSC E‑mail Approval of Carpenter Shoofly Tech Memo.pdf) reflects Geocon’s role in designing temporary traffic and access features during remediation.

**RDIP Figures**  
Geocon produced the *RDIP All Figures* (Remedial Design and Implementation Plan graphics), which includes:
- Vicinity map and site plan
- Stockpile grading plans (Plates 1–5)
- Generalized cross‑sections for Stockpiles 1 and 2 and the Emerald Avenue off‑channel area (July 18)
- Soil transportation plan

**Geocon Project Numbering**  
The following Geocon file prefixes are documented in the case record:  
- **S9800** — Stormwater sampling, March 2016  
- **S9525** — Groundwater monitoring and DTSC workplan response (2012–2013)  
- **S1200** — Stormwater sampling, October 2016 and March 2018  
- **S2350** — Stormwater sampling, February 2025  
- **06A2542ct** — Groundwater report, March 2023  

### Regulatory Status
Geocon operates under DTSC oversight as the primary environmental consultant for Caltrans District 10. Groundwater and stormwater monitoring reports are submitted to DTSC for review and approval. The firm’s workplans and technical memoranda are subject to DTSC comment and formal approval; documented approvals include the Stockpile 3 confirmation sampling plan and the Carpenter Shoofly tech memo.
