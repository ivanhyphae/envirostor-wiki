## Geocon

### Overview
Geocon Consultants, Inc. (Geocon) is the environmental consulting firm retained by Caltrans District 10 as the lead technical contractor for investigation, monitoring, and remediation at the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. Geocon manages all phases of the DTSC-oversight program, including groundwater monitoring, stormwater sampling, remedial action implementation, well destruction, clean fill characterization, and regulatory correspondence. The firm's project-specific document numbering system (e.g., S9525, S1200, S9108, S1908) provides the primary filing structure for the case record.

### Site Record

**Project Identification and Regulatory Correspondence**  
Geocon prepared the *Fenceline Workplan* (document 7083_S9525-06-44, September 2012), which addressed a site survey conducted on September 17, 2012 with representatives of DTSC, Caltrans, and Geocon to review and adjust proposed boring locations. The workplan proposed 29 five-foot-deep fenceline borings, 22 three-foot-deep perimeter borings, and five 20-foot-deep cadmium borings within Stockpiles 2 and 3.

**Groundwater Monitoring Well Destruction**  
Geocon supervised the destruction of six groundwater monitoring wells (MW1, MW2, MW3, MW5, MW7, MW8) on April 29–30, 2019, documented in the *Groundwater Monitoring Well Destruction Report* (Project No. S1200-01-01, June 19, 2019). Gregg Drilling performed the physical destruction under Geocon supervision by Geocon staff member Lauren Short. The City of Modesto issued Encroachment Permit ENC2019-50174 for the work. Geocon project manager Rebecca L. Silva signed the report.

**Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP) Figures**  
Geocon produced the *RDIP All Figures* (Project No. S1200-01-01, January 2019), which includes:
- Vicinity map and site plan showing monitoring well locations (MW8 to be protected in-place; MW5 to be abandoned)
- Stockpile grading plans (Figures 3-4, revised May 2019) depicting existing barium-containing soil stockpiles
- Generalized cross-sections for Stockpiles 1 and 2 and the Emerald Avenue off-channel area
- Soil transportation plan

**BCS Removal Verification — Stockpile 2**  
Geocon authored the *Stockpile 2 BCS Removal Verification Testing* technical memorandum (March 9, 2020), documenting BCS removal at the west and east ends of Stockpile 2 adjacent to Emerald Avenue and State Route 99. Verification thresholds were barium 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection) and lead 80 mg/kg (residential screening level) per the RDIP. Key results:
- West end initial excavation (elevation 82 ft MSL): sample S2W 1-0 returned barium 3,200 mg/kg; sample S2W 3-0 returned 1,100 mg/kg. After deepening to 81 ft MSL, confirmation samples S2W 1A-0 (barium 140 mg/kg) and S2W 3A-0 (barium 64 mg/kg) met thresholds.
- East end initial excavation: sample S2E 3-0 returned barium 1,400 mg/kg. After deepening, sample S2E 8-0 returned 4,100 mg/kg. One additional verification sample (S2E 8A-0, barium 63 mg/kg) after further excavation to 81 ft MSL met thresholds.
- Final verification sample barium concentrations ranged from 54 to 760 mg/kg; lead ranged from 1.5 to 35 mg/kg. All samples met RDIP verification thresholds.

**Clean Fill Characterization — Carpenter Road Shoofly**  
Geocon prepared the *Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Characterization Testing* technical memorandum (April 12, 2021). John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG served as project manager. Twenty-four discrete soil samples were collected from six vertical transects (CSF1 through CSF6) at depths of 2, 5, 10, and 15 feet from the top of slope. Eight 3-part composite samples were analyzed for barium and lead via EPA Method 6010B. Barium concentrations ranged from 28 to 96 mg/kg, all below the maximum site-specific background of 120 mg/kg established in the April 9, 2020 Variance to RDIP. Lead ranged from below reporting limit (1.3 mg/kg) to 3.8 mg/kg, at or below the background maximum of 3.8 mg/kg. Approximately 5,000 cubic yards of native soil from the Carpenter Road Shoofly was approved as clean capping material for Stockpiles 1 and 2.

**Stockpile 3 BCS Removal**  
On March 13, 2020, Geocon submitted the *Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Verification Testing* technical memorandum. DTSC project manager Dean Wright approved via email on March 18, 2020, stating that the sampling results "indicate that the Stockpile 3 BCS has been removed and that the remaining soil meets the RDIP verification thresholds" and that "no further BCS RDIP monitoring requirements are necessary."

**Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan**  
On June 11, 2020, John Juhrend (Geocon) submitted the *Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan* to Caltrans for DTSC review. The sampling frequency was based on the adjacent previously tested SR 132 roadway cut section east of Carpenter Road. Geocon assumed the Carpenter technical memo was applicable.

### Regulatory Status
Geocon operates under DTSC oversight as the primary environmental consultant for Caltrans District 10. Groundwater monitoring reports, stormwater sampling reports, and technical memoranda are submitted to DTSC for review and approval. Key documented approvals include:
- DTSC acceptance of the Stockpile 3 BCS removal verification memo (Dean Wright, March 18, 2020)
- DTSC approval of the Carpenter Shoofly clean fill characterization memo (via email referenced in the record)
- Caltrans submission and DTSC review of the Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan (June 2020)

### Open Items
No unresolved items specific to Geocon's contractual role or performance are identified in the provided source record. The Basin 5 sampling plan was submitted for DTSC approval; the approval status is not documented in available sources.
