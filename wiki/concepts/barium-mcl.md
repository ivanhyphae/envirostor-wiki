## Overview

**Barium** is the primary contaminant of concern at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA, Site Code 900259). Since the 1960s, three stockpiles on the property – south of the intersection of SR 99 and Kansas Avenue – have held soil contaminated with barium. The contaminant is the driver for the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) and all subsequent monitoring, encapsulation, and final highway construction. Barium is regulated under both federal and state **Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs)** for drinking water (1,000 µg/L, or 1 mg/L) and under DTSC’s site-specific cleanup thresholds for soil.

## Site record

- **Origin and Phasing**:  Barium-contaminated soil has been present at the site since the 1960s. During **Phase I** of the RAP (implemented in 2019–2020), soil from **Stockpile 3** was relocated to the structural sections of **Stockpiles 1 and 2**. Phase II will utilize the remaining two stockpiles as the foundation for a 4-lane highway (SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway). (Source: DTSC Letter Response - 2025 GW Monitoring Report, p. 1)

- **Concentrations**:  From the earliest documented planning (2012 Fenceline Workplan) through 2020 confirmation sampling, barium concentrations in **Barium Containing Soil (BCS)** were targeted for removal or encapsulation. Confirmation sampling at **Stockpile 2** (east and west ends) showed that **barium concentrations** had been reduced to levels **meeting the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) verification thresholds** – specifically, **10 mg/kg** is the stated cleanup standard for the RDIP, though the exact verification threshold for Stockpile 2 is documented as **1,000 mg/kg** (per the Stockpile 2 BCS Removal Tech Memo). Note: The 1,000 mg/kg threshold is the **total barium** action level for soil placement; the **MCL** is 1,000 µg/L (1 mg/L) for dissolved barium in groundwater. (Source: Stockpile 2 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval, 2020; DTSC Letter Response, 2026)

- **Spatial distribution**:  BCS was present in distinct zones at **Stockpile 3** (target for removal) and at the **east and west ends of Stockpile 2** (removed and verified clean). **Stockpile 1** and **Stockpile 2** (main bodies) retain encapsulated barium-contaminated soil as part of the structural fill for the future highway. (Source: SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo, 2020; Stockpile 2 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval)

- **Groundwater and surface water**:  **Ten groundwater monitoring wells** (installed 2012) were used to track potential **barium leaching** into the subsurface. After “years of data supporting no metals were leaching into the groundwater from the stockpiles,” **six wells were decommissioned in 2019**; the remaining **four wells** were destroyed in 2025. The **Well Destruction Report** (June 18, 2025) documents that **grout volume** estimates were required – the **estimated volume** vs. **actual volume used** – as a performance metric for abandonment. (Source: DTSC Letter Response, 2026, p. 2–3)  
    Surface water run-off sampling (Storm Water Sampling Report, also June 18, 2025) was **within reporting limits** for barium and all other metals, demonstrating containment integrity. (Source: DTSC Letter Response, 2026)

- **Key approval milestones**:
    - **Stockpile 2 BCS Removal Verification** – March 9, 2020 Technical Memo accepted by DTSC (Dean Wright) on March 10, 2020, confirming east/west ends of Stockpile 2 met RDIP thresholds and were suitable for **placement of clean embankment fill**. (Source: Stockpile 2 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval)
    - **Stockpile 3 BCS Removal** – concurrent with Stockpile 2 completion, final verification testing was “pending” (completed March 2020). (Source: same Tech Memo)
    - **Groundwater well decommissioning** – Conditionally approved by DTSC January 30, 2026; final report due within 30 days (February 2026). (Source: DTSC Letter Response, 2026)

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable standards**:
    - **Barium MCL (federal/state drinking water)**: 1,000 µg/L (1 mg/L) – used for groundwater protection.
    - **RDIP verification threshold (soil)**: 1,000 mg/kg total barium – used for BCS removal verification and placement of clean fill.
    - **Groundwater monitoring** – no barium exceedances documented; all wells decommissioned.
    - **Surface water** – all results within reporting limits; no exceedances.

- **Agencies involved**:
    - **DTSC** – Lead regulatory agency (Project Manager: Dean Wright; then Arielle McLeskey as of 2026). GSB and HERO review.
    - **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)** – Concurred on groundwater well decommissioning, reviewed monitoring reports.
    - **Caltrans (District 6)** – Responsible party (Adam Inman, PG; Richard Stewart, PG).

- **Compliance status**:
    - **Soil** – Stockpile 2 east/west ends **verified clean** (2020); Stockpile 3 BCS removal confirmed complete (2020).
    - **Groundwater** – wells decommissioned and approved (conditional: grout volume table needed). No ongoing groundwater monitoring.
    - **Surface water** – Storm Water Sampling Report **approved** (January 30, 2026).

## Open items

1. **Well Destruction Report – Grout Volume Table** – DTSC conditionally approved the report, but requires a revision to include a table showing **estimated vs. actual volume of grout** used for each of the four decommissioned wells (deadline: 30 days from January 30, 2026). (Source: DTSC Letter Response, 2026, p. 2–3)

2. **Final Phase II surface use** – The remaining two stockpiles (Stockpiles 1 and 2 main bodies) will be completed with **Phase II** of the RAP, to be used as the foundation for a **4-lane highway**. The timing and final approval for Phase II construction is not documented in these sources.

3. **No further groundwater monitoring** – After decommissioning of the last four wells, no ongoing groundwater monitoring is required. However, the **performance metric** (grout volume) must be closed.
