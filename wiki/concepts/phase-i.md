## Definition

**Phase I** most commonly refers to **Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ESA)**, a preliminary investigation of a property’s historical and current use to identify potential environmental contamination. In the context of the provided source (Hwy 132 Clean Fill Source Testing Exhibit), Phase I denotes the initial stage of testing clean fill material to determine whether it meets regulatory criteria for uncontaminated soil before reuse or disposal. More broadly, Phase I can also refer to the first stage of clinical trials, project management, or other phased processes, but this article focuses on the environmental assessment meaning.

## How it works

A Phase I ESA is a non‑intrusive evaluation that follows the ASTM E1527 standard or local equivalents. It typically involves:

- **Records review** – Examination of historical aerial photographs, fire insurance maps, city directories, regulatory databases, and title records to identify past uses that could have caused contamination.
- **Site reconnaissance** – A physical walk‑through of the property and adjoining areas to observe signs of potential contamination (e.g., stained soil, storage tanks, waste disposal areas).
- **Interviews** – Discussions with current and former owners, occupants, and local government officials to gather additional information on site history.
- **Report preparation** – Documentation of findings, including a list of recognized environmental conditions (RECs) and recommendations for further action, such as Phase II testing (intrusive sampling).

For clean fill source testing (as referenced in the source), Phase I typically involves verifying that the source material (e.g., excavated soil intended for clean fill) has no evidence of prior contamination through similar record and reconnaissance methods.

## Variants

- **Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ESA)** – Broadly applied to commercial and industrial property transactions, often required for lender due diligence under the U.S. EPA’s All Appropriate Inquiries rule.
- **Phase I Clinical Trial** – The first stage of testing a new drug or treatment in human subjects, focusing on safety, dosage, and side effects.
- **Phase I Project Management** – The initiation or conceptual phase of a project, where feasibility, scope, and budget are defined.
- **Phase I Archaeological Survey** – A preliminary field survey to identify archaeological resources without excavation.

In the environmental context, a Phase I ESA is sometimes called a “Transaction Screen” or “Desk‑Based Assessment” in other regions.

## Trade-offs

- **Cost vs. depth** – Phase I is relatively inexpensive compared to intrusive testing but cannot confirm the absence of contamination; it only identifies potential issues.
- **Time** – A standard Phase I can be completed in a few weeks, but complex histories may require extended research.
- **Limitations** – Relies on available records and site access; hidden contamination (e.g., buried tanks, historical spills not documented) may go undetected. A “clean” Phase I does not guarantee the property is free of contamination.
- **Liability** – Properly performed Phase I helps establish the “innocent landowner” defense under CERCLA, but errors or omissions can expose the user to legal risk.
