## John Juhrend

### Overview

John Juhrend, P.E., C.E.G., C.E.M., was the Senior Engineer at Geocon Consultants, Inc., serving as the primary technical lead and point of contact for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Barium-Containing Soil (BCS) removal project at State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. He authored and submitted the verification testing technical memoranda to regulatory agencies and managed the field sampling and laboratory coordination for Stockpile 2 and Stockpile 3 remedial actions. His role encompassed project oversight from sample collection through regulatory approval.

### Site Record

**Documented Correspondence and Submittals**

On March 9, 2020, Juhrend emailed the "Stockpile 2 BCS Removal Verification Testing" technical memorandum to Richard C. Stewart (Caltrans Environmental Division), copying WSP and Geocon staff. The email noted that "BCS removal at Stockpile 3 should be completed tomorrow pending the results of final verification testing." Stewart subsequently forwarded the memo to Dean Wright (DTSC) on the same day for approval.

**Stockpile 3 BCS Removal (February–March 2020)**

Juhrend directed the Stockpile 3 BCS removal program, which involved excavating to elevations between 82.5 and 84 feet above MSL (original ground surface elevation 84 feet MSL). A total of 24 verification samples (S3 1-0 through S3 24-0) were collected between February 28 and March 12, 2020, plus two berm samples (S3 SE BERM, S3 SW BERM) collected February 27, 2020.

Key exceedance event documented in Juhrend's reports:
- Initial sampling detected barium at 7,000 mg/kg (S3 9-0), 5,100 mg/kg (S3 10-0), and 1,500 mg/kg (S3 11-0), all exceeding the 1,000 mg/kg verification threshold
- These three locations were deepened approximately one foot and resampled as S3 9A-0, S3 10A-0, and S3 11A-0, yielding barium results of 160 mg/kg, 160 mg/kg, and 220 mg/kg, respectively
- Final barium concentrations across all locations ranged from 46 mg/kg (S3 SW BERM) to 970 mg/kg (S3 16-0 from storm drain backfill)
- Final lead concentrations ranged from 1.

All samples were analyzed by Advanced Technology Laboratories (ATL) under chain of custody protocol using EPA Method 6010B, with Juhrend listed as the report recipient on analytical results dated March 2, 6, 9, 12, and 13, 2020.

**Stockpile 2 BCS Removal**

Juhrend's March 9, 2020 technical memorandum described removal of barium-containing soil from east and west ends of Stockpile 2, with confirmation sampling demonstrating achievement of RDIP cleanup levels.

### Regulatory Status

**DTSC Approval**

On March 10, 2020, Dean Wright, P.G. (DTSC Project Manager) emailed Richard Stewart accepting the Stockpile 2 technical memorandum, stating: "The sampling results indicate that the east and west ends of Stockpile 2 have met the RDIP verification thresholds and DTSC agrees that these areas are now suitable for the placement of clean embankment fill."

For Stockpile 3, Juhrend's technical memorandum concluded that "the Stockpile 3 BCS removal has met the barium and lead verification thresholds and is suitable for placement of clean embankment fill and other highway improvements without further BCS RDIP monitoring requirements." This information was to be included in the Removal Action Completion Report to be submitted following Stockpile 1 and 2 capping activities.

**Verification Thresholds**
- Barium: 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection)
- Lead: 80 mg/kg (residential screening level)

### Open Items

The Removal Action Completion Report incorporating Stockpile 3 results, to be submitted after Stockpile 1 and 2 clean fill capping activities, was pending at the time of the March 2020 technical memorandum. No subsequent updates are documented in the provided sources.
