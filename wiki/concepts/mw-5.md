## Overview

**Mw 5** (also referred to as MW-5 in project records) is a groundwater monitoring well installed in 2006 as part of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site investigation along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. The well was one of eight original monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-8) installed adjacent to three legacy soil stockpiles containing barium- and strontium-impacted materials from former Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC) evaporation pond excavations performed during 1961 construction of SR 99. Mw 5 was subsequently abandoned (destroyed) on April 29, 2019, under the Remedial Design Implementation Plan – Interim Phase 1.

## Site record

### Installation and construction
- **Installed**: May 22, 2006 (Geocon Consultants, Inc.)
- **TOC elevation**: 87.78 feet above MSL
- **Casing**: 2-inch diameter Schedule 40 PVC
- **Total boring depth**: 45 feet; completed well depth: 45 feet
- **Screened interval**: 35–45 feet (0.010-inch slot)
- **Filter pack**: #2/12 sand, interval 33.7–46.5 feet
- **Borehole diameter**: 8 inches

### Location
Mw 5 was located south of Kansas Avenue, west of North Emerald Avenue, adjacent to Stockpile 1. The RDIP Figure 2 (January 2019) identifies Mw 5 as a monitoring well "to be abandoned."

### Groundwater chemistry – dissolved metals (April 2019 sampling)
The April 9, 2019, sample (laboratory ID 1901428-03) showed:

- **Barium**: 370 µg/L (below MCL of 1,000 µg/L)
- **Strontium**: 1,300 µg/L (below EPA Health Advisory of 4,000 µg/L)
- **Chromium**: 8.7 µg/L (MCL 50 µg/L)
- **Vanadium**: 26 µg/L (Notification Level 50 µg/L)
- **Manganese**: 59 µg/L (exceeds Secondary MCL of 50 µg/L)
- **Zinc**: 31 µg/L (J-flagged; below applicable thresholds)
- **Cobalt**: 1.2 µg/L (J-flagged)
- **Nickel**: 3.9 µg/L (J-flagged)
- **Selenium**: 1.5 µg/L (J-flagged)

Lead, arsenic, antimony, cadmium, mercury, silver, and thallium were not detected at or above their respective MDLs.

### Groundwater chemistry – general minerals (April 2019)
- **Nitrate as N**: 40 mg/L (exceeds Primary MCL of 10 mg/L)
- **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)**: 680 mg/L (exceeds Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L)
- **Chloride**: 39 mg/L
- **Sulfate**: 26 mg/L
- **Calcium**: 110,000 µg/L (110 mg/L)
- **Magnesium**: 35,000 µg/L (35 mg/L)

The Stiff diagram classification for Mw 5 has been consistently **calcium-dominant** across all sampling events (2012 through April 2019).

### Historical exceedances documented in project record
- **July 2012**: Nitrate as N at 20 mg/L (exceeds Primary MCL 10 mg/L); TDS at 580 mg/L (exceeds Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L). Barium at 280 µg/L (below MCL). Strontium at 1,100 µg/L (below threshold).
- **April 2019**: Nitrate as N at 40 mg/L; TDS at 680 mg/L; Manganese at 59 µg/L (exceeds Secondary MCL 50 µg/L) – reported as an exceedance in Geocon's conclusions.

### Purge characteristics (July 2012)
During the July 17, 2012, sampling event, Mw 5 went dry after purging 0.75 gallons. The well was allowed to recover, an additional 0.75 gallons were purged, and the well was allowed to recover a second time before sampling.

### Destruction
On April 29, 2019, Mw 5 was destroyed by Gregg (contractor), supervised by Geocon staff member Lauren Short, with Caltrans personnel onsite. The Well Completion Report documents the destruction under Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Resources permit ENC2019-50174, dated April 26, 2019.

## Regulatory status

Applicable water quality thresholds for this site:
- **Barium**: Primary MCL 1,000 µg/L (California Department of Public Health)
- **Strontium**: EPA Drinking Water Health Advisory 4,000 µg/L
- **Nitrate as N**: Primary MCL 10 mg/L
- **TDS**: Secondary MCL 500 mg/L
- **Manganese**: Secondary MCL 50 µg/L

As of the April 2019 sampling event (the final sampling event prior to well destruction), Mw 5 showed exceedances of:
- Nitrate (Primary MCL)
- TDS (Secondary MCL)
- Manganese (Secondary MCL)

Barium and strontium remained below their respective thresholds. The April 2019 report concluded that only manganese in Mw 5 exceeded dissolved metals thresholds. The well was destroyed as part of the Phase 1 implementation under DTSC oversight.

## Open items

- No post-destruction replacement monitoring well at the Mw 5 location has been documented in the provided source materials. The RDIP Figure 2 identifies Mw 5 as "to be abandoned" with no replacement noted.
- Specific verification sampling results from the well destruction process are not included in the available Well Destruction Report excerpts.
