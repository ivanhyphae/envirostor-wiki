## Overview

Lead is the primary contaminant of concern (COC) driving the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation on State Route 132 in Stanislaus County. The site consists of multiple stockpiles of soil excavated during prior Caltrans highway improvements. These stockpiles contain elevated lead concentrations at levels that pose potential risks to human health and the environment, necessitating regulatory oversight and remedial action. Project documents—including the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) figures, a September 2012 groundwater monitoring report, and a March 2018 surface water sampling report—document the characterization and management of lead at the site.

## Regulatory Status

The lead contamination at the Modesto Stockpiles is regulated under a cleanup order or oversight agreement with the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC). Applicable standards include:

- **Soil**: DTSC residential and industrial screening levels (e.g., California Human Health Screening Levels – CHHSLs). For lead, the residential screening level is typically 80 mg/kg for direct exposure, though site-specific risk‑based levels may apply.
- **Groundwater**: California MCL for lead (15 µg/L). The 2012 monitoring report evaluates compliance with this standard.
- **Surface Water**: California Toxics Rule criteria for lead (dissolved) – 14 µg/L (chronic) and 82 µg/L (acute) for waters with hardness typical of the Central Valley. The 2018 sampling report assesses attainment.
