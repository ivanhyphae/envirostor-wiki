## Lead

### Overview

Lead is a primary contaminant of concern (COC) at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation site on State Route 132 in Stanislaus County. It was identified by DTSC, along with barium and strontium, as one of the primary chemicals of concern in groundwater for the site. Lead contamination is present in soil stockpiles, groundwater, and surface water runoff, with documented exceedances of applicable standards that have driven remedial actions.

### Site Record

#### Soil Stockpiles

- **Stockpile 1 (2004):** Lead was detected at 5.3 mg/kg at depth 0.15 ft, and 6.1 mg/kg at 4.5 ft (source: Fenceline Workplan, Table 1).
- **Stockpile 3 (2004):** Lead was detected at 26 mg/kg at depth 0.15 ft (source: Fenceline Workplan, Metals Concentrations, Stockpile 3 Samples).
- **Stockpile 2 BCS Removal (2020):** Verification testing used a lead threshold of **80 mg/kg** (residential screening level) as specified in the RDIP. Final confirmation samples from the excavation base showed lead concentrations ranging from **1.5 to 35 mg/kg**, all below the verification threshold. Specifically:
  - West end initial samples: lead up to 4.2 mg/kg (S2W 1-0)
  - West end final confirmation: 1.5 mg/kg (S2W 1A-0), 1.7 mg/kg (S2W 3A-0)
  - East end initial samples: lead up to 23 mg/kg (S2E 8-0)
  - East end final confirmation: 1.6 mg/kg (S2E 8A-0)
  (source: Stockpile 2 BCS Removal Tech Memo, Table 1)

- **Clean Fill Characterization (Carpenter Road Shoofly, April 2021):** Lead was detected in 9 of 10 composite samples at concentrations ranging from **1.4 to 3.8 mg/kg**, with one sample non-detect (<1.3 mg/kg). These values were at or below the maximum site-specific background of **3.8 mg/kg** for lead, confirming suitability as clean capping material (source: Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Tech Memo, Table 1).

#### Groundwater

- **July 2012 (MW-5):** Dissolved lead was **not detected** above the PQL of 1.0 µg/L in MW-5 (source: July 2012 GW Report).
- **July 2017 (MW-1):** Lead reported at **5.0 µg/L** (estimated, J-flagged) — below the MCL of 15 µg/L (source: April 2019 GW Report, Table 2).
- **April 2019:** Lead was **not reported** at concentrations equal to or greater than its method detection limit in any of the eight groundwater samples collected (source: April 2019 GW Report, Dissolved Metals section). MDL was 3.6 µg/L.
- **Trend:** Lead concentrations in groundwater have remained consistently below the California MCL of 15 µg/L throughout monitoring from 2012 through 2019 (source: April 2019 GW Report, Figure 6, Lead Concentrations vs. Time).

#### Surface Water

- **March 22, 2018 (PL3):** Dissolved lead was detected at **46 µg/L**, which exceeds the primary MCL for drinking water of **15 µg/L**. This sample was collected outside the Caltrans right-of-way along North Emerald Avenue (source: March 2018 SW Sampling Report, Dissolved Metals section).
- **Other locations (BG1, PL1, PL2, PL4):** Lead concentrations ranged from non-detect to 7.4 µg/L, all below the MCL (source: March 2018 SW Sampling Report, Dissolved Metals lab data).

### Regulatory Status

- **Soil:** The residential screening level of **80 mg/kg** (California Human Health Screening Level) was used as the verification threshold for lead in the Stockpile 2 BCS removal. All verification samples were below this threshold.
- **Groundwater:** The California MCL for lead is **15 µg/L**. Groundwater monitoring from 2012 through 2019 shows continuous compliance.
- **Surface Water:** The California Toxics Rule criteria for dissolved lead are **14 µg/L (chronic)** and **82 µg/L (acute)** at hardness typical of the Central Valley. The March 2018 result of 46 µg/L at PL3 exceeded the chronic criterion, though it was collected outside the right-of-way.
- **Agency oversight:** DTSC is the lead regulatory agency. The Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB) also reviews groundwater and surface water data.
- **Approvals:** DTSC approved the Stockpile 2 BCS Foundation Spoil Assessment Workplan (Tech Memo) on March 4, 2022 (source: Approval Letter Stockpile 2 Spoils Workplan).

### Open Items

- The elevated surface water lead concentration (46 µg/L at PL3, March 2018) was located outside the Caltrans right-of-way. Whether this indicates off-site migration from the stockpiles or an unrelated source has not been resolved in the reviewed documents. Additional surface water sampling during qualifying rain events was planned for 2018 but results are not included in the provided source set.
- The Verification Sampling for Stockpile 2 BCS removal confirmed compliance, but final Removal Action Completion Report (RACR) documenting all stockpile capping activities has not been provided in the source documents.
- Lead data for Stockpile 4 and Stockpile 5 are not present in the provided sources.
