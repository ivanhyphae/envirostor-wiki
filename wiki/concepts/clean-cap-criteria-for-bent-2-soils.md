## Definition

**Clean Cap Criteria For Bent 2 Soils** refers to the acceptance criteria used to determine when soils excavated from or associated with the **Bent 2** project area can be classified as “clean cap” material. In this context, “clean cap” means soil that meets the project’s environmental and engineering requirements for use as a protective cover layer, typically because it is sufficiently free of contamination and suitable for placement above impacted material.

The source document, an email acceptance technical memo, indicates that these criteria were established for reviewing Bent 2 soils and determining whether they are acceptable for clean-cap use.

## How it works

Clean cap criteria typically function as a screening and decision framework:

1. **Sampling and analysis**
   - Soil samples are collected from the Bent 2 source material.
   - Laboratory testing is performed for the contaminants or properties of concern.

2. **Comparison to acceptance thresholds**
   - Analytical results are compared against predefined clean-cap thresholds.
   - These thresholds may be based on regulatory standards, project-specific cleanup goals, or engineering suitability requirements.

3. **Material classification**
   - If results fall below the acceptance criteria, the soil may be approved as clean cap material.
   - If results exceed the criteria, the material is typically rejected for clean-cap use or subject to additional evaluation, segregation, or treatment.

4. **Documentation and approval**
   - The acceptance decision is documented in a technical memo or correspondence.
   - The memo serves as the project record supporting reuse, placement, or disposal decisions.

In practice, the criteria ensure that cap material does not introduce unacceptable contamination into a remediation system and that it performs adequately as a physical barrier.

## Variants

Possible variants of clean cap criteria include:

- **Chemical-only criteria**: acceptance based primarily on contaminant concentrations.
- **Chemical + physical criteria**: combines contaminant limits with grain size, moisture, or compaction requirements.
- **Project-specific criteria**: thresholds tailored to the Bent 2 site and its remediation objectives.
- **Regulatory-based criteria**: derived from applicable environmental cleanup standards.
- **Reused-site soil criteria**: acceptance rules for excavated soil intended for beneficial reuse on the same project.

Depending on the site, criteria may differ by contaminant class, soil type, or placement location within the cap system.

## Trade-offs

Key considerations and limitations include:

- **Protectiveness vs. reuse efficiency**
  - Stricter criteria improve environmental protection but reduce the amount of soil eligible for reuse.
- **Sampling uncertainty**
  - Soil heterogeneity can make it difficult to confirm that all material meets criteria.
- **Administrative burden**
  - Acceptance requires testing, documentation, and review, which can slow field operations.
- **Material variability**
  - Even within the same source area, soil quality may vary enough to require segregation or blending.
- **Engineering suitability**
  - Soil that is chemically acceptable may still be unsuitable if it lacks the physical properties needed for cap performance.
