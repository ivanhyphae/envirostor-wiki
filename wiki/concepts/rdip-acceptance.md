## RDIP Acceptance

**Rdip Acceptance** refers to the formal regulatory approval of the Errata to the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project, located along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. The project addresses soil stockpiles containing primarily arsenic and lead contamination inherited from a former pesticide/chemical facility. This acceptance, documented in an errata transmittal dated February 6, 2020, and the supporting *S1200-01-01 Caltrans Modesto Stockpile RDIP*, confirms that the revised design for stockpile characterization and removal has been reviewed and accepted by the oversight agency, advancing the project from planning into active field implementation.

## Site Record

- **Documentation:** The acceptance is recorded in the *Errata Transmittal 2-6-2020.md* source file, which states: "RDIP Errata is accepted." This transmittal was sent by Stephan L. Muchow, PE (Principal/Senior Project Manager) on behalf of the project team. The supporting reference document is *S1200-01-01 Caltrans Modesto Stockpile RDIP_01.19.md*, which details the full Remedial Design Implementation Plan from January 2019.
- **Project Location:** Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route 132, Stanislaus County, California — specifically at a former facility property identified as a source of the stockpile contamination.
- **Purpose of Errata:** The errata updated the original RDIP to better address site conditions identified during Phase I characterization. Key changes included:
  - Revised soil stockpile volume estimations based on new survey data from 2018-2019.
  - Updated contaminant concentration ranges for arsenic (25–120 mg/kg) and lead (80–450 mg/kg) in the main SV-2 stockpile, based on 32 perimeter and surface samples collected in November 2018.
  - Clarified that a second stockpile (area SV-1) was found to contain a thin layer of contaminated material (arsenic 15–35 mg/kg, lead 40–110 mg/kg) overlying clean fill, not requiring removal as a full stockpile.
  - Established acceptance criteria: removal to achieve arsenic ≤ 25 mg/kg and lead ≤ 80 mg/kg in post-excavation verification samples, matching background soil thresholds.
- **Implementation Plan:** Under the accepted RDIP, the contractor (Stantec) was required to execute Phase I excavation of SV-2 in March-April 2020. Verification soil sampling was to be conducted at 15 locations across the cleared area, with samples analyzed for arsenic and lead via EPA Method 6010B.
- **Verification Criteria:** Post-excavation verification samples must demonstrate arsenic ≤ 25 mg/kg and lead ≤ 80 mg/kg. If any sample exceeds these thresholds, the site is "not closed" and further excavation is required in that specific area.

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable Standards:** The action levels are based on background soil concentrations at the site, established as:
  - Arsenic: 25 mg/kg (action level)
  - Lead: 80 mg/kg (action level)
- **Agency Oversight:** The acceptance was issued under the oversight of the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), which has jurisdiction over the project under the California Hazardous Substance Account Act. The Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB) also retains oversight for potential groundwater impacts, though the RDIP focuses on soil removal.
- **Compliance Status:** As of the date of the errata acceptance (February 6, 2020), the project was in compliance with the accepted plan. Field implementation (Phase I excavation) was scheduled to begin within 30-60 days of the acceptance date. No post-excavation verification results are documented in these sources.

## Open Items

- **Phase I Verification Results:** The errata acceptance does not report whether Phase I excavation and verification sampling have been completed or if verification criteria were met. This remains a pending deliverable.
- **SV-1 Stockpile:** The second stockpile area (SV-1) was identified as not requiring full removal, but the errata notes that further characterization may be needed if future activities disturb the thin contaminated layer. No such characterization or action is documented.
- **Phase II Planning:** The RDIP allows for a potential Phase II if Phase I does not achieve criteria or if additional stockpiles are identified. No Phase II authorization has been documented in these sources.
