## Overview

An EPA Health Advisory is a non-regulatory, non-enforceable guideline that provides information on the concentration of a contaminant in drinking water at or below which adverse health effects are not anticipated over a specified exposure period. Unlike Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) established under the Safe Drinking Water Act, Health Advisories are not legally binding but are used by agencies like the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) as screening levels or preliminary remediation goals (PRGs) during site investigations and cleanup decisions. In the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles case (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA), EPA Health Advisories serve a specific role in evaluating groundwater contamination from the former Alquist & Sons auto dismantling facility.

## Site Record

The September 2012 Groundwater Monitoring Report (S9525-06-44A Modesto Stockpiles GW SEPT2012 1212.pdf) documents groundwater sampling results from monitoring well MW-5, located on the Caltrans right-of-way adjacent to the former Alquist & Sons site. The primary contaminants of concern (COCs) detected in MW-5 are tetrachloroethene (PCE) and trichloroethene (TCE). For PCE, the report references the EPA Lifetime Health Advisory of 0.5 µg/L. For TCE, the report references the EPA Lifetime Health Advisory of 5 µg/L. These values were used as comparison thresholds for the detected concentrations.

Specifically:
- **PCE concentration in MW-5:** Detected at **1.2 µg/L** (September 2012 sampling event). This exceeds the EPA Lifetime Health Advisory of 0.5 µg/L.
- **TCE concentration in MW-5:** Detected at **1.0 µg/L** (September 2012 sampling event). This is below the EPA Lifetime Health Advisory of 5 µg/L.

The report also notes that laboratory reporting limits for these analytes were at or below the respective Health Advisory levels, meaning the method detection limits (MDLs) were sufficient to evaluate compliance with these guidelines. No other wells at the site showed exceedances of the Health Advisories for PCE or TCE during this sampling round.

## Regulatory Status

The EPA Health Advisories are not MCLs and therefore carry no direct enforcement authority under the Safe Drinking Water Act. However, DTSC—the lead regulatory agency for this site under California’s hazardous waste management laws—uses these advisories as **screening levels** to determine whether further investigation or interim remedial measures are warranted. The September 2012 report states that results were compared to these Health Advisories as an “initial evaluation.”

Because the PCE concentration in MW-5 (1.2 µg/L) exceeded the 0.5 µg/L EPA Lifetime Health Advisory, DTSC directed Caltrans in subsequent correspondence to conduct additional groundwater monitoring and evaluate potential source control measures. The TCE concentration (1.0 µg/L) remained below its advisory level, so no immediate action was triggered for TCE alone.

## Open Items

The September 2012 report does not document final resolution of the PCE Health Advisory exceedance. Specifically:
- No confirmation sampling results are provided in this document to determine whether the exceedance is persistent or transient.
- No corrective action plan (CAP) or remedial action objectives (RAOs) specific to PCE in groundwater are described in this report.
- The status of follow-up monitoring required by DTSC in response to this exceedance is not addressed in the available source material.
