## Overview

**Fenceline Stations** are monitoring points established at the property boundary of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA) to detect potential off‑site migration of contaminants from stockpiled soils. Their purpose is to verify that remediation and stockpile management activities do not cause unacceptable releases to adjacent properties or receptors, and to demonstrate compliance with cleanup standards set by the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC). Two stations are identified in the project record: one appears to be a groundwater monitoring well (MW‑5) and one a stormwater sampling location.  

## Site record

- A response document (**7084_S9525-06-44 Response to DTSC Workplan Comments**, dated September 12, 2025 [internal project numbering]) reports a sample from fenceline station **MW‑5** with a barium concentration of **760 mg/kg**. The applicable maximum contaminant level (MCL) cited in the same document is **1,000 µg/L**; the apparent unit mismatch (mg/kg vs. µg/L) suggests the reported value is from a soil matrix and the MCL is a groundwater standard. The document does not specify whether this value represented an exceedance or a compliance result.  

- A stormwater sampling report (**S2350-01-02_2.4.2025 Stormwater Sampling Report**, dated February 4, 2025) references a “Fenceline Station” but the extracted text is too fragmented to identify which station or any analytical results. This report is labeled as covering the period through February 4, 2025, and includes sample identifiers consistent with perimeter monitoring.  

- The project record places the soil stockpiles within **Phase 1 (2019–2020)** of remediation. The fenceline monitoring network was likely installed and operational during this phase, though the source documents do not explicitly confirm the installation date.  

## Regulatory status

- DTSC is the lead regulatory agency overseeing the remediation and monitoring of the stockpiles. The fenceline stations are part of the approved monitoring plan, subject to DTSC review through workplan comment processes.  
- Applicability of the **MCL of 1,000 µg/L for barium** indicates oversight under California’s drinking water standards (Title 22 CCR). Any exceedances at fenceline groundwater stations would require corrective action.  
- The current compliance status cannot be determined from the available source material; no statements of compliance or non‑compliance are extractable.  

## Open items

- The unit discrepancy between the reported MW‑5 barium value (760 mg/kg) and the cited MCL (1,000 µg/L) is unresolved. Clarification is needed on whether the sample was from soil or groundwater and which threshold (e.g., soil screening level or groundwater MCL) actually applies.  
- The stormwater sampling report from February 2025 has not been fully reviewed; pending deliverables may include additional fenceline data that have not been presented to DTSC.  
- No verification sampling results from later phases or confirmatory analyses are documented in the provided excerpts.
