## MW-1

## Overview

MW-1 is a groundwater monitoring well associated with the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation case file (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA). It was installed on June 2, 2006 by Caltrans as part of the initial monitoring network (MW-1 through MW-8) at locations adjacent to Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3. The well was constructed into the shallow zone of the upper aquifer within the Modesto Formation, screened from 32 to 42 feet below ground surface (bgs), with a top of casing (TOC) elevation of 80.39 feet above MSL. MW-1 was destroyed on April 29, 2019 by Gregg under Geocon supervision (Well Destruction Report, June 2019, Project No. S1200-01-01).

**Groundwater Elevation Data** (Table 1, July 2012 GW Report; Table 1, April 2019 GW Report):

**General Minerals April 2019**: Chloride 19 mg/L, Sulfate 19 mg/L, Calcium 87 mg/L, Magnesium 34 mg/L, Sodium 21 mg/L, Potassium 2.9 mg/L, Bicarbonate Alkalinity 360 mg/L, Sulfide <0.010 mg/L (Table 3, April 2019 GW Report; Lab Report 1901404-02, Appendix B).

**Quarterly/Annual Monitoring**: Groundwater monitoring was performed semi-annually (depth to water) and annually (full sampling) through April 2019. MW-1 was sampled in June 2006, October 2006, March 2012, May 2012, July 2012, and subsequent events through April 2019. The well was destroyed on April 29, 2019 per Stanislaus County permit ENC2019-50174 (Well Destruction Report, June 2019).

**April 2019 sampling note**: Initial filtering attempt failed; an unfiltered sample was collected on 4/8/2019; a filtered sample for dissolved metals was collected on 4/9/2019 (Appendix A, April 2019 GW Report).

## Regulatory Status

- **Primary MCLs (California CDPH)**: Nitrate as N consistently exceeds the 10 mg/L MCL; all metals below their respective MCLs.
- **Secondary MCLs**: TDS exceeds 500 mg/L; manganese exceeded the 50 µg/L Secondary MCL in April 2019.
- **DTSC and CVRWQCB oversight**: The agencies received copies of the groundwater monitoring reports and well destruction report; no documented enforcement action specific to MW-1 is cited in the sources.
- **Destruction**: Well was properly abandoned per DWR and City/County permitting requirements; no post-destruction monitoring is required.

## Open Items

- The final fate of purge water from MW-1 (and other wells) disposed at Inviro-Tec Disposal (May 16, 2019) is noted but no closure documentation is provided.
- The April 2019 manganese exceedance (53 µg/L) relative to the Secondary MCL was not specifically addressed as an actionable exceedance in the conclusions.
- The nitrate exceedance in MW-1 (an upgradient well) was noted as possibly representative of natural background (July 2012 GW Report, page 15), but no formal background determination or exemption appears in the record.
