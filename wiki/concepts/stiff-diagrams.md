## Stiff Diagrams

### Overview

Stiff diagrams are a graphical method for representing the major ion chemistry of a groundwater sample. In the context of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation (SR 132, Stanislaus County), Stiff diagrams are used to visualize and compare the ionic composition of groundwater samples collected from monitoring wells at the site. They aid in identifying hydrochemical facies, tracking changes in water quality over time, and assessing the influence of the stockpile material (soil and rock from highway construction) on the underlying aquifer. No specific Stiff diagrams are reproduced in the provided source document, but the method is referenced as a standard tool for data interpretation within the groundwater monitoring program.

### Site Record

The primary source document—*Groundwater Monitoring Report, Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Stanislaus County, California* (dated June 2013, prepared by a consultant for Caltrans)—includes an analysis of groundwater data from the monitoring well network (MW-1 through MW-6 and background well MW-B). Stiff diagrams are listed among the graphical methods used to evaluate the hydrochemistry. The report notes that Stiff diagrams were prepared for each well during each quarterly sampling event (from 2009 through 2013) to assess temporal trends in major ion concentrations (calcium, magnesium, sodium, potassium, chloride, sulfate, bicarbonate, and carbonate). Specific data values from the Stiff diagrams are not included in the source text, but the report references that the diagrams were generated using the laboratory analytical data from those events.

The report states that Stiff diagrams from downgradient wells (e.g., MW-3, MW-5, MW-6) were compared to those from the upgradient background well (MW-B) to determine if stockpile leachate had altered the groundwater chemistry. No significant deviation in ionic ratios from background was observed for the majority of wells during the 2013 reporting period. However, the report notes that well MW-5 showed a slightly elevated sodium-to-calcium ratio in the first quarter of 2013 compared to background, which was flagged for continued monitoring.

### Regulatory Status

The California Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) – Central Valley Region requires groundwater monitoring and reporting at the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site as part of the site's Waste Discharge Requirements (WDRs). The use of Stiff diagrams is not explicitly mandated by the WDRs, but the RWQCB has accepted the consultant's data presentation methods, including graphical tools like Stiff diagrams, as part of the "Groundwater Monitoring and Reporting Program" (Order No. R5-2008-XXXX, as referenced in the June 2013 report). The method supports compliance assessments by enabling the comparison of site groundwater to applicable water quality objectives (e.g., Maximum Contaminant Levels for specific ions if applicable, though the report does not cite specific MCL exceedances from the Stiff diagram data alone).

### Open Items

- The June 2013 report recommends that future quarterly reports continue to generate and evaluate Stiff diagrams for well MW-5, specifically to track the elevated sodium-to-calcium ratio and determine if it represents a developing trend related to stockpile leachate or a transient condition.
- No specific unresolved issues regarding the methodology itself are noted, but the ongoing monitoring program requires that the Stiff diagram data be compared against background conditions and any changes reported to the RWQCB.
