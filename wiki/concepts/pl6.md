## Overview

PL6 is a surface water sampling location established during the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA) project under Contract No. 06A2542, Task Order No. 97. It was added to the surface water sampling network during a November 2, 2022 Site walk attended by the Caltrans TO Manager and Stantec. PL6 monitors a stormwater discharge point on the north side of SR 132 near SR 99. The location was designated to assess potential runoff-related impacts from the soil stockpiles. No active runoff was observed at PL6 during the February 28, 2023 surface water inspection, the only documented sampling event in the current record.

## Site record

PL6 was identified as a new surface water sampling location on November 2, 2022, during a Site walk with Caltrans and Stantec representatives. It is described as “a surface water discharge point on the north side of SR 132 near SR 99” (Figure 2 in the referenced letter report). The location was added to the surface water sampling network alongside PL7, which is at a discharge pipe along stormwater basin 5.

On February 28, 2023, Stantec mobilized to the Site at approximately 8:45 am to perform a surface water and road embankment inspection following an overnight rain event. Precipitation measured 0.09 inches on that day. At PL6, field observations noted “no active runoff from the stormwater pipe” (Surface Water Report – February 28, 2023). A photographic record (Appendix A, Photographs 9 and 10) documents the basin containing PL6 and its discharge area, all showing no flowing water at the time of inspection.

No analytical sampling results for PL6 are included in the source document. The only documented activity at PL6 is the visual inspection of February 28, 2023.
