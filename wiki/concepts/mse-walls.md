## Overview
MSE (Mechanically Stabilized Earth) walls are engineered retaining structures used at the Caltrans SR 132 Modesto Soil Stockpiles site to form the containment zones around Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2. Their primary function is to physically confine and isolate soil stockpiles within the designated restricted areas. The only project record referencing these walls is the March 18, 2025 annual inspection.

## Site record
The **Annual Stockpile Inspection Form** dated 3/18/25 (inspector Adam Inman) lists MSE walls as separate line items under both the Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2 containment zone inspections. The inspection criterion was “bulging areas, out of plane.”

- **Stockpile 1 MSE Wall:** The form shows “Yes □ No ☒” under *Surface Condition OK?* and “Yes □ No ☒” under *Repairs/Maintenance Required?* No narrative observations are provided for this item.
- **Stockpile 2 MSE Wall:** Identical checkmark pattern (“Yes □ No ☒” in both columns). No specific comments.

The “Other” entry for Stockpile 1 states “No issues observed”; for Stockpile 2, “No major issues observed; asphalt patch repairs at northbound off ramp.” The checkmark pattern may be interpreted as indicating the condition was *not* satisfactory (☒ in the “No” column for Condition OK?) yet no repairs were marked as required—an apparent inconsistency. Alternatively, the notation could reflect a different marking convention. Because no written observations accompany the MSE wall line items, the inspection record does not confirm or describe any bulging, out‑of‑plane displacement, or other defects.

No design drawings, construction records, or prior inspection reports for the MSE walls are included in the provided source material.

## Regulatory status
The inspection form is part of the Operation & Maintenance (O&M) monitoring program. No specific regulatory standard (e.g., Caltrans structural acceptance criteria, OSHA slope stability thresholds) is cited in the source. The form’s own criteria—absence of “bulging areas” and “out of plane” condition—are the only documented performance indicators. As of the 3/18/25 inspection, no corrective actions were documented, implying a presumptive acceptable condition for compliance purposes.
