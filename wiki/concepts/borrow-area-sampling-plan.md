## Overview

The Borrow Area Sampling Plan is a work plan governing the characterization of backfill soil from an off-site borrow area proposed as clean cover material for capping Stockpiles 1 and 2 at the State Route 132 soil stockpile remediation site (Stanislaus County, CA). The plan was submitted by Caltrans (Richard Stewart) to the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) for review and approval under the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP). Approval was communicated via e-mail on February 13, 2020, with a single modification to the compositing procedure. A subsequent Technical Memorandum (dated February 27, 2020) reported the characterization results, which DTSC accepted on March 4, 2020, confirming the borrow material was suitable for its intended cap use.

## Site record

- **Plan submission and review period**: The plan was under DTSC review at least by February 12–13, 2020. On February 13, 2020, DTSC Project Manager Dean Wright (P.G.) notified Caltrans that “Perry didn't have any additional comments on the backfill sampling plan” and that the only remaining request was to **composite 3 samples instead of 4** (email 2/13/2020 7:53 AM). This indicates the original plan specified 4 subsamples per composite; DTSC required reduction to 3 subsamples per composite.
- **Technical Memorandum (Tech Memo)**: Dated February 27, 2020, prepared by Geocon Consultants (John Juhrend, P.E., CEG), titled “Clean Cap Borrow Area Tech Memo.” It described the backfill area characterization and composite soil sample results. The memo was submitted to Caltrans for forwarding to DTSC and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (Steve Meeks).
- **DTSC acceptance (March 4, 2020)**: Dean Wright sent an e-mail stating: “DTSC agrees with the Memo that the composite soil sample results are consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations and suitable as clean cover material for capping Stockpiles 1 and 2.” This e-mail served as DTSC’s formal acceptance of the Memo.
- **Sampling procedure modification**: The only condition on approval was the change from 4‑ to 3‑subsample composites. No further modifications were required.
- **Concentrations**: No specific numerical results are presented in the source e-mails; the Tech Memo itself is referenced but not reproduced. The acceptance stated only that results were “consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations.”

## Regulatory status

- **Lead agency**: DTSC (Site Evaluation and Remediation Unit), with oversight from DTSC project managers Dean Wright and Perry Myers.
- **Applicable standards**: The plan and memo were evaluated against background concentrations for naturally occurring constituents; no action levels or MCLs were explicitly cited in the approval correspondence.
- **Compliance**: The borrow area was approved as meeting the suitability criteria for clean cover material. No exceedances, non‑compliance issues, or conditional requirements beyond the compositing modification are documented in the provided sources.

## Open items

None. The plan received conditional approval on February 13, 2020, and the results were accepted unconditionally on March 4, 2020. No further deliverables, corrective actions, or pending standards are identified in the source materials.
