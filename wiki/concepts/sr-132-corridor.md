## Overview
The SR 132 Corridor is the geographic and operational focus of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project in Stanislaus County, California. The corridor encompasses three stockpile sites (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) located along State Route 132 near Modesto, which contain legacy soils impacted by historic agricultural and industrial activities. These stockpiles are subject to a Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) corrective action under a March 14, 2018-approved Removal Action Plan (RAP) ("3.14.2018 - Approval letter for SR 132 RAP.pdf").

## Site Record
The SR 132 Corridor stockpiles are identified by project designations Stockpile 1, Stockpile 2, and Stockpile 3. The site includes a "Carpenter Shoofly" area, for which a technical memo received DTSC e-mail approval (see "DTSC E-mail Approval of Carpenter Shoofly Tech Memo.pdf").

**Remedial actions:**
- Stockpile 3 underwent bulk chemical separation (BCS) removal, documented in "SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo.pdf." Confirmation sampling for Stockpile 3 was subsequently approved by DTSC ("Stockpile 3 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval.pdf").
- Stockpiles 1 and 2 are associated with an MSE (mechanically stabilized earth) wall sampling plan (see "Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE Wall Sampling Plan.pdf").
- The Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) and grading plans (Figures 3-1 through 3-5) apply to the corridor ("RDIP All Figures.pdf").

**Monitoring:**
- Stormwater sampling events are documented for March 5, 2016 (S9800-01-17A), October 2016 (S1200-01-01), and February 2025 (S2350-01-02). The 2025 report is dated June 2025.
- Groundwater monitoring data is reported in the TO97 report ("06A2542ct_TO97_GW Rpt_final.20230308.pdf").

**Key constituents and thresholds:**
The RAP and DTSC correspondence reference site-specific action levels. Specific numerical values for contaminants (e.g., barium, MCL exceedances) are not fully extractable from the fragmented PDF source text provided. The DTSC letter response to the 2025 groundwater monitoring report ("DTSC Letter Response - 2025 GW Monitoring Report (900259).pdf") indicates ongoing regulatory review of groundwater data.

## Regulatory Status
The project operates under DTSC oversight with the following approved framework:
- Removal Action Plan (RAP) approved March 14, 2018 ("3.14.2018 - Approval letter for SR 132 RAP.pdf")
- Response to DTSC workplan comments submitted ("7084_S9525-06-44 Response to DTSC Workplan Comments.0912.pdf")
- An errata transmittal dated February 6, 2020 addresses corrections to project documents ("Errata Transmittal 2-6-2020.pdf")

Standards applied include California MCLs for groundwater and DTSC-approved screening/action levels for soil, though the specific numeric values were not recoverable from the encrypted/compressed source PDF text for all documents.
