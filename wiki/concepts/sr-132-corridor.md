## Overview

The SR 132 Corridor is the geographic and operational focus of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project in Stanislaus County, California. The corridor encompasses three legacy soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) located along State Route 132 near Modesto, which contain soils impacted by historic barium processing operations at the former FMC Corporation facility. The stockpiles are subject to a DTSC corrective action under a March 14, 2018-approved Removal Action Plan (RAP). The ultimate remediation strategy, selected in the Final Feasibility Study (FS) dated June 24, 2014 and concurred by DTSC on June 30, 2014, is Alternative 4 – Containment, which uses the stockpile material as embankment fill for the SR-132 West Expressway project.

## Site Record

**Stockpile Descriptions (from Final FS, June 2014)**
- **Stockpile #1**: South of Kansas Avenue, west of Emerald Avenue; ~600 ft E-W × 160 ft wide; ~34,000 yd³; bounded by commercial/light industrial (north) and residential (south).
- **Stockpile #2**: South of Kansas Avenue between Emerald Ave and SR-99; ~1,650 ft × 160 ft; ~102,000 yd³.
- **Stockpile #3**: South of Kansas Avenue, east of SR-99; curvilinear, ~1,100 ft × 120 ft; ~24,000 yd³. Total volume of all three stockpiles estimated >970,000 yd³ (Stantec 2023 GW report).

**Site History (Stantec 2023 reports)**
Barium Products Ltd. occupied 1200 Barium Road (now Graphics Drive) starting ~1930, processing barite and celestite. Acquired by Westvaco in 1943, merged with FMC in 1948. Liquid processing residue was discharged to unlined evaporation/percolation ponds through the late 1970s. In 1961, Caltrans purchased a 4.3-acre parcel at the southwest corner of the FMC facility for SR-99 right-of-way. Soil from a former holding pond was excavated and stockpiled within the ROW.

**Primary COPCs**: Barium (elevated above commercial/industrial CHHSLs in PSI 2004), lead, and strontium. Groundwater COPCs include metals and general minerals (nitrate, TDS). Cadmium was detected in 8 of 25 borings in Stockpile 2 and 2 of 10 in Stockpile 3 in 2004 PSI, but was not identified as a COPC in the 2007 HHRA due to lack of elevated concentrations in 2006 SI.

**Groundwater Monitoring**
Eight wells (MW-1 through MW-8) installed in 2006; upgradient wells MW-9 and MW-10 installed in 2012. Monthly monitoring from March 2012 began; quarterly from June 2013. Six wells (MW-1, MW-2, MW-3, MW-5, MW-7, MW-8) were decommissioned in 2019 to accommodate construction; remaining wells: MW-4, MW-6, MW-9, MW-10 (Stantec 2023 GW report). Results from 2006–2013 showed COPC concentrations below California MCLs.

**Containment Construction (Phased)**
- **Phase 1 earthwork began January 2020** (Errata RDIP; Stantec reports).
- **Stockpile 3 BCS Removal (March 2020)**: ~38,440 yd³ (3,075 truckloads) excavated and transported to Stockpile 1 fill containment zone. Verification thresholds: barium 1,000 mg/kg, lead 80 mg/kg (RDIP). Excavation to elevations 82.5–84 ft MSL; 24 verification samples collected Feb 28–March 12, 2020. Final barium ranged 46–970 mg/kg, lead 1.6–40 mg/kg — all below thresholds (Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo, March 13, 2020).
- **Stockpile 2 Confirmation Sampling (March 2020)**: East and west ends of Stockpile 2 met RDIP verification thresholds; DTSC approved for clean embankment fill placement (DTSC email approval March 10, 2020).
- **Clean Cap Borrow Areas**: DTSC approved two borrow areas: East of Carpenter Road (Tech Memo approved March 4, 2020) and Basin 5 (Sampling Plan approved June 12, 2020). Composite sampling results consistent with background concentrations.
- **Stockpile 2 BCS Foundation Spoil Workplan (Feb 2022)**: Approved March 4, 2022 for spoils from weather station and Overhead Sign #1 installation — material below background may be reused on-site; above background to be disposed at Forward Landfill, Manteca.
- **Completion**: Majority of Stockpiles 1 and 2 encapsulated beneath SR-132 structural section and with clean soil/vegetation on north slopes in October 2021. SR-132 opened to traffic November 2022.

**Surface Water Monitoring**
Original SAP from January 2006 (Shaw). Revised sampling locations (PL6, PL7, BG3) established November 2022 after construction eliminated earlier locations (PL1–PL5). February 28, 2023 inspection found no active runoff or visible discharges; minor erosion observed at several locations on north side of Stockpile 1; some slopes lacked vegetation.

**Annual O&M Inspection (March 18, 2025)**
Stockpile 1 and 2 containment zones inspected: MSE walls, highway pavement, medians, north slopes, storm drains — all reported no issues.

## Regulatory Status

- **Feasibility Study**: Final FS (June 24, 2014) approved by DTSC June 30, 2014 — Alternative 4 (Containment via SR-132 project) selected.
- **Removal Action Plan (RAP)**: Approved March 14, 2018.
- **Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) – Interim Phase 1**: Conditional approval August 20, 2018; final approval April 3, 2019 by DTSC and CVRWQCB.
- **Applicable Thresholds**: Barium verification threshold: 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection); lead: 80 mg/kg (residential screening level). Groundwater standards: California MCLs. Clean fill material must be consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations.
- **Regulatory Oversight**: DTSC (Project Manager Dean Wright, PG) and Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (Steven Meeks, PE). Current status: post-construction with ongoing groundwater monitoring and annual O&M inspections.
