## Overview

Storm drains at the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site are part of the containment infrastructure that manages stormwater runoff within each stockpile containment zone. They are inspected annually as components of the O&M site inspection to identify debris, obstructions, or erosion around inlets and outlets that could compromise drainage or containment integrity.

## Site record

The only documented reference to storm drains appears in the **Annual Stockpile Inspection Form** dated **3/18/25**, completed by Inspector **Adam Inman**. For **Stockpile 1 Containment Zone**, the form lists “Storm Drains – debris/obstructions, erosion around inlets/outlets” as a separate line item. The inspector marked the “Surface Condition OK?” column as **No** (☒) and the “Repairs/Maintenance Required” column as **No** (☒), though the adjacent “Other” row notes “No issues observed.” No specific observations or corrective actions were recorded for storm drains on this date.

The form for **Stockpile 2 Containment Zone** is truncated in the source, but the same storm drain inspection item appears. No findings are documented for that stockpile either.

## Regulatory status

The inspection form is part of ongoing O&M (operations and maintenance) requirements for the site’s containment system. No permits, action levels, or enforcement thresholds specific to storm drains are described in the source. Compliance with applicable stormwater management regulations (e.g., Caltrans MS4 permit, NPDES) is inferred but not explicitly stated in the provided document.

## Open items

None documented. The inspection indicates no immediate issues with storm drains at either stockpile as of 3/18/25. Future annual inspections will continue to monitor condition.
