# Stockpile 2 BCS Foundation Spoil Assessment Workplan

## Overview
The Stockpile 2 BCS (Barium Containing Soil) Foundation Spoil Assessment Workplan is a Technical Memorandum (Tech Memo) dated February 28, 2022, submitted by Geocon Consultants Inc. on behalf of Caltrans to the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC). The workplan describes planned sampling and management of barium-containing soil spoils generated from installation of the Weather Station and Overhead Sign #1 on Stockpile 2 at the State Route 132 West project in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California (Site Code: 900259). DTSC approved the workplan via letter dated March 4, 2022.

## Site Record
- **Document**: "Technical Memorandum for Stockpile 2 Barium Containing Soil (BCS) Foundation Spoil Assessment Workplan," dated February 28, 2022
- **Preparer**: Geocon Consultants Inc. (Mr. John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG, Project Manager)
- **Submitting Entity**: California Department of Transportation (Caltrans)
- **Regulatory Reviewer and Approver**: DTSC, Site Evaluation and Remediation Unit, Site Mitigation and Restoration Program (Mr. Dean Wright, PG, Project Manager)
- **Approval Date**: March 4, 2022
- **Project**: State Route 132 West Project, Modesto, Stanislaus County, California
- **Stockpile**: Stockpile 2 (barium containing soil stockpile)
- **Infrastructure Generating Spoil**: Weather Station and Overhead Sign #1
- **Analytes of Concern**: Barium and lead (specifically referenced in DTSC approval letter)
- **Decision Framework**: Two disposition pathways based on analytical results relative to site-specific background concentrations

## Regulatory Status
- **Governing Agency**: DTSC, Department of Toxic Substances Control
- **Applicable Standard**: Site-specific background concentrations for barium and lead (thresholds not specified in the approval letter — values would be in the original Tech Memo)
- **DTSC Concurrence**: DTSC concurred with all stockpiling and sampling procedures described in the Tech Memo
- **Disposition Criteria**:
  - If sampling results show barium and lead concentrations are **below** established site-specific background: material may be reused on-site at locations mutually agreed upon by DTSC and Caltrans
  - If sampling results show concentrations **above** background: material must be profiled for disposal at Forward Landfill in Manteca, California, or another acceptable landfill facility
- **Compliance Status**: The workplan was approved with no identified compliance issues at the time of the approval letter

## Open Items
- The specific site-specific background concentrations for barium and lead are not enumerated in the approval letter — the underlying Tech Memo (February 28, 2022) would contain these values and is referenced but not reproduced in the approval letter
- Future mutual agreement between DTSC and Caltrans is required to determine on-site reuse locations if spoils are below background
- No approval expiration or work completion deadline was specified in the approval letter
- Sampling results data from implementation of this workplan are not included in the approval letter — the letter only documents DTSC's concurrence with the plan
