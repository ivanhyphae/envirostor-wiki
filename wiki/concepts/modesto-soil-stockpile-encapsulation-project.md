## Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project

### Overview

The Modesto Soil Stockpile Encapsulation Project (the "Site") is a California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) remediation project located south of the intersection of Kansas Avenue with State Route (SR) 99 in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. The project involves three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1 through 3) that are encapsulated *in situ* to manage contaminated material. Groundwater monitoring is performed to assess potential impacts from the stockpiles to the shallow aquifer beneath the Site. The project operates under Contract No. 06A2542, Task Order No. 97 (TO97), with Stantec Consulting Services Inc. (Stantec) performing the groundwater monitoring and sampling.

### Site Record

The groundwater monitoring network consists of nine monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-9) installed between May 2006 and May 2012. Well construction details are documented in the groundwater monitoring reports. All wells are constructed of SCH 40 PVC casing, 2-inch diameter, with 0.010-inch slot screens set in #2/12 sand filter packs. Depths range from 39 to 45.5 feet below ground surface.

**Key Monitoring Events:**
- **June 2006 – October 2006**: Initial baseline sampling (Shaw Environmental, Inc., 2007)
- **October 2018 – April 2019**: Sampling by Geocon Consultants, Inc.
- **November 2019 – February 2020**: Sampling by Stantec (July 2020 report)
- **August 2020 – March 2021**: Sampling by Stantec (May 2021 report)
- **October 2021 – March 2022**: Sampling by Stantec (April 2022 report)
- **November/December 2022 – March 2023**: The most recent documented sampling event (March 8, 2023 report)

**Contaminants of Concern:**
The primary constituents monitored are Title 22 metals (dissolved), general minerals, and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs). Specifically, Table 3 of the March 2023 report lists: antimony, arsenic, barium, beryllium, cadmium, chromium, cobalt, copper, lead, manganese, molybdenum, nickel, selenium, silver, thallium, vanadium, zinc, strontium, and mercury. Table 4 covers dissolved calcium, magnesium, chloride, nitrate (as N), sulfate, potassium, sodium, sulfide, alkalinity, total dissolved solids, and PAHs (via SIM).

**Notable Analytical Results (from Table 3):**
- **Barium**: Concentrations in MW-1 ranged from 105 to 150 µg/L across sampling events (2006–2012). The MCL is 1,000 µg/L.
- **Strontium**: Detected at 960 µg/L (MW-1, 3/12/2012) and 1,100 µg/L (MW-1, 5/17/2012 and 7/16/2012). The California Department of Public Health Notification Level for Drinking Water is 1,000 µg/L.
- **Lead**: Detected at 2.0 µg/L (MW-1, 10/5/2006) and 1.4 µg/L (MW-1, 7/16/2012), below the MCL of 15 µg/L.
- **Arsenic**: Ranged from 1.6 to 2.3 µg/L in MW-1 (2006–2012), below the MCL of 10 µg/L.

**Quality Control Notes:**
- Split samples (designated "S" in Table 3) were submitted by the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB) to Excelchem Environmental Labs for verification.
- Results qualified with "J" are estimated concentrations.
- "U" indicates non-detect due to equipment blank contamination.

### Regulatory Status

**Applicable Standards:**
- **Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs)**: Per California EPA (January 2016) – used for comparing Title 22 metals results.
- **Secondary MCLs**: For aesthetic or organoleptic parameters.
- **California Department of Public Health Regulatory Action Levels**: For constituents without MCLs.
- **EPA Drinking Water Health Advisories**: Used for non-regulated compounds.
- **California Department of Public Health Notification Levels for Drinking Water**: Used for strontium (1,000 µg/L).

**Enforcement Agencies:**
- **CVRWQCB**: Has submitted split samples for independent analysis, indicating regulatory oversight.
- **DTSC**: While not explicitly named in this source, is the standard regulatory agency for Caltrans soil stockpile projects of this type.

**Compliance Status:**
Based on the documented analytical results, dissolved metal concentrations at the Site generally remain below applicable MCLs and notification levels. However, strontium has been detected at or near the notification level (1,000 µg/L) in MW-1 during multiple sampling events (March 2012, May 2012, July 2012), prompting continued monitoring.

### Open Items

- **Strontium Trend**: Concentrations in MW-1 have shown persistence near the notification level (1,100 µg/L in 2012). The March 2023 report should be reviewed for updated strontium data to determine if a trend is established.
- **CVRWQCB Split Sample Results**: The source references split samples submitted to Excelchem but does not include those results. Comparison of CVRWQCB-verified data with project data is needed to confirm laboratory accuracy.
- **PAH Data**: Table 4 includes PAH analysis (SIM) but the March 2023 report does not provide complete PAH results in the excerpted tables. Full PAH results for the November 2022 – March 2023 sampling event are not documented in the available source text.
- **Future Monitoring**: The March 2023 report references groundwater monitoring through November/December 2022 and March 2023, but no subsequent deliverables are specified. The next required sampling event under TO97 is not documented here.
