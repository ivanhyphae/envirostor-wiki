## Overview

The **Stockpile 3 Excavation** was a removal action conducted in September 2012 as part of Caltrans' rehabilitation of the State Route 99 Kansas Avenue northbound off-ramp in Modesto, Stanislaus County. Approximately **2,800 yd³** of soil embankment from Stockpile 3 were excavated over ten days (September 7–26, 2012) and disposed of offsite at the **Forward Class II landfill** in Manteca, California, as non-hazardous waste. The action was driven by highway safety improvements—widening off-ramp shoulders and constructing a retaining wall against the Stockpile 3 embankment—and was performed under a *Stockpile 3 Excavation Monitoring Plan* (June 2012) overseen by Geocon project scientists supervised by a California Professional Geologist.

## Site record

- **Pre-excavation investigation:** Geocon completed eight direct-push borings and eleven hand-auger borings within the embankment area. **Barium** was detected in every sample, with concentrations ranging from **34 to 1,600 mg/kg**—all below the residential and commercial/industrial California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs) for barium of 5,200 mg/kg and 63,000 mg/kg, respectively.
- **Regulatory concurrence:** Based on these data and review by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (**DTSC**), the excavated soil was designated for offsite disposal as **non-hazardous soil**. DTSC conveyed in a letter dated **August 30, 2012** that offsite management of the Stockpile 3 soil did not pose a threat to human health or the environment.
- **Excavation details:** Excavation occurred over ten days, with material directly loaded into covered trucks under non-hazardous waste manifests. Dust suppression consisted of pre-soaking and water spray during excavation. Perimeter air monitoring using **pDR-1200** real-time particulate counters tracked total airborne dust, with a **Fence Line Total Dust Action Level of 4.0 mg/m³**. Hourly time-weighted average readings were recorded.
- **Air monitoring results:** Direct-read and laboratory air sample results indicated that airborne levels of **lead and barium** were well below levels of concern during excavation.
- **Completion verification:** The project scientist provided a determination when planned construction excavation limits were reached, exposing native soil of the **Modesto Formation**. Confirmation sampling was conducted and subsequently approved; the *Stockpile 3 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval* documents regulatory acceptance of the sampling approach and results.
- **Documentation:** The removal is recorded in the *Stockpile 3 Excavation Summary Report, Modesto Ramp Rehabilitation Project, State Route 99 Kansas Avenue Northbound Off-Ramp, Modesto, California*, dated March 15, 2013. Key dates for supporting documents include:
  - *Site Investigation Workplan*: April 13, 2012
  - *Transmittal of Site Investigation Data*: April 24, 2012
  - *Stockpile 3 Excavation Monitoring Plan*: June 13, 2012
  - *Transmittal of Stockpile 3 Excavation Monitoring Data*: October 22, 2012

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable standards:** The primary thresholds were **California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs)** for barium—residential 5,200 mg/kg and commercial/industrial 63,000 mg/kg. Barium concentrations in Stockpile 3 (34–1,600 mg/kg) were below both levels. The residential CHHSL for barium was also used as a reference; no site-specific action levels are documented for this action.
- **Agency oversight:** DTSC reviewed the pre-excavation data and issued a concurrence letter (August 30, 2012) that offsite management did not pose a threat. The confirmation sampling plan was approved by regulatory authorities (likely DTSC, though the issuing agency is not explicitly named in the available approval memo).
- **Compliance:** The excavation and disposal were conducted in accordance with DTSC's determination, and the confirmation sampling was approved. No non-compliance is documented for this action.
