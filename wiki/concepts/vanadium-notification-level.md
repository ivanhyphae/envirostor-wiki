## Overview

The **Vanadium Notification Level** is a California regulatory threshold for vanadium in groundwater, established under the California Safe Drinking Water Act (Title 22, California Code of Regulations). Exceeding the notification level does not constitute a drinking water standard violation but triggers mandatory reporting to the State Water Resources Control Board and may require additional monitoring or public notification. In the Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation case (State Route 132, Stanislaus County), vanadium is a contaminant of potential concern in soil stockpiles and underlying groundwater. The project record includes the *July 2012 Groundwater Monitoring Report* (document ID S9525‑06‑44) which is expected to present vanadium monitoring data relative to this notification level.
