## Overview

Cadmium borings refer to soil samples analyzed for cadmium concentrations collected during subsurface investigations at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, California). Cadmium is a regulated heavy metal of concern in soil and groundwater at this site due to historical operations that generated metal-contaminated soil stockpiles. These borings were conducted as part of the site characterization and remediation workplan process overseen by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC). The data from cadmium borings are used to delineate the extent of contamination, evaluate risks, and guide remedial action decisions.

## Site record

The primary documented source for cadmium borings at this site is **Response to DTSC Workplan Comments (Document No. 7084_S9525-06-44, dated September 12, 2005)** . This document is a formal response to DTSC comments on the site workplan. The following facts are found in this source:

- **Sampling locations:** The borings targeted soil stockpiles at the Modesto site located adjacent to State Route 132 in Stanislaus County. Specific boring identifiers are not listed in the available source material, but the workplan response indicates that sampling grids were designed to characterize the stockpiles.
- **Concentrations reported:** The document notes that cadmium was detected in soil samples at concentrations ranging from **2.3 mg/kg to 12.8 mg/kg** across multiple borings. The highest value (12.8 mg/kg) was reported from a boring in the central stockpile area.
- **Depth intervals:** The borings extended to depths of 0–5 feet below ground surface, with the highest cadmium concentrations typically occurring in the 0–2 foot interval.
- **Exceedances:** The DTSC screening level for industrial/commercial soil (direct exposure) at the time of the workplan review was **10 mg/kg** for cadmium. The 12.8 mg/kg result exceeded this screening level, triggering further evaluation.
- **Verification:** The document states that confirmation borings were planned for areas where cadmium exceeded action levels, but no verification results are included in this source.
- **Comparison to background:** No site-specific background cadmium data are provided in this document, but the response notes that regional background is typically below 1.0 mg/kg.

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable standard:** The DTSC modified screening level for cadmium in soil under the industrial/commercial use scenario at this site is **10 mg/kg** (total cadmium based on direct exposure pathway). The federal MCL for cadmium in drinking water is 5 µg/L, but this is not directly applicable to soil stockpiles.
- **Agency oversight:** The project is under the regulatory authority of **DTSC** (California Department of Toxic Substances Control) as part of the Caltrans cleanup program. The site is not currently subject to a formal Record of Decision (ROD) based on available documents.
- **Compliance status:** Based on the single exceedance (12.8 mg/kg above the 10 mg/kg screening level), the site is not fully compliant with DTSC action levels for this metal. However, the workplan response indicates that the exceedance is limited and will be addressed through targeted excavation or capping.

## Open items

- **Confirmation borings:** The workplan response indicates that additional confirmatory borings were required at locations where cadmium exceeded 10 mg/kg to better define the lateral extent of the hot spot. The results of these confirmatory borings are not included in the available source material and may be pending or documented in subsequent reports.
- **Final removal decision:** No final determination has been documented regarding whether the elevated cadmium soil (≥12.8 mg/kg) will be excavated and removed, capped in place, or treated. This decision is conditional on the results of the confirmatory borings and DTSC approval.
- **Groundwater evaluation:** Although the source document focuses on soil, cadmium is also a potential groundwater concern. No groundwater monitoring data for cadmium at this site are provided.
