## Dean Wright

### Overview
Dean Wright, P.G., was the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) Project Manager for the [[caltrans-modesto-soil-stockpiles]] remediation (SR 132, Stanislaus County). In that capacity he issued two key regulatory decisions documented in the project record: final approval of the Interim Phase 1 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) in April 2019, and acceptance of confirmation sampling that verified cleanup of barium-containing soil at Stockpile 2 in March 2020.

### Site record
- **April 3, 2019** – Wright signed and transmitted the *Final Approval of the Remedial Design Implementation Plan — Interim Phase 1* (letter to Geocon Consultants’ John Juhrend). The approved plan described the removal and re-deposition of a portion of the Modesto Soil Stockpiles to support State Route 122 construction, including safety measures and sampling requirements. DTSC and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) reviewed and approved the RDIP and the associated “City of Modesto Book 2 of 3 100% Special Provisions.” No additional comments were noted.
- **March 10, 2020** – Wright sent an email to Caltrans’ Richard Stewart accepting the *Technical Memorandum dated March 9, 2020* that documented removal of Barium Containing Soil (BCS) from the east and west ends of Stockpile 2 and the subsequent confirmation sampling. The memo stated that the RDIP verification thresholds had been achieved, and Wright agreed the areas were “now suitable for the placement of clean embankment fill.” This email serves as DTSC’s acceptance of the memo. CC included Perry Myers (DTSC) and Steven Meeks (RWQCB).

### Regulatory status
Wright acted as the DTSC project manager with delegated authority to approve RDIPs and accept verification results under the Site Mitigation and Restoration Program. The two decisions he issued are final for the Interim Phase 1 scope (no conditions or pending follow-up documented in these sources). The applicable verification thresholds for barium in soil were those established in the RDIP; the confirmation sampling from Stockpile 2 met those thresholds.

### Open items
None documented in the two source documents. Wright’s actions represent complete steps in the project record: the RDIP approval and the Stockpile 2 acceptance. No further actions, deliverables, or contested claims are referenced.
