## Overview

The **Remedial Design Implementation Plan – Interim Phase 1** (RDIP) is the work plan governing the excavation, relocation, and containment of barium‑containing soil (BCS) from the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County. It was prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. for Caltrans (District 6) and submitted on January 30, 2019. The RDIP implements the “containment alternative” selected in the June 2014 Final Feasibility Study and the DTSC‑approved Remedial Action Plan. It describes the removal of stockpile material to facilitate construction of the SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway Phase 1 project, including verification sampling thresholds, worker safety and air monitoring, stormwater controls, groundwater well abandonment, and clean fill capping.

The RDIP was formally approved by the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB), on April 3, 2019. A post‑contract‑award errata was accepted by DTSC on February 5, 2020, updating roles and responsibilities after construction contracts were awarded.

## Site record

- **Document dates**: The RDIP (Interim Phase 1) is dated January 30, 2019. An errata sheet titled *Post Construction Contract Award Errata to the Remedial Design Implementation Plan – Interim Phase 1* was transmitted by Caltrans on February 5, 2020 and accepted by DTSC the same day.
- **Approval**: DTSC (Dean Wright, P.G.) issued a Final Approval Letter on April 3, 2019. Minor comments on the 100% design plans and special provisions were resolved prior to approval.
- **Verification thresholds** set in the RDIP:  
  – Barium: 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection)  
  – Lead: 80 mg/kg (residential screening level)
- **Stockpile 2 BCS removal** (west and east ends): Verification testing performed in February–March 2020. Excavation depths at the west end were initially to ~82 ft MSL, deepened to ~81 ft after elevated barium was found in two samples (S2W 1‑0 and S2W 3‑0). At the east end, excavation started at ~83 ft MSL, deepened to ~82 ft, and locally to ~81 ft. Final verification samples (eight at west end plus two deepenings; twelve at east end plus one deepening) all showed barium ≤ 760 mg/kg and lead ≤ 35 mg/kg, below RDIP thresholds. (Tech Memo, March 9, 2020)
- **Stockpile 3 BCS removal**: Approximately 38,440 cubic yards (3,075 truck‑loads) were excavated and placed in Stockpile 1. Excavation to elevations 82.5–84 ft MSL. Twenty‑four verification samples plus two berm samples were collected (Feb–Mar 2020). Areas around samples S3 9‑0, 10‑0, and 11‑0 were deepened ~1 ft after elevated results. Final barium concentrations ranged 46–970 mg/kg, lead 1.6–40 mg/kg, all below thresholds. (Tech Memo, March 13, 2020)
- **2014 Final Feasibility Study**: DTSC concurrence letter dated June 30, 2014 (Randy S. Adams, C.E.G.). This study established the basis for the containment alternative implemented by the RDIP.

## Regulatory status

The RDIP was approved by DTSC under the authority of the Site Mitigation and Restoration Program, with concurrence from CVRWQCB. The verification thresholds (barium 1,000 mg/kg, lead 80 mg/kg) were the applicable action levels for this project; they are not generic MCLs or background values but site‑specific limits derived from the risk assessment and Feasibility Study. As of the March 2020 tech memos, the BCS removal at Stockpiles 2 and 3 met these thresholds. The RDIP remains in effect for future Interim Phase 1 activities, including capping and final grading. A “Removal Action Completion Report” was to be submitted after final clean‑fill capping of Stockpiles 1 and 2, but its status is not documented in the provided sources.

## Open items

- **Removal Action Completion Report**: Not yet submitted as of the March 2020 tech memos; intended to follow completion of clean‑fill capping at Stockpiles 1 and 2.
- **Groundwater monitoring wells**: The RDIP notes that well MW‑5 was to be abandoned and MW‑8 protected in place; confirmation of abandonment is not in the provided documents.
- **Long‑term monitoring and maintenance**: Not specified in the RDIP excerpts; may be addressed in the final completion report.
