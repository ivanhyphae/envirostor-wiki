## Overview

The **Phase 1 Environmental Site Assessment (ESA)** for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles project (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA) is a preliminary due diligence investigation that evaluated the historical use and environmental condition of properties where soil stockpiles were generated. Completed in phases between 2019 and 2020, this ESA identified recognized environmental conditions (RECs) that led to subsequent soil and groundwater testing, revealing elevated concentrations of metals, pesticides, and other contaminants in stockpiled soils. The ESA served as the foundational document guiding the need for further Phase 2 investigation and eventual remediation planning.

## Site Record

### Document Basis
The primary source for this article is the *Hwy 132 Clean Fill Source Testing Exhibit.pdf*, which references Phase 1 and Phase 2 assessment work conducted for Caltrans District 10. The Phase 1 ESA was performed by a qualified environmental consultant (firm not named in the exhibit) and covered multiple locations along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County.

### Scope and Findings
- **Date of Investigation:** 2019–2020 (Phase 1 completed prior to September 2020).
- **Properties Assessed:** The Phase 1 ESA evaluated historical uses of agricultural lands, adjacent roadways, and former industrial/commercial properties near SR 132 that contributed soils to the Modesto stockpiles.
- **Recognized Environmental Conditions (RECs):** The Phase 1 identified the following RECs:
  - Historical agricultural use with documented application of arsenic-based pesticides and lead-arsenate insecticides.
  - Proximity to former orchard lands (walnut, almond) where organochlorine pesticides (DDT, dieldrin) were historically used.
  - Potential undocumented fill from nearby industrial sources (no specific facilities named in the exhibit).
  - Elevated background levels of arsenic and lead in Stanislaus County alluvial soils (natural occurrence reported in regional geochemical surveys).

### Trigger for Phase 2
The Phase 1 ESA recommended Phase 2 soil sampling due to the RECs. Subsequent testing (referenced in the same exhibit) was performed on stockpile materials, with results including:
- **Arsenic:** Detected up to 15 mg/kg (exceeding California Human Health Screening Level of 0.24 mg/kg for residential soil; non-residential threshold not specified).
- **Lead:** Detected up to 760 mg/kg in one composite sample from Stockpile A-4.
- **DDT (total):** Detected at 2.3 mg/kg in Stockpile B-1, exceeding the EPA Regional Screening Level (RSL) for industrial soil (1.7 mg/kg).
- **Dieldrin:** Detected at 0.15 mg/kg in Stockpile C-3, exceeding the EPA RSL for residential soil (0.042 mg/kg).

These exceedances confirmed that the stockpile soils were not clean fill as originally assumed, necessitating a revised waste management approach.

## Regulatory Status

### Applicable Standards
- **Soil Screening Levels:** The Phase 2 testing used California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs) and EPA Regional Screening Levels (RSLs) for both residential and industrial land use scenarios, as stated in the exhibit.
- **Hazardous Waste Classification:** Soils with lead > 100 mg/kg (Title 22 California Code of Regulations) or pesticide concentrations above hazardous waste thresholds triggered waste characterization requirements.

### Agency Involvement
- **California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC):** Not explicitly named in the exhibit for the Phase 1 phase, but DTSC standards were referenced for screening levels.
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB):** The project area falls under CVRWQCB jurisdiction for groundwater protection. The Phase 1 ESA noted potential impacts to shallow groundwater beneath stockpile sites.

### Current Compliance
The Phase 1 ESA was completed and accepted by Caltrans as the basis for proceeding to Phase 2. No regulatory enforcement actions were documented in the exhibit. However, the stockpile soils identified as contaminated require further management under Caltrans’ Remedial Action Plan (RAP) as of the file date.
