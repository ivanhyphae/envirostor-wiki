# Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE Wall Sampling Plan

## Overview

The **MSE Wall Sampling Plan** is a DTSC- and Water Board-approved field investigation program developed to characterize soils within Mechanically Stabilized Earth (MSE) wall footing excavations at Stockpiles 1 and 2, located along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. The plan provides a ten-location soil sampling grid that supports regulatory classification of excavated soils for on-site beneficial reuse as clean capping material, abutment/embankment fill, or placement in a BCS containment zone.

## Site Record

### Regulatory Decision and Date

- **Agency:** California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the State Water Resources Control Board
- **Action:** Formal acceptance of the sampling plan
- **Date:** April 2, 2020
- **Document:** E-mail from **Dean Wright, P.G., DTSC Project Manager** to **Richard C. Stewart, Caltrans**, subject "RE: Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE Wall Sampling Plan"
- **Statement:** "DTSC and the Water Board have reviewed the MSE Wall Sampling Plan e-mail and figures received March 31, 2020 (Sampling Plan). There are no comments from either DTSC or the Water Board and this e-mail serves as our acceptance of the Sampling Plan."

### Sampling Design

- **Proponent:** Geocon, Inc. (John Juhrend, PE, CEG, CEM)
- **Requestor:** Caltrans (Richard C. Stewart)
- **Field Supervisor:** Geocon's **Dave Watts, CAC** (onsite for RDIP monitoring including air/dust perimeter sampling)
- **Sampling method:** Borings or trenches located at the midpoint of the wall footing excavation
- **Locations:** Ten (10) identified sampling points spaced at approximate **200-foot intervals**
- **Depth:** One-foot intervals to the planned excavation depth of **~4 to 5 feet** (discrete soil samples)
- **Analytes:** Barium and lead
- **Turnaround:** Expedited **48-hour** laboratory analysis

### Work Sequencing

- Excavation was to begin **March 31, 2020** on the Stockpile 1 southern slope zone BCS (Backfill Containment System) excavation.
- Sampling was to occur "as the BCS slope zone is removed."

## Regulatory Status

The plan has been formally accepted by both DTSC and the Water Board. No further agency action was required as of the April 2, 2020 e-mail. The project is proceeding under the approved sampling framework.
