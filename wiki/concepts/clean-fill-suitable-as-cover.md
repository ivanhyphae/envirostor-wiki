## Overview

Clean Fill Suitable As Cover is a regulatory classification used by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) for soil materials that may be placed as final cover material over remediated areas at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site, located along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County. This designation was applied specifically to the Carpenter Shoofly soil stockpile and is distinct from the "Fill Suitable for Reuse" classification applied to other stockpiles at the site.

## Site Record

The Clean Fill Suitable As Cover classification was formally established through DTSC's review and approval of two key documents in 2022:

1. **Carpenter Shoofly Technical Memorandum** – Approved by DTSC via email (source: raw/DTSC E-mail Approval of Carpenter Shoofly Tech Memo.pdf). This document established that soil from the Carpenter Shoofly stockpile met the chemical criteria for use as clean cover material.

2. **Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Sampling Plan** – Approved by DTSC via email (source: raw/DTSC Approval E-mail for Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Sampling Plan.pdf). This plan defined the sampling protocol to verify that stockpiled soil met clean fill standards before placement as cover.

The technical evaluation demonstrated that Carpenter Shoofly soil concentrations were below the applicable Human Health Risk-Based Screening Levels for unrestricted land use, which served as the basis for the clean cover designation. Specific analyte concentrations are not detailed in the source documents but were compared against DTSC-modified screening levels for residential land use scenarios.

The volume of material classified as Clean Fill Suitable As Cover from the Carpenter Shoofly stockpile is not specified in the available source documentation.

## Regulatory Status

The Clean Fill Suitable As Cover designation is governed by:

- **Primary Agency**: DTSC (California Department of Toxic Substances Control)
- **Applicable Standard**: DTSC-modified human health risk-based screening levels for residential land use scenarios
- **Sampling Requirements**: As defined in the approved Clean Fill Sampling Plan (Carpenter Shoofly)

DTSC's conditional approval of both the Technical Memorandum and the Sampling Plan represents regulatory concurrence that the Carpenter Shoofly stockpile material could be used as cover, subject to the verification sampling protocols. The site remains subject to DTSC oversight through the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Remediation case file.
