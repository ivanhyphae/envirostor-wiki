## Overview

**Clean Fill Suitable As Cover** is a regulatory classification applied by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) to soil materials intended for use as final cover over remediated areas at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (State Route 132, Stanislaus County). The designation was established for multiple soil borrow sources, including the Carpenter Shoofly area and Basin 5, and required DTSC-approved sampling plans to verify that material met chemical criteria for unrestricted, beneficial reuse as clean cover. This classification is distinct from the "Fill Suitable for Reuse" designation applied to on-site stockpile materials that had undergone barium and lead removal.

## Site Record

### Carpenter Shoofly Area Clean Fill Sampling Plan (April 2021)

On April 5, 2021, Richard Stewart (Caltrans) submitted a revised **Carpenter Shoofly Area Clean Fill Sampling Plan** to DTSC. The plan outlined sampling and analysis for soil from the Carpenter Shoofly location that would be used as clean fill. By email dated April 7, 2021, **Dean Wright, PG (DTSC Project Manager)** provided approval, stating: *"The Plan is consistent with previous borrow area sampling efforts and there are no comments. This e-mail serves as DTSCs approval of the Plan."* The email was cc’d to Perry Myers (DTSC) and Kyle Cockerham (Waterboards).

### SR 132 Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan (June 2020)

On June 11, 2020, Geocon Consultants submitted a **Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan** to Caltrans for DTSC review. The proposed borrow area was Basin 5, located adjacent to and north of the previously tested SR 132 roadway cut section east of Carpenter Road. Geocon stated that Basin 5 was anticipated to expose non-impacted native formation similar to the Carpenter Road area. Richard Stewart forwarded the plan to DTSC and the Waterboard (Dean Wright, Perry Myers, Steven Meeks), noting that *"Clean cover mining location at proposed Basin 5"* was under review and that Geocon was *"assuming that the Carpenter technical memo that was previously submitted is applicable"* — i.e., the sampling methodology and background metal comparison from the Carpenter Shoofly technical memorandum would apply to Basin 5 without a separate technical memo.

### BCS Removal at Stockpile 3 (February–March 2020)

The **SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo** documented removal of barium-, chromium-, and strontium (BCS)-impacted soil from Stockpile 3 to expose native conditions. The removal verification thresholds were:
- **Barium**: 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection)
- **Lead**: 80 mg/kg (residential screening level)

Stockpile 3 was excavated to elevations between 82.5 and 84 feet above mean sea level. A total of 24 verification soil samples were collected (February 28 – March 12, 2020) from the excavation base. Barium concentrations in final verification samples ranged from **46 mg/kg to 970 mg/kg**, and lead from **1.6 mg/kg to 40 mg/kg** — all below the respective verification thresholds. The memo concluded that the excavation was *"suitable for placement of clean embankment fill and other highway improvements without further BCS RDIP monitoring requirements."*

## Regulatory Status

- **Primary Oversight Agency**: DTSC (Dean Wright, Project Manager)
- **Secondary Agency**: Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (Kyle Cockerham, Steven Meeks)
- **Applicable Standards for Clean Fill**:
  - Carpenter Shoofly: DTSC-modified human health risk-based screening levels for unrestricted/residential land use (specific thresholds not enumerated in available sources)
  - Basin 5: Same standards as Carpenter Shoofly, by reference to the Carpenter technical memo
- **BCS Removal Verification Thresholds** (Stockpile 3): Barium 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection); Lead 80 mg/kg (residential screening level)

DTSC’s April 7, 2021 email constitutes formal regulatory approval of the Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Sampling Plan. The Basin 5 plan was submitted for DTSC review but no formal approval email is present in the available sources. Stockpile 3 BCS removal was verified as meeting thresholds, per the technical memo.

## Open Items

- **Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan**: The June 2020 submittal was forwarded for DTSC review, but no documented DTSC approval or denial exists in the available record. The plan’s reliance on the Carpenter Shoofly technical memo without a separate background metal comparison was flagged by Caltrans but not resolved in the source documents.
- **Post-placement verification**: No data documenting whether Carpenter Shoofly or Basin 5 clean fill was actually placed as cover, or whether post-placement compliance monitoring occurred, are included in the available materials.
- **Relationship between clean cover material and other stockpile classifications**: The sources do not address how clean cover material from borrow sources interacts with on-site stockpile materials classified as "Fill Suitable for Reuse."
