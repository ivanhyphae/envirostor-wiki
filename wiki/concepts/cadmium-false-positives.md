## Cadmium False Positives

### Overview
Cadmium false positives refer to analytical results for cadmium in soil and groundwater samples from the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA) that were later determined to be erroneous due to laboratory interference or sampling artifacts. These false positives complicated the site characterization and risk assessment by indicating cadmium concentrations that exceeded applicable screening levels, triggering unnecessary remedial evaluation and delaying project decisions. The issue was identified during the Feasibility Study (FS) phase and resolved through data validation and re-analysis.

### Site Record
The Final Feasibility Study Report (FSR, dated June 2014, Document No. S9800-01-17) documents the false positive issue in Section 3.0 (Nature and Extent of Contamination) and Appendix E (Data Validation). Key findings include:

- **Initial Results**: Laboratory analysis of soil samples from stockpile areas (e.g., Stockpile A, B, and C) reported cadmium concentrations ranging from 2.5 to 12.4 mg/kg, exceeding the California Human Health Screening Level (CHHSL) for residential soil (1.7 mg/kg) and the Industrial/Commercial Screening Level (4.0 mg/kg) at multiple locations.
- **False Positive Identification**: Data validation flagged high cadmium concentrations in samples with elevated total organic carbon (TOC) and silt/clay content. The FSR notes that "cadmium results in fine-grained, organic-rich materials were suspect due to potential matrix interference" (FSR, p. 3-12). Re-analysis of archived sample splits using a different analytical method (EPA Method 3050B/6020 instead of initial EPA Method 3051A/6010) showed cadmium concentrations below detection limits (0.1 mg/kg) in 14 out of 16 re-analyzed samples.
- **Groundwater Samples**: False positives also occurred in groundwater monitoring wells (MW-5, MW-7, and MW-8) where initial cadmium results ranged from 2.5 to 5.8 µg/L, exceeding the MCL of 5 µg/L. Re-analysis confirmed concentrations <1 µg/L, attributed to turbidity-induced contamination (total suspended solids >50 mg/L in unfiltered samples).
- **Spatial Distribution**: False positives were spatially correlated with fine-grained soil units (silty clay loam) in the western half of the stockpile areas, where organic matter content was 3–5% (compared to <1% in sandy areas with no false positives).

### Regulatory Status
- **Applicable Standards**: The relevant thresholds were the California MCL for cadmium in groundwater (5 µg/L) and the EPA Regional Screening Level (RSL) for cadmium in soil (industrial/construction worker: 85 mg/kg; residential: 1.7 mg/kg). These were cited in the FSR.
- **Agency Review**: DTSC reviewed the FSR and accepted the false positive explanation in a letter dated November 14, 2014 (DTSC File No. 2012-004-TH). DTSC concurred that cadmium was not a constituent of concern (COC) at the site, based on the re-analysis data.
- **Current Compliance**: After validation, all sample locations met the applicable standards, and cadmium was removed from the list of COCs for the site. No post-remediation monitoring for cadmium is required.

### Open Items
- The FSR does not specify if the lab that produced the false positives (Analytical Lab A) was audited or if corrective action was taken. A follow-up audit by Caltrans (if any) is not documented in the FSR.
- Two re-analysis results (Stockpile C, sample depth 4–6 ft) showed cadmium at 0.15 mg/kg, just above the detection limit but below screening levels. The FSR does not address whether these trace levels warrant further investigation or if they reflect residual matrix interference.
