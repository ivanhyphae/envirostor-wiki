# Caltrans Division of Environmental Analysis

## Overview
The Caltrans Division of Environmental Analysis (DEA) is the organizational unit within the California Department of Transportation responsible for environmental compliance, including oversight of remediation activities at Caltrans-owned properties. In the context of the SR 132 Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, the DEA serves as the Caltrans entity directing groundwater and stormwater monitoring programs, coordinating with regulatory agencies, and managing consultant contracts for site characterization and cleanup. The DEA is the signatory authority on monitoring reports and the point of contact for regulatory submittals to the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board.

## Site Record
The DEA is documented as the authorizing agency for all environmental monitoring at the Modesto Stockpiles site, which includes five stockpile areas (Stockpiles 1 through 5) along SR 132 between Emerald Avenue and Gates Avenue in Stanislaus County. The DEA contracted with consultant firm CRA (formerly Cornerstone Earth Group) to conduct quarterly groundwater monitoring and stormwater sampling.

Groundwater monitoring activities managed by DEA include:
- **September 2012 event** (reported December 2012, document S9525-06-44A): Sampling of wells MW-1 through MW-10, with detections of arsenic, barium, chromium, lead, nickel, vanadium, and zinc above background or MCL thresholds. Barium concentrations ranged up to 760 mg/kg in soil and up to 1,000 µg/L in groundwater (MCL).
- **June 2013 event** (reported June 2013, document S9525-01-44B): Continued quarterly monitoring observing persistent exceedances of groundwater MCLs for arsenic, nickel, and vanadium in multiple monitoring wells.
- **March 2023 event** (reported March 8, 2023, document 06A2542ct_TO97_GW Rpt_final): Tenth quarterly sampling event under current work plan, with groundwater results compared to site-specific background thresholds established with DTSC approval.

Stormwater sampling directed by DEA includes:
- **March 2016 event** (report dated April 2016, document S9800-01-17A): Sampling of surface water runoff from stockpile areas. Dissolved metals (copper, lead, zinc) detected at concentrations exceeding Basin Plan water quality objectives for the Tuolumne River.
- **October 2016 event** (report dated December 2016, document S1200-01-01): Semi-annual stormwater monitoring showing continued exceedances of copper and zinc.
- **March 2018 event** (report dated April 2018, document S1200-01-01): Sampling following 0.35 inches of precipitation. Copper was detected at 23 µg/L at station SW-1, exceeding the Basin Plan objective of 9 µg/L.
- **February 2025 event** (report dated June 2025, document S2350-01-02): Most recent stormwater sampling. Total copper at SW-1 measured 45 µg/L; dissolved copper at SW-2 was 28 µg/L, both above applicable criteria.

The Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP) figures (document RDIP All Figures.pdf) show the DEA-approved grading and capping plan for all five stockpiles, with cross-sections indicating soil cover thickness between 2 and 5 feet.

## Regulatory Status
The DEA operates the Modesto Stockpiles site under oversight of:
- **DTSC** (as lead regulatory agency under the California Hazardous Substance Account)
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board** (for surface water and groundwater quality under the Basin Plan)

Applicable standards enforced through DEA-managed monitoring include:
- **California MCLs** for groundwater drinking water standards (e.g., barium MCL 1,000 µg/L, arsenic MCL 10 µg/L, nickel MCL 0.1 mg/L)
- **Basin Plan water quality objectives** for the Tuolumne River (e.g., copper 9 µg/L dissolved, zinc 120 µg/L dissolved)
- **Site-specific background thresholds** established with DTSC approval for groundwater metals

As of the most recent reporting (2025 stormwater report), selected stormwater discharge points remain in exceedance of Basin Plan objectives, though the DEA has implemented erosion control measures and stockpile capping to reduce runoff contact.

## Open Items
The following issues remain pending based on the project record:
- **Stormwater compliance**: Copper concentrations at SW-1 and SW-2 continue to exceed Basin Plan objectives as of February 2025, requiring continued sampling under the DEA's stormwater monitoring plan.
- **Groundwater trend evaluation**: The March 2023 groundwater report notes that several monitoring wells (MW-2, MW-4, MW-6, MW-8) show no statistically significant downward trend in metals concentrations over five years of quarterly data.
- **Final remedy completion**: The RDIP grading plan figures indicate soil capping is not yet complete for Stockpiles 3 and 5, with no documented completion date in the records reviewed.
