## Overview

The **Well Destruction Report** documents the procedures and results for decommissioning the remaining four groundwater monitoring wells at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA). The report was prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. on behalf of Caltrans, dated June 18, 2025. Ten monitoring wells were originally installed in 2012 to detect potential migration of barium from three encapsulated soil stockpiles to groundwater. Six wells were decommissioned in 2019 during Phase I construction. The Well Destruction Report covers the final four wells, whose abandonment was necessary to allow continued highway construction.

## Site Record

- **Regulatory lead**: California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), coordinated by Project Manager Arielle McLeskey.
- **Reviewing entities**: DTSC’s Geological Services Branch (GSB) reviewed the Well Destruction Report; DTSC’s Human and Ecological Risk Office (HERO) reviewed the accompanying Storm Water Sampling Report.
- **Disposition**: The report received **conditional approval** via DTSC letter dated January 30, 2026 (Adam Inman, Caltrans District 6, to Project Manager).
- **Condition**: The report must be revised to include a table comparing the **estimated volume of grout** needed to decommission each well versus the **actual volume of grout used**. This data is required as a performance metric for well abandonment.
- **Due date**: Final version with the requested table due within **30 calendar days** of the January 30, 2026 approval letter.
- **Background on wells**: The ten monitoring wells were installed in 2012. After years of groundwater monitoring data demonstrated no leaching of metals (specifically barium) from the stockpiles, the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board and DTSC agreed to decommission them. Six wells were destroyed in 2019 to enable Phase I highway construction. The four remaining wells were addressed in the 2025 report.
- **Accompanying report**: The Storm Water Sampling Report (also June 18, 2025, by Geocon) was approved without conditions based on GSB and HERO review; all storm water sample results were within reporting limits.

## Regulatory Status

- **Conditional approval**: The Well Destruction Report is not yet fully approved. DTSC conditionally approved it pending receipt of a final version incorporating the grout volume comparison table.
- **Agencies involved**: DTSC (lead), Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (consulted for groundwater monitoring well decommissioning concurrence).
- **Compliance**: The Storm Water Sampling Report is in full compliance and approved. The Well Destruction Report will be in compliance upon submittal of the revised final version.

## Open Items

- **Grout volume documentation**: The final Well Destruction Report must be revised to include a table of estimated vs. actual grout volumes for each of the four decommissioned wells. No other open items are documented in the source material.
