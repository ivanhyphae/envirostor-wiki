## DTSC

### Overview
The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) is the lead regulatory agency overseeing the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Remedial Action at SR 132 in Stanislaus County. DTSC exercised direct oversight of all phases of the remedial action, including workplan review, remedial design, implementation, and long-term monitoring. DTSC acted as the certifying agency under CEQA and co‑regulated with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB).

### Site Record
DTSC’s involvement is documented across the project record:

- **Remedial Action Plan (RAP)** – DTSC formally approved Alternative 4 (Containment) as the selected remedy on **March 14, 2018** (Approval letter for SR 132 RAP). The remedy consists of retaining walls, bridge abutments, roadway construction over stockpiles, and clean fill cover on unpaved areas. DTSC determined no significant environmental effect with implementation of institutional controls.
- **Technical document approvals** – DTSC approved multiple project‑related submittals:
  - Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Sampling Plan (e‑mail approval, no date specified in source).
  - Carpenter Shoofly Tech Memo (DTSC e‑mail approval, file name `DTSC E-mail Approval of Carpenter Shoofly Tech Memo.pdf`).
  - Stockpile 3 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo (`Stockpile 3 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval.pdf`).
  - Final version of the Well Destruction Report (`DTSC Approval Response for final version of Well Destruction Report.pdf`).
  - RDIP (Remedial Design Implementation Plan) errata (`RDIP Errata Acceptance.pdf`).
  - Final Stormwater Management Plan (SMP) (`Final SMP Approval E-mail.pdf`).
- **Workplan comments** – DTSC provided comments on the Workplan; a response document is dated September 2012 (`Response to DTSC Work Plan Comments.0912.pdf`).
- **Monitoring review** – DTSC reviewed the 2025 Groundwater Monitoring Report and issued a letter response (`DTSC Letter Response - 2025 GW Monitoring Report (900259).pdf`).
- **Stormwater sampling** – DTSC received stormwater sampling results from March 2016 and October 2016 (`Modesto Stockpiles Mar 5 2016 Stormwater Sampling_04 16.pdf` and `Modesto Stockpiles Oct 2016 SW Sampling_12.16.pdf`).

### Regulatory Status
DTSC’s authority derives from the California Health and Safety Code, Division 20, Chapter 6.5 (Hazardous Substance Account). Under the RAP, DTSC imposed the following conditions:

- Land use covenant restricting future use to prevent exposure.
- Annual pavement inspection and groundwater monitoring.
- Five‑year remedy evaluation.
- Phased implementation: Phase 1 (2019–2020), Phase 2 (2028–2029) per the approval letter.
- Requirement for a Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) before each construction phase.
- Two‑week public notice (English/Spanish) before construction.

The remedy is currently in the long‑term operation and maintenance (O&M) phase. Contamination remains in place under the containment cap. DTSC has not issued any notice of non‑compliance based on the provided sources; the site is subject to ongoing monitoring and five‑year reviews.

### Open Items
- The O&M Plan / O&M Manual (OMA/OMP) may require revision after Phase 2 (2028–2029) per the RAP approval letter.
- No formal five‑year remedy evaluation report has been identified in the sources; such a report is required under the remedy conditions.
- Groundwater monitoring data from 2025 has been submitted and reviewed by DTSC, but any follow‑up actions (if needed) are not documented in the available record.
