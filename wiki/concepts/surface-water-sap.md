## Overview

The **Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP)** for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (Site) is the governing protocol document for all surface water monitoring activities at the property. It establishes the collection methods, analytical parameters, quality assurance/quality control procedures, and reporting requirements for assessing surface water quality in the drainage features adjacent to Stockpiles 1 through 3. The SAP was prepared by Shaw Environmental, Inc., dated January 2006, and approved by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC). A later addendum, dated February 20, 2013, was issued by Geocon to update or refine the original plan.

## Site record

- **Document identification**: *Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP)*, Shaw Environmental, Inc., January 2006; *Addendum to Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan*, Geocon, February 20, 2013.
- **Project context**: Both documents were prepared under Caltrans Contract No. 06A2184, Task Order No. 1, EA No. 10-0X2700 for the Site located south of the intersection of State Route (SR) 99 and Kansas Avenue, Stanislaus County, California.
- **Implementation**: Surface water samples were collected on March 22, 2018 "in general accordance with protocols approved by DTSC as established in the Final SAP (January 2006) and the Addendum (February 20, 2013)."
- **Locations**: Sampling locations were situated relative to Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3 and are depicted on Figure 2 of the March 2018 sampling report.
- **Laboratory analysis**: Samples were submitted to a California-certified laboratory; specific analytes and results are documented in the March 22, 2018 Surface Water Sampling Report (Geocon, April 24, 2018).
- **Regulatory coordination**: All sampling under the SAP was conducted under the oversight of DTSC and Caltrans District 6 Hazardous Waste Branch.

## Regulatory status

The SAP was reviewed and approved by DTSC. The Addendum was issued under the same regulatory framework. As of the April 2018 report, all sampling events referenced in the case file (e.g., March 22, 2018) were performed in accordance with the approved SAP and Addendum. Compliance with the SAP is a condition of the ongoing Site investigation.

## Open items

No unresolved issues, pending deliverables, or contested claims are identified in the single source document. The SAP itself is a guiding document; any deviations or future amendments would require DTSC concurrence.
