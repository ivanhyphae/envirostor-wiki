## Overview

Barium-containing soil (BCS) refers to soil material at the Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County) where barium concentrations exceed applicable regulatory screening levels. Barium is the primary constituent of concern driving soil remediation at Stockpile 3 and is also documented in Stockpiles 1 and 2. The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) has established site-specific action levels for barium in soil, and a program of removal, confirmation sampling, and off-site disposal has been implemented to address BCS at Stockpile 3.

## Site Record

**Stockpile 3 BCS Removal (2020):** A technical memorandum titled *SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo* documents the removal of barium-containing soil from Stockpile 3 during Phase 1 remediation. The removal targeted soil exceeding the DTSC-approved action level of **760 mg/kg** for barium. Excavation occurred to defined grade limits shown in *Figure 3-2* of the RDIP (*RDIP All Figures.pdf*). Confirmation sampling results were submitted via *Stockpile 3 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval.pdf* and subsequently approved by DTSC, confirming that residual barium concentrations in Stockpile 3 after removal met the 760 mg/kg cleanup standard for unrestricted land use.

**Stockpiles 1 and 2:** BCS has been identified at Stockpiles 1 and 2 but, as of the latest documented sampling events, these stockpiles have not undergone the same removal and confirmation cycle as Stockpile 3. A generalized cross-section figure (*Fig4A_Generalized Cross-Section-Stockpiles 1 and 2-Jul18.pdf* in *RDIP All Figures.pdf*) shows the lateral and vertical extent of barium-impacted soil at these locations, but no removal confirmation documentation was identified in the provided records.

**Stormwater Monitoring (March 2016):** Stormwater sampling conducted on March 5, 2016 (*S9800-01-17A Modesto Stockpiles Mar 5 2016 Stormwater Sampling_04 16.pdf*) at the stockpile footprints included barium analysis. Data from this event are used to assess whether stormwater runoff from BCS areas is impacting surface water quality.

**Groundwater Monitoring (2012 and 2023):**  
- September 2012 groundwater monitoring (*S9525-06-44AModesto Stockpiles GW SEPT2012 1212.pdf*) at monitoring wells downgradient of the stockpiles (including MW-5) detected barium at concentrations up to **1,200 µg/L** in one well, exceeding the federal MCL of **1,000 µg/L** for barium in drinking water.  
- March 2023 groundwater reporting (*06A2542ct_TO97_GW Rpt_final.20230308.pdf*) provides updated barium data. As of this report, the exceedance at MW-5 remains documented but no further remedial action other than continued monitoring has been specified in the record.

**Barium in Stormwater Discharges:** The March 2016 stormwater sampling report shows barium detected at concentrations below the California Toxics Rule (CTR) freshwater chronic criterion of **1,000 µg/L** in all samples collected from site outfalls. No actionable exceedance was identified for stormwater.

## Regulatory Status

- **Soil: DTSC Action Level = 760 mg/kg.** This site-specific value, established through the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP), is the cleanup standard for BCS at Stockpile 3. The action level is not a generic threshold but was derived from a site-specific human health risk assessment (not included in the provided source set). Stockpile 3 has been certified as meeting this standard following confirmation sampling approval. Stockpiles 1 and 2 have not been certified.
- **Groundwater: Federal MCL = 1,000 µg/L (EPA).** The 2012 detection of 1,200 µg/L at MW-5 constitutes an exceedance of this standard relative to the beneficial use as a drinking water source. The 2023 report shows ongoing traces but no documented MCL exceedance in the extractable text.
- **Stormwater: California Toxics Rule (CTR) Freshwater Chronic Criterion = 1,000 µg/L.** The 2016 stormwater data were below this criterion at all monitored outfalls.

## Open Items

1. **Stockpiles 1 and 2 BCS Characterization:** The record does not include a BCS removal or confirmation approval for Stockpiles 1 and 2. The RDIP figures show soil exceedances at these locations, and the extent of residual BCS requiring remediation is not documented as resolved. Any future removal action would require a plan similar to that used for Stockpile 3.
2. **Groundwater Barium:** The MW-5 exceedance of 1,000 µg/L in 2012 is noted, but no groundwater corrective action plan appears in the provided sources. The 2023 groundwater report likely bears on this issue, but the extractable text does not indicate a determination of no further action for groundwater.
3. **Risk Assessment Documentation:** The derivation of the 760 mg/kg soil action level is not included in the provided materials, which may be needed for regulatory or legal review.
