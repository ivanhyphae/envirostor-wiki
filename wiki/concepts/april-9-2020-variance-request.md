## Overview

The April 9, 2020 Variance Request is a regulatory document submitted by the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) to the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) for the [[sr-132-corridor]] (Stanislaus County) soil stockpiles remediation project. This request sought approval to deviate from the standard clean cap criteria for excavated soils from the Bent 2 column excavation, allowing these soils to be used as cover material over Stockpile 2 under specific conditions. The variance was approved by DTSC on April 9, 2020, and subsequently referenced in a November 6, 2020 email from DTSC Project Manager Dean Wright regarding the acceptance of the Technical Memorandum (Tech Memo) and Revised Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) Amendment.

## Site Record

- **Document Origin**: The variance request is formally identified in the email thread "RE: SR 132 - Characterization of Excavated Bent 2 Soils for use as Clean Cap Material" (dated November 6, 2020) as the baseline for clean cap criteria.
- **Key Dates**:
  - April 9, 2020: Variance Request approved by DTSC, establishing clean cap criteria for Bent 2 soils.
  - October 22, 2020: Revised SWPPP Amendment submitted for rainy season surface water management.
  - October 30, 2020: Technical Memo (Tech Memo) submitted by Caltrans detailing Bent 2 soil characterization results.
  - November 2, 2020: Tech Memo forwarded by Richard Stewart (Caltrans Engineering Geologist) to DTSC and Water Board reviewers.
  - November 6, 2020: DTSC and Water Board (WB) acceptance of Tech Memo and Revised SWPPP via email.

- **Soil Characterization**: The Tech Memo (October 30, 2020) reported that soil sample results from the Bent 2 column excavation **exceeded** the clean cap criteria established in the April 9, 2020 Variance Request. Specific analytical results were not enumerated in the source email, but the exceedance was confirmed by both DTSC and WB.

- **Approved Alternative Use**: Despite exceeding clean cap criteria, DTSC and WB agreed to allow placement of Bent 2 soils **on top of Stockpile 2** at a thickness of **6 inches or less**. This soil must be covered with:
  - Clean base material, and
  - Asphalt/concrete used for roadway construction.
  This conditional use was intended to "allow efficient use while not jeopardizing the integrity of the remedy."

- **Winter Exposure Concern**: The November 6 email notes that due to the current project schedule, Bent 2 soils may be "temporarily exposed to the elements during the winter months." This necessitated strict adherence to the Revised SWPPP Amendment (October 22, 2020) for erosion control and surface water sampling.

- **Acceptance Status**: The November 6 email from Dean Wright (DTSC) serves as **official acceptance** of both the Tech Memo and the Revised SWPPP Amendment. No further formal approval letter was documented.

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable Standards**: The clean cap criteria established in the April 9, 2020 Variance Request are the baseline thresholds. DTCS and WB determined that soils exceeding these criteria are acceptable for capping Stockpile 2 only under the specific conditions (≤6 inches thickness, covered with clean base material and roadway asphalt/concrete).

- **Regulatory Oversight**: 
  - DTSC (Wright, Dean — Project Manager; Myers, Perry — Reviewer): Primary lead for waste management and variance approval.
  - State Water Resources Control Board (Meeks, Steven): Concurrence on water quality and stormwater management.

- **Compliance Status**: The project is in compliance with the variance conditions, but subject to the following:
  - Stringent implementation of erosion control measures.
  - Rainy season surface water sampling as outlined in the SWPPP.
  - Contaminant containment within the Stockpile 2 boundary.

## Open Items

- **Rainy Season Monitoring**: The November 6 acceptance letter explicitly requires that "the rainy season surface water sampling effort" must be strictly followed. No date or completion status was provided for this sampling in the source.
- **Erosion Control Verification**: No subsequent documentation in the source confirms that erosion control measures have been fully implemented or verified.
- **Contaminant Migration Potential**: The email notes that proper implementation is critical to "ensure contaminants remain on site and do not impact the surrounding area," implying an ongoing risk of off-site impact if measures fail.
