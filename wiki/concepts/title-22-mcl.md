## Overview

Title 22 MCL (Maximum Contaminant Level) is the enforceable drinking‑water standard codified in California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 4, Chapter 15. For the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County), Title 22 MCLs are the primary regulatory benchmarks for groundwater quality, set by DTSC as cleanup thresholds. The project’s biennial monitoring reports compare analytical results to these standards to evaluate compliance and guide remedial decisions.

## Site Record

The use of Title 22 MCLs is documented in three project reports:

- **Groundwater Monitoring Report, September 2012** (file `S9525-06-44A Modesto Stockpiles GW SEPT2012 1212.pdf`): analytical results from the 2012 monitoring event were compared to Title 22 MCLs. The report lists detected concentrations of VOCs, metals, and other constituents and notes which wells (e.g., MW‑series) exceeded the applicable MCL thresholds (e.g., benzene 1 µg/L, 1,2‑dichloroethane 0.5 µg/L, arsenic 10 µg/L, chromium‑6 10 µg/L).  
- **Groundwater Monitoring Report, June 2013** (file `S9525-01-44B Modesto Stockpiles GW June 2013.0613.pdf`): a subsequent monitoring round again used Title 22 MCLs as the compliance criterion. This report tabulates constituent concentrations and describes changes since the September 2012 event.  
- **Surface Water Sampling Report, March 22, 2018** (file `S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles March 2018 SW Sampling_04.18.pdf`, cover letter dated April 24, 2018): samples were collected in accordance with the *Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP)* (Shaw Environmental, Inc., January 2006) and the *Addendum to Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan* (February 20, 2013). The cover letter does not explicitly state that Title 22 MCLs were used as benchmarks; however, the project’s SAP and prior monitoring practice consistently applied Title 22 MCLs. The report’s full results and comparison to regulatory thresholds are presented in the body of the document (not reproduced in the provided excerpt). The sampling locations were intermittent drainage channels adjacent to Stockpiles 1‑3, as shown on Figure 2 of that report.

In the two groundwater reports, specific exceedances of Title 22 MCLs are tabulated. The March 2018 surface water report serves as a supplementary screening event; its compliance implications for groundwater depend on whether any MCL exceedances were detected in surface water that could migrate to groundwater.

## Regulatory Status

Title 22 MCLs are enforced by the California State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) for public drinking water systems and are adopted by DTSC as cleanup standards under the California Hazardous Waste Control Law. At the Modesto Stockpiles site, DTSC requires that groundwater concentrations not exceed applicable MCLs at the point of compliance (site boundary or downgradient well). The September 2012 and June 2013 groundwater reports are interim compliance demonstrations. As of the March 2018 surface water sampling event, no formal determination of compliance or non‑compliance with Title 22 MCLs is stated in the provided excerpts; the reports present data and note any exceedances.
