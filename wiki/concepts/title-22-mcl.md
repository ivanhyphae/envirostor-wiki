## Overview

Title 22 MCL (Maximum Contaminant Level) refers to the enforceable drinking‑water standard established under California Code of Regulations, Title 22, Division 4, Chapter 15 (domestic water quality). In the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles case (SR 132, Stanislaus County), Title 22 MCLs serve as the primary regulatory benchmark for groundwater quality, both for interim monitoring and for final remediation goals. The project is overseen by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), which applies Title 22 MCLs as cleanup thresholds for groundwater impacted by historical soil stockpile operations.

## Site Record

Title 22 MCLs are explicitly referenced in the following project documents:

- **Groundwater Monitoring Report, September 2012** (file `S9525-06-44AModesto Stockpiles GW  SEPT2012 1212.pdf`) – analytical results from the biennial groundwater‑monitoring event were compared to Title 22 MCLs. The document presents detected concentrations of volatile organic compounds (VOCs), metals, and other constituents in monitoring wells (e.g., MW‑series) and evaluates exceedances relative to MCL thresholds.  
- **Groundwater Monitoring Report, June 2013** (file `S9525-01-44B Modesto Stockpiles GW June 2013.0613.pdf`) – a subsequent monitoring round again used Title 22 MCLs as the compliance criterion. The report includes tabulated data showing which constituents (if any) exceeded the MCL and notes any changes in concentration since the September 2012 event.  
- **Surface Water Sampling Report, March 2018** (file `S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles March 2018 SW Sampling_04.18.pdf`) – although surface water, not groundwater, this report used Title 22 MCLs as screening‑level benchmarks for comparison, likely because the sampled surface water bodies (intermittent drainage channels adjacent to the stockpiles) could potentially affect shallow groundwater.

In all three documents, the MCLs are applied to individual analytes (e.g., benzene 1 µg/L, 1,2‑dichloroethane 0.5 µg/L, arsenic 10 µg/L, chromium‑6 10 µg/L—values standard under Title 22). Specific exceedances are noted in the source tables, but the truncated text provided does not include those numeric results. The reports collectively indicate that groundwater beneath and downgradient of the stockpiles was compared to MCLs during each monitoring period.

## Regulatory Status

Title 22 MCLs are enforced by the California State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) for public drinking water systems and are adopted by DTSC as cleanup standards under the California Hazardous Waste Control Law. At the Modesto Stockpiles site, DTSC requires that groundwater concentrations not exceed the applicable MCLs at the point of compliance (typically the site boundary or the nearest downgradient well). The September 2012 and June 2013 groundwater reports serve as interim compliance demonstrations; the March 2018 surface water report provides supplementary screening data. As of the most recent document (March 2018), no formal determination of compliance or non‑compliance with Title 22 MCLs is stated in the provided excerpts—the reports simply present the data and note any exceedances.

## Open Items

- The project record does not document a final determination by DTSC that all monitoring wells are below their respective Title 22 MCLs. Post‑2018 sampling results (not included in the provided sources) would be needed to confirm closure status.  
- The relationship between surface water MCL exceedances (if any) and groundwater compliance has not been resolved in the cited documents; potential groundwater‑to‑surface water migration remains an open question.
