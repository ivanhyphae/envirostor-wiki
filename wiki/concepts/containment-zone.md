## Overview

A **Containment Zone** at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County) is a designated and physically bounded area within which specific remedial or operational components—such as stockpiles, engineered structures, and monitoring features—are located. The term appears in the project’s operational monitoring documentation to delineate discrete spatial units subject to routine inspection for structural integrity, surface condition, and maintenance needs.

The site comprises two distinct Containment Zones: **Stockpile 1 Containment Zone** and **Stockpile 2 Containment Zone**, each containing a mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall system, highway pavement, median and shoulder areas, a north slope, and storm drain infrastructure. These zones are the subject of the *Caltrans State Route 132 Modesto Soil Stockpiles O&M Site Inspection Form* dated **3/18/25**, prepared by **Adam Inman**.

## Site Record

The containment zones are defined in the **Annual Stockpile Inspection Form** (source document: `raw-md/Annual Stockpile Inspection Form.md`). The inspection form lists the following components within each containment zone, with checkboxes for condition status (“OK?”) and notes for repairs/maintenance (“Yes □ No ☒”):

- **MSE Walls** – bulging areas, out of plane
- **Highway Pavement** – cracks, potholes
- **Median and Shoulders** – cracks, potholes
- **North Slope** – vegetation, rills, slumping
- **Storm Drains** – debris/obstructions, erosion around inlets/outlets
- **Other** – (open text field)

On the **3/18/25** inspection, each component for both Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2 Containment Zones was marked with a “No” check (i.e., condition not OK) for the “Surface Condition OK?” question, and a “No” check for “Repairs/Maintenance Required.” The sole narrative observation recorded was: *“No issues observed”* (under the “Other” field for Stockpile 1). No specific condition deficiencies or required corrective actions were noted.

The spatial extent, dimensions, or precise boundaries of the containment zones are not defined in the available source. No soil or groundwater analytical data were recorded on the inspection form; the form is exclusively a visual/physical inspection checklist.

Based solely on the inspection record, the containment zones appear to be in a routine monitoring phase with no reported compliance exceedances or enforcement actions.
