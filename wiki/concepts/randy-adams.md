# Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Remediation Case File (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA)

## Overview

The Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site comprises excess native soils and pond tailings generated during construction of a segment of State Route 99 north of Kansas Avenue in the early 1960s. The materials were excavated from a portion of a 4.3‑acre parcel formerly owned by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC), which operated an evaporation pond in the southernmost corner. The primary chemicals of concern (COCs) are **barium**, **lead**, and **strontium**. The site is managed under a regulatory framework led by DTSC’s Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program, with concurrence from the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB).

## Site Record

### Source Material and Origin
- The soil stockpiles consist of **excess native soils** and **pond tailings** generated when Caltrans constructed SR 99 north of Kansas Avenue. The excavation traversed a 4.3‑acre parcel purchased from FMC, a former chemical manufacturer that processed barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores. *(November 29, 2012 DTSC letter to Richard Stewart)*

### Groundwater Monitoring (2012)
- Monitoring wells **MW-9** and **MW-10** were installed in June 2012. MW-9 is located south of Kansas Avenue and west of SR 99; MW-10 is located south of Kansas Avenue and east of SR 99. *(June 2012 Groundwater Monitoring Report)*
- Groundwater monitoring reports for **May**, **June**, and **July 2012** were submitted by Geocon Consultants (November 2012) and reviewed by DTSC in consultation with RWQCB. *(November 29, 2012 DTSC letter)*
- DTSC **concurred** with all three reports and requested future monitoring as data becomes available. *(November 29, 2012 DTSC letter)*

### Feasibility Study (2014)
- A **Final Feasibility Study** was submitted by Caltrans’ contractor, Geocon Consultants, Inc., dated **June 24, 2014**, and addressed DTSC’s comments on the Draft Final Feasibility Study (December 18, 2013; DTSC comments February 11, 2014). *(June 30, 2014 DTSC letter)*
- DTSC **concurred** with the Final Feasibility Study. *(June 30, 2014 DTSC letter)*

## Regulatory Status

### Applicable Standards
- Primary COCs: **barium**, **lead**, and **strontium** (based on FMC’s historical processing of barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores). *(November 29, 2012 letter)*
- Groundwater monitoring is ongoing; no specific MCL exceedances or action levels are documented in the source materials beyond the identification of these COCs.
- The project is subject to DTSC oversight under the **Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program** and consultation with **RWQCB**.

### Compliance Status
- DTSC **concurred** with all 2012 groundwater monitoring reports and the 2014 Final Feasibility Study. No compliance violations or non-concurrence actions are documented in the provided sources.
- No further action or closure determination is documented in the available record.
