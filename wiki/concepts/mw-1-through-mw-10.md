## Overview

Monitoring well Mw 1 through Mw 10 are groundwater monitoring wells installed at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132) in Stanislaus County, California. These wells form the groundwater monitoring network used to assess water quality impacts from stockpiled soil materials. The wells are referenced in the September 2012 groundwater monitoring report submitted to regulatory agencies. Their primary function is to detect and quantify concentrations of contaminants of concern (including heavy metals and volatile organic compounds) in the shallow aquifer beneath the stockpile areas.

## Site Record

The September 2012 groundwater monitoring report (S9525-06-44A Modesto Stockpiles GW SEPT2012 1212.pdf) documents the installation and initial sampling results for Mw 1 through Mw 10. Specific well construction details (depths, screen intervals, and lithologic logs) are provided in the report. Groundwater samples were collected during the September 2012 monitoring event. Key analytes included total and dissolved metals (barium, arsenic, chromium, lead, etc.), volatile organic compounds (VOCs), and general water quality parameters (pH, conductivity, turbidity).

## Regulatory Status

The groundwater monitoring program is conducted under oversight from the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC). Applicable thresholds include:
- Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs) for drinking water (e.g., barium MCL = 1,000 µg/L; arsenic MCL = 10 µg/L)
- DTSC screening levels for soil and groundwater
- Background concentrations for site-specific parameters

The September 2012 report indicates monitoring is ongoing. Compliance status is determined relative to established action levels for each well and analyte.
