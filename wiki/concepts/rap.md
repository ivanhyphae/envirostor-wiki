## Remedial Action Plan (RAP)

### Overview

The Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA) is the regulatory decision document that selects and specifies the remedy for approximately 121,000 cubic yards of barium-impacted fill embankment located within Caltrans right-of-way (ROW) west and east of SR 99, south of Kansas Avenue. The RAP was prepared under an Interagency Agreement between Caltrans, the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), and the Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) to address human health and environmental risks posed by soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1–3) generated from former Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC Corporation) operations (1930s–1970s).

The selected remedy is a **containment alternative**, which involves incorporating the stockpiles as raised roadway fill embankments and bridge abutments during construction of the future SR 132 highway alignment, with remedial and contour cut/fill grading to achieve final finish grades and consolidate barium-impacted soil. The RAP was approved by DTSC prior to April 2018, with formal approval documented in an **March 14, 2018 approval letter** (DTSC, 2018).

### Site Record

The RAP was developed through the following documented milestones:

- **2003**: Hazardous waste initial site assessment (ISA) identified potential residual chemicals from former FMC Modesto impoundments in SR 99/SR 132 ROW stockpiles.
- **2004**: Preliminary site investigation (PSI) consisting of 50 borings; analytical results indicated elevated barium concentrations in Stockpiles 2 and 3. Cadmium impacts were also identified in Stockpiles 2 and 3.
- **2005**: GeoTrans January 2005 *Addendum to Comprehensive Remedial Investigations Report* characterized hydrogeology: first encountered groundwater at approximately 30 feet bgs under confined to semi-confined conditions, with flow toward the southeast at a gradient of 0.002 ft/ft.
- **2006**: Site investigation installed monitoring wells MW-1 through MW-8 adjacent to stockpiles; two groundwater sampling events conducted. Two distinct groundwater types identified beneath the site.
- **2007**: Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) concluded no COPCs posed potential health risks or hazards to current or future receptors under multiple exposure scenarios.
- **2012**: July 26, 2012 meeting between Caltrans, DTSC, and RWQCB identified data gaps requiring fenceline perimeter sampling, lateral stockpile limits definition, and additional cadmium sampling. Fenceline Workplan submitted September 2012.
- **2018 (March 14)**: DTSC issued approval letter for the RAP, based on the containment alternative.
- **2018 (July 18)**: Draft Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) submitted by Geocon Consultants, Inc.
- **2018 (August 20)**: DTSC conditional approval of draft RDIP pending administrative text revisions and review of 95% and 100% design plans.
- **2018 (October 12)**: DTSC conditional approval of revised RDIP incorporating recommended text revisions.
- **2019 (January 30)**: Final RDIP approved by DTSC on April 3, 2019.
- **2020 (February 6)**: Errata Transmittal to January 2019 RDIP updating roles and responsibilities post-construction contract award, including identification of Bay Cities Paving and Grading, Inc. as contractor and striking Geocon as Caltrans' agent.

Key contaminants of concern include **barium**, **cadmium** (elevated in Stockpiles 2 and 3), and polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs). The RAP specifies that barium-impacted soil will be consolidated and capped as part of roadway fill—no ex-situ treatment or off-site disposal is required under the containment alternative.

### Regulatory Status

- **Lead agencies**: DTSC (primary oversight), RWQCB (cooperative oversight), Caltrans (responsible party).
- **Applicable standards**: DTSC-approved containment alternative; HHRA concluded no unacceptable human health risks; groundwater MCLs (e.g., barium MCL 1,000 µg/L) were evaluated but no groundwater remedy was selected in the RAP.
- **Compliance**: The project was deemed approvable under conditions specified in DTSC's conditional approval letters (August 20, 2018; October 12, 2018). The RDIP was finalized and approved April 3, 2019, with post-contract award errata issued February 2020. Current compliance status is **conditional**—Caltrans is responsible for verifying construction conformance with RAP/RDIP requirements, including monitoring well abandonment, barium soil excavation/placement observation, verification sampling, clean soil import source sampling, air monitoring, and stormwater monitoring.

### Open Items

- **Constructed remedy verification**: The RDIP requires periodic observation and photodocumentation of capping activities, removal verification soil sampling, and stormwater monitoring—results and compliance status as of the latest source (February 2020) are not documented.
- **Long-term stewardship**: The RAP selects containment (in-place use as roadway fill), but no post-construction monitoring or land use restrictions (e.g., deed restrictions) are explicitly described in the available source documents.
- **Groundwater**: While groundwater was assessed (MW-1 through MW-8, two monitoring events), the RAP does not appear to require ongoing groundwater monitoring—this may need confirmation.
- **Cadmium**: The 2012 Fenceline Workplan identified elevated cadmium in Stockpiles 2 and 3 as a data gap requiring supplemental characterization—the extent of cadmium remediation or handling under the RAP is not fully detailed in available sources.
