## Overview

The **O M Plan** (Operation and Maintenance Plan) is a regulatory document governing the long-term stewardship of the clean cap cover over two barium-containing soil (BCS) stockpiles located beneath State Route 132 (SR 132) in Stanislaus County, California. The plan, drafted by Geocon Consultants Inc. on behalf of the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans), is scheduled for inclusion as Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report (RACR) for the **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles** site (Site Code: 900259). The cap cover consists of the SR 132 freeway pavement and associated roadway features constructed over the stockpiles during the Interim Phase 1 West Project.

The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) issued formal comments on the Draft O&M Plan on August 1, 2022, through Project Manager Dean Wright, P.G. The comments were developed in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB). Six specific revisions were requested prior to the plan’s finalization and incorporation into the RACR.

## Site Record

The Draft O&M Plan was submitted to DTSC in March 2022. It outlines inspection, maintenance, and repair activities for the portion of SR 132 used as the cap cover over two BCS stockpiles. The plan includes a blank O&M Site Inspection Form for field crews to document cap condition.

Key documented facts from DTSC’s August 1, 2022 comment letter (referenced as: raw-md/OM Plan Comments (900259).md):

- **Document title**: "Comments on the Draft Operation and Maintenance Plan, Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Interim Phase 1 State Route 132 West Project, Stanislaus County, California, Dated March, 2022"
- **Submitted by**: Geocon Consultants Inc., 3160 Gold Valley Drive, Suite 800, Rancho Cordova, California 95742
- **Project Manager**: John E. Juhrend, PE, CEG
- **Regulatory reviewer**: Dean Wright, P.G., DTSC Site Evaluation and Remediation Unit, Site Mitigation and Restoration Program

The DTSC’s requested revisions include:
1. **Subject line typo**: "Maintenance" misspelled
2. **Section 2.1 (Notification)**: Add a minimum 10 working days advance notice to DTSC before any BCS disturbance, with requirement for prior written authorization
3. **Section 2.4 (Cover thickness)**: Reference Health and Safety Code (HSC) 25123.3(b)(4)(A) requirements; note that minimum 6 millimeters thickness meets HSC requirements (though HSC uses mils, not millimeters); correct spelling of "millimeter"
4. **Section 3.1 (Inspection frequency)**: Add inspections after fires, substantial rainfall (≥1 inch in 24 hours), significant seismic events, and traffic accidents potentially damaging the cap – especially critical for the vegetated north slope (pending final freeway phase)
5. **Section 2.7 (Periodic patrols)**: Include monitoring for unauthorized access (e.g., homeless encampments) that could impact the cap, particularly on the north vegetated slope
6. **O&M Site Inspection Form**: Expand with specific subheadings per feature:
   - MSE Wall: Bulging areas, out of plane
   - Highway Pavement: Cracks, potholes
   - Median and Shoulders: Cracks, potholes
   - North Slope: Vegetation, Rills, Slumping
   - Storm Drains: Debris/obstructions, Erosion around inlets/outlets

## Regulatory Status

The Draft O&M Plan is not yet finalized. DTSC’s August 1, 2022 letter constitutes a conditional approval path – the revisions must be incorporated before the plan is accepted as Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report. The applicable regulatory standards referenced include:

- **Health and Safety Code Section 25123.3(b)(4)(A)** – governs minimum cover thickness requirements (stated in mils; DTSC notes the 6 mm provided will meet requirements)
- DTSC and RWQCB are the co-regulatory agencies providing oversight

The project’s compliance status is currently **pending** – final O&M Plan acceptance is contingent on addressing all six DTSC comments.

## Open Items

As of the August 1, 2022 DTSC letter, the following remain unresolved:

1. Notification timeframe for BCS disturbance (DTSC requires 10 working days; not yet incorporated)
2. Explicit reference to HSC 25123.3(b)(4)(A) and correction of thickness unit terminology (mm vs. mils)
3. Expansion of post-event inspection triggers (fires, ≥1 inch/24 hr rainfall, seismic events, traffic accidents)
4. Inclusion of unauthorized access monitoring in patrols
5. Enhanced detail on the O&M Site Inspection Form format

No subsequent correspondence or revised document is included in the provided source material to indicate whether these items have been addressed.
