## Definition

**No Additional Variance Required** is an engineering review conclusion indicating that the proposed change, detail, or revision does **not** introduce a meaningful increase in uncertainty, load variability, or design demand beyond what was already addressed in the governing analysis or prior approval. In practice, it means the reviewer determined that the existing design basis is sufficient and that no further adjustment to the assumed variance, safety margin, or contingency is necessary.

This phrase is commonly used in structural, geotechnical, and construction document review to document acceptance of a modified condition when the change remains within the scope of the original design assumptions.

## How it works

A determination of “no additional variance required” typically follows a technical comparison between:

- the **original design assumptions**,
- the **proposed revision or field condition**, and
- the **applicable acceptance criteria**.

The reviewer evaluates whether the change affects the factors that would normally require redesign or added conservatism, such as:

- factored loads or load combinations,
- soil bearing or settlement response,
- footing geometry,
- reinforcement demand,
- structural continuity,
- drainage or hydrostatic conditions,
- construction tolerances,
- or other sources of uncertainty relevant to the element.

If the revised condition remains sufficiently close to the original design envelope, the reviewer may conclude that the existing analysis already covers the condition and that no extra variance, adjustment, or exception is needed. In that case, the project can proceed without changing the structural basis of design.

In the source material, the phrase appears in the context of an e-mail acceptance associated with the Stockpile 2 Wall Footing Tech Memo, suggesting formal approval of a footing-related technical determination without requiring further design variance.

## Variants

Common variants and closely related forms include:

- **No additional variance**
- **No additional structural variance required**
- **No additional design variance required**
- **Within existing design assumptions**
- **Acceptable as-is**
- **No further revision required**
- **Approved without modification**

Related implementation contexts:

- **Structural acceptance memo**: reviewer documents that the submitted detail is acceptable without further design changes.
- **Field change acceptance**: a constructed or proposed condition is accepted because it does not exceed the design envelope.
- **Engineering equivalency determination**: an alternative detail is judged equivalent to the original for design purposes.
- **Exception/waiver review**: the reviewer decides that a requested exception does not materially affect performance.

## Trade-offs

### Advantages
- Preserves the original design intent without unnecessary redesign.
- Speeds review and approval.
- Reduces cost and schedule impacts from overcorrection.
- Provides a clear technical record of acceptance.

### Limitations
- The phrase depends on the quality of the underlying analysis; if assumptions are incomplete, the conclusion may be fragile.
- It can be interpreted too broadly if not paired with explicit criteria or calculations.
- It does not mean “no risk,” only that additional variance is not justified by the review.
- It may be specific to the exact condition reviewed and not transferable to similar conditions elsewhere.

### Practical considerations
- The acceptance should identify what was reviewed and what limits apply.
- If site conditions change, the conclusion may no longer hold.
- For regulated or safety-critical work, “no additional variance required” should be backed by documented calculations, code references, or professional judgment.
