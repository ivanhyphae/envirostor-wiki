## Overview

The **Barium Contaminated Soil Slope Zone (BCS Slope Zone)** is a defined area of barium-impacted soil on the southern slope of Stockpile 1 at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA). This zone required targeted excavation and characterization prior to construction of a mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall footing for the site’s remediation and capping system. The zone is spatially distinct from Stockpiles 1 and 2 and is a regulatory focus due to barium concentrations exceeding background levels and applicable screening thresholds.

## Site Record

- **Documentation**: The BCS Slope Zone is referenced in the “Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE Wall Sampling Plan,” transmitted via email on March 31, 2020, from Geocon’s John Juhrend to Caltrans’ Richard Stewart, and forwarded to DTSC (Dean Wright, Perry Myers) and Water Boards (Steven Meeks) for approval.
- **Location**: Southern slope zone of Stockpile 1, adjacent to the planned MSE wall footing. The footing excavation extends approximately 4 to 5 feet deep, with sampling at ten intervals spaced at ~200 feet.
- **Barium thresholds used in the sampling plan**:
  - **Maximum site-specific background**: 120 mg/kg (referenced as the threshold for clean capping material reuse)
  - **RDIP BCS removal threshold**: 1,000 mg/kg (groundwater protection basis)
  - **Construction worker ESL**: 3,000 mg/kg
  - **Residential ESL**: 15,000 mg/kg
  - **Commercial ESL**: 220,000 mg/kg
- **Lead thresholds** (also sampled, though not the primary BCS focus):
  - **Maximum site-specific background**: 3.8 mg/kg
  - **DTSC residential**: 80 mg/kg
- **Sampling protocol**: Discrete soil samples from one-foot intervals to the full depth of the footing excavation (4–5 ft). Analysis for barium and lead under expedited 48-hour turnaround time.
- **Work timeline**: Excavation of the BCS slope zone was to begin on or shortly before March 31, 2020. Air/dust perimeter monitoring was conducted during excavation by Geocon (Dave Watts, RDIP monitor).
- **Reuse criteria**: Analytical data from the footing excavation was to determine suitability for: (a) clean capping material, (b) abutment/embankment fill, or (c) placement in BCS containment zones, depending on barium concentrations relative to the above thresholds.

## Regulatory Status

- **Agencies**: DTSC (Dean Wright, Perry Myers) and Water Boards (Steven Meeks) are the reviewing regulators. DTSC approval was requested for the sampling plan to proceed.
- **Applicable standards**: The sampling plan integrates multiple regulatory screening levels—site-specific background (120 mg/kg barium), RDIP action level (1,000 mg/kg), and ESLs—but no single “MCL” for barium is cited in the plan. (Barium MCL under federal SDWA is 2,000 µg/L, but this is groundwater, not soil; the RDIP threshold appears to be the dominant soil action level for groundwater protection.)
- **Compliance status**: As of the March 31, 2020 email, the sampling plan was under review and pending approval. No further regulatory decisions are documented in the provided source.
