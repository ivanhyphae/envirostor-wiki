## Overview

**PL7** is a designated surface water monitoring location established for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project (Contract No. 06A2542, Task Order No. 97) in Stanislaus County, California. It was added to the surface water sampling network during a November 2, 2022 site walk with the Caltrans TO Manager and Stantec. PL7 monitors discharge from a stormwater pipe entering stormwater Basin 5. Its purpose is to assess whether contaminated runoff from the soil stockpiles along SR 132 is migrating offsite via surface water pathways.

## Site record

### Location and installation
- **Location:** North side of SR 132, at the discharge pipe along stormwater basin 5, northeast corner of SR 132 and North Carpenter Road. See figure 2 of the referenced letter.
- **Date designated:** November 2, 2022, during the site walk.
- **Document:** *Surface Water Report – February 28, 2023* (letter dated March 9, 2023, from Stantec to Mr. Richard Stewart, PG, Caltrans).

### February 28, 2023 inspection results
- **Weather conditions:** Rain event had occurred overnight; only minimal rain during inspection. Measured precipitation: 0.09 inches on February 28, 2023.
- **Observation at PL7:** Trash and debris were observed blocking the end of the discharge pipe. There was **no active runoff** from the stormwater pipe at the time of inspection.
- **Associated basin condition:** Soil erosion was observed on the east side of stormwater basin 5 (Photograph 4, Appendix A).
- **Overall project area:** No visible discharges or runoff of surface water were observed throughout the project area on this date.

### Photographic documentation
Appendix A provides a photographic record with the following entries relevant to PL7:
- **Photograph 3:** View looking southeast at the discharge area near PL7, showing the debris-blocked pipe with no active runoff.
- **Photograph 4:** View looking west at erosion on the east side of Basin 5.

No analytical sampling results for PL7 are reported in the source document—only field observations of flow status and physical conditions.

## Regulatory status

The applicable regulatory framework for surface water at this site is not detailed in the source letter regarding PL7. The document does not specify applicable water quality standards (e.g., Basin Plan objectives, MCLs, or numeric action levels) for surface water monitoring at PL7. No compliance determination is made in the available record.

The project is subject to California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) oversight and the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) jurisdiction under the soil stockpiles remediation program.

## Open items

- No analytical data have been collected from PL7 to date; only field observations of no flow and debris blockage have been documented.
- Sampling at PL7 is contingent on active runoff conditions during qualifying rain events.
- Soil erosion observed on the east side of Basin 5 near PL7 may require corrective action or additional erosion control measures.
- The debris blocking the discharge pipe at PL7 should be cleared to allow proper monitoring during future storm events.
