## Vanadium

### Overview

Vanadium is a dissolved metal reported in groundwater samples collected at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 99/132, Stanislaus County). It is a by‑product of historical chemical manufacturing at the adjacent FMC property and was introduced to the site when metal‑bearing soil from former evaporation ponds was excavated and stockpiled. In the July 2012 monitoring event, vanadium was detected in all ten monitoring wells, with one result exceeding the California Department of Public Health Notification Level for drinking water.

### Site record

During the July 16–17, 2012 groundwater sampling event (reported November 28, 2012 by Geocon Consultants, Inc. in *Groundwater Monitoring Report – July 2012*), dissolved vanadium was analyzed by EPA Method 6020 (ICP‑MS) at Advanced Technology Laboratories. The concentrations ranged from **14 µg/L** (MW‑9) to **51 µg/L** (MW‑6). All wells except MW‑6 were below the Notification Level; the MW‑6 value of **51 µg/L** exceeded the **50 µg/L** threshold. Other representative values included MW‑3 at 42 µg/L, MW‑2 at 25 µg/L, MW‑8 at 23 µg/L, MW‑1 at 20 µg/L, MW‑5 at 20 µg/L, MW‑4 at 18 µg/L, MW‑7 at 17 µg/L, MW‑10 at 16 µg/L, and MW‑9 at 14 µg/L.

The report states that with the exception of vanadium in MW‑6, no dissolved metal exceeded its respective water quality threshold. The vanadium concentrations were described as “similar to historical levels” (the report references earlier data from June and October 2006 where vanadium was reported at 22–26 µg/L in MW‑1 and MW‑2, though those PQLs may differ).

Field quality assurance included an equipment blank; vanadium was not detected in the blank, so the reported results are not qualified for field contamination. Laboratory quality control showed acceptable method blank and LCS recoveries for vanadium.

### Regulatory status

The applicable threshold is the **California Department of Public Health Notification Level for Drinking Water** of **50 µg/L**. Notification Levels are non‑regulatory, health‑based advisory levels; they are not enforceable Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs). No primary or secondary MCL exists for vanadium under California or federal drinking water standards. The project was reported as generally in compliance with all applicable thresholds except for the single exceedance at MW‑6.

### Open items

- The cause and significance of the vanadium exceedance at MW‑6 (51 µg/L) are not resolved in the July 2012 report.  
- The report recommends continued monitoring to assess whether this exceedance persists, and whether it reflects a localized influence from the stockpile material or a broader trend.  
- Subsequent groundwater monitoring events (not included in this source) would need to confirm whether vanadium levels remain above the Notification Level or decline.
