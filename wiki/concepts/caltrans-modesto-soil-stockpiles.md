## Overview
The Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles are excavated soils generated during highway construction and maintenance activities along State Route 132 (SR 132) in Stanislaus County, California. These stockpiles were staged at a Caltrans maintenance yard near Modesto for potential reuse. Subsequent sampling revealed concentrations of heavy metals and semi-volatile organic compounds exceeding residential screening levels, triggering regulatory oversight by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC). The stockpiles became a distinct project unit under the larger SR 132 corridor remediation effort.

## Site record

- **Lead**: 320 mg/kg (screening level 80 mg/kg)
- **Benzene**: 1.2 mg/kg (screening level 0.002 mg/kg)

Stockpile volume: approximately 15,000 cubic yards, staged in three piles (SP-1, SP-2, SP-3). No leaching data were provided; however, total concentrations exceed DTSC’s direct exposure screening levels for residential use. The stockpiles were not previously covered; one sample from SP-2 showed surface soil concentrations 3× higher than 12-inch depth samples, indicating potential recent contamination.
