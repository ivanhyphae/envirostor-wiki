## Overview

The California Primary Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) is the enforceable drinking‑water standard established by the State Water Resources Control Board, Division of Drinking Water. At the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County), these MCLs serve as the regulatory benchmarks for groundwater quality assessment. The December 2013 Groundwater Monitoring Report (document S9800‑01‑17) identifies the MCLs for each constituent of concern (COC) and compares detected site‑wide concentrations against those thresholds.

## Site record

The December 2013 monitoring report is the primary project document that applies California Primary MCLs to the Modesto Stockpiles groundwater data. The report presents analytical results from site monitoring wells (e.g., MW‑5, MW‑6, and others) and evaluates them against the corresponding MCL values. Specific contaminants tracked include barium, chromium, arsenic, and others typical of the stockpiled soil material. The report records whether any well exceeded its respective MCL for a given sampling event, and it provides the data necessary to assess spatial and temporal trends. All concentrations are reported in µg/L (for dissolved constituents) with the corresponding MCL footnoted from California regulations.

## Regulatory status

The California Primary MCL is the applicable limit for groundwater that is a potential drinking water source. The Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) oversees the site and uses MCL exceedances to trigger additional investigation or remedial action. The December 2013 report documents compliance status at that time; any wells found to exceed an MCL are flagged in the report’s exceedance summary. The site is subject to ongoing monitoring under DTSC oversight, and subsequent reports (not provided) would update compliance.

## Open items

The December 2013 report may identify specific monitoring wells with MCL exceedances that require follow‑up sampling or a Corrective Action Plan. Any unresolved exceedances or data gaps noted in the report are open items to be addressed in later monitoring events or submissions to DTSC.
