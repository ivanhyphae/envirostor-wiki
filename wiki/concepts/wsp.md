## Overview

WSP is the environmental consulting firm retained by Caltrans to prepare technical documentation and oversee the removal action for Stockpile 3 at the SR 132 BCS (Borrow Channel Site) in Stanislaus County, California. WSP authored the *SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo*, which serves as the primary project record for the characterization and off‑site disposal of approximately 5,700 cubic yards of chromium‑impacted soil from Stockpile 3. The memo documents the soil’s contamination profile, verification sampling results, and the final disposition of material to a permitted landfill.

## Site Record

### Background and Scope
Stockpile 3 was part of the larger SR 132 BCS remediation program addressing historical chromium ore processing residue (COPR) impacts. The stockpile contained soil that had been excavated during earlier phases of the project and temporarily staged. WSP was tasked with:
- Assessing chemical characteristics of the stockpiled material for off‑site disposal;
- Performing verification sampling after removal;
- Preparing the removal action technical memorandum for regulatory review.

### Contaminant Profile (Stockpile 3)
- **Total Chromium (Cr):** Concentrations ranged from **760 mg/kg to 2,100 mg/kg** (dry weight basis) across 12 characterization samples.
- **Hexavalent Chromium (Cr(VI)):** Detected at **4.8 mg/kg to 62 mg/kg** (the regulatory threshold for Cr(VI) in non‑residential soil is typically 10 mg/kg under California’s Title 22, though the memo does not explicitly state which threshold applies).
- **Barium:** Reported at **760 mg/kg**, a value exceeding the California Title 22 soluble threshold limit (STLC) for barium (1,000 µg/L) but below the total threshold limit concentration (TTLC) of 50,000 mg/kg.
- **Other metals (lead, arsenic, etc.):** Below California hazardous waste thresholds.
- **pH:** Ranged from **8.5 to 11.2**, consistent with COPR‑impacted material.

### Removal Action
- **Volume removed:** Approximately **5,700 cubic yards** (based on survey volume calculations).
- **Disposal facility:** The material was transported to **Kettleman Hills** (Kings County, CA), a Class I landfill permitted to accept hazardous waste.
- **Verification sampling:** After removal, **four (4) verification soil samples** were collected from the base of the former stockpile area. Results showed:
  - Total Cr: **45–210 mg/kg** (all below the site‑specific action level of 500 mg/kg).
  - Cr(VI): **<0.5 mg/kg** (non‑detect) in all four samples.
  - Barium: **22–105 mg/kg**.

### Regulatory Approvals
- The removal and disposal were conducted under **Caltrans’ oversight** and in coordination with **DTSC** (Department of Toxic Substances Control).
- The memo does not identify a specific DTSC order or consent agreement, but notes that the removal was “approved by DTSC as a component of the SR 132 BCS overall remediation strategy.”

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable thresholds:** California Title 22 hazardous waste criteria (TTLC and STLC), and site‑specific action levels for total Cr and Cr(VI) set by Caltrans/DTSC (total Cr action level: 500 mg/kg; Cr(VI) trigger: 10 mg/kg).
- **Compliance:** The stockpile material exceeded total Cr and Cr(VI) thresholds for non‑hazardous disposal and was appropriately classified as non‑RCRA hazardous waste (California hazardous waste code D‑007 for chromium). The verification results confirm that the base of the stockpile area now meets residential/industrial cleanup standards (all analytes below action levels).
- **Current status:** Removal is complete; the memo serves as the final record of action for Stockpile 3. No ongoing monitoring or institutional controls are noted.

## Open Items

- **DTSC formal approval letter:** The memo references verbal or email‑based approval; a formal written DTSC concurrence letter is not appended to the memo. Project staff should confirm that a signed approval letter exists in the larger case file.
- **Stockpile 3 area closure documentation:** The memo does not address whether the cleared pad will be used for future staging or re‑vegetation. If the area remains within the active SR 132 BCS site, a closure report or notice of completion may be required.
