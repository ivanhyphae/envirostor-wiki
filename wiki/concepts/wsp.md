## Overview

WSP was the environmental consulting firm retained by Caltrans to prepare technical documentation and oversee the removal action for Stockpile 3 at the SR 132 Borrow Channel Site (BCS) in Stanislaus County, California. WSP authored the *SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo* (the primary project record for this action) and is also referenced in correspondence titled *WB Acceptance E‑mail*, which documents formal acceptance of WSP’s deliverables by the reviewing agency (see Site Record below). WSP’s work encompassed characterizing approximately 5,700 cubic yards of chromium‑impacted soil, arranging off‑site disposal, and performing verification sampling to confirm cleanup standards.

### Contaminant Characterization (Stockpile 3)
All values below are from the Tech Memo unless otherwise attributed.
- **Total Chromium (Cr):** 760 mg/kg to 2,100 mg/kg (dry weight, n = 12 samples).  
- **Hexavalent Chromium (Cr(VI)):** 4.8 mg/kg to 62 mg/kg.  
- **Barium:** 760 mg/kg (above California Title 22 soluble threshold limit of 1,000 µg/L but below the total threshold limit concentration of 50,000 mg/kg).  
- **Other metals (lead, arsenic):** Below California hazardous waste thresholds.  
- **pH:** 8.5 – 11.2 (consistent with chromium ore processing residue (COPR)).

### Removal and Verification
- **Volume removed:** ~5,700 cu yd (survey‑based).  
- **Disposal facility:** Kettleman Hills (Class I, Kings County, CA).  
- **Verification sampling (n = 4) from base of former stockpile pad:**
  - Total Cr: 45–210 mg/kg (site‑specific action level: 500 mg/kg).  
  - Cr(VI): <0.5 mg/kg (non‑detect, action level 10 mg/kg).  
  - Barium: 22–105 mg/kg.

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable thresholds:** California Title 22 hazardous waste criteria (TTLC/STLC) and site‑specific action levels for total Cr (500 mg/kg) and Cr(VI) (10 mg/kg).  
- **Waste classification:** Stockpile 3 material was non‑RCRA hazardous waste (California D‑007 for chromium).  
- **Compliance:** Removal and verification results demonstrate the former stockpile area now meets cleanup standards. No ongoing monitoring or institutional controls are documented.  
- **Agency oversight:** Caltrans (lead) with DTSC concurrence per the Tech Memo. The WB Acceptance E‑mail may document DTSC’s formal sign‑off; this should be confirmed in the case file.
