## Overview
The Revised SWPPP Amendment is a regulatory document (dated October 22, 2020) that updates the Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project (SR 132, Stanislaus County). It was submitted in response to the failure of excavated soils from the Bent 2 column to meet clean cap criteria established in the April 9, 2020 Variance (approved by DTSC). The amendment addresses two key requirements: 1) placement of Bent 2 soils as a thin (≤6 inches) surrogate clean cap layer over Stockpile 2, provided it is covered with clean base material and roadway asphalt/concrete; and 2) implementation of rainy season surface water sampling and erosion control measures to prevent contaminant migration during winter exposure.

## Site Record
- **Document identification:** Revised SWPPP Amendment dated October 22, 2020 (cited in DTSC acceptance email of November 6, 2020).
- **Triggering event:** Technical Memorandum (Tech Memo) dated October 30, 2020, from Caltrans (Richard C. Stewart) to DTSC and Water Board, reporting analytical results from excavated Bent 2 soils. The results confirmed these soils “exceed the background criteria” for use as clean cap cover material per the April 9, 2020 Variance.
- **Regulatory acceptance:** Formal acceptance via email on November 6, 2020, from Dean Wright (DTSC Project Manager, PG). The email states: “This e-mail serves as DTSC’s official acceptance of the Tech Memo and Revised SWPPP Amendment.” Acceptance was coprincipal with Water Board (Steven Meeks).
- **Conditions of acceptance:** The amendment requires that Bent 2 soil be placed on Stockpile 2 at a thickness of **6 inches or less**, and be covered with clean base material and the asphalt/concrete roadway construction. This ensures “efficient use” of the soil while not “jeopardizing the integrity of the remedy.”
- **Temporal context:** The amendment acknowledges that placement will occur during winter months (November–March 2020–2021), making the soil **temporarily exposed** to rainfall. Therefore, strict adherence to the SWPPP’s erosion control and surface water sampling is mandated.
- **Specific performance requirements:** The amendment mandates the “rainy season surface water sampling effort” as part of the SWPPP. No specific monitoring locations (e.g., Stockpile 2 perimeter) or constituent lists are detailed in the source, but the intent is to demonstrate that “contaminants remain on site and do not impact the surrounding area.”
- **Volume/area:** Not specified in source. Only thickness (≤6 inches) and application location (over Stockpile 2) are documented.

## Regulatory Status
- **Applicable standard:** Clean cap criteria as defined in the April 9, 2020 Variance (approved by DTSC). The Bent 2 soils **do not meet** these criteria. However, the alternative placement (thin cap + roadway cover) is **deemed acceptable** by both DTSC and Water Board.
- **Enforcement agencies:** DTSC (Project Manager: Dean Wright); Water Board (Steven Meeks). Caltrans (Richard C. Stewart) is the responsible party.
- **Compliance:** The project is currently in compliance with the terms of the Revised SWPPP Amendment, provided conditions are met. The acceptance is conditional—the amendment must be “followed as strictly as possible.”
- **Expected next action:** The rainy season sampling results will determine whether further corrective measures (e.g., removal of Bent 2 soils, additional capping) are needed. No further action is documented based on existing sources.

## Open Items
- **Sampling results pending:** As of the acceptance date (November 6, 2020), rainy season surface water sampling has not been completed. The results are required to confirm that contaminants remain on-site. No data on this outcome is present in the source.
- **Erosion control effectiveness:** The amendment requires monitoring of erosion control measures; their performance during winter storms is not yet assessed.
- **Long-term liability:** The acceptance allows short-term exposure but does not address long-term fate of the soil (e.g., whether it will remain after roadway construction or be removed). The source does not specify a timeline for removal or permanent encapsulation.
