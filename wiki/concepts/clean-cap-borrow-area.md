## Definition

A **Clean Cap Borrow Area** is a designated source location from which uncontaminated soil, sand, gravel, or other earthen material is excavated for use as a clean cover (cap) over contaminated areas—such as landfills, brownfields, or remediation sites—to isolate contaminants, prevent exposure, and support site closure. The material must meet stringent chemical and physical quality standards to ensure it does not introduce new contamination or compromise the cap’s integrity.

## How it works

Clean cap borrow areas function within the framework of environmental remediation cover systems. The process typically follows these steps:

1. **Screening and characterization:** Potential borrow areas are identified based on geology, land use history, and accessibility. A sampling plan (e.g., *Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan*) is executed to test for contaminants of concern (heavy metals, VOCs, SVOCs, pesticides, etc.) and physical properties (grain size, compaction, permeability). Results must confirm compliance with applicable cleanup standards (e.g., residential or commercial use criteria).

2. **Approval:** Regulatory agencies review sampling data and site conditions to approve the borrow area. The area may be delineated into zones with varying quality levels.

3. **Excavation and stockpiling:** Approved material is mechanically removed, sometimes stockpiled for quality control and moisture conditioning.

4. **Transport and placement:** Clean fill is hauled to the cap construction site and placed in lifts (typically 0.5–1 ft thick) over the contaminated area, then compacted to meet density specifications. A final cover may include a barrier layer (e.g., geosynthetic clay liner), drainage layer, and vegetative soil layer.

5. **Documentation:** Borrow area usage is tracked in a technical memorandum (e.g., *SR 132 Clean Borrow Area Technical Memo*) and supporting appendices (e.g., *Draft Interim RACR, Appendices D–G*) to demonstrate source quality and volume.

The concept is central to “cut-and-cover” remediation where borrow material constitutes the primary cover medium.

## Variants

- **On-site borrow areas:** Material sourced from the same property as the cap (e.g., clean soil excavated during site grading). Reduces transportation but requires careful segregation from contaminated zones.
- **Off-site commercial borrow pits:** Licensed pits with pre‑characterized material, often used for large projects. May be subject to depletion and price fluctuations.
- **Recycled/alternative fill:** Use of construction demolition debris, dredged material, or treated industrial by‑products that meet clean fill criteria. Requires additional processing and testing.
- **Dedicated borrow area for cap:** A borrow area specifically permitted for a particular remediation project, with sampling protocols tailored to cap performance requirements (higher compaction, low organic content).

## Trade-offs

| Consideration | Advantages | Challenges |
|---------------|------------|------------|
| **Material quality** | Guarantees no new contamination if properly characterized. Requires thorough sampling (cost and time). | Risk of undiscovered contamination; regulatory rejection after sampling; limited supply of high‑quality material near site. |
| **Transportation** | Local borrow reduces haul distance and emissions. | Long haul distances increase cost, carbon footprint, and traffic impacts. |
| **Volume and availability** | Large borrow areas ensure sufficient material for cap construction. | Over‑excavation may cause environmental damage (habitat loss, groundwater disruption). Permitting can be delayed by public opposition. |
| **Cost** | On‑site material is essentially free (only handling cost). | Off‑site purchase and hauling may dominate project expense ($10–$30 per cubic yard delivered). |
| **Long‑term performance** | Clean cap maintains integrity, reduces post‑closure maintenance. | Inadequate compaction or improper placement can lead to settlement or erosion, requiring future repairs. |
| **Regulatory compliance** | Well‑documented borrow area supports smooth site closure. | Multiple layers of approvals (local, state, federal) can lengthen project timeline. |
