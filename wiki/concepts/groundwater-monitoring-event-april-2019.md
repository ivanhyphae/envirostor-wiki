## Definition

**Groundwater Monitoring Event April 2019** refers to a specific groundwater sampling and analytical event conducted in April 2019 for the Modesto Stockpiles site. The event is documented in *S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles GW April 2019_06.19.pdf* and forms part of the site's environmental monitoring record.

In this context, a “groundwater monitoring event” is a scheduled field and laboratory assessment of groundwater conditions at one or more monitoring wells. The purpose is typically to evaluate whether site activities have affected groundwater quality and to compare measured concentrations against historical trends and regulatory criteria.

## How it works

A groundwater monitoring event generally follows a standard workflow:

1. **Field preparation**
   - Monitoring locations are identified in advance.
   - Sampling equipment, bottles, preservatives, and chain-of-custody forms are prepared.
   - Wells may be checked for accessibility, integrity, and reference water levels.

2. **Well measurement and purging**
   - Static groundwater levels are measured.
   - Wells are often purged before sampling to remove stagnant water from the casing.
   - Purging may be done using low-flow methods or conventional displacement methods, depending on site protocol.

3. **Sample collection**
   - Groundwater is collected from each designated monitoring well.
   - Samples are placed into analytical containers appropriate for the target analytes.
   - Field parameters such as pH, temperature, conductivity, dissolved oxygen, turbidity, and oxidation-reduction potential may be recorded if required.

4. **Sample handling and laboratory analysis**
   - Samples are preserved, stored on ice, and shipped under chain of custody.
   - An accredited laboratory analyzes the samples for site-specific constituents of concern.
   - Results are reported with detection limits, qualifiers, and QA/QC information.

5. **Data evaluation**
   - Analytical results are compared with prior monitoring rounds to assess trends.
   - Exceedances, non-detects, and anomalies are reviewed in context.
   - Findings are typically summarized in a monitoring report.

For the April 2019 event, the PDF serves as the documentation package for this monitoring round, likely including sampling notes, analytical tables, and interpretation of groundwater conditions at the Modesto Stockpiles location.

## Variants

Groundwater monitoring events can vary by purpose, design, and analytical scope:

- **Routine compliance monitoring**  
  Scheduled sampling conducted to satisfy permit, remedial, or regulatory requirements.

- **Investigation monitoring**  
  Targeted sampling used to define the extent of contamination or to follow up on a release.

- **Low-flow sampling**  
  A sampling method that minimizes disturbance to the well and aquifer.

- **Event-based monitoring**  
  Sampling tied to a specific date or condition, such as seasonal monitoring, post-remediation verification, or quarterly/annual reporting.

- **Site-specific analyte panels**  
  Different monitoring rounds may test for metals, volatile organic compounds, nutrients, hydrocarbons, or other constituents depending on site history.

- **Alternative groundwater assessments**  
  In some cases, monitoring may be supplemented or replaced by direct-push sampling, grab sampling, or multi-depth profiling.

## Trade-offs

Key considerations for a groundwater monitoring event include:

- **Representativeness vs. practicality**  
  The sampled water must accurately reflect aquifer conditions, but field constraints can limit ideal sampling methods.

- **Temporal variability**  
  Groundwater quality can change with season, recharge, pumping, or nearby land use, so a single event is only a snapshot.

- **Well condition effects**  
  Damaged or poorly developed wells can bias results or make sampling difficult.

- **Detection limits and qualifiers**  
  Laboratory reporting limits may affect the ability to identify low-level contamination.

- **Cost and logistics**  
  Each monitoring event requires field labor, analytical costs, shipping, and reporting time.

- **Regulatory interpretation**  
  Results must be evaluated in light of site-specific standards, background conditions, and historical data rather than as isolated values.

For the April 2019 Modesto Stockpiles event, the main value lies in providing a dated record of groundwater conditions that can be compared with prior and subsequent monitoring rounds.
