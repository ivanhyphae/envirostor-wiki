## Definition

The **Modesto Soil Stockpiles Stormwater Sampling Plan Addendum** is a site-specific addendum to an existing stormwater sampling plan (SAP) for soil stockpile operations associated with the Modesto project. Its purpose is to document additional or revised stormwater monitoring requirements for stockpiled soil areas, typically in response to permitting, construction sequencing, or changes in site conditions.

In practice, such an addendum clarifies how stormwater runoff from soil stockpile areas will be sampled, inspected, and documented so that the project remains aligned with applicable stormwater compliance obligations.

## How it works

A stormwater sampling plan addendum usually supplements the base SAP rather than replacing it. It provides focused instructions for the specific condition of soil stockpiles, which can create elevated risk of sediment transport and pollutant discharge during rain events.

Typical functions include:

- identifying the stockpile area(s) covered by the addendum
- defining when sampling or inspections must occur, especially before, during, or after qualifying storm events
- specifying runoff collection points, if any are designated for sampling
- describing field procedures for visual observation, grab sampling, or event-based sampling
- listing parameters or indicators to evaluate, such as turbidity, suspended sediment, or visible contamination concerns
- documenting responsibilities for field staff, laboratory handling, recordkeeping, and reporting
- tying the sampling program back to the project’s best management practices (BMPs), such as cover, berming, diversion controls, or stabilization

The addendum is operationally important because soil stockpiles can change quickly as material is added, redistributed, or removed. The plan therefore functions as a control document that adapts the SAP to current field conditions and helps demonstrate due diligence during stormwater inspection and compliance review.

## Variants

Common variants of this kind of addendum include:

- **Stockpile-specific SAP addendum**: Focused solely on soil stockpiles and their runoff controls
- **Construction-phase SAP addendum**: Applies during active grading or earthwork phases
- **Permit-driven addendum**: Prepared to satisfy a permit condition or regulatory request
- **Temporary-condition addendum**: Used when stockpiles are present only for a limited period
- **Alternative monitoring approach**: May rely more heavily on visual inspections and BMP verification when runoff sampling points are not practical

Related implementation differences may depend on:

- site drainage layout
- stockpile size and location
- whether stockpiles are covered or stabilized
- whether runoff is discharged to a ditch, basin, or other receiving feature
- local permitting requirements and sampling triggers

## Trade-offs

Key trade-offs and limitations include:

- **Specificity vs. flexibility**: More detailed instructions improve consistency, but may be less adaptable when stockpile locations or site conditions change
- **Sampling practicality**: Stormwater from stockpiles may be intermittent or difficult to capture, making event-based sampling logistically challenging
- **Cost vs. assurance**: More frequent monitoring improves documentation and response capability but increases labor and analytical costs
- **Representativeness**: Samples taken from a limited number of events or locations may not fully characterize all runoff conditions
- **Operational burden**: The addendum adds compliance steps for field crews, project managers, and environmental staff
- **Dependency on BMPs**: Effectiveness depends heavily on maintaining controls such as covers, berms, stabilization, and sediment containment

Because it is an addendum, it also depends on the accuracy and continued relevance of the underlying SAP. If field conditions change substantially, the addendum may need revision to remain effective.
