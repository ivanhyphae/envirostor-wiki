## Mw-8

## Overview
Mw-8 (also referred to as MW8) is a groundwater monitoring well at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. It was installed as part of the site’s groundwater monitoring network to assess impacts from historical soil stockpile operations related to the early 1960s construction of SR 99, which traversed a portion of a parcel formerly owned by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC). The primary chemicals of concern at the site are barium, lead, and strontium. Mw-8 was destroyed on April 30, 2019, under Geocon Consultants supervision, as part of the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) – Interim Phase 1 well abandonment program.

## Site record
### Destruction
Mw-8 was destroyed on April 30, 2019, by Gregg (contractor) under the supervision of Geocon staff member Lauren Short, with Caltrans personnel onsite. The destruction is documented in the *Well Destruction Report* (Geocon Project No. S1200-01-01, dated June 2019). The well was located within the State Right-of-Way boundary along SR 132, per the Site Plan (Figure 2 of the RDIP – Interim Phase 1, January 2019; also shown on Figure 2 of the Well Destruction Report). The destruction was permitted by Stanislaus County Department of Environmental Resources and the City of Modesto (Permit No. ENC2019-50174, dated April 26, 2019).

### Prior monitoring status
In the RDIP – Interim Phase 1 Site Plan (January 2019), Mw-8 was designated as a monitoring well to be “protect[ed] in-place” as part of long-term monitoring. However, by the time of the Well Destruction Report in June 2019, the well was re-designated for abandonment. The Well Destruction Report notes that Mw-8 was a former “monitoring” well and was destroyed on the same date as MW3 and MW7.

### Analytical data
The source documents provided do not contain specific concentration data, sampling dates, or exceedances for Mw-8. The routine groundwater monitoring reports for May, June, and July 2012 (reviewed and concurred with by DTSC on November 29, 2012) reference the monitoring network for barium, lead, and strontium but do not include Mw-8 by name. No analytical results from Mw-8 are available in the provided source materials.

## Regulatory status
### Applicable standards
The primary chemicals of concern (barium, lead, strontium) are subject to federal and state maximum contaminant levels (MCLs) and screening levels. However, no specific MCLs, action levels, or background thresholds were applied to Mw-8 in the documents provided.

### Regulatory concurrence
DTSC, in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region (CVRWQCB), concurred with the 2012 groundwater monitoring reports via letter dated November 29, 2012 (signed by Randy S. Adams, Senior Engineering Geologist). The concurrence applies to the monitoring network as a whole; no specific compliance determination for Mw-8 is documented.
