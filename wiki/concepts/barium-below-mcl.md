## Overview

Barium Below MCL refers to the documented determination that **barium** in groundwater at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (State Route 132, Stanislaus County) has remained **below the California Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) of 1,000 µg/L** throughout the monitoring period, and that the encapsulated soil stockpiles are not impacting groundwater. The term originates from project reports and regulatory correspondence showing that barium—present in the stockpile soils since the 1960s—has not migrated to groundwater at concentrations exceeding the MCL. This finding is central to the regulatory decision to discontinue groundwater monitoring and decommission monitoring wells.

## Site record

### Source of barium contamination

Soil contaminated with barium has been present at the site since the 1960s, stored in three stockpiles. The *DTSC Letter Response - 2025 GW Monitoring Report* states: “The Site consisted of three stockpiles that contain soil contaminated with barium since the 1960s.”

### Groundwater monitoring (2012–2014)

Ten groundwater monitoring wells were installed in 2012 to monitor for potential contamination migrating to groundwater while the encapsulation plan was being prepared. The *July 2012 GW Report* (7453_S9525-06-44) documents the initial sampling. The *Dec 2013 GW Mon Report* (S9800-01-17) provides subsequent data. The *Approval of GW Statistical Evaluation* (May 3, 2024) references a 2014 evaluation that showed the “consistency with the earlier 2014 evaluation” supporting no impact.

### Regulatory determination (2024)

The *Approval of the Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data* (May 3, 2024) from DTSC, in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), concluded:

- “DTSC and the RWQCB agree that the data indicates the encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater and that it will not impact groundwater in the future.”
- “the consistency with the earlier 2014 evaluation and the recent encapsulation of the soil beneath the Express Way support this conclusion.”

Based on this, DTSC and RWQCB concurred with the recommendation to **discontinue groundwater monitoring** and **decommission the four remaining monitoring wells** (MW4, MW6, MW9, and MW10).

### Well decommissioning (2025–2026)

Six of the ten wells were decommissioned in 2019. The *Well Destruction Report* and *Storm Water Sampling Report* (dated June 18, 2025) document the destruction of the remaining four wells. The *DTSC Letter Response - 2025 GW Monitoring Report* (January 30, 2026) conditionally approved the Well Destruction Report, requiring a table of estimated vs. actual grout volumes. The Storm Water Sampling Report was approved as submitted, with results “all within reporting limits.”

### Stormwater monitoring (2016–2025)

Surface water runoff sampling has been conducted to ensure metals are not displaced into surface water. The *Oct 2016 SW Sampling* and *February 2025 Stormwater Sampling Report* (dated June 25, 2025) both reported no barium exceedances. The 2025 report results were “all within reporting limits.”

### BCS Removal at Stockpiles 2 and 3

- **Stockpile 2**: The *Stockpile 2 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval* (March 10, 2020) documents that removal of barium-containing soil from the east and west ends of Stockpile 2 and subsequent confirmation sampling achieved cleanup levels established in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP). DTSC agreed these areas were suitable for clean embankment fill.
- **Stockpile 3**: The *SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo* describes biological/chemical stabilization removal activities. During 2020, as part of Phase I of the Remedial Action Plan, soil from Stockpile 3 was moved to the structural section of Stockpiles 1 and 2.

### Encapsulation beneath SR 132 Express Way

The *DTSC Letter Response - 2025 GW Monitoring Report* notes that remaining stockpiles “will be completed with the implementation of Phase II of the Remedial Action Plan to be utilized as a 4-lane highway, State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway.”

### Barium concentrations in soil vs. groundwater

The *Approval of GW Statistical Evaluation* references “barium and lead impacted soil stockpiles.” The barium MCL (1,000 µg/L) applies to groundwater. No specific barium groundwater concentrations (µg/L values) are provided in the 2024 or 2026 letters beyond the general finding of no impact. The 2012 GW report is the primary source for concentration data (80–720 µg/L, with MW-5 at 720 µg/L).

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable standard**: California MCL for barium in drinking water: **1,000 µg/L** (Title 22 California Code of Regulations).
- **Agencies**: Primary oversight by **DTSC** (Site Mitigation and Restoration Program) with consultation by **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB)**.
- **Compliance**: As of the most recent determinations (2024 statistical evaluation; 2025/2026 well destruction and stormwater approval), **barium in groundwater and stormwater is in full compliance with the MCL**. Groundwater monitoring has been discontinued based on DTSC and RWQCB concurrence.
- **Remaining action**: The Well Destruction Report requires finalization with a grout volume table (due within 30 days of January 30, 2026). The Storm Water Sampling Report is approved as submitted.

## Open items

- **Final Well Destruction Report**: The *DTSC Letter Response - 2025 GW Monitoring Report* (January 30, 2026) conditionally approves the report pending inclusion of a table showing estimated vs. actual grout volumes for each decommissioned well. A digital copy of the revised report is due within 30 calendar days.
- **Phase II Remedial Action**: The *DTSC Letter Response* notes that Phase II of the Remedial Action Plan will complete the encapsulation of the remaining two stockpiles for use as the SR 132 West Freeway/Expressway. No timeline or specific requirements are documented in the provided sources.
- **No open issues for barium in groundwater**: The 2024 statistical evaluation closed the barium groundwater concern. No further groundwater monitoring is required.
