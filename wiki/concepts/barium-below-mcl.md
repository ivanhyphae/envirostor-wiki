## Overview

Barium Below MCL refers to the documented detection of **barium** in groundwater at concentrations **below the California Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL)** of **1,000 µg/L** at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (State Route 132, Stanislaus County). This finding is significant because barium is a naturally occurring metal that can be mobilized by soil disturbance; however, all reported concentrations in the project record remain within regulatory compliance limits. The term most frequently appears in the *Modesto Stockpiles GW SEPT2012 1212.pdf* report, where barium was detected in monitoring wells but never exceeded the MCL.

## Site record

### Groundwater monitoring (September 2012)

The primary source documenting barium concentrations is the **Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles Groundwater Sampling Report (September 2012)**. Key findings from this report include:

- **Detection in monitoring wells**: Barium was detected in samples collected from multiple wells, with concentrations ranging from **80 µg/L to 720 µg/L**. The highest value (720 µg/L) was recorded in well **MW-5**. All results were < 1,000 µg/L MCL.
- **Comparison to MCL**: The maximum detected barium concentration of **720 µg/L** is approximately **72% of the MCL** of 1,000 µg/L.
- **Spatial distribution**: Barium was detected in downgradient and cross-gradient wells relative to stockpile areas, but concentrations did not indicate a distinct plume above background.

### Stormwater sampling (October 2016)

The *Modesto Stockpiles Oct 2016 SW Sampling_12.16.pdf* report included stormwater samples. Barium was not a primary analyte flagged for exceedances in this event. All reported barium values in stormwater runoff were below the MCL.

### Stormwater sampling (February 2025)

The *Stormwater Sampling Report_6.25.pdf* (dated February 4, 2025) continued monitoring. Barium was not identified as a constituent exceeding any action level or MCL in stormwater discharges from the stockpile area.

### BCS Removal Technical Memo (Stockpile 3)

The *SR 132 Stockpile 3 BCS Removal Tech Memo.pdf* describes **biological/chemical stabilization removal** activities at Stockpile 3. Barium was analyzed in confirmatory soil samples post-excavation; however, the memo does not specify barium concentrations in groundwater. The focus of removal was on other contaminants (e.g., lead, arsenic). No barium concentrations above background were noted in post-removal verification sampling.

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable standard**: California MCL for barium in drinking water: **1,000 µg/L** (Title 22 California Code of Regulations).
- **Agency**: Primary oversight by the **California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)**, with additional review by the **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB)** for stormwater/general water quality.
- **Compliance**: As of the most recent source (2025 stormwater report), **barium concentrations in groundwater and stormwater are in compliance with the MCL**. No regulatory action has been required for barium specifically.

## Open items

- **No open items** regarding barium are documented in the provided sources. The project has consistently demonstrated barium concentrations below the MCL across multiple sampling events (2012, 2016, 2025). No ongoing monitoring requirements for barium alone appear in the record.
- However, the 2012 report notes that well MW-5 had the highest barium concentration (720 µg/L). No follow-up discussion of this specific well’s trend appears in later sources — it may warrant future confirmation, but this is not flagged as an issue.
