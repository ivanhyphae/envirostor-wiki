## Overview

**MW-4** is a groundwater monitoring well installed on May 8, 2006 as part of the original eight-well network (MW‑1 through MW‑8) at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site along State Route 99/132 in Stanislaus County, California. The well is constructed with 2‑inch diameter SCH 40 PVC casing to a completed depth of 40.00 feet below ground surface, with a screened interval from 30 to 40 feet (0.010‑inch slots). The top of casing elevation is 82.47 feet above mean sea level. MW‑4 is one of four wells that remain active following the decommissioning of MW‑1, MW‑2, MW‑3, MW‑5, MW‑7, and MW‑8 in 2019 to accommodate highway construction. The current monitoring network comprises MW‑4, MW‑6, MW‑9, and MW‑10.

## Site Record

**Installation and Construction** – MW‑4 was installed on May 8, 2006. The borehole was 8 inches in diameter, drilled to 42 feet total depth. The filter pack (No. 2/12 sand) was placed from 26 to 42 feet. Construction details are documented in Table 1 of the March 8, 2023 Groundwater Sampling Report (Contract No. 06A2542, Task Order No. 97).

**Groundwater Monitoring History** – The well was sampled for Title 22 metals (dissolved) and general minerals from initial installation through at least 2019. Available analytical data for MW‑4 is limited in the source documents; however, the well’s location near Stockpile 2 (south of Kansas Avenue, between North Emerald Avenue and SR 99) positions it to monitor potential downgradient impacts from the stockpiled materials.

**Recent Conditions (2022–2023)** – During the November 2, 2022 gauging event, MW‑4 was found dry with no residual water. Total depth measured 39.95 feet BTOC. On February 21, 2023, Stantec again observed no groundwater in MW‑4. The well box showed no signs of damage or tampering. These findings are recorded in the groundwater gauging field sheets appended to the March 2023 report.

**Historic Performance** – Prior to the 2019 well decommissioning program, MW‑4 consistently yielded measurable groundwater. The well construction details and historical water level data (reproduced from Shaw Environmental, Inc., May 14, 2007 Site Investigation Report) show the well penetrated the shallow aquifer beneath the site. The apparent drying of MW‑4 coincides with encapsulation of Stockpiles 1 and 2 beneath SR 132 in October 2021 and may reflect reduced infiltration or regional groundwater decline.

**Contaminants of Concern** – The primary chemicals of concern at the site are barium, lead, and strontium, derived from historic processing of barium sulfate and strontium sulfate ores by Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC) and its predecessors. The applicable MCL for barium is 1,000 µg/L. MW‑4 was included in the analytical program for Title 22 metals; however, the source documents do not include tabulated results specific to MW‑4 for the post‑2019 period.

## Regulatory Status

Oversight of the site is provided by the **California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)** in consultation with the **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)** . DTSC concurred with the May, June, and July 2012 groundwater monitoring reports in a letter dated November 29, 2012 (Randy S. Adams to Richard Stewart). The applicable groundwater standard for barium is the California MCL of 1,000 µg/L. For the period covered by available sources, MW‑4 has not produced sampleable water since at least November 2022, rendering compliance status indeterminate. Historic analytical results for MW‑4 are not reproduced in the source excerpts.

## Open Items

- No groundwater samples have been collected from MW‑4 since it went dry in late 2022. The annual sampling requirement cannot be fulfilled until the well recharges. The next scheduled gauging event (semi‑annual) and sampling event (annual) should document whether water has returned.
- The source documents do not contain a final approved work plan or DTSC/CVRWQCB determination regarding the monitoring frequency or potential well abandonment if MW‑4 remains dry.
