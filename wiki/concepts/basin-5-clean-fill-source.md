## Definition

**Basin 5 Clean Fill Source** refers to the approved supply source, sampling plan, and supporting technical documentation used to qualify excavated material intended for use as **clean fill** at or for the Basin 5/SR 132 project area. In practice, it is the designated material source that has been evaluated to confirm it meets the project’s clean-fill acceptance criteria and can therefore be used for placement without introducing unacceptable contamination.

The concept combines two related elements:

1. A **technical memorandum** documenting why the source is acceptable, and  
2. A **sampling plan** describing how the material was characterized and verified.

## How it works

The Basin 5 clean fill source process follows a standard environmental due-diligence workflow for soil reuse:

1. **Source identification**  
   A candidate excavation or borrow source is identified for use as fill material at Basin 5.

2. **Sampling and characterization**  
   The material is sampled according to an approved plan. Sampling is designed to represent the source material adequately and to support decisions about whether the material qualifies as clean fill.

3. **Laboratory analysis**  
   Samples are analyzed for the contaminants and properties specified in the sampling plan and technical memo. The exact analyte list is governed by project requirements and applicable regulatory criteria.

4. **Comparison to acceptance criteria**  
   Analytical results are compared against the project’s clean-fill standards. These criteria are intended to ensure the material is suitable for reuse and will not adversely affect the receiving site.

5. **Technical review and approval**  
   Results are compiled into a technical memo, which is reviewed and approved by the responsible reviewer or agency contact. The approval indicates the source may be used as clean fill under the terms of the project documentation.

6. **Use in construction or grading**  
   Once approved, the source material can be imported or reused for Basin 5 earthwork activities, subject to any handling, segregation, or documentation requirements specified in the plan.

In short, the source is not merely a pile of dirt; it is a **qualified fill source** whose acceptability has been established through a documented sampling and approval process.

## Variants

Common variants of a basin clean-fill source process include:

- **Revised technical memo version**  
  Updated findings, clarifications, or revised approval language may be issued after the initial memo.

- **Sampling plan-only approval**  
  In some workflows, the sampling plan is approved first, and the source is later approved after laboratory results are reviewed.

- **Multiple source locations**  
  A project may evaluate more than one excavation area or stockpile as candidate clean-fill sources.

- **Different clean-fill classes**  
  Depending on jurisdiction or project standards, “clean fill” may mean unrestricted reuse, limited reuse, or reuse subject to specific screening thresholds.

- **Project-specific acceptance criteria**  
  Basin 5 may have its own criteria that differ from broader agency definitions, especially if the receiving area has sensitive environmental constraints.

## Trade-offs

Key considerations and limitations include:

- **Representativeness of sampling**  
  A source can only be approved if the samples adequately represent the material. Heterogeneous fill sources may require more sampling density.

- **Changes over time**  
  Conditions at the source can change after approval if additional excavation exposes different material layers. Approval may not automatically extend to later-discovered material.

- **Handling and segregation**  
  Approved material can become unsuitable if mixed with debris, native contaminated soil, or construction waste during transport or stockpiling.

- **Documentation burden**  
  Clean-fill approval depends on maintaining the sampling plan, laboratory reports, chain-of-custody records, and memo approval together as a traceable record.

- **Regulatory dependence**  
  The meaning of “clean fill” is project- and regulator-specific. Material accepted under one project may not qualify under another.

- **Conservative thresholds**  
  Acceptance criteria are often intentionally strict to minimize environmental risk, which can reduce the amount of material that qualifies for reuse.
