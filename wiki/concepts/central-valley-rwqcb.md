# Central Valley RWQCB (Regional Water Quality Control Board)

## Overview
The Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (Central Valley RWQCB, also referred to as CVRWQCB, RWQCB, or Water Board) is the state regulatory agency with primary authority over surface water and groundwater quality at the [[caltrans-modesto-soil-stockpiles]] remediation project on State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. The RWQCB operates under the California Water Code and the Porter-Cologne Water Quality Control Act, administering the Basin Plan for the Sacramento and San Joaquin River basins. At this site, the RWQCB acts in a consultative and parallel regulatory role with the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), which retains lead oversight for soil cleanup under the Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program.

## Site record

### Regulatory coordination
The RWQCB has been documented as a reviewer and concurring agency in multiple project milestones:

- **September 20, 2012** – DTSC letter to Caltrans approving the *Final Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan* (Geocon, September 2012). The letter states DTSC reviewed the workplan "in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region (RWQCB)." (source: *09.20.2012 Final Caltrans Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan 2.md*)

- **June 27, 2012** – DTSC approval of the *Groundwater Monitoring Report, March 2012*. The letter notes the RWQCB collected "split" water samples during the March 12, 2012 sampling event, confirming RWQCB field participation. (source: *GND Water Monitoring Rpt March 2102.md*)

- **November 29, 2012** – DTSC concurrence with three groundwater monitoring reports (May, June, July 2012). The approval letter includes RWQCB as a consulting agency. (source: *11 29 2012 Caltrans GND Water Monitoring Rpts (2).md*)

- **April 4, 2013** – DTSC approval of the *Revised Supplemental Site Investigation and Human Health Risk Assessment Update*. The letter states "DTSC in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Regional (RWQCB)" reviewed the reports. (source: *04 04 2013 Final SSI and HHRA 8.md*)

### Groundwater monitoring oversight
The RWQCB has overseen a network of 10 monitoring wells (MW-1 through MW-10) installed between 2006 and 2012. Key RWQCB personnel documented in project records:

- **Steven Meeks, P.E.** – Senior Water Resources Control Engineer, Chief, Site Cleanup, Central Valley Region (address: 11020 Sun Center Drive, #200, Rancho Cordova). Named in multiple approval letters.
- **Roberto Cervantes, P.E.** – Water Resources Control Engineer, Central Valley Region.
- **Kyle Cockerham** – Site Cleanup Unit, identified in 2024 correspondence.

### Stormwater management
The RWQCB has been the recipient of stormwater sampling reports from 2013 through 2025:

- **March 2018 stormwater sampling report** (S1200-01-01, Geocon, April 2018) – Includes data from sample locations PL1–PL5 and BG1–BG2. The report states "The sample locations were approved by the DTSC and Central Valley Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)." (source: *S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles March 2018 SW Sampling_04.18.md*)

- **February 2025 stormwater sampling report** (S2350-01-02, Geocon, June 2025) – Submitted to DTSC and RWQCB. DTSC approval letter (January 30, 2026) notes the RWQCB was part of the review process. (source: *DTSC Letter Response - 2025 GW Monitoring Report (900259).md*)

### Well decommissioning
- **May 3, 2024** – DTSC and RWQCB jointly approved the *Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data*, concluding that "the encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater." Both agencies concurred with discontinuation of groundwater monitoring and decommissioning of wells MW-4, MW-6, MW-9, and MW-10. (source: *Approval of GW Statistical Evaluation (900259).md*)

- **January 30, 2026** – DTSC conditional approval of the *Well Destruction Report* for the remaining four wells, with RWQCB review noted. (source: *DTSC Letter Response - 2025 GW Monitoring Report (900259).md*)

### Clean fill and borrow area approvals
The RWQCB is listed as a co-reviewer on multiple clean fill material approvals:

- **March 4, 2020** – DTSC acceptance of the *Clean Cap Borrow Area Technical Memo* (Geocon, February 27, 2020). Email cc: includes Steven Meeks (Water Board). (source: *Borrow Area Results Tech Memo Approval.md*)

- **June 12, 2020** – DTSC and "the Waterboard" approved the *Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan*. (source: *Approval E-mail_ SR 132 Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan.md*)

- **November 6, 2020** – DTSC and "the Water Board" accepted the *Bent 2 Soils Technical Memo* (Geocon, October 30, 2020) for use as clean cap material at 6-inch thickness or less. (source: *E-mail Acceptance of Bent 2 Soils Tech Memo.md*)

- **April 22, 2021** – DTSC and "the Water Board" accepted the *Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Technical Memorandum*. (source: *DTSC E-mail Approval of Carpenter Shoofly Tech Memo.md*)

## Regulatory status
The RWQCB enforces site-specific standards through the Basin Plan, including:

- **Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs)** – Primary drinking water standards applied to groundwater. Barium MCL: 1,000 µg/L. The site's primary COC (barium) has been consistently below this threshold in all monitored wells since 2012.
- **Water Quality Objectives** – Narrative and numeric objectives for surface water. Stormwater sampling data demonstrate runoff remains within Caltrans right-of-way.
- **NPDES permit requirements** – The site operates under a Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) that governs erosion control and stormwater management.

As of the 2024 statistical evaluation, both DTSC and RWQCB agree that the encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater and that no future impact is expected. The site is in compliance with water quality objectives.

## Open items
- **Stormwater monitoring continuation** – The February 2026 DTSC letter approves the FY24/25 stormwater report but does not indicate termination of the program. Future qualifying rain event sampling may be required.
- **Well destruction completion** – DTSC's January 30, 2026 conditional approval (Comment 1) requires the *Well Destruction Report* to include a table of estimated vs. actual grout volume as a performance metric. This is pending final submittal.
- **Interagency coordination** – No documentation found confirms RWQCB issuance of a final site closure letter or termination of its regulatory oversight for surface water.
