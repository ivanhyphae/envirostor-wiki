# Central Valley RWQCB (Regional Water Quality Control Board)

## Overview
The Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (Central Valley RWQCB) is the primary regulatory agency overseeing water quality compliance for the [[modesto-soil-stockpiles]] remediation project on State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. The RWQCB serves as the lead regulatory authority for stormwater management and surface water/groundwater protection at the site, working in coordination with the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), which oversees soil cleanup under a separate regulatory framework.

## Site record
The Central Valley RWQCB is documented in project records as the recipient and reviewer of stormwater sampling reports:

- **March 2016 stormwater sampling** (March 5, 2016, documented in *S9800-01-17A Modesto Stockpiles Mar 5 2016 Stormwater Sampling_04 16.pdf*): Stormwater sampling event conducted during a rain event. The report identifies "Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA" as the project location. Data were submitted to the RWQCB as part of the project's stormwater monitoring requirements.

- **October 2016 stormwater sampling** (October 2016, documented in *S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles Oct 2016 SW Sampling_12.16.pdf*): A subsequent stormwater sampling event at the same location. This report was also submitted for RWQCB review.

- **February 2025 stormwater sampling report** (dated 2.4.2025, documented in *S2350-01-02_2.4.2025 Stormwater Sampling Report_6.25.pdf*): A more recent stormwater sampling report, indicating ongoing RWQCB oversight of stormwater management at the site through 2025.

Regulatory approvals involving the RWQCB include:

- **Stockpile 3 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval** (*Stockpile 3 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval.pdf*): Approval of confirmation sampling results for Stockpile 3. While this document is a DTSC approval, the RWQCB's water quality standards would apply to any stormwater runoff or groundwater impacts from stockpile removal.

- **Carpenter Shoofly Tech Memo Approval** (*DTSC E-mail Approval of Carpenter Shoofly Tech Memo.pdf*): DTSC approval of a technical memorandum regarding the Carpenter Shoofly area, with RWQCB having concurrent jurisdiction over water quality aspects.

- **Final Stormwater Management Plan (SMP) Approval** (*Final SMP Approval E-mail.pdf*): Approval of the final SMP, which governs how stormwater is managed to prevent contaminant migration from stockpiles to surface waters. The RWQCB typically has authority over SMP compliance.

## Regulatory status
The Central Valley RWQCB regulates this project under:
- **California Water Code** (Porter-Cologne Water Quality Control Act)
- **National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES)** permit requirements for stormwater discharges
- **Regional Water Quality Control Plan (Basin Plan)** for the Sacramento River and San Joaquin River basins

Applicable standards enforced by the RWQCB include:
- **Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs)** for groundwater protection (e.g., barium MCL of 1,000 µg/L, referenced in project records for groundwater monitoring)
- **Water Quality Objectives** for surface water and groundwater in the Central Valley region

As of the latest document (February 2025 stormwater sampling report), the project remains under active RWQCB oversight for stormwater compliance.
