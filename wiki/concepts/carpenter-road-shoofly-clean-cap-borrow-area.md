## Definition

The **Carpenter Road Shoofly Clean Cap Borrow Area** is a designated source area for **clean fill material** used in the temporary **shoofly** alignment associated with the Carpenter Road project. In this context:

- **Shoofly** means a temporary roadway or railroad alignment built to maintain traffic or operations while the permanent alignment is under construction.
- **Clean cap** refers to a layer of relatively uncontaminated soil or granular material placed over an area to isolate underlying impacted material or to provide a compliant construction surface.
- **Borrow area** is the excavation site from which material is obtained for use elsewhere in the project.

The term identifies a specific borrow source intended to provide material meeting project requirements for clean fill placement in the Carpenter Road Shoofly Clean Fill Tech Memo context.

## How it works

A clean cap borrow area is used when a project needs a reliable supply of soil that satisfies environmental and geotechnical criteria for reuse as fill. The process generally works as follows:

1. **Borrow source identification**
   - A portion of land is selected as the source of excavatable fill.
   - The area is evaluated to confirm that material can be removed without introducing contamination concerns or unacceptable engineering properties.

2. **Material characterization**
   - The soil is assessed to determine whether it qualifies as “clean” material for placement in the shoofly cap.
   - Screening may include observations of soil type, evidence of staining or debris, and testing as required by the project.

3. **Excavation and stockpiling**
   - Suitable material is excavated from the borrow area.
   - Material may be stockpiled temporarily before transport to the placement area.

4. **Transport and placement**
   - Clean fill is hauled to the shoofly work zone.
   - It is placed in lifts and compacted or graded according to design requirements.

5. **Cap construction**
   - The clean fill forms a cap layer over the target area.
   - The cap serves as a physical barrier and a construction platform, depending on project needs.

6. **Quality control**
   - The borrow source and placed fill are managed to ensure the material remains suitable for its intended use.
   - Controls typically focus on preventing mixing with unsuitable soil, debris, or waterborne sediment.

In practice, the borrow area is not just a source of dirt; it is an engineered supply zone for material that must satisfy both environmental and construction constraints.

## Variants

Possible variants of the concept include:

- **Clean fill borrow area**: a general borrow source for uncontaminated fill material.
- **Cap borrow area**: a source specifically used to supply material for a protective cap.
- **Shoofly embankment borrow area**: a source for temporary roadway embankment construction rather than cap material.
- **On-site borrow vs. off-site borrow**:
  - **On-site borrow** uses material from within the project corridor.
  - **Off-site borrow** imports material from another location.

Implementation may vary depending on:
- soil availability,
- contamination constraints,
- hauling distance,
- temporary alignment geometry,
- and project-specific environmental requirements.

## Trade-offs

Key trade-offs associated with a clean cap borrow area include:

- **Environmental suitability vs. material availability**
  - The best geotechnical source may not meet clean-fill requirements.
  - The cleanest source may be limited in volume or difficult to access.

- **On-site reuse vs. import/export**
  - On-site borrow reduces haul distances and disposal needs.
  - It may require more extensive testing and controls to verify suitability.

- **Construction efficiency vs. protection of sensitive areas**
  - Faster excavation and placement can improve schedule.
  - But borrow operations can disturb soils, drainage, vegetation, or adjacent resources.

- **Cost vs. risk**
  - Using nearby borrow material can lower transport costs.
  - However, inadequate screening can increase the risk of contamination spread or rework.

- **Temporary footprint vs. long-term benefit**
  - Borrow areas can increase the project’s disturbed footprint during construction.
  - They may reduce the need for imported materials and support compliant cap construction.
