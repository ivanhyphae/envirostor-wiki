## Nitrate MCL

### Overview
The Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) for nitrate is the enforceable drinking-water standard applicable to groundwater at and near the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles project (State Route 132, Stanislaus County). The federal and California MCL for nitrate as nitrogen (N) is 10 mg/L (45 mg/L as NO₃). In this project, the MCL serves as the primary health-based threshold for evaluating nitrate impacts from historical stockpile leachate.

### Site record
Groundwater monitoring data from the project record show sporadic exceedances of the nitrate MCL in downgradient wells. In the **Phase 1 Baseline Groundwater Report (June 2019)**, sample results from monitoring well MW-5R indicated nitrate‑N at 14.2 mg/L, exceeding the MCL of 10 mg/L. Well MW-7 (near the southeast corner of Stockpile Area B) also had concentrations ranging from 11.8 to 13.5 mg/L during the first four quarters of 2020, as documented in the **Quarterly Monitoring Summary (Q4 2020)**. Spatial distribution is consistent with infiltration beneath the older, uncovered stockpiles; upgradient wells (e.g., MW-1, MW-2) remained below detection (0.5 mg/L).

Trend analysis in the **2021 Annual Data Report** shows nitrate levels in MW-5R decreasing gradually (14.2 → 11.0 → 9.8 mg/L over 2019–2021) after removal of the stockpile source in early 2020. However, MW-7 showed no clear trend, with levels remaining between 11.0 and 13.8 mg/L through Q3 2021. Verification sampling conducted in accordance with the **Remedial Action Workplan (RAWP), Rev. 2 (September 2020)** showed that post‑removal soil nitrate concentrations in the stockpile footprint were below the site-specific cleanup goal of 20 mg/kg (nitrate‑N), but residual soil‑leachate continues to contribute to groundwater.

### Regulatory status
The project is subject to the California Safe Drinking Water Act and the **USEPA MCL of 10 mg/L nitrate‑N** (40 CFR 141.62). The lead regulatory agency is DTSC, which incorporated this MCL as a groundwater protection standard in the **Imminent and Substantial Endangerment Order (ISE Order, February 2018)**. DTSC also established a **site-specific action level of 8.0 mg/L** for early notification triggers, below the MCL.

As of the latest **Semi‑Annual Groundwater Compliance Report (December 2022)**, the project was in compliance with the MCL at all monitoring wells except MW-7, which remained at 11.2 mg/L. DTSC granted a temporary waiver for MW-7 (valid through June 2023) pending implementation of additional in‑situ nitrate reduction measures described in the **Supplemental Remedial Design (June 2022)**.

### Open items
- **MW‑7 compliance**: DTSC required a **Corrective Action Plan Addendum** by March 2023 to demonstrate attainment of the MCL. As of the last project file update, that addendum had not been approved.
- **Long‑term monitoring frequency**: The **Operation and Maintenance Plan (2021)** requires quarterly monitoring for nitrate. However, DTSC has requested evaluation of reducing frequency to semi‑annual after two consecutive quarters of MCL compliance in all wells. That request is pending a trend assessment due Q1 2024.
- **Background threshold verification**: An alternative background concentration of 8.5 mg/L has been proposed by Caltrans for the shallow aquifer unit; DTSC has not formally accepted this, and dispute continues in the administrative record.
