## Overview

MW-10 is a groundwater monitoring well installed in May 2012 as part of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles monitoring network (State Route 99/132, Stanislaus County, California). The well is located south of Kansas Avenue and east of State Route 99, adjacent to Stockpile 3 and was intended to provide upgradient groundwater quality data for the site. It was installed by Geocon Consultants Inc. and is constructed of 2‑inch Sch 40 PVC with a screened interval from 29.5 to 39.5 feet below ground surface. The well is completed in the shallow zone of the upper aquifer (Modesto Formation).

## Site record

**Installation and Location**  
MW‑10 was installed on 30 May 2012 (Table 1, *Groundwater Sampling Report – November/December 2022 and February 2023*, Stantec, 8 Mar 2023). A DTSC letter of 29 Nov 2012 confirms the well’s location: “south of Kansas Avenue and east of SR 99.” The surveyed top‑of‑casing (TOC) elevation is 82.53 ft above mean sea level. Construction details: total boring depth 40 ft, completed well depth 39.65 ft, borehole diameter 8 in, slot size 0.010 in, filter pack of #2/12 sand set from 27.5 to 40 ft (Table 4, *Groundwater Monitoring Report – July 2012*, Geocon, Nov 2012; Table 1, Stantec 2023).

**Groundwater Elevations**  
- July 2012: depth to water (DTW) from TOC was 39.74 ft? Actually MW‑10 is not shown in the July 2012 table excerpt; the range for other wells was 31.34–39.74 ft. The April 2019 report states MW‑10 was dry.  
- October 2018: MW‑10 could not be located beneath brush and weeds (April 2019 report).  
- November 2022: MW‑10 could not be located due to a thick layer of wood mulch; the original well box had been destroyed during site work but the PVC casing was undamaged and replaced (Stantec 2023).  
- December 2022 and February 2023: MW‑10 was dry (no measurable water; Stantec 2023).

**Chemical Results**  
*July 2012 (first sampling after installation):*  
- Dissolved metals: barium 130 µg/L, strontium 210 µg/L, selenium 4.4 µg/L (highest among all wells for selenium), chromium 1.3 µg/L, vanadium 14 µg/L, zinc 15 µg/L, nickel 1.9 µg/L (*Summary of Groundwater Analytical Results – Title 22 Metals*, Geocon July 2012 report). All metals were below primary MCLs and health advisories.  
- General minerals: chloride 39 mg/L, nitrate‑as‑N 9.8 mg/L (below the primary MCL of 10 mg/L), sulfate 110 mg/L, TDS 710 mg/L (exceeds secondary MCL of 500 mg/L). Total cations 10 meq/L, total anions 11 meq/L. The Stiff diagram for July 2012 showed calcium‑dominant water, a change from sodium‑dominant in June 2012 (Geocon July 2012 report, Conclusions).  
- No dissolved lead was detected above the PQL of 1.0 µg/L.

*Historical consistency:* Barium and strontium concentrations in MW‑10 remained significantly below numeric thresholds throughout the monitoring period (Figures 5 and 7 in *Groundwater Monitoring Report – April 2019*, Geocon). Nitrate and TDS at MW‑10 were consistently elevated but similar to background levels observed in upgradient well MW‑1.

**Dry Conditions**  
From at least April 2019 onward, MW‑10 was dry during gauging events (April 2019 report; Stantec 2023). No groundwater samples were collected after 2017 (the last data shown in the 2023 report are from earlier events). The well has not been sampled since 2019 due to lack of water.

## Regulatory status

The DTSC, in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board, concurred with the July 2012 groundwater monitoring report (letter 29 Nov 2012). Applicable thresholds for the site are California primary and secondary MCLs for drinking water. For MW‑10, reported metals never exceeded primary MCLs; TDS exceeded the secondary MCL of 500 mg/L, and nitrate remained below 10 mg/L. No compliance or enforcement actions have been taken regarding MW‑10. The well remains part of the monitoring network, but no further sampling is possible while dry.

## Open items

No unresolved issues, pending deliverables, or required future actions specific to MW‑10 are documented in the provided sources. The well’s continued dry status may require abandonment or replacement if monitoring objectives change.
