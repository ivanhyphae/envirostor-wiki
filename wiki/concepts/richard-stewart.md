## Overview

**Richard Stewart** is the Engineering Geologist (PG 7926) assigned to the Caltrans Central Region Environmental Division, Hazardous Waste & Paleontology Branch, based in Fresno, CA. On this project, Stewart acts as Caltrans' lead technical contact for the Stockpile 2 remediation at the SR 132 site in Stanislaus County. He is responsible for transmitting verification documentation to regulatory agencies and obtaining their approval before proceeding with cover placement.

## Site Record

On March 9, 2020, Stewart forwarded the "Stockpile 2 BCS Removal Verification Testing" Technical Memorandum (Memo dated March 9, 2020) to Dean Wright (DTSC Project Manager), Perry Myers (DTSC), and Steven Meeks (Water Boards). The Memo described removal of barium-containing soil (BCS) from the east and west ends of Stockpile 2, and subsequent confirmation soil sampling verifying attainment of cleanup levels established in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP).

The e-mail chain shows that:
- Geocon Consultants' John Juhrend transmitted the verification memo to Stewart on March 9, 2020 at 5:29 PM, noting that BCS removal at Stockpile 3 was expected to be completed the following day pending final verification testing.
- Stewart forwarded the memo to regulators at 5:43 PM the same day, requesting approval for placement of clean soil cover on Stockpile 2.
- Dean Wright (DTSC) responded on March 10, 2020 at 1:28 PM, confirming that the sampling results demonstrated the east and west ends of Stockpile 2 met the RDIP verification thresholds. Wright stated: "DTSC agrees that these areas are now suitable for the placement of clean embankment fill." Wright's e-mail served as DTSC's formal acceptance of the Memo.

## Regulatory Status

DTSC (Department of Toxic Substances Control) is the lead regulatory agency reviewing the Stockpile 2 confirmation data. The applicable standard for closure was the verification thresholds established in the RDIP. Wright's March 10, 2020 approval confirmed that the barium-containing soil removal areas comply with those thresholds. Water Boards staff (Steven Meeks) was also copied but no Water Boards response is documented in this source chain.

## Open Items

- The Memo itself was not included in the source material, so the specific barium concentrations detected in confirmation samples, the volumes of soil removed, and the exact verification thresholds are not documented here.
- Stockpile 3 BCS removal timing and verification status remain pending as of March 9-10, 2020, with Geocon noting removal should be completed "tomorrow pending the results of final verification testing."
- No information is provided on Stockpile 1 status or overall site closure.
