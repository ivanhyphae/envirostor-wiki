## Overview

The **State Route 132 West Freeway Expressway Project** (SR 132 Project) is a Caltrans infrastructure undertaking in Stanislaus County, California, involving the construction of a new alignment for State Route 132 between the existing SR 132 and the City of Modesto. As part of this project, extensive earthwork is required, necessitating the identification and importation of clean fill material for use as structural backfill, embankment construction, and grading. The project is governed by a Remedial Action Plan (RAP) approved by the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), which mandates that all imported fill must meet specific chemical quality standards to prevent introduction of contaminants to the project site.

The **Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles** are a key component of this remediation case file, representing a source of potential clean fill material sourced from a property in Modesto, California. The Soil Stockpiles are subject to testing and regulatory approval under the SR 132 Project’s RAP and the DTSC’s oversight.

## Site Record

The documented facts regarding the SR 132 West Freeway Expressway Project and the Modesto Soil Stockpiles derive primarily from the document **"Hwy 132 Clean Fill Source Testing Exhibit.pdf"**, dated August 2022 (referred to herein as the "Exhibit").

- **Project Phases:** The Exhibit references the SR 132 Project as a multi-phase undertaking. Phase 1 occurred between 2019–2020, during which the first batch of clean fill was imported. The Exhibit addresses a second, subsequent phase of fill importation.
- **Fill Source:** The proposed source for the second phase of import is the **Modesto Soil Stockpiles**, consisting of soil excavated from a property located at **500 S. 9th Street, Modesto, CA** (Assessor’s Parcel Number [APN] 020-008-018). The soil was stockpiled at a temporary site in Modesto prior to testing.
- **Testing Results:** The Exhibit summarizes analytical data from samples collected from the Stockpiles. Key parameters and concentrations reported include:
    - **Arsenic:** Maximum detected concentration of **12 mg/kg** (total metals analysis, EPA Method 6010B).
    - **Barium:** Maximum detected concentration of **760 mg/kg** (total metals analysis, EPA Method 6010B).
    - **Chromium (Total):** Maximum detected concentration of **160 mg/kg** (total metals analysis, EPA Method 6010B).
    - **Lead:** Maximum detected concentration of **58 mg/kg** (total metals analysis, EPA Method 6010B).
    - **Mercury:** Maximum detected concentration of **0.23 mg/kg** (total metals analysis, EPA Method 7471A).
    - **Selenium:** Maximum detected concentration of **2.3 mg/kg** (total metals analysis, EPA Method 6010B).
    - **Silver:** Maximum detected concentration of **1.2 mg/kg** (total metals analysis, EPA Method 6010B).
    - **Thallium:** Maximum detected concentration of **0.54 mg/kg** (total metals analysis, EPA Method 6010B).
    - **Zinc:** Maximum detected concentration of **190 mg/kg** (total metals analysis, EPA Method 6010B).
    - **Total Petroleum Hydrocarbons (TPH) as Diesel:** Maximum detected concentration of **210 mg/kg** (EPA Method 8015B).
    - **Total Petroleum Hydrocarbons (TPH) as Motor Oil:** Maximum detected concentration of **220 mg/kg** (EPA Method 8015B).
- **Verification Results:** The Exhibit states that the test results for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles indicated all analytes (metals, TPH, volatile organic compounds, and pesticides/PCBs) were below applicable thresholds, specifically the **DTSC California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs) for Residential Use** and the **Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) Environmental Screening Levels (ESLs)**. No exceedances were noted.
- **Volumes:** The Exhibit does not specify the total volume (in cubic yards) of soil in the Stockpiles. It references a second phase of import but does not provide a specific quantity for that phase.

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable Standards:** The Exhibit explicitly references the use of **DTSC California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs) for Residential Use** and **RWQCB Environmental Screening Levels (ESLs)** as the evaluation criteria for clean fill acceptance. These thresholds were applied to all tested analytes (metals, TPH, VOCs, pesticides/PCBs).
- **Agency Decisions:** The Exhibit was prepared as part of a request to **DTSC** for approval to use the Modesto Soil Stockpiles as clean fill. The Exhibit states that based on the testing, the material meets the criteria for clean fill under the SR 132 Project RAP.
- **Compliance Status:** As of the date of the Exhibit (August 2022), the Modesto Soil Stockpiles were deemed compliant with the applicable clean fill standards. The project was proceeding with importation under DTSC oversight. No further conditions were documented in the Exhibit.

## Open Items

- The Exhibit notes that the Modesto Soil Stockpiles were tested per a sampling plan approved by DTSC. The status of any other potential stockpiles or fill sources for the project beyond this specific batch is not documented in the provided source.
- The Exhibit does not address post-import verification testing (e.g., of placed fill) or final certification of the completed backfill.
