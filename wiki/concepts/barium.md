## Overview

Barium is the primary contaminant of potential concern (COPC) driving remediation at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR‑132, Stanislaus County). It originates from historical barite (barium sulfate) processing by Barium Products Ltd. and later Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC), whose liquid tailings were discharged to unlined evaporation ponds in the 1950s–1970s. When the State purchased a portion of the FMC property in 1961 for SR‑99 construction, pond sediments and underlying soils were excavated and placed as three stockpiles within the current right‑of‑way. The site-wide remedial strategy—containment beneath roadways, retaining walls, and bridge abutments—is designed to manage barium-impacted soil in place.

## Site record

### Soil concentrations

- **Shaw 2004 PSI** (January 2004): Barium detected at maximum total concentrations of **1,730 mg/kg** (Stockpile 1), **60,700 mg/kg** (Stockpile 2), and **44,900 mg/kg** (Stockpile 3). Background soil barium ranged from **57 to 888 mg/kg** (source: FS Report §2.2.1).
- **Shaw 2006 SI** (May 2006): Maximum concentrations of **130 mg/kg** (Stockpile 1), **64,000 mg/kg** (Stockpile 2), and **72,000 mg/kg** (Stockpile 3). Background ranged from **17 to 120 mg/kg** (source: FS Report §2.2.2).
- **Geocon 2012 SSI** (September 2012): Surface soil from fenceline borings along Stockpile 2’s north side contained barium up to **4,300 mg/kg**; deeper samples (3–5 ft) fell to 42–680 mg/kg. In cadmium borings, barium reached **130,000 mg/kg** in one sample, but the 95% UCL for all 2012 barium data was **7,556 mg/kg**—well below the residential CHHSL of **5,200 mg/kg** and industrial CHHSL of **63,000 mg/kg** (source: FS Report §2.2.3, §3.2.2–3.2.4).

None of the reported barium concentrations exceeded California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs) for residential or commercial/industrial land use.

### Soluble barium and waste classification

Thirty-three stockpile soil samples from the 2006 SI were analyzed for soluble barium via WET and DI‑WET. Soluble barium (WET) ranged from **39 to 2,300 mg/L**; 28 of 33 samples exceeded the Title 22 STLC of **100 mg/L**. Soluble barium (DI‑WET) ranged from **1.8 to 220 mg/L**; nine exceeded the STLC. Shaw noted that the barium compounds are primarily barite (barium sulfate), making the Title 22 criteria not strictly applicable (source: FS Report §2.2.2).

### Stormwater

A single stormwater sample (SW03, March 2006) collected from the northwestern side of Stockpile 3 contained barium at **2,000 µg/L**, exceeding the MCL of **1,000 µg/L**. The six other stormwater samples ranged from **16 to 190 µg/L**. DTSC concluded the elevated result was isolated and that runoff in that area was confined to Caltrans ROW; surface water was not considered a chronic exposure pathway (source: FS Report §1.6.5).

### Groundwater

Groundwater monitoring wells installed in 2006 (MW‑1 through MW‑8) and 2012 (MW‑9 and MW‑10) have consistently shown dissolved barium concentrations below the California/federal MCL of **1,000 µg/L**:

| Date | Well | Barium (µg/L) | MCL (µg/L) | Source |
|------|------|----------------|------------|--------|
| July 2012 | MW‑5 | 280 | 1,000 | GW Report Table 2 |
| July 2012 | MW‑4 | 240 | 1,000 | GW Report Table 2 |
| July 2012 | MW‑1 | 130 | 1,000 | GW Report Table 2 |
| July 2012 | MW‑8, MW‑9 | 51 | 1,000 | GW Report Table 2 |
| July 2012 | MW‑10 | 59 | 1,000 | GW Report Table 2 |

The 2024 *Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data* (Geocon, Feb 26, 2024) concluded that the encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater and will not do so in the future. DTSC and the RWQCB concurred, approving discontinuation of groundwater monitoring and decommissioning of the remaining four wells (MW‑4, MW‑6, MW‑9, MW‑10) per the approved RDIP (source: Approval of GW Statistical Evaluation, May 3, 2024).

### Stockpile 2 BCS foundation spoil workplan (2022)

Geocon’s Technical Memorandum dated February 28, 2022 described sampling and management of BCS spoils generated during installation of a weather station and overhead sign on Stockpile 2. DTSC approved the plan on March 4, 2022, agreeing that if barium and lead concentrations fall below site‑specific background, the material may be reused on‑site; if above background, it must be profiled for disposal at the Forward Landfill in Manteca or another acceptable facility (source: Approval Letter Stockpile 2 Spoils Workplan).

### MSE wall footing sampling (2020)

For the Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE wall footing excavations, discrete soil samples collected at ~200‑ft intervals were analyzed for barium and lead and compared to the following RDIP/regulatory screening levels:

- **Maximum site‑specific background**: 120 mg/kg
- **RDIP BCS removal threshold (groundwater protection)**: 1,000 mg/kg
- **Construction worker ESL**: 3,000 mg/kg
- **Residential ESL**: 15,000 mg/kg
- **Commercial ESL**: 220,000 mg/kg

DTSC and the Water Board approved the sampling plan via email on April 2, 2020 (source: Acceptance E-mail – Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE Wall Sampling Plan).

### Clean cap borrow area (2020)

Composite soil samples from the proposed clean cap borrow area were analyzed and found consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations. DTSC accepted the Technical Memorandum on March 4, 2020, approving the soil as suitable clean cover material for capping Stockpiles 1 and 2 (source: Borrow Area Results Tech Memo Approval).

### Bent 2 excavation soils (2020)

Soil from the Bent 2 column excavation exceeded background criteria for clean cap material. Nevertheless, DTSC and the Water Board agreed it could be placed on top of Stockpile 2 at a thickness of **6 inches or less**, provided it was covered with clean base material and roadway pavement. A Revised Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) Amendment dated October 22, 2020 was accepted concurrently (source: E-mail Acceptance of Bent 2 Soils Tech Memo, Nov 6, 2020).

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable standard**: California and federal MCL for barium in drinking water is **1,000 µg/L (1.0 mg/L)**. All groundwater detections at the site remain below this threshold.
- **Soil screening levels**: Residential CHHSL **5,200 mg/kg**; industrial CHHSL **63,000 mg/kg**. Site concentrations are below these levels.
- **Waste classification**: Soluble barium exceeds the Title 22 STLC (100 mg/L) in many samples, but the barium is predominantly barite, to which the STLC criteria are not strictly applicable. Soil excavated from Stockpile 3 (2012) was disposed as non-hazardous.
- **Agency oversight**: DTSC (lead) and Central Valley RWQCB (consultation). DTSC concurred with the 2013 HHRA Update finding no unacceptable risk or hazard under current management (letter dated April 4, 2013). The 2024 groundwater evaluation confirmed no impact; monitoring has been discontinued.
- **Remedy status**: Phase I (2020) moved Stockpile 3 soil into the structural section of Stockpiles 1 and 2. The final remedy—containment beneath the SR‑132 Expressway—was approved in the Feasibility Study and Remedial Action Plan. Phase II will complete the 4‑lane highway.

## Open items

- The 2025 Well Destruction Completion Report for the four remaining monitoring wells (MW‑4, MW‑6, MW‑9, MW‑10) was conditionally approved by DTSC on January 30, 2026, pending addition of a table comparing estimated vs. actual grout volumes used for each well. A final revised report is due within 30 calendar days of the approval letter (source: DTSC Letter Response – 2025 GW Monitoring Report, Jan 30, 2026).
- Quarterly stormwater/surface water monitoring will continue during construction to verify containment integrity.
