## Overview

Barium is a naturally occurring alkaline earth metal that has been identified as a constituent of concern in groundwater at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County). The primary exposure pathway is groundwater ingestion, making compliance with the federal and California Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) the relevant benchmark.

## Site record

Detectable barium concentrations have been reported in groundwater monitoring samples:

- **2012 (September)**: Groundwater sampling event (S9525-06-44A) recorded barium at **0.85 mg/L** (850 µg/L) in a monitoring well at Stockpile 3 (source: *Stockpile 3 Confirmation Sampling Tech Memo Approval.pdf* and *06A2542ct_TO97_GW Rpt_final.20230308.pdf*). This value is below the MCL of 1.0 mg/L.

- **2023 (March)**: The groundwater monitoring report (06A2542ct_TO97_GW Rpt_final.20230308.pdf) again lists barium at **0.85 mg/L** for the same well, indicating stable concentrations over the intervening period.

No soil barium exceedances were identified in the reviewed documents. Stormwater sampling (March 2016) did not report barium above applicable screening levels (per S9800-01-17A).

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable standard**: California and federal MCL for barium in drinking water is **1,000 µg/L (1.0 mg/L)**. The site’s observed groundwater concentration (0.85 mg/L) is below this threshold.
- **Agency oversight**: DTSC (Department of Toxic Substances Control) has regulatory authority under the corrective action program. The 2023 groundwater report confirms ongoing compliance with MCLs for barium.

No additional remediation triggers or compliance actions related to barium are documented in the provided sources.

## Open items

- The long-term stability of barium concentrations and any potential migration toward receptors has not been fully addressed in the available record. Future groundwater monitoring reports (e.g., as required by the Final SMP) may provide additional data.
- No specific barium-related action levels (e.g., soil screening levels) were identified; if such thresholds exist, they are not documented here.
