## Overview

Phase 1 Consolidation refers to the initial remedial action implemented at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA) to address barium‑containing soils (BCS) from Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3. The work was conducted under the approved Remedial Action Plan (RAP) and Interim Phase 1 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP), and is documented in the Draft Interim Removal Action Completion Report (Draft Phase 1 RACR) dated February 24, 2022. The consolidation involved excavating Stockpile 3 entirely, trimming the western and eastern ends of Stockpile 2, and removing soil from the southern slopes of Stockpiles 1 and 2 to support construction of the new SR 132 Expressway. All excavated BCS was relocated as embankment fill within the containment zones of Stockpiles 1 and 2. The final consolidation and capping activities are described as completed for Phase 1, with a clean cap cover forming the SR 132 freeway surface and vegetated north slope.

## Site record

- **Pre‑remediation stockpile volumes** (Draft Phase 1 RACR):
  - Stockpile 1: ~34,000 yd³
  - Stockpile 2: ~102,000 yd³
  - Stockpile 3: ~24,000 yd³

- **Excavation and transport volumes**:
  - Stockpile 3: excavated ~27,675 yd³; transported by truck to Stockpile 1 containment zone.
  - Western and eastern ends of Stockpile 2: removed via scrapers and placed directly into the Stockpile 2 containment zone.

- **Confirmation sampling**:
  - 29 confirmation samples from Stockpile 3 excavation bottom; 3 locations exceeded 1,000 mg/kg barium cleanup goal (deepened ~1 ft, resampled to compliance).
  - 8 samples from Stockpile 2 western end, 12 from eastern end; initial results showed 4 areas above 1,000 mg/kg barium (deepened 1 ft, resampled to compliance).
  - Final confirmation samples for both stockpile areas showed all remaining soil below cleanup goals of 1,000 mg/kg barium and 80 mg/kg lead (per Draft Phase 1 RACR).

- **Cap construction** (Draft O&M Plan, March 2022, Appendix G of RACR):
  - The cap consists of pavement (SR 132 freeway surface) and vegetated north slope, with a minimum thickness meeting Health and Safety Code Section 25123.3(b)(4)(A) (per DTSC comment No. 3, August 1, 2022). DTSC noted that the draft used “millimeter” (misspelled) and recommended using “mils” to align with HSC.

- **Groundwater monitoring wells** (RDIP Figures, January 2019):
  - Well MW8 to be protected in place; well MW5 to be abandoned; well MW7 also to be abandoned.

## Regulatory status

- Applicable cleanup goals:
  - Barium: 1,000 mg/kg (soil)
  - Lead: 80 mg/kg (soil)
- Agencies: DTSC (lead) with Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) consultation.
- Compliance:
  - DTSC accepted the Draft Phase 1 RACR on February 24, 2022, as describing consolidation and capping activities completed to date.
  - The Draft O&M Plan, submitted March 2022, was reviewed by DTSC on August 1, 2022, with comments for revision (misspellings, advance notification requirements, post‑event inspections, and expanded inspection form).
  - A revised O&M Plan was required for inclusion as Appendix G of the Remedial Action Completion Report (RACR). No subsequent correspondence in the sources confirms final acceptance.

## Open items

- The Draft Phase 1 RACR was accepted as describing “completed to date” activities; no indication of a final RACR or DTSC closure letter for Phase 1.
- The Draft O&M Plan (March 2022) received six DTSC comments (August 1, 2022) requiring revision, including:
  - 10 working days advance notice to DTSC for any BCS disturbance.
  - Inclusion of HSC 25123.3(b)(4)(A) specifications.
  - Post‑event inspection triggers (fires, ≥1 in/24 hr rainfall, significant seismic events, traffic accidents).
  - Monitoring for unauthorized access (e.g., homeless encampments) on north vegetated slope.
  - Expanded inspection form line items (MSE wall bulging, highway pavement cracks, median/shoulders, north slope rills/slumping, storm drain debris).
- No updated O&M Plan or final RACR is included in the source documents reviewed.
