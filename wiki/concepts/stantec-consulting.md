## Stantec Consulting

### Overview
Stantec Consulting Services Inc. (Stantec) is the environmental consulting firm retained by Caltrans under Contract No. 06A2542, Task Order No. 97 (TO97) to perform surface water discharge inspections and sampling at the Modesto Soil Stockpiles Encapsulation Project in Stanislaus County, California. Stantec assumed these responsibilities from previous contractors Shaw Environmental, Inc. (Shaw) and Geocon Consultants, Inc. (Geocon), who conducted surface water monitoring between 2006 and 2019. Stantec’s scope includes site inspections, surface water sample collection, and reporting to Caltrans, DTSC, and CVRWQCB.

### Site Record
Stantec’s documented activities at the site include:

- **Transition from Prior Contractors:** Stantec performed surface water sampling events from 2019 through 2020 at the same sampling locations previously established by Geocon (PL1 through PL5, BG1, and BG2). These locations were eliminated when Phase I of the SR 99/132 expressway construction (January 2020 – November 2022) removed the need for them.

- **Revised Sampling Network (November 2, 2022):** During a site walk with the Caltrans TO Manager, Stantec identified new surface water and background sampling locations:
  - Removed background location BG2.
  - Added new background location BG3, located along the east side of North Carpenter Road, approximately 700 feet south of the southeast corner of North Carpenter Road and Kansas Avenue.
  - Added surface runoff locations PL6 and PL7. PL6 is at a surface water discharge point on the north side of SR 132 near SR 99. PL7 is along the north side of SR 132 at the discharge pipe for stormwater basin 5, located at the northeast corner of SR 132 and North Carpenter Road.

- **February 28, 2023 Inspection:** Stantec mobilized at approximately 8:45 AM. Precipitation measured 0.09 inches on February 28, 2023. No surface water samples were collected because there was no standing surface water or active runoff. Observations included:
  - No ponding at BG1 or BG3.
  - No active runoff from stormwater pipes at PL6 or PL7.
  - Trash and debris blocking the end of the discharge pipe at PL7.
  - Soil erosion on the east side of stormwater basin 5.
  - Minor erosion at several locations on the north side of Stockpile 1.
  - Areas on the north side of Stockpile 2 that were hydroseeded but lacked vegetation.
  - BMPs in place (straw waddles and jute mesh) in areas where improvements had occurred.
  - No visible surface water discharges or runoff throughout the project area.

- **Documentation:** The inspection results were reported in the *Surface Water Report – February 28, 2023*, dated March 9, 2023, in letter form to Mr. Richard Stewart, PG, of Caltrans Central Region Environmental Division. The report includes a Vicinity Map (Figure 1) and a Surface Water Sampling Site Plan (Figure 2). A photographic record is included as Appendix A.

### Regulatory Status
Stantec conducted the surface water inspection in accordance with protocols approved by DTSC, as established in:
- *Final Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP)*, prepared by Shaw Environmental, Inc., dated January 2006.
- *Addendum to Surface Water Sampling and Analysis Plan (SAP Addendum)*, prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc., dated February 20, 2013.

Sample locations were previously approved by DTSC and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB). No exceedances or compliance issues were identified during the February 28, 2023 inspection, as no surface water was present to sample. The stormwater gate valve, which would release water from the drainage basin to the Tuolumne River if opened, remains closed unless the basin fills to near capacity and jeopardizes SR 99 northbound lanes.

### Open Items
- The surface water sampling event scheduled for the qualifying rain event of February 27–28, 2023 did not occur due to insufficient rainfall (0.09 inches) and lack of standing water or runoff. Future qualifying rain events may trigger sampling at the revised stations (PL6, PL7, BG3) under Stantec’s ongoing TO97 scope.
- Erosion observed on the east side of stormwater basin 5 and minor erosion on the north side of Stockpile 1 have not been addressed in the source document; these may require corrective BMP maintenance.
- Vegetation establishment failures in hydroseeded areas on the north side of Stockpile 2 may affect erosion control performance.
- Trash and debris blocking the PL7 discharge pipe may impede future sampling or drainage.
