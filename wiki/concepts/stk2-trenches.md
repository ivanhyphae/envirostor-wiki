## Overview

The **Stk2 Trenches** are excavation trenches within Stockpile 2 at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA). They were dug as part of a targeted sampling investigation of the mechanically stabilized earth (MSE) wall backfill, documented in the *Stockpile 2 MSE Wall Sampling Tech Memo*. The trenches allowed collection of soil samples to characterize contamination—primarily barium—associated with the MSE wall material and to inform remedial decisions.

## Site record

The *Stockpile 2 MSE Wall Sampling Tech Memo* (source document, truncated) reports the following site‑specific data:

+ **Barium concentration in soil:** 760 mg/kg (value legible in the PDF object stream).  
+ **Applicable threshold:** The Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) for barium in groundwater is 1,000 µg/L; the memo likely compared soil concentrations to DTSC screening levels.  
+ **Monitoring well MW‑5** is referenced in the document, indicating a nearby groundwater monitoring point.  
+ **Phase 1 activities** are identified with the date range 2019–2020.  
+ The memo includes “verification results” and “exceedances,” suggesting that some trench samples exceeded applicable criteria and that follow‑up verification sampling was performed.  

The exact number, dimensions, and spatial layout of the Stk2 Trenches are not legible in the available source fragment.

## Regulatory status

The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) is the regulatory agency overseeing the site. Barium levels in the MSE wall backfill are the primary constituent of concern. The groundwater MCL for barium (1,000 µg/L) serves as an indirect benchmark for soil leachability. The project’s regulatory status is currently dependent on the conclusions of the *Stockpile 2 MSE Wall Sampling Tech Memo*; no final approval or closure decision is documented in the available excerpt.
