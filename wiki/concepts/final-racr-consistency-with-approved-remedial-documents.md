## Definition

**Final RACR consistency with approved remedial documents** is the quality-check condition that a final RACR (Remedial Action Completion Report) must accurately align with, and be supportable by, all **approved remedial documents** associated with the project or site. In practice, this means the final RACR’s statements, dates, figures, remedial actions, outcomes, and closure claims must match the approved scope, findings, response actions, and documented approvals in the underlying remedial record.

This concept is about **documentary consistency at closure**: the final report should not introduce facts, conclusions, or completion claims that are unsupported by the approved remedial documents, and it should reflect any revisions, conditions, or exceptions contained in those documents.

## How it works

The consistency check is typically performed at the end of a remedial project or corrective action process, before the final RACR is accepted or used as a closure document.

### 1. Establish the approved remedial record
The reviewer identifies the set of documents that define what was approved, such as:
- approved remedial action plans or work plans
- approval letters
- interim corrective action or response documents
- revised scopes or amendments
- sampling and verification plans
- closure criteria or acceptance conditions

These documents define the authoritative basis for what the final RACR must report.

### 2. Compare the final RACR against the approved documents
The final RACR is checked for alignment with the approved record in several areas:
- **Project scope**: Are the remedial areas, contaminants, or media addressed the same as approved?
- **Activities completed**: Do the described field actions match what was authorized?
- **Dates and sequence**: Are initiation, completion, sampling, and approval dates consistent?
- **Analytical results**: Do reported lab results or confirmation data match the approved datasets?
- **Conclusions**: Does the report only claim closure or completion to the extent supported by approvals?
- **Exceptions and conditions**: Are any restrictions, residual risks, or follow-up obligations carried forward correctly?

### 3. Resolve discrepancies
If differences are found, they are assessed to determine whether they are:
- clerical or typographical errors
- updated information that was formally approved later
- substantive inconsistencies requiring correction
- missing attachments or references
- unsupported closure statements

A final RACR should be revised, supplemented, or held until the discrepancy is resolved.

### 4. Confirm approval traceability
A key part of consistency is traceability. The final RACR should show how its conclusions derive from:
- approved remedial documents
- documented field implementation
- verification and analytical evidence
- formal acceptance or acceptance-letter language, where applicable

Where an acceptance letter exists, the final RACR should remain consistent with the acceptance conditions and any explicit scope limits stated in that letter.

### 5. Use the final RACR as a closure record
When consistency is confirmed, the final RACR functions as the consolidated record demonstrating that the remedial work was completed in accordance with the approved documents and that any required remedial milestones were satisfied.

## Variants

### Full consistency review
A comprehensive line-by-line comparison of the final RACR against all approved remedial documents. This is common when closure depends on strict compliance documentation.

### Scope-limited consistency review
Focuses only on the approved elements relevant to closure, such as a specific operable unit, release area, or corrective action segment.

### Acceptance-letter-based consistency
The final RACR is checked against the terms of an acceptance letter or formal approval letter, especially where the letter sets conditions for interim or final acceptance.

### Interim-to-final continuity review
Used when an interim RACR or interim remedial report was previously accepted. The final RACR must be consistent with the interim record unless later approved changes were made.

### Corrected or amended final RACR
If inconsistencies are discovered after issuance, an amended final RACR may be submitted to align the record with the approved documents.

## Trade-offs

### Benefits
- Improves regulatory and administrative defensibility
- Reduces the risk of closure rejection or rework
- Creates a clear audit trail from approval to completion
- Helps ensure that closure claims are accurate and supportable

### Limitations
- Can delay final acceptance if documents are incomplete or inconsistent
- Requires careful document control across multiple versions and approvals
- May be difficult when approvals were issued in stages or with changing scopes
- Can expose discrepancies between field implementation and approved plans

### Practical considerations
- Version control is essential; the final RACR should reference the correct approved document set.
- Minor editorial differences may be acceptable, but substantive differences in scope, results, or conclusions are not.
- If an acceptance letter or approval condition imposes specific wording or requirements, the final RACR should reproduce those accurately.
- Supporting attachments, tables, and certifications should be checked for internal consistency as well as consistency with the approved remedial record.
