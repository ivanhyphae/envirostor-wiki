## Definition

An **Interim Removal Action Completion Report (Interim RACR)** is a formal cleanup document used in environmental response work to summarize the completion of a **removal action** that was implemented on an interim basis, before final site remedy selection or full remedial closure. It records what action was taken, why it was taken, the extent of work performed, confirmation of completion, and any remaining conditions, monitoring, or follow-up needs.

In practice, an Interim RACR serves as the official bridge between field execution and administrative closeout for a removal action. It documents that the immediate threat or release condition addressed by the removal action has been sufficiently managed at that stage of the project.

## How it works

An Interim RACR is typically prepared after a removal action has been implemented and field activities are complete. It is based on project records, sampling data, construction or excavation logs, disposal manifests, photographs, figures, and tables. The report generally follows a structured format to show:

1. **Site and action background**
   - Identifies the site, the release or hazard addressed, and the regulatory or programmatic basis for the action.
   - Describes the purpose of the removal action, such as reducing acute risk, limiting contaminant migration, or addressing an immediate exposure pathway.

2. **Description of the removal action**
   - Summarizes the scope of work.
   - Explains what was removed, treated, stabilized, contained, or otherwise managed.
   - Describes methods used, such as excavation, debris removal, system installation, waste handling, or temporary controls.

3. **Implementation and completion documentation**
   - Documents when the work occurred and how completion was achieved.
   - May include confirmation sampling, waste characterization, disposal records, as-built drawings, and photographic evidence.
   - States whether the intended interim objective was met.

4. **Remaining conditions and next steps**
   - Notes residual contamination, access restrictions, institutional controls, or the need for long-term monitoring.
   - Clarifies whether the interim removal action is standalone or part of a larger response strategy.
   - Identifies any unresolved issues to be addressed in later reports or final decision documents.

5. **Administrative closeout**
   - Provides a formal record that the interim removal action is complete for the purposes described in the report.
   - Supports project management, agency review, and public or stakeholder documentation.

The report is usually organized with supporting appendices for detailed tables, figures, sampling results, and technical records. In many cases, the appendices contain the evidence needed to verify the action’s scope and completion.

## Variants

Common variants or closely related implementations include:

- **Final RACR**: A completion report for a removal action intended to document final closeout rather than an interim milestone.
- **Emergency removal action report**: Focuses on urgent short-term response to an immediate hazard.
- **Non-time-critical removal action documentation**: Similar in structure, but tied to a planned removal action rather than an emergency response.
- **Interim action completion memorandum or report**: A lighter-weight administrative equivalent used in some programs.
- **Removal action closeout report**: A broader term that may cover interim or final closeout depending on agency practice.

Differences among these variants are usually procedural rather than conceptual. The essential function remains the same: to document that a removal action was completed and to define what that completion means within the larger cleanup process.

## Trade-offs

Key considerations and limitations include:

- **Interim, not necessarily final**: Completion of an interim removal action does not mean the site is fully cleaned up. It may only mean the immediate response objective was achieved.
- **Dependence on supporting data**: The credibility of the report depends on accurate field records, analytical results, and waste documentation.
- **Potential for incomplete resolution**: Residual contamination or future remedial actions may still be required, so the report must clearly avoid overstating closure.
- **Administrative complexity**: Preparing a thorough RACR can require extensive coordination among technical staff, regulators, contractors, and laboratories.
- **Traceability requirements**: Because the document may support regulatory decisions, it must carefully distinguish between action completion, risk reduction, and final remedy selection.
- **Variability by agency/program**: Different programs may use different templates, terminology, and approval steps, which can complicate comparisons across sites.
