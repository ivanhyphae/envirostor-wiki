## Definition

An **Interim Removal Action Completion Report** (Interim RACR) is a formal environmental documentation that summarizes the planning, execution, and results of an interim removal action conducted under the Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act (CERCLA) or similar regulatory frameworks. It certifies that an interim—typically non-final—remedial measure has been completed, documenting site conditions, waste volumes removed, sampling data, and the achievement of action-specific cleanup goals. Unlike a final Removal Action Completion Report (RACR), an Interim RACR addresses a partial or phase-specific cleanup before the overall site remediation is finished.

## How it works

The Interim RACR is prepared after the physical removal or stabilization activities are concluded but before the final site-wide remediation is certified. The process typically follows these steps:

1. **Regulatory framework**: The report is prepared in accordance with an Action Memorandum (or similar decision document) that authorized the interim removal action. It demonstrates compliance with the scope, objectives, and cleanup standards defined in that memorandum.

2. **Documentation of activities**: The report includes:
   - Description of the removal action (e.g., excavation, stockpile management, off-site disposal)
   - Volume of contaminated material removed or treated (often measured in cubic yards or tons)
   - Waste characterization and classification (e.g., hazardous vs. non-hazardous)
   - Transportation and disposal documentation (e.g., manifests, landfill receipts)

3. **Sampling and analytical results**: Soil, groundwater, or air monitoring data are presented to show that action-specific cleanup levels (e.g., California Human Health Screening Levels, Regional Water Board objectives) were attained. Statistical comparisons of pre- and post-removal sampling are included.

4. **Corrective actions and deviations**: Any changes from the approved removal plan, along with justifications and regulatory concurrence, are documented. This includes modifications to excavation boundaries, treatment methods, or waste handling procedures.

5. **Certification and closure**: The report is signed by the responsible party’s project manager and reviewed by regulatory agencies (e.g., EPA, state Department of Toxic Substances Control, or local water boards). Approval of the Interim RACR indicates that the interim action is complete and protective of human health and the environment for the phase addressed.

## Variants

- **Interim Removal Action Completion Report (RACR)**: As described above, used for partial cleanups under CERCLA or state equivalents.
- **Final Removal Action Completion Report (Final RACR)**: Documents completion of all removal actions at a site, often leading to deletion from registry lists (e.g., NPL) or regulatory closure.
- **Remedial Action Completion Report (RACR) for remedial actions**: Applies to longer-term remedial actions (rather than removals) under CERCLA’s remedy selection process.
- **Non-Time-Critical Removal Action Completion Report**: For actions where there is no imminent threat, allowing more time for planning and documentation.
- **Time-Critical Removal Action Completion Report**: For emergency or near-immediate responses, often with condensed documentation requirements.

## Trade-offs

- **Comprehensiveness vs. timeliness**: Interim RACRs allow early documentation and closure of a phase, but may require later amendment or a separate final report for the overall site, increasing administrative burden.
- **Partial certainty**: Meeting interim cleanup goals does not guarantee that final site-wide cleanup objectives will be achieved; future actions may be needed based on additional data.
- **Regulatory scrutiny**: Interim RACRs often undergo detailed agency review, especially if deviations from the approved plan occurred. Incomplete documentation can delay project closure.
- **Liability implications**: Completion of an interim action may reduce immediate risks but does not typically release the responsible party from residual liability until final closure is certified.
- **Stakeholder communication**: The report must clearly distinguish between what is accomplished and what remains, to avoid confusion among regulators, affected communities, or property buyers.
