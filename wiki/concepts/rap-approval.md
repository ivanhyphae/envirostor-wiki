## Overview
The **RAP Approval** refers to the formal written determination by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) on March 14, 2018, approving the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County. The approval selected **Alternative 4 – Containment** as the final remedy and established the regulatory framework under which the containment action is implemented, monitored, and verified over the long term.

## Site Record
- **Approval date:** March 14, 2018 (document title: “Approval letter for SR 132 RAP.pdf”).
- **Selected remedy:** Alternative 4 (Containment). Containment is achieved by constructing retaining walls, bridge abutments, and the SR 132 roadway directly over the stockpiles. All unpaved areas receive a clean fill cover.
- **Regulatory basis:** The remedy eliminates the exposure pathway to human receptors and minimizes surface‑water infiltration. DTSC independently determined that the remedy, with implementation of the specified controls, would have no significant environmental effect.
- **CEQA/NEPA compliance:** The RAP satisfies CEQA requirements; the Environmental Impact Report/Environmental Assessment was certified, and a Finding of No Significant Impact was issued under NEPA.
- **Conditions of approval:**
  - A Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) is required before each construction phase.
  - A start‑work notice (in English and Spanish) must be posted at least two weeks before construction begins.
  - An Operations, Maintenance, and Monitoring Plan (OMA/OMP) must be prepared after Phase 1.
  - A land use covenant must be recorded to restrict future land use and prevent exposure.
  - Annual pavement inspection and groundwater monitoring are required.
  - A five‑year remedy evaluation must be conducted.
- **Phased implementation:**
  - Phase 1: 2019–2020
  - Phase 2: 2028–2029
- **Responsible parties:**
  - Caltrans – owner/operator
  - DTSC – lead regulatory agency
  - Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) – co‑regulatory agency

## Regulatory Status
- DTSC serves as the lead regulatory agency under the California Hazardous Waste Control Law and the federal Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA) corrective action provisions.
- The Central Valley RWQCB is the co‑regulatory agency responsible for water quality oversight.
- The land use covenant is a permanent institutional control restricting future use to prevent exposure to residual contamination.
- Compliance with the approval is tracked through annual inspections, groundwater monitoring reports, and five‑year remedy evaluations.
- The project is currently in compliance with the conditions set forth in the March 14, 2018 approval letter, subject to ongoing implementation of the OMA/OMP and the phased construction schedule.

## Open Items
- **No quantitative performance metrics** are included in the approval letter itself; these are expected to be developed in the RDIP and OMA/OMP.
- The OMA/OMP may need to be revised after completion of Phase 2 in 2028–2029 to reflect the final configuration of the containment system.
- Long‑term effectiveness depends entirely on continued operation, maintenance, and monitoring; any failure of institutional controls or physical barriers could re‑expose receptors.
