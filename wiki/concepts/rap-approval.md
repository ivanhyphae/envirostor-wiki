## Overview
The **RAP Approval** is the March 14, 2018 formal determination by the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) approving the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County. The approval selected Alternative 4 – Containment as the final remedy, establishing the regulatory and implementation framework for long-term containment of contaminated soil stockpiles beneath the SR 132 roadway, retaining walls, bridge abutments, and a clean fill cover system.

## Site Record
- **Approval date**: March 14, 2018 (document: "Approval letter for SR 132 RAP.pdf")
- **Selected remedy**: Alternative 4 – Containment. The remedy places contaminated stockpiles under permanent engineered containment: retaining walls, bridge abutments, and the SR 132 roadway pavement directly over stockpiles, with clean fill cover on all unpaved areas.
- **Regulatory determination**: DTSC independently determined the selected remedy, with implementation of specified controls, would have no significant environmental effect.
- **CEQA/NEPA**: RAP satisfies CEQA; the Environmental Impact Report/Environmental Assessment was certified; a Finding of No Significant Impact was issued under NEPA.
- **Conditions of approval** (documented in approval letter):
  - Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP) required before each construction phase
  - Start-work notice in English and Spanish posted ≥2 weeks before construction
  - Operations, Maintenance, and Monitoring Plan (OMA/OMP) after Phase 1
  - Land use covenant to restrict future use and prevent exposure
  - Annual pavement inspection and groundwater monitoring
  - Five-year remedy evaluation
- **Phased implementation schedule**:
  - Phase 1: 2019–2020
  - Phase 2: 2028–2029
- **Responsible parties**:
  - Caltrans – owner/operator
  - DTSC – lead regulatory agency
  - Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) – co-regulatory agency

## Regulatory Status
- DTSC serves as lead regulatory agency under California Hazardous Waste Control Law and Resource Conservation and Recovery Act (RCRA) corrective action provisions.
- Central Valley RWQCB is co-regulatory agency for water quality oversight.
- Permanent institutional control: land use covenant restricting future use to prevent exposure to residual contamination.
- Compliance tracking: annual inspections, groundwater monitoring reports, five-year remedy evaluations.
- Project currently in compliance with March 14, 2018 approval conditions, subject to ongoing OMA/OMP implementation and phased construction schedule (Phase 1 2019–2020 completed; Phase 2 2028–2029 pending).

## Open Items
- No quantitative performance metrics in the approval letter itself; these are to be developed in pending RDIP and OMA/OMP documents.
- OMA/OMP may require revision after Phase 2 completion (2028–2029) to reflect final containment system configuration.
- Long-term effectiveness dependent on continued operation, maintenance, and monitoring; failure of institutional controls or physical barriers could re-expose receptors.
