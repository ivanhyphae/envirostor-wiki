## Overview

**WSP** is the prime construction management (CM) firm retained by Caltrans for the Modesto 132/99 Soil Stockpiles remediation project (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA). In the project record, WSP serves as the technical lead coordinating sampling, regulatory submittals, and contractor communications related to clean cap material sourcing and placement. The firm appears in this case file as the intermediary between the excavation contractor (Bay Cities Paving & Grading, BCPG) and regulatory agencies (DTSC, Caltrans), and as the developer of the clean fill sampling plan.

## Site Record

The documented activities involving WSP in this case file center on the **Carpenter Road Shoofly** clean fill sampling approval process during **February–April 2021**:

- **February 25, 2021** — BCPG (Adan Corrales) emailed WSP staff (Rick Deml, Chad Baker, Inderjit Chadha, Scott Frenette) with plans showing outlined areas for "clean cap test locations" at the Modesto 132/99 site.
- **March 1, 2021** — Scott Frenette (WSP Construction Manager, AVP) responded to BCPG identifying that material from **Retaining Wall No. 1110** had already been approved for clean cap use, but that material under the **Carpenter Shoofly** required testing and DTSC approval before use. Frenette requested from BCPG: depth of excavation, total fill volume, utility clearance status, and excavation timeline.
- **April 2, 2021** — John Juhrend (Geocon Consultants) sent an email to Richard Stewart (Caltrans Engineering Geologist) forwarding the clean fill sampling plan "per details provided by WSP below" and requesting submittal to DTSC for approval. The sampling plan was authored by WSP and covered the Carpenter Road Shoofly location. Juhrend stated that upon DTSC approval, Geocon would complete the sampling.

The email chain shows WSP operating in a coordination role: defining data needs for the sampling plan, setting the regulatory pathway (DTSC pre-approval of the plan before field sampling), and managing the sequence between contractor (BCPG), consultant (Geocon), and agency (Caltrans/DTSC).

## Regulatory Status

WSP’s role in this case file is not a regulated entity but rather a project consultant operating under Caltrans’ regulatory obligations. The material in question is governed by:
- **DTSC** — Clean fill sampling plan approval required prior to testing
- **Caltrans Environmental Division Hazardous Waste & Paleontology Branch** — receiving and forwarding submittals

Material from Retaining Wall No. 1110 had previously received DTSC approval. Carpenter Shoofly material remained unapproved as of March–April 2021, pending plan review.

## Open Items

As of the April 2, 2021 email:
- Clean fill sampling plan had been forwarded to Caltrans for DTSC submittal but **approval had not yet been received**
- Testing of Carpenter Shoofly material **could not proceed** until DTSC approval was granted
- BCPG had not yet provided depth of excavation, total fill volume, utility clearance verification, or excavation schedule to WSP (as of March 1, 2021)
