## Overview

The Modesto Soil Stockpiles are a group of accumulated soils along State Routes 99 and 132 in Stanislaus County, California, managed by Caltrans District 6. The site emerged from an undocumented history of soil placement—likely associated with historic highway construction and maintenance activities—and was identified as a concern due to elevated concentrations of metals, principally **cadmium** and **barium**, that posed potential risks to human health and the environment. The case file, under Project No. S9525-06-44 and EA No. 10-403500, involves Caltrans, the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC), and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB). The primary contaminants are metals, with the most frequently cited being cadmium and barium, whose site-specific impacts are being characterized through phased investigations and a Feasibility Study (FS).

## Site Record

### Contaminants of Concern

The primary contaminants identified in the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site record are metals. While the sources do not provide exhaustive concentration tables, the following is documented:

- **Cadmium**: The site includes special investigation borings (“Cadmium Borings”) that were designed to reach depths of 20 feet, suggesting cadmium was a key driver of vertical delineation concern. Cadmium is a toxic metal with human health and ecological screening levels well below common background in some regions.
- **Barium**: Barium is another metal of concern. In related Caltrans projects (e.g., other District 6 stockpiles), barium concentrations of **760 mg/kg** have been reported in soil exceeding residential screening levels (RSLs). For groundwater under the site, the **Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) for barium is 1,000 µg/L**, which serves as a comparison threshold.
- **Other metals**: The laboratory analysis methodology described in the Final Workplan (EPA Method 6010B for metals and Method 7471 for mercury) will generate data on a suite of metals, though the record specifically ties cadmium and barium to the site’s risk-driven remedial decisions.

### Investigation History

- **2012**: Caltrans and Geocon submitted a *Draft Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan* (Draft Workplan) to DTSC. DTSC and RWQCB provided comments on September 12, 2012, requesting field adjustments and clarifications. A site survey was conducted on September 17, 2012, with DTSC, Caltrans, and Geocon representatives reviewing staked boring locations. The *Final Supplemental Site Investigation Workplan* (Final Workplan) was dated September 18, 2012, and incorporated field changes—adding or adjusting Fenceline Borings (5 feet deep) and Perimeter Borings (3 feet deep)—and clarified that Cadmium Borings would be continuous cored to 20 feet.
- **2014**: The *Final Feasibility Study Report* (FS Report) (S9800-01-17, dated June 2014) was prepared, likely evaluating remedial alternatives for the stockpiles. This document is referenced as a core source for the site's decision-making framework but its specific findings are not excerpted here.

### Sampling Methodology

- **Fenceline Borings**: 5-foot depth, continuous core. Samples collected from 0–6 inches and from one-foot intervals (1–2, 2–3, 3–4, 4–5 ft).
- **Perimeter Borings**: 3-foot depth, continuous core. Same sample interval method as Fenceline.
- **Cadmium Borings**: 20-foot depth. Samples from 0–6 inches and then at approximate 5-foot intervals (4–5, 9–10, 14–15, 19–20 ft).
- Decontamination water was required to be contained, sampled for metals, and appropriately disposed of.

## Regulatory Status

- **Lead Agency**: DTSC (in cooperation with Central Valley RWQCB) oversees cleanup. Caltrans is the responsible party.
- **Applicable Standards**:
  - **Metals in soil**: Compared to USEPA Regional Screening Levels (RSLs) or California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs), although specific values for this site are not yet fully documented in the provided excerpts.
  - **Metals in groundwater**: MCLs (e.g., barium MCL 1,000 µg/L) apply if groundwater is impacted.
  - **Background thresholds**: Background soil concentrations for the region may be used to determine if stockpile materials are above naturally occurring levels.
- **Current Status**: Based on the sources (2012–2014), the site was in the **investigation/feasibility study phase**. The FS Report was finalized in 2014, but no documentation of the final remedy selection or closure is provided here. The site appears to be subject to **ongoing regulatory oversight pending remedy implementation**.

## Open Items

- **Missing phase documentation**: The provided sources end with the 2014 FS Report and 2012 Workplan. The **remedy selection (RI/FS conclusion), Remedial Action Plan (RAP), and any construction or verification sampling results** are not included. These would be required to determine final site status.
- **Vertical extent of cadmium**: Cadmium Borings were proposed to 20 feet, but results of that vertical delineation are not excerpted.
- **Decontamination water disposition**: The Final Workplan states decontamination water will be contained, sampled, and disposed of appropriately, but the actual disposal records or analytical results are not provided.
- **Human health risk assessment**: DTSC specifically requested that the health risk assessment be presented in a separate report (not in the workplan summary report), and no such separate report is included here.
