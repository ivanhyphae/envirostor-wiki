## Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Tech Memo

### Overview
The Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Tech Memo is a technical memorandum dated **April 12, 2021**, prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. (John Juhrend, PE, CEG, CEM). The memo describes the sampling and analytical program used to characterize soil from the **Carpenter Shoofly** area for use as clean fill on the SR 132 (Stanislaus County) project. On **April 22, 2021**, the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (Water Board) formally accepted the memo via email, confirming that the composite soil sample results were consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations and met the **Barium Containing Soil clean cap criteria**. This acceptance authorized the use of the material as clean fill at the project site.

### Site record
- **Document**: *Carpenter Shoofly Clean Fill Technical Memorandum*, dated April 12, 2021.
- **Prepared by**: John Juhrend, Geocon Consultants, Inc. (Rancho Cordova, CA).
- **Submitted to**: Richard C. Stewart (Caltrans, Central Region Environmental Division) via email on April 12, 2021.
- **Forwarded to DTSC**: April 20, 2021, by Richard Stewart with a request for review.
- **Regulatory review**: DTSC (Dean Wright, PG) and Water Board (Kyle Cockerham) reviewed the memo.
- **Agency decision**: DTSC acceptance transmitted via email on April 22, 2021 (subject line: “RE: SR 132 - DTSC Approval of Clean Fill Sampling Plan for Carpenter Shoofly”). The email states: *“The agencies agree that the composite soil sample results are consistent with naturally occurring background concentrations and meet the Barium Containing Soil clean cap criteria.”*
- **Sampling scope**: Composite soil samples were collected from the Carpenter Shoofly area to characterize the material as clean fill. The specific number of samples, depths, and analytical parameters are not provided in this source; complete data reside in the memo.
- **No exceedances** documented in the approval email; all results were within acceptable thresholds.

### Regulatory status
- **Applicable standard**: Barium Containing Soil clean cap criteria – concentration thresholds established for the SR 132 project to allow use of barium-impacted soil as a clean cap.
- **Agencies**: DTSC (lead oversight) and Water Board (concurrence). Approval was communicated by Dean Wright (DTSC) with Kyle Cockerham (Water Board) copied.
- **Compliance**: The Carpenter Shoofly material meets the criteria and is approved for use as clean fill. No compliance deficiencies or conditions were imposed in the approval email.

### Open items
No unresolved issues or required future actions are documented in the source. The memo is accepted as final; the material may be used without further agency notification unless the criteria change or other project constraints arise.
