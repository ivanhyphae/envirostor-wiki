## Definition

**Food Machinery And Chemical Corporation** (FMC) is an American chemical manufacturing company that originated from a food-processing machinery business. It has evolved into a global agricultural sciences, health, and nutrition company. The term often refers to the corporation itself or its legacy operations, including historical environmental liabilities. In the context of the provided source material, FMC is associated with a 2012 California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) groundwater monitoring report, suggesting involvement in regulatory environmental monitoring, likely at a former or current industrial site.

## How it works

FMC operates through a diversified business model:

- **Agricultural Sciences**: Develops and sells insecticides, herbicides, fungicides, and plant health products. R&D focuses on novel active ingredients and formulation technologies.
- **Health and Nutrition**: Produces food ingredients, pharmaceutical excipients, and dietary supplements, leveraging extraction and processing technologies.
- **Legacy Industrial Operations**: Historically manufactured chemicals such as lithium, phosphorus derivatives, and hydrogen peroxide; many of these sites are now remediated or sold, but may be subject to ongoing groundwater monitoring as seen in the Caltrans report.

The corporation's financial structure involves separate operating segments, with innovation driven by patent portfolios and regulatory approvals.

## Variants

- **FMC Agricultural Solutions** – primary division for crop protection and plant health.
- **FMC Health and Nutrition** – acquired business units focusing on food and ingredient markets.
- **Former Divisions**: Include the original Food Machinery business (1948–1998), lithium production (sold 2015), and industrial chemical operations (divested or closed). The “Food Machinery And Chemical Corporation” name is historical; the modern company is simply “FMC Corporation.”

## Trade-offs

- **Benefits**: Provides high‑yield crop protection chemicals that support global food production; produces essential ingredients for pharmaceuticals and processed foods.
- **Limitations/Controversies**: Historical operations have left legacy contamination (e.g., chlorinated solvents, pesticides) at former manufacturing sites, leading to extensive groundwater remediation programs and legal liabilities. The Caltrans report indicates active regulatory oversight.
- **Risk**: Dependence on pesticide registration and susceptibility to regulatory bans (e.g., organophosphates) affects product portfolio.
- **Costs**: Environmental cleanup expenses and litigation can impact profitability and public perception.
