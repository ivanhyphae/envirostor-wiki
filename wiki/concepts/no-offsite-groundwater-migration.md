## Overview

**No Offsite Groundwater Migration** is a regulatory determination that barium and lead from the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (SR 132, Stanislaus County) have not migrated beyond the site boundary via groundwater. This finding is documented through multiple lines of evidence reviewed by DTSC and the Central Valley RWQCB, culminating in approval to discontinue groundwater monitoring and decommission remaining wells. The determination is the basis for site closure under the Remedial Action Plan, confining liability to the encapsulation footprint and avoiding offsite groundwater cleanup obligations.

## Site record

The groundwater monitoring network consisted of ten wells installed in 2012 (MW-1 through MW-10) to monitor potential metals leaching from three soil stockpiles contaminated with barium since the 1960s. Sampling was performed by Geocon Consultants, Inc. and later Stantec Consulting Services Inc.

**Regulatory concurrence timeline:**

- **May 3, 2024** — DTSC, in consultation with RWQCB, formally approved the *Updated Comparative Evaluation of Groundwater Data* (February 26, 2024, prepared by Geocon). The letter states both agencies "agree that the data indicates the encapsulated soil is not impacting groundwater and that it will not impact groundwater in the future." The Evaluation Report noted consistency with an earlier 2014 evaluation and that recent encapsulation of soil beneath the Express Way supported the conclusion.

- **January 30, 2026** — DTSC conditionally approved the Well Destruction Completion Report and approved the Storm Water and Surface Water Monitoring Report (FY24/25). DTSC explicitly stated that "after years of data supporting no metals were leaching into the groundwater from the stockpiles" both DTSC and RWQCB agreed to decommissioning.

**Well decommissioning:**

- Six of ten wells were decommissioned in 2019 to allow highway construction.
- The remaining four wells (MW-4, MW-6, MW-9, MW-10) were decommissioned per the 2024 DTSC approval. The Well Destruction Report (June 18, 2025, Geocon) documented the process. DTSC's January 30, 2026 approval required revision to include a table of estimated vs. actual grout volumes for each well as a performance metric.

**Surface water monitoring:**

- The Storm Water Sampling Report (FY24/25) evaluated containment system integrity during qualifying rain events. DTSC's January 2026 letter confirms "all results were within reporting limits."

**Analytical history:**

Groundwater monitoring from 2012 through 2024 showed:
- No metals (barium, lead) exceeded California MCLs or DTSC human health screening levels in offsite-designated wells.
- Barium concentrations in all wells remained below the MCL of 1,000 µg/L.
- All exceedances were confined to on-site wells within the stockpile boundary; downgradient wells showed no statistically significant increase above background.
- The 2023 final groundwater monitoring report (Stantec, March 8, 2023) incorporated data through November/December 2022 and March 2023, confirming declining trends and absence of offsite migration.

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable standards**: California MCLs (barium 1,000 µg/L), DTSC human health screening levels, site-specific background thresholds.
- **Agencies**: California DTSC (lead) and Central Valley RWQCB (oversight).
- **Compliance status**: Full compliance. No enforcement actions related to offsite migration. Groundwater monitoring has been discontinued. Remaining wells decommissioned (2025), subject to final grout-volume documentation.

## Open items

- **Well destruction completion**: DTSC's January 30, 2026 conditional approval requires revision of the Well Destruction Report to include a table comparing estimated vs. actual grout volumes for each well. This is a one-time documentation condition.
- **Ongoing surface water monitoring**: Storm water/surface water monitoring continues per the Remedial Action Plan to verify encapsulation integrity.
