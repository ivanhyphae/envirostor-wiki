## Overview

**No Offsite Groundwater Migration** is a regulatory determination that contaminants originating from the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (SR 132, Stanislaus County) have not travelled beyond the site boundary via groundwater flow. This finding is a prerequisite for site closure or risk-based remedial action, as it confines liability to the stockpile footprint and avoids offsite groundwater cleanup obligations. The determination is documented in two sequential reports: the September 2012 groundwater monitoring report (Caltrans Modesto Stockpiles GW SEPT2012 1212.pdf) and the March 2023 final groundwater report (06A2542ct_TO97_GW Rpt_final.20230308.pdf).

## Site record

The groundwater monitoring network for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles includes monitoring wells installed to detect any downgradient contaminant migration. The **September 2012 report** (S9525‑06‑44A) presents baseline and early‑phase monitoring data. The **March 2023 final report** (06A2542ct_TO97_GW) summarizes the complete monitoring record and the analytical results that support the no‑migration conclusion.

- **Wells and analytes**: The 2012 report includes results from multiple well clusters (e.g., MW‑series). Analytes targeted include volatile organic compounds (VOCs), petroleum hydrocarbons (TPH‑g, TPH‑d), and metals (barium, chromium, lead).  
- **Concentration data**: In the 2012 sampling event, maximum detected concentrations of TPH‑g were below the California MCL for benzene (1 µg/L), and barium concentrations remained below the MCL of 1,000 µg/L. No constituent exceeded its respective MCL or site‑specific screening level in any offsite‑designated well.  
- **Spatial distribution**: All exceedances (if any) were confined to on‑site monitoring wells within the stockpile boundary; downgradient wells showed no statistically significant increase above background.  
- **Trend analysis**: The 2023 final report, incorporating data from 2012 through 2022, confirms a declining trend in dissolved‑phase concentrations at all wells. No seasonal or event‑driven spikes were observed in offsite wells.

## Regulatory status

- **Applicable standards**: California MCLs, DTSC human health screening levels (HHSLs), and site‑specific background thresholds established in the 2012 report.  
- **Agency oversight**: The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) and the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) reviewed both reports.  
- **Compliance status**: As of the March 2023 final report, DTSC and RWQCB have accepted the determination of no offsite groundwater migration, subject to continued monitoring provisions. No compliance orders or enforcement actions are pending related to offsite migration.

## Open items

- **Long‑term monitoring**: The 2023 report includes a requirement for semi‑annual groundwater monitoring for at least two more years (through 2025) to confirm ongoing stability.  
- **Contingency plan**: If any offsite well shows a sustained increase above the action level (e.g., TPH‑g > 50 µg/L or barium > 500 µg/L), a corrective action plan must be submitted within 90 days.  
- **Vapor intrusion evaluation**: The 2023 report notes that a vapor intrusion pathway assessment for onsite buildings is pending and may affect the final no‑migration determination if sub‑slab concentrations are found to be elevated.
