## Definition

**Basin 5 Clean Cap Borrow Area** is a designated source area for obtaining **clean fill material** used in the construction or maintenance of a **clean cap** over contaminated or otherwise unsuitable sediments and soils in Basin 5. In this context, “borrow area” means a location from which suitable material is excavated and transported to another area where it is needed for engineering or environmental capping work.

The term appears to refer to a project-specific material source associated with the Basin 5 clean fill / cap work documented in the technical memorandum and referenced in the interim RACR appendices. The borrow area is intended to supply material that meets cleanliness and placement requirements for cap construction, rather than functioning as a disposal site.

## How it works

A clean cap borrow area supports cap construction by providing a controlled source of material with the physical and chemical properties needed for a protective cover layer. The general workflow is:

1. **Material identification and screening**
   - Candidate borrow material is evaluated to ensure it is sufficiently clean for use in a cap.
   - Evaluation typically considers contaminants of concern, grain size, moisture content, and suitability for handling and compaction.

2. **Excavation and stockpiling**
   - Approved material is excavated from the borrow area.
   - Material may be temporarily stockpiled and managed to prevent recontamination.

3. **Transport**
   - Clean fill is hauled from the borrow area to the Basin 5 cap construction area.

4. **Placement in cap lifts**
   - The material is placed in designed layers to achieve required thickness, grading, and surface performance.
   - Placement may be controlled to avoid mixing with underlying contaminated material.

5. **Quality assurance / quality control**
   - Sampling, inspection, and documentation confirm that the borrow material meets project specifications.
   - QA/QC also verifies that cap thickness and grading meet design intent.

The engineering purpose of the borrow area is to ensure a reliable supply of suitable fill so the cap can isolate contaminants, reduce exposure pathways, and support long-term stability.

## Variants

Possible variants or closely related implementations include:

- **On-site borrow area**: clean fill is sourced from within the project footprint.
- **Off-site borrow source**: fill is obtained from a separate approved location.
- **Temporary borrow area**: material is excavated from a site only during project construction and then reclaimed.
- **Graded cap fill**: borrow material selected to create a specific permeability or drainage profile.
- **Armored or surface-cap variant**: clean fill may be combined with coarser material, erosion protection, or vegetation support depending on design needs.

In project documentation, the precise configuration can vary based on availability of material, cleanup objectives, and environmental constraints.

## Trade-offs

Key trade-offs and considerations include:

- **Material cleanliness vs. availability**
  - The cleaner the material must be, the fewer borrow sources may qualify.
  - Tight acceptance criteria can increase cost and logistics complexity.

- **Excavation impact vs. benefit**
  - Borrow areas can disturb existing soils, habitats, or infrastructure.
  - This disturbance must be balanced against the need to build an effective cap.

- **Transport distance**
  - Longer haul distances raise cost, emissions, and project duration.

- **Geotechnical suitability**
  - Material must not only be clean, but also workable, stable, and compatible with the cap design.
  - Poor gradation or excess fines can affect drainage, settlement, or erosion resistance.

- **Recontamination risk**
  - Clean fill can be compromised during handling, stockpiling, or transport if controls are weak.

- **Long-term performance**
  - The borrow material must support durable cap performance, including resisting erosion, settlement, and differential movement.
