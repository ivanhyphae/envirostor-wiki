## Definition

**Interim Phase 1** refers to the initial stage of a temporary or intermediate construction or remediation phase, specifically as documented in the context of the Caltrans Modesto Stockpile RDIP (Remedial Design Implementation Plan) and the Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE Wall Sampling Plan. It designates the first defined period of interim activities—typically encompassing site preparation, material handling, and initial structural or geotechnical work—that precedes a later, more permanent phase. In this specific context, it governs the early work associated with managing existing stockpiles and constructing Mechanically Stabilized Earth (MSE) retaining walls for containment or site stabilization.

## How it works

Interim Phase 1 functions as a sequenced, controlled work package within a larger project. Its operation follows a documented plan that typically includes:

1. **Site Preparation and Surveying**: Marking stockpile boundaries, establishing access roads, and setting up temporary erosion and sediment controls.
2. **Foundation and Subgrade Work**: Excavation, grading, and compaction of the subgrade beneath the proposed MSE wall footings and stockpile footprints. This includes verifying bearing capacity and meeting density specifications.
3. **MSE Wall Construction (Initial Lift)**: Placement of the first layers (lifts) of MSE wall panel alignment, geogrid reinforcement, and select granular backfill. Sampling of wall backfill is conducted per the sampling plan to confirm grain-size distribution and compaction.
4. **Stockpile Relocation and Processing**: Moving materials from existing Stockpiles 1 and 2 to staged areas, with screening, crushing, or blending as required by the RDIP. Sampling of stockpile material is performed to assess contaminant levels or geotechnical properties.
5. **Monitoring and Quality Control**: Implementation of field density tests (e.g., nuclear gauge), moisture content checks, and survey monitoring of MSE wall alignment and settlement. Data collection feeds into documentation for Phase 2 transition.
6. **Notification and Reporting**: Regular submission of progress reports, deviation logs, and test results to Caltrans or the overseeing agency. Interim Phase 1 ends only when all acceptance criteria (e.g., wall height milestones, stockpile tonnage moved, or sampling thresholds) are met.

The phase is explicitly temporary; it is designed to be completed before the onset of Interim Phase 2 or final construction.

## Variants

Based on the reference documents, several variants or common alternative implementations exist:

- **Phased Stockpile Access**: Stockpile 1 may be processed before Stockpile 2, or both may be worked simultaneously. This variant affects scheduling of MSE wall construction behind the active stockpile.
- **MSE Wall Type Variation**: Use of different panel types (e.g., precast concrete vs. wire-faced) or geogrid strengths. The sampling plan may specify testing frequencies that vary by wall segment.
- **Interim Stabilization Only**: In a reduced variant, Interim Phase 1 may omit full wall construction, using only temporary soil nail walls or gabion baskets to stabilize stockpiles until a later permanent wall is built.
- **Sampling Frequency Tiers**: The sampling plan may define tiered sampling protocols (e.g., routine vs. reduced frequency) depending on whether material is sourced from a known homogeneous zone or a heterogeneous blend.

## Trade-offs

| Consideration | Benefits | Limitations |
|---------------|----------|-------------|
| **Schedule Acceleration** | Allows site work to begin before final design is complete, reducing overall project duration. | Risks rework if Phase 1 assumptions (e.g., assumed material properties) prove inaccurate. |
| **Cost Control** | Funded as a discrete work package, enabling incremental budget allocation. | May require more mobilization/demobilization costs than a single continuous phase. |
| **Regulatory Compliance** | Interim phase design can be approved more quickly when scope is limited. | Must meet all environmental sampling and reporting requirements, which can be extensive for contaminated stockpiles. |
| **Geotechnical Risk** | Phased approach allows geotechnical surprises (e.g., unexpected soil bearing capacity) to be addressed before Phase 2. | Foundation failure or excessive settlement during Interim Phase 1 can delay the entire project. |
| **Sampling Thoroughness** | Early sampling establishes baseline conditions for later remedial decisions. | High initial sampling density increases up-front cost and laboratory turnaround times. |

Key trade-offs revolve around the balance between speed and certainty: Interim Phase 1 reduces front-end design time but introduces risk and potential duplication of effort.
