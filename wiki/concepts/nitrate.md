## Nitrate

### Overview
Nitrate is a regulated groundwater contaminant at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation site along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. Elevated nitrate concentrations are a documented constituent of the groundwater plume originating from the stockpiled soils. Nitrate serves as an indicator of potential impacts from historical soil stockpiling activities and is tracked as part of the site’s ongoing groundwater monitoring program.

### Site Record
Groundwater monitoring data from the September 2012 report (“Modesto Stockpiles GW SEPT2012 1212.pdf”) document nitrate as one of the chemical parameters evaluated in the monitoring well network. Specific concentration values, well identifiers, and sampling dates are not extractable from the provided source material due to PDF encryption and binary encoding. ### Regulatory Status
- **Applicable Standard:** California Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) for nitrate (as N) is 10 mg/L (10,000 µg/L); the MCL for nitrate + nitrite is also regulated.
- **Enforcing Agency:** California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) oversees the remediation and monitoring at the Modesto Stockpiles site. The Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB) is likely involved.
- **Compliance:** The September 2012 report was prepared for regulatory review. Whether nitrate concentrations at specific wells exceeded the MCL during that sampling event is not determinable from the source material provided.
