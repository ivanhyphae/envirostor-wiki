## Overview

**N Emerald Avenue** is a designated clean fill source site approved by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) for use in the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. The site was evaluated and approved to supply clean fill material for backfilling excavated areas during the removal of contaminated soil stockpiles (Project No. 04-1C260). Approval was based on analytical testing of soil samples collected from the site, documented in the *Hwy 132 Clean Fill Source Testing Exhibit.pdf* (dated June 24, 2020). The exact physical location (e.g., latitude/longitude, parcel number) is not specified in the source document, but the site is referenced as "N Emerald Avenue" in the context of Stanislaus County.

## Site record

- **Source document**: *Hwy 132 Clean Fill Source Testing Exhibit.pdf* (dated June 24, 2020), prepared for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles project.
- **Purpose**: The site was evaluated as a potential source of clean fill to backfill excavated areas at the SR 132 project site after removal of contaminated soil stockpiles.
- **Testing results**: The source document includes analytical data for soil samples collected from N Emerald Avenue. Specific contaminants tested include arsenic, barium, cadmium, chromium, lead, mercury, and selenium. Key results:
  - **Arsenic**: 4.2 mg/kg (below DTSC screening level of 12 mg/kg for residential direct exposure)
  - **Barium**: 76 mg/kg (below DTSC screening level of 5,200 mg/kg)
  - **Cadmium**: 1.2 mg/kg (below DTSC screening level of 1.7 mg/kg for residential direct exposure)
  - **Chromium (total)**: 15 mg/kg (below DTSC screening level of 100,000 mg/kg for hexavalent chromium)
  - **Lead**: 8.3 mg/kg (below DTSC screening level of 80 mg/kg for residential direct exposure)
  - **Mercury**: 0.05 mg/kg (below DTSC screening level of 23 mg/kg for elemental mercury)
  - **Selenium**: 0.21 mg/kg (below DTSC screening level of 390 mg/kg)
- **Sampling methodology**: The document does not specify the number of samples collected, depth intervals, or analytical methods (e.g., EPA methods), but references "composite samples" consistent with standard fill source evaluation protocols.

## Regulatory status

- **Approval agency**: DTSC, under the Caltrans-overseen remediation program for the Modesto Soil Stockpiles project.
- **Applicable standards**: DTSC's Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) screening levels for residential direct exposure (as cited in the source document). The source document does not reference California Title 22 hazardous waste thresholds or MCLs for groundwater, as the fill is intended for clean backfill in an upland soil remedy, not as a water source.
- **Compliance**: The N Emerald Avenue source passed all tested analytes against DTSC screening levels, with no exceedances reported. The source document states: "All results are below DTSC residential screening levels and the material is acceptable for use as clean fill."
