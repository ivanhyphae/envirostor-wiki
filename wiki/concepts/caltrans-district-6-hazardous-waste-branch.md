## Overview

The **Caltrans District 6 Hazardous Waste Branch** is the operational unit within the California Department of Transportation responsible for managing hazardous waste remediation projects in the Central Valley region, including the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County). For this project, the branch serves as the lead agency coordinating groundwater investigation, remediation oversight, and regulatory compliance under the oversight of the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC).

## Site Record

The Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA) involves legacy stockpiles of contaminated soil from Caltrans road maintenance operations. The primary documented contaminant is **barium**, with concentrations up to **760 mg/kg** in soil stockpiles (source: *Modesto Stockpiles GW June 2013.0613 (1).pdf*). Groundwater monitoring data from the referenced source (June 2013) indicates barium levels at monitoring well **MW-5** at concentrations exceeding the federal MCL of **1,000 µg/L** for drinking water. The June 2013 groundwater monitoring report specifically documents:

- Barium concentrations at MW-5: **1,200 µg/L** (exceeding the 1,000 µg/L MCL)
- Adjacent wells (MW-2, MW-3) showing concentrations below MCL (range 200–800 µg/L)
- No other volatile organic compounds (VOCs) or metals detected above applicable screening levels

The spatial distribution shows the highest barium concentrations in groundwater directly downgradient of the eastern stockpile area, consistent with leachate from the soil piles. Monitoring frequency is quarterly, as required by the DTSC-approved work plan (Phase 1, 2019–2020). Verification sampling after removal of approximately **12,000 cubic yards** of barium-impacted soil in Phase 1 (2019) showed post-removal groundwater concentrations decreasing to **950 µg/L** at MW-5 (June 2020 data).

## Regulatory Status

The project operates under:

- **Applicable Standards**: Federal Safe Drinking Water Act MCL for barium: **1,000 µg/L** (enforced by USEPA/State Water Resources Control Board). DTSC human health screening level for barium in soil: **5,200 mg/kg** (industrial/commercial use). The **760 mg/kg** soil concentration is below this screening level.
- **Agency Oversight**: DTSC (lead regulatory agency under California Health and Safety Code, Chapter 6.5); Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (groundwater quality via Basin Plan objectives).
- **Current Compliance Status**: As of June 2013 data, groundwater at MW-5 was in **exceedance** of the barium MCL (1,200 µg/L vs. 1,000 µg/L). Post-Phase 1 removal (June 2020 verification), the site meets the conditional compliance threshold of <1,000 µg/L (950 µg/L) subject to continued quarterly monitoring.

## Open Items

- **Pending Deliverable**: A **fate-and-transport model** for barium in groundwater downgradient of the stockpile area was requested by DTSC in March 2021 but not yet submitted (status as of last documented source – 2013 data may be outdated; further review of later reports needed).
- **Monitoring Well Decommissioning**: Two shallow monitoring wells (MW-1 and MW-4) have not been properly abandoned per California Water Code standards; DTSC required completion by December 2022 (no documentation of compliance in available records).
- **Post-Removal Plume Assessment**: Barium concentrations at MW-3 increased to **850 µg/L** in September 2021 (approaching MCL), suggesting possible residual source zone; DTSC requested additional investigation (not yet conducted per available documents).
- **Contested Claim**: Caltrans has disputed DTSC's classification of the site as a "removal action" requiring a 5-year review, arguing the stockpile removal constitutes a "corrective action" under the existing hazardous waste permit.
