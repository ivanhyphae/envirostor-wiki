## Definition

**Supplemental Site Investigation and Human Health Risk Assessment Update** is an additional round of environmental field investigation and risk evaluation performed after an initial Site Investigation and Human Health Risk Assessment have already been completed.

Its purpose is to:
- refine the understanding of contamination conditions at a site,
- collect missing or higher-quality data,
- update exposure assumptions and toxicity information,
- reassess whether contaminants in soil, groundwater, sediment, soil vapor, or other media pose unacceptable risks to human health, and
- support decisions about cleanup, land use controls, or further investigation.

In practice, this type of update is used when earlier investigation results are incomplete, when new data indicate a different contamination pattern, or when regulators require a more defensible assessment before finalizing site management decisions.

## How it works

A supplemental site investigation and HHRA update typically proceeds in stages:

1. **Review of prior data**
   - Existing sampling results, conceptual site models, and previous risk calculations are examined.
   - Gaps are identified, such as uncharacterized areas, insufficient vertical delineation, outdated analytical methods, or missing exposure pathways.

2. **Supplemental field investigation**
   - Additional environmental sampling is conducted to fill those gaps.
   - Common media include:
     - surface soil and subsurface soil,
     - groundwater,
     - soil vapor or indoor air,
     - sediment and surface water,
     - construction materials or fill, where relevant.
   - Sampling locations are often targeted based on prior results, suspected source areas, and likely human exposure points.

3. **Data validation and interpretation**
   - Laboratory results are checked for quality assurance/quality control.
   - Data are compared with background concentrations, screening levels, and previous findings.
   - Contaminant distribution is evaluated horizontally and vertically.

4. **Update of the conceptual site model**
   - The Conceptual Site Model is revised to reflect current knowledge of:
     - contaminant sources,
     - transport mechanisms,
     - receptors,
     - exposure routes,
     - and site-specific conditions such as land use or hydrogeology.

5. **Human health risk reassessment**
   - The Human Health Risk Assessment is updated using the new dataset.
   - This usually involves:
     - identifying chemicals of potential concern,
     - selecting exposure scenarios for current and future land uses,
     - estimating exposure doses,
     - applying toxicity values,
     - calculating cancer and noncancer risk metrics,
     - and comparing results to regulatory risk criteria.

6. **Decision support**
   - Findings are used to determine whether:
     - no further action is warranted,
     - additional focused sampling is needed,
     - remediation is required,
     - or institutional controls and monitoring should be implemented.

The technical goal is not simply to add more data, but to produce a more reliable and defensible assessment of actual human health risk under realistic site conditions.

## Variants

Common variants and closely related approaches include:

- **Targeted supplemental investigation**  
  Focused sampling in a specific area, depth, or medium where uncertainties remain.

- **Delineation investigation**  
  Sampling designed to define the lateral and/or vertical extent of contamination.

- **Exposure-pathway-specific update**  
  A risk update centered on one pathway, such as soil ingestion, vapor intrusion, or groundwater ingestion.

- **Revised HHRA after remedial action**  
  A post-remediation reassessment to verify that residual contamination no longer presents unacceptable risk.

- **Iterative phased investigation**  
  A staged process in which each investigation round informs the next until the site model is sufficiently complete.

- **Screening-level vs. refined risk assessment**  
  Some updates rely on conservative screening values; others use site-specific exposure parameters and more detailed modeling.

## Trade-offs

Key considerations and limitations include:

- **Improved accuracy vs. higher cost**  
  Supplemental work increases confidence in the risk conclusions, but it requires additional sampling, analysis, and technical review.

- **More data vs. schedule delays**  
  Better delineation often extends project timelines.

- **Reduced uncertainty vs. diminishing returns**  
  After a point, additional samples may add little value if the contamination pattern is already well understood.

- **Site-specific realism vs. model complexity**  
  Refining the HHRA can improve relevance, but more complex assumptions can make the results harder to communicate and defend.

- **Protectiveness vs. conservatism**  
  Risk assessments intentionally err on the side of caution, but overly conservative assumptions may overstate actual risk and lead to unnecessary action.

- **Regulatory alignment**  
  The update must remain consistent with applicable guidance, cleanup criteria, and acceptable risk thresholds used by regulators.

- **Data quality dependence**  
  The credibility of the update depends on sample representativeness, analytical sensitivity, and a sound conceptual site model.
