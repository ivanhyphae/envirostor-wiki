## Bent 2 Area

**Confidence: high**

### Overview
The Bent 2 Area is an excavation zone along State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California, from which soils were excavated during the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project. The primary documented significance of this area is that the excavated soils were proposed for reuse as clean cap material on the project site, but analytical results demonstrated they exceeded the background criteria established for that purpose.

### Site record
A Technical Memorandum (Tech Memo) dated October 30, 2020, titled "Characterization of Excavated Bent 2 Soils for use as Clean Cap Material," was submitted by Richard Stewart (Caltrans) to DTSC and the Water Board on November 2, 2020. The memo documented the characterization of soils from the Bent 2 column excavation.

According to the November 2, 2020 email from Richard Stewart, the analytical results for the Bent 2 soils "exceed the background criteria for use of the soil as clean cap cover material." The specific exceedances and chemical constituents are not detailed in the available correspondence, but the results failed to meet the Clean Cap Criteria established in the April 9, 2020 Variance Request approved by DTSC.

### Regulatory status
On November 6, 2020, Dean Wright (DTSC Project Manager) issued formal acceptance of the Tech Memo via email. DTSC and the Water Board (Steven Meeks) agreed that while the soil did not meet clean cap criteria, it could be placed on top of Stockpile 2 at a thickness of **6 inches or less**, provided it was covered with:
- Clean base material
- The asphalt/concrete used to construct the roadway

This conditional approval was intended to allow efficient use of the Bent 2 soil while not jeopardizing the integrity of the remedy. The agencies emphasized that due to the project schedule, the Bent 2 soils might be temporarily exposed to the elements during the 2020–2021 winter months. Two specific conditions were attached:
1. The rainy season surface water sampling effort (as outlined in the Revised Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan [SWPPP] Amendment dated October 22, 2020) must be followed strictly.
2. Erosion control measures in the Revised SWPPP Amendment must be followed strictly.

DTSC's official acceptance covered both the October 30, 2020 Tech Memo and the October 22, 2020 Revised SWPPP Amendment.

### Open items

- It is not documented whether the Bent 2 soils were ultimately placed on Stockpile 2 as approved, or whether the temporary exposure conditions during winter resulted in any off-site impacts.
