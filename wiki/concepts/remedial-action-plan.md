## Remedial Action Plan – Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (SR 132, Stanislaus County)

### Overview
The Remedial Action Plan (RAP) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles Site documents the selected remedy for petroleum‑impacted soils and soil‑gas contamination at the former Stockpile Storage Area adjacent to State Route 132. The RAP was approved by the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) on March 14, 2018, which selected **Alternative 4 – Containment** as the final remedy. The RAP satisfies California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) requirements; an Environmental Impact Report/Environmental Assessment (EIR/EA) was certified with a Finding of No Significant Impact under NEPA. DTSC independently determined that implementation of the specified engineering and institutional controls will result in no significant environmental effect.

### Site record
- **Approval date:** March 14, 2018 (DTSC approval letter, document ID not given in source).
- **Selected remedy:** Alternative 4 (Containment). Construction elements include:
  - Retaining walls and bridge abutments placed over stockpiles.
  - Roadway constructed over stockpiles.
  - Unpaved areas covered with clean fill.
- **Phased implementation:**
  - Phase 1 – 2019 to 2020.
  - Phase 2 – 2028 to 2029.
- **Approval conditions (from DTSC letter):**
  - A Remedial Design and Implementation Plan (RDIP) is required before each phase.
  - A start‑work notice (in both English and Spanish) must be provided at least two weeks before construction begins.
  - An Operations, Maintenance, and Monitoring Plan (OMA/OMP) is required after Phase 1 completion.
  - A [[land-use-covenant]] must be recorded.
  - Annual pavement inspection and groundwater monitoring must be performed.
  - A five‑year remedy evaluation is required.
- **Contamination status:** Contamination remains in place indefinitely; the remedy relies on containment rather than removal. Quantitative performance metrics (e.g., concentration targets, reduction endpoints) are not provided in the approval letter.

### Regulatory status
- **Lead regulatory agency:** DTSC
- **Co‑regulatory agency:** Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB)
- **Responsible party:** California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) – owner/operator.
- **Applicable standards:** Not explicitly listed in the approval letter; the remedy is designed to eliminate exposure pathways to human receptors and minimize surface water infiltration. Because contaminants remain on site, long‑term compliance depends on continued operation, maintenance, and monitoring (O&M) plus the land use covenant restricting future land use.
- **Current compliance status:** The RAP is approved and the remedy has been implemented under Phase 1. Effectiveness will be assessed through annual inspections, groundwater monitoring, and five‑year evaluations.

### Open items
- The OMA/OMP may require revision after Phase 2 (2028–2029).
- No quantitative performance criteria were established in the approval letter; performance standards will need to be defined in subsequent plans (RDIP, OMA/OMP) or maintained through the five‑year remedy evaluation process.
- Long‑term remedy reliability depends on sustained funding, periodic inspections, and covenant enforcement.
