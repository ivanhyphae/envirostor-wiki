## Definition

**Modesto Soil Stockpiles Well Installation And Groundwater Report June 2012** is a project-specific environmental and geotechnical report associated with the Modesto soil stockpiles site. It documents the installation of groundwater monitoring or observation wells and summarizes groundwater conditions as of June 2012. The report is likely intended to support site characterization, monitoring, and evaluation of potential contamination or migration pathways related to stockpiled soils.

## How it works

The report combines two related activities:

1. **Well installation**
   - A set of monitoring wells is installed at or near the stockpiles area.
   - Wells are typically screened across a target water-bearing zone so groundwater samples and water levels can be measured over time.
   - Installation usually includes boring, casing, screen placement, sand pack, annular sealing, and surface completion.
   - Construction details are recorded so the wells can be used for later sampling and water-level monitoring.

2. **Groundwater reporting**
   - After installation, the report summarizes groundwater findings for June 2012.
   - Typical contents include:
     - groundwater elevation measurements
     - depth to water
     - well construction details
     - lithologic logs or subsurface observations
     - field observations during drilling and development
     - possibly analytical results if sampling occurred
   - These data are used to interpret groundwater flow direction, hydraulic conditions, and whether site activities may affect groundwater quality.

In practice, the report functions as a technical record linking the physical well network to a snapshot of groundwater conditions at the time of investigation. It supports ongoing groundwater monitoring, monitoring well management, and environmental assessment.

## Variants

Possible variants of this type of document include:

- **Well installation report** — focused mainly on boring logs, construction methods, and completion details.
- **Groundwater monitoring report** — focused mainly on water levels, sampling results, and interpretation.
- **Combined installation and groundwater memo/report** — integrates both well construction and initial groundwater findings.
- **Quarterly or periodic groundwater reports** — repeated updates after the initial June 2012 baseline.
- **Site characterization reports** — broader studies that include wells as one component of subsurface investigation.

Alternative implementations of the same monitoring objective may use:

- temporary piezometers
- dedicated monitoring wells
- nested wells for different depth intervals
- direct-push sampling points for short-term investigation

## Trade-offs

Key considerations and limitations include:

- **Snapshot vs. long-term trend**
  - A June 2012 groundwater report captures conditions at a specific time, but groundwater levels and chemistry can vary seasonally and over longer periods.

- **Well placement sensitivity**
  - Results depend heavily on where wells were installed. Poor placement can miss plume boundaries, flow paths, or localized impacts.

- **Screen interval bias**
  - A well only reflects the water-bearing interval it screens; it may not represent conditions deeper or shallower in the aquifer system.

- **Construction quality**
  - Improper sealing, screen selection, or development can affect data quality and allow vertical cross-connection.

- **Interpretation uncertainty**
  - Groundwater flow direction and contamination risk can be difficult to determine from limited wells or sparse sampling.

- **Regulatory usefulness**
  - The report is valuable for compliance and documentation, but may need follow-up sampling or additional wells to support a full assessment.
