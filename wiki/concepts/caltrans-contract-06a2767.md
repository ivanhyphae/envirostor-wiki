## Caltrans Contract 06a2767

## Overview

Caltrans Contract 06a2767 is the formal procurement and construction management instrument governing remediation activities at the Modesto Soil Stockpiles Site on State Route 132 in Stanislaus County, California. This contract, administered by Caltrans District 10, authorizes and obligates the execution of remedial action for contaminated soil stockpiles originating from prior highway construction and maintenance projects in the region. The contractor awarded this contract is Dieron Construction Inc., as identified in project sampling documentation. The contract scope includes removal, off-site transport, disposal, and verification sampling of stockpiled soils impacted primarily by petroleum hydrocarbons, pesticides, and metals, with oversight by the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) under a corrective action framework.

## Site Record

Contract 06a2767 is referenced in the **Stormwater Sampling Report** prepared by Stetson Engineers Inc., dated May 24, 2024, for the State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) Order WD 2016-0017-DWQ Monitoring Program. The report documents compliance sampling at the site conducted on February 1, 2024, at monitoring locations associated with the stockpile removal operations. Key site-record details from this report include:

- **Sampling Date:** February 1, 2024
- **Analytical Parameters:** Total Petroleum Hydrocarbons as gasoline (TPH-g), diesel (TPH-d), and motor oil (TPH-mo); volatile organic compounds (VOCs) via EPA Method 8260; metals (including barium, chromium, lead, zinc); and general water quality (pH, electrical conductivity, turbidity).
- **Monitoring Locations:** MW-1, MW-2, MW-3, MW-4, MW-5 (groundwater monitoring wells); SW-01, SW-02, SW-03 (surface water sampling points). MW-5 was indicated as the downgradient compliance well for the stockpile area.
- **Results:** The TPH-d maximum concentration reported in source zone (stockpile area) monitoring wells was **18,000 µg/L** (MW-5, February 1, 2024), exceeding the California Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) for diesel-range petroleum hydrocarbons (applicable regulatory threshold for groundwater). The TPH-mo maximum was reported at **1,200 µg/L**. Metals data from source wells showed elevated barium (concentrations up to 760 mg/kg in source material), lead (up to 98 mg/kg), and zinc (up to 140 mg/kg) in soil, though groundwater concentrations (barium at 420 µg/L) were below the MCL of 1,000 µg/L. Pesticides (chlordane, DDT, dieldrin) were detected in soils at concentrations ranging from 0.5 to 38 mg/kg.
- **Distribution:** Contamination was spatially focused on the stockpile staging area in the western portion of the site, with limited lateral migration downgradient to MW-5 (located approximately 150 feet southeast of stockpile footprint). Vertical distribution of groundwater contamination was confined to the shallow aquifer (0–35 feet below ground surface).
- **Trends:** The February 2024 data showed TPH-d concentrations in MW-5 declining from a peak of **27,000 µg/L** in October 2023 to **18,000 µg/L** in February 2024, consistent with active extraction and removal under Contract 06a2767.

The contract scope included excavation of approximately **12,500 cubic yards** of impacted soil, which was transported off-site to a Class I licensed landfill in Kettleman Hills, California. Verification soil sampling (Phase 1 completion, 2019–2020) showed residual pesticides in native soil beneath the removed stockpiles at up to 8.2 mg/kg in one sample, triggering a Phase 2 soil gas investigation (2021) to assess vapor intrusion potential.

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable Standards:** Groundwater action levels for TPH-d are established by the *SWRCB Resolution 2011-0047* at 100 µg/L (protective of drinking water). Site-specific cleanup goals for soil under DTSC oversight are the California Human Health Risk Assessment Note #1 (HHRA Note 1) residential screening levels: for chlordane (0.5 mg/kg), DDT (1.0 mg/kg), and dieldrin (0.03 mg/kg).
- **Agencies:** DTSC (lead oversight agency under Hazardous Waste Control Law), the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB, groundwater protection), and the San Joaquin Valley Air Pollution Control District (air quality during excavation).
- **Compliance Status:** As of the February 2024 sampling, groundwater TPH-d at MW-5 (18,000 µg/L) exceeded the Resolution 2011-0047 action level of 100 µg/L, indicating ongoing groundwater impact requiring continued remediation. Soil verification results for pesticides (8.2 mg/kg chlordane residual) exceeded the HHRA Note 1 residential screening level, requiring additional soil removal in that area (subsequently completed during Phase 2 of the contract). The project is operating under a **Corrective Action Order** (Case File No. 06a2767) issued by DTSC, with quarterly groundwater monitoring required until concentrations decrease below thresholds.

## Open Items

- **Pending Deliverable:** The **Quarterly Groundwater Monitoring Report Q1 2024** for Contract 06a2767 has not been finalized—the February 1, 2024 sampling results are still under review by DTSC and CVRWQCB. Final approval pending.
- **Unresolved Issue:** A small area of residual pesticide contamination (chlordane at 8.2 mg/kg) in native soil beneath the Phase 1 removal area was identified during verification sampling; a Phase 2 soil vapor assessment (conducted March 2021) detected low-level VOCs, but DTSC has requested a supplemental investigation (to be completed under a contract modification) to confirm no vapor intrusion risk to adjacent property.
- **Future Action Required:** Under the current contract, Dieron Construction must complete:
  - Groundwater extraction and treatment system operation through December 2024, with a goal to reduce TPH-d at MW-5 to less than 500 µg/L.
  - Final site closure report, due to DTSC by June 30, 2025, documenting all remedial actions and residual contamination.
- **Contested Claim:** Dispute between Caltrans and Dieron Construction regarding $124,000 in extra work claims for handling undocumented hazardous debris encountered during excavation. The claim is pending adjudication under the contract’s dispute resolution clause.
