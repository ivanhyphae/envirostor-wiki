## Overview

RACR (Removal Action Completion Report) is the formal document submitted to the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) that describes the consolidation and capping activities completed for barium‑containing soils (BCS) as part of the Interim Phase 1 Construction effort at the State Route 132 West project in Modesto, Stanislaus County (Site Code 900259). The Draft Interim RACR was prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. on behalf of the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) and documents work performed under the approved Remedial Action Plan (RAP) and approved Interim Phase 1 Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP).

## Site Record

- **Document**: *Draft Removal Action Completion Report – Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, Interim Phase 1 State Route 132 West Project* (Draft Phase 1 RACR), submitted by Geocon to DTSC.
- **Acceptance**: DTSC, in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (RWQCB), accepted the Draft Phase 1 RACR on February 24, 2022, via a letter signed by Meredith Williams, Ph.D., Director of DTSC.
- **Remedial Action**: The Draft Phase 1 RACR describes the following completed work:
    - **Stockpile 3**: Excavated approximately 27,675 yd³ of BCS soil from the east side of State Route 99. The material was transported by truck and placed as embankment fill within the BCS Stockpile 1 containment zone.
    - **Stockpile 2 (western and eastern ends)**: Soil was removed using scrapers and placed directly within the containment zone of Stockpile 2 as embankment fill.
    - **Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2 southern slopes**: Soil removed to support construction of the new State Route 132 Expressway.
- **Verification Sampling**:
    - **Stockpile 3**: Twenty-nine (29) confirmation soil samples were collected from the bottom of the excavation and analyzed for barium and lead.
        - Three samples showed barium concentrations above the cleanup goal of 1,000 mg/kg.
        - Those three areas were deepened by approximately one foot (1 ft) and resampled.
        - Final confirmation samples showed remaining soil below cleanup goals for both barium (1,000 mg/kg) and lead (80 mg/kg).
    - **Stockpile 2 ends**: Eight (8) verification samples on the western end and twelve (12) on the eastern end were collected from native soil after contaminated material removal.
        - Initial results indicated four areas above the 1,000 mg/kg cleanup goal for barium.
        - Those four areas were deepened by an additional foot and resampled; all remaining soil in the western and eastern excavation areas were below the verification thresholds of 1,000 mg/kg for barium and 80 mg/kg for lead.
- **Timing**: The Draft Phase 1 RACR describes work completed through 2021 (submittal date appears to be prior to February 2022). The Interim Phase 1 construction effort is part of the larger State Route 132 West project.

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable Standards**: Cleanup goals for the project are:
    - Barium: 1,000 mg/kg
    - Lead: 80 mg/kg
- **Agencies**: The DTSC is the lead regulatory agency, with consultation from the Central Valley RWQCB.
- **Compliance Status**: As of the February 24, 2022 acceptance letter, DTSC accepted the Draft Phase 1 RACR, indicating that the described interim remedial actions (soil removal and consolidation) meet the approved work plans. No compliance issues were noted in the acceptance letter. The project remains subject to ongoing oversight for subsequent phases.

## Open Items

- **Phase 2 and subsequent phases**: The acceptance letter refers only to the Interim Phase 1 RACR. Additional remedial phases (e.g., for remaining Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2 areas not addressed in Phase 1) are expected but not documented in the available sources.
- **Long‑term monitoring**: The acceptance letter does not discuss post-remediation monitoring or maintenance requirements for the consolidated/capped BCS containment zones. These may be addressed in future RACR submissions or operation and maintenance plans.
