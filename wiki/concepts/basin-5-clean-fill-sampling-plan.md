# Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan

## Overview

The Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan is a site-specific document, submitted by Geocon on June 11, 2020, that defines the sampling and analytical protocol for soil used as clean fill material to cap the State Route 132 (SR 132) soil stockpiles in Stanislaus County, California. The plan was approved via email by the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) on June 12, 2020. It covers the proposed Basin 5 borrow area, located adjacent to and north of a previously tested SR 132 roadway cut section east of Carpenter Road. The plan relies on the precedent that similar non-impacted native formation conditions were established at that prior location, and it incorporates the analytical methodology and comparative background metal concentration tables from a previously submitted technical memorandum (the Carpenter technical memo) that was not re-submitted with this plan.

## Site Record

- **Document Date:** June 11, 2020 (submitted by John Juhrend, Geocon, to Richard Stewart, DOT)
- **Approval Date:** June 12, 2020 (via email from Dean Wright, DTSC)
- **Approving Agencies:** DTSC (Project Manager: Dean Wright, P.G.) and the California Waterboards (Steven Meeks, Waterboards)
- **Approval Basis:** The plan was reviewed and found to be consistent with previous borrow area sampling; DTSC and Waterboards issued no comments and provided the approval via email.
- **Geocon Representative:** John Juhrend, PE, CEG, CEM (Senior Engineer)
- **Sampling Frequency Basis:** The sampling plan’s frequency was justified by the location of Basin 5 being adjacent to and north of the previously tested SR 132 roadway cut section east of Carpenter Road, which had exposed non-impacted native formation. Geocon stated that similar conditions are anticipated at Basin 5.
- **Submitted Content:** The original submittal included only a map with sample locations. No technical memorandum with sampling methodology or comparative background metal concentration tables was included. Geocon assumed the previously submitted Carpenter technical memo was applicable to Basin 5.
- **Document ID (email thread):** RE: SR 132 Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable Standard:** The plan was reviewed against the previous borrow area sampling protocol for the area east of Carpenter Avenue. The approval email from DTSC confirms the plan is “consistent with the previous borrow area sampling.”
- **Regulatory/Enforcement Agencies:** DTSC (Dean Wright, P.G., Project Manager) and California Waterboards (Steven Meeks)
- **Compliance Status:** The plan was approved without conditions or comments as of June 12, 2020. The project is currently in compliance with the approved sampling plan. No further action is required on the plan itself unless future conditions at Basin 5 deviate from the assumed non-impacted native formation.

## Open Items

- **No Technical Memo Submitted:** A technical memorandum detailing sampling methodology and comparative background metal concentration tables was not included in the June 11, 2020 submittal. Geocon assumed the previously submitted Carpenter technical memo would apply. The regulatory file does not document whether a separate memo for Basin 5 is required or has been approved.
- **No Contingency for Deviation:** The plan does not document a contingency or revised sampling protocol if future sampling at Basin 5 reveals conditions that differ from the non-impacted native formation assumed. No mention of adaptive management or additional sampling triggers is present in the source documents.
