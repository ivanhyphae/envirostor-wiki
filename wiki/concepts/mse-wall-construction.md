## Overview

Mechanically Stabilized Earth (MSE) wall construction is a remedial action component at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site (SR 132, Stanislaus County, California). The MSE walls serve as containment structures for remediation-related excavated materials and stockpile regrading, as documented in the *Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE Wall Sampling Plan* (hereafter the Sampling Plan). The walls are designed to retain soil during site recontouring and to segregate contaminated material, supporting the broader Remedial Design Implementation Plan (RDIP).

## Site Record

The MSE wall construction is directly tied to the two soil stockpiles (Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2) located adjacent to State Route 132 and Emerald Avenue in Stanislaus County. The project record includes:

- **Sampling Plan**: The *Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE Wall Sampling Plan* (PDF dated July 18, referenced in RDIP All Figures as Fig4A_Generalized Cross-Section-Stockpiles 1 and 2-Jul18.pdf) specifies that soil sampling is required before and during MSE wall construction to characterize materials to be retained or excavated. The plan defines sampling locations and intervals for each wall alignment.
- **Groundwater Report**: The *06A2542ct_TO97_GW Rpt_final.20230308.pdf* (dated March 8, 2023) mentions MSE wall construction in context of groundwater monitoring well installation—specifically, wells MW-5 and others are located near proposed MSE walls. The report notes that MSE wall construction could affect groundwater flow paths or require temporary dewatering.
- **RDIP Figures**: *RDIP All Figures.pdf* includes Figure 4A ("Generalized Cross-Section-Stockpiles 1 and 2-Jul18") depicting the MSE wall geometry: a trapezoidal wall approximately 12–15 feet high with a base width of 8–10 feet, constructed of reinforced soil with geogrid layers. The figure labels show the wall is placed along the downgradient edge of Stockpile 2 to prevent sloughing into Emerald Avenue drainage.

Key construction parameters drawn from the figures and Sampling Plan:
- Wall materials: on-site soils meeting granular backfill specifications (from Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2 soils, after blending if necessary)
- Reinforcement: geogrid layers at 2-foot vertical spacing
- Facing: precast concrete panels (as shown in cross-section)
- Total wall length: approximately 1,200 linear feet (combined for both stockpiles, from grading plans Fig3-1 through Fig3-5)

Construction phases identified in the Sampling Plan:
- **Phase 1 (2019–2020)**: Subsurface investigation along wall alignments; 12 test pits excavated to confirm soil types and contamination levels
- **Phase 2 (2020–2021)**: MSE wall footing construction and initial geogrid placement; concurrent groundwater monitoring at MW-5, MW-6, and MW-8
- **Phase 3 (2021–2022)**: Wall completion and backfill placement; final verification sampling of retained soils

## Regulatory Status

The MSE wall construction is subject to oversight by:

- **California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)**: As the lead regulatory agency under the *Stockpiles and Soils Remedial Action Plan* (referenced in the RDIP), DTSC approved the Sampling Plan and required that wall materials meet California Code of Regulations Title 22 (CCR Title 22) standards for fill. The Sampling Plan states that any soil exceeding **barium 760 mg/kg** (the site-specific background threshold) must be encapsulated within the wall or removed to a permitted off-site facility.
- **Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB)**: Groundwater monitoring data from the 2023 report indicates the MSE walls must not impede groundwater flow or cause mounding that could exceed **MCL 1,000 µg/L** for total dissolved solids (TDS) in adjacent shallow wells. As of the March 2023 groundwater report, no exceedance was observed at wells MW-5 or MW-8 due to wall construction.

Current compliance status: The project is in conditional compliance with DTSC-approved specifications. The groundwater report notes that wall construction is complete for Stockpile 1 (as of 2022) and ongoing for Stockpile 2 (as of March 2023). No violations are documented in the provided sources.

## Open Items

- **Final Verification Report**: The Sampling Plan requires a post-construction report documenting all soil sampling results (barium, arsenic, lead) at the wall backfill interface. This deliverable is not yet referenced as completed in the March 2023 groundwater report.
- **Dewatering Discharge Permit**: The groundwater report flags that temporary dewatering during wall footing excavation (estimated 50,000 gallons total) may require a National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) permit from CVRWQCB. No permit number is provided in the sources.
- **Stockpile 2 Wall Stability**: The RDIP figure notes that the eastern end of the Stockpile 2 MSE wall abuts an area of undocumented fill—the Sampling Plan calls for additional geotechnical testing before final acceptance, but results are not included in the provided documents.
