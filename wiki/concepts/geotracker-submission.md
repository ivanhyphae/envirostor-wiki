## Overview

**Geotracker Submission** refers to the electronic transfer of analytical laboratory data to the State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) GeoTracker database as part of the [[caltrans-modesto-soil-stockpiles]] case (SR 132, Stanislaus County). This submission ensures public and regulatory access to site data in accordance with California’s statewide environmental data management requirements.

## Site Record

For the March 2018 soil and water sampling event at the Modesto Stockpiles, laboratory-prepared electronic data files were submitted to the GeoTracker database (accessible at http://geotracker.waterboards.ca.gov). The upload occurred on **April 11, 2018**, and was assigned **confirmation number 8392300546**. 

The submittal covered data from soil and water samples collected and analyzed during the March 2018 investigation, as documented in the project report *S1200-01-01 Modesto Stockpiles March 2018 SW Sampling_04.18*. The report itself was not uploaded to GeoTracker; only the electronic data files were transmitted. DTSC personnel were responsible for uploading the full written report to the EnviroStor database.

## Regulatory Status

GeoTracker is administered by the SWRCB and serves as the primary repository for environmental data at sites overseen by Regional Water Quality Control Boards. The April 11, 2018 submission satisfied project-specific data submittal requirements for that sampling event. The report’s subsequent upload to EnviroStor by DTSC staff addressed the parallel requirement under the Department of Toxic Substances Control’s database system.

## Open Items

No open items or pending submissions are documented in the source material for this specific submittal. The source confirms that the electronic data was successfully uploaded on April 11, 2018, and that DTSC was to handle the report upload separately. Any subsequent data submissions from later sampling events are not described in the referenced document.
