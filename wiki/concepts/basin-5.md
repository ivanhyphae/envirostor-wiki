## Basin 5

### Definition

**Basin 5** is a specific geographical and administrative unit within the California State Route 132 (SR 132) corridor project, designated for the placement, management, and environmental certification of clean fill material. It is subject to a dedicated sampling and testing plan to ensure that imported or on‐site fill meets regulatory standards for uncontaminated soil, as part of the broader site remediation and construction fill program.

### How it works

Basin 5 functions as a controlled fill area where only material classified as “clean fill” (i.e., soil and rock free of hazardous constituents above target levels) may be placed. The process is governed by the *Basin 5 Clean Fill Sampling Plan*, which specifies:

- **Sampling frequency and pattern** – Typically a grid or random stratified design to achieve statistical confidence that the material satisfies cleanup goals.
- **Analytical parameters** – Based on historical site contamination, likely including heavy metals, petroleum hydrocarbons, VOCs, and pesticides/herbicides.
- **Approval mechanism** – The plan must be reviewed and approved by regulatory agencies (e.g., the California Department of Toxic Substances Control or local oversight). The approval e‑mail (source file) confirms that the sampling plan meets required standards.
- **Documentation** – Results are compiled in a Remedial Action Completion Report (RACR) – see *Draft Interim RACR App D‑G* – which demonstrates that Basin 5 fill achieves the “clean fill” definition and can be used without further restriction.

Once sampling confirms compliance, fill material is placed and compacted per engineering specifications. Ongoing monitoring may be required if the basin is used as a long‐term storage or burial disposal site.

### Variants

- **Phased implementation** – Basin 5 may be completed in multiple lifts or stages, each with its own sampling event.
- **Comparison with other basins** – The SR 132 project likely includes Basin 1–4 (and possibly 6+), each with unique contamination histories and fill requirements. Basin 5 may represent a specific geographic subarea or a particular waste type (e.g., exclusively uncontaminated native soil vs. imported material).
- **Interim vs. final closure** – The *Draft Interim RACR* indicates that Basin 5 may have an interim status, meaning a later, more comprehensive report will confirm final closure.

### Trade‑offs

- **Cost vs. certainty** – A rigorous sampling plan reduces long‐term liability but increases upfront analytical and labor costs. The approval e‑mail suggests that regulators required additional scrutiny, implying a trade‑off between schedule and data quality.
- **Volume constraints** – The basin’s capacity is fixed; using clean fill instead of on‐site reuse may require importing material, increasing truck traffic and emissions.
- **Regulatory risk** – If sampling reveals unexpected contamination, the basin may need reclassification as a remediation unit, delaying construction and incurring remediation costs.
- **End use** – Clean fill can often be left in place, but if future land use changes, Basin 5’s contamination status may need reevaluation, creating potential future obligations.
