## Overview

**Pl3** is a designated surface water sample location at the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles site in Stanislaus County, California. It is situated along the southern edge of Stockpile 2, outside the Caltrans right-of-way, adjacent to the north terminus of Bennett Street. Pl3 serves as a monitoring point for stormwater runoff potentially originating from or migrating through the vicinity of Stockpile 2.

## Site Record

- **Location Context**: Pl3 is located at a puddle of water where Bennett Street intersects the alley behind Loletta Street, beyond a chainlink fence that encloses the south side of Stockpile 2 (see [[caltrans-modesto-soil-stockpiles]]). It is outside the Caltrans right-of-way.
- **Sampling History**: Surface water samples have been collected from Pl3 since at least April 2013 as part of an on-call monitoring program approved by DTSC and the Central Valley Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB).
- **Key Detection – March 22, 2018**: In the March 22, 2018 sampling event, sample Pl3 (collected at 13:00) contained elevated lead at a concentration of **46 micrograms per liter (µg/L)**. This exceeds the primary Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) for lead in drinking water (15 µg/L).
- **Other Metals (March 22, 2018)**: Manganese was detected at 370 µg/L, exceeding the secondary MCL of 50 µg/L. Barium was reported at 770 µg/L; calcium, magnesium, and potassium were also detected at elevated levels (see laboratory report for full suite). No other detected metals exceeded primary or secondary MCLs.
- **Historical Context – 2006**: An earlier surface water sampling event (March 2006) at the stockpiles found an isolated elevated barium concentration of **2,000 µg/L** in sample SW03, collected from the northwestern side of Stockpile 3. This was attributed to confined runoff within the Caltrans right-of-way. The 2018 event did not target SW03 but observed barium in Pl3 at 770 µg/L, below the primary MCL.
- **Observations**: During the March 22, 2018 event, no runoff was observed migrating offsite from the stockpile area. The interceptor berm at the base of the south side access ramp to Stockpile 2 (north end of Bennett Street) appeared to function as designed, directing runoff to remain within the stockpile boundary.

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable Thresholds**: The California MCL for lead (primary, drinking water) is 15 µg/L; for manganese (secondary, taste/odor) is 50 µg/L. The project's current surface water monitoring program is approved by DTSC and CVRWQCB.
- **Compliance Status**: Sample Pl3's lead concentration (46 µg/L) exceeded the primary MCL, but this sample was collected from a puddle outside the Caltrans right-of-way, and no migration of runoff offsite was observed during the event. The finding is considered an isolated detection; no action level was triggered for the stockpile area itself. The site remains under routine monitoring.

## Open Items

- **Future Sampling**: Caltrans plans to conduct additional surface water sampling during qualifying rain events in 2018. As of the March 2018 report, no further events had been documented.
- **Gate Valve Operation**: Sample location PL5 (at the drainage basin adjacent to Stockpile 3) could not be sampled in 2018 due to insufficient water. The gate valve there is only opened if the basin nears capacity that could jeopardize SR 99 northbound lanes; it was not opened during this event. Its status for future events is not specified in the record.
