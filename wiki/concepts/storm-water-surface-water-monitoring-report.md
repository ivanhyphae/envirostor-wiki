## Overview: Storm Water Surface Water Monitoring Report (FY24/25)

This article covers the *Storm Water and Surface Water Monitoring Report (FY24/25)* for the **Caltrans Encapsulated Soil Stockpiles** (Site Code 900259), located south of the intersection of State Route 99 and Kansas Avenue in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. The report documents surface water runoff sampling from the three soil stockpiles (Stockpiles 1, 2, and 3) that contain soil contaminated with barium since the 1960s. The report was prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc., submitted to DTSC on June 18, 2025, and approved by DTSC via letter dated January 30, 2026. Its purpose is to evaluate the integrity of the stockpile containment system during qualifying rain events, ensuring that metals (specifically barium) are not displaced into surface water.

## Site Record

- **Report by:** Geocon Consultants, Inc., on behalf of California Department of Transportation (Caltrans).
- **Submitted:** June 18, 2025.
- **Approved:** January 30, 2026, by Katherine M. Butler, MPH, Director, DTSC (via letter).
- **Approval included review by:** DTSC Geological Services Branch (GSB) and DTSC Human and Ecological Risk Office (HERO).
- **Monitoring context:** The report covers storm water sampling at the stockpile site. Results were **all within reporting limits** (no exceedances or detection of barium above reporting limits in surface water samples). See **Regulatory Status** for standards applied.
- **Historical note:** The three stockpiles were originally created to contain barium-contaminated soil. In 2020, during Phase I of the Remedial Action Plan (RAP) implementation, soil from Stockpile 3 was moved to the structural section of Stockpiles 1 and 2. Stockpiles 1 and 2 remain for Phase II of the RAP (planned 4-lane highway, State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway).
- **Related groundwater well decommissioning:** Ten groundwater monitoring wells (installed 2012) were installed to monitor for potential contamination migration to groundwater. Six were decommissioned in 2019; the remaining four were decommissioned in the *Well Destruction Completion Report* (also June 18, 2025). The storm water report is separate from the well destruction report (see Open Items).

## Regulatory Status

- **Applicable standards:** The report does not specify the exact analytical methods or thresholds. The DTSC approval letter states results were **"all within reporting limits"** — this implies comparison to standard laboratory reporting limits (e.g., for barium in surface water at µg/L level), not to MCLs (which apply to groundwater, not surface water). The site is under DTSC oversight under the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (Site Code 900259).
- **Agencies:** DTSC (primary), with Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB) having reviewed historical groundwater monitoring data before agreeing to well decommissioning.
- **Compliance status:** The storm water monitoring report is **approved** (no conditions). The site is in compliance with respect to storm water monitoring for FY24/25.

## Open Items

- **None for this report.** The report is fully approved. However, the companion *Well Destruction Completion Report* (June 18, 2025) has a **conditional approval** requiring:
    - Add a table showing **estimated volume of grout needed** to decommission each well and **actual volume of grout used**. This data is a performance metric for well abandonment.
    - Submit digital copy of final version with revision to DTSC project manager **Arielle McLeskey** within **30 calendar days** of the approval letter (i.e., by March 1, 2026).
    - After approval, mail hard copies to DTSC (8800 Cal Center Drive, Sacramento).
