## Human Health Risk Assessment Update

### Overview

The Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) Update is a site-specific risk evaluation prepared for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles (State Route 132/99, Stanislaus County, California). It was developed by Geocon Consultants Inc. (Geocon) under contract to the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) as part of the remedial planning process for soil stockpiles along the proposed State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway corridor. The HHRA Update revised an earlier draft (December 17, 2012) in response to comments from the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC). DTSC, in consultation with the Regional Water Quality Control Board, Central Valley Region (RWQCB), formally concurred with the final HHRA Update (dated March 1, 2013) via a letter on April 4, 2013.

The purpose of the update was to reassess potential human health risks from contaminants in the soil stockpiles, incorporating data from the concurrent Supplemental Site Investigation (SSI) (Geocon, March 1, 2013). The HHRA Update supports the selection and finalization of a remedy documented in the [[rap]] (RAP) for management of hazardous substances in the stockpiles. The DTSC concurrence letter (April 4, 2013) serves as the principal approval record for this assessment in the project file.

### Site Record

- **Document:** “Human Health Risk Assessment (HHRA) Update, Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles, State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project, Stanislaus County, California” (Geocon, March 1, 2013).
- **Draft version:** Dated December 17, 2012; submitted to DTSC for review.
- **DTSC comments:** Issued February 15, 2013; addressed in Geocon’s “Response to DTSC Comments” (March 1, 2013), which also revised the SSI and HHRA Update.
- **Agency action:** DTSC concurrence letter from Randy S. Adams, C.E.G., Senior Engineering Geologist, Brownfields and Environmental Restoration Program, dated April 4, 2013. The letter states: “DTSC concurs with the reports titled ‘SSI …’ and ‘HHRA Update …’ (Geocon, March 1, 2013).”
- **Regulatory oversight:** Conducted under an Interagency Agreement and Task Order between Caltrans and DTSC, with consultation from RWQCB.
- **Supporting data:** The HHRA Update relied on additional characterization data collected in late September 2012 per the approved “Final Supplemental Site Characterization Workplan” (Geocon, September 18, 2012). The workplan was approved by DTSC on September 20, 2012 (per the April 4, 2013 letter).

No specific contaminant concentrations, risk values, or exposure scenarios are documented in the source letter; the HHRA Update report itself would contain those details.

### Regulatory Status

- **Applicable standards:** The HHRA Update was prepared to evaluate risks from hazardous substances in the soil stockpiles, consistent with DTSC’s human health risk assessment guidance. No specific threshold (e.g., cancer risk level, hazard index) is mentioned in the concurrence letter.
- **Agency approval:** DTSC (with RWQCB consultation) concurred with the HHRA Update as of April 4, 2013. This concurrence indicates the update satisfied regulatory requirements for informing the RAP.
- **Current compliance:** The project status (as of the date of the letter) was in the remedial planning phase; the HHRA Update was an approved component. No subsequent modifications or compliance issues are noted in the source.

### Open Items

Based on the source letter, no unresolved issues were identified for the HHRA Update at the time of DTSC concurrence. However, the letter does not address any future deliverables or long-term monitoring. Any pending items would be documented in the RAP or subsequent correspondence.
