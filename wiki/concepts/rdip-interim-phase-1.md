## Overview

The **Remedial Design Implementation Plan – Interim Phase 1 (RDIP-Interim Phase 1)** is the regulatory-approved work plan governing the first construction phase of the State Route 132 West Freeway/Expressway Project as it relates to remediation of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles. Prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. on behalf of Caltrans and dated January 30, 2019, the RDIP-Interim Phase 1 describes the removal and re‑deposition of a portion of the stockpiles to support highway construction, and details safety measures, sampling protocols, and post‑construction monitoring. The plan was approved by the Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) on April 3, 2019, in consultation with the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB). Following contract award, a Post Construction Contract Award Errata (undated, but subsequent to the April 3, 2019 approval) updated the RDIP to reflect changes in responsible parties, formally removing Geocon as the consultant identified to perform the work and confirming Caltrans as the responsible party for implementation.

## Site Record

- **Document Dates and Approvals:**
  - The RDIP-Interim Phase 1 was submitted to DTSC on **January 30, 2019**.
  - DTSC issued **Final Approval** on **April 3, 2019** in a letter signed by Dean Wright, PG (Project Manager, DTSC Site Mitigation and Restoration Program). The letter states that DTSC, in consultation with CVRWQCB, reviewed the RDIP-Interim Phase 1 and “have no additional comments” and that the plan **is approved**.
  - Also reviewed concurrently was the DTSC March 26, 2019 comment letter on the “City of Modesto Book 2 of 3 100% Special Provisions” document; a revised version addressing those comments was received March 28, 2019 and was found acceptable.

- **Scope of Interim Phase 1:**
  - The RDIP-Interim Phase 1 covers the removal and re‑deposition of **a portion** of the Modesto Soil Stockpiles.
  - Activities include: barium-containing soil excavation, clean fill placement and capping, verification soil sampling, clean soil import source sampling, worker zone and perimeter air monitoring, and periodic stormwater monitoring.
  - The plan includes excerpts of the **100% design plans** for the project.

- **Regulatory Basis:**
  - The RDIP was prepared pursuant to a previously approved **Remedial Action Plan (RAP)** for mitigation of the stockpiles.
  - Implementation is under regulatory oversight by DTSC, in cooperation with CVRWQCB.

- **Post-Contract Award Errata:**
  - The Errata document updates Section **2.0 Project Organization** and Section **2.1 Project Team Responsibilities**.
  - Key change: **Geocon Consultants, Inc.** is removed as the consultant identified to perform the work. **Caltrans** is confirmed as the responsible party to ensure work is performed in accordance with the RDIP.
  - Identified project team members after errata: 
    - Site Owner/Responsible Party: **State of California/Caltrans**
    - Regulatory Agencies: **DTSC** and **CVRWQCB**
    - SR-132 West Project Proponent: **City of Modesto/Stanislaus Council of Governments**
    - Project Proponent’s Civil Engineer: **Dokken Engineering**
    - Project Proponent’s Construction Manager: **WSP, USA**
    - Contractor: **Bay Cities Paving and Grading, Inc.**

## Regulatory Status

- **Approving Agencies:** DTSC (lead), in consultation with CVRWQCB.
- **Approval Date:** April 3, 2019 (Final Approval Letter).
- **Standards:** The RDIP-Interim Phase 1 implements the “containment alternative” from the previously approved RAP. Specific action levels, MCLs, or screening levels are not enumerated in the approval letter or errata; these would be contained within the RDIP document and the 100% design specifications.
- **Current Compliance Status:** Not explicitly stated in the sources. The approval is final, and no further iterative approvals from DTSC are documented in the provided materials. The Errata confirms the project moved to construction with a defined contractor (Bay Cities Paving and Grading, Inc.) and management team.
