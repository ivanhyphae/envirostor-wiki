## Overview

The Mechanically Stabilized Earth (MSE) Wall is a structural retaining wall system constructed as part of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA). In this project, the MSE wall footing excavation generated soil that required characterization for potential reuse as fill material within the Stockpile 1 and Stockpile 2 containment zones. The regulatory decision process for this material is documented in multiple technical memos and e-mail acceptances from March–April 2020.

## Site Record

### Sampling Plan and Execution

- **March 31, 2020**: John Juhrend (Geocon) submitted a sampling plan to Richard Stewart (Caltrans) for DTSC and Water Board review. The plan proposed **10 sampling locations** at **approximately 200-foot intervals** along the Stockpiles 1 and 2 MSE wall footing excavation. Samples were to be collected as **discrete 1-foot-interval samples** to the planned excavation depth of **~4–5 feet**.
- **April 2, 2020**: Dean Wright (DTSC) issued **e-mail acceptance** of the sampling plan. No comments were provided by DTSC or the Water Board.
- **Sampling targets**: At each location, borings or trenches were to be sampled from the **mid-point of the wall footing excavation**. Each discrete sample was analyzed for **barium** and **lead** under **48-hour turnaround**.
- **Analytical results (Technical Memo, April 6, 2020)**: The **95% Upper Confidence Limit** of the sampled soil exceeded the **maximum site-specific background of 120 mg/kg** for barium.

### Regulatory Determination

- **April 6, 2020**: The Technical Memo proposed the material could be used as **Stockpile 1 and 2 capping material** (i.e., clean cover).
- **April 7, 2020**: DTSC and Water Board responded with **conditional acceptance**:
  - **Disagreed** with using MSE wall footing soil as capping material due to the **95% UCL exceeding background**.
  - **Required** that the material be **placed within the Stockpiles 1 and 2 fill containment zones** or used as **general bridge embankment fill**.
  - **Noted** the material falls **outside the designated borrow area** described in the Remedial Design Implementation Plan.

### Screening Criteria Applied

| Parameter | Screening Level | Source |

## Regulatory Status

**Compliance condition**: The material is **not approved for use as clean capping material** over Stockpiles 1 and 2. It may be used as:
- Fill within the **Stockpile 1 and 2 containment zones** (i.e., beneath the cap)
- **General bridge embankment fill**

The conditional acceptance was issued via **e-mail April 7, 2020**, and constitutes the **official acceptance** of the Technical Memo by DTSC.

## Open Items

- **Stockpile 2 BCS (Background Concentration Soil) south slope**: Once the Stockpile 2 BCS southerly slope material is removed, **additional wall footing excavation sampling** will be completed in that area. The April 6 Technical Memo notes: "Once the Stockpile 2 BCS southerly slope material is removed we will complete the wall footing excavation sampling in that area." This sampling is **not yet documented** in the provided sources.
- **Pending closure**: No final determination for the **Stockpile 2 MSE wall footing** material has been made in the record. The April 2020 acceptance applies only to **Stockpile 1** wall footing material.
