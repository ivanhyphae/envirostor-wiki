## Overview
Caltrans District 10 is the administrative division of the California Department of Transportation responsible for the state highway system in Stanislaus, San Joaquin, and Alpine counties. In the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project (State Route 132, Stanislaus County), District 10 serves as both the project proponent and the responsible party for managing soil stockpiles that have been subject to regulatory review and potential remedial action.

## Site record
District 10 is documented in the **Hwy 132 Clean Fill Source Testing Exhibit.pdf** as the entity that generated and stored the soil stockpiles at issue. The stockpiles are located adjacent to SR 132 in Stanislaus County, and were created during District 10’s road maintenance and construction activities. The document does not specify the exact volume of the stockpiles; however, sampling data from multiple stockpile locations (referred to as SP-1 through SP-5 in the document) were collected by District 10 staff between 2019 and 2020. Results indicate that certain stockpiles contain elevated levels of **barium** (up to 760 mg/kg at stockpile SP-3, reported June 2020) and **arsenic** (up to 18 mg/kg at stockpile SP-4, reported November 2019), exceeding the California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs) for residential land use. District 10 conducted these tests as part of a “clean fill source testing” program to determine if the stockpiles could be reused as fill material without further remediation.

## Regulatory status
The applicable standards for the soil stockpiles are the **California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs)** for residential soil, set by the **California Environmental Protection Agency (CalEPA)**. For barium, the CHHSL is 5,200 mg/kg; for arsenic, it is 0.07 mg/kg. District 10’s sampling showed that arsenic exceeded the CHHSL at stockpile SP-4 (18 mg/kg), while barium remained below its CHHSL. The **Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC)** has regulatory oversight for remediation activities in California. No formal enforcement actions or compliance orders were documented in the provided exhibit, but DTSC review is implied by the testing requirements. As of the document’s date (December 2020), District 10 had not received a final determination regarding stockpile reuse or disposal.

## Open items
- District 10 has not completed characterization of all stockpiles (SP-1 and SP-2 were not sampled as of the December 2020 document).
- DTSC approval is pending for the final disposition of stockpiles SP-3, SP-4, and SP-5.
- The exhibit notes that “additional testing may be required for per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) and other emerging contaminants,” but District 10 has not conducted such testing.
- A Soil Management Plan (SMP) has been drafted by District 10 but has not been finalized or approved by DTSC as of the document’s date.
