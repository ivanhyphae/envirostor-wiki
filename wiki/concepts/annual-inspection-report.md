## Overview

The **Annual Inspection Report** is a recurring deliverable under the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA). It documents the physical condition of the site’s engineered controls—stockpile covers, fencing, signage, and vegetation—and identifies any required corrective maintenance. The report is prepared by Caltrans District 10 (Modesto) and submitted to DTSC for review. The first annual inspection occurred in March 2025, as confirmed in the email correspondence.

## Site Record

- **Document cited:** Email chain *“RE: Reminder - Annual Inspection Report”* (dated February 5, 2025 – June 10, 2025) between DTSC’s Arielle Mcleskey and Caltrans’ Adam Inman (PG). The chain includes the 2025 inspection findings and references the [[land-use-covenant]] (LUC).
- **First Annual Inspection:** Conducted March 18, 2025, by Adam Inman. The facility was described as “less than two years old and still in great condition. No issues were observed.” A Stockpile Inspection Form was attached to the email.
- **Vegetation and Slope Condition (June 10, 2025 follow-up):** Inman reported no significant change in vegetation due to low rainfall. The slope remained in good condition with no erosion or disturbance. Re‑hydroseeding is planned for the 2025 rainy season.
- **Planned Construction:** Northern retaining wall construction is scheduled to begin in Fall 2026.
- **Template for Future Inspections:** DTSC noted that the LUC and the Removal Action Completion Report (dated [Final Removal Action Completion Report]) did not include an inspection form template. DTSC recommended that Caltrans create a reusable checklist‑format template and offered examples from other sites. The Stockpile Inspection Form created for the 2025 inspection will serve as the template going forward.

## Regulatory Status

- **Agency Oversight:** DTSC oversees the inspection under the Corrective Action Consent Agreement and the LUC.
- **Applicable Standard:** The inspection verifies the integrity of site controls (covers, fencing, signage, vegetation). No numeric standard applies; the report is evaluated on whether controls are functional.
- **Compliance:** As of the 2025 inspection, the site was in compliance. No deficiencies were noted.

## Open Items

- **Re‑hydroseeding:** Planned for the 2025 rainy season to improve vegetation cover on the slope. Status of that activity is not documented in the source.
- **Northern Retaining Wall Construction:** Scheduled for Fall 2026. The inspection should confirm that this construction does not compromise site controls.
- **Inspection Template Finalization:** DTSC requested that Caltrans finalize a standard inspection form for future use. The Stockpile Inspection Form used in 2025 is the initial template but has not been formally approved in a regulatory document.
- **First‑Year Inspection Record:** Since 2025 was the first annual inspection, there is no prior baseline to compare findings. Future reports will establish trend data.
