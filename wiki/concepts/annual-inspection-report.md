## Overview

The **Annual Inspection Report** is a recurring deliverable under the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project (SR 132, Stanislaus County, CA). It documents the physical condition of the site’s engineered controls (stockpile covers, fencing, signage) and the need for any corrective maintenance. The report is produced by the project’s environmental consultant and submitted to the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) for review and concurrence.

## Site Record

- **Document Cited:** *TO97 GW Rpt_final.20230308.pdf* (groundwater monitoring report dated March 8, 2023) includes reference to an annual inspection conducted for the stockpile area. The report notes that covers are intact, no erosion or damage was observed, and access controls remain functional.
- **Inspection Frequency:** Annual, typically coinciding with the wet-season groundwater monitoring event (late winter/early spring).
- **Findings (2023):** No deficiencies reported. Stockpile covers (HDPE geomembrane) remained in place; no tears, wind damage, or signs of settlement. Perimeter fencing was intact. Warning signs legible and present.
- **Report Owner:** Caltrans Modesto District (District 10), prepared by their environmental contractor (not named in available sources).

## Regulatory Status

- **Agency Oversight:** DTSC regulates the stockpile covers as a Site Management Activity under the Corrective Action Consent Agreement. The annual inspection report satisfies a requirement to verify the integrity of interim remedial measures.
- **Applicable Standard:** No specific numeric standard for inspection results; the report is evaluated on whether the covers and controls are functional and whether any corrective actions are needed.
- **Compliance:** As of the 2023 report, the site was in compliance with cover maintenance conditions.
