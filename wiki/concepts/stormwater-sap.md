## Overview
The Stormwater SAP (Stormwater Sampling and Analysis Plan) is a regulatory document approved by the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB) on December 23, 2019, as part of the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles remediation project (SR 132, Stanislaus County). It specifies procedures for collecting stormwater runoff samples from the site’s stockpile areas to verify that discharge meets water quality standards during and after remediation. The SAP is referenced under source file S2350-01-02 Stormwater SAP_12.23.md and governs all stormwater monitoring activities for the project.

## Site record
The SAP was developed by Caltrans in coordination with DTSC to address stormwater runoff from soil stockpiles at the SR 132 remediation site. Documented specifications include:
- **Sampling locations**: Designated stormwater collection points at stockpile perimeter, including unnamed discharge points (Discharge Point 001) and upstream/downstream monitoring sites (SW-1 and SW-2). Coordinates and specific identifiers are not listed in the source; only general locations are noted.
- **Parameters**: Total suspended solids (TSS), pH, turbidity, heavy metals (barium, chromium, lead), and petroleum hydrocarbons. Thresholds include barium at 760 mg/kg (stockpile soil) and MCL of 1,000 µg/L for water; chromium at 100 mg/kg; lead at 150 mg/kg; total petroleum hydrocarbons (TPH) at 10 mg/L.
- **Frequency**: Quarterly sampling during storm events (October–April) for Phase 1 (2019–2020), with monthly sampling in high-flow periods. Post-Phase 1 verification sampling is reduced to semi-annual if no exceedances are recorded.
- **Results**: As of the SAP approval date (December 23, 2019), no exceedances of MCLs or action levels were documented in stormwater samples from the initial stockpile monitoring. Two samples (SW-1, October 14, 2019; SW-2, November 5, 2019) showed TSS below 30 mg/L, meeting the NPDES threshold. Barium in SW-1 was 1.2 µg/L, below MCL.
- **Verification**: CVRWQCB approved the SAP as compliance documentation for the NPDES permit No. CAS000001 (general construction stormwater permit), contingent on adherence to sampling protocols and quarterly reporting.

## Regulatory status
The SAP is subject to:
- **Agencies**: CVRWQCB (primary), DTSC (advisory), and EPA (NPDES oversight). Standards are based on CVRWQCB Basin Plan (water quality objectives) and NPDES permit conditions.
- **Thresholds**: MCLs (e.g., barium 1,000 µg/L, chromium 100 mg/L), action levels (TSS <30 mg/L, turbidity <5 NTU), and background levels (stockpile soil contaminants below 90th percentile for Stanislaus County).
- **Compliance**: The site is in full compliance as of SAP approval; no corrective actions or penalties are pending. The SAP is a continuing requirement for Phase 2 (2020–2021) unless modified.

## Open items
- **Post-Phase 1 review**: A verification report is due by April 15, 2020, per SAP Section 10, to confirm if stormwater data meet MCL action levels. If exceedances are detected, a revised SAP must be submitted within 30 days.
- **Contingency**: If TSS exceeds 50 mg/L in two consecutive quarterly samples, CVRWQCB may require additional sediment containment, as noted in SAP Section 12. No such data are documented yet.
- **Pending**: The project’s final stormwater discharge status (e.g., ambient vs. permit closure) is unresolved; CVRWQCB has not issued a certification of completion.
