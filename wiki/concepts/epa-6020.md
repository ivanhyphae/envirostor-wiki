## Definition

**EPA Method 6020** (SW‑846 Method 6020) is an analytical procedure published by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) for the determination of trace elements (metals and metalloids) in aqueous samples, extracts, and digests using **inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry (ICP-MS)**. It is widely applied in environmental monitoring programs, including groundwater investigations where low detection limits are required.

## How it works

Method 6020 relies on ICP‑MS technology:

1. **Sample introduction**: A liquid sample is nebulized into an aerosol and transported into an argon plasma (typically ~6000–8000 K) by a peristaltic pump and nebulizer.

2. **Ionization**: The high‑temperature plasma atomizes the sample and ionizes the analyte elements, producing singly charged positive ions.

3. **Mass separation**: The ions are extracted through a series of cones (sampler and skimmer) into a vacuum system and focused into a quadrupole mass filter. The quadrupole separates ions based on their mass‑to‑charge ratio (m/z).

4. **Detection**: A pulse‑counting or analog electron multiplier detects the ions. The signal is processed and quantitated against certified calibration standards.

5. **Data handling**: Interferences (e.g., polyatomic species, isobaric overlaps) are minimized using collision/reaction cells, mathematical correction equations (e.g., for argon‑chloride interference on arsenic), or internal standardization (e.g., indium, yttrium).

EPA Method 6020 specifies instrument performance criteria, calibration procedures (initial and continuing), quality control requirements (blanks, duplicates, matrix spikes, laboratory control samples), and acceptance limits for precision and accuracy.

## Variants

- **SW‑846 Method 6020A** – A minor revision published in 1998 that clarified quality control criteria and updated acceptable performance parameters. It is functionally equivalent to the original 6020 but is the most commonly cited version.

- **SW‑846 Method 6020B** – A later revision (published in 2014) that provides expanded guidance on the use of kinetic energy discrimination (KED) and other interference‑reduction techniques, as well as updated calibration and tuning requirements.

- **EPA Method 200.8** – An alternative ICP‑MS method for trace elements in drinking water and wastewater, developed by the EPA Office of Water. It shares many technical features with 6020 but differs in scope and QC specifications.

- **Field‑deployable ICP‑MS systems** – While not a formal variant of the method, portable ICP‑MS instruments have been used in some monitoring programs, following method 6020 principles with modifications for field operation.

## Trade‑offs

| Advantage | Limitation |
|---|---|
| Very low detection limits (sub‑ppb for most elements) | High instrument and per‑sample cost |
| Multi‑element capability (20+ elements per run) | Requires skilled operator and rigorous maintenance |
| Rapid analysis (minutes per sample) | Sample matrix can cause interferences (e.g., high total dissolved solids) |
| Isotope ratio information available | Sample preparation (acid digestion/filtration) is often required |
| Widely accepted for regulatory compliance | Not suitable for volatile or organometallic species without special handling |

Specific considerations for groundwater applications (as seen in the Modesto stockpiles reports):

- High dissolved solids in groundwater can cause cone blockage and signal drift.
- Iron and calcium can form polyatomic interferences on elements like arsenic and selenium.
- Method 6020 may need to be supplemented with EPA Method 6020A/6020B quality control steps to meet project‑specific data quality objectives.
