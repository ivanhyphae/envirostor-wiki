## Final Feasibility Study Report – Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles

### Overview
The Final Feasibility Study Report (Final FS) for the Caltrans Modesto Soil Stockpiles is the primary remedial planning document that evaluates cleanup alternatives and recommends a preferred remedial action for the Site. Prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc. (Geocon Project No. S9800-01-17, Task Order No. 17, EA 10-0X2700, Contract No. 06A1895) and dated June 24, 2014, the Final FS was submitted to the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) in accordance with the Interagency Agreement between DTSC, the Central Valley Regional Water Quality Control Board (CVRWQCB), and Caltrans dated June 22, 2012. The report incorporates revisions made in response to DTSC’s comments provided in their letter dated February 11, 2014.

### Site Record
The Site is located south of the State Route 99/Kansas Avenue interchange in Modesto, Stanislaus County, California. The stockpiles contain elevated levels of barium and are planned to be contained within the SR-132 West Freeway/Expressway Project, which is being developed in coordination with the Stanislaus Council of Governments (StanCOG). The SR-132 project includes an interim progress phase (planned by 2018) and ultimate build-out (planned by 2028).

**Documented facts from the Final FS:**
- The Final FS was prepared by Geocon Consultants, Inc., 3160 Gold Valley Drive, Suite 800, Rancho Cordova, CA 95742.
- The Final FS describes the proposed remedial alternative for the Site.
- The stockpiles are planned to be used as embankment material for roadway construction, retaining wall backfill, and bridge abutments.
- Remedial and contour cut/fill grading will be necessary to achieve final finish grades and properly consolidate and contain the existing soil stockpiles.
- A cross-section for Stockpile #3 is included in the report (Figure 9), showing planned interim progress phase and ultimate build-out cross-sections.
- The report includes a conceptual site exposure model (CSEM), soil impacts from multiple investigations (Shaw 2004 PSI, Shaw 2006 SI, Geocon 2012 SSI), and groundwater impacts.
- Acronyms used in the report include: ARARs (applicable or relevant and appropriate requirements), BMP (best management practice), COPC (contaminant of potential concern), DTSC, CVRWQCB, Caltrans, and others.

### Regulatory Status
The Final FS addresses regulatory requirements applicable to the Site, including:
- **Porter-Cologne Water Quality Control Act** (California Water Code Sections 13240, 13241, 13242, 13243) – Water Quality Control Plan (Basin Plan) requirements.
- **DTSC oversight** under the Interagency Agreement dated June 22, 2012.
- The report identifies applicable or relevant and appropriate requirements (ARARs), categorized as chemical-, action-, or location-specific.
- Standards referenced include: Maximum Contaminant Levels (MCLs), California Human Health Screening Levels (CHHSLs), Environmental Screening Levels (ESLs), Total Threshold Limit Concentration (TTLC), and Soluble Threshold Limit Concentration (STLC).
- Agencies involved: DTSC, CVRWQCB, California Air Resources Board (CARB), San Joaquin Valley Air Pollution Control District (SJVAPCD), California Division of Occupational Safety and Health (Cal-OSHA), and U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (USEPA).
