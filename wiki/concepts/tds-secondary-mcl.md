## Overview

The **TDS Secondary MCL** refers to the California Secondary Maximum Contaminant Level for total dissolved solids (TDS), established under Title 22 of the California Code of Regulations. For this project (SR 132, Stanislaus County), the Secondary MCL of 500 mg/L (recommended) and 1,000 mg/L (upper limit) for drinking water was applied as a screening benchmark during characterization and remediation of the Modesto Soil Stockpiles. It is not a cleanup standard for the stockpiles (which are not a drinking water source) but was used to evaluate potential groundwater impact from stockpile leachate.

## Site record

Documented monitoring data in the project record indicate that TDS concentrations in groundwater from on‑site wells (MW‑5, MW‑6, MW‑7) ranged from 620 mg/L to 1,480 mg/L in samples collected between July 2021 and June 2022 (Quarterly Groundwater Monitoring Reports, 2021–2022). Several samples exceeded the Secondary MCL upper limit of 1,000 mg/L, notably at MW‑5 and MW‑7 during the 2021 dry season. Soil stockpile leachate samples (collected from lysimeters installed in Stockpiles A and C) showed TDS values from 890 mg/L to 2,100 mg/L (Stockpile Characterization Summary, March 2022). The highest TDS was associated with Stockpile C, which contained gypsum‑amended soils from prior construction. No specific remedial action was triggered by TDS exceedances, as the Secondary MCL is advisory rather than enforceable for this project. However, the data were included in the Risk Assessment Report (November 2022) to document baseline groundwater quality.

## Regulatory status

The California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) approved the use of the Title 22 Secondary MCL as a screening level in the **Site Management Plan** (Revision 3, 2020). DTSC does not require remediation for TDS above the SMCL unless it threatens a Beneficial Use (e.g., a domestic water supply). Because the project site is not a source of drinking water and no downgradient wells were affected, the secondary exceedances were not escalated to an action level. The project is currently in compliance with all applicable regulatory standards, including the Secondary MCL, as documented in the **2023 Compliance Summary Report**.

## Open items

- A pending evaluation of whether TDS migration from Stockpile C has reached the shallow aquifer beneath the SR 132 right‑of‑way is required under Condition 14 of the **Removal Action Workplan Approval Letter** (DTSC, May 2021). The evaluation is due with the next annual report (February 2025).
- No other open items related specifically to TDS Secondary MCL were identified in the reviewed project file.
