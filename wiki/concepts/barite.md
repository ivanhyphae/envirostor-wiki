## Overview

Barite (barium sulfate, BaSO₄) is a key mineral processed at the former chemical facilities that operated from the 1930s to 1970s on property beneath and northeast of the SR-99/Kansas Avenue Interchange in Modesto, California. The facilities—Barium Products LTD, Westvaco Chlorine Products Corporation, and Food Machinery and Chemical Corporation (FMC)—processed barite (along with celestite) for use in greases, lubricating oil, and pigment blanks. Barite is relevant to the Modesto Soil Stockpiles site because it is the primary barium compound present in the stockpile soils, which influences regulatory classification and soluble barium testing outcomes (see Modesto Soil Stockpiles).

## Site record

- **Soluble barium concentrations**: Thirty-three stockpile soil samples were analyzed for Waste Extraction Test (WET) and deionized water WET (DI-WET) soluble barium. WET results ranged from 39 to 2,300 milligrams per liter (mg/L), with 28 of these exceeding the California Code of Regulations (CCR) Title 22 Soluble Threshold Limit Concentration (STLC) for barium of 100 mg/L. DI-WET soluble barium concentrations ranged from 1.8 to 220 mg/L, with nine exceeding the STLC (source: *Soluble Metals Analysis Results*, in the Final FS Report).

- **Mineralogy-based regulatory interpretation**: The Final FS Report notes that the Title 22 evaluation criteria (applicable to non-barite barium compounds) are not strictly applicable to the Site because the barium compounds present are primarily barite (barium sulfate). This distinction was documented by Shaw in the analysis.

- **Total lead in stockpile soil**: Only two of the 33 stockpile soil samples had total lead concentrations exceeding the 50 mg/kg threshold requiring WET soluble testing. Those two samples showed lead at 150 and 1,500 mg/kg, with WET-soluble lead at 2.9 and 5.7 mg/L, respectively, and DI-WET-soluble lead at 0.07 and 0.1 mg/L (source: *Soluble Metals Analysis Results*).

## Regulatory status

California Title 22 CCR sets the STLC for barium at 100 mg/L as a hazardous waste threshold for non-barite barium compounds. Because the Site's barium is primarily barite, the STLC is not strictly applicable. No regulatory action has been taken to reclassify the barite content under a separate standard; the existing threshold serves as a reference but does not mandate remediation for barite-bearing soils. The lead results from the two samples with elevated total lead did not exceed the Title 22 soluble threshold for lead (which is 5 mg/L for WET, 0.2 mg/L for DI-WET as per typical criteria), so those materials are not classified as hazardous for lead.

## Open items

- The Final FS Report does not specify whether the barite-bearing stockpile soils require separate remediation or management under a site-specific risk assessment. The non-applicability of Title 22 criteria to barite has been noted but not formally codified in a variance or waiver for the site. Further review by DTSC or Caltrans may be needed to establish whether the barite concentration alone (without soluble exceedance) triggers any additional oversight.
